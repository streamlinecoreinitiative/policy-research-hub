# micro-insurance for smallholder drought risk in the Horn of Africa: cutting disaster losses for vulnerable groups

**Enhancing Climate Resilience in Sub-Saharan Africa Through Integrated Water Security and Clean Energy Solutions**

### Executive Summary

Sub-Saharan Africa faces significant challenges related to water security, clean energy access, and climate adaptation due to limited infrastructure and resources. To address these issues, we recommend implementing micro-hydropower plants, rainwater harvesting systems, and solar-powered irrigation pumps in rural areas to enhance resilience against droughts and floods. This integrated approach can improve the lives of millions while contributing to broader development goals.

### Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa is characterized by arid and semi-arid regions with limited access to reliable water sources. Climate change intensifies droughts and floods, making traditional infrastructure unsustainable. Lack of clean energy exacerbates the situation, contributing to health risks from indoor air pollution. Addressing these issues requires a holistic approach that integrates water security and climate resilience.

According to World Bank data (2024), only 32.4% of the population in Sub-Saharan Africa has access to safely managed drinking water [1]. Access to electricity remains low at 53.3%, disproportionately affecting rural areas [2].

### Analysis

Water security is a critical issue in Sub-Saharan Africa, where access to safe drinking water is alarmingly low (32.4%) compared to the global average of 73.7% [1]. Clean energy access is also a challenge, with only 53.3% of the population having access to electricity, which exacerbates existing vulnerabilities in rural areas [2].

Climate adaptation needs are pressing, as increased frequency and intensity of climate-related events necessitate robust water security and clean energy solutions.
