**Executive Summary**

The Ethiopian Highlands are a critical agricultural region facing severe water scarcity due to climate change, characterized by erratic rainfall patterns. Traditional irrigation methods exacerbate poverty among smallholder farmers by being energy-intensive and costly. This brief explores the potential of integrating agroforestry with micro-hydro systems as a sustainable solution for climate-resilient irrigation in the Ethiopian Highlands.

**Water Scarcity in the Ethiopian Highlands**

The Ethiopian Highlands are one of the most critical agricultural regions in Africa, with approximately 70% of the country's population relying on rain-fed agriculture (World Bank, 2024). However, climate change has led to erratic rainfall patterns, resulting in severe water scarcity. The region experiences a significant decline in rainfall during the dry season.

**Current Irrigation Practices and Challenges**

Traditional irrigation methods in the Ethiopian Highlands include boreholes and canals, which are energy-intensive and costly. These systems suffer from high water loss through evaporation and seepage (World Bank Reports).

Micro-hydro technology is a small-scale hydroelectric system that can provide renewable energy for irrigation purposes. These systems reduce reliance on diesel generators and increase energy efficiency (World Bank Reports).
