**Agroforestry-Based Micro-Hydro Systems for Climate-Resilient Irrigation in the Central African Rainforest**

### Executive Summary

The Central African Rainforest faces severe water scarcity due to climate change, impacting agriculture and smallholder farmers' livelihoods. Integrating agroforestry with micro-hydro systems can provide a sustainable solution enhancing both water security and renewable energy use.

### Introduction to Water Scarcity

Climate change affects agriculture in the Central African Rainforest through erratic rainfall patterns and increased evaporation from wetlands, impacting water availability for crops. Rising temperatures and altered precipitation patterns are projected to continue, exacerbating these challenges.

*   **Erratic Rainfall Patterns:** [Verify] Climate models predict a decrease in annual rainfall.
*   **Increased Evaporation:** Wetland areas will experience increased evaporation rates due to warmer temperatures.

### Current Irrigation Practices and Challenges

Traditional irrigation methods are energy-intensive and costly, exacerbating economic challenges for smallholder farmers. Existing infrastructure includes boreholes and canals, which suffer from high water loss through evaporation and seepage.

*   **Water Loss:** [Verify] Up to 50% of irrigation water is lost due to evaporation and seepage.
*   **Economic Costs:** Labor, maintenance, and fuel costs associated with traditional irrigation methods are substantial.

Micro-hydro technology provides renewable energy for irrigation systems. Successful micro-hydro projects have enhanced irrigation efficiency by reducing reliance on diesel generators.
