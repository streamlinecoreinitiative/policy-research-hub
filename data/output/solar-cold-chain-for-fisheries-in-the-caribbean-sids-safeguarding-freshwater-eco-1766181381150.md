## Introduction to Water Scarcity in the Central America Dry Corridor

The Central America Dry Corridor (CADC) is one of the most critical agricultural regions globally, facing severe water scarcity due to climate change. Erratic rainfall patterns and increased evaporation from vast wetlands exacerbate droughts, affecting food security and economic stability for smallholder farmers. Traditional irrigation methods are energy-intensive and costly, further straining resources in communities already vulnerable to drought.

### Climate Change Impacts on Agriculture

*   Rising temperatures increase water demand for crops.
*   Changing precipitation patterns lead to more frequent droughts and floods.
*   Increased evaporation from wetlands reduces available water resources.

### Importance of Resilient Irrigation Methods

*   Food security is directly linked to access to reliable irrigation systems.
*   Economic stability depends on sustainable agricultural practices that minimize water waste.
*   Climate-resilient irrigation methods can help communities adapt to changing environmental conditions.

## Current Irrigation Practices and Challenges
