# watershed co-management with indigenous councils in the Mekong basin: stabilizing food security during droughts

**Watershed Co-management with Indigenous Councils in the Mekong Basin: Stabilizing Food Security during Droughts**

**Executive Summary**

The Ethiopian Highlands face severe water scarcity due to climate change, affecting agricultural production and food security. Integrating agroforestry with micro-hydro systems can enhance water security and reduce poverty among smallholder farmers.

**Section 1: Introduction to Water Scarcity in the Ethiopian Highlands**

Climate change has significant implications for agriculture in Ethiopia:

*   Rising temperatures
*   Changes in rainfall patterns, including more frequent droughts and floods
*   Impacts on crop yields, reduced productivity, and lower quality crops

Sustainable irrigation methods are essential for mitigating these impacts.

**Section 2: Current Irrigation Practices and Challenges**

Existing irrigation systems in the Ethiopian Highlands include:

*   Boreholes: drilled wells that extract groundwater
*   Canals: surface water diversion for irrigation

Agroforestry systems offer numerous benefits for climate resilience:

*   Soil health improvement
*   Biodiversity conservation
*   Carbon sequestration

Case studies demonstrate the effectiveness of agroforestry systems in improving water retention and reducing erosion.

To support the adoption of these systems at scale, policy recommendations include:

1.  **Financial incentives**: subsidies or low-interest loans to encourage farmers to adopt agroforestry-micro-hydro systems.
2.  **Capacity building**: training programs for farmers, extension agents, and policymakers on integrated watershed management.
