# water loss reduction in secondary cities in the Andean highlands: improving health outcomes in heatwaves

**Water Security, Clean Energy, and Climate Adaptation: Strengthening Resilience in Sub-Saharan Africa**

### Executive Summary

In order to address the pressing challenges of water security, clean energy access, and climate adaptation in sub-Saharan Africa, we recommend prioritizing infrastructure development for water supply systems, investing in renewable energy projects, and enhancing climate-resilient water management systems. By doing so, we can improve health outcomes, reduce economic losses, and foster sustainable development in one of the world's most vulnerable regions.

### Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa faces significant challenges in achieving its Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs), particularly in relation to water security and clean energy access. According to World Bank data, only 32.4% of the population in sub-Saharan Africa has access to safely managed drinking water [1], while the region's electricity access rate stands at a mere 53.3%, far below global averages [2]. Furthermore, renewable energy consumption remains minimal at 19.7%, hindering efforts towards climate resilience and sustainability [3].

### Analysis

The region's limited infrastructure and resources pose significant challenges in achieving sustainable development goals. For instance, the lack of sufficient investments in water supply systems leads to inadequate access to safe drinking water sources.

* **Water Security**: Sub-Saharan Africa faces severe water scarcity issues with only 32.4% of its population having access to safely managed drinking water [1]. This figure highlights the urgent need for improved water management systems.
* **Clean Energy**: The region's reliance on fossil fuels is a major obstacle in achieving its renewable energy targets, which stand at just 19.7%. Sub-Saharan Africa must invest more in solar and wind power projects to meet global climate goals.

### Policy Recommendations

We recommend the following policy interventions:

1. **Enhance Water Security**:
	* Invest in infrastructure development for water supply systems, including the construction of boreholes and pipelines.
	* Improve access to safe drinking water sources through the implementation of climate-resilient water management systems.
2. **Promote Renewable Energy**:
	* Encourage renewable energy projects such as solar farms and small hydroelectric plants.
	* Provide financial incentives and subsidies to promote adoption of clean energy technologies.
3. **Climate Adaptation**:
	* Implement early warning systems for droughts and floods.
	* Enhance disaster risk reduction measures and support climate-resilient agriculture practices.

### Implementation Considerations

While the costs associated with infrastructure development can be substantial, they are offset by savings from reduced waterborne diseases and improved health outcomes. Investment in renewable energy projects requires government grants or concessional loans. Potential barriers include resistance to change from communities who may not understand the benefits of new technologies, bureaucratic hurdles, and limited financial resources.

### Conclusion

Strengthening resilience through enhanced water security, clean energy access, and climate adaptation is crucial for sub-Saharan Africa. By prioritizing these initiatives now, we can mitigate future risks and foster sustainable development in one of the world's most vulnerable regions.
