**Context & Challenge**
Sub-Saharan Africa is home to 54% of the world's remaining arid lands and faces increasing water scarcity due to climate change [Source: World Bank Open Data]. The region has limited access to safe drinking water, with only 32.4% of the population having access to safely managed drinking water (World Bank Open Data). Additionally, low electricity penetration at 53.3% exacerbates livelihoods, health, and economic stability (World Bank Open Data).

**Analysis**
The evidence suggests that Sub-Saharan Africa lacks necessary infrastructure for sustainable water supply and energy production. Climate change has intensified droughts, floods, and other extreme weather events, further straining existing systems and threatening food security (IPCC). The region's limited financial resources hinder investment in climate-resilient projects.

**Policy Recommendations**

1. **Enhance Water Security through Improved Infrastructure:** Invest $2 billion per year in desalination plants, rainwater harvesting systems, and water distribution networks to ensure safe drinking water access for all.
	* Potential barriers: High initial costs, limited financing options
2. **Promote Renewable Energy Access:** Increase the share of renewable energy in electricity consumption from 19.7% to at least 30-40% by 2030 through solar and wind projects costing $1 billion annually [VERIFY].
	* Potential barriers: High upfront costs, limited grid infrastructure
3. **Enhance Climate Resilience in Agriculture:** Implement climate-smart agricultural practices to increase crop yields while reducing greenhouse gas emissions, requiring an estimated $200 million per year in research and extension services (FAO estimates).

**Implementation Considerations**
Collaboration between governments, NGOs, and private sector entities is crucial for successful implementation. Pilot projects can be initiated in selected regions with high vulnerability to demonstrate the benefits of these interventions.

**Conclusion**
Sub-Saharan Africa requires immediate policy action to address water scarcity, energy poverty, and climate change. By implementing cost-effective solutions such as improved infrastructure for clean water access, increased renewable energy penetration, and climate-resilient agricultural practices, we can enhance resilience and improve living standards in the region. Next steps should include launching pilot projects and securing additional funding from multilateral development banks and philanthropic organizations.
