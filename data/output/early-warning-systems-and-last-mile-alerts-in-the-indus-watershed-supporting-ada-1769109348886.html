Enhancing Water Security, Clean Energy Access, and Climate Resilience in Sub-Saharan Africa: A Comprehensive Policy Framework

Executive Summary

Sub-Saharan Africa faces significant challenges in water security, clean energy access, and climate resilience. With only 32.4% of its population having access to safely managed drinking water (World Bank Open Data, 2024) [1] and 53.3% having access to electricity (World Bank Open Data, 2023), the region is vulnerable to frequent droughts, floods, and other extreme weather events. To build a resilient future, governments must prioritize investments in water infrastructure, expand renewable energy sources, and implement climate-resilient agricultural practices.

Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa's basic services like clean water and reliable power are severely lacking due to limited resources and political instability (World Bank Open Data, 2024) [1]. Climate change exacerbates these challenges, leading to more frequent droughts, floods, and other extreme weather events. Without effective interventions, these issues will further strain already fragile economies.
