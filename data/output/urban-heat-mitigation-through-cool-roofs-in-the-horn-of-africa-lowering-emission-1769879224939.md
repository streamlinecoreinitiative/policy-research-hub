## Executive Summary

Sub-Saharan Africa faces significant challenges with water scarcity, limited access to electricity, and climate vulnerability. To address these issues, we recommend prioritizing investments in water infrastructure, renewable energy projects, and climate adaptation measures.

## Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa is one of the regions most vulnerable to climate change impacts. Water scarcity and limited access to electricity are major challenges that exacerbate poverty and hinder economic development. According to World Bank data, only 32.4% of Sub-Saharan Africa's population has access to safely managed drinking water (World Bank Open Data), while 53.3% have access to electricity (World Bank Open Data). This lack of power hinders development and exacerbates water scarcity by reducing the efficiency of water pumps.
