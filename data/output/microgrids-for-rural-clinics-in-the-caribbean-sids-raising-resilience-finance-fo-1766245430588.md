# microgrids for rural clinics in the Caribbean SIDS: raising resilience finance for communities

**Microgrids for Rural Clinics in the Caribbean SIDS: Enhancing Resilience Finance through Sustainable Energy Solutions**

**Executive Summary**

Climate change poses significant threats to water security and clean energy in lower-income countries, particularly in the Caribbean Small Island Developing States (SIDS). This brief focuses on microgrids as a solution for rural clinics in the Caribbean SIDS, enhancing resilience finance for communities.

**I. Introduction**

The Caribbean SIDS face numerous environmental challenges, including water scarcity, unreliable energy supply, and increased vulnerability to climate-related disasters. Rural clinics are often at the forefront of these challenges, requiring reliable access to clean energy and water for healthcare services. Microgrids offer a promising solution by providing decentralized renewable energy systems that can improve energy security and enhance community resilience.

**II. Water Security and Clean Energy in the Caribbean SIDS**

### **Water Scarcity and Climate Change Impacts**

* Erratic rainfall patterns and increased evaporation rates contribute to water scarcity.
* Agriculture, a significant sector for many Caribbean SIDS, is heavily reliant on irrigation systems.
* High water loss percentages from existing irrigation systems are reported, but specific data is needed [verify].

### **Renewable Energy and Microgrids**

* The potential of renewable energy sources (solar, wind, hydro) is significant in the Caribbean SIDS.
* Microgrids offer a decentralized approach to energy generation and distribution, reducing reliance on diesel generators.

**III. Climate Adaptation through Integrated Agroforestry-Micro-Hydro Systems**

### **Agroforestry Benefits**

* Soil health improvement
* Biodiversity enhancement
* Carbon sequestration

### **Micro-Hydro Technology**

* Small-scale hydroelectric systems for efficient water use
* Reduced reliance on diesel generators and increased energy efficiency

**IV. Policy Recommendations and Future Research Directions**
