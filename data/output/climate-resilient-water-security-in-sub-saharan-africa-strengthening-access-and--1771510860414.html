Climate Resilient Water Security in Sub-Saharan Africa: Strengthening Access and Energy for Sustainable Development

Executive Summary

This policy brief highlights the critical need to enhance climate resilience in water security and clean energy access in Sub-Saharan Africa. With only 32.4% of the region's population having access to safely managed drinking water (World Bank, 2024) and just over half (53.3%) accessing electricity (World Bank, 2023), addressing these gaps is urgent for improving health outcomes and reducing economic disparities. We recommend implementing targeted policies for water security, promoting renewable energy adoption, and enhancing climate adaptation strategies.

Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa faces significant challenges in accessing safe drinking water and reliable energy sources. The region's infrastructure lacks the capacity to meet basic needs, exacerbating health risks and economic challenges (World Bank, 2023). Climate variability intensifies existing vulnerabilities, making it essential to bolster resilience in these critical sectors.

According to World Bank data (2024), only 32.4% of the population has access to safely managed drinking water, with a stark disparity among rural populations and women. Similarly, the region lags behind other regions in electricity access, with just over half (53.3%) having access to electricity (World Bank, 2023).
