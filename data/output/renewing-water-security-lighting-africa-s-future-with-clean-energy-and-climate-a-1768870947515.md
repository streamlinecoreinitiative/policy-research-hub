# Renewing Water Security, Lighting Africa’s Future with Clean Energy and Climate Adaptation

**Renewing Water Security, Lighting Africa’s Future with Clean Energy and Climate Adaptation**

## Executive Summary

Immediate action is needed to address the significant challenges in accessing clean water and electricity in lower-income countries of Sub-Saharan Africa. This policy brief recommends investing $20 billion over five years in renewable energy infrastructure to expand solar and wind power generation, improving access to safe drinking water by constructing or upgrading water supply systems in rural areas, and promoting climate-resilient practices through early warning systems and drought-resistant crop varieties.

## Context & Challenge

Lower-income countries in Sub-Saharan Africa face substantial challenges in meeting basic needs. According to the World Bank (2024), only 32.4% of the population has access to safely managed drinking water, far below the global average at 73.7%. Similarly, the region's electricity access rate is 53.3%, lagging behind the global average of 91.6% (World Bank, 2023). Climate change exacerbates these challenges by increasing the frequency of droughts and floods, further straining existing infrastructure.

## Analysis

- **Water Security**: The critical need for improved water infrastructure and sanitation facilities is evident in the stark contrast between Sub-Saharan Africa's 32.4% access to safely managed drinking water (World Bank, 2024) and the global average at 73.7%. This highlights the urgent need for investment in water security.

- **Climate Adaptation**: Climate change impacts such as increased frequency of droughts and floods not only disrupt water supply but also damage existing infrastructure, exacerbating already limited resources.

## Policy Recommendations

1. **Invest in Renewable Energy Infrastructure**:
   - Invest $20 billion over the next five years to expand solar and wind power generation.
   - Source: World Bank (Renewable energy consumption % of TFEC, 2024)

2. **Enhance Water Security through Improved Infrastructure**:
   - Construct or upgrade water supply systems in rural areas to ensure access to safe drinking water for at least 75% of the population by 2030.
   - Source: World Bank (Access to safely managed drinking water %, Sub-Saharan Africa, 2024)

3. **Promote Climate Resilient Practices**:
   - Implement early warning systems and drought-resistant crop varieties in vulnerable regions.
   - Source: UN-Water/WHO (Global population lacking safe water, 2022)
