# Solar Cold-Chain for Fisheries in the Great Lakes Region: Lowering Emissions from Critical Sectors

## Executive Summary

This brief focuses on environmental resilience in lower-income countries, specifically addressing water security, clean energy, and climate adaptation through a case study of the Great Lakes region's fisheries. The proposed solution is to implement solar-powered cold-chain systems for fish storage and transportation.

## Environmental Resilience in Lower-Income Countries

### Water Security Challenges

*   Severe water scarcity affects agriculture, industry, and domestic use.
*   Erratic rainfall patterns exacerbate droughts and floods.
*   Current irrigation systems (e.g., boreholes, canals) are inefficient and prone to water loss.

### Clean Energy Opportunities

*   Renewable energy sources like solar power can reduce reliance on fossil fuels.
*   Micro-hydro technology offers increased energy efficiency and reduced emissions.
*   Agroforestry practices improve soil health, biodiversity, and carbon sequestration.

### Climate Adaptation Strategies

*   Integrated agroforestry and micro-hydro systems demonstrate climate resilience.
*   Pilot projects show cost-effectiveness and potential for scaling up.
*   Policy recommendations support adoption at scale, including subsidies and infrastructure development.
