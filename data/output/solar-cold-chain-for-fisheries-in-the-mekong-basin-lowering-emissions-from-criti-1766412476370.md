**Solar Cold-Chain for Fisheries in the Mekong Basin: Lowering Emissions from Critical Sectors**

**Executive Summary**

The Mekong Basin is home to over 60 million people who rely on fisheries as a primary source of income and food security [verify]. Climate change poses significant threats to fisheries, including rising water temperatures, changes in fish migration patterns, and increased frequency of extreme weather events. Traditional cold-chain systems for storing and transporting fish are energy-intensive and often rely on diesel generators, contributing to greenhouse gas emissions and air pollution.

This brief explores the potential of solar-powered cold-chains as a sustainable solution for fisheries in the Mekong Basin. By integrating renewable energy with efficient cooling technologies, fisheries can reduce their reliance on fossil fuels and lower emissions while maintaining quality.

The Mekong Basin is one of the most biodiverse regions globally, supporting a large number of fish species [verify]. Climate change poses significant threats to fisheries, including:

* Rising water temperatures
* Changes in fish migration patterns
* Increased frequency of extreme weather events

To address these challenges, the Mekong Basin countries have implemented various initiatives to promote sustainable fishing practices and enhance climate resilience.

**Section 2: Current Challenges and Opportunities**

Traditional cold-chains for storing and transporting fish in the Mekong Basin rely heavily on diesel generators, contributing to greenhouse gas emissions and air pollution. The sector's energy consumption is a significant portion of the region's overall energy demand [verify].

However, there are opportunities for innovation and transformation:

* Solar-powered cold-chains can provide a reliable source of power for fisheries
* Energy-efficient cooling technologies can reduce energy consumption while maintaining quality and safety standards

**Section 3: Renewable Energy Solutions**

Agroforestry and micro-hydro systems have been successfully integrated in various regions to enhance water security and promote sustainable agriculture practices. These approaches can be adapted for the Mekong Basin's context:

* Agroforestry systems can improve soil health, reduce erosion, and increase water retention
* Micro-hydro systems can provide a reliable source of renewable energy for fisheries and agricultural operations
