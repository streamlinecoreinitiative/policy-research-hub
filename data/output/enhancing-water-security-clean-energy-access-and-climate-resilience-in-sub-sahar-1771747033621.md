Sub-Saharan Africa faces significant challenges in accessing clean water and electricity, exacerbating climate vulnerabilities. * Allocate a significant portion of national budgets towards renewable energy projects to meet SDG 7 targets by 2030.
* Implement large-scale water infrastructure projects, including desalination plants and improved rural water supply systems.

These interventions are crucial for improving resilience while aligning with global sustainable development goals. ## Policy Recommendations

We recommend the following actionable steps:

1. **Renewable Energy Investment**: Allocate a significant portion of national budgets towards renewable energy projects to meet SDG 7 targets by 2030.
2. **Water Security Initiatives**: Implement large-scale water infrastructure projects, including desalination plants and improved rural water supply systems.
3. **Community Engagement**: Foster collaboration between government agencies, NGOs, and local communities to ensure equitable access to clean water and electricity.

## Implementation Considerations

While costs associated with renewable energy projects can be substantial, they are justified by long-term benefits in terms of health outcomes and economic growth. Potential barriers include bureaucratic delays, funding shortages, and lack of skilled labor for construction and maintenance. Innovative financing mechanisms like green bonds can help leverage additional financing through international partnerships.
