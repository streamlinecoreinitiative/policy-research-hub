# Mangrove Restoration and Blue Carbon Finance in the Ganges-Brahmaputra Delta: Enhancing Disaster Resilience for Vulnerable Groups

## Executive Summary

The Ganges-Brahmaputra delta is highly vulnerable to climate change, with frequent cyclones, floods, and droughts affecting millions of people. This brief highlights the importance of mangrove restoration and blue carbon finance in reducing disaster losses and promoting environmental resilience. We propose an integrated approach combining agroforestry-based micro-hydro systems for climate-resilient irrigation and mangrove restoration to support vulnerable communities.

## Environmental Resilience in Lower-Income Countries: A Focus on Water Security, Clean Energy, and Climate Adaptation

### I. Introduction to Water Scarcity in the Ganges-Brahmaputra Delta

The Ganges-Brahmaputra delta is home to over 150 million people, with a significant portion relying on agriculture for their livelihoods. Climate change has led to erratic rainfall patterns, increased water scarcity, and more frequent natural disasters, severely impacting food security, economic stability, and human well-being.

### II. Current Irrigation Practices and Challenges

Traditional irrigation systems in the region are often inefficient, leading to significant water loss through evaporation and seepage. The use of diesel generators for pumping water exacerbates energy insecurity and contributes to greenhouse gas emissions.
