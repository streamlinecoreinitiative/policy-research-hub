# Nature-Based Solutions for Landslide Risk in the Mekong Basin: Enhancing Water Security and Renewable Energy Use

## Executive Summary

The Mekong River Basin faces significant climate change impacts, including droughts and landslides that threaten food security. Traditional irrigation methods are often energy-intensive and costly, exacerbating economic challenges for smallholder farmers vulnerable to these disasters. This brief explores nature-based solutions integrating agroforestry with micro-hydro systems as a sustainable approach to enhance both water security and renewable energy use in the region.

## Introduction to Water Scarcity in the Mekong Basin

The Mekong River Basin is one of the most biodiverse regions globally, supporting over 60 million people. Climate change has led to severe droughts and landslides affecting agriculture and food security. Erratic rainfall patterns and increased evaporation from wetlands exacerbate water scarcity issues.

## Current Irrigation Practices and Challenges

Existing irrigation systems in the Mekong Basin include boreholes and canals, which suffer from high water loss through evaporation and seepage, reducing efficiency. Diesel generators power many irrigation pumps, contributing to greenhouse gas emissions and increasing energy costs for farmers.
