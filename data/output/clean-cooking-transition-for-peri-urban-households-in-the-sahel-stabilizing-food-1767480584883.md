# clean cooking transition for peri-urban households in the Sahel: stabilizing food security during droughts

**Clean Cooking Transition for Peri-Urban Households in the Sahel: Stabilizing Food Security During Droughts**

**Executive Summary**

The Sahel region is experiencing frequent droughts and erratic rainfall patterns due to climate change, affecting agricultural practices and food security in vulnerable communities. This brief proposes an integrated approach combining agroforestry with micro-hydro systems to enhance both water security and renewable energy use.

**Introduction**

Climate change has exacerbated the issues of droughts and erratic rainfall patterns in the Sahel region, leading to reduced water availability for crops. Traditional irrigation methods are often energy-intensive and costly, further exacerbating economic challenges for smallholder farmers.

**Agroforestry-Based Micro-Hydro Systems: A Sustainable Solution**

Agroforestry combines trees with agricultural practices to promote soil health, biodiversity, and water retention. Integrating agroforestry with micro-hydro systems can provide a sustainable solution that enhances both water security and renewable energy use.
