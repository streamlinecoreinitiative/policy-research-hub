**Executive Summary**

The Sudano-Sahelian belt is highly vulnerable to climate change, facing severe water scarcity and poverty. This brief highlights the importance of integrating cool roofs with clean energy solutions to mitigate urban heat island effects and promote resilience finance in communities. By leveraging agroforestry-based micro-hydro systems for irrigation efficiency and renewable energy adoption, we can ensure economic benefits while addressing immediate water scarcity issues.

**Section 1: Introduction to Water Scarcity and Urban Heat Island Effects**

The Sudano-Sahelian belt is characterized by high temperatures and low rainfall, making it one of the most susceptible regions to droughts. Climate change exacerbates these conditions, leading to severe water scarcity and poverty. The urban heat island effect further amplifies these challenges, with cities experiencing higher temperatures than surrounding rural areas due to increased built-up infrastructure.
