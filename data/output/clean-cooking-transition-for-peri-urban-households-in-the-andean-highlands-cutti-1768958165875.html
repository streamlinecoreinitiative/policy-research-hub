Building Resilient Water Security, Clean Energy Access, and Climate Adaptation in Sub-Saharan Africa

Executive Summary

Sub-Saharan Africa faces significant challenges in accessing safe drinking water and electricity, exacerbating climate change impacts through increased droughts and floods. This brief recommends implementing micro-hydropower projects, strengthening watershed management with indigenous councils, enhancing early warning systems for extreme weather events, and promoting renewable energy adoption to enhance resilience.

Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa is grappling with limited access to clean drinking water, where only 32.4% of the population has access to safely managed water sources (World Bank Open Data, 2024). In contrast, the global average stands at 73.7% (World Bank Open Data, 2024). Similarly, electricity access remains low, with only 53.3% of Sub-Saharan African populations having access compared to 91.6% globally (World Bank Open Data, 2023).

Climate change compounds these challenges by increasing frequency and intensity of droughts and floods. Analysis

Sub-Saharan African countries must urgently address their water scarcity issues through sustainable solutions. Micro-hydropower projects offer an efficient way to provide electricity, particularly in remote rural areas where grid extension is impractical. These projects can also help generate additional income for communities by powering small businesses and irrigation systems.

Indigenous councils play a vital role in local watershed management. Their traditional knowledge can be harnessed to identify sustainable water sources and manage resources effectively. Strengthening these partnerships will not only improve water security but also enhance long-term resilience against climate change impacts (UN-Water/WHO, 2022).

Early warning systems are crucial for managing extreme weather events, which have become more frequent due to climate change. These systems help communities prepare and mitigate the adverse effects of floods and droughts, thereby safeguarding their livelihoods and reducing vulnerability.

Additionally, promoting renewable energy sources such as solar power can reduce reliance on fossil fuels and improve access to clean energy.
