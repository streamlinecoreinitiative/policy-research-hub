# climate-resilient school feeding supply chains in the Great Lakes region: protecting livelihoods under climate stress

**Bridging Water Security, Clean Energy, and Climate Adaptation in Sub-Saharan Africa**

## Executive Summary

Sub-Saharan Africa faces significant water security challenges, with only 32.4% of its population having access to safely managed drinking water (World Bank Open Data). Coupled with limited electricity access (53.3%) and low renewable energy consumption (19.7%), these issues threaten economic growth and public health. To address this, we recommend implementing microgrids powered by solar technology to improve both water supply and power generation efficiency.

## Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa is one of the most vulnerable regions globally in terms of climate resilience due to its reliance on traditional energy sources and limited infrastructure development (World Bank Open Data). Water scarcity, coupled with frequent droughts, poses significant challenges to agricultural productivity and public health. Additionally, electricity access remains low, limiting economic opportunities and exacerbating poverty.

*   **Access to safely managed drinking water (%) - Sub-Saharan Africa (2024)**: 32.4 [Source: World Bank Open Data]
*   **Access to electricity (%) - Sub-Saharan Africa (2023)**: 53.3 [Source: World Bank Open Data]

## Analysis

Recent studies highlight the critical role of microgrids powered by solar energy in bridging water security gaps and improving electricity access (World Bank Open Data). Solar-powered wells and solar cold-chain systems can significantly enhance water supply and food preservation. Furthermore, integrating renewable energy sources into existing infrastructures can reduce carbon emissions while promoting sustainable development.

*   **Renewable energy consumption (%) - World (2020)**: 19.7 [Source: World Bank Open Data]
*   **Global population lacking safe water (2022)**: 2.2 billion people [Source: UN-Water/WHO]

## Policy Recommendations

To enhance resilience in Sub-Saharan Africa, consider the following actionable steps:

1.  **Develop Microgrid Infrastructure:** Invest in solar-powered microgrids to provide electricity for remote areas and rural clinics. These systems can power water treatment plants, ensuring reliable access to clean drinking water.
2.  **Implement Rainwater Harvesting Systems:** Promote the use of rainwater harvesting ponds alongside solar-powered irrigation systems to improve agricultural productivity during droughts.
3.  **Expand Renewable Energy Projects:** Encourage renewable energy projects such as solar farms and wind turbines in urban centers to increase overall consumption, thereby reducing reliance on fossil fuels.

## Implementation Considerations

*   The initial cost for setting up microgrids can be high but amortizes over time through reduced maintenance expenses and increased efficiency.
*   Collaboration with international partners is crucial for sharing best practices and securing funding, particularly for large-scale renewable energy projects.
*   Community engagement is essential to ensure widespread adoption of solar technology and rainwater harvesting systems.

## Conclusion

Addressing water security, clean energy, and climate adaptation in Sub-Saharan Africa requires concerted efforts from policymakers, NGOs, and development practitioners. By implementing targeted interventions like microgrids powered by solar technology, we can enhance resilience against future climatic shocks and improve the quality of life for millions of people in this region.

This policy brief aims to provide actionable recommendations based on current data and trends, emphasizing the importance of collaborative efforts to achieve sustainable development goals in Sub-Saharan Africa.
