# Watershed Co-Management in the Mekong Basin: Enhancing Climate Resilience and Sustainable Development

## Executive Summary

The Mekong River Basin is one of the most biodiverse regions on Earth, supporting over 60 million people across six countries. Climate change poses significant threats to water security, food production, and livelihoods in the region. This brief explores the potential for watershed co-management with indigenous councils as a strategy to support adaptation targets in Nationally Determined Contributions (NDCs).

## Current Challenges and Opportunities

### Water Security in the Mekong Basin

* Climate change is altering precipitation patterns, leading to more frequent droughts and floods.
* Over-extraction of water for agriculture, industry, and domestic use threatens aquatic ecosystems.

### Indigenous Knowledge and Management Practices

* Indigenous councils have traditionally managed watersheds using sustainable practices, such as rotational farming and fish-friendly dams.
* These practices promote biodiversity conservation and improve ecosystem services.

### Modern Technologies and Policies

* Watershed co-management can integrate modern technologies, like precision irrigation and renewable energy systems.
* Policy frameworks, such as the Mekong Agreement and the Paris Agreement, provide a foundation for collaborative management and climate action.

## Case Studies and Best Practices

Several case studies demonstrate the effectiveness of watershed co-management with indigenous councils in the Mekong Basin:

* The Nam Ou River Basin in Laos has implemented community-led conservation efforts.
* In Cambodia's Tonle Sap Lake, a collaborative management approach has enhanced fisheries productivity.

## Policy Recommendations

To support adaptation targets in NDCs, we recommend:

1. **Strengthening policy frameworks**: Enhance the Mekong Agreement and Paris Agreement to prioritize watershed co-management and climate action.
2. **Empowering indigenous councils**: Recognize and support the role of indigenous councils in managing watersheds and promoting traditional knowledge.
3. **Investing in infrastructure and capacity building**: Develop sustainable infrastructure and enhance institutional capacities.

## Actions/Recommendations

To advance watershed co-management with indigenous councils in the Mekong Basin:

1. Conduct further research on the effectiveness of integrated approaches to climate adaptation and sustainable development.
2. Establish a regional platform for knowledge sharing and collaboration among governments, civil society, and indigenous organizations.
3. Develop a comprehensive policy framework that prioritizes watershed co-management.

## Closing Outlook

Watershed co-management with indigenous councils offers a promising strategy to support adaptation targets in NDCs and promote sustainable development in the Mekong Basin. By integrating traditional knowledge, modern technologies, and policy frameworks, we can enhance climate resilience and conserve biodiversity.
