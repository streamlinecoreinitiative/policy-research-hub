# solar cold-chain for fisheries in the Sahel: stabilizing food security during droughts

**Solar Cold-Chain for Fisheries in the Sahel: Enhancing Food Security during Droughts**

**Executive Summary**

The Sahelian region is highly vulnerable to climate change, with frequent droughts and water scarcity severely impacting agriculture and food security. The fisheries sector, a crucial source of livelihood for millions, is particularly affected by these conditions. This brief explores the potential of integrating solar-powered cold-chain technology with traditional fishing practices in the Sahel region.

**Water Scarcity and Climate Change Impacts**

The Sahelian region is characterized by arid conditions, making water scarcity a persistent challenge for agriculture and human consumption. Climate change has exacerbated these conditions through increased evaporation from highland wetlands and erratic rainfall patterns. The impacts of climate change on the fisheries sector are concerning, with fish populations declining due to reduced water quality and habitat degradation.

**Current Irrigation Practices and Challenges**

Traditional irrigation methods in the Sahel region rely heavily on diesel-powered pumps, which contribute to energy costs and greenhouse gas emissions. These systems suffer from high water loss through evaporation and seepage, reducing their efficiency. The use of solar-powered cold-chain technology can help mitigate these challenges by providing a reliable source of energy for fishing communities.

**Micro-Hydro Systems for Irrigation Efficiency**

Micro-hydro technology offers a promising solution for irrigation efficiency in the Sahel region. These small-scale hydroelectric systems can be powered by solar energy, reducing reliance on diesel generators and increasing energy efficiency. Pilot projects have demonstrated the effectiveness of micro-hydro systems in improving water supply and reducing energy costs.

**Integration of Agroforestry and Micro-Hydro in Climate-Resilient Water Supply**

Pilot projects integrating agroforestry with micro-hydro systems have shown promising results in enhancing climate resilience. These integrated systems can provide a reliable source of clean water for irrigation, while also promoting biodiversity and soil health. Economic analysis has demonstrated the cost-effectiveness of these solutions.

To support the adoption of agroforestry-micro-hydro systems:

**Closing Outlook**

The Sahelian region faces significant challenges in maintaining food security during droughts. The integration of solar-powered cold-chain technology with traditional fishing practices offers a promising solution for stabilizing food security in this region. By promoting the adoption of agroforestry-micro-hydro systems, we can enhance climate resilience and improve livelihoods for millions of people in the Sahel.
