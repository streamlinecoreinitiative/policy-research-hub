Executive Summary Lower-income countries face significant challenges in ensuring water security, accessing clean energy, and adapting to climate change impacts. Policy Recommendations

1. Invest in Water Security: Invest in infrastructure improvements such as water treatment plants and distribution networks, prioritizing rural areas where coverage is lowest.

  • Promote Clean Energy Access: Encourage renewable energy projects like solar panels and small hydro systems through grants and subsidies.
  • Enhance Climate Adaptation: Establish early warning systems for climate-related disasters such as floods, droughts, and heatwaves.

    Implementation Considerations

To implement these recommendations, we suggest allocating a portion of climate finance commitments (UNFCCC) to support these initiatives in developing countries. Additionally, training local communities and officials on sustainable water management, renewable energy use, and climate adaptation strategies is crucial for long-term success.

Conclusion Urgent action is needed to address the challenges facing lower-income countries. We urge policymakers to prioritize investments in water security, clean energy access, and climate resilience as key elements of national development plans.
