## Executive Summary

To address the pressing challenges of water security, clean energy access, and climate adaptation in Sub-Saharan Africa, we recommend **increasing investment in water infrastructure**, particularly in rural areas. This includes expanding access to piped water connections (World Bank, 2024), promoting rainwater harvesting and other innovative technologies, and supporting community-based initiatives to enhance local ownership and sustainability.

## Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa faces significant environmental challenges due to its limited resources for addressing issues such as water scarcity, energy poverty, and increased frequency of extreme weather events. Despite global commitments under the United Nations Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs), progress in achieving these goals has been slow, particularly in Sub-Saharan Africa where 2.2 billion people lack access to safe drinking water (UN-Water/WHO, 2022). The region also lags behind in renewable energy consumption and electricity access.

## Analysis

Access to safe drinking water is a fundamental necessity for health and well-being. In Sub-Saharan Africa, only **[VERIFY] 32.4% of the population has access to safely managed drinking water** (World Bank, 2024), compared to 73.7% globally (World Bank, 2024). This disparity underscores the urgent need for improved infrastructure and investment in water supply systems.

Electricity access is another critical issue, with only **[VERIFY] 53.3% of Sub-Saharan Africa's population having access to electricity** (World Bank, 2023), compared to 91.6% globally (World Bank, 2023). The gap is particularly wide in rural areas where access is even more limited.

Renewable energy consumption remains low at just **[VERIFY] 19.7% of total final energy consumption worldwide** (World Bank Open Data, 2020). Sub-Saharan Africa lags behind in this area as well, with no specific data available for the region.

## Policy Recommendations

To address these challenges, we propose the following policy recommendations:

1. **Increase investment in water infrastructure**: Governments should prioritize funding for improved water supply systems, including expanding access to piped water connections (World Bank, 2024) and promoting rainwater harvesting and other innovative technologies.
2. **Enhance renewable energy development**: Encourage the adoption of solar power through subsidies for households and businesses in rural areas (World Bank, 2023). Implement policies that mandate a certain percentage of electricity generation from renewables by 2030.
3. **Climate adaptation measures**: Promote climate-resilient agricultural practices such as drought-resistant crop varieties and improved irrigation systems (FAO, 2017). Establish early warning systems for extreme weather events to minimize damage and loss.

## Implementation Considerations

Implementing these recommendations will require careful planning and coordination at multiple levels of government. Collaboration with international agencies and NGOs can facilitate knowledge sharing, technology transfer, and financial support. Monitoring progress through regular evaluations is essential to ensure that the interventions are effective and sustainable over time.

## Conclusion

By prioritizing water security, clean energy access, and climate adaptation in Sub-Saharan Africa, we can pave the way for a more resilient and prosperous future. While challenges remain, concrete policy actions based on evidence-based recommendations provide actionable steps towards achieving these goals. We must work together to create a sustainable development roadmap that leaves no one behind.
