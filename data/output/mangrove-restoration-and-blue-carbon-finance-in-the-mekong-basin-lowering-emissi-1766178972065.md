## Executive Summary

The Lower Mekong Basin faces severe water scarcity due to climate change, threatening food security and economic stability for vulnerable communities. This brief explores an innovative solution: integrating agroforestry with micro-hydro systems for climate-resilient irrigation. Agroforestry enhances soil health, biodiversity, and carbon sequestration, while micro-hydro technology reduces reliance on diesel generators and increases energy efficiency.

## Introduction to Water Scarcity in the Lower Mekong Basin

The Lower Mekong Basin is a vital agricultural region facing significant water scarcity challenges due to climate change. Rising sea levels and increased salinity intrusion are affecting crop yields and food security, particularly for vulnerable communities (UN Environment Programme, 2020).

## Micro-Hydro Systems for Irrigation Efficiency

Micro-hydro technology offers a sustainable solution for irrigation efficiency. Small-scale hydroelectric systems can generate energy from water flows, reducing reliance on diesel generators and increasing energy efficiency (World Bank, 2020).

## Integration of Agroforestry and Micro-Hydro in Climate-Resilient Water Supply

Pilot projects have demonstrated the combined use of agroforestry and micro-hydro technology for climate-resilient irrigation. These integrated systems can improve water security, reduce energy costs, and enhance community resilience to climate-related shocks (UN Environment Programme, 2020).
