# climate-resilient school feeding supply chains in the Andean highlands: protecting livelihoods under climate stress

**Enhancing Water Security, Clean Energy Access, and Climate Adaptation in Sub-Saharan Africa**

## Executive Summary

To address the pressing challenges of water security, clean energy access, and climate adaptation in Sub-Saharan Africa, this policy brief recommends developing solar-powered wells and rainwater harvesting ponds to improve access to safe drinking water and electricity. By investing in renewable energy infrastructure and climate-resilient infrastructure, governments, NGOs, and international partners can work together to achieve significant progress towards Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs) 6, 7, and 13.

## Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa is one of the most vulnerable regions globally to climate change impacts such as droughts and floods. The region's limited access to safe drinking water ([VERIFY] 32.4% of the population has safely managed drinking water, [NEEDS SOURCE]) and electricity (53.3% have access to electricity, World Bank, 2023) exacerbates these challenges, leading to health issues, economic losses, and social instability.

## Analysis

- **Water Security**: Sub-Saharan Africa's inadequate access to safely managed drinking water is a critical issue. According to the World Bank (2024), only [VERIFY] 32.4% of the population has this level of service. This shortfall impacts public health and economic productivity.
- **Clean Energy Access**: The region lags behind in electricity access, with just [NEEDS SOURCE] 53.3% of the population benefiting from reliable power supply (World Bank, 2023). Renewable energy solutions like solar-powered wells could significantly improve this situation by providing clean, sustainable energy for water pumping and other essential services.
- **Climate Adaptation**: The region is highly susceptible to climate change impacts, including erratic rainfall patterns and extreme weather events. Climate-resilient infrastructure and renewable energy can help mitigate these risks.

## Policy Recommendations

1. **Develop Solar-Powered Wells and Rainwater Harvesting Ponds**:
   - Implement solar-powered wells in rural areas where water sources are inaccessible or unreliable (World Bank, 2024). These solutions reduce the need for diesel generators and improve access to safe drinking water.
   - Promote rainwater harvesting ponds to collect and store rainwater for irrigation purposes. This not only enhances water security but also reduces reliance on groundwater depletion (FAO, 2019).

2. **Expand Renewable Energy Infrastructure**:
   - Invest in solar-powered energy generation systems that can be integrated into existing water infrastructure (World Bank, 2025). Solar panels can power pumps and other equipment needed for water supply.
   - Encourage the use of renewable energy sources like solar PV to drive electricity access. This not only improves energy availability but also reduces greenhouse gas emissions (IRENA, 2024).

3. **Invest in Climate-Resilient Infrastructure**:
   - Build climate-resilient infrastructure such as flood barriers and early warning systems to protect water resources from extreme weather events (UNDP, 2018).
   - Implement sustainable land use practices like agroforestry value chains that enhance soil moisture retention and reduce erosion (FAO, 2017).

## Implementation Considerations

- **Cost**: The initial investment in solar-powered wells and renewable energy infrastructure can be substantial. However, these investments are offset by long-term savings on fuel costs and reduced maintenance expenses.
- **Barriers**: Overcoming political resistance to change and ensuring equitable access to resources will require strong leadership and public-private partnerships (World Bank, 2025).

## Conclusion

Sub-Saharan Africa needs targeted interventions to enhance water security, clean energy access, and climate resilience. By adopting solar-powered wells, renewable energy solutions, and sustainable infrastructure, the region can achieve significant progress towards SDG 6, 7, and 13. Implementing these recommendations will require collaborative efforts from governments, NGOs, and international partners.
