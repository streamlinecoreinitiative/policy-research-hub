**Agroforestry-Based Micro-Hydro Systems for Climate-Resilient Irrigation in the Ethiopian Highlands**

## Executive Summary

The Ethiopian Highlands face severe water scarcity due to climate change-induced erratic rainfall patterns, impacting agriculture and affecting millions of people. This brief presents an integrated solution combining agroforestry with micro-hydro systems to address this challenge.

## Addressing Water Scarcity through Integrated Solutions

### Introduction to Water Scarcity

Climate change impacts agriculture by causing erratic rainfall patterns. In Ethiopia's Highlands, average annual rainfall ranges from 800-1200 mm [verify], with precipitation variability between 600-1400 mm (NOAA climate dataset for specific regions in Ethiopia). Traditional irrigation methods are energy-intensive and costly.

### Agroforestry's Role in Climate Resilience

Agroforestry improves soil health, biodiversity, and carbon sequestration. Local surveys and case studies demonstrate that agroforestry can increase:

* Soil Organic Matter by 2-8% [verify]
* Biodiversity by 15-30 native species [verify]
* Carbon Sequestration by 3-7 tons/ha/year [verify]

### Micro-Hydro Systems for Irrigation Efficiency

Micro-hydro systems provide sustainable and renewable energy, generating power ranging from 10-50 kW per system with an efficiency of 70-80%. Economic analysis indicates cost savings compared to traditional irrigation methods.

## Integration and Policy Recommendations

### Integration of Agroforestry and Micro-Hydro in Climate-Resilient Water Supply
