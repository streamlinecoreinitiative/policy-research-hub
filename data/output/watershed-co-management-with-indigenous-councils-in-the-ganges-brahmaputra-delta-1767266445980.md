# Watershed Co-management in the Ganges-Brahmaputra Delta: Enhancing Food Security during Droughts

## Executive Summary

The Ganges-Brahmaputra delta is a densely populated and vulnerable region to climate change, experiencing frequent droughts that severely impact food security. This brief highlights the importance of watershed co-management with indigenous councils as a critical strategy for stabilizing food security in this region.

## Environmental Resilience in Lower-Income Countries

### Water Security

Water scarcity is a pressing issue in the Ganges-Brahmaputra delta, exacerbated by climate change. Droughts have become more frequent and severe, affecting agriculture and food security. Existing irrigation systems are often inefficient, leading to significant water loss through evaporation and seepage.

### Clean Energy

The region's energy landscape is dominated by diesel generators, which contribute to greenhouse gas emissions and fuel insecurity. Micro-hydro technology offers a promising solution, providing clean energy while reducing reliance on fossil fuels.

### Climate Adaptation

Agroforestry-based micro-hydro systems can help communities adapt to climate change by enhancing water retention, reducing erosion, and increasing carbon sequestration. This integrated approach has been successfully demonstrated in pilot projects.
