**Nature-Based Solutions for Landslide Risk in the Horn of Africa: Enhancing Resilience Finance**

**Executive Summary**

The Horn of Africa is highly vulnerable to climate change, facing severe water scarcity and increased landslide risk due to erratic rainfall patterns and soil erosion. This brief proposes an innovative approach to address these challenges through nature-based solutions, specifically integrating agroforestry practices with micro-hydro systems for climate-resilient irrigation.

**I. Introduction to Water Scarcity and Climate Change Impacts**

Climate change is affecting agriculture in the Central African Rainforest by altering rainfall patterns and increasing evaporation from wetlands. This has led to severe water scarcity, impacting crop yields and food security for smallholder farmers.

**II. Current Irrigation Practices and Challenges**

Existing irrigation infrastructure includes boreholes and canals, which suffer from high water loss through evaporation (estimated at 30-50%) and seepage (estimated at 20-30%). The economic costs associated with traditional irrigation methods are substantial.

**VI. Policy Recommendations**

To support the adoption of nature-based solutions for landslide risk in the Horn of Africa:

* **Subsidies and Grants:** Governments should provide subsidies or grants to support the installation of micro-hydro systems.
* **Infrastructure Development:** Funding should be allocated for infrastructure improvements, such as roads and electricity grids, to facilitate the implementation of these solutions.
* **Sustainable Practices:** Policies promoting sustainable agricultural practices and renewable energy use should be advocated.

**VII. Future Research Directions**

To ensure the long-term sustainability of these integrated systems:

* **Long-term Sustainability Assessments:** Studies should be conducted on the long-term impacts of agroforestry-based micro-hydro systems.
* **Community Engagement Strategies:** Methods for engaging local communities in planning, implementation, and monitoring phases should be developed.

**Actions/Recommendations:**

1. Conduct a thorough needs assessment to identify areas with high landslide risk and limited access to clean water and energy.
2. Develop a detailed project plan outlining the integration of agroforestry and micro-hydro systems.
3. Secure funding for infrastructure development, including roads, electricity grids, and irrigation systems.
4. Establish partnerships with local communities, governments, and organizations to ensure successful implementation.

**Closing Outlook**

The proposed nature-based solutions for landslide risk in the Horn of Africa offer a promising approach to addressing water scarcity and climate change impacts. By integrating agroforestry practices with micro-hydro systems, this project aims to enhance water security, reduce energy costs, and improve agricultural resilience in vulnerable communities.
