Clean Cooking Transition for Peri-Urban Households in the Central America Dry Corridor: Cutting Disaster Losses for Vulnerable Groups

Executive Summary

Governments must prioritize environmental resilience through targeted investments in water security, clean energy access, and climate adaptation measures to improve public health outcomes, enhance economic stability, and secure sustainable development pathways for their populations. We recommend implementing community-led water management initiatives, promoting public-private partnerships for renewable energy projects, and developing early warning systems for climate-related disasters.

Context & Challenge

Lower-income countries face significant challenges in ensuring water security, accessing clean energy, and adapting to climate change impacts. According to the World Bank, 32.4% of people in Sub-Saharan Africa lack access to safely managed drinking water (World Bank Open Data), while 53.3% have no access to electricity (World Bank Open Data). Climate change exacerbates these vulnerabilities, leading to more frequent droughts, floods, and extreme weather events that threaten water availability and energy supply.
