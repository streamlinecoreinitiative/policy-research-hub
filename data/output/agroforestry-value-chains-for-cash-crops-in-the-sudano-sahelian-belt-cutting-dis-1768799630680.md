# Agroforestry-Based Micro-Hydro Systems for Climate-Resilient Irrigation in the Andean Highlands

## Executive Summary

The Andean Highlands face severe water scarcity, exacerbated by climate change impacts. Traditional irrigation methods are inefficient and unsustainable, leading to economic instability for farmers and communities. This brief explores the potential of agroforestry-based micro-hydro systems to address these challenges.

## Introduction to Water Scarcity in the Andean Highlands

The Andean Highlands are characterized by a semi-arid climate, with limited rainfall and high evaporation rates. Historical droughts have had significant impacts on agriculture, leading to economic instability for farmers and communities. Current challenges include:

* Limited access to reliable irrigation systems
* Economic instability due to reduced crop yields
* High dependency on traditional methods like surface water and rain-fed systems
