Sub-Saharan Africa is one of the least developed regions globally, characterized by high poverty rates, limited infrastructure, and frequent natural disasters exacerbated by climate change. Access to safe drinking water remains a significant challenge, with only 32.4% of the population having access (World Bank). This disparity impacts health outcomes, economic productivity, and social stability.

Climate change further complicates these challenges by increasing frequency and intensity of extreme weather events such as droughts, floods, and cyclones, which can severely impact water resources and energy infrastructure.
