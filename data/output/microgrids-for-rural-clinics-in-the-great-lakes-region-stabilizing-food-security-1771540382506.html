Enhancing Environmental Resilience: Investing in Water Security, Clean Energy, and Climate Adaptation for Sub-Saharan Africa

Executive Summary

Sub-Saharan Africa faces significant challenges in accessing safe water, electricity, and renewable energy sources, exacerbating climate vulnerability. To enhance environmental resilience, we recommend investing in clean cooking solutions for peri-urban households, solar cold-chain systems for fisheries, early warning systems for droughts and floods, and climate-resilient agricultural practices. Implementing these measures will require targeted funding, community engagement, and policy support.

Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa is one of the most water-stressed regions globally, with only 32.4% of its population having access to safely managed drinking water (World Bank Open Data, 2024). The region's electricity access rate stands at 53.3%, far below global averages (World Bank Open Data, 2023), and renewable energy consumption accounts for only 19.7% of total final energy consumption (World Bank Open Data, 2020).

Climate change is intensifying these challenges with frequent droughts and floods, further straining water supplies and power grids. Vulnerable populations are particularly at risk from both environmental degradation and climate-induced shocks.
