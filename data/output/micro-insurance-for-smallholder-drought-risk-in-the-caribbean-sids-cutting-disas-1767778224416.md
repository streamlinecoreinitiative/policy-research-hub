# Agroforestry-Based Micro-Hydro Systems for Climate-Resilient Irrigation in the Central African Rainforest

## Executive Summary

Climate change and water scarcity pose significant challenges to agricultural production in the Central African Rainforest, affecting smallholder farmers' livelihoods. This brief presents a comprehensive approach to address these issues through the integration of agroforestry and micro-hydro systems.

## Addressing Water Scarcity and Climate Change Impacts

### Introduction to Water Scarcity

Water scarcity is a pressing issue in the Central African Rainforest, affecting agricultural productivity and food security. Successful agroforestry projects have demonstrated improved soil moisture retention and reduced erosion. However, current irrigation practices face significant challenges.

### Agroforestry's Role in Addressing Water Scarcity

Agroforestry plays a crucial role in addressing water scarcity through improved soil health benefits:

*   Improved soil structure with increased moisture retention
*   Higher crop yields due to agroforestry practices
*   Biodiversity enhancement leading to natural pest control and pollination services

### Micro-Hydro Systems for Renewable Energy

Micro-hydro systems offer a renewable energy source for irrigation, reducing reliance on diesel generators:

*   Overview of micro-hydro technology: A reduction in diesel generator usage
*   Example Project: Pilot micro-hydro system showing improved water delivery
*   Energy savings and cost reductions: Savings from reduced reliance on diesel generators

## Integration of Agroforestry and Micro-Hydro Systems

The integration of agroforestry and micro-hydro systems offers a sustainable solution for climate-resilient irrigation:

*   Pilot project results: Increased crop yields with self-sufficient renewable energy supply
*   Economic benefits: Savings from reduced fuel and maintenance costs
