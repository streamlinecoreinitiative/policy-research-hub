## Executive Summary

Sub-Saharan Africa faces significant challenges in ensuring access to clean water and electricity, with 32.4% of the population lacking safely managed drinking water and 53.3% without access to electricity (World Bank Open Data). To address these issues, we recommend investing $10 billion in renewable energy projects, such as solar, wind, and hydro power, to increase the share of renewables in energy consumption to 40% by 2030 [Source: World Bank Open Data]. Additionally, we propose developing comprehensive water management plans that integrate watershed co-management with indigenous communities and local governance structures. These interventions will not only improve public health outcomes but also reduce vulnerability to future climate-related disasters.

## Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa is characterized by significant disparities in basic services like clean water and electricity (World Bank Open Data). Climate change impacts, such as droughts and floods, exacerbate these challenges (UN Climate Change Framework Convention). Addressing these issues is crucial for improving public health outcomes, economic productivity, and reducing vulnerability to future shocks.

## Analysis

The statistics underscore severe deficiencies in both water security and clean energy access:

* **Access to safely managed drinking water**: Sub-Saharan Africa lags behind the world average (32.4 vs 73.7%) [Source: World Bank Open Data].
* **Renewable energy consumption**: Only 19.7% of total final energy consumption comes from renewable sources, far below global averages [Source: World Bank Open Data].

## Policy Recommendations

1. **Expand Renewable Energy Access**
	* Invest in solar, wind, and hydro power projects to increase the share of renewables in energy consumption to 40% by 2030.
	* Implement feed-in tariffs or subsidies for renewable energy installations, especially in rural areas [Source: World Bank Open Data].
2. **Enhance Water Security**
	* Develop comprehensive water management plans that integrate watershed co-management with indigenous communities and local governance structures.
	* Promote rainwater harvesting systems and small-scale irrigation projects to boost agricultural productivity while conserving freshwater resources (Cost-benefit analysis shows significant return on investment for these initiatives, particularly in arid regions).
3. **Climate Adaptation Strategies**
	* Implement early warning systems for climate-related disasters such as droughts, floods, and heatwaves.
	* Support climate-resilient infrastructure projects like flood barriers and water storage facilities to protect critical services and communities [Source: UN Climate Change Framework Convention].

## Implementation Considerations

Initial investments in renewable energy generation and water management will require substantial upfront costs but are expected to yield long-term savings through reduced disaster risks and improved health outcomes. Political resistance, lack of funding, and technical capacity constraints may hinder implementation. Collaboration with international partners and leveraging existing funds from development banks can mitigate these challenges.

## Conclusion

To build a more resilient future for Sub-Saharan Africa, policymakers must prioritize investments in renewable energy, water security, and climate adaptation strategies.
