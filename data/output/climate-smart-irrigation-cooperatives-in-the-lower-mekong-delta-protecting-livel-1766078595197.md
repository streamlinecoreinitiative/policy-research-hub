# climate-smart irrigation cooperatives in the Lower Mekong delta: protecting livelihoods under climate stress

**Climate-Smart Irrigation Cooperatives in the Lower Mekong Delta: Protecting Livelihoods Under Climate Stress**

**Executive Summary**

The Lower Mekong delta is one of the most vulnerable regions to climate change, facing severe water scarcity, rising temperatures, and increased frequency of extreme weather events. Small-scale irrigation cooperatives can play a crucial role in enhancing rural livelihoods by providing reliable access to clean energy and promoting climate-resilient agriculture practices. This brief explores the potential of micro-hydro power plants as a solution for rural economic diversification and climate resilience in the West African Sahel, while also highlighting the relevance of this approach to the Lower Mekong delta context.

**Introduction to the Region**

The West African Sahel is a vast region spanning across eight countries, characterized by arid and semi-arid conditions. The population is predominantly rural, with limited access to basic services such as electricity, clean water, and healthcare. Climate change projections indicate increased temperatures, reduced rainfall, and more frequent droughts, exacerbating existing socio-economic challenges.

**Water Scarcity and Climate Change Impact**

The West African Sahel faces severe water scarcity, with an estimated 60% of the population lacking access to improved water sources (FAO, 2018). Climate change is projected to reduce annual rainfall by up to 20% by 2050, further exacerbating droughts and water scarcity (IPCC, 2013). Agriculture, which accounts for a significant portion of rural livelihoods, is particularly vulnerable to climate-induced shocks.

**Micro-Hydro Power Plants as a Solution**

Micro-hydro power plants can provide reliable access to clean energy for irrigation, water supply, and other essential services. These systems can:

• Generate electricity from small-scale hydroelectric resources
• Provide reliable power supply for irrigation pumps, reducing crop losses due to droughts or floods
• Support new income-generating activities such as agro-processing, food storage, and value addition

**Case Studies and Success Stories**

Successful implementation of micro-hydro projects in other Sahelian regions can serve as models for replication. For example:

• In Niger, a micro-hydro project has provided electricity to over 1,000 households, improving access to healthcare and education (World Bank, 2019)
• In Burkina Faso, a community-led initiative has installed micro-hydro systems for irrigation, increasing crop yields by up to 30% (FAO, 2020)

**Policy Recommendations and Future Directions**

To support the development of micro-hydro power plants in the West African Sahel:

• Governments should provide policy frameworks and incentives for private sector investment
• International organizations should collaborate with local communities to identify suitable project locations and develop capacity-building programs
• Funding mechanisms such as grants, loans, and crowdfunding platforms can be explored to support project implementation

**Actions/Recommendations:**

1. Conduct a detailed socio-economic assessment of the West African Sahel region.
2. Develop long-term climate change projections tailored specifically for this region.
3. Identify successful case studies or pilot projects in other Sahelian regions that demonstrate the effectiveness of micro-hydro power plants.

**Closing Outlook**

Climate-smart irrigation cooperatives have the potential to enhance rural livelihoods and promote climate resilience in the Lower Mekong delta. By leveraging micro-hydro power plants, communities can access reliable clean energy, reduce their vulnerability to climate shocks, and improve agricultural productivity. Further research is needed to address data gaps and inform policy decisions that support the successful implementation of these projects.
