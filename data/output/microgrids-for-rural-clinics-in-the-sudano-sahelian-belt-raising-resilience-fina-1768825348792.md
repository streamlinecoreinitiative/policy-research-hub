# Microgrids for Rural Clinics in the Sudano-Sahelian Belt: Raising Resilience Finance for Communities

## Executive Summary

The Sudano-Sahelian belt is one of the most vulnerable regions to climate change, facing severe water scarcity and poverty. This brief explores the potential of microgrids for rural clinics in the region, highlighting the need for integrated solutions that leverage agroforestry and renewable energy systems.

## Challenges and Opportunities

### Introduction to Water Scarcity in the Sudano-Sahelian Belt

The Sudano-Sahelian belt is characterized by limited access to clean water. Climate change exacerbates these challenges, with rising temperatures and changing precipitation patterns affecting agricultural productivity and water availability. [Verify] 30% of households lack access to basic drinking water services (World Bank).

### Current Practices and Challenges

Existing irrigation systems in the region are often inefficient, relying on diesel-powered pumps that consume large amounts of fuel. This not only contributes to greenhouse gas emissions but also increases operational costs for farmers.

### Agroforestry's Role

Agroforestry practices have been shown to enhance soil fertility and improve water retention in the Sudano-Sahelian belt (FAO). By integrating agroforestry with traditional irrigation methods, communities can reduce their reliance on diesel-powered pumps and promote more sustainable agricultural practices. Micro-hydro systems can provide a reliable source of power for these integrated systems.

### Integration in Climate-Resilient Water Supply

The integration of micro-hydro systems and agroforestry has the potential to enhance climate resilience in rural clinics. By providing access to clean water and energy, communities can reduce their vulnerability to droughts and other climate-related shocks.
