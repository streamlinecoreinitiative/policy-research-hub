## Executive Summary

Sub-Saharan Africa faces significant challenges in water security, clean energy access, and climate adaptation. To address these issues comprehensively, we recommend the following:

* Implement community-based water management projects to increase access to safe drinking water.
* Increase public financing for renewable energy projects through concessional loans.
* Develop early warning systems for droughts and floods to inform timely adaptation measures.

## Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa is the least electrified region globally, with only 53.3% of its population having access to electricity (World Bank, 2023). Water security remains a critical issue: Only 32.4% of the sub-region's population has access to safely managed drinking water (World Bank, 2024), impacting health and economic productivity.

Climate change is intensifying these challenges with increasing droughts and floods, threatening existing infrastructure and livelihoods. Rising temperatures and extreme weather events are affecting agricultural productivity, water resources, and public health systems. The region's climate resilience is insufficient to cope with these challenges (World Bank, 2024).

The sub-region lags behind other continents in renewable energy consumption, with just 19.7% of total final energy consumption coming from renewables (World Bank, 2020). This disparity exacerbates existing power shortages and air pollution.

### Climate Adaptation

Rising temperatures and extreme weather events are affecting agricultural productivity, water resources, and public health systems. The region's climate resilience is insufficient to cope with these challenges (World Bank, 2024).

## Policy Recommendations

1. **Increase Access to Safe Drinking Water**:
	* Implement community-based water management projects in areas lacking access.
	* Invest in desalination plants for coastal regions and groundwater extraction techniques where feasible.
	* Provide subsidies or grants for household water treatment systems.
2. **Boost Renewable Energy Consumption**:
	* Increase public financing through concessional loans to support renewable energy projects (World Bank, 2023).
	* Encourage private sector investment in solar and wind power by offering tax incentives and feed-in tariffs.
	* Implement inter-utility trading agreements for surplus renewable energy production.
3. **Enhance Climate Resilience**:
	* Develop early warning systems for droughts and floods to inform timely adaptation measures (World Bank, 2024).
	* Promote climate-resilient agricultural practices through subsidies and training programs.
	* Invest in infrastructure that can withstand extreme weather events such as flood barriers and storm shelters.

## Conclusion

By prioritizing water security, renewable energy access, and climate adaptation, Sub-Saharan Africa can significantly improve its resilience. This requires a concerted effort from governments, international organizations, and the private sector. Immediate action is needed to address existing gaps and build sustainable pathways towards a more resilient future.
