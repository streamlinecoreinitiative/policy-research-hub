# Early-Warning Systems and Last-Mile Alerts in the Sudano-Sahelian Belt: Improving Health Outcomes in Heatwaves

## Executive Summary

The Sudano-Sahelian belt is highly vulnerable to climate change, facing severe water scarcity, heatwaves, and related health issues. This brief highlights the importance of early-warning systems and last-mile alerts in improving health outcomes during heatwaves.

## Environmental Resilience in Lower-Income Countries: Water Security, Clean Energy, and Climate Adaptation

### Introduction to Water Scarcity in the Sudano-Sahelian Belt

The region's semi-arid climate with limited rainfall makes it prone to severe droughts. Water scarcity affects agriculture, food security, and economic stability. Climate change exacerbates this issue through erratic rainfall patterns, increased evaporation due to rising temperatures, and altered water cycles.

### Current Irrigation Practices and Challenges

Traditional irrigation systems in the Sudano-Sahelian belt include boreholes, canals, and small dams. However, these systems often suffer from low efficiency, high energy consumption, and significant water loss through evaporation and seepage. Farmers rely heavily on diesel generators for pumping water, contributing to greenhouse gas emissions.

### Micro-Hydro Systems for Irrigation Efficiency

Micro-hydro technology offers a promising solution to irrigation efficiency in the Sudano-Sahelian belt. Small-scale hydroelectric systems can harness energy from water flows, reducing reliance on diesel generators and decreasing greenhouse gas emissions. Micro-hydro systems also promote energy independence and increase crop yields through efficient irrigation.

### Integration of Agroforestry and Micro-Hydro in Climate-Resilient Water Supply

Pilot projects have successfully integrated agroforestry and micro-hydro systems to provide climate-resilient water supply in the Sudano-Sahelian belt. These initiatives demonstrate the potential for scaling up this approach, improving food security, and enhancing economic stability.

## Actions/Recommendations

1.  **Develop Early-Warning Systems:** Establish a network of weather stations and monitoring systems to detect heatwaves and droughts.
2.  **Implement Last-Mile Alerts:** Develop mobile-based alert systems to reach farmers and communities in remote areas.
3.  **Promote Agroforestry and Micro-Hydro:** Support the adoption of agroforestry and micro-hydro systems through training programs, subsidies, and incentives.
4.  **Foster Community Engagement:** Engage local communities in decision-making processes and involve them in project planning and implementation.

## Closing Outlook

The Sudano-Sahelian belt faces significant environmental challenges, including water scarcity, heatwaves, and related health issues. Early-warning systems and last-mile alerts can improve health outcomes during heatwaves by enabling timely interventions and reducing the risk of heat-related illnesses. The integration of agroforestry and micro-hydro systems offers a promising solution to climate-resilient water supply in the region.
