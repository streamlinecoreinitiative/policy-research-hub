**Water Security, Clean Energy, and Climate Adaptation in Sub-Saharan Africa: Strengthening Resilience**

### Executive Summary

Sub-Saharan Africa faces significant water scarcity and energy challenges that threaten the region's economic development and public health. With only 32.4% of the population having access to safely managed drinking water (World Bank Open Data), and a mere 53.3% accessing electricity (World Bank Open Data), there is an urgent need for sustainable solutions. Climate change exacerbates these issues, making it imperative to invest in resilient infrastructure that can withstand environmental shocks while promoting economic growth.

We recommend prioritizing investments in clean energy, climate-resilient water systems, and equitable distribution of resources. This includes:

* Enhancing renewable energy deployment by increasing investment in solar, wind, and hydroelectric projects to meet electricity needs while reducing reliance on fossil fuels.
* Promoting climate-resilient water infrastructure through desalination plants, rainwater harvesting systems, and improved irrigation technologies that can withstand changing climatic conditions.
* Implementing equitable resource distribution by ensuring universal access to clean drinking water through subsidies for household water treatment systems and community-based water management initiatives.
