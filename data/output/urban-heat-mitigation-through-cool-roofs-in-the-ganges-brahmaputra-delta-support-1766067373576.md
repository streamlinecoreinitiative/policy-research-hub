# urban heat mitigation through cool roofs in the Ganges-Brahmaputra delta: supporting adaptation targets in NDCs

**Urban Heat Mitigation through Cool Roofs in the Ganges-Brahmaputra Delta: Supporting Adaptation Targets in NDCs**

**Executive Summary**

The Ganges-Brahmaputra delta is one of the most densely populated and climate-vulnerable regions worldwide, facing significant challenges from rising temperatures, sea-level rise, and extreme weather events. This brief explores the potential of cool roofs as a cost-effective urban heat mitigation strategy to support adaptation targets in Nationally Determined Contributions (NDCs). By integrating micro-insurance solutions for agricultural risk management and renewable energy technologies, smallholder farmers can enhance their resilience to climate-related disasters.

**Climate Vulnerability in Southeast Asian Highlands**

The mountainous regions of Southeast Asia are highly susceptible to climate change impacts. Rising temperatures and changing precipitation patterns lead to unpredictable rainfall patterns and increased frequency of extreme weather events such as flooding and droughts. These areas require innovative insurance solutions that can complement traditional agricultural practices, while also integrating renewable energy technologies for sustainable long-term resilience.
