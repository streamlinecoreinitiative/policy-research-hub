# Flood-Resilient Transport Corridors in the Great Lakes Region: Supporting Adaptation Targets in NDCs

## Executive Summary

The Great Lakes region is vulnerable to climate change impacts, including increased precipitation variability and frequency of extreme events. This brief focuses on flood-resilient transport corridors as a critical component of supporting adaptation targets in Nationally Determined Contributions (NDCs). We examine the current state of water security, clean energy, and climate adaptation in the region, highlighting key challenges and opportunities for integrated solutions.

## Current State of Water Security, Clean Energy, and Climate Adaptation

### Water Scarcity and Flood Risk

* The Great Lakes region experiences significant precipitation variability, with average annual rainfall ranging from 800-1200 mm [verify].
* Precipitation patterns are projected to become more erratic due to climate change, exacerbating flood risk.
* Current irrigation practices in the region rely heavily on traditional methods.

### Clean Energy and Renewable Energy Potential

* The Great Lakes region has significant potential for renewable energy development, particularly wind and solar power.
* However, existing infrastructure and policies often hinder the adoption of clean energy technologies.

### Climate Adaptation and Resilience

* The region's transportation corridors are critical for economic growth and trade, but are also vulnerable to climate-related disruptions.
* Integrated solutions that combine flood-resilient design with renewable energy systems can enhance resilience and support adaptation targets in NDCs.

* Micro-hydro systems provide sustainable and renewable energy, reducing reliance on fossil fuels.
