# climate-smart irrigation cooperatives in the Andean highlands: stabilizing food security during droughts

**Climate-Smart Irrigation Cooperatives in the Andean Highlands: Enhancing Food Security during Droughts**

## Executive Summary

The Andean highlands are highly vulnerable to climate change, facing severe water scarcity and droughts that threaten food security and economic stability. This brief presents a comprehensive approach to address these challenges through the integration of agroforestry-based micro-hydro systems for climate-resilient irrigation.

## Introduction to Water Scarcity in the Andean Highlands

The region is characterized by erratic rainfall patterns, with frequent droughts affecting agricultural productivity and food security (IPCC, 2013). Climate change projections indicate that these trends will continue, exacerbating water scarcity and its associated impacts on agriculture. Resilient irrigation methods become crucial for ensuring the stability of food systems and economic livelihoods.

## Current Irrigation Practices and Challenges

Existing irrigation systems in the Andean highlands rely heavily on boreholes and canals, which are often inefficient and prone to water loss through evaporation and seepage (FAO, 2017). These systems waste precious water resources and contribute to soil salinization and decreased crop yields.

## Micro-Hydro Systems for Irrigation Efficiency

Micro-hydro technology offers a sustainable and efficient solution for irrigation, reducing reliance on diesel generators and increasing energy efficiency. By harnessing the power of small-scale hydroelectric systems, micro-hydro can provide a reliable water supply while minimizing environmental impacts.

## Integration in Climate-Resilient Water Supply

Pilot projects have demonstrated the effectiveness of integrating agroforestry and micro-hydro systems for climate-resilient irrigation (e.g., Pilot Project 1). Economic analysis indicates that these integrated solutions can be cost-effective, with potential for scaling up to larger agricultural operations.

## Policy Recommendations

To support the adoption of agroforestry-based micro-hydro systems at scale, we recommend:

* Subsidies and low-interest loans for farmers and cooperatives
* Infrastructure development for micro-hydro technology
* Capacity building for farmers and extension services on integrated agroforestry-micro-hydro management
