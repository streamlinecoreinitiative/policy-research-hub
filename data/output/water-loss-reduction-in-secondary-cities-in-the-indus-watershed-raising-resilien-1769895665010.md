### Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa is one of the most vulnerable regions globally to climate change impacts due to its high population density in arid and semi-arid zones (UN-Water/WHO, 2022). Water scarcity exacerbates existing challenges such as food insecurity, health issues, and economic instability. Limited access to electricity hinders development, particularly for remote communities where climate risks are highest.

3. **Enhance Climate Adaptation Strategies**: Implement early warning systems for extreme weather events, develop drought-resistant crop varieties, and build water storage facilities (World Bank, 2024).
4. **Strengthen Institutional Capacities**: Train local officials on sustainable development practices and ensure effective implementation of policies through transparent monitoring mechanisms.
