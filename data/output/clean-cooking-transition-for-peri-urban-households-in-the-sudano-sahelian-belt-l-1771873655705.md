## Executive Summary

To address the pressing challenges of water security, clean energy access, and climate resilience in Sub-Saharan Africa, we recommend a multi-faceted approach. Firstly, governments should prioritize investments in water infrastructure, particularly in rural areas, to increase access to safely managed drinking water (32.4% currently) [1]. Secondly, policies that incentivize the adoption of renewable energy sources like solar, wind, and hydroelectric power should be implemented, with a focus on grid modernization to support distributed renewable energy systems [2]. Lastly, climate resilience programs should be enhanced through early warning systems for climate-induced disasters and implementation of climate-resilient agricultural practices.

## Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa faces significant challenges related to water security, clean energy access, and climate adaptation. Despite progress, the region's population has limited access to safely managed drinking water (32.4% [1]) and electricity (53.3% [2]). These disparities threaten economic growth and exacerbate climate vulnerabilities. The Sudano-Sahelian belt, which spans across several countries in West Africa, is particularly vulnerable due to its arid climate and limited resources.

1. **Expand Water Infrastructure Investments**:
   - Increase funding for water supply and sanitation projects, particularly in rural areas.
   - Promote community-based management of water resources to ensure sustainable use.

2. **Accelerate Renewable Energy Deployment**:
   - Implement policies that incentivize the adoption of renewable energy sources like solar, wind, and hydroelectric power.
   - Invest in grid modernization to support distributed renewable energy systems.

3. **Enhance Climate Resilience Programs**:
   - Develop early warning systems for climate-induced disasters, such as droughts and floods [5].
