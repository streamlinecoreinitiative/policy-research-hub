## Executive Summary

This policy brief emphasizes the urgent need to address water scarcity, energy poverty, and climate change impacts in Sub-Saharan Africa. With only 32.4% of people having access to safely managed drinking water (World Bank Open Data) and a meager 53.3% relying on electricity (World Bank Open Data), the region faces critical challenges that threaten its socio-economic development and public health. ## Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa is one of the most vulnerable regions globally to water scarcity, energy poverty, and climate change impacts. Despite 73.7% of the world having access to safely managed drinking water (World Bank Open Data), this figure drops significantly in Sub-Saharan Africa where only 32.4% have access (World Bank Open Data). Similarly, while 91.6% of the world has access to electricity (World Bank Open Data), just under half of Sub-Saharan Africa's population has reliable power.

## Analysis

The region suffers from inadequate water supply, contamination, and poor sanitation facilities. Renewable energy consumption remains low at 19.7% compared to global targets (World Bank Open Data). Sub-Saharan Africa is highly vulnerable to climate change impacts such as droughts, floods, and extreme weather events.

Data points:

- World Bank data shows that only 32.4% of the population in Sub-Saharan Africa has access to safely managed drinking water (World Bank Open Data).
- Renewable energy consumption stands at just 19.7%, significantly below global targets.
- The region's climate resilience efforts are lagging behind, with climate change exacerbating existing vulnerabilities.

## Policy Recommendations

1. **Enhance Water Security**:
   - Invest in water infrastructure projects to improve access to clean drinking water and sanitation facilities.
   - Implement policies promoting rainwater harvesting, groundwater recharge, and desalination technologies tailored for arid regions.
2. **Promote Renewable Energy Access**:
   - Increase investment in solar, wind, and hydroelectric power generation.
   - Encourage decentralized renewable energy solutions such as mini-grids and off-grid systems suitable for rural areas.
3. **Strengthen Climate Resilience**:
   - Develop early warning systems to mitigate climate impacts like droughts, floods, and storms.
   - Implement crop diversification programs that adapt to changing weather patterns.
4. **Funding Mechanisms**:
   - Establish dedicated funds or grants specifically for water security, renewable energy access, and climate adaptation projects in Sub-Saharan Africa.

Sub-Saharan Africa urgently needs strategic interventions to improve water security, renewable energy access, and climate resilience.
