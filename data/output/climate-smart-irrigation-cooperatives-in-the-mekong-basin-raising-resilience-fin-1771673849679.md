## Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa faces critical challenges in water security, clean energy access, and climate adaptation. Over 32.4% of the region's population lacks safely managed drinking water (World Bank, 2024), while only 53.3% have access to electricity (World Bank, 2023). These disparities not only affect basic health and safety but also impede economic development and sustainable growth.

### Climate Adaptation

* The region is particularly vulnerable to climate-related shocks such as droughts and floods due to weak infrastructure and limited capacity for adaptation.
* Developing early warning systems for climate-related events like floods and droughts can help mitigate these risks.

## Policy Recommendations

1. **Enhanced Renewable Energy Investment:**
	* Increase investment in solar, wind, and hydroelectric power generation to meet energy demands.
	* Implement supportive policies like feed-in tariffs and tax incentives to encourage private sector participation (World Bank, 2023).
2. **Watershed Management and Water Supply Improvements:**
	* Invest in infrastructure projects such as water treatment plants and pipelines to improve access to clean drinking water.
	* Promote community-based watershed management programs to enhance natural water resources sustainably (UN-Water/WHO, 2022).
3. **Climate Resilience Strategies:**
	* Develop early warning systems for climate-related events like floods and droughts.
	* Implement climate-resilient agricultural practices and disaster risk reduction measures in vulnerable communities.
