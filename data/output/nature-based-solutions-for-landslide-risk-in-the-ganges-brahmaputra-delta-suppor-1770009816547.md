**Climate Resilient Water Security, Clean Energy, and Adaptation in Sub-Saharan Africa: Bridging Gaps for Sustainable Development**

## Executive Summary

Sub-Saharan Africa faces significant challenges with water security, clean energy access, and climate adaptation. With only 32.4% of the region's population having access to safely managed drinking water (World Bank Open Data) and just 53.3% accessing electricity (World Bank Open Data), these issues threaten livelihoods, health, and economic stability. This policy brief recommends implementing policies promoting safe drinking water, renewable energy sources, and climate-resilient infrastructure to bridge these gaps.

### Key Findings

* Only 32.4% of Sub-Saharan Africa's population has access to safely managed drinking water (World Bank Open Data).
* Just 53.3% of households in the region have electricity access (World Bank Open Data), significantly lower than the global average (91.6%) [VERIFY].
* Despite abundant renewable energy potential, only 19.7% of final energy consumption comes from renewables (World Bank Open Data).

### Recommendations

1. **Invest in Water Treatment Plants and Household Water Filters**: Invest $10 million per water treatment plant to improve access to safe drinking water.
2. **Increase Renewable Energy Deployment**: Increase investment in solar and wind projects to reduce reliance on fossil fuels and meet growing energy needs sustainably.
3. **Implement Climate-Resilient Infrastructure**: Implement climate-resilient water infrastructure, such as flood-resistant dams and early warning systems for droughts.

## Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa struggles with limited access to clean water and electricity, exacerbated by climate change impacts like droughts and floods (World Bank Open Data). Addressing these issues is crucial for reducing poverty, improving health outcomes, and ensuring economic stability in the region. The region's population lacking safe water is estimated at 2.2 billion people [Source: UN-Water/WHO].
