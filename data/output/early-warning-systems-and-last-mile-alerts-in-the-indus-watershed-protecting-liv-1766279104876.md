# Early-Warning Systems and Last-Mile Alerts in the Indus Watershed: Protecting Livelihoods Under Climate Stress

## Executive Summary

The Indus watershed supports over 100 million people's livelihoods across Pakistan, India, China, and Afghanistan. Climate change poses significant risks to this region's water security, with increasing frequency and severity of droughts and floods threatening agricultural production and food security. This brief highlights the importance of early-warning systems and last-mile alerts in protecting livelihoods under climate stress.

## Introduction to Water Scarcity in the Indus Watershed

Climate change is altering precipitation patterns in the Indus watershed, leading to increased variability in water availability. This has significant implications for agriculture, which relies heavily on irrigation systems that are often inefficient and unsustainable.

* Erratic rainfall creates challenges for traditional irrigation methods.
* Traditional systems are energy-intensive and costly.
* Climate change impacts agriculture, specifically characterized by erratic rainfall patterns.

## Early-Warning Systems and Last-Mile Alerts for Climate Resilience

Early-warning systems and last-mile alerts can provide critical support to farmers in the Indus watershed by enabling them to prepare for and respond to climate-related shocks. These systems rely on real-time monitoring of weather conditions, soil moisture, and crop health.

* Early-warning systems enable farmers to anticipate and prepare for climate-related events.
* Last-mile alerts ensure that timely information reaches farmers at the local level.
* Climate-resilient agriculture practices can be promoted through these systems.
