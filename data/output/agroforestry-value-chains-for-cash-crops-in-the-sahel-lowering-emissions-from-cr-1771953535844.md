## Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa is grappling with significant challenges in accessing safe drinking water due to population growth, inadequate infrastructure, and economic constraints (UN-Water/WHO). The region's climate change impacts are exacerbated by rising global temperatures (+1.1°C since pre-industrial levels) (IPCC AR6), leading to more frequent droughts and floods that threaten existing water supply systems and energy infrastructures.

## Analysis

The critical need for improved water security in Sub-Saharan Africa is underscored by a global population lacking safe drinking water at 2.2 billion people (UN-Water/WHO). This issue is compounded by climate change, with temperatures having risen to +1.1°C since pre-industrial levels (IPCC AR6), making traditional water management practices unsustainable. Additionally, only 74% of the global population has access to safely managed drinking water under SDG 6 targets (UN SDG Report 2023), highlighting the urgent need for comprehensive and innovative solutions.

## Policy Recommendations

To improve water security, clean energy access, and climate resilience in Sub-Saharan Africa, we recommend the following actionable steps:

1. **Invest in sustainable water management systems**: Invest in rainwater harvesting and small-scale desalination plants to provide safe drinking water for communities (UN-Water/WHO). Implement community-led initiatives to promote hygiene practices and safe drinking water distribution.
2. **Develop off-grid solar energy solutions**: Develop affordable off-grid solar energy solutions for rural communities through microfinance schemes and public-private partnerships (IRENA).
3. **Integrate climate-smart agriculture practices**: Integrate drought-resistant crop varieties and early warning systems into farming communities to enhance resilience against climate-related shocks (UNFCCC).
