# Nature-Based Solutions for Landslide Risk in Caribbean SIDS: Stabilizing Food Security during Droughts

## Executive Summary

Climate change exacerbates water scarcity and landslides in Small Island Developing States (SIDS) of the Caribbean, threatening food security and economic stability. This brief proposes nature-based solutions to mitigate landslide risk and ensure resilient irrigation systems for agriculture.

Agroforestry-based micro-hydro systems are integrated to provide efficient and sustainable water supply, leveraging renewable energy sources. By addressing immediate challenges and contributing to broader sustainable development goals, this approach ensures long-term food security and economic benefits.

## Climate Change Impacts on Water Scarcity in Caribbean SIDS

Climate change alters precipitation patterns, leading to droughts and increased landslide risk in the Caribbean. Rising temperatures affect agriculture, reducing crop yields and impacting livelihoods [verify]. Severe water scarcity threatens food security, economic stability, and community resilience.
