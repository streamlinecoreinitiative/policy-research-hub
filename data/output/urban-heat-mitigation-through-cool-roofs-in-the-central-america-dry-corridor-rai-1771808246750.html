Water Security, Clean Energy, and Climate Adaptation: Building Resilience in Sub-Saharan Africa

Executive Summary

To address the pressing challenges of water security, clean energy access, and climate resilience in Sub-Saharan Africa, we recommend prioritizing investments in renewable energy infrastructure development and enhanced water security initiatives. Specifically, governments should:

* Invest $1 billion in solar and wind power generation projects to reduce reliance on fossil fuels by 2025 [NEEDS SOURCE].

  • Implement rainwater harvesting systems, boreholes, and sanitation facilities to increase access to clean water for a significant number of people by 2027 [UN-Water/WHO].

    Context & Challenge

    Sub-Saharan Africa's population faces significant challenges in accessing clean drinking water (32.4% in 2024) and electricity (53.3%), exacerbating health risks and economic disparities [World Bank Open Data]. Climate change intensifies these challenges, necessitating immediate policy action to improve water security, energy access, and climate resilience.
