# water loss reduction in secondary cities in the Sahel: safeguarding freshwater ecosystems

**Strengthening Water Security, Access to Clean Energy, and Climate Resilience in Sub-Saharan Africa: A Path to Sustainable Development**

### Executive Summary

To address the pressing challenges of water security, clean energy access, and climate resilience in Sub-Saharan Africa, we recommend investing $10 billion over the next 5 years in infrastructure projects that improve water supply systems, promote renewable energy adoption, and enhance climate adaptation strategies. This investment will help bridge the significant gaps in safely managed drinking water (32.4% of the population has access) [1] and electricity access (53.3%), while also promoting the use of renewable energy sources to meet 30% of total final energy consumption by 2025.

### Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa is characterized by arid conditions, resulting in limited natural freshwater resources and frequent droughts [2]. This region lacks adequate drinking water facilities, leading to public health issues. Additionally, electricity access is unevenly distributed across the continent, with Sub-Saharan Africa lagging behind other regions. The adoption of renewable energy sources remains insufficient, posing challenges for sustainable development.

### Analysis

Access to safe drinking water in Sub-Saharan Africa remains a significant challenge. Only 32.4% of the population has access to safely managed drinking water [3], putting public health at risk. In contrast, globally, this figure stands at 73.7% [1]. The disparity in electricity access is even more pronounced: Sub-Saharan Africa lags behind other regions with only 53.3% of households having access to electricity [4], compared to a global average of 91.6% [5].

### Policy Recommendations

1. **Water Security Initiatives**:
   - Invest $2 billion in infrastructure projects to improve water supply systems.
   - Promote rainwater harvesting and other sustainable water management practices.
   - Support research and development to enhance water efficiency technologies.

2. **Clean Energy Deployment**:
   - Implement feed-in tariffs or subsidies for renewable energy projects.
   - Encourage private sector investments through tax incentives and grants.
   - Develop comprehensive policies that promote the adoption of solar, wind, and hydroelectric power.

3. **Climate Adaptation Programs**:
   - Establish early warning systems to mitigate climate impacts on water resources and energy supply.
   - Invest in climate-resilient infrastructure projects such as flood-resistant buildings and irrigation systems.
   - Collaborate with local communities to develop adaptive strategies.

### Implementation Considerations

- **Costs**: Initiatives will require substantial financial resources. Public-private partnerships can help mobilize additional funding from international organizations and philanthropic entities.
- **Potential Barriers**: Political resistance may hinder policy implementation due to vested interests in traditional energy sources or water management practices. Community engagement is crucial for successful execution of climate adaptation programs.

### Conclusion

Sub-Saharan Africa must prioritize measures to enhance water security, promote clean energy access, and strengthen climate resilience. By adopting the recommended policies, the region can achieve sustainable development goals aligned with global targets. Stakeholders should collaborate closely to overcome implementation challenges and ensure effective deployment of these interventions.

- **Call to Action**: Governments, NGOs, and international organizations must work together to implement these recommendations in a coordinated manner.
- **Next Steps**: Conduct pilot projects in various regions to assess effectiveness and refine strategies for broader rollout.
