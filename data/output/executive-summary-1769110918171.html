Climate Resilience and Energy Access in Rural Sub-Saharan Africa: Strengthening Water Security, Clean Energy Adoption, and Adaptation Strategies

Executive Summary

The region's lack of safe drinking water (32.4%)[1] and reliable electricity (53.3%) threatens health outcomes and economic development. To address these issues, this policy brief recommends expanding access to solar power and investing in climate-resilient infrastructure to ensure sustainable development.

Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa faces significant challenges in accessing safe drinking water and reliable electricity. The region's population lacks access to safely managed drinking water (32.4%)[1], which is critical for health outcomes and economic development. Climate change intensifies these challenges, with more frequent droughts and floods affecting water availability and energy infrastructure reliability.
