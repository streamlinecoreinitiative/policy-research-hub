# Agroforestry-Based Micro-Hydro Systems for Climate-Resilient Irrigation in the Ethiopian Highlands

## Executive Summary

The Ethiopian Highlands face severe water scarcity due to climate change characterized by erratic rainfall patterns. Traditional irrigation methods exacerbate poverty among smallholder farmers, making them energy-intensive and costly. This brief explores the integration of agroforestry with micro-hydro systems as a solution to address these challenges.

## Introduction

Climate change impacts have become increasingly evident in the Ethiopian Highlands, posing significant challenges to smallholder farmers who rely heavily on traditional irrigation methods. These systems not only exacerbate poverty among farmers but also contribute to environmental degradation through high energy consumption and water loss.
