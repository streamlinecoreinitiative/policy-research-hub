## Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa is one of the most vulnerable regions globally to climate change impacts. Water scarcity, unreliable electricity supply, and limited access to clean energy exacerbate existing socio-economic challenges. Ensuring sustainable water security, reliable energy sources, and resilient infrastructure can mitigate immediate health risks and foster economic growth.

* Background on the issue: Sub-Saharan Africa is particularly susceptible to droughts, which can have devastating impacts on food production and security [Source: World Bank Open Data].
* Why it matters now: The region's population is projected to reach 2.1 billion by 2050, putting further pressure on already strained resources [UN Department of Economic and Social Affairs Population Division].
