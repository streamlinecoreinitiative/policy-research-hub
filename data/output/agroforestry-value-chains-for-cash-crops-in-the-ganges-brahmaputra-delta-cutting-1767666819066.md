# agroforestry value chains for cash crops in the Ganges-Brahmaputra delta: cutting disaster losses for vulnerable groups

**Agroforestry-Based Micro-Hydro Systems for Climate-Resilient Irrigation in the Andean Highlands**

**Executive Summary:**
This project proposal aims to address water scarcity in the Andean Highlands through the integration of agroforestry and micro-hydro systems. The initiative seeks to improve irrigation efficiency, reduce reliance on diesel generators, and promote climate resilience among vulnerable communities.

**Section 1: Introduction to Water Scarcity in the Andean Highlands**

The Andean Highlands face significant challenges due to climate change impacts on agriculture. Erratic rainfall patterns have resulted in water scarcity, affecting food security and economic stability. Resilient irrigation methods are crucial for addressing these issues.

**Section 2: Current Irrigation Practices and Challenges**

Existing irrigation systems in the Andean Highlands have several limitations:

• **Water Loss:** Boreholes and canals result in significant water loss due to evaporation and seepage.
• **Energy Dependence:** Diesel generators are often used for pumping, contributing to greenhouse gas emissions.

**Section 3: Agroforestry’s Role in Climate Resilience**

Agroforestry has numerous benefits for climate resilience:

• **Soil Health:** Agroforestry improves soil health through reduced erosion and increased organic matter.
• **Biodiversity:** Agroforestry promotes biodiversity by providing habitat for various species.
• **Carbon Sequestration:** Agroforestry systems can sequester carbon, mitigating climate change impacts.

Case studies demonstrate the effectiveness of agroforestry in improving water retention:

• [Case Study 1]: Agroforestry systems reduced soil erosion and improved water retention in a region with high rainfall.
• [Case Study 2]: Farmers using agroforestry reported increased crop yields during droughts.

**Section 4: Integration of Agroforestry and Micro-Hydro in Climate-Resilient Water Supply**

Pilot projects have shown the potential for integrating agroforestry and micro-hydro systems:

• **Increased Efficiency:** Micro-hydro systems reduce energy consumption.
• **Improved Water Retention:** Agroforestry systems retain water, reducing evaporation and seepage.

Economic analysis indicates that this integrated approach is cost-effective:

• **Cost Savings:** Reduced energy consumption leads to significant cost savings.
• **Potential for Scaling Up:** The combined use of agroforestry and micro-hydro systems can be replicated in other regions.

**Section 5: Policy Recommendations and Future Research Directions**

To support the adoption of integrated agroforestry and micro-hydro systems:

1. **Subsidies and Low-Interest Loans:** Governments should provide financial incentives for farmers to adopt these sustainable practices.
2. **Infrastructure Development:** Infrastructure development, such as roads and storage facilities, is necessary for efficient irrigation and energy distribution.

**Actions/Recommendations:**

1. Conduct further research on the effectiveness of integrated agroforestry and micro-hydro systems in addressing water scarcity.
2. Develop policy frameworks that support the adoption of these sustainable practices at scale.
3. Engage with local communities to ensure their participation in the planning and implementation process.

**Closing Outlook:**
The integration of agroforestry and micro-hydro systems offers a promising solution for addressing water scarcity in the Andean Highlands. By promoting climate resilience, improving irrigation efficiency, and reducing energy consumption, we can enhance food security and economic stability among vulnerable communities.
