**Water Security, Clean Energy, and Climate Adaptation in Sub-Saharan Africa: Strengthening Resilience for Sustainable Development**

## Executive Summary

Sub-Saharan Africa faces a critical juncture where water scarcity, energy poverty, and climate change threaten the region's progress. To address these challenges, we recommend prioritizing water security through improved access and management, promoting clean energy solutions like solar power, and enhancing climate adaptation strategies.

Key findings:

* Only 32.4% of the population in Sub-Saharan Africa has access to safely managed drinking water (World Bank, 2024).
* Less than half of Sub-Saharan African countries meet their electricity needs (World Bank, [VERIFY] 2023).
* Climate change poses significant threats to water security and agricultural productivity.

Recommendations:

1. **Enhance Water Security:** Invest in rainwater harvesting, improve groundwater management, and expand desalination technologies.
2. **Promote Clean Energy Solutions:** Accelerate the deployment of solar power systems for both electricity generation and water pumping.
3. **Climate Adaptation Strategies:** Develop early warning systems, strengthen infrastructure resilience to climate impacts, and support climate-resilient agriculture practices.

Immediate policy interventions are essential to address these challenges and ensure sustainable development in Sub-Saharan Africa.

## Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa is the poorest region in terms of access to safe drinking water (32.4% vs 73.7% worldwide) (World Bank, 2024). The region's high population growth, limited energy resources, and increased frequency of extreme weather events exacerbate existing vulnerabilities.

Why it matters now: Failure to address these issues will hinder progress towards achieving sustainable development goals, particularly SDG 6, which aims to ensure access to safely managed drinking water for all (UN SDG Report, 2023).

## Analysis

Evidence:

* Sub-Saharan Africa's electricity access is still low at 53.3% compared to the global average of 91.6% (World Bank, [VERIFY] 2023).
* Climate change poses significant threats to water security and agricultural productivity, with increased frequency and intensity of droughts and floods.

Comparative approaches:

* Investing in clean energy solutions like solar power can reduce reliance on fossil fuels and improve access to electricity.
* Enhancing climate adaptation strategies can strengthen infrastructure resilience and support climate-resilient agriculture practices.

Uncertainties:

* Data on the effectiveness of different interventions is limited, and more research is needed to inform policy decisions.
