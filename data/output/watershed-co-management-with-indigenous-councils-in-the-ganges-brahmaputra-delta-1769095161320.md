## Executive Summary
Strengthening community water systems, increasing access to clean energy, and implementing climate adaptation strategies are critical for enhancing environmental resilience in lower-income countries. The current state of water security and energy poverty in Sub-Saharan Africa poses significant challenges to achieving Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs). To address this, we recommend investing in infrastructure projects such as wells, boreholes, and rainwater harvesting systems, promoting decentralized renewable energy solutions like solar home systems and micro-grids, and developing early warning systems for climate adaptation.

## Policy Recommendations

1. **Strengthen Community Water Systems**: Invest $10-$25 per person in infrastructure projects like wells, boreholes, and rainwater harvesting systems to ensure sustainable use of resources (World Bank, 2020).
2. **Increase Access to Clean Energy**: Promote decentralized renewable energy solutions like solar home systems and micro-grids to support off-grid households.
3. **Implement Climate Adaptation Strategies**: Develop early warning systems for climate-related hazards and enhance water resource management practices.

## Implementation Considerations
Potential barriers include low public awareness, limited funding, and political resistance to change. Addressing these challenges requires collaborative efforts between government agencies, NGOs, and local communities. Costs associated with these interventions can range from $10-$25 per person (World Bank, 2020).

## Conclusion
Enhancing environmental resilience in lower-income countries is a critical imperative for achieving SDGs. Strengthening community water systems, increasing access to clean energy, and implementing climate adaptation strategies are essential steps towards stabilizing food security during droughts. We urge governments and NGOs to prioritize these interventions for effective implementation.
