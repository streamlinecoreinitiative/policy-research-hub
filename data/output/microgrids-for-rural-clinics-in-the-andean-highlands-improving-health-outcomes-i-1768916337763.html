Microgrids for Rural Clinics in the Andean Highlands: Improving Health Outcomes in Heatwaves

Executive Summary

Sub-Saharan Africa faces significant challenges in accessing safe water, reliable electricity, and resilient infrastructure. To address these issues, we recommend prioritizing investments in clean water sources, renewable energy projects, and early warning systems to enhance resilience against climate impacts.

Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa is home to 13% of the global population but only 6% of global freshwater resources [1]. The region's climate challenge is exacerbated by a +1.1¬∞C global temperature rise, with projections indicating further increases by 2050 [2].

Analysis

Water Security

Despite progress towards SDG 6, only 39% of Sub-Saharan Africa's population has access to safe drinking water [3]. This is a significant challenge for rural communities, where access to clean water is often limited. In the Andean highlands, microgrids can provide reliable electricity for water treatment and pumping systems.

Clean Energy

Electrification rates in Sub-Saharan Africa are low at 58% of the population [4]. Renewable energy projects, such as solar power generation, can increase electricity supply while reducing reliance on fossil fuels. Microgrids can also provide off-grid solutions for rural communities.

Climate Adaptation

Vulnerable communities are disproportionately affected by climate-related events like droughts and floods. Early warning systems can significantly reduce disaster impacts but require substantial investment [5].
