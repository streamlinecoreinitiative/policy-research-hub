**Agroforestry-Based Micro-Hydro Systems for Climate-Resilient Irrigation in the Ethiopian Highlands**

**Executive Summary:**
The proposal aims to address severe water scarcity through integrated agroforestry and micro-hydro systems in the Ethiopian Highlands. By leveraging these sustainable solutions, we can improve irrigation efficiency, reduce energy consumption, and promote climate resilience in agriculture. This approach has the potential to contribute significantly to the achievement of Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs) 2 and 13.

**Section 1: Introduction to Water Scarcity**

The Ethiopian Highlands are characterized by a semi-arid climate with average annual rainfall ranging from 800-1200 mm, accompanied by significant precipitation variability. Traditional irrigation systems in this region experience high water loss rates due to evaporation (30%) and seepage (5%). These inefficiencies exacerbate the existing water scarcity issues, impacting agricultural productivity and food security.

**Section 2: Current Irrigation Practices and Challenges**

Existing irrigation methods in the Ethiopian Highlands rely heavily on borehole pumps, which consume substantial amounts of energy. Canal systems also suffer from significant water loss due to evaporation (30%) and seepage (5%). The economic burden associated with these traditional irrigation practices is substantial.
