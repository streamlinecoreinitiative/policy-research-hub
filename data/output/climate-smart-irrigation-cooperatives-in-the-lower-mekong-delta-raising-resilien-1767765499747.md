# climate-smart irrigation cooperatives in the Lower Mekong delta: raising resilience finance for communities

**Climate-Smart Irrigation Cooperatives in the Lower Mekong Delta: Raising Resilience Finance for Communities**

Executive Summary
-----------------

The Lower Mekong delta is a critical region for agriculture, supporting millions of people through rice production. Climate change has led to increased frequency and severity of droughts and floods, exacerbating water scarcity. This brief highlights the importance of integrated solutions that address these challenges through agroforestry-based micro-hydro systems for climate-resilient irrigation.

**Section 1: Introduction to Water Scarcity in the Lower Mekong Delta**

The region faces severe water scarcity due to climate change, affecting agriculture and communities. Existing irrigation systems suffer from significant water loss due to evaporation and seepage. This brief will explore the role of agroforestry-based micro-hydro systems in improving water efficiency.

**Section 2: Current Irrigation Practices and Challenges**

Current irrigation practices are often inefficient, relying on diesel generators for energy. This contributes to greenhouse gas emissions and increases costs for farmers. The lack of access to clean energy and water resources hinders economic development and exacerbates poverty.

Agroforestry practices have been shown to improve soil health, biodiversity, and carbon sequestration. By integrating trees into agricultural landscapes, farmers can reduce erosion and increase water retention. Case studies demonstrate that agroforestry improves crop yields and reduces the need for fertilizers and pesticides.

**Section 4: Micro-Hydro Systems for Irrigation Efficiency**

Micro-hydro systems are small-scale hydroelectric power plants that harness energy from rivers and streams. These systems offer a reliable source of clean energy, reducing reliance on diesel generators and increasing energy efficiency. By integrating micro-hydro with agroforestry, communities can improve water security.

**Section 5: Integration in Climate-Resilient Water Supply**

Pilot projects have demonstrated the potential for agroforestry-based micro-hydro systems to improve climate resilience in irrigation. These integrated solutions enhance water efficiency and promote sustainable agriculture practices, improving crop yields and reducing poverty.

----------------

Climate-smart irrigation cooperatives have the potential to transform the Lower Mekong delta's agricultural landscape. By integrating agroforestry and micro-hydro systems, communities can improve their resilience to climate change while promoting economic development and poverty reduction.
