## Executive Summary

The Sudano-Sahelian belt is highly vulnerable to climate change, facing severe water scarcity, droughts, and extreme weather events. Integrating agroforestry with micro-hydro systems can provide a sustainable solution that enhances both water security and renewable energy use.

## Introduction to Water Scarcity in the Sudano-Sahelian Belt

The Sudano-Sahelian belt is a critical agricultural region facing significant water scarcity issues exacerbated by climate change. Climate change has led to erratic rainfall patterns, increased evaporation from vast wetlands, and droughts that have devastating impacts on agriculture and livelihoods.

*   Erratic rainfall patterns: The region experiences frequent droughts and floods, making it challenging for farmers to predict and prepare for water availability.
*   Increased evaporation: The Sudano-Sahelian belt has extensive wetlands, which contribute significantly to the regional water cycle. However, increased temperatures due to climate change have led to higher evaporation rates, reducing available water resources.

## Current Irrigation Practices and Challenges

Existing irrigation systems in the region are often inefficient and costly:

*   Boreholes: While providing a reliable source of water, boreholes require significant energy inputs for pumping and maintenance.
*   Canals: Canals are prone to seepage and evaporation losses, reducing the overall efficiency of irrigation.

Micro-hydro technology offers a sustainable solution:

*   Reduced reliance on diesel generators: Micro-hydro systems can generate electricity from water flow, reducing dependence on fossil fuels.
*   Increased energy efficiency: Micro-hydro systems have higher energy conversion rates compared to traditional irrigation methods.
