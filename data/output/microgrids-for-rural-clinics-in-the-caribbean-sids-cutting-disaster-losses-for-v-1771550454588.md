## Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa faces significant challenges in water security, clean energy access, and climate adaptation due to limited resources and infrastructure. As of 2023, only about one-third (32.4%) [1] of the population has access to safely managed drinking water, while electricity access hovers around 50% (53.3%) [2]. Climate change exacerbates these challenges by increasing droughts and floods, further straining already fragile systems.

## Analysis

- **Water Security**: Sub-Saharan Africa faces significant water scarcity issues with only one-third of its population having access to safely managed drinking water (32.4%) [1]. This is exacerbated by poor sanitation facilities, lack of hygiene education, and inadequate wastewater management.
- **Clean Energy**: Access to electricity in the region is limited (53.3%) compared to 91.6% globally [2], with Sub-Saharan Africa lagging behind. Renewable energy consumption stands at only 19.7%, far below global averages [3].
- **Climate Adaptation**: Climate change impacts are severe, including increased frequency of droughts and floods, which further strain water resources and power generation systems.
