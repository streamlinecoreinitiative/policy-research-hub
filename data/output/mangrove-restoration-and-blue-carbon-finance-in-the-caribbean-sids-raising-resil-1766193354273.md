**Mangrove Restoration and Blue Carbon Finance in the Caribbean SIDS: Enhancing Climate Resilience**

**Executive Summary**

The Caribbean Small Island Developing States (SIDS) are highly vulnerable to climate change impacts, including sea-level rise, increased storm intensity, and saltwater intrusion into freshwater sources. Mangrove forests play a critical role in coastal resilience by providing natural barriers against storms, erosion, and flooding. This brief highlights the importance of mangrove restoration and blue carbon finance in enhancing climate resilience for communities in the Caribbean SIDS.

**Section 1: Water Security and Climate Change Impacts**

The Caribbean SIDS face significant water security challenges due to limited freshwater resources. Climate change exacerbates this issue through altered rainfall patterns, increased evaporation from warmer waters, and saltwater intrusion into freshwater sources. Mangrove forests help maintain water quality by filtering pollutants and sediments, while their roots stabilize soil and prevent erosion.
