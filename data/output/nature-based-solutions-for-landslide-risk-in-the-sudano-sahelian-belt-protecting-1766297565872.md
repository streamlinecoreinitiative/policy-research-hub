**Executive Summary**

The Sudano-Sahelian belt faces severe climate-related challenges that threaten food security and economic stability due to water scarcity. This brief proposes an integrated agroforestry-micro-hydro system as a solution to address these issues.

**Section 1: Introduction to Water Scarcity in the Sudano-Sahelian Belt**

The Sudano-Sahelian belt spans across several countries in West Africa, including Nigeria, Chad, and parts of Mali. Climate change has significantly impacted this region, leading to erratic rainfall patterns that exacerbate water scarcity. Agriculture is a critical sector in these economies, but its productivity is heavily dependent on reliable irrigation systems.

*   **Climate Change Impacts:** Erratic rainfall patterns have become more frequent, making it challenging for farmers to predict and plan their crop cycles.
*   **Importance of Resilient Irrigation Methods:**
    *   Food security is threatened by water scarcity, leading to reduced agricultural productivity and income losses for smallholder farmers.
    *   Economic stability in these countries relies heavily on agriculture, which accounts for a significant share of GDP.
