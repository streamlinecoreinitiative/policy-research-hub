## Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa faces significant challenges related to water security, clean energy access, and climate adaptation. With only [32.4%] of the population having access to safely managed drinking water (World Bank Open Data) and over a quarter relying on traditional biomass for cooking (UN-Water/WHO), these issues jeopardize public health and economic stability.

## Analysis

The context of water scarcity is exacerbated by inadequate infrastructure, particularly in rural areas where access to safe drinking water remains limited. According to the World Bank (2024), only [32.4%] of Sub-Saharan Africa's population has access to safely managed drinking water, compared to a global average of [73.7%]. This disparity is further compounded by traditional biomass use for cooking, which contributes significantly to indoor air pollution and exacerbates respiratory diseases (UN-Water/WHO).

## Policy Recommendations

1. **Water Security**: Invest in water infrastructure projects such as wells, boreholes, and rainwater harvesting systems to improve access to safe drinking water (World Bank Open Data).
2. **Clean Energy**: Promote the adoption of renewable energy sources like solar power, wind turbines, and biogas systems for cooking and electricity generation (World Bank Open Data).
3. **Climate Adaptation**: Develop early warning systems, resilient infrastructure, and climate-resilient agricultural practices to mitigate the impacts of extreme weather events (NOAA climate dataset).
