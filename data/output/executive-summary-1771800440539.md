# Executive Summary

**Harnessing Renewable Energy and Water Security for Sub-Saharan Africa: Strengthening Climate Resilience Through Evidence-Based Policies**

## Executive Summary

To enhance climate resilience in Sub-Saharan Africa, we recommend investing in renewable energy infrastructure to increase access to electricity and implementing water conservation measures to improve water security. This will require significant upfront investment but yield long-term savings through reduced energy costs and improved water security.

## Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa is grappling with severe water scarcity, unreliable electricity access, and climate-induced vulnerabilities. According to the World Bank (2024), 32.4% of the population has access to safely managed drinking water, while only 53.3% have access to electricity (World Bank, 2023). Climate change exacerbates existing vulnerabilities, leading to more frequent droughts and heatwaves.

### Electricity Access

### Renewable Energy Consumption

- The region's renewable energy consumption stands at just 19.7% of total final energy consumption (World Bank, 2020), far below global averages.

## Policy Recommendations

1. **Invest in Renewable Energy Infrastructure**: Prioritize solar and wind projects to reduce dependency on fossil fuels and increase access to electricity.
2. **Implement Water Conservation Measures**: Promote rainwater harvesting systems, wastewater treatment plants, and efficient irrigation technologies.
3. **Enhance Community Engagement**: Collaborate with local communities in watershed management and climate adaptation initiatives.

## Implementation Considerations

- Implementing these measures would require significant upfront investment but yield long-term savings through reduced energy costs and improved water security.
- Potential challenges include limited financial resources and resistance from traditional power sectors.

## Conclusion

By focusing on renewable energy and water security, Sub-Saharan African nations can build a more resilient future that protects lives and livelihoods against climate-induced shocks. We urge governments to take decisive action to implement evidence-based policies that enhance the region's resilience in the face of climate change.
