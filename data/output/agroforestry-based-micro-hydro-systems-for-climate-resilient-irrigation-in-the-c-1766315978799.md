**Agroforestry-Based Micro-Hydro Systems for Climate-Resilient Irrigation in the Central African Rainforest**

Executive Summary
-----------------

The Central African Rainforest faces severe water scarcity due to climate change, impacting agriculture and smallholder farmers' livelihoods. Traditional irrigation methods are energy-intensive and costly, exacerbating economic challenges. This brief proposes integrating agroforestry with micro-hydro systems as a sustainable solution for climate-resilient irrigation.

### Introduction to Water Scarcity in the Central African Rainforest

Climate change alters rainfall patterns, leading to erratic precipitation and increased evaporation from vast wetlands. This results in water scarcity, affecting agriculture, a critical sector for food security and economic stability in the region. Resilient irrigation methods are essential to mitigate these impacts.

### Current Irrigation Practices and Challenges

Existing irrigation systems suffer from significant water loss through evaporation and seepage. These traditional methods waste resources and contribute to greenhouse gas emissions due to energy consumption. Smallholder farmers, already vulnerable to droughts, face additional economic challenges.

### Micro-Hydro Systems for Irrigation Efficiency

Micro-hydro technology harnesses the energy potential of small water flows to generate electricity. This approach reduces reliance on diesel generators, decreasing greenhouse gas emissions and operational costs. By integrating micro-hydro systems with agroforestry, communities can achieve more efficient irrigation practices while promoting renewable energy use.

### Integration of Agroforestry and Micro-Hydro in Climate-Resilient Water Supply

Pilot projects have demonstrated the potential of combining agroforestry and micro-hydro for climate-resilient water supply. These integrated systems enhance water security and provide economic benefits through reduced energy costs and increased crop yields.
