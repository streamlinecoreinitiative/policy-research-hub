# Enhancing Water Security in the Central America Dry Corridor: Integrating Agroforestry and Micro-Hydro Systems for Climate Resilience

## Executive Summary

The Central America dry corridor is highly vulnerable to climate change, facing severe water scarcity, poverty, and food insecurity. This brief highlights the importance of early-warning systems and last-mile alerts in supporting adaptation targets in Nationally Determined Contributions (NDCs). We focus on two key areas: agroforestry-based micro-hydro systems for climate-resilient irrigation and the integration of renewable energy sources to enhance water security.

## Water Security in the Central America Dry Corridor

### Introduction to Water Scarcity in the Mesoamerican Highlands

Climate change is projected to exacerbate water scarcity in the Mesoamerican Highlands, impacting agriculture, food security, and economic stability. Rising temperatures and changing precipitation patterns will lead to increased evaporation rates and reduced soil moisture.

### Current Irrigation Practices and Challenges

Existing irrigation systems in the region are often inefficient, with significant water losses due to evaporation and seepage. These losses reduce water availability and increase energy consumption for pumping and treatment.
