Executive Summary

Sub-Saharan Africa faces a critical need to enhance environmental resilience through improved water security, clean energy access, and climate adaptation strategies. With only 32.4% of the population having access to safely managed drinking water (World Bank, 2024) [1] and less than half (53.3%) enjoying electricity (World Bank, 2023) [2], immediate action is required to address these challenges. This policy brief outlines actionable recommendations for government officials, NGO leaders, and development practitioners to enhance environmental resilience in the region.

Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa is one of the most vulnerable regions in terms of water scarcity and energy access. The region's population faces significant challenges in accessing safe drinking water, with only 32.4% having access to safely managed drinking water (World Bank, 2024) [1]. Furthermore, less than half (53.3%) of the population enjoys electricity access (World Bank, 2023) [2], exacerbating the challenges faced by communities. Climate change impacts are also a pressing concern, leading to more frequent droughts and floods that necessitate urgent interventions in water security, energy access, and climate adaptation.

Analysis

The region faces significant gaps in both infrastructure and policy frameworks for sustainable development. While renewable energy consumption stands at only 19.7% of total final energy consumption (World Bank, 2020) [3], the potential exists to significantly increase this through targeted investments. Water security is equally crucial; with just over one-third of the population having access to safely managed drinking water (32.4%) and less than half having electricity access (53.3%), there are substantial opportunities for improvement.
