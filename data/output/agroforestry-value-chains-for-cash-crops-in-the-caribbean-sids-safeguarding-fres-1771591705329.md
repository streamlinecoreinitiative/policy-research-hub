## Executive Summary

Immediate action is needed to address the significant water security challenges facing Sub-Saharan Africa, where only 32.4% of the population has access to safely managed drinking water (World Bank Open Data). ## Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa struggles with limited access to safe drinking water, with only 32.4% of the population benefiting from reliable clean water sources (World Bank Open Data). This disparity highlights a critical gap in ensuring public health and sustainable development, particularly given climate change's exacerbating effects on already vulnerable ecosystems. ## Analysis

The current situation underscores the necessity of integrating climate adaptation strategies into existing policies. For instance, solar-powered desalination plants could significantly augment safe drinking water supplies in arid regions (UN-Water/WHO). Additionally, expanding renewable energy sources like wind and solar power can reduce reliance on fossil fuels for water treatment and distribution infrastructure.

## Policy Recommendations

1. **Integrate Climate Adaptation Strategies**: Embed climate-resilient infrastructure into national development plans to ensure long-term sustainability.
2. **Promote Renewable Energy Use**: Increase investment in solar, wind, and hydro renewables to meet growing energy demands without exacerbating environmental degradation.
3. **Enhance Water Infrastructure**: Upgrade existing water treatment facilities with state-of-the-art technology adapted for climate resilience.
