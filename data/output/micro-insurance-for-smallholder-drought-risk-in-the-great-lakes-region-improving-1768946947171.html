Enhancing Environmental Resilience: Water Security, Clean Energy Access, and Climate Adaptation in Southeast Asia

Executive Summary

This policy brief highlights the critical need to enhance environmental resilience in Southeast Asia by improving water security, clean energy access, and climate adaptation. The region faces significant challenges in accessing safe drinking water (28.6% of population has access to safely managed drinking water [Source: World Bank Open Data]) and reliable electricity (40.1% of population has access to electricity [Source: World Bank Open Data]). To address these gaps, governments and international partners should prioritize the following actionable steps:

* Expand access to safe drinking water by increasing investment in water sanitation projects and promoting community-based management of public water sources.

  • Promote renewable energy adoption through government subsidies and incentives for renewable energy projects like solar panels, wind turbines, and micro-hydropower systems.
  • Enhance climate resilience infrastructure by investing in early warning systems and disaster preparedness programs.

    Context & Challenge

    Southeast Asia is one of the regions most vulnerable to climate change impacts such as droughts, floods, and sea-level rise. The region's water security and access to clean energy are critical for its economic development and social stability. According to the World Bank Open Data, only 28.6% of Southeast Asian countries meet WHO/UN standards for safely managed drinking water [Source: World Bank Open Data], far below the global average (73.7%, [Source: World Bank Open Data]). Similarly, electricity access remains unevenly distributed with 40.1% of the population having access to reliable power in the region compared to 91.6% globally [Source: World Bank Open Data].
