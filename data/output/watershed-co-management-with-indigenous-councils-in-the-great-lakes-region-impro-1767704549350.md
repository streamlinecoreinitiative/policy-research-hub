# Agroforestry-Based Micro-Hydro Systems for Climate-Resilient Irrigation in the Andean Highlands

## Executive Summary

The Andean Highlands face significant water scarcity challenges due to climate change, affecting agriculture and food security. This project proposes integrating agroforestry with micro-hydro systems to improve irrigation efficiency and resilience. The approach combines the benefits of agroforestry for soil health, biodiversity, and carbon sequestration with the energy efficiency of micro-hydro technology.

## Introduction to Water Scarcity in the Andean Highlands

Climate change has led to erratic rainfall patterns in the Andean Highlands, exacerbating water scarcity issues. Agriculture is particularly vulnerable, as it relies heavily on irrigation systems that are often inefficient and prone to water loss through evaporation and seepage.

### Current Irrigation Practices and Challenges

Existing irrigation systems in the Andean Highlands include boreholes and canals. However, these systems suffer from significant water losses due to inefficient design and maintenance. Water scarcity has severe consequences for local communities, including reduced crop yields and decreased income.

Agroforestry is a land-use practice that integrates trees into agricultural landscapes. It offers numerous benefits for soil health, biodiversity, and carbon sequestration. Case studies demonstrate how agroforestry systems improve water retention and reduce erosion.

### Micro-Hydro Systems for Irrigation Efficiency

Micro-hydro technology involves the use of small-scale hydroelectric systems to generate electricity. This approach reduces reliance on diesel generators and increases energy efficiency. By harnessing the power of local water sources, micro-hydro systems can provide a reliable source of energy for irrigation pumps and other agricultural equipment.

### Integration of Agroforestry and Micro-Hydro in Climate-Resilient Water Supply

Pilot projects have demonstrated the potential for integrating agroforestry with micro-hydro systems to create climate-resilient water supplies. These integrated systems not only improve irrigation efficiency but also provide a source of renewable energy, reducing greenhouse gas emissions.
