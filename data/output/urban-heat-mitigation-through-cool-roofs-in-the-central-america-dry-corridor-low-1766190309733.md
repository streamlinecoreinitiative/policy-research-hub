# urban heat mitigation through cool roofs in the Central America dry corridor: lowering emissions from critical sectors

**Urban Heat Mitigation through Cool Roofs in the Central America Dry Corridor: Lowering Emissions from Critical Sectors**

**Executive Summary**

The Central America dry corridor is highly vulnerable to climate change, facing severe water scarcity and increased temperatures. Traditional irrigation methods are energy-intensive and costly, exacerbating economic challenges for smallholder farmers in communities already vulnerable to droughts. This brief explores the potential of integrating agroforestry with micro-hydro systems as a sustainable solution for enhancing both water security and renewable energy use.

**Introduction to Water Scarcity in the Central America Dry Corridor**

The Central America dry corridor spans across several countries, including Guatemala, Honduras, El Salvador, Nicaragua, Costa Rica, and Panama. Climate change has led to erratic rainfall patterns, increased evaporation from its vast wetlands, and higher temperatures. These changes have severe impacts on agriculture, with many smallholder farmers struggling to access reliable water sources for irrigation.

*   **Climate Change Impacts:**
    *   Erratic rainfall patterns
    *   Increased evaporation from wetlands
    *   Higher temperatures
*   **Importance of Resilient Irrigation Methods:**
    *   Food security and economic stability

**Current Irrigation Practices and Challenges**

Existing irrigation systems in the region include boreholes, canals, and rainwater harvesting. However, these methods are often energy-intensive and costly, leading to water loss through evaporation and seepage.

*   **Description of Existing Irrigation Systems:**
    *   Boreholes
    *   Canals
    *   Rainwater harvesting
*   **Analysis of Water Loss:**
    *   Evaporation
    *   Seepage

Micro-hydro technology involves the use of small-scale hydroelectric systems to generate electricity. This approach can reduce reliance on diesel generators and increase energy efficiency.

*   **Overview of Micro-Hydro Technology:**
