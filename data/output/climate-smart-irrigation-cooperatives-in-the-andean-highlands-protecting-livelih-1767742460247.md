# Climate-Smart Irrigation Cooperatives in the Andean Highlands: Protecting Livelihoods Under Climate Stress

## Executive Summary

Climate change is exacerbating water scarcity in the Andean highlands, posing significant threats to agriculture and food security. This brief outlines a comprehensive approach to address this challenge through the integration of agroforestry and micro-hydro systems for climate-resilient irrigation.

## Introduction to Water Scarcity in the Andean Highlands

The Andean highlands are characterized by unpredictable rainfall patterns, resulting from climate change impacts on regional weather systems. This unpredictability leads to severe water scarcity, affecting agriculture – a critical sector for local economies. Irrigation methods must be resilient and efficient to ensure food security and economic stability.

## Current Irrigation Practices and Challenges

Existing irrigation systems in the Andean highlands include boreholes and canals. However, these methods often result in significant water loss through evaporation and seepage, further exacerbating scarcity. The lack of reliable and efficient irrigation infrastructure hinders agricultural productivity and threatens food security.

## Micro-Hydro Systems for Irrigation Efficiency

Micro-hydro technology offers a reliable and efficient solution for irrigation, utilizing small-scale hydroelectric power to pump water. This approach reduces reliance on diesel generators, decreasing energy consumption and costs.

### Benefits:

* **Energy Efficiency:** Reduced energy consumption by an estimated 30-40% compared to traditional methods.
* **Water Savings:** Improved water efficiency through optimized pumping schedules.
