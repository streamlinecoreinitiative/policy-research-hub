# Nature-Based Solutions for Landslide Risk in Pacific SIDS: Enhancing Environmental Resilience and Health Outcomes

## Executive Summary

The Pacific Small Island Developing States (SIDS) are vulnerable to climate change impacts, including increased frequency and severity of heatwaves, droughts, and landslides. This brief highlights the importance of nature-based solutions for enhancing environmental resilience in these regions.

## Environmental Resilience in Pacific SIDS

### Climate Change Impacts

*   Rising temperatures exacerbate heat stress and heat-related illnesses.
*   Increased frequency and severity of droughts affect agriculture and water availability.
*   Landslides become more frequent due to heavy rainfall events, posing significant risks to human life and infrastructure.
