# Micro-insurance for Smallholder Drought Risk in the Mekong Basin: Safeguarding Freshwater Ecosystems

## Executive Summary

The Mekong Basin is one of the most water-rich regions globally, yet its smallholder farmers face significant drought risks due to climate change. This brief explores the integration of agroforestry and micro-hydro systems as a solution for climate-resilient irrigation in lower-income countries. By combining these two approaches, we can address severe water scarcity while promoting soil health, biodiversity, and carbon sequestration. The proposed project aims to develop a micro-insurance program for smallholder farmers in the Mekong Basin, providing financial protection against drought-related losses.

## Addressing Water Scarcity in Lower-Income Countries

### Introduction to Water Scarcity in the Mekong Basin (Modified)

The Mekong Basin is one of the most water-rich regions globally. However, climate change has exacerbated drought risks through erratic rainfall patterns and increased evaporation due to warmer temperatures. The impacts on agriculture are severe, resulting in food insecurity and economic instability for local communities.

### Current Irrigation Practices and Challenges

Existing irrigation systems in the Mekong Basin rely heavily on boreholes and canals, which often suffer from water loss through evaporation and seepage. These inefficiencies exacerbate water scarcity, further straining local resources.

## Micro-Hydro Systems for Irrigation Efficiency

Micro-hydro technology offers a viable solution for efficient irrigation, reducing reliance on diesel generators and increasing energy efficiency. By harnessing the power of small-scale hydroelectric systems, we can provide clean energy for irrigation while minimizing environmental impacts.

## Integration of Agroforestry and Micro-Hydro in Climate-Resilient Water Supply

Pilot projects have demonstrated the combined use of agroforestry and micro-hydro systems to be a cost-effective solution for climate-resilient water supply. Economic analysis has shown that these integrated systems can provide long-term benefits, including increased crop yields and improved livelihoods for smallholder farmers.

## Policy Recommendations

To support the adoption of integrated agroforestry-micro-hydro systems at scale, we recommend:

*   Subsidies for smallholder farmers to invest in agroforestry and micro-hydro infrastructure
*   Low-interest loans for community-led irrigation projects
*   Infrastructure development to enhance access to clean energy and water resources
