# Flood-Resilient Transport Corridors in the Central America Dry Corridor: Supporting Adaptation Targets in NDCs

## Executive Summary

The Central America dry corridor is highly vulnerable to climate change, experiencing frequent droughts and floods that severely impact transportation infrastructure and agricultural productivity. This brief proposes an integrated approach to enhancing flood resilience along transport corridors through agroforestry-based micro-hydro systems.

## Policy Recommendations and Future Research Directions

Policy measures can create a supportive environment for the adoption of agroforestry-micro-hydro systems:

*   Subsidies for Farmers: Incentives provided to encourage adoption of integrated agroforestry-micro-hydro systems
*   Grid Integration: Grid integration with micro-hydro systems to enhance reliability and efficiency
*   Infrastructure Development: Roads, storage facilities, and other infrastructure improvements

## Actions/Recommendations

1.  **Conduct a comprehensive analysis** of the economic benefits and costs associated with implementing agroforestry-micro-hydro systems in transportation corridors.
2.  **Develop policy frameworks** that incentivize farmers to adopt integrated agroforestry-micro-hydro systems, including subsidies and grid integration programs.
3.  **Invest in infrastructure development**, such as roads and storage facilities, to support the adoption of these systems.

## Closing Outlook

The Central America dry corridor is one of the most vulnerable regions to climate change. By integrating agroforestry with micro-hydro systems, countries can enhance flood resilience along transport corridors while supporting their NDCs under the Paris Agreement. We recommend that governments and stakeholders collaborate on implementing this solution, which has the potential to reduce economic and social impacts while promoting sustainable water management and renewable energy generation.
