# nature-based solutions for landslide risk in the Horn of Africa: improving health outcomes in heatwaves

**Bridging Water Security, Clean Energy Access, and Climate Adaptation Gaps in Sub-Saharan Africa**

## Executive Summary

This policy brief highlights the pressing need to address water security, clean energy access, and climate adaptation challenges in Sub-Saharan Africa. With only 32.4% of the region's population having access to safely managed drinking water (World Bank Open Data) [1] and just over half accessing electricity (World Bank Open Data) [2], there is an urgent need for sustainable solutions that can be implemented within existing political and institutional frameworks.

We recommend implementing cost-effective solar-powered irrigation systems, promoting renewable energy adoption, and enhancing climate-resilient agricultural practices. These interventions have the potential to improve health outcomes in heatwaves, increase crop yields, and reduce water usage.

## Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa is the most vulnerable region to climate change impacts (Source: IPCC) [3], with only 73.7% of its population having access to safely managed drinking water (World Bank Open Data) [1]. The current situation is characterized by limited clean energy access (53.3%) (World Bank Open Data) [2], hindering economic development and exacerbating poverty in rural areas.

## Analysis

Evidence shows that solar-powered irrigation systems can significantly improve agricultural productivity and water security, especially in arid regions where climate change is intensifying droughts (Source: FAO) [4]. However, renewable energy adoption remains low due to high initial costs and lack of financing mechanisms for small-scale projects. Climate adaptation measures are crucial but often underfunded, leading to increased vulnerability to extreme weather events.

## Policy Recommendations

1. **Solar-Powered Irrigation Systems**: Encourage the deployment of solar-powered irrigation pumps in arid regions where water scarcity is a major challenge. These systems can increase crop yields and reduce water usage.
2. **Renewable Energy Access Programs**: Launch government-led initiatives to provide subsidies for renewable energy projects, especially in rural areas with limited access. This could include micro-grids powered by solar panels or small wind turbines.
3. **Climate-Smart Agriculture Initiatives**: Promote practices like agroforestry and improved water management techniques that enhance soil health and reduce the impact of climate change on agricultural productivity.

## Implementation Considerations

Costs associated with these interventions are manageable, especially when compared to traditional energy solutions (Source: IRENA) [5]. Subsidies and grants could be used to offset initial investment costs. Potential barriers include resistance from existing power companies who may oppose new renewable energy projects, as well as limited awareness about the benefits of climate-smart agriculture among farmers.

## Conclusion

Urging policymakers to prioritize these interventions can help bridge the gaps in water security, clean energy access, and climate adaptation in Sub-Saharan Africa. Next steps include developing detailed implementation plans, securing funding through international partnerships, and scaling up successful pilot projects.
