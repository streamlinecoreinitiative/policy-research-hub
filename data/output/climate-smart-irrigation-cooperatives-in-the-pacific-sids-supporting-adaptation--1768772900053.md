# climate-smart irrigation cooperatives in the Pacific SIDS: supporting adaptation targets in NDCs

**Climate-Smart Irrigation Cooperatives in Pacific SIDS: Supporting Adaptation Targets in NDCs**

**Executive Summary**

The Pacific Small Island Developing States (SIDS) face significant challenges in ensuring water security and climate adaptation due to their vulnerability to climate change impacts. This brief highlights the importance of integrating agroforestry-based micro-hydro systems for climate-resilient irrigation, which can enhance resilience to water scarcity, economic instability, and climate variability.

**Climate-Resilient Irrigation Systems: A Key Component of Sustainable Development**

### **Introduction to Water Scarcity in Pacific SIDS**

Pacific SIDS face severe water scarcity due to historical droughts and current challenges such as limited access to reliable irrigation systems. Farmers and communities rely heavily on traditional methods like surface water and rain-fed systems, which are often inefficient and unsustainable.

### **Current Practices and Challenges**

Existing irrigation systems in Pacific SIDS have several limitations:

* High dependency on traditional methods (e.g., surface water, rain-fed)
* Limited efficiency and sustainability
* Economic pressures exacerbate existing challenges

Farmers' reliance on traditional methods has led to economic instability due to reduced crop yields.
