# Agroforestry-Based Micro-Hydro Systems for Climate-Resilient Irrigation in the Mesoamerican Highlands

## Executive Summary

The Mesoamerican Highlands face severe water scarcity and poverty due to climate change impacts on agriculture. Existing irrigation systems suffer from high water loss percentages, including evaporation (30%) and seepage (5%). This brief explores the potential of agroforestry-based micro-hydro systems for climate-resilient irrigation in this region.

## Introduction to Water Scarcity in the Mesoamerican Highlands

Climate change is exacerbating droughts and altering precipitation patterns in the Mesoamerican Highlands. This region relies heavily on irrigation for agriculture, but existing systems are inefficient and unsustainable. Boreholes and canals account for significant water loss due to evaporation (30%) and seepage (5%). To address this issue, it is essential to explore innovative solutions that balance economic benefits with environmental sustainability.

## Agroforestry-Based Micro-Hydro Systems: A Climate-Resilient Solution

Agroforestry practices have been shown to improve soil health, biodiversity, and carbon sequestration. By integrating agroforestry with micro-hydro systems, communities can reduce water loss and increase energy efficiency. Case studies demonstrate the effectiveness of this approach in improving water retention and reducing erosion.

### Benefits of Agroforestry-Based Micro-Hydro Systems

*   Improved soil health through increased organic matter
*   Enhanced biodiversity through a diverse range of plant species
*   Increased carbon sequestration through agroforestry practices
*   Reduced water loss through efficient irrigation systems
*   Increased energy efficiency through small-scale hydroelectric power generation
