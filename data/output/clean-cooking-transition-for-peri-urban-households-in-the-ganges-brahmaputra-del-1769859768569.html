Enhancing Water Security, Clean Energy, and Climate Adaptation in Sub-Saharan Africa: Feasible Interventions for Sustainable Development

Executive Summary

Sub-Saharan Africa is grappling with severe water scarcity, limited access to electricity, and heightened vulnerability to climate change. To address these pressing challenges, we recommend prioritizing renewable energy development, improving water infrastructure, and implementing climate-resilient projects.

Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa faces critical challenges in ensuring sustainable access to clean water, electricity, and resilience against climate impacts. The region's limited resources exacerbate these issues, making immediate intervention necessary for achieving Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs). Water scarcity affects millions across the continent, while the lack of reliable electricity undermines economic development and public health.

According to World Bank data, only 32.4% of the population has access to safely managed drinking water in Sub-Saharan Africa [1], far below global levels at 73.7% [2]. The region lags behind in renewable energy consumption (19.7%) compared to a world average of 19.7% [3]. Immediate action is required to mitigate the adverse effects of climate change and ensure equitable development opportunities for all.
