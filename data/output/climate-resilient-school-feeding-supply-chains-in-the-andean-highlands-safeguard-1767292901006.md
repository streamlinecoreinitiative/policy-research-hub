# Climate-Resilient School Feeding Supply Chains in the Andean Highlands: Safeguarding Freshwater Ecosystems

## Executive Summary

Climate change exacerbates water scarcity in the Andean highlands, posing significant challenges to agriculture and food security. This project proposal outlines a comprehensive plan for integrating agroforestry and micro-hydro systems to create climate-resilient irrigation infrastructure.

## Addressing Water Scarcity in the Andean Highlands

The Andean highlands experience erratic rainfall patterns and limited access to reliable water sources, with climate change projected to further exacerbate these conditions. Current irrigation systems are often inefficient and prone to water loss through evaporation and seepage.

## Agroforestry's Role in Climate Resilience

Agroforestry practices offer benefits for soil health, biodiversity, and carbon sequestration. Studies have shown that agroforestry systems can improve water retention and reduce erosion (e.g., [Case Study 1], [Case Study 2]). Integrating agroforestry with micro-hydro systems can create climate-resilient irrigation infrastructure.

## Micro-Hydro Systems for Irrigation Efficiency

Micro-hydro technology involves the use of small-scale hydroelectric systems to generate electricity, reducing reliance on diesel generators and increasing energy efficiency. This approach provides a reliable source of energy for irrigation pumps and other agricultural equipment.

## Integration in Climate-Resilient Water Supply

Pilot projects have demonstrated the effectiveness of integrating agroforestry and micro-hydro systems to create climate-resilient water supply infrastructure, offering benefits such as:

* Reduced water loss through evaporation and seepage
* Increased energy efficiency and reduced reliance on diesel generators
* Promoting sustainable land use practices and biodiversity conservation
