## I. Introduction to Water Scarcity in the Horn of Africa

The Horn of Africa, comprising countries such as Ethiopia, Eritrea, and Somalia, faces severe water scarcity due to historical droughts and current challenges like limited access to reliable irrigation systems and economic instability caused by reduced crop yields. Farmers and communities rely heavily on traditional methods like surface water and rain-fed systems.

## II. Current Practices and Challenges

Existing irrigation systems in the region have significant limitations, including high dependency on traditional methods, limited efficiency, and sustainability. Farmers face economic pressures that exacerbate existing challenges, leading to reduced crop yields and financial instability.
