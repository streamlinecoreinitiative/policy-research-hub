## Executive Summary

Sub-Saharan Africa faces significant challenges in accessing clean water (32.4% of the population) [Source: World Bank Open Data] and electricity (53.3%), exacerbating climate-related shocks like droughts and floods. To address this pressing issue, we recommend strengthening public-private partnerships for safe drinking water, promoting renewable energy adoption, and enhancing early warning systems. ## Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa is severely challenged by inadequate access to clean water and electricity, exacerbated by climate variability leading to droughts and floods (IPCC, 2022). Climate change further strains already limited resources, necessitating urgent action to ensure sustainable development pathways for vulnerable populations. The region's population growth and urbanization compound these challenges, underscoring the need for innovative solutions.
