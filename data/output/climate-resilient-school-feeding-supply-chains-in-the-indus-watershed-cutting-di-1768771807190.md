## Executive Summary

The Indus watershed faces severe water scarcity, affecting agriculture, food security, and human well-being. This brief focuses on climate-resilient school feeding supply chains as a critical component in addressing disaster losses for vulnerable groups.

## Addressing Severe Water Scarcity and Poverty through Integrated Solutions

### Introduction to Water Scarcity in the Indus Watershed

The Indus watershed is characterized by severe water scarcity, exacerbated by climate change, population growth, and unsustainable agricultural practices. The region's water resources are under immense pressure, with significant implications for food security and human well-being.

*   Current state of water resources: Limited availability of surface and groundwater resources [verify]
*   Key challenges:
    *   Climate change impacts (droughts, floods)
    *   Population growth (increased demand for water)
    *   Unsustainable agricultural practices (water-intensive crops)

### Current Practices and Challenges

Existing irrigation systems in the Indus watershed are inefficient, with high operational costs and limited access to electricity for micro-hydro systems. Socio-economic factors contribute to water scarcity, including:

*   Limited access to clean energy sources
*   Inadequate infrastructure for water management
*   Insufficient support for small-scale farmers
