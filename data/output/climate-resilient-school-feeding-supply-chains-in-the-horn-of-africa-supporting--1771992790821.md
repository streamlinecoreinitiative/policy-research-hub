**Climate-Resilient School Feeding Supply Chains in the Horn of Africa: Supporting Adaptation Targets in NDCs**

## Executive Summary

The Horn of Africa is one of the most vulnerable regions to climate change, with frequent droughts and floods affecting food security and livelihoods. To address these challenges, we recommend implementing climate-resilient school feeding supply chains that integrate climate adaptation measures into existing programs. This approach can improve access to nutritious food for children, enhance community resilience, and support countries' Nationally Determined Contributions (NDCs) under the Paris Agreement.

Key findings:

* 73.7% of the global population lacks access to safely managed drinking water [Source: World Bank Open Data].
* Only 53.3% of the Sub-Saharan Africa's population has access to electricity [Source: World Bank Open Data].
* Renewable energy consumption stands at 19.7% globally, with significant room for growth [Source: World Bank Open Data].

Recommendations:

1. **Enhance Water Infrastructure**: Invest in piped water supply, sanitation facilities, and hygiene promotion programs to improve access to safe drinking water.

3. **Invest in Climate Adaptation**: Allocate resources for early-warning systems, climate-resilient infrastructure like flood-resistant transport corridors, and micro-insurance programs to mitigate the impacts of extreme weather events.
