Sub-Saharan Africa faces unprecedented challenges in accessing safe drinking water, reliable electricity, and sustainable energy sources. With only 32.4% of the population having access to safely managed drinking water (World Bank, 2024) [1] and 53.3% accessing electricity (World Bank, 2023) [2], urgent action is needed. 1. **Enhance Renewable Energy Investment**: Increase investment in renewable energy projects such as solar farms and wind turbines to reduce reliance on fossil fuels and provide access to electricity.

3. **Climate Resilience Measures**: Integrate climate adaptation strategies into development plans, including early warning systems for extreme weather events and drought-resistant crop varieties.
