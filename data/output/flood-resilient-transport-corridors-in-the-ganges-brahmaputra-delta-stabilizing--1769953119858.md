**Strengthening Water Security, Clean Energy Adoption, and Climate Resilience in Sub-Saharan Africa**

## Executive Summary

Sub-Saharan Africa is facing a triple threat of water scarcity, energy poverty, and climate vulnerability. With nearly 53.3% of the population lacking access to electricity [Source: World Bank, 2023] and less than 40% having access to safely managed drinking water [Source: World Bank, 2024], the region is at risk from both economic instability and environmental stressors like droughts and floods. To address this challenge, we recommend prioritizing electrification projects in rural areas, investing in renewable energy infrastructure for decentralized power generation, and accelerating water supply improvements through community-based initiatives.

## Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa is the most vulnerable region globally to climate change impacts, with frequent droughts and floods [Source: IPCC, 2020]. The region's population faces significant challenges in accessing basic services like electricity and clean water. According to the World Bank (2024), only 32.4% of people in Sub-Saharan Africa have access to safely managed drinking water, while 53.3% lack access to electricity [Source: World Bank, 2023].

## Analysis

### Water Security

The region faces severe water scarcity, with only 32.4% having access to safely managed drinking water [Source: World Bank, 2024]. This disparity is a critical barrier to sustainable growth. In contrast, the global average for safely managed drinking water access is 73.7% [Source: World Bank Open Data].

### Clean Energy Access

Sub-Saharan Africa lags behind the world in electricity access, with less than half of its population benefiting from reliable power sources [Source: World Bank, 2023]. The region's renewable energy consumption is also lower compared to other regions, at 19.7% of global consumption [Source: World Bank Open Data].

### Climate Adaptation Needs

Increasing frequency and intensity of climate-related events necessitate robust water management strategies and resilient energy systems. According to the IPCC (2020), Sub-Saharan Africa is highly vulnerable to climate change impacts.
