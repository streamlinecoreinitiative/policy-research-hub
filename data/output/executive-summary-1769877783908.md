## Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa is facing a severe water security crisis with only 32.4% of the population having access to safely managed drinking water sources (World Bank Open Data). This lack of basic sanitation facilities contributes significantly to health issues and economic hardship. Coupled with limited electricity access, the region struggles to harness clean energy and adapt to climate change impacts, exacerbating its vulnerability. The statistics underscore the urgent need to strengthen water security, increase clean energy access, and bolster climate adaptation strategies.

## Policy Recommendations

1. **Enhance Water Security**:
	* Invest $50 million in water harvesting systems.
	* Improve sanitation facilities through government subsidies for household water filters and solar-powered pumps.
2. **Increase Clean Energy Access**:
	* Promote renewable energy projects such as solar PV installations, wind turbines, and small hydroelectric plants.
	* Implement feed-in tariffs and provide incentives for private sector investment in clean energy infrastructure.
3. **Climate Adaptation Strategies**:
	* Develop early warning systems for climate-related disasters.
	* Invest in resilient water management practices.
	* Support community-based adaptation initiatives that emphasize local knowledge and traditional practices.
