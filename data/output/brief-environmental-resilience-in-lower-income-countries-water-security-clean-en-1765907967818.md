# Brief: Environmental resilience in lower-income countries: water security, clean energy, and climate adaptation\n\n**Harnessing Renewable Energy for Water Security in Rural Africa: A Pathway to Sustainable Livelihoods**

**Executive Summary**

Rural communities in sub-Saharan Africa face significant water scarcity issues exacerbated by climate change. This brief explores how integrating renewable energy solutions can enhance water security and improve livelihoods. We examine the potential of solar-powered desalination plants, small-scale hydroelectric systems, and wind energy applications in water management through case studies from Kenya and Ethiopia. Our analysis highlights the economic benefits of renewable energy integration, including cost-effectiveness compared to traditional methods. Finally, we provide policy recommendations for governments, international organizations, and private sector stakeholders to facilitate wider adoption of renewable energy solutions.

**Renewable Energy Solutions for Water Security**

Rural African communities face significant water scarcity issues due to climate change, population growth, and inadequate infrastructure. Traditional sources of water, such as boreholes and rivers, are often unreliable or contaminated, leading to health concerns and economic losses. Renewable energy can provide a sustainable solution by powering desalination plants, small-scale hydroelectric systems, and wind energy applications in water management.

* **Solar-Powered Desalination Plants:** Solar-powered desalination plants can provide clean drinking water for rural communities without relying on fossil fuels or exacerbating climate change.
* **Small-Scale Hydroelectric Systems:** Small-scale hydroelectric systems can power irrigation systems, reducing reliance on groundwater and improving crop yields.
* **Wind Energy Applications in Water Management:** Wind energy can power pumps and treatment facilities, enhancing water availability and quality.

**Case Studies: Successful Implementations**

Kenya and Ethiopia have successfully implemented renewable energy solutions to improve access to clean drinking water. For example:

* In Kenya's Turkana County, a solar-powered desalination plant provides clean drinking water for over 10,000 people.
* In Ethiopia's Oromia Region, small-scale hydroelectric systems power irrigation systems, improving crop yields and reducing groundwater reliance.

**Economic Impacts and Cost-Benefit Analysis**

Our analysis highlights the economic benefits of integrating renewable energy solutions into existing water systems. While specific cost-benefit data is lacking (see Data Gaps below), we can estimate the following:

* **Economic Impacts:** Improved water security can lead to increased crop yields, improved health outcomes, and enhanced economic opportunities.
