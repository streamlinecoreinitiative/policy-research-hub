**Micro-insurance for Smallholder Drought Risk in the Indus Watershed: Lowering Emissions from Critical Sectors**

### Executive Summary

The Indus watershed faces severe drought risks, impacting smallholder farmers and their livelihoods. Climate change exacerbates these challenges, emphasizing the need for innovative solutions that address water security, clean energy, and climate adaptation. This brief explores the integration of agroforestry with micro-hydro systems as a resilient irrigation method in the region.

### Introduction to Water Scarcity in the Indus Watershed

The Indus watershed is a critical agricultural region facing severe water scarcity due to climate change. Rising temperatures and changing precipitation patterns threaten food security and economic stability for smallholder farmers. Climate projections indicate that droughts will become more frequent and intense, underscoring the need for resilient irrigation methods.

### Current Irrigation Practices and Challenges

Existing irrigation systems in the Indus watershed rely heavily on boreholes and canals, resulting in significant water losses through evaporation and seepage. These inefficiencies exacerbate water scarcity issues, affecting smallholder farmers' livelihoods. Moreover, diesel generators often power these systems, contributing to greenhouse gas emissions.

### Micro-Hydro Systems for Irrigation Efficiency

Micro-hydro technology offers a renewable energy solution for irrigation systems, reducing reliance on diesel generators. These systems have been shown to increase energy efficiency and decrease greenhouse gas emissions. By harnessing local water resources, micro-hydro systems can provide clean energy for irrigation, contributing to climate resilience.

### Integration of Agroforestry and Micro-Hydro in Climate-Resilient Water Supply

Pilot projects demonstrate the potential of combining agroforestry with micro-hydro systems. These integrated solutions have been shown to improve water retention and reduce erosion. Economic analyses indicate that these solutions are cost-effective, offering a viable option for scaling up.
