# Mangrove Restoration and Blue Carbon Finance in the Indus Watershed: Improving Health Outcomes in Heatwaves

## Executive Summary

Climate change exacerbates water scarcity, heatwaves, and related health issues in lower-income countries. This brief explores the intersection of environmental resilience, water security, clean energy, and climate adaptation through mangrove restoration and blue carbon finance in the Indus Watershed.

## Environmental Resilience in Lower-Income Countries: Challenges and Opportunities

Lower-income countries face significant environmental challenges, including water scarcity, deforestation, and climate change impacts. The Indus Watershed is particularly vulnerable due to its unique geography and climate conditions.

### Key Challenges:

*   Water scarcity: Limited access to clean water for irrigation, drinking, and sanitation
*   Deforestation: Loss of mangrove forests due to coastal development, overfishing, and climate change
*   Climate change: Rising temperatures, changing precipitation patterns, and increased frequency of heatwaves

### Opportunities for Mangrove Restoration and Blue Carbon Finance:

*   **Mangrove restoration**: Enhance ecosystem services, including carbon sequestration, shoreline protection, and biodiversity conservation
*   **Blue carbon finance**: Leverage financial mechanisms to support mangrove restoration, promoting sustainable development and climate resilience
