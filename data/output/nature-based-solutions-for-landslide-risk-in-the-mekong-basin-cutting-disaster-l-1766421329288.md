# nature-based solutions for landslide risk in the Mekong basin: cutting disaster losses for vulnerable groups

**Nature-Based Solutions for Landslide Risk in the Mekong Basin: Cutting Disaster Losses for Vulnerable Groups**

Executive Summary
-----------------

The Mekong Basin is highly vulnerable to natural disasters, particularly landslides, which pose a significant threat to millions of people's lives and livelihoods. Climate change exacerbates this risk by altering precipitation patterns, increasing extreme weather events, and degrading soil quality.

**Introduction**

The Mekong Basin spans across six countries: Cambodia, China, Laos, Myanmar, Thailand, and Vietnam. The region is characterized by complex terrain, high population density, and limited economic resources, making it particularly vulnerable to natural disasters. Landslides are a major concern, causing significant loss of life, property damage, and displacement of communities.

**Current Challenges and Opportunities**

*   Climate change impacts:
    *   Erratic rainfall patterns
    *   Increased frequency and severity of extreme weather events
    *   Soil degradation and landslides
*   Traditional irrigation methods:

**Nature-Based Solutions for Landslide Risk**

Agroforestry-based micro-hydro systems offer a promising approach to addressing landslide risk in the Mekong Basin. These integrated solutions combine:

*   Agroforestry practices:
    *   Improved soil health
    *   Increased biodiversity ([verify]%)
    *   Carbon sequestration (tons/ha/year)
*   Micro-hydro systems:

    *   Cost savings compared to traditional irrigation methods

**Implementation and Policy Recommendations**

To implement NBS for landslide risk reduction, we recommend:

1.  **Capacity building:** Provide training and technical assistance to farmers, communities, and local governments on integrated agroforestry-micro-hydro systems.
2.  **Policy support:** Encourage policy makers to adopt incentives for farmers (subsidies, low-interest loans) and grid integration with micro-hydro systems.
3.  **Infrastructure development:** Invest in roads, storage facilities, and other infrastructure to support the implementation of NBS.

**Closing Outlook**

The proposed integrated approach to addressing landslide risk through nature-based solutions offers a promising pathway for reducing disaster losses in vulnerable communities. By combining agroforestry practices with micro-hydro systems, we can create climate-resilient irrigation systems that not only reduce water scarcity but also promote sustainable development and poverty reduction.

**Actions/Recommendations:**

1.  **Conduct further research:** Investigate the effectiveness of NBS in reducing landslide risk and improving livelihoods.
2.  **Develop pilot projects:** Establish pilot projects to demonstrate the feasibility and benefits of integrated agroforestry-micro-hydro systems.
3.  **Engage stakeholders:** Collaborate with local governments, farmers, and communities to ensure that NBS are tailored to their needs and priorities.
