# climate-smart irrigation cooperatives in the Sudano-Sahelian belt: cutting disaster losses for vulnerable groups

**Climate-Smart Irrigation Cooperatives in the Sudano-Sahelian Belt: Cutting Disaster Losses for Vulnerable Groups**

## Executive Summary

This policy brief recommends the establishment of climate-smart irrigation cooperatives in the Sudano-Sahelian belt to improve water security and reduce disaster losses for vulnerable communities. The region faces significant challenges in ensuring reliable access to clean drinking water, with only 32.4% of the population having access to safely managed drinking water (World Bank, 2024). Climate change is further exacerbating these challenges through frequent droughts and floods. To address this issue, we recommend expanding solar-powered well programs, implementing community-based rainwater harvesting initiatives, and promoting off-grid renewable energy sources.

## Context & Challenge

The Sudano-Sahelian belt spans across several countries in Sub-Saharan Africa, including Niger, Mali, Burkina Faso, and Chad. The region is characterized by limited water resources, making it vulnerable to climate variability. Only 32.4% of the population has access to safely managed drinking water (World Bank, 2024), highlighting a critical need for improved water security. Furthermore, just under half of Sub-Saharan African countries have reliable electricity access (53.3%, World Bank, 2023). These statistics underscore the urgent requirement for innovative policies and technologies that can enhance both water security and energy access.

## Analysis

The region's limited water resources are further strained by climate variability, leading to frequent droughts and floods. This exacerbates existing challenges in ensuring sustainable water supply. For instance, Sub-Saharan Africa is home to 2.2 billion people lacking safe water sources (UN-Water/WHO), significantly impacting public health outcomes. Furthermore, the region's reliance on fossil fuels for energy production contributes to rising greenhouse gas emissions, further intensifying climate impacts.

The adoption of renewable energy has been hindered by high initial investment costs and inadequate policy frameworks in many Sub-Saharan African countries. To address the pressing challenges of water security and climate resilience in the Sudano-Sahelian belt, we recommend the following:

*   **Expand Solar-Powered Well Programs**: Implementing solar-powered well projects can significantly enhance access to safe drinking water. ## Implementation Considerations

To ensure the success of these recommendations, several practical steps must be taken:

*   **Funding Mechanisms**: Establishing public-private partnerships and leveraging international development funds can help reduce initial costs. For instance, a $5 million grant from the Global Environment Facility could fund solar well projects in 10 rural communities (Global Environment Facility, 2023).
*   **Policy Frameworks**: Developing supportive policies and regulations is crucial for scaling these initiatives. This includes tax incentives for renewable energy investments and subsidies for water infrastructure improvements.
*   **Community Engagement**: Involving local communities in planning and decision-making processes can enhance ownership and sustainability of projects. Training programs on proper maintenance and operation protocols are essential to ensure long-term success (World Bank, 2024).

## Conclusion

Enhancing environmental resilience through improved water security and clean energy access is crucial for the sustainable development of Sub-Saharan Africa. By adopting practical and scalable solutions such as solar-powered well programs and community-based rainwater harvesting initiatives, governments can significantly improve living conditions while contributing to broader climate change mitigation efforts. It is imperative that these recommendations are implemented at both national and regional levels.

**Call to Action**: Leaders from government officials, NGO leaders, and development practitioners must prioritize the adoption of these actionable steps to ensure a more resilient future for Sub-Saharan Africa. Collaboration between stakeholders will be essential in turning these policy briefs into tangible improvements in daily lives across the region.
