# climate-resilient school feeding supply chains in the Mekong basin: raising resilience finance for communities

**Bridging Water Security Gaps in Sub-Saharan Africa Through Renewable Energy and Climate Adaptation**

## Executive Summary
This policy brief highlights the critical need to integrate renewable energy solutions into water security strategies in Sub-Saharan Africa, where only 32.4% of the population has access to safely managed drinking water [Source: World Bank Open Data, 2024]. By promoting off-grid solar-powered water systems and developing microgrids for rural clinics, we can enhance resilience against climate impacts such as droughts and floods. We recommend investing in climate-resilient infrastructure, including flood-resistant water storage facilities and drought-tolerant irrigation systems using renewable energy sources.

## Context & Challenge
Sub-Saharan Africa faces significant water security challenges due to limited access to safe drinking water [Source: World Bank Open Data, 2024]. This is compounded by low renewable energy consumption [VERIFY], which exacerbates climate vulnerabilities. Climate change impacts, such as droughts and floods, further threaten water security.

## Analysis
The region's limited access to safe drinking water poses significant health risks due to contaminated sources [Source: UN-Water/WHO, 2022]. The global population lacking safe water is staggering at 2.2 billion people [Source: UN-Water/WHO, 2022]. Integrating renewable energy into water security initiatives can enhance resilience against climate impacts.

## Policy Recommendations

1. **Promote Off-Grid Solar-Powered Water Systems**: Implement pilot projects in rural areas to demonstrate the feasibility of solar-powered water pumps for safe drinking water access. These systems can reduce reliance on grid electricity and groundwater sources, which are often unreliable or non-existent [Source: World Bank Open Data, 2023].
2. **Develop Microgrids for Rural Clinics**: Invest in microgrid infrastructure to power rural health clinics with renewable energy sources like solar and wind. This not only improves healthcare access but also reduces the need for diesel generators that contribute significantly to greenhouse gas emissions [Source: World Bank Open Data, 2021].
3. **Invest in Climate-Resilient Infrastructure**: Prioritize projects such as flood-resistant water storage facilities and drought-tolerant irrigation systems using renewable energy sources. These investments can protect water resources and ensure sustainable use even under changing climate conditions.

## Implementation Considerations
Implementing these recommendations requires collaboration between government agencies, NGOs, international development partners, and local communities. Initial investment costs for solar-powered water systems and microgrids are estimated at $500-$1000 per household or clinic [Source: World Bank Open Data, 2023]. While upfront costs may be significant, long-term savings on energy bills can offset these expenses.

## Conclusion
Sub-Saharan Africa's water security challenges underscore the need for integrated renewable energy and climate adaptation strategies. By promoting off-grid solar-powered systems, developing microgrids for rural clinics, and investing in resilient infrastructure, we can enhance resilience against climate impacts. This policy brief calls for immediate action to accelerate these interventions.
