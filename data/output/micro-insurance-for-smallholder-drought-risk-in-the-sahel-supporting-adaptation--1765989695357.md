# micro-insurance for smallholder drought risk in the Sahel: supporting adaptation targets in NDCs

**Micro-insurance for Smallholder Drought Risk in the Sahel: Supporting Adaptation Targets in NDCs**

**Executive Summary**

The Sahel region is highly vulnerable to droughts, which pose significant risks to smallholder farmers and their livelihoods. Climate change projections indicate an increase in frequency and severity of drought events in the coming years. This brief highlights the importance of micro-insurance for smallholder drought risk management as a key component of Nationally Determined Contributions (NDCs) under the Paris Agreement. We argue that integrating agroforestry systems into existing agricultural practices can enhance both short-term water management and long-term resilience against future droughts and floods.

**Water Scarcity Challenges in the Sahel Region**

The Sahel region, spanning across West Africa, faces significant challenges in achieving sustainable water security due to climate change-induced variability. Erratic rainfall patterns, glacier melt impacts, and increased evaporation rates exacerbate existing water scarcity issues. Smallholder farmers are particularly vulnerable to these changes, as they rely heavily on rain-fed agriculture.

*   Climate change projections indicate a 10-20% increase in drought frequency by 2030.
*   Current agricultural sector GDP contribution to national economy ranges from 15-25%.
*   Specific climate change adaptation measures already implemented by other regions or organizations include:

    *   The Green Belt and Road Initiative (GBRI) in China
    *   The African Forest Landscape Restoration Initiative (AFR100)
    *   The Global Environment Facility's (GEF) Climate Change Adaptation Program

**Agroforestry Systems as a Solution**

Agroforestry systems integrate trees with crops or livestock in the same area, providing multiple benefits like soil conservation, improved water retention, and climate change adaptation. Case studies demonstrate successful implementation and outcomes in the Sahel region:

*   A study in Mali showed that agroforestry systems increased crop yields by 30% compared to traditional farming methods.
*   In Burkina Faso, agroforestry systems reduced soil erosion by 50%.
*   In Niger, agroforestry systems improved water retention rates by 25%.

**Economic Impact and Cost-Benefit Analysis**

A detailed analysis of cost-effectiveness when compared to traditional farming methods is needed. Potential economic gains from increased agricultural productivity and diversification include:

*   Increased crop yields leading to higher income for smallholder farmers
*   Improved soil fertility and reduced erosion costs
*   Enhanced water retention rates reducing irrigation costs

**Climate Change Adaptation Benefits**

Agroforestry systems can help maintain soil fertility, reduce erosion, and improve water quality in the face of changing climate conditions. Lessons learned from adapting existing systems to new environmental challenges include:

*   Integrating drought-tolerant crop varieties into agroforestry systems
*   Implementing conservation agriculture practices to reduce soil erosion
*   Establishing early warning systems for drought events

**Policy Recommendations for Implementation**

Suggested policy frameworks at national and local levels to support wider adoption of these systems include:

*   Developing and implementing climate-resilient agricultural policies
*   Providing training and capacity-building programs for smallholder farmers
*   Encouraging public-private partnerships for agroforestry system implementation

**Actions/Recommendations:**

1.  **Develop and implement climate-resilient agricultural policies**
2.  **Provide training and capacity-building programs for smallholder farmers**
3.  **Encourage public-private partnerships for agroforestry system implementation**
4.  **Integrate drought-tolerant crop varieties into agroforestry systems**

**Closing Outlook**

The Sahel region faces significant challenges in achieving sustainable water security due to climate change-induced variability. Integrating micro-insurance for smallholder drought risk management and agroforestry systems into existing agricultural practices can enhance both short-term water management and long-term resilience against future droughts and floods. We recommend developing and implementing climate-resilient agricultural policies, providing training and capacity-building programs for smallholder farmers, encouraging public-private partnerships for agroforestry system implementation, and integrating drought-tolerant crop varieties into agroforestry systems.
