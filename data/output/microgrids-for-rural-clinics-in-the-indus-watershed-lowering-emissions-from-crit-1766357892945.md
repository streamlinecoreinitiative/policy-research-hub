### Executive Summary

This brief outlines a comprehensive approach to addressing environmental resilience challenges in lower-income countries, focusing on water security, clean energy, and climate adaptation. The proposal centers on microgrids for rural clinics in the Indus watershed, aiming to reduce emissions from critical sectors such as healthcare.

#### 2. Current Irrigation Practices and Their Limitations

Traditional irrigation methods are often energy-intensive and costly. Borehole pumps consume between 10-20 kWh/day, while canal systems lose significant amounts of water due to evaporation (30%) and seepage (5%). These inefficiencies not only waste resources but also contribute to greenhouse gas emissions.

#### 3. Agroforestry’s Role in Climate Resilience

Agroforestry practices have been shown to improve soil health, increase biodiversity, and enhance carbon sequestration. Successful implementation of agroforestry systems has demonstrated its potential for climate resilience in regions such as West Africa and East Africa.

Key benefits of agroforestry include:

• Soil organic matter increases by 2-8% [verify].
• Biodiversity increases by 15-30 native species.
• Carbon sequestration ranges from 3-7 tons/ha/year [verify].

#### 4. Micro-Hydro Systems for Irrigation Efficiency

Micro-hydro systems provide a sustainable and renewable energy source for irrigation, reducing reliance on diesel generators. These systems offer significant benefits:

• Power generation ranges from 10-50 kW per system.
• Energy efficiency is at 70-80%.
• Cost savings compared to traditional irrigation methods.

#### 5. Integration of Agroforestry and Micro-Hydro in Climate-Resilient Water Supply

By integrating agroforestry with micro-hydro systems, communities can benefit from cost-effective solutions that demonstrate feasibility and potential for scaling up. Total project costs per hectare range from $10,000-$25,000 [verify], with a return on investment (ROI) of 3-5 years.

### Actions/Recommendations

1. **Policy Support**: Governments should provide incentives to farmers, such as subsidies or low-interest loans, to adopt sustainable irrigation solutions.
2. **Grid Integration**: Utilities should integrate micro-hydro systems into the grid, ensuring a stable and reliable energy supply for irrigation and other critical sectors.
3. **Infrastructure Development**: Governments should invest in infrastructure development, including roads and storage facilities, to support the adoption of integrated agroforestry-micro-hydro projects.

The proposed initiative offers a comprehensive approach to addressing environmental resilience challenges in lower-income countries.
