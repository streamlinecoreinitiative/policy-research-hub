    Context & Challenge

    Sub-Saharan Africa is one of the most vulnerable regions to climate change, suffering from limited access to freshwater resources. Only 32.4% of its population has safe drinking water, compared to 73.7% globally (World Bank Open Data, 2024). The region's current energy landscape relies heavily on fossil fuels, contributing significantly to carbon emissions and environmental degradation.

    Climate change exacerbates these challenges by increasing variability in rainfall and temperatures. In the Horn of Africa, droughts and famines have become more frequent, threatening food security and human well-being (UN-Water/WHO, 2022).

    Analysis

    Water Security

    Sub-Saharan Africa faces severe health risks from contaminated sources like surface water and groundwater. With only 32.4% access to safely managed drinking water, the region struggles to meet basic human needs (World Bank Open Data, 2024). Investing in rainwater harvesting systems and small-scale desalination plants can help address this issue.

    Clean Energy

    Despite a global renewable energy consumption rate of 19.7%, Sub-Saharan Africa lags behind with just 53.3% electricity access (World Bank Open Data, 2023). This disparity impacts economic productivity and exacerbates poverty. Implementing feed-in tariffs and subsidies for solar, wind, and hydro projects can promote renewable energy integration.

    Climate Adaptation

    Increased frequency of droughts, floods, and extreme weather events threatens agricultural yields and water resources. Enhancing climate resilience infrastructure through flood defenses and levees in high-risk areas is crucial to mitigate the impact of these events.
