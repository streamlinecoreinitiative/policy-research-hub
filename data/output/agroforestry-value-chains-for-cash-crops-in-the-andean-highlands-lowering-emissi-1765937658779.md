# agroforestry value chains for cash crops in the Andean highlands: lowering emissions from critical sectors

**Agroforestry Value Chains for Cash Crops in the Andean Highlands: Lowering Emissions from Critical Sectors**

**Executive Summary:**

Rural communities in lower-income countries face significant challenges in accessing clean water and reliable energy due to climate variability. This brief explores the potential of biochar-based agroforestry systems integrated with renewable energy solutions to improve both water security and energy access. In the context of Senegal, a country plagued by rural water insecurity and limited energy access, this approach offers a promising solution. By combining soil-enhancing biochar with agroforestry practices and renewable energy sources like solar and wind power, communities can increase water retention, reduce emissions, and promote sustainable development.

**Challenges and Solutions**

Potential challenges to implementation include:

* Initial investment costs: Higher upfront expenses may deter communities from adopting new technologies.
* Community buy-in: Ensuring community acceptance and participation in planning and decision-making is crucial for long-term success.
* Technical expertise: Local capacity-building and training are necessary to ensure effective installation, maintenance, and operation of renewable energy systems.

To address these challenges, policy makers can consider:

* Providing incentives for initial investment costs
* Establishing community-led planning and decision-making processes
* Offering technical assistance and capacity-building programs

**Policy Recommendations**

To support the adoption of biochar-based agroforestry systems and renewable energy in rural Senegal, we recommend:

1. **Incentivize Initial Investment Costs**: Offer subsidies or grants to cover initial investment costs for communities adopting integrated solutions.
2. **Establish Community-Led Planning Processes**: Foster community-led planning and decision-making to ensure that projects meet local needs and priorities.
3. **Provide Technical Assistance**: Offer training and capacity-building programs to enhance local technical expertise in renewable energy installation, maintenance, and operation.
