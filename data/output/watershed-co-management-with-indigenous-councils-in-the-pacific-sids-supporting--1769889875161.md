## Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa is home to over 1.3 billion people, with millions lacking access to safe drinking water and electricity. According to World Bank data, only 32.4% of the population has access to safely managed drinking water, compared to 73.7% globally (World Bank Open Data). Similarly, electrification rates stand at 53.3%, significantly lower than the global average of 91.6% (World Bank Open Data).

Climate change exacerbates these existing vulnerabilities, with increased frequency and severity of droughts, floods, and other extreme weather events. In fact, climate-related disasters have already displaced millions in Sub-Saharan Africa, highlighting the urgent need for climate resilience strategies.
