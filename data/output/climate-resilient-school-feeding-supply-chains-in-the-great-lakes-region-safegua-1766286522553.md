# Climate-Resilient School Feeding Supply Chains in the Great Lakes Region: Safeguarding Freshwater Ecosystems

## Executive Summary

The Great Lakes region is home to over 400 million people, relying heavily on freshwater resources for agriculture, industry, and domestic use. However, climate change poses significant threats to water security, affecting agricultural productivity, food security, and economic stability. This brief highlights the importance of integrating climate-resilient practices into school feeding supply chains in the region.

## Introduction to Water Scarcity in the Great Lakes Region

Climate change is exacerbating water scarcity in the Great Lakes region through:

* Erratic rainfall patterns
* Increased evaporation due to rising temperatures
* Changes in precipitation distribution, leading to more frequent droughts and floods

These impacts threaten food security, economic stability, and human well-being. Resilient irrigation methods are essential for ensuring stable agricultural productivity.
