**Water Security, Clean Energy, and Climate Adaptation: Strategic Priorities for Lower-Income Countries**

## Executive Summary

Lower-income countries face significant challenges in ensuring water security, accessing clean energy, and building climate resilience. To address this gap, we recommend investing in infrastructure like wells and rainwater harvesting systems to boost water security; prioritizing renewable energy sources like solar power for clean energy solutions; and enhancing climate adaptation through early warning systems and community-based watershed management.

Key Findings:

* Only 32.4% of the population in Sub-Saharan Africa has access to safely managed drinking water [1].
* The global population lacking safe water is approximately 2.2 billion people [2].
* Transitioning to clean energy sources can significantly reduce reliance on fossil fuels and enhance resilience against climate shocks.

## Context & Challenge

Lower-income countries, particularly Sub-Saharan Africa, face significant challenges in providing safe drinking water and electricity. According to the World Bank, only 32.4% of the population has access to safely managed drinking water [1], while 53.3% have access to electricity [3]. These figures are stark compared to global averages, highlighting the critical need for urgent interventions.

## Analysis

### Water Security

The lack of safe drinking water disproportionately affects Sub-Saharan Africa, where nearly three-quarters of the population lacks adequate sanitation facilities (62%) [4]. Improving this situation requires investment in infrastructure such as wells and rainwater harvesting systems. For example, solar-powered wells can provide clean water for communities without access to traditional water sources.

### Clean Energy

Access to renewable energy remains limited. In 2023, only 53.3% of Sub-Saharan African households have access to electricity [3]. Transitioning to clean sources like solar power could significantly reduce reliance on fossil fuels while enhancing resilience against climate shocks.

### Climate Adaptation

Climate change impacts are becoming more pronounced. The World Bank's latest projections show that global temperatures have risen by 1.1°C since pre-industrial times, intensifying droughts and heatwaves in vulnerable regions [5]. Implementing early warning systems and integrating community-based watershed management can help communities better prepare for these challenges.

## Implementation Considerations

Initial investments in water security projects can range from $100-$500 per household [6], while renewable energy systems costing slightly more. Community-led initiatives are often the most effective. Collaboration between government agencies, NGOs, and private sector entities is crucial to overcome resistance from traditional power structures and limited financial resources.

## Conclusion

Lower-income countries must prioritize water security, clean energy access, and climate adaptation to ensure sustainable development. By adopting evidence-based strategies like investing in renewable energy and community-led watershed management, we can create resilient societies better equipped to handle future challenges.
