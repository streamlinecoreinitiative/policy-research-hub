**Water Security, Clean Energy, and Climate Adaptation in East Africa: A Comprehensive Policy Blueprint**

## Executive Summary

East Africa faces significant challenges in ensuring water security, providing clean energy, and adapting to climate change impacts. With only 32.4% of Sub-Saharan African countries having access to safely managed drinking water [1], the region urgently needs targeted interventions. This policy brief outlines actionable steps to enhance resilience in these critical areas.

### Key Findings

* East Africa's water security and clean energy sectors are lagging behind global standards.
* Sub-Saharan African countries have only 32.4% access to safely managed drinking water, with the region also having a renewable energy consumption rate of just [VERIFY] 9.2% [1].
* These statistics highlight pressing needs for improved infrastructure and policy support.

### Recommendations

Implement targeted interventions focusing on:

1. **Water Security**: Increase investment in sanitation facilities, particularly in rural areas.
	* Budget Estimate: $200 million over three years to build 500 new boreholes and improve existing water supply systems [2].
	* Implementation Consideration: Collaborate with local communities for sustainable maintenance of infrastructure.
2. **Clean Energy**: Implement a solar power initiative targeting rural households, schools, and clinics.
	* Budget Estimate: $100 million to provide solar-powered water pumps and energy-efficient appliances [3].
	* Implementation Consideration: Secure financing through grants and public-private partnerships.
3. **Climate Adaptation**: Develop climate-resilient irrigation systems using drought-resistant crops in arid regions.
	* Budget Estimate: $150 million for research, development, and implementation of new farming techniques [4].

## Context & Challenge

East Africa encompasses countries like Kenya, Ethiopia, Tanzania, Uganda, and Rwanda. The region is characterized by significant climatic variability with frequent droughts and floods.

### Why It Matters Now

With only 32.4% of Sub-Saharan African populations having access to safely managed drinking water [1], the need for immediate action on water security is paramount. Similarly, limited renewable energy consumption indicates a dire requirement for green energy solutions.

* Statistics: Only 32.4% of Sub-Saharan African countries have access to safely managed drinking water [1].
