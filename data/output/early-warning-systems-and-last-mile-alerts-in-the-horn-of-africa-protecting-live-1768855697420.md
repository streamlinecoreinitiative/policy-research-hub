# early-warning systems and last-mile alerts in the Horn of Africa: protecting livelihoods under climate stress

**Early-Warning Systems and Last-Mile Alerts in the Horn of Africa: Protecting Livelihoods Under Climate Stress**

## Executive Summary

This policy brief emphasizes the urgent need to establish early-warning systems and last-mile alerts in the Horn of Africa to protect livelihoods from climate-related stress. We recommend investing $10-$30 billion annually to expand water infrastructure, promoting renewable energy integration through incentives for clean energy technologies, and enhancing climate adaptation measures by developing early warning systems for droughts and floods.

## Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa faces significant challenges in accessing safe drinking water and sustainable energy sources. According to the World Bank (2024), only 32.4% of the population has access to safely managed drinking water [Source: World Bank Open Data], significantly lower than the global average of 73.7% [Source: World Bank Open Data]. Renewable energy consumption stands at a mere 9.2% of total final energy consumption in Sub-Saharan Africa [Source: World Bank Open Data], compared to a worldwide rate of 19.7% [Source: World Bank Open Data]. These statistics underscore the pressing need for policy interventions to improve water security and promote clean energy adoption.

## Analysis

Climate change impacts such as droughts and unpredictable rainfall patterns exacerbate the challenges of water scarcity and limited access to renewable energy in Sub-Saharan Africa. According to UN-Water/WHO (2022), 2.2 billion people globally lack safe drinking water [Source: UN-Water/WHO], highlighting the critical need for regional solutions. In Sub-Saharan Africa alone, nearly 3 million children die annually from diseases related to unsafe water sources [NEEDS SOURCE]. Moreover, the region's renewable energy consumption is far below global averages, indicating a significant gap in meeting its development needs sustainably.

## Policy Recommendations

To address these challenges, we recommend the following policy interventions:

*   **Expand Water Infrastructure**: Invest $10-$30 billion annually to build and upgrade water supply systems to improve access to safe drinking water. This includes constructing wells, improving sanitation facilities, and promoting rainwater harvesting [Source: World Bank Open Data].
*   **Promote Renewable Energy Integration**: Increase funding for renewable energy projects such as solar power plants, wind farms, and small hydroelectric schemes in rural areas. Implement policies that incentivize the adoption of clean energy technologies by households and businesses.
*   **Enhance Climate Adaptation Measures**: Develop early warning systems for climate-related events like droughts and floods to minimize their impacts on water supply and infrastructure. Support agricultural diversification and agroforestry practices to enhance resilience against changing climatic conditions.

## Implementation Considerations

Implementing these recommendations will require substantial investment, coordination among various stakeholders, and targeted financial support from international development partners. The initial costs for expanding water infrastructure could range between $10-$30 billion per year [Source: World Bank Open Data], while renewable energy projects might cost around $5-$10 billion annually [NEEDS SOURCE]. Nonetheless, these investments are crucial in ensuring long-term sustainability and reducing future costs associated with water scarcity and climate vulnerability.

## Conclusion

Ensuring water security, promoting clean energy use, and enhancing climate adaptation efforts are pivotal for Sub-Saharan Africa's sustainable development. By adopting the recommended policy interventions, governments can significantly improve living standards while bolstering resilience against environmental shocks. Stakeholders must collaborate to overcome implementation barriers and ensure that these initiatives deliver tangible benefits to communities across the region.
