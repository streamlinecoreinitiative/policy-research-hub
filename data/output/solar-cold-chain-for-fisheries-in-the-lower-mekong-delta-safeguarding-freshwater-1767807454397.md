# Solar Cold-Chain for Fisheries in the Lower Mekong Delta: Safeguarding Freshwater Ecosystems

## Executive Summary

The Lower Mekong delta is one of the most productive freshwater fisheries globally, providing a vital source of income and nutrition for millions of people. However, the region faces significant environmental challenges, including water scarcity, climate change, and pollution.

## Introduction

The Lower Mekong delta is a critical ecosystem supporting diverse aquatic life, agriculture, and human settlements. The region's unique geography and climate make it highly vulnerable to climate change impacts, including rising temperatures, changing precipitation patterns, and increased frequency of extreme weather events.

## Current Challenges

*   Water scarcity: The Lower Mekong delta faces significant water scarcity due to over-extraction for agriculture, industry, and domestic use.
*   Climate change: Rising temperatures and changing precipitation patterns impact fish growth, reproduction, and migration patterns.
*   Pollution: Agricultural runoff, industrial waste, and human activities contribute to pollution, affecting aquatic life and human health.

## Solar Cold-Chain Systems

Solar-powered cold-chain systems can help address these challenges by:

1.  Reducing post-harvest losses: By maintaining a consistent refrigerated temperature, solar cold-chains can reduce spoilage and improve fish quality.
2.  Enhancing fisheries resilience: By reducing the need for ice and other energy-intensive cooling methods, solar cold-chains can help fisheries adapt to changing climate conditions.
