## Executive Summary

Sub-Saharan Africa faces significant challenges in water security, clean energy access, and climate resilience. To address these issues simultaneously, we recommend a multi-faceted approach that includes expanding water infrastructure, increasing renewable energy access, and implementing climate adaptation measures. By doing so, we can create a more resilient future for the region's communities, economies, and ecosystems.

## Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa is home to one-third of the world's population without safe drinking water. Despite global progress towards universal electricity access (91.6% worldwide), only 53.3% of people in sub-Saharan Africa have access to reliable power (Source: World Bank Open Data). Climate change exacerbates existing challenges, leading to more frequent droughts and floods.

### Clean Energy Access:

### Climate Adaptation:

Sub-Saharan Africa experiences more frequent extreme weather events such as droughts and floods due to climate change impacts. Without adequate water security and clean energy infrastructure, these communities are increasingly vulnerable, exacerbating existing vulnerabilities.
