# Nature-Based Solutions for Landslide Risk in the Lower Mekong Delta: Raising Resilience Finance for Communities

## Executive Summary

The Lower Mekong delta is highly vulnerable to climate change, facing severe water scarcity and increased landslide risk due to rising temperatures and changing precipitation patterns. This brief presents a comprehensive approach to addressing these challenges through nature-based solutions, specifically agroforestry-based micro-hydro systems for climate-resilient irrigation.

## Introduction

The Lower Mekong delta is home to over 20 million people, with agriculture being the primary source of livelihood for many communities. Climate change has severely impacted the region's agricultural sector, causing water scarcity and increased landslide risk due to altered precipitation patterns.

## Current Irrigation Practices and Challenges

Traditional irrigation methods in the Lower Mekong delta are often energy-intensive and costly, relying on borehole pumps that consume a significant amount of energy. Canal systems experience losses due to evaporation and seepage, straining local resources and contributing to environmental degradation.
