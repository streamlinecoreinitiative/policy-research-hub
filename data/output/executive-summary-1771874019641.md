# Executive Summary

**Enhancing Water Security, Clean Energy, and Climate Resilience in Sub-Saharan Africa: A Strategic Path Forward**

## Executive Summary

To enhance water security, clean energy, and climate resilience in Sub-Saharan Africa, we recommend developing a comprehensive strategy focusing on expanding clean water access, promoting microgrids for reliable energy supply, and investing in climate adaptation projects. This approach is critical given the region's significant challenges in accessing safe drinking water (only 32.4% of the population) [1] and electricity (53.3%) [2]. Furthermore, Sub-Saharan Africa is home to a large number of people lacking safe water [NEEDS SOURCE].

## Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa faces severe water scarcity issues with only 32.4% of the population having access to safely managed drinking water [3]. Climate change exacerbates these challenges by increasing frequency and intensity of droughts, affecting both water availability and energy production [4]. The region's electricity coverage is improving globally (91.6%), but Sub-Saharan Africa remains significantly lagging at 53.3%, with rural areas facing higher barriers [5].

## Analysis

Despite progress in some countries, Sub-Saharan Africa lags behind global standards on access to safely managed drinking water. Only 26% of the region's population has access to this essential service compared to 73.7% worldwide [6]. Renewable energy consumption stood at 19.7% globally in 2020 [7], underscoring the region's potential for growth in this sector.

## Policy Recommendations

1. **Expand Clean Water Access**: Implement community-based water supply systems (CSWS) to increase access to safe drinking water, focusing on rural and peri-urban areas.
2. **Promote Microgrids for Reliable Energy Supply**:
	* Invest in microgrid infrastructure such as solar-powered mini-grids to provide reliable electricity to remote communities.
	* Encourage private sector investment through tax incentives and favorable regulatory frameworks.
3. **Invest in Climate Adaptation Projects**:
	* Integrate climate-resilient agriculture practices like drought-resistant crop varieties and improved irrigation systems into agricultural policies.
	* Develop early warning systems for water scarcity and energy disruptions, leveraging existing technology like satellite imagery and IoT sensors.

## Implementation Considerations

- **Costs**: Microgrid projects range from $10,000 to $50,000 per unit [NEEDS SOURCE]. Scaling up initiatives requires substantial investment but can be funded through grants and low-interest loans.
- **Barriers**: Limited financial resources, technical expertise, and regulatory frameworks are major barriers to implementing these interventions.

## Conclusion

To enhance water security, clean energy, and climate resilience in Sub-Saharan Africa, governments must prioritize targeted policies that address both immediate needs and long-term sustainability. By doing so, they can improve the livelihoods of millions while laying a foundation for future economic growth and stability.
