# Agroforestry-Based Micro-Hydro Systems for Climate-Resilient Irrigation in the Mesoamerican Highlands

## Executive Summary

The Mesoamerican Highlands face severe water scarcity and poverty, exacerbated by climate change impacts on agriculture. This brief explores agroforestry-based micro-hydro systems as a solution to ensure economic benefits and sustainability.

## Introduction to Water Scarcity in the Mesoamerican Highlands

Climate change has severe implications for agriculture in the Mesoamerican Highlands, characterized by:

*   **Water scarcity**: Reduced rainfall and increased evaporation due to rising temperatures.
*   **Food insecurity**: Decreased crop yields and reduced agricultural productivity.
*   **Economic instability**: Losses in agricultural production and decreased economic stability.

Resilient irrigation methods are crucial for food security and economic stability. However, current practices face significant challenges:

## Policy Recommendations and Future Research Directions

To support the adoption of agroforestry-based micro-hydro systems at scale, we recommend:

1.  **Policy support**: Governments should provide incentives for farmers to adopt integrated agroforestry-micro-hydro systems.

3.  **Research and development**: Further research is needed to improve the efficiency and cost-effectiveness of micro-hydro technology.
