**Executive Summary**

The Mekong basin is a critical agricultural region facing severe water scarcity due to climate change, threatening food security and economic stability for millions of people. This brief explores the potential of integrating agroforestry with micro-hydro systems as a solution to address water scarcity in the Mekong Basin.

**Climate Change Impacts on Agriculture in the Mekong Basin**

The Mekong basin is one of the most vulnerable regions to climate change, with projected temperature increases and altered precipitation patterns. Rising temperatures are expected to lead to increased evaporation rates, further exacerbating water scarcity. Changes in precipitation patterns will also impact agricultural productivity.

**Current Irrigation Practices and Challenges**

Existing irrigation systems in the Mekong Basin rely on boreholes and canals, which suffer from significant water losses due to evaporation and seepage. These inefficiencies result in wasted resources, reduced crop yields, and increased energy consumption.
