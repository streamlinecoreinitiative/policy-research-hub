Executive Summary

Sub-Saharan Africa faces significant challenges in accessing safe drinking water and reliable electricity, with climate change exacerbating these issues. To mitigate these challenges, we recommend that governments prioritize renewable energy deployment, invest in clean water infrastructure, and implement climate-resilient agricultural practices. By doing so, they will not only improve the lives of millions but also contribute to global efforts towards achieving sustainable development goals.

Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa's population is rapidly growing while resources remain scarce. Limited access to safe drinking water and electricity poses health risks and hinders economic development. According to World Bank Open Data (2024), Sub-Saharan Africa has only 32.4% access to safely managed drinking water, far below the global average of 73.7% [1]. Similarly, only 53.3% of the population in Sub-Saharan Africa has access to electricity, compared to 91.6% globally [2].

Climate change intensifies these challenges, with increasing frequency of droughts and floods affecting water availability and energy supply. The global population lacking safe water is estimated at 2.2 billion people [3], with Sub-Saharan Africa contributing significantly to this number.
