## Analysis

### Water Security

Sub-Saharan Africa has a significant shortage of safe drinking water, with only 32.4% of its population having access to safely managed drinking water [1]. This is compared to a global average of 73.7% [3]. The lack of infrastructure and resources hinders the ability to provide clean water.

### Energy Access

With only 53.3% of the population having access to electricity, Sub-Saharan Africa lags behind other regions in energy security [2]. This shortfall affects health outcomes, economic productivity, and climate resilience efforts.

### Climate Impacts

Climate change causes frequent droughts and floods, which compound existing water scarcity issues. These events further strain already limited resources.

- **Costs**: Initial investments in renewable energy projects can be substantial but are expected to yield long-term savings due to reduced reliance on fossil fuels. Water harvesting systems require upfront costs but have significant returns in terms of water security.
