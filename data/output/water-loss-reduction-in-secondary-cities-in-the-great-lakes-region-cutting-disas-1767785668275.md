# Water Loss Reduction in Secondary Cities in the Great Lakes Region: Enhancing Resilience through Sustainable Solutions

## Executive Summary

This brief highlights the importance of addressing water security and climate adaptation in lower-income countries, specifically focusing on secondary cities in the Great Lakes region. The document provides an overview of current challenges faced by these regions, including severe water scarcity and limited access to renewable energy sources. It also explores innovative solutions that have been successfully implemented in similar contexts.

## Current Challenges and Opportunities

### Water Scarcity and Inefficient Irrigation Systems

* The Great Lakes region is characterized by high precipitation variability (annual range of 600-1400 mm), leading to severe water scarcity [verify].
* Traditional irrigation methods result in significant water losses due to evaporation and seepage.
* Limited access to renewable energy sources hinders the adoption of efficient irrigation technologies.

### Climate Change Impacts

* Rising temperatures and changing precipitation patterns exacerbate water scarcity, affecting agricultural productivity and food security.
* Increased frequency and severity of extreme weather events pose significant risks to human settlements and infrastructure in secondary cities.

## Innovative Solutions: Agroforestry-Based Micro-Hydro Systems

* Agroforestry practices improve soil health, biodiversity, and carbon sequestration, reducing the need for synthetic fertilizers and pesticides.
* Micro-hydro systems provide sustainable and renewable energy solutions [verify].
* Integration of agroforestry and micro-hydro systems reduces water losses and increases crop yields.

## Policy Recommendations and Future Research Directions
