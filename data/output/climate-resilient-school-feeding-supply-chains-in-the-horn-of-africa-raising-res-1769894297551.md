**Bridging Water Security, Clean Energy Access, and Climate Resilience in Sub-Saharan Africa**

### Executive Summary

Immediate action is needed to address the critical challenges of water security, clean energy access, and climate resilience in Sub-Saharan Africa. With only 32.4% of the region's population having access to safely managed drinking water (World Bank Open Data, 2024), and 53.3% accessing electricity (World Bank Open Data, 2023), these challenges pose significant barriers to sustainable development and food security. To bridge this gap, we recommend investing in water infrastructure development, promoting renewable energy adoption by incentivizing households and businesses to switch from non-renewable sources, and implementing climate adaptation measures such as early warning systems and resilient infrastructure.

### Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa faces significant gaps in essential services like drinking water and electricity. According to the World Bank Open Data (2024), only 32.4% of Sub-Saharan African populations have access to safely managed drinking water, while 53.3% access electricity (World Bank Open Data, 2023). These figures are even more concerning given that globally, these percentages stand at 73.7% and 91.6%, respectively (World Bank Open Data, 2024). The region's reliance on non-renewable energy sources for over 90% of its power generation further complicates the situation, with only 19.7% of total final energy consumption coming from renewable sources (World Bank Open Data, 2020).

Climate change is also intensifying these challenges through more frequent and severe weather events such as droughts and floods. The region's population lacking safe water stands at approximately 2.2 billion people (UN-Water/WHO, 2022). These factors collectively threaten Sub-Saharan Africa's ability to achieve the Sustainable Development Goal 6 on safely managed water.

### Analysis

The current state of water security, clean energy access, and climate resilience in Sub-Saharan Africa underscores the urgent need for intervention. The low levels of safe drinking water and electricity availability highlight a significant gap in meeting basic needs. Furthermore, the region's over-reliance on non-renewable energy sources contributes to environmental degradation and exacerbates climate change impacts. Climate change is further complicating these issues by increasing frequency and severity of droughts and floods.
