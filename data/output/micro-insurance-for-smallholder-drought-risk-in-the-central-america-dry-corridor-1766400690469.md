## Executive Summary

The Central America dry corridor is highly vulnerable to drought, affecting smallholder farmers and their families who rely on agriculture as a primary source of income. Climate change exacerbates water scarcity, leading to crop failures, reduced yields, and economic instability. This brief explores the integration of agroforestry-based micro-hydro systems for climate-resilient irrigation in the Mesoamerican Highlands as a solution to address severe water scarcity and poverty through integrated solutions.

## Introduction to Water Scarcity in the Mesoamerican Highlands

Climate change has significant impacts on agriculture, leading to severe water scarcity in the Mesoamerican Highlands. Rising temperatures and changing precipitation patterns reduce crop yields, affecting food security and economic stability for smallholder farmers. Resilient irrigation methods are crucial for addressing these challenges.
