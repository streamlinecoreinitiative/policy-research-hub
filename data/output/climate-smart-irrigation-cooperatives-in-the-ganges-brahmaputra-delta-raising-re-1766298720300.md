# climate-smart irrigation cooperatives in the Ganges-Brahmaputra delta: raising resilience finance for communities

**Climate-Smart Irrigation Cooperatives in the Ganges-Brahmaputra Delta: Raising Resilience Finance for Communities**

### Executive Summary

The Ganges-Brahmaputra delta is one of the world's most densely populated and vulnerable regions to climate change, facing severe water scarcity that threatens food security and economic stability. This brief explores the potential of integrating agroforestry with micro-hydro systems as a climate-resilient irrigation solution for smallholder farmers in the region.

### Introduction to Water Scarcity in the Ganges-Brahmaputra Delta

Climate change is exacerbating water scarcity in the Ganges-Brahmaputra delta through erratic rainfall patterns and increased evaporation from its vast wetlands. Smallholder farmers are particularly vulnerable, as traditional irrigation methods are energy-intensive and costly.

### Current Irrigation Practices and Challenges

Existing irrigation systems in the Ganges-Brahmaputra delta rely heavily on diesel-powered pumps, which contribute to greenhouse gas emissions and increase water loss through evaporation and seepage. These methods are also often inefficient, leading to wasted resources and reduced crop yields.

*   **Boreholes:** Boreholes provide a reliable source of water but can be expensive to drill and maintain.
*   **Canals:** Canals are prone to siltation and require frequent cleaning, which can lead to water loss and decreased efficiency.

Agroforestry systems have been shown to improve soil health, biodiversity, and carbon sequestration. By integrating trees into agricultural landscapes, farmers can reduce erosion, increase water retention, and promote more efficient use of resources.

*   **Soil Health:** Tree roots help maintain soil structure and fertility.
*   **Biodiversity:** Agroforestry systems support a wider range of plant and animal species.
*   **Carbon Sequestration:** Trees absorb carbon dioxide from the atmosphere.

### Micro-Hydro Systems for Irrigation Efficiency

Micro-hydro technology harnesses the energy potential of small water flows, providing a renewable and sustainable source of power. By integrating micro-hydro systems with agroforestry, farmers can reduce their reliance on diesel generators and increase energy efficiency.

*   **Renewable Energy:** Micro-hydro systems generate electricity from flowing water.
*   **Energy Efficiency:** These systems are often more efficient than traditional diesel-powered pumps.

### Integration of Agroforestry and Micro-Hydro in Climate-Resilient Water Supply

Pilot projects have demonstrated the potential for integrated agroforestry-micro-hydro systems to improve irrigation efficiency, reduce energy costs, and enhance community resilience. These cooperatives can provide a model for scaling up climate-resilient water management practices across the region.

*   **Economic Analysis:** Studies have shown that these integrated systems can be cost-effective and financially viable.
*   **Community Acceptance:** Participatory approaches to project design and implementation have fostered community engagement and ownership.

### Policy Recommendations

To support the adoption of climate-resilient irrigation cooperatives, policymakers should:

1.  **Promote Agroforestry Practices:** Encourage farmers to adopt agroforestry systems through incentives, training programs, and policy support.
2.  **Invest in Micro-Hydro Infrastructure:** Develop and maintain micro-hydro infrastructure, including dams, canals, and turbines.
3.  **Foster Community Engagement:** Support participatory approaches to project design and implementation, ensuring community ownership and benefits.
