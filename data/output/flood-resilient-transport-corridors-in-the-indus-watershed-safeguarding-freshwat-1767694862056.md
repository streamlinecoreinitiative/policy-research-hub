# Flood-Resilient Transport Corridors in the Indus Watershed: Safeguarding Freshwater Ecosystems

## Executive Summary

The Indus watershed is a critical freshwater ecosystem supporting over 300 million people across four countries (Pakistan, India, China, and Afghanistan). Climate change impacts, such as erratic rainfall patterns and increased evaporation from wetlands, pose significant water security challenges. This brief proposes integrating agroforestry with micro-hydro systems for climate-resilient irrigation in the Indus watershed.
