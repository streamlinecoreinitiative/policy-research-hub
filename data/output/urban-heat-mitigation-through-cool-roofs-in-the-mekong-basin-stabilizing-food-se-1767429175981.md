# Agroforestry-Based Micro-Hydro Systems for Climate-Resilient Irrigation in the Mesoamerican Highlands

## Executive Summary

The Mesoamerican Highlands face severe water scarcity and poverty, exacerbated by climate change impacts on agriculture. This brief explores the potential of agroforestry-based micro-hydro systems as a solution to ensure climate-resilient irrigation methods for food security and economic stability.

## Introduction to Water Scarcity in the Mesoamerican Highlands

Climate change is altering precipitation patterns, leading to more frequent and severe droughts in the Mesoamerican Highlands. This region's agricultural sector relies heavily on irrigation systems, which often suffer from inefficiencies, water loss, and high energy costs.

## Policy Recommendations and Future Research Directions

To support adoption at scale:

1. **Policy Support:** Governments should provide incentives for agroforestry-based micro-hydro systems.
2. **Capacity Building:** Training programs for farmers, technicians, and policymakers can enhance knowledge sharing and promote best practices.
3. **Community Engagement:** Engage local communities in the planning and implementation process.

Future research directions:

1. **Economic Impact Analysis:** Conduct thorough economic assessments of agroforestry-based micro-hydro systems.
2. **Scalability and Replicability:** Investigate ways to scale up successful pilot projects, addressing challenges such as land ownership and community buy-in.
3. **Climate Change Adaptation:** Explore the potential for these integrated systems to adapt to changing climate conditions.
