# Agroforestry-Based Micro-Hydro Systems for Climate-Resilient Irrigation in the Sudano-Sahelian Belt

## Executive Summary

The Sudano-Sahelian belt faces significant water scarcity challenges due to erratic rainfall patterns, threatening food security and economic stability. This brief explores an innovative solution: integrating agroforestry with micro-hydro systems for climate-resilient irrigation.

## Environmental Resilience in Lower-Income Countries

### Water Security

The Sudano-Sahelian belt experiences low rainfall, with average annual precipitation ranging from 200 to 1,000 mm [verify]. Existing irrigation systems suffer from significant water loss: up to 30% due to evaporation and 5% due to seepage. Agroforestry-based micro-hydro systems offer a more efficient solution.

### Clean Energy

Micro-hydro technology harnesses the energy of small-scale hydroelectric systems, providing reliable power for irrigation pumps and agricultural needs. Integrating micro-hydro with agroforestry reduces reliance on diesel generators – decreasing greenhouse gas emissions and increasing energy efficiency.

### Climate Adaptation

Agroforestry enhances soil health, biodiversity, and carbon sequestration, playing a crucial role in climate resilience. Case studies demonstrate how agroforestry improves water retention and reduces erosion, making it an essential component of integrated watershed management strategies.

## Integration of Agroforestry and Micro-Hydro Systems

Pilot projects have successfully combined agroforestry with micro-hydro systems to create climate-resilient irrigation solutions. These initiatives showcase the potential for scaling up these technologies across the Sudano-Sahelian belt, improving agricultural productivity while mitigating climate change impacts.

### Economic Benefits

Economic analysis highlights the cost-effectiveness and financial viability of agroforestry-micro-hydro projects. By reducing water loss and increasing energy efficiency, communities can save on fuel costs and improve their overall economic stability.
