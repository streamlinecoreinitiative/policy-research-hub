**Bridging Water Security, Clean Energy, and Climate Adaptation Gaps in Sub-Saharan Africa**

## Executive Summary

To address the pressing environmental resilience gaps in water security, clean energy access, and climate adaptation in Sub-Saharan Africa, we recommend prioritizing investments in infrastructure, promoting renewable energy adoption, and enhancing climate-resilient water management practices. With only 32.4% of the region's population having access to safely managed drinking water (World Bank Open Data), this brief highlights actionable steps for governments and organizations to address these critical issues effectively.

## Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa is one of the regions most vulnerable to climate change impacts, such as droughts and floods, exacerbating existing challenges like inadequate access to clean water and electricity. The region's limited financial resources and governance capacity hinder effective implementation of environmental resilience strategies. According to World Bank data (2024), Sub-Saharan Africa lags behind globally in accessing safe drinking water (32.4%) and electricity (53.3%). Renewable energy consumption stands at 19.7% of total final energy consumption, significantly lower than the global average.
