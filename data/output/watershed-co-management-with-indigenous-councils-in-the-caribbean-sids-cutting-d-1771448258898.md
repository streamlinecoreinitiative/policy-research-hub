## Executive Summary

Improving access to safe drinking water, expanding clean energy production, and enhancing climate resilience are critical steps towards achieving sustainable development in Sub-Saharan Africa. To address the severe challenges in water security, clean energy access, and climate adaptation, we recommend:

* Increasing access to safe drinking water by implementing large-scale water treatment plants and improving piped water systems in rural areas.
* Expanding renewable energy production by investing in solar, wind, and hydroelectric power projects.

## Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa faces significant challenges in water security, clean energy access, and climate adaptation due to its fragile environmental conditions and limited financial resources. According to World Bank data, access to safely managed drinking water remains low at 32.4% (2024) compared to the global average of 73.7% (World Bank Open Data). Electricity access stands at only 53.3%, despite a global rate of 91.6% (World Bank Open Data).
