Sub-Saharan Africa faces significant disparities in basic services like clean water and electricity. Only 32.4% of the population has access to safely managed drinking water, compared to 73.7% globally [1]. Additionally, only 53.3% have access to electricity, with rural areas often lagging behind [2]. Renewable energy consumption stands at 9.2%, far below global averages (19.7%) [3]. Climate change compounds these issues by increasing the frequency and intensity of extreme weather events.

## Policy Recommendations

1. **Expand Public-Private Partnerships (PPP) for Water Infrastructure**:
   - Develop PPP models tailored to local contexts.
   - Increase public investment in water supply and sanitation projects.
2. **Promote Solar Power Initiatives**:
   - Implement feed-in tariffs or solar power subsidies.
   - Invest in rural electrification programs using off-grid solar solutions.
3. **Integrate Early-Warning Systems for Climate Events**:
   - Establish national climate early warning centers.
   - Enhance community engagement to ensure system effectiveness and sustainability.
