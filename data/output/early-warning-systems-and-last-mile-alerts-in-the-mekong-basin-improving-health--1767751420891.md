**Agroforestry-Based Micro-Hydro Systems for Climate-Resilient Irrigation in the Central African Rainforest**

### Executive Summary

Climate change exacerbates water scarcity in the Central African Rainforest, threatening agricultural livelihoods and food security. This project proposes integrating agroforestry with micro-hydro systems to enhance climate resilience and improve irrigation efficiency.

### Introduction to Water Scarcity

Climate change alters precipitation patterns in the Central African Rainforest, leading to reduced water availability and increased frequency of droughts. Agroforestry has been shown to improve soil health, increasing moisture retention (Case Study 1). Another example highlights a project demonstrating improved water retention and reduced erosion (Case Study 2).

Economic challenges hinder irrigation development, with high labor costs for constructing and maintaining systems.

### Agroforestry's Role in Climate Resilience

Agroforestry practices enhance biodiversity, leading to natural pest control and pollination services. Improved soil structure increases moisture retention (Soil Health Benefits). A pilot project showed increased crop yields (Example Project).

### Micro-Hydro Systems for Efficiency and Energy Savings

Micro-hydro technology provides a renewable energy source for irrigation systems, reducing reliance on diesel generators. Pilot projects have shown improved water delivery and reduced diesel generator usage.

Energy savings are reported to be $10 per day [verify] or $50 monthly maintenance costs.

### Integration of Agroforestry and Micro-Hydro

Pilot projects integrating agroforestry and micro-hydro systems demonstrate increased crop yields and self-sufficient renewable energy supply. Economic benefits include reduced fuel and maintenance costs ($50 per month savings over five years) [verify].

### Closing Outlook

The integration of agroforestry and micro-hydro systems offers a promising solution to address water scarcity and climate change impacts in the Central African Rainforest. By promoting sustainable land use practices, renewable energy sources, and community-led development, this approach can enhance agricultural resilience and improve health outcomes for vulnerable communities.
