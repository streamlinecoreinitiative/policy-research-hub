# Flood-Resilient Transport Corridors in the Sahel: Lowering Emissions from Critical Sectors

## Executive Summary

The Sahel region is highly vulnerable to climate change impacts, including increasing temperatures and changing precipitation patterns. This brief focuses on flood-resilient transport corridors as a critical sector for emissions reduction in lower-income countries. We explore the integration of agroforestry-based micro-hydro systems with existing infrastructure to enhance water security, promote clean energy use, and support climate adaptation efforts.

## Water Security and Climate Adaptation

### Introduction to Water Scarcity in the Sahel Region

The Sahel region is characterized by arid conditions, limited rainfall, and increasing temperatures. These factors exacerbate water scarcity, affecting agriculture, livestock, and human consumption. ### Current Irrigation Practices and Challenges

Existing irrigation systems in the Sahel region rely heavily on groundwater extraction, often through boreholes or canals. However, these methods are inefficient, leading to significant water loss through evaporation and seepage. The high energy demand for pumping groundwater contributes to greenhouse gas emissions.

## Clean Energy and Transport Infrastructure

### Micro-Hydro Systems for Irrigation Efficiency

Micro-hydro technology offers a low-carbon alternative for irrigation, reducing reliance on diesel generators and increasing energy efficiency. Integrated agroforestry-micro-hydro systems can provide a reliable source of clean energy for transport corridors.

### Integration in Flood-Resilient Transport Corridors

Pilot projects demonstrate the feasibility of combining agroforestry and micro-hydro systems to support flood-resilient transport corridors. These integrated solutions enhance water security, promote clean energy use, and contribute to climate adaptation efforts.
