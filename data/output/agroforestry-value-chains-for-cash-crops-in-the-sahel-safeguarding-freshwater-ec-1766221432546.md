# Agroforestry-Based Micro-Hydro Systems for Climate-Resilient Irrigation in the Mesoamerican Highlands: A Sustainable Solution to Water Scarcity and Poverty

## Executive Summary

The Mesoamerican Highlands face severe water scarcity due to climate change, posing significant challenges to food security and economic stability. Integrating agroforestry with micro-hydro systems can provide sustainable solutions, addressing poverty and promoting renewable energy adoption.

## Water Scarcity in the Mesoamerican Highlands

Climate change has exacerbated water scarcity in these regions, threatening the livelihoods of millions of people who depend on agriculture for their income. Rising temperatures and changing precipitation patterns have led to reduced crop yields, decreased water availability, and increased competition for this limited resource.

### Current Irrigation Practices and Challenges

Existing irrigation systems are often inefficient, with significant water loss due to evaporation and seepage. These losses reduce the amount of water available for crops and increase energy consumption through diesel-powered pumps.

### Micro-Hydro Systems for Irrigation Efficiency

Micro-hydro systems are small-scale hydroelectric power plants that harness the energy potential of rivers and streams. These systems offer a sustainable alternative to diesel-powered pumps, reducing greenhouse gas emissions and increasing energy efficiency.

## Integration of Agroforestry and Micro-Hydro in Climate-Resilient Water Supply

Pilot projects have demonstrated significant improvements in water retention and reduced erosion when combining agroforestry with micro-hydro practices. Economic analyses highlight the cost-effectiveness of these integrated solutions, but further research is needed to determine their scalability.
