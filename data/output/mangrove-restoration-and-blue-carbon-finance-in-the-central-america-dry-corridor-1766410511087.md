# Mangrove Restoration and Blue Carbon Finance in the Central America Dry Corridor: Safeguarding Freshwater Ecosystems

## Executive Summary

The Central America dry corridor is highly vulnerable to climate change, facing severe droughts and water scarcity. This brief highlights the importance of mangrove restoration and blue carbon finance as a solution to safeguard freshwater ecosystems in this region. By integrating agroforestry with micro-hydro systems, communities can improve soil health, biodiversity, and carbon sequestration while providing sustainable and renewable energy solutions for irrigation.

## Water Scarcity and Climate Change Impacts

The Central America dry corridor is characterized by erratic rainfall patterns, with significant precipitation variability (600-1400 mm annual range). Traditional irrigation methods are energy-intensive and result in substantial water losses due to evaporation and seepage. The economic burden of these methods is high.
