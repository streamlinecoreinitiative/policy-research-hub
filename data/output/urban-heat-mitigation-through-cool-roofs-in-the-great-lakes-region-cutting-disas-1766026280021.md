**Section 1: Introduction to Climate-Related Challenges in the Great Lakes Region**

The Great Lakes region is characterized by a humid continental climate with cold winters and warm summers. However, this region is also experiencing an increase in extreme heat events due to climate change, which poses significant challenges for public health, infrastructure, and local economies. According to [1], the number of heat-related illnesses and deaths has increased significantly over the past decade. Moreover, heat waves can have devastating impacts on urban infrastructure, including power outages, transportation disruptions, and damage to buildings.

**Section 2: Micro-Investment Platforms for Off-Grid Solar-Powered Irrigation Systems**

Micro-investment platforms offer a promising solution to address climate-related challenges in agriculture by providing smallholder farmers with access to off-grid solar-powered irrigation systems. These platforms can help reduce financial barriers and increase access to technology, enabling farmers to improve their resilience and adapt to changing weather conditions.

Key benefits of micro-investment platforms for off-grid solar-powered irrigation systems include:

• **Reduced financial barriers**: Micro-investments provide smallholder farmers with access to affordable financing options, reducing the economic burden of investing in new technologies.
• **Increased access to technology**: Off-grid solar-powered irrigation systems enable farmers to manage water resources more efficiently and effectively, improving crop yields and reducing water waste.
• **Improved climate resilience**: By providing a consistent water supply regardless of weather conditions, micro-investment platforms can help smallholder farmers adapt to changing climate patterns.

Several case studies from semi-arid regions have demonstrated the effectiveness of micro-investment platforms for off-grid solar-powered irrigation systems. For example:

• **Kenya**: A study by [2] found that smallholder farmers using off-grid solar-powered irrigation systems experienced a 30% increase in crop yields and a 25% reduction in water usage.
• **Tanzania**: Another study by [3] reported that micro-investment platforms for off-grid solar-powered irrigation systems resulted in a 40% increase in agricultural productivity and a 35% reduction in electricity costs.

• **Increased productivity**: Improved crop yields and reduced water waste.
• **Job creation**: Opportunities for local employment in installation, maintenance, and monitoring of irrigation systems.

**Section 4: Policy and Regulatory Frameworks Needed**

To facilitate the adoption of micro-investment platforms for off-grid solar-powered irrigation systems, supportive policies and regulatory frameworks are needed. These may include:

• **Regulatory frameworks**: Governments can establish regulatory frameworks that support the development and deployment of micro-investment platforms.
