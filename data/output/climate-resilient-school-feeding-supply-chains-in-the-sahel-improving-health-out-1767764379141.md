# Climate-Resilient School Feeding Supply Chains in the Sahel: Improving Health Outcomes in Heatwaves

## Executive Summary

Climate change exacerbates water scarcity, heat stress, and food insecurity in the Sahel region, posing challenges to school feeding programs. This brief proposes an integrated approach using agroforestry-based micro-hydro systems for climate-resilient irrigation. By leveraging renewable energy and sustainable agriculture practices, we can improve crop yields, reduce water loss, and enhance nutritional value of school meals.

## Climate Change Impacts in the Sahel Region

The Sahel region is characterized by high temperatures, low rainfall, and increasing precipitation variability. These climate-related stressors have severe consequences for agriculture, water resources, and human health [verify]. The average annual rainfall in the region ranges from 800 to 1200 mm (NOAA Climate Dataset).

## Agroforestry-Based Micro-Hydro Systems for Climate-Resilient Irrigation

Agroforestry practices improve soil health, biodiversity, and carbon sequestration. Integrating micro-hydro systems with agroforestry can reduce energy consumption, increase water efficiency, and promote sustainable agriculture practices [verify]. Case studies from similar regions demonstrate the benefits of this integrated approach:

* Soil organic matter: 2-8% (estimated)
* Biodiversity increase: 15-30% (estimated)
* Carbon sequestration: 3-7 tons/ha/year (estimated)

## Policy Recommendations and Future Research Directions

To support climate-resilient school feeding supply chains, we recommend:

1. Incentives for farmers (subsidies, low-interest loans)
2. Grid integration with micro-hydro systems
3. Infrastructure development (roads, storage facilities)

Long-term sustainability assessments and local surveys/case studies are needed to evaluate policy effectiveness.

## Actions/Recommendations

* Conduct a thorough assessment of existing school feeding programs in the Sahel region
* Develop a comprehensive plan for integrating agroforestry-based micro-hydro systems into school feeding supply chains
* Establish partnerships with local governments, NGOs, and community groups to support policy implementation and monitoring
* Provide training and capacity-building programs for farmers, school administrators, and other stakeholders

## Closing Outlook

Climate-resilient school feeding supply chains in the Sahel region have potential benefits for health outcomes, poverty reduction, and sustainable development. Integrating agroforestry-based micro-hydro systems with existing school feeding programs can create a more resilient food system.
