**Executive Summary**

The Andean highlands face severe water scarcity due to limited access to reliable irrigation systems and economic instability caused by reduced crop yields. This brief explores the potential of integrated agroforestry and micro-hydro systems to address these challenges, ensuring sustainable water management, economic development, and climate resilience.

**Addressing Water Scarcity in the Andean Highlands**

The Andean highlands have historically experienced droughts, which have become more frequent and severe due to climate change. Current challenges include:

* Limited access to reliable irrigation systems
* Economic instability due to reduced crop yields
* High dependency on traditional methods like surface water and rain-fed systems

Farmers and communities face significant economic pressures, exacerbating existing challenges.

**Agroforestry-Based Micro-Hydro Systems for Climate-Resilient Irrigation**

Agroforestry practices offer numerous benefits, including:

* Improved water retention and recharge through increased vegetation cover
* Enhanced ecosystem services such as pollination, pest control, and carbon sequestration

Case studies from other regions demonstrate the effectiveness of agroforestry-micro-hydro systems in similar climates or contexts. In the Andean highlands context, these integrated solutions can:

* Increase agricultural productivity due to improved soil health and water management
* Reduce vulnerability to climate variability
