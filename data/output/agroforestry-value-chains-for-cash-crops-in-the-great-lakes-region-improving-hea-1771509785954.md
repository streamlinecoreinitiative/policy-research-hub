# agroforestry value chains for cash crops in the Great Lakes region: improving health outcomes in heatwaves

**Fostering Climate Resilience in Lower-Income Countries: A Holistic Approach to Water Security, Clean Energy, and Adaptation Strategies**

### Executive Summary

Lower-income countries face significant challenges in accessing safe drinking water (32.4%)[1] and electricity (53.3%) [2], highlighting critical gaps in basic infrastructure. To address these issues, we recommend investing in community-led total sanitation (CLTS) programs in rural areas, promoting water harvesting and storage solutions, and encouraging decentralized renewable energy projects such as solar-powered mini-grids for off-grid communities.

### Context & Challenge

Climate-related disasters, including droughts and floods, are increasingly common, exacerbating existing water scarcity issues [3]. Limited access to electricity poses significant barriers to economic development and health outcomes, particularly in Sub-Saharan Africa where only 53.3% of the population has access to electricity [2]. Renewable energy consumption is also low (19.7%) [4], leading to environmental degradation and increased vulnerability to climate shocks.

### Analysis

Inadequate water security leads to poor hygiene conditions, contributing to diseases such as cholera, dysentery, and typhoid fever [5]. Sub-Saharan Africa lags behind in accessing electricity (53.3%) compared to the world average (91.6%) [2], exacerbating economic disparities and health risks.

### Policy Recommendations

1. **Enhance Access to Safe Drinking Water**:
	* Implement community-led total sanitation (CLTS) programs in rural areas.
	* Promote water harvesting and storage solutions, particularly through small-scale rainwater harvesting systems.
2. **Promote Renewable Energy Adoption**:
	* Invest in solar-powered mini-grids for off-grid communities.
	* Encourage decentralized renewable energy projects such as biogas digesters and solar cookstoves to reduce reliance on fossil fuels.
3. **Support Climate-Resilient Agriculture**:
	* Implement climate-smart agriculture practices, including drought-resistant crop varieties and improved irrigation systems.
	* Enhance early warning systems for extreme weather events through partnerships with meteorological agencies.

### Implementation Considerations

1. **Costs and Financing**:
	* Estimate initial investment costs for solar-powered mini-grids (approximately $10,000 per household) [6] and water harvesting tanks ($50-100).
	* Explore financing options such as grants from international development agencies and microfinance institutions.
2. **Potential Barriers**:
	* Cultural resistance to new technologies may hinder adoption of solar-powered mini-grids.
	* Infrastructure constraints (e.g., lack of grid connectivity, roads for distribution) need to be addressed.

### Conclusion

To achieve sustainable climate resilience in lower-income countries, it is crucial to integrate solutions that address water security, promote clean energy, and enhance adaptation strategies. By scaling up successful pilot projects and securing adequate financing, we can make significant strides towards a more resilient future.
