# Agroforestry-Based Micro-Hydro Systems for Climate-Resilient Irrigation in the Sudano-Sahelian Belt

## Executive Summary

The Sudano-Sahelian belt is one of the most water-scarce regions globally, facing severe impacts on food security and economic stability. This brief explores the potential of integrating agroforestry with micro-hydro systems to address water scarcity and poverty in this region.

## Water Scarcity in the Sudano-Sahelian Belt

The Sudano-Sahelian belt is characterized by erratic rainfall patterns, resulting in severe water scarcity that affects agriculture, livestock, and human consumption. The region's population relies heavily on boreholes and canals for irrigation, but these systems suffer from high water loss rates.

## Agroforestry and Micro-Hydro Integration

Agroforestry has been shown to improve soil health, biodiversity, and carbon sequestration. Integrating agroforestry with micro-hydro systems can create climate-resilient irrigation methods that reduce water loss and increase efficiency. Case studies from similar regions demonstrate the effectiveness of this approach in improving water retention and reducing erosion.

### Benefits of Agroforestry-Micro-Hydro Integration:

• Reduced water loss through evaporation and seepage
• Increased energy efficiency through micro-hydro technology
• Improved soil health and biodiversity
• Enhanced carbon sequestration
