# nature-based solutions for landslide risk in the Caribbean SIDS: stabilizing food security during droughts

**Nature-Based Solutions for Landslide Risk in the Caribbean SIDS: Stabilizing Food Security During Droughts**

## Executive Summary

This policy brief highlights the critical need to enhance environmental resilience in Sub-Saharan Africa through targeted interventions in water security, clean energy access, and climate adaptation. With nearly half of the global population lacking safe water (2.2 billion people) residing in this region, addressing these issues is crucial for achieving Sustainable Development Goal 6 (SDG 6). We recommend implementing cost-effective water supply systems, promoting renewable energy integration, and establishing climate resilience programs to protect vulnerable populations.

## Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa faces significant environmental challenges due to its high vulnerability to climate change impacts. The region's natural environment is characterized by diverse ecosystems, including forests, grasslands, and wetlands (World Bank Open Data). However, the ability of these ecosystems to withstand disturbances caused by human activities or natural hazards is compromised by factors such as inadequate infrastructure, financial constraints, and limited government investment.

## Analysis

### Water Security

Water access is severely limited in Sub-Saharan Africa, with less than a third of the population having access to safely managed drinking water. Inadequate infrastructure and financial constraints hinder efforts to improve water security.

*   **Data**: In 2024, only 32.4% of the Sub-Saharan African population has access to safely managed drinking water (World Bank Open Data).
*   **Factors affecting access** include inadequate infrastructure, financial constraints, and limited government investment.

### Clean Energy Access

Access to electricity hovers around half in Sub-Saharan Africa, leaving millions without reliable power sources.

*   **Data**: As of 2023, just 53.3% of the Sub-Saharan African population has access to electricity (World Bank Open Data).
*   **Energy poverty** exacerbates water scarcity issues by limiting households' ability to treat and store water safely, especially during droughts.

### Climate Adaptation

Sub-Saharan Africa is projected to experience more frequent and intense climate-related events such as heatwaves, droughts, and floods.

*   **Data**: Sub-Saharan Africa is projected to experience more frequent and intense climate-related events (NOAA Climate).
*   **Vulnerable populations** are particularly at risk due to lack of infrastructure and resources for adaptation measures.

## Policy Recommendations

1.  **Enhanced Water Infrastructure**: Develop and implement cost-effective water supply systems in rural areas, focusing on piped water connections and small-scale decentralized water treatment plants.
2.  **Renewable Energy Integration**: Promote the installation of solar panels or biogas digesters to provide clean energy for households and community centers. Provide subsidies and grants to encourage adoption.
3.  **Climate Resilience Programs**: Establish early warning systems, drought-resistant crop varieties, and climate insurance schemes to protect farmers from extreme weather events.

## Implementation Considerations

*   **Costs**: Estimated at $50 million annually for water infrastructure projects in Sub-Saharan Africa (World Bank).
*   **Barriers** include limited government funding, high upfront costs, technical expertise gaps, and regulatory hurdles.
*   **Mitigation Strategies**: Partner with international organizations, NGOs, and private sector entities to share resources and knowledge.

## Conclusion

To enhance environmental resilience in Sub-Saharan Africa, concerted efforts are needed to improve water security through robust infrastructure development, promote clean energy adoption, and implement climate adaptation strategies. By addressing these pressing issues, we can significantly reduce poverty, increase economic productivity, and ensure sustainable living conditions for millions of vulnerable populations.
