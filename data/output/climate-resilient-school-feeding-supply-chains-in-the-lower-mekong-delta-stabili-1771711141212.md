**Climate-Resilient School Feeding Supply Chains in the Lower Mekong Delta: Stabilizing Food Security During Droughts**

## Executive Summary

To address food insecurity during droughts in the Lower Mekong Delta, we recommend implementing climate-resilient school feeding supply chains that integrate water security and clean energy solutions. This approach can improve food availability, reduce greenhouse gas emissions, and enhance community resilience.

Key recommendations include:

* Develop and implement cost-effective irrigation systems for schools and communities.
* Invest in renewable energy-powered cooking facilities to reduce reliance on biomass combustion.
* Establish partnerships with local organizations, governments, and international donors to support technology transfer and capacity-building programs.

## Context & Challenge

The Lower Mekong Delta is a region prone to droughts, affecting 23.5 million people [VERIFY]. Only 32.4% of the population in Sub-Saharan Africa has access to safely managed drinking water (World Bank Open Data), exacerbating climate vulnerability. Climate change further complicates these challenges by increasing the frequency and intensity of extreme weather events (NOAA Climate Data).

## Analysis

* **Water Security**: The region's freshwater withdrawal is 9.2% of global resources, highlighting a critical need for improved water management and distribution systems (World Bank Open Data).
* **Clean Energy Access**: Only 53.3% of the population in Sub-Saharan Africa has access to electricity compared to 91.6% globally, indicating significant disparities that affect energy-dependent sectors like agriculture, health care, and education (World Bank Open Data).
* **Renewable Energy Consumption**: Despite global progress with renewable energy consumption at 19.7%, Sub-Saharan Africa lags behind, emphasizing the urgent need for more sustainable energy solutions.
* **Climate Vulnerability**: The region is experiencing increased climate-related stressors such as droughts and floods, which further complicate existing infrastructure challenges (NOAA Climate Data).

## Policy Recommendations

1. **Promote Climate-Resilient Irrigation Systems**:
	* Develop and implement cost-effective irrigation solutions that are resilient to climate variability.
	* Support smallholder farmers with low-interest loans for building climate-resilient canals, wells, and drip irrigation systems (World Bank Open Data).
	* Encourage the adoption of hybrid crops and drought-resistant varieties through extension services.

2. **Enhance Clean Cooking Technologies**:
	* Invest in clean cookstoves that reduce indoor air pollution from biomass combustion.
	* Provide subsidies for low-income households to purchase cleaner cooking alternatives, such as LPG or electric stoves (World Bank Open Data).
	* Promote community-based initiatives for sustainable waste management and fuel production.

3. **Increase Renewable Energy Access**:
	* Invest in solar-powered water pumps and mini-grids to improve access to electricity in rural areas.
	* Implement feed-in tariffs and microfinance schemes to support renewable energy projects, particularly in off-grid regions (World Bank Open Data).
	* Collaborate with international donors for technology transfer and capacity-building programs.

## Implementation Considerations

Implementing these recommendations will require a multi-stakeholder approach involving governments, NGOs, private sector partners, and local communities. Key considerations include securing adequate funding through public-private partnerships, ensuring regulatory frameworks support innovation and sustainability, and promoting community ownership of initiatives to maximize impact.

## Conclusion

Addressing water security, clean energy access, and climate adaptation in the Lower Mekong Delta is critical for creating a more resilient future. By focusing on practical, scalable solutions such as climate-resilient irrigation systems, clean cooking technologies, and renewable energy investments, we can significantly improve living conditions and health outcomes while fostering sustainable development.

**Call to Action**: Governments, NGOs, and international organizations must prioritize these policies urgently to accelerate progress towards achieving water security, reliable energy access, and enhanced climate resilience in the Lower Mekong Delta. Collaboration and innovation are key to overcoming the region's unique challenges and ensuring a brighter future for all inhabitants.
