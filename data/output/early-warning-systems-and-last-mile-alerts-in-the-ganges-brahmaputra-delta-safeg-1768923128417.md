## Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa lacks basic water security and energy access, with only 32.4% of the population having access to safely managed drinking water [Source: World Bank Open Data]. Climate change impacts threaten existing infrastructure, making it critical to implement climate-resilient solutions. The region is expected to experience increased frequency of droughts and floods, exacerbating existing challenges in accessing safe drinking water and renewable energy.

## Analysis

The region lags behind global progress on SDG 6 (water security), with only 74% coverage compared to a global average of 73.7% [Source: UN SDG Report 2023]. This disparity is exacerbated by limited renewable energy consumption, which stands at 19.7% globally [Source: World Bank Open Data]. Developing countries need $5.8-5.9 trillion annually for climate adaptation [Source: UNFCCC], highlighting the significant investment required in Sub-Saharan Africa.
