## Executive Summary

This brief focuses on enhancing environmental resilience in lower-income countries through integrated watershed co-management and indigenous council-led initiatives. The proposed approach combines agroforestry-based micro-hydro systems with climate adaptation strategies to improve water security, clean energy access, and community livelihoods.

## Introduction

The Great Lakes region is characterized by severe water scarcity and climate change impacts on agriculture, affecting vulnerable communities' livelihoods. Traditional irrigation systems face high labor costs and fuel expenses, while agroforestry has demonstrated improved soil health benefits and biodiversity enhancement.
