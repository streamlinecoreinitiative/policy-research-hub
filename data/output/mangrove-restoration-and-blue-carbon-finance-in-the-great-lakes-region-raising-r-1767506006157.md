# Mangrove Restoration and Blue Carbon Finance in the Great Lakes Region: Raising Resilience Finance for Communities

## Executive Summary

The Great Lakes region is one of the most biodiverse areas on the planet, facing significant environmental challenges. Climate change, water scarcity, and poverty are interconnected issues that require integrated solutions to ensure economic benefits and sustainability. This brief explores the potential of mangrove restoration and blue carbon finance in raising resilience finance for communities in the Great Lakes region.

## Introduction

The Great Lakes region is home to over 40 million people and supports a rich array of ecosystems, including mangroves, which provide essential ecosystem services such as shoreline protection, water filtration, and habitat creation. However, these ecosystems are under threat from climate change, deforestation, and pollution. Mangrove restoration and blue carbon finance offer opportunities for communities in the region to raise resilience finance and address these challenges.

### Water Security

Water scarcity is a pressing issue in the Great Lakes region, with many communities facing difficulties accessing clean water. Climate change exacerbates this problem by altering precipitation patterns and increasing evaporation rates. Agroforestry-based micro-hydro systems can help address water scarcity by providing efficient irrigation methods that reduce water loss through evaporation and seepage.

### Clean Energy

The Great Lakes region has significant potential for renewable energy production, particularly from hydroelectric power. Micro-hydro systems can provide clean energy for communities while reducing reliance on diesel generators and increasing energy efficiency.

### Climate Adaptation

Climate change poses a major threat to the ecosystems of the Great Lakes region. Mangrove restoration and blue carbon finance offer opportunities for communities to adapt to climate change by creating resilient ecosystems that can withstand extreme weather events and sea-level rise.

## Integration of Agroforestry, Micro-Hydro, and Blue Carbon Finance

Pilot projects in the Great Lakes region have demonstrated the potential of integrating agroforestry, micro-hydro, and blue carbon finance. These projects have shown that:

*   Agroforestry can improve soil health, biodiversity, and carbon sequestration while reducing erosion and improving water retention.
*   Micro-hydro systems can provide efficient irrigation methods and clean energy for communities.
*   Blue carbon finance can mobilize investment in mangrove restoration and create economic benefits for local communities.
