### Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa is one of the most vulnerable regions globally to environmental degradation and climate change impacts. Recent data highlights stark disparities in access to safe drinking water and electricity across the region:

* **Access to safely managed drinking water:** 32.4% of Sub-Saharan African population (World Bank Open Data, 2024)
* **Access to electricity:** 53.3% of Sub-Saharan African population (World Bank Open Data, 2023)

These figures underscore urgent needs for policy interventions that enhance water security, promote clean energy adoption, and bolster climate resilience.

### Analysis

The reliance on fossil fuels for energy production contributes to greenhouse gas emissions and exacerbates climate change impacts. In contrast, renewable energy sources offer a cleaner alternative:

* **Renewable energy consumption:** 19.7% of global energy consumption (World Bank Open Data, 2020)

Climate change also poses significant risks to water security, with droughts and floods affecting millions of people each year.

### Policy Recommendations

To address these challenges effectively, governments should prioritize the following:

1. **Enhanced Water Management:**
	* Invest in infrastructure improvements such as water storage facilities, desalination plants, and wastewater treatment systems.
	* Implement rainwater harvesting initiatives to augment available freshwater resources.
2. **Promoting Renewable Energy:**
	* Increase the share of renewable energy consumption by developing wind, solar, hydro, and geothermal power projects.
	* Provide incentives for households and businesses to adopt clean energy solutions like rooftop solar panels and microgrids.
3. **Climate Adaptation Strategies:**
	* Develop early warning systems for natural disasters such as floods and droughts.
	* Implement climate-resilient agricultural practices and support community-based disaster risk reduction initiatives.
