Bridging Water Security, Clean Energy, and Climate Adaptation Gaps in Sub-Saharan Africa

Executive Summary

To address the pressing challenges of water security, clean energy access, and climate resilience in Sub-Saharan Africa, we recommend enhancing water infrastructure through targeted investments and promoting renewable energy adoption, particularly solar-powered mini-grids for off-grid areas. This approach will not only improve access to safely managed drinking water but also reduce reliance on fossil fuels and enhance energy access. By implementing these evidence-based policies and practical implementation steps, governments can significantly improve the lives of millions while contributing to global sustainability goals.

Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa is one of the most vulnerable regions globally to climate change impacts such as droughts, floods, and extreme weather events (UN-Water/WHO, 2022). Water scarcity affects millions, leading to malnutrition and health issues (UN-Water/WHO, 2022). Inadequate access to electricity further exacerbates existing challenges by limiting opportunities for economic growth and social development. Ensuring sustainable water security, clean energy access, and climate resilience is essential for achieving the United Nations Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs).
