Water Security, Clean Energy, and Climate Adaptation in Sub-Saharan Africa: Strengthening Resilience

Executive Summary

Sub-Saharan Africa faces a critical triple threat of water scarcity, energy poverty, and climate vulnerability. To address these challenges, we recommend prioritizing investments in water infrastructure, renewable energy projects, and climate adaptation measures to enhance resilience.

* Implement large-scale irrigation projects and small-scale rainwater harvesting systems to improve water availability (World Bank, "Sub-Saharan Africa Water Sector Investment Database").

  • Invest in solar photovoltaic (PV) and wind energy projects to reduce reliance on fossil fuels and enhance grid stability (International Renewable Energy Agency (IRENA), "Renewable Power Generation Costs in 2019").
  • Develop early warning systems for climate events like droughts and floods, equipping communities with the tools to mitigate impacts (World Meteorological Organization (WMO) Global Framework for Climate Services).

    Context & Challenge

    Sub-Saharan Africa is among the least equipped regions globally for addressing environmental challenges such as water scarcity and energy poverty due to historical underinvestment and current resource constraints. Climate change intensifies these challenges by increasing frequency of droughts and storms, threatening existing infrastructure and exacerbating health risks associated with inadequate access to clean water.

    According to World Bank data (2024), only 32.4% of the region's population has access to safely managed drinking water, leaving millions vulnerable to diseases such as cholera and dysentery [Source: World Bank Open Data]. In contrast, globally, 73.7% of the world's population has access to safely managed drinking water (World Bank Open Data).
