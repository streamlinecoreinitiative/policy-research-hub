Executive Summary

Sub-Saharan Africa is facing a critical water security and clean energy crisis, with 32.4% of its population lacking access to safe drinking water (World Bank Open Data) [1] and only 53.3% having access to electricity (World Bank Open Data) [2]. This policy brief argues that investing in clean energy, water infrastructure, and climate adaptation strategies is essential for bridging this gap. We recommend prioritizing investments in renewable energy integration, water security projects, and climate resilience initiatives.

Key Recommendations:

* Integrate Water Security into National Development Plans by allocating dedicated budgets for water infrastructure projects.

  • Promote Renewable Energy Integration through community-led initiatives for solar energy installations and grid integration.
  • Strengthen Climate Resilience Strategies by implementing early warning systems, improving irrigation techniques, and enhancing disaster preparedness plans.
