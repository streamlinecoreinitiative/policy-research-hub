**Executive Summary**

The Caribbean Small Island Developing States (SIDS) are highly vulnerable to climate change, with water scarcity and drought being major concerns. This brief highlights the importance of implementing sustainable agricultural practices that ensure water security, focusing on drip irrigation and agroforestry as key interventions. By adopting these climate-smart approaches, communities can improve their resilience to climate-related shocks and stresses. The brief provides an overview of the current water scarcity situation in the Caribbean SIDS, the benefits of drip irrigation and agroforestry, case studies from successful implementations, and policy recommendations for scaling up these practices.

**Closing Outlook**
