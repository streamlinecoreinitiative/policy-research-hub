**Urban Heat Mitigation through Cool Roofs in the Lower Mekong Delta: Supporting Adaptation Targets in NDCs**

**Executive Summary**

The Lower Mekong delta is highly vulnerable to climate change, facing severe water scarcity and rising temperatures. This brief explores the potential of cool roofs as a cost-effective solution to mitigate urban heat islands and support adaptation targets in Nationally Determined Contributions (NDCs). By integrating cool roof technology with existing infrastructure, cities can reduce energy consumption, improve public health, and enhance resilience to climate-related disasters.

**Section 1: Introduction to Water Scarcity in the Lower Mekong Delta**

The Lower Mekong delta is a critical region for agriculture, supporting a significant portion of Vietnam's rice production. Climate change has led to increased water scarcity, affecting crop yields and food security. Rising temperatures have also exacerbated urban heat islands.

**Actions/Recommendations:**

1. **Develop and implement cool roof policies**: Cities should develop and implement policies to promote the use of cool roofs in new buildings and retrofit existing infrastructure.
2. **Invest in micro-hydro technology**: Governments and private sector investors should support the development and deployment of micro-hydro systems for irrigation efficiency.
3. **Promote agroforestry practices**: Agricultural extension services and research institutions should promote agroforestry practices among farmers, highlighting their benefits for soil health, biodiversity, and climate resilience.

**Closing Outlook**

The Lower Mekong delta faces significant challenges in adapting to climate change. However, by integrating cool roof technology with existing infrastructure and promoting agroforestry-micro-hydro systems, cities can reduce urban heat islands, improve public health, and enhance resilience to climate-related disasters.
