# Agroforestry-Based Micro-Hydro Systems for Climate-Resilient Irrigation in the Sudano-Sahelian Belt

## Executive Summary

The Sudano-Sahelian belt is one of the most water-scarce regions globally, facing severe climate-related challenges that threaten food security and economic stability. This brief presents an integrated approach to addressing water scarcity through agroforestry-based micro-hydro systems for irrigation.

## Addressing Water Scarcity in the Sudano-Sahelian Belt

### Introduction to Climate Change Impacts

The Sudano-Sahelian belt is characterized by erratic rainfall patterns, with projected increases in temperature and variability in precipitation patterns. The consequences are severe droughts, reduced crop yields, and increased poverty.

### Current Irrigation Practices and Challenges

Existing irrigation systems in the Sudano-Sahelian belt rely heavily on boreholes and canals, resulting in significant water losses through evaporation (30%)[verify] and seepage (5%)[verify]. These inefficiencies exacerbate water scarcity, strain local resources, and undermine food security.

### Micro-Hydro Systems for Irrigation Efficiency

Micro-hydro technology offers a sustainable solution for irrigation efficiency by harnessing the energy potential of small-scale hydroelectric systems. These systems reduce reliance on diesel generators and provide a reliable source of renewable energy. Pilot projects in the region have demonstrated cost-effectiveness, with payback periods as short as 5 years.

## Integration of Agroforestry and Micro-Hydro in Climate-Resilient Water Supply

Pilot projects integrating agroforestry and micro-hydro systems have shown promising results. These integrated systems not only improve irrigation efficiency but also provide a reliable source of renewable energy, reducing reliance on fossil fuels and decreasing greenhouse gas emissions.

## Policy Recommendations and Future Research Directions

To support adoption at scale, policymakers should:

1.  Develop policies that incentivize investment in agroforestry-based micro-hydro systems.
2.  Provide training and capacity-building programs for farmers and technicians on integrated system design and maintenance.
3.  Establish monitoring and evaluation frameworks to track the effectiveness of these solutions.
