**Executive Summary**

The Central African Rainforest is facing severe water scarcity due to climate change, affecting agriculture and food security in vulnerable communities. Traditional irrigation methods are energy-intensive and costly, exacerbating economic challenges for smallholder farmers. Integrating agroforestry with micro-hydro systems can provide a sustainable solution that enhances both water security and renewable energy use.

**Introduction to Water Scarcity in the Central African Rainforest**

The Central African Rainforest is one of the world's most biodiverse regions, supporting a significant portion of global agricultural production. Climate change is having a devastating impact on agriculture in this region, including erratic rainfall patterns and increased evaporation from its vast wetlands. This has resulted in severe water scarcity, affecting not only food security but also economic stability for smallholder farmers.
