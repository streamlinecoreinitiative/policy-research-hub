# flood-resilient transport corridors in the Sahel: safeguarding freshwater ecosystems

**Enhancing Water Security, Clean Energy Access, and Climate Resilience in Sub-Saharan Africa**

## Executive Summary

To address the pressing challenges of water security, clean energy access, and climate adaptation in Sub-Saharan Africa, we recommend implementing microgrids for rural clinics and solar cold-chains for fisheries. These interventions will improve energy access, reduce economic losses from climate shocks, and enhance resilience to environmental stressors.

Key findings:

* Only 32.4% of the region's population has access to safely managed drinking water (World Bank, 2024).
* Electricity access is limited, with only 53.3% of the population having access (World Bank, 2023).
* Renewable energy consumption stands at just 19.7%, significantly below global targets and crucial for reducing carbon emissions associated with climate change.

## Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa faces significant environmental challenges due to its diverse ecosystems and climate-induced water scarcity. The region's vast biodiversity is threatened by increased competition for resources among communities, especially in arid regions. Climate change exacerbates existing environmental stressors like droughts and floods.
