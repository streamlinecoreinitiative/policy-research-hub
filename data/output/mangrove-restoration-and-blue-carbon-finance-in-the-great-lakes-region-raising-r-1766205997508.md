# Mangrove Restoration and Blue Carbon Finance in the Great Lakes Region: Enhancing Resilience through Sustainable Solutions

## Executive Summary

The Great Lakes region is home to numerous mangrove forests that provide essential ecosystem services, including carbon sequestration, shoreline protection, and fisheries support. However, these critical habitats are under threat from climate change, deforestation, and pollution, compromising the livelihoods of communities dependent on them.

## Environmental Resilience in Lower-Income Countries: Water Security, Clean Energy, and Climate Adaptation

### Introduction to Water Scarcity in the Great Lakes Region

The region is experiencing severe water scarcity due to climate change. Traditional irrigation methods are energy-intensive and costly, exacerbating economic challenges for smallholder farmers.

### Current Irrigation Practices and Challenges

Existing irrigation systems suffer from significant water loss through evaporation and seepage. This inefficiency wastes precious water resources and increases energy consumption.

### Integration of Mangrove Restoration and Micro-Hydro in Climate-Resilient Water Supply

Pilot projects have demonstrated the combined use of mangrove restoration and micro-hydro systems, showcasing improved water retention, reduced erosion, and enhanced energy efficiency. Economic analysis indicates that these integrated solutions are cost-effective.
