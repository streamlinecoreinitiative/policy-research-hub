# climate-resilient school feeding supply chains in the Great Lakes region: safeguarding freshwater ecosystems

**Climate-Resilient School Feeding Supply Chains in the Great Lakes Region: Safeguarding Freshwater Ecosystems**

Executive Summary:

The Great Lakes region faces significant environmental challenges due to climate change, including water scarcity and reduced crop yields. This brief proposes integrating agroforestry-based micro-hydro systems for climate-resilient irrigation in the Andean Highlands. This approach combines traditional farming practices with modern technology to improve water management and increase agricultural productivity.

**Section 1: Introduction to Water Scarcity in the Andean Highlands**

The Andean Highlands experience severe water scarcity due to historical droughts and limited access to reliable irrigation systems. Farmers rely heavily on traditional methods, which are inefficient and unsustainable. Economic pressures exacerbate existing challenges, leading to reduced crop yields and financial instability.

**Section 2: Current Practices and Challenges**

Existing irrigation systems in the Andean Highlands have limitations, including high dependency on traditional methods and limited efficiency and sustainability. The economic impacts of water scarcity are significant, with reduced crop yields contributing to financial instability.

**Section 3: Agroforestry's Role in Addressing Water Scarcity**

Agroforestry practices offer several benefits for addressing water scarcity in the Andean Highlands:

* Improved water retention and recharge through increased vegetation cover
* Enhanced ecosystem services such as pollination, pest control, and carbon sequestration

Case studies from other regions demonstrate the effectiveness of agroforestry-based micro-hydro systems. Integrating agroforestry practices can lead to increased agricultural productivity due to improved soil health and water management.

**Section 4: Micro-Hydro Systems for Efficiency**

Micro-hydro systems offer several benefits for irrigation, including reduced water usage compared to traditional methods. These systems can be integrated with agroforestry practices to enhance resilience and reduce environmental degradation.

**Actions/Recommendations:**

1. **Develop public-private partnerships** to support project development and implementation.
2. **Establish regulatory frameworks** that encourage the adoption of climate-resilient irrigation systems.
3. **Involve local communities** in decision-making processes to ensure ownership and maintenance of integrated agroforestry-micro-hydro systems.

Closing Outlook:

Climate change poses significant challenges for water security, clean energy, and climate adaptation in lower-income countries. The Andean Highlands are particularly vulnerable due to severe water scarcity and reduced crop yields. Integrating agroforestry-based micro-hydro systems offers a promising solution for addressing these issues.
