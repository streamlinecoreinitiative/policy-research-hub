# Enhancing Resilience in the Sudano-Sahelian Belt: Leveraging Early-Warning Systems and Agroforestry-Micro-Hydro Integration

## Executive Summary

The Sudano-Sahelian belt is highly vulnerable to climate change, facing severe water scarcity, droughts, and heatwaves. This brief highlights the importance of early-warning systems and last-mile alerts in enhancing community resilience in this region. By integrating agroforestry and micro-hydro systems for climate-resilient irrigation, communities can adapt to changing weather patterns.

## Introduction to Water Scarcity

Climate change has significantly impacted the Sudano-Sahelian belt, leading to erratic rainfall patterns, droughts, and heatwaves. Traditional irrigation methods are energy-intensive and costly.

## Agroforestry's Role in Climate Resilience

Agroforestry has been shown to improve soil health, biodiversity, and carbon sequestration. In the Sudano-Sahelian belt, agroforestry can increase soil organic matter by 2-8% [verify], biodiversity by 15-30 native species [verify], and carbon sequestration by 3-7 tons/ha/year [verify].

## Micro-Hydro Systems for Irrigation Efficiency

Micro-hydro systems provide sustainable and renewable energy, generating power in the range of 10-50 kW per system with an efficiency rate of 70-80% [verify]. Compared to traditional irrigation methods, micro-hydro systems offer significant cost savings.

## Integration of Agroforestry and Micro-Hydro in Climate-Resilient Water Supply

The integration of agroforestry and micro-hydro systems offers a cost-effective solution for addressing severe water scarcity in the Sudano-Sahelian belt. The total project cost per hectare ranges from $10,000-$25,000 [verify], with a return on investment (ROI) of 3-5 years [verify].
