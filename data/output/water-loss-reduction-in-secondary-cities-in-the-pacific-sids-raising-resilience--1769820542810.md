## Executive Summary

To address the triple threat of water scarcity, energy poverty, and climate vulnerability in Sub-Saharan Africa, we recommend that governments prioritize investments in desalination plants and rainwater harvesting projects to expand access to safely managed drinking water. This should be complemented by introducing feed-in tariffs to encourage private sector investment in renewable energy projects, particularly solar and wind farms. Additionally, developing climate change adaptation plans at the national and regional levels is crucial for building climate-resilient infrastructure.

## Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan African countries face significant challenges related to water security, clean energy, and climate adaptation. With only 32.4% of the population having access to safely managed drinking water (World Bank, 2024), improving this metric is critical for health and economic development. ## Analysis

### Water Security

The lack of access to safe drinking water in Sub-Saharan Africa is a major public health concern (UN-Water/WHO, 2022). To address this, governments should prioritize the construction and maintenance of improved water supply systems. Investments in rainwater harvesting and desalination plants can also help increase water availability during droughts.

### Clean Energy

Renewable energy sources like solar, wind, and hydro offer viable alternatives to traditional fossil fuels. However, Sub-Saharan Africa's current reliance on 53.3% renewable energy consumption is insufficient (World Bank, 2024). Implementing policies that incentivize the adoption of clean energy technologies can accelerate this transition.

### Climate Adaptation

Climate change exacerbates water scarcity and energy poverty in Sub-Saharan Africa. Building climate-resilient infrastructure such as flood barriers and drought-resistant crops can help mitigate these impacts (NOAA, 2024). Additionally, promoting early warning systems for extreme weather events can save lives and reduce economic losses.

## Policy Recommendations

1. **Expand access to improved water supply systems**: Invest in desalination plants and rainwater harvesting projects to increase water availability during droughts.
2. **Promote renewable energy adoption**: Introduce feed-in tariffs to encourage private sector investment in solar and wind farms, aiming for at least [VERIFY] % of total final energy consumption from renewables by 2030.
3. **Develop climate change adaptation plans**: Allocate dedicated funds for climate-resilient infrastructure projects such as flood barriers and drought-resistant crops.

## Implementation Considerations

Securing adequate funding is crucial but challenging given limited public budgets. Collaborate with international development partners, multilateral organizations, and philanthropic foundations to secure additional resources. Train local communities on the use of advanced water purification technologies and renewable energy systems.

## Conclusion

Addressing water security, clean energy, and climate resilience in Sub-Saharan Africa requires a multi-faceted approach that combines policy interventions with community engagement. By prioritizing access to safe drinking water, promoting the use of renewable energy sources, and building climate-resilient infrastructure, policymakers can create a more sustainable future for millions living in poverty.
