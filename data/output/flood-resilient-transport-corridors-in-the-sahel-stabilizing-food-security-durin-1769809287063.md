**Bridging Water Security, Clean Energy, and Climate Adaptation in Sub-Saharan Africa: A Path to Resilient Development**

### Executive Summary

To address the pressing needs of Sub-Saharan Africa, we recommend investing in sustainable water supply systems powered by renewable energy sources, such as rainwater harvesting and boreholes. This initiative should be implemented in conjunction with community-based water management initiatives to ensure equitable access. By doing so, we can improve water security, enhance clean energy generation, and build climate-resilient infrastructure.

### Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa is one of the most vulnerable regions to climate change impacts, including droughts, floods, and rising temperatures (World Bank Open Data). The region's limited progress towards achieving Sustainable Development Goal 6 (SDG 6) on water security highlights the urgent need for innovative policies. Improved water supply and energy access are crucial for health outcomes, economic growth, and social stability.

### Policy Recommendations

To address the pressing needs of Sub-Saharan Africa, we propose the following:

1. **Increase Access to Safe Drinking Water**:
	* Invest in sustainable water supply systems such as rainwater harvesting and boreholes powered by renewable energy sources.
	* Implement community-based water management initiatives to ensure equitable access.
2. **Enhance Renewable Energy Generation**:
	* Develop and deploy solar-powered mini-grids to provide electricity to remote communities, reducing reliance on diesel generators.
	* Promote hybrid systems combining solar panels with small-scale hydroelectricity or wind power for off-grid areas.
3. **Implement Climate Resilient Infrastructure**:
	* Build water storage facilities such as rainwater harvesting ponds and reservoirs capable of withstanding extreme weather events like floods and droughts.
	* Integrate climate-resilient materials in infrastructure projects to withstand rising temperatures and reduce energy consumption.
