# Executive Summary

**Climate Resilient Water Security, Clean Energy Access, and Climate Adaptation: Strategic Interventions for Lower-Income Countries**

### Executive Summary

Lower-income countries in sub-Saharan Africa face severe water scarcity, limited electricity access, and climate-induced vulnerabilities. To address these issues, we recommend investing $15 billion over five years to achieve the following objectives:

* Increase clean energy access by 2030 through solar, wind, and hydro projects, targeting rural areas with microgrids and off-grid solutions (World Bank, 2025).
* Enhance water supply security by implementing large-scale water storage facilities, desalination plants, and drip irrigation systems to improve access in arid regions (UN-Water/WHO, 2022).
* Develop and fund climate adaptation programs focusing on early warning systems, disaster-resistant infrastructure, and crop diversification (World Bank, 2024).

### Context & Challenge

Lower-income countries struggle with inadequate infrastructure, leading to widespread water scarcity, limited electricity access, and increased vulnerability to climate impacts. Sub-Saharan Africa faces significant gaps in these basic services:

### Policy Recommendations

1. **Invest in Renewable Energy**: Prioritize solar, wind, and hydro projects to increase clean energy access by 2030 (World Bank, 2025). Target rural areas with microgrids and off-grid solutions.
2. **Enhance Water Supply Security**: Implement large-scale water storage facilities, desalination plants, and drip irrigation systems to improve access in arid regions (UN-Water/WHO, 2022).
3. **Climate Resilience Programs**: Develop and fund climate adaptation programs focusing on early warning systems, disaster-resistant infrastructure, and crop diversification (World Bank, 2024).

### Implementation Considerations

* Estimated total investment of $15 billion over five years to achieve targets (World Bank, 2026).

### Conclusion

Addressing water security, clean energy access, and climate adaptation requires bold and coordinated action by governments, NGOs, and the private sector. Immediate investments can not only improve lives but also contribute to global sustainability goals.

**Call to Action**: Governments and development partners should prioritize this agenda in their next fiscal years with dedicated resources and partnerships. Next steps include detailed planning, stakeholder consultations, and policy advocacy efforts.
