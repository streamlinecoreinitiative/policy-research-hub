# Nature-Based Solutions for Landslide Risk in the Central America Dry Corridor: Stabilizing Food Security During Droughts

## Executive Summary

The Central America dry corridor is highly vulnerable to climate change, experiencing frequent droughts and landslides that severely impact food security. This brief proposes an integrated approach to address water scarcity, clean energy, and climate adaptation through nature-based solutions. Specifically, it focuses on agroforestry-based micro-hydro systems for climate-resilient irrigation in the Andean Highlands.

## Current Challenges and Opportunities

### Water Scarcity in the Andean Highlands

The Andean Highlands are prone to erratic rainfall patterns due to climate change, resulting in severe water scarcity. Existing irrigation systems suffer from high evaporation and seepage losses, exacerbating the problem.

### Agroforestry's Role in Climate Resilience

Agroforestry has been shown to improve soil health, biodiversity, and carbon sequestration, while also reducing erosion and improving water retention [verify]. Case studies demonstrate the effectiveness of agroforestry systems in improving water availability and reducing landslide risk.

### Micro-Hydro Systems for Efficiency

Micro-hydro technology offers a promising solution to reduce reliance on diesel generators and increase energy efficiency. Small-scale hydroelectric systems can be integrated with agroforestry practices to provide clean energy for irrigation [verify].
