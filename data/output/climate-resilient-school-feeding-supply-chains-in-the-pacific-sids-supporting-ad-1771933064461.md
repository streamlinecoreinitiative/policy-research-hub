## Context & Challenge

Lower-income countries are disproportionately affected by water scarcity, lack of clean drinking water, and inadequate access to electricity. These issues compound the impacts of climate change, including more frequent droughts and extreme weather events. According to the World Bank (2024), Sub-Saharan Africa has only [32.4%] access to safely managed drinking water, compared to 73.7% globally. Similarly, the region lags in electricity access with just [53.3%] coverage (World Bank, 2023), versus 91.6% worldwide.

## Policy Recommendations

1. **Water Security**:
	* Invest in rainwater harvesting systems, boreholes, and water purification technologies to improve access to safe drinking water.
	* Implement water pricing reforms that encourage efficient use of resources while ensuring affordability for vulnerable populations.
2. **Clean Energy**:
	* Prioritize renewable energy projects such as solar panels, small hydro plants, and wind turbines in rural areas where grid connections are limited.
	* Encourage public-private partnerships to finance and manage these initiatives, leveraging grants and concessional loans from international development banks.
3. **Climate Adaptation**:
	* Develop early warning systems for climate-related disasters like floods and droughts using satellite imagery and ground-based sensors.
	* Implement land use planning that incorporates climate resilience measures such as afforestation and agroforestry to mitigate the impacts of climate change on freshwater ecosystems.

## Implementation Considerations

Initial investments in water security and clean energy projects can be substantial, but long-term savings from reduced healthcare costs due to improved sanitation and increased productivity will offset these expenses. Political instability, corruption, and limited technical capacity may hinder implementation. Addressing these issues through targeted training programs and anti-corruption measures is crucial.

## Conclusion

Addressing water security, clean energy, and climate adaptation in lower-income countries requires concerted efforts by governments, NGOs, and development partners. By adopting the recommendations outlined here, stakeholders can enhance resilience and improve living standards for vulnerable populations.
