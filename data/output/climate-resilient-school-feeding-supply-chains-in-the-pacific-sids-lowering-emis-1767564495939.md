# climate-resilient school feeding supply chains in the Pacific SIDS: lowering emissions from critical sectors

**Climate-Resilient School Feeding Supply Chains in Pacific SIDS: Enhancing Sustainability and Reducing Emissions**

**Executive Summary**

The Pacific Small Island Developing States (SIDS) face significant environmental challenges, including water scarcity and limited access to clean energy. Climate change exacerbates these issues, threatening food security and economic development. This brief proposes an integrated approach to address climate resilience in school feeding supply chains, focusing on sustainable agriculture practices and renewable energy.

**Section 1: Addressing Water Scarcity through Sustainable Agriculture**

Water scarcity is a pressing issue in the Pacific SIDS, with erratic rainfall patterns and limited water resources. Traditional irrigation methods are energy-intensive and costly, contributing to greenhouse gas emissions. Agroforestry-based micro-hydro systems offer a sustainable solution by integrating renewable energy with efficient irrigation practices.

**Section 2: Climate Resilience and Renewable Energy**

Agroforestry improves soil health, biodiversity, and carbon sequestration. Micro-hydro systems provide sustainable and renewable energy. Integrated agroforestry-micro-hydro systems can enhance irrigation efficiency.

**Section 3: Policy Recommendations and Future Research Directions**

Policy measures can create a supportive environment for climate-resilient school feeding supply chains. Subsidies for farmers, grid integration with micro-hydro systems, and infrastructure development are essential for long-term sustainability.

* **Key Data Points:**
	+ Subsidies for Farmers: Incentives provided to encourage adoption of agroforestry-micro-hydro systems
	+ Grid Integration: Grid integration with micro-hydro systems to enhance reliability and efficiency
	+ Infrastructure Development: Roads, storage facilities, and other infrastructure improvements
