# nature-based solutions for landslide risk in the Mekong basin: lowering emissions from critical sectors

**Bridging Water Security, Clean Energy, and Climate Adaptation in Sub-Saharan Africa: A Path to Resilient Development**

## Executive Summary

To address the severe gaps in access to safe drinking water (32.4%) [1] and electricity (53.3%) [2] in Sub-Saharan Africa, governments and development partners must prioritize a holistic approach that integrates renewable energy sources with sustainable water management practices.

## Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa faces significant challenges in water security and energy access. According to World Bank data, only 32.4% of the population has access to safely managed drinking water [1], while just 53.3% can access electricity [2]. Climate change exacerbates these existing challenges, necessitating multifaceted interventions that address both short-term needs and long-term sustainability.

## Analysis

The region's reliance on traditional energy sources like coal and diesel generators contributes to environmental degradation and health risks. For instance, the lack of reliable power leads to inefficient water treatment facilities, increasing costs and reducing quality [3]. Inadequate water management practices also contribute to water scarcity, which is exacerbated by climate change-induced variability in precipitation patterns.

## Policy Recommendations

1. **Enhanced Water Security**:
	* Implement watershed co-management programs with indigenous councils to protect freshwater ecosystems.
	* Promote micro-insurance schemes for smallholder farmers who face drought risks and other climate-related shocks.
2. **Clean Energy Transition**:
	* Invest in solar-powered water pumping systems that can be installed across arid regions where traditional energy sources are scarce or unreliable.
	* Develop agroforestry value chains to produce bioenergy crops, which can serve as a reliable source of clean energy while also improving soil health and biodiversity.
3. **Climate Adaptation Measures**:
	* Invest in climate-smart irrigation cooperatives that use renewable energy for water pumping and distribution systems.
	* Encourage the adoption of rainwater harvesting ponds to manage seasonal variations in precipitation without compromising existing infrastructure.

## Implementation Considerations

The cost of implementing these measures ranges from $50 million to $100 million per year, depending on scale and scope. However, these investments are crucial for building resilient communities that can withstand future climate-related challenges. Potential barriers include resistance from traditional energy providers who may resist changes to their business models, as well as limited financing opportunities due to high upfront costs.

## Conclusion

Sub-Saharan Africa must adopt a holistic approach to address water security, clean energy, and climate adaptation. By leveraging renewable energy sources and sustainable water management practices, the region can build resilient communities that are better equipped to cope with future environmental challenges. Policymakers should prioritize these interventions as part of broader efforts to achieve SDG 6 (Clean Water and Sanitation) and accelerate progress towards a more sustainable development pathway.
