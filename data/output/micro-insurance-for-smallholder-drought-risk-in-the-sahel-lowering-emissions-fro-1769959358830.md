# micro-insurance for smallholder drought risk in the Sahel: lowering emissions from critical sectors

**Bridging Water Security, Clean Energy, and Climate Resilience in Sub-Saharan Africa: A Comprehensive Policy Blueprint**

## Executive Summary

To address the pressing issues of water scarcity, limited access to clean energy, and heightened vulnerability to climate change impacts in Sub-Saharan Africa, this policy blueprint recommends increasing investment in small-scale water infrastructure and promoting renewable energy adoption. Specifically, we propose:

* Increasing investment in rainwater harvesting ponds and other low-cost solutions to improve access to clean water for rural populations who lack piped supplies.
* Promoting solar-powered wells and similar technologies to increase electricity access while reducing reliance on fossil fuels.

## Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa is one of the most vulnerable regions globally to climate change impacts, including droughts, floods, and erratic rainfall patterns. Despite significant progress in some areas, widespread challenges remain in ensuring basic water security and electricity access. For instance, only 32.4% of Sub-Saharan Africans have access to safely managed drinking water [Source: World Bank Open Data, 2024], compared to a global average of 73.7% [Source: World Bank Open Data, 2024]. Similarly, the region lags behind globally with just 53.3% of its population accessing electricity [Source: World Bank Open Data, 2023].

## Policy Recommendations

To enhance water security, we recommend:

1. **Increase investment in small-scale water infrastructure**: Implement rainwater harvesting ponds and other low-cost solutions to improve access to clean water.
2. **Promote renewable energy adoption**: Invest in solar-powered wells and similar technologies to increase electricity access while reducing reliance on fossil fuels.

To expand clean energy access, consider the following strategies:

1. **Invest in grid-connected renewable energy projects**: Develop large-scale solar and wind farms to provide reliable power supply.
2. **Support off-grid renewable energy solutions**: Encourage decentralized systems like biogas digesters and small hydroelectric plants for rural areas.

For climate resilience, here are some key recommendations:

1. **Enhance early warning systems**: Establish robust weather forecasting networks to issue timely alerts on heatwaves and other extreme events.
2. **Implement nature-based solutions**: Invest in afforestation projects and green infrastructure to protect water resources and reduce the risk of landslides.

## Implementation Considerations

Implementation will involve overcoming several challenges, including resource constraints and uneven institutional capacity across different regions and sectors. Therefore, it is crucial to develop targeted training programs for local officials and community leaders to ensure successful implementation of these interventions.

## Conclusion

Addressing water security, clean energy access, and climate resilience requires concerted efforts from governments, NGOs, and development partners. This policy blueprint offers a roadmap that addresses the unique challenges faced by Sub-Saharan Africa while leveraging available resources effectively. By adopting these recommendations, stakeholders can contribute to achieving SDG 6 and other related goals, thereby improving lives in this region.
