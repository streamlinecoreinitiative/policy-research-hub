## Executive Summary

To address water security, clean energy access, and climate resilience challenges in lower-income countries, we recommend investing in flood-resilient transport corridors in the Horn of Africa. This initiative will not only reduce greenhouse gas emissions but also improve transportation infrastructure, enhance economic growth, and protect lives and livelihoods from extreme weather events.

## Context & Challenge

The Horn of Africa is one of the most vulnerable regions to climate change, with frequent droughts and floods affecting millions of people. According to World Bank data (2024), only 32.4% of Sub-Saharan Africa's population has access to safely managed drinking water [Source: World Bank Open Data]. Climate change exacerbates these challenges by increasing extreme weather events like droughts and floods.

## Analysis

The region's transportation infrastructure is critical for economic development, but it is also vulnerable to climate-related disasters. A study by the World Bank found that investing in flood-resilient transport corridors can reduce greenhouse gas emissions [Source: World Bank Report, 2022]. Moreover, a report by the African Development Bank highlighted the need for climate-resilient infrastructure projects, including sea walls and flood barriers, to protect coastal communities [African Development Bank Report, 2023].
