# Ensuring Water Security, Clean Energy, and Climate Resilience in Sub-Saharan Africa

**Ensuring Water Security, Clean Energy, and Climate Resilience in Sub-Saharan Africa**

### Executive Summary

This policy brief highlights the urgent need to address water security and clean energy access in Sub-Saharan Africa, where only 32.4% of the population has access to safely managed drinking water [1] and less than half have reliable electricity [2]. To enhance resilience against future climate shocks, we recommend implementing targeted policies to improve access to electricity and safe drinking water while promoting renewable energy use.

### Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa faces significant challenges related to water security and clean energy access. The region's population growth is projected to increase by over 50% by 2050 [3], exacerbating existing resource constraints. Climate change further threatens water availability, with projections indicating a decrease in rainfall in some areas [4]. These issues not only impact daily lives but also hinder economic growth and sustainable development goals.

### Analysis

Recent data from the World Bank indicates that Sub-Saharan Africa's access to safe drinking water is among the lowest globally. The region lags behind other continents in terms of renewable energy consumption at just 9.2% [5]. These statistics underscore the need for urgent action to enhance resilience and ensure sustainable development.
