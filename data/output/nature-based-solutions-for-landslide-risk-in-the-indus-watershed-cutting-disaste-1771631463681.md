## Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa faces significant challenges in ensuring water security, clean energy access, and climate adaptation. With only 32.4% of its population having access to safely managed drinking water (World Bank Open Data, 2024), and 53.3% relying on substandard or no electricity (World Bank Open Data, 2023), the region's infrastructure is severely underdeveloped. Climate change poses additional threats, with frequent droughts and floods leading to significant water shortages and soil degradation.

The current state of water security in Sub-Saharan Africa is dire: less than [VERIFY] 10% of its freshwater resources are available for withdrawal (World Bank Open Data, 2021). Climate change impacts are exacerbating existing vulnerabilities. Recent data indicates that Sub-Saharan Africa experiences frequent droughts and floods, leading to significant water shortages and soil degradation. These conditions not only impede daily living but also hinder economic growth and sustainable development.
