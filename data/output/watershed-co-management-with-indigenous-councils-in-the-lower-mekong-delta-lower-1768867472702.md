**Renewing Water Security, Lighting Africa’s Future with Clean Energy and Climate Adaptation**

## Executive Summary

Sub-Saharan Africa faces a critical triple threat of water scarcity, energy poverty, and climate vulnerability. This policy brief highlights specific interventions to bolster resilience in these areas. Our key finding is that the region has limited access to safe drinking water (32.4%) and electricity (53.3%), posing significant health and economic challenges [1]. We recommend implementing community-based water supply systems, promoting solar-powered mini-grids for off-grid areas, and increasing public-private partnerships to finance clean energy projects.

## Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa struggles with inadequate infrastructure and resources, particularly in rural and remote regions. Water scarcity affects 32.4% of the population [1], while only 53.3% have access to electricity [2]. Climate change intensifies these challenges, leading to increased droughts and floods. The region is highly vulnerable, with limited adaptive capacity.

## Analysis

### Water Security

Sub-Saharan Africa’s water scarcity affects approximately 20 million people who are chronically undernourished [Source: FAO] [3]. Water stress hinders agricultural productivity and public health. According to the World Bank Open Data, freshwater withdrawal accounts for 9.2% of resources globally [4].

### Clean Energy

Renewable energy consumption remains low at 19.7%, despite potential for significant growth through solar mini-grids [5]. This limits economic development and exacerbates poverty in off-grid areas.

### Climate Adaptation

Climate change impacts, including more frequent droughts and floods, threaten water supply and energy infrastructure. These challenges necessitate immediate action to enhance resilience.

## Policy Recommendations

1. **Community-Based Water Supply Systems**:
	* Invest in decentralized water treatment plants that can serve rural communities effectively.
	* Implement small-scale solar-powered pumps for irrigation, improving agricultural productivity while reducing water demand.
2. **Promote Solar-Powered Mini-Grids**:
	* Develop and finance mini-grids to electrify off-grid areas, ensuring access to lighting, basic services, and energy-efficient appliances.
	* Encourage public-private partnerships (PPP) through grants and tax incentives for project development.
3. **Increase Renewable Energy Consumption**:
	* Support the installation of solar panels on schools and hospitals to serve as community hubs, promoting education and healthcare in remote areas.
	* Offer subsidies or low-interest loans to households and small businesses for rooftop solar installations.
