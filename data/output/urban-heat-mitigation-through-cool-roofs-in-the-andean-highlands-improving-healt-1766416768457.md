# Agroforestry-Based Micro-Hydro Systems for Climate-Resilient Irrigation in the Mesoamerican Highlands

## Executive Summary

The Mesoamerican Highlands face severe water scarcity and poverty issues exacerbated by climate change impacts on agriculture. This brief explores the potential of agroforestry-based micro-hydro systems to improve irrigation efficiency, reduce reliance on diesel generators, and promote economic benefits and sustainability.

## Introduction to Water Scarcity in the Mesoamerican Highlands

The Mesoamerican region is characterized by a semi-arid climate with limited rainfall patterns. Climate change projections indicate increased drought frequency and severity (NOAA dataset query pending). The agriculture sector is particularly vulnerable due to inefficient irrigation systems that are prone to water loss.

### Current Irrigation Practices and Challenges

Existing irrigation systems in the Mesoamerican Highlands include boreholes and canals, which suffer from significant water losses due to evaporation and seepage. This reduces crop yields and increases energy consumption for pumping and maintenance.

## Micro-Hydro Systems for Irrigation Efficiency

Micro-hydro technology involves the installation of small-scale hydroelectric systems that harness energy from rivers or streams. These systems provide a reliable and renewable source of energy for irrigation pumps, reducing reliance on diesel generators and associated greenhouse gas emissions.
