# Solar Cold-Chain for Fisheries in the Sudano-Sahelian Belt: Supporting Adaptation Targets in NDCs

## Executive Summary

The Sudano-Sahelian belt is highly vulnerable to climate change, facing severe water scarcity and erratic rainfall patterns that threaten food security and economic stability. This brief explores the potential of solar cold-chain technology for fisheries in the region as a means of supporting adaptation targets outlined in Nationally Determined Contributions (NDCs). By integrating renewable energy sources with cold-chain management, this approach can enhance fish quality, reduce post-harvest losses, and improve livelihoods for small-scale fishers.

## Addressing Water Scarcity and Climate Change Impacts

### Introduction to Water Scarcity in the Sudano-Sahelian Belt

The Sudano-Sahelian belt is characterized by a semi-arid climate with limited rainfall, making it one of the most water-scarce regions globally. Climate change has exacerbated this issue, leading to erratic rainfall patterns that further strain water resources.

*   **Climate Change Impacts on Agriculture:** Erratic rainfall patterns and increased temperatures have led to crop failures, reduced yields, and decreased agricultural productivity.
*   **Importance of Resilient Irrigation Methods:** Effective irrigation systems are crucial for maintaining food security and economic stability in the face of climate-related challenges.

### Current Irrigation Practices and Challenges

Existing irrigation practices in the Sudano-Sahelian belt often rely on boreholes and canals, which are prone to water loss through evaporation and seepage. These inefficiencies exacerbate water scarcity and strain already limited resources.

*   **Water Loss through Evaporation and Seepage:** Inefficient irrigation systems result in significant water losses, further straining an already water-scarce environment.
*   **Analysis of Water Use Patterns:** Understanding current water use patterns is essential for developing targeted solutions to address the region's specific challenges.

*   **Benefits of Micro-Hydro:** Reduced energy costs, increased efficiency, and decreased reliance on non-renewable energy sources.
*   **Pilot Projects:** Successful pilot projects demonstrate the potential for scaling up micro-hydro systems to address irrigation needs in the region.
