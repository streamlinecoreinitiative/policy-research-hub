## Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa is one of the most vulnerable regions to climate impacts due to its high population density, limited resources, and already fragile ecosystems. The region faces significant challenges related to water security, clean energy access, and climate adaptation. For instance:

* Only [32.4%](https://data.worldbank.org/indicator/SH.H2O.SAFE.ZS?locations=ZG) of the region's population has access to safely managed drinking water (World Bank Open Data, 2024).
* Sub-Saharan Africa lags behind with only [53.3%](https://data.worldbank.org/indicator/EG.PCAP.ACCS.ZS?locations=ZG) of its population having electricity access (World Bank Open Data, 2023).
* Rising temperatures and increased variability in rainfall patterns threaten agricultural productivity and food security, further straining already limited resources.

Our analysis is based on recent data and trends. We examine the current state of water security, clean energy access, and climate adaptation in Sub-Saharan Africa:
