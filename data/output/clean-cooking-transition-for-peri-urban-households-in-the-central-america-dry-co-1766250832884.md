# Clean Cooking Transition for Peri-Urban Households in the Central America Dry Corridor: Protecting Livelihoods Under Climate Stress

## Executive Summary

The Central America dry corridor is highly vulnerable to climate change, experiencing severe water scarcity and increased frequency of droughts. This brief focuses on transitioning peri-urban households from traditional cooking methods to cleaner alternatives, ensuring food security and economic stability under climate stress.

## Water Security in the Central America Dry Corridor

### Overview of Climate Change Impacts

Climate change is exacerbating droughts and erratic rainfall patterns in the Central America dry corridor, affecting agricultural productivity and food security. Rising temperatures are altering precipitation patterns, leading to increased frequency and severity of droughts.

### Importance of Resilient Irrigation Methods

Resilient irrigation methods are crucial for maintaining food security and economic stability in the region. Traditional irrigation systems often rely on fossil fuels, contributing to greenhouse gas emissions and water pollution.

## Current Cooking Practices and Challenges

### Description of Existing Cooking Systems

Peri-urban households in the Central America dry corridor primarily use traditional cooking methods, including charcoal and wood stoves. These methods are inefficient, polluting, and contribute to climate change.
