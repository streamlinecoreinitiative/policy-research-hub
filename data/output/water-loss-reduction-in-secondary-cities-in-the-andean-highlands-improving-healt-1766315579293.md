# Climate-Resilient Irrigation in Secondary Cities: Integrating Agroforestry and Micro-Hydro Systems for Improved Health Outcomes in Heatwaves

## Executive Summary

The Andean highlands are experiencing severe water scarcity due to climate change-induced erratic rainfall patterns, affecting agriculture and food security. This brief explores the integration of agroforestry and micro-hydro systems as a solution for climate-resilient irrigation in secondary cities in the region.

## Water Scarcity in the Andean Highlands

Climate change is having a significant impact on agriculture in the Andean highlands, leading to erratic rainfall patterns and increased water scarcity. Irrigation systems are often inefficient and prone to water loss through evaporation and seepage [verify].

## Agroforestry's Role in Climate Resilience

Agroforestry integrates trees into farming systems, providing benefits for soil health, biodiversity, and carbon sequestration. Studies have shown that agroforestry systems can improve water retention and reduce erosion [verify]. By incorporating agroforestry into irrigation systems, communities can enhance their resilience to climate-related shocks.

## Micro-Hydro Systems for Irrigation Efficiency

Micro-hydro technology is a small-scale hydroelectric system providing reliable and renewable energy for irrigation. This approach reduces reliance on diesel generators and increases energy efficiency [verify].

## Integration in Climate-Resilient Water Supply

Pilot projects have demonstrated the effectiveness of combining agroforestry and micro-hydro systems for climate-resilient irrigation, reducing water loss and improving energy efficiency.

## Policy Recommendations

To support the adoption of these integrated solutions at scale, we recommend:

1. **Policy incentives**: Governments should provide financial incentives for communities to adopt agroforestry-micro-hydro systems.
2. **Capacity building**: Training programs should be established to build capacity among local farmers and technicians on the design and implementation of these integrated systems.
3. **Research and development**: Further research is needed to validate the effectiveness of these solutions.

## Actions/Recommendations

1. Conduct a thorough analysis of existing irrigation systems in secondary cities in the Andean highlands.
2. Develop a comprehensive plan for integrating agroforestry and micro-hydro systems at scale.
3. Establish partnerships with local governments, NGOs, and community organizations to support the adoption of these integrated solutions.

## Closing Outlook

The integration of agroforestry and micro-hydro systems offers a promising solution for addressing water scarcity in secondary cities in the Andean highlands. By reducing water loss and improving energy efficiency, these integrated solutions can contribute to climate-resilient development.
