# climate-resilient school feeding supply chains in the Indus watershed: improving health outcomes in heatwaves

**Climate-Resilient School Feeding Supply Chains in the Indus Watershed: Improving Health Outcomes in Heatwaves**

**Executive Summary**

The Indus watershed is one of the most water-scarce regions globally, affecting millions of people's livelihoods and health outcomes. Climate change exacerbates this issue, with rising temperatures and changing precipitation patterns threatening food security and increasing heat-related illnesses. This brief explores the potential of agroforestry-based micro-hydro systems to address these challenges in climate-resilient school feeding supply chains.

**Climate-Resilient Water Management: A Critical Component of Sustainable Development**

### Introduction

Water scarcity is a pressing issue in the Indus watershed, with historical droughts and current challenges such as limited access to reliable irrigation systems and economic instability due to reduced crop yields. Farmers and communities rely heavily on traditional methods like surface water and rain-fed systems.

### Current Practices and Challenges

Existing irrigation systems in the region have several limitations:

* High dependency on traditional methods
* Limited efficiency and sustainability
* Economic pressures exacerbate existing challenges
* Reduced crop yields leading to financial instability

**Agroforestry's Role in Addressing Water Scarcity**

Integrating agroforestry practices can address water scarcity by improving soil health, reducing erosion, and increasing water retention.
