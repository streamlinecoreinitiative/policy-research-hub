# Watershed Co-Management with Indigenous Councils in the Sahel: Stabilizing Food Security During Droughts

## Executive Summary

The Sahel region is highly vulnerable to climate change, characterized by frequent droughts and water scarcity. This brief highlights the importance of watershed co-management with indigenous councils as a crucial strategy for stabilizing food security during droughts.

## Introduction

The Sahel region spans across several countries in West Africa, including Senegal, Mali, Burkina Faso, Niger, Chad, Sudan, Eritrea, Ethiopia, Djibouti, Somalia, and Kenya. The region is characterized by a semi-arid climate with limited rainfall, making it prone to droughts and water scarcity.

## Current Challenges and Opportunities

### Context of Water Scarcity:

*   Historical droughts have had a profound impact on the region's agricultural sector.
*   The current challenges faced by farmers and communities include limited access to reliable irrigation systems, economic instability due to reduced crop yields, and increased reliance on traditional methods. [verify]

### Current Practices and Challenges

*   Existing irrigation systems in the Sahel region are often inefficient and unsustainable.
*   Farmers' reliance on traditional methods has led to economic pressures exacerbating existing challenges.

## Watershed Co-Management with Indigenous Councils

### Benefits of Integrating Agroforestry Practices:

*   Improved water retention and recharge through increased vegetation cover
*   Enhanced ecosystem services such as pollination, pest control, and carbon sequestration
*   Increased agricultural productivity due to improved soil health and water management
*   Reduced vulnerability to climate variability

## Integration in Climate-Resilient Water Supply

### How Agroforestry and Micro-Hydro Systems Can Work Together:

*   Reduced environmental degradation through integrated management practices
*   Increased water security by diversifying water sources
*   Reduced risk of crop failure due to climate variability
*   Community empowerment through local ownership and maintenance
