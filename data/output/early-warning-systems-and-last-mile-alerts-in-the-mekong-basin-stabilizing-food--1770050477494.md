# early-warning systems and last-mile alerts in the Mekong basin: stabilizing food security during droughts

**Enhancing Environmental Resilience in Sub-Saharan Africa: Access to Clean Water, Reliable Energy, and Climate Adaptation**

## Executive Summary

Immediate action is required to address the significant gaps in access to clean water and electricity in Sub-Saharan Africa. This policy brief recommends implementing evidence-based interventions focusing on improving water security through sanitation facilities, promoting renewable energy adoption with supportive policies, and enhancing climate resilience via early warning systems and community preparedness programs.

## Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa faces severe infrastructure deficits, particularly concerning access to safe drinking water (32.4%) and electricity (53.3%), exposing millions to health risks and hindering economic development and climate adaptation efforts [1]. The region's climate variability further complicates these challenges by increasing vulnerability to droughts, floods, and other extreme weather events.

## Analysis

- **Water Security**: According to the World Bank data, 73.7% of the world has access to safely managed drinking water (2024), while only 32.4% in Sub-Saharan Africa does so [1]. This disparity is a critical challenge affecting public health and economic growth.
- **Clean Energy Access**: With renewable energy consumption at just 19.7% globally (World Bank, 2020) [2], the situation is even more dire for Sub-Saharan Africa where access remains extremely limited. The region's lack of reliable energy sources hampers development efforts and exacerbates climate vulnerability.
- **Climate Adaptation**: Climate change impacts such as increased frequency and intensity of extreme weather events require robust adaptation strategies. Early warning systems, improved disaster preparedness, and resilient infrastructure are essential for safeguarding communities.

## Policy Recommendations

1. **Enhance Access to Clean Water**:
   - Implement water supply projects in underserved areas.
   - Invest in sanitation facilities (latrines, hand washing stations) to reduce waterborne diseases.
   - Support community-led initiatives like rainwater harvesting and borehole drilling for safe drinking water.

2. **Promote Renewable Energy Adoption**:
   - Develop supportive policies such as subsidies and tax incentives for renewable energy projects.
   - Invest in grid modernization to integrate renewable sources into existing power grids.
   - Encourage private sector investment through PPPs and public-private partnerships (PPPs).

3. **Climate Adaptation Measures**:
   - Establish early warning systems for extreme weather events using satellite data, local meteorological stations, and community-based monitoring networks.
   - Develop climate-resilient infrastructure such as flood barriers, water storage facilities, and drought-resistant crops.
   - Strengthen public health services to address immediate needs during disasters.

## Implementation Considerations

- **Costs**: Initial investments in water supply projects can range from $50-$100 million per project (World Bank estimates) [3]. Renewable energy deployment costs vary but generally start at around $300 per kW of installed capacity.
- **Barriers**: Political opposition, lack of funds, and technical challenges like unreliable grid connections are common hurdles. Addressing these requires collaborative efforts between governments, NGOs, and international partners.

## Conclusion

Sub-Saharan Africa must prioritize enhancing access to clean water, promoting reliable energy sources, and strengthening climate adaptation strategies. These measures not only improve immediate quality of life but also lay the foundation for sustainable development in a region severely impacted by environmental challenges. By adopting evidence-based policies and engaging all stakeholders, governments can significantly reduce vulnerability and foster resilience.
