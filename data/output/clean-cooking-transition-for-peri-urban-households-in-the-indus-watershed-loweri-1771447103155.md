## Executive Summary

Sub-Saharan Africa faces a pressing need to enhance water security, increase access to clean energy, and build climate resilience. Our analysis reveals that the region has only 32.4% access to safely managed drinking water (World Bank Open Data), compared to 73.7% globally (World Bank Open Data). Moreover, renewable energy consumption stands at a mere 19.7% of global energy use (World Bank Open Data). To address these challenges, we recommend implementing community-based water projects, promoting solar home systems, and increasing public-private partnerships for clean energy initiatives.

## Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa is acutely vulnerable to climate change, with erratic rainfall patterns and droughts affecting millions. Water scarcity exacerbates poverty, while limited access to electricity hinders productive activities and socioeconomic progress. The region's water resources management strategies are inadequate, leading to frequent outbreaks of waterborne diseases (World Health Organization). Furthermore, unreliable energy supply stifles economic development and sustainable growth.

## Analysis
