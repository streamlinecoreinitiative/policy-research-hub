### Executive Summary

We recommend that governments in Sub-Saharan Africa prioritize investments in renewable energy projects and climate-resilient infrastructure to enhance water security and promote clean energy adoption. This approach is critical given the region's severe limitations in accessing safe drinking water ([VERIFY] 32.4%) and electricity ([VERIFY] 53.3%), which are exacerbated by climate change-induced droughts, floods, and extreme weather events.

### Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa is home to approximately 1 billion people, with only a third having access to safely managed drinking water ([Source: World Bank Open Data, 2024]) and less than half having access to electricity ([Source: World Bank Open Data, 2023]). According to World Bank data (2024), climate change amplifies these challenges by increasing temperatures and variability of rainfall patterns, leading to more frequent droughts and floods.

### Analysis

The statistics are stark: while [VERIFY] 91.6% of the world population has access to electricity ([Source: World Bank Open Data, 2023]), this figure drops significantly to 53.3% in Sub-Saharan Africa. Similarly, renewable energy consumption is low globally at [VERIFY] 19.7% ([Source: World Bank Open Data, 2020]), but even lower in Sub-Saharan Africa.

In terms of water security, only [Source: World Bank Open Data, 2024] 32.4% of the population has access to safely managed drinking water in Sub-Saharan Africa, compared to [Source: World Bank Open Data, 2024] 73.7% globally. These disparities underscore the urgent need for targeted policies that integrate renewable energy solutions with climate-resilient infrastructure development.

### Policy Recommendations

To address these challenges, we recommend:

1. **Increase investment in renewable energy projects**: Encourage governments to allocate more funds towards solar, wind, and hydroelectric power generation, including incentives for private sector participation.
2. **Develop climate-resilient infrastructure**: Invest in water supply systems that can withstand extreme weather events like droughts and floods, including constructing resilient dams, desalination plants, and ensuring proper drainage systems to manage excess rainfall.
3. **Promote energy-efficient technologies**: Encourage the adoption of solar-powered irrigation pumps, biogas stoves, and other clean energy solutions for cooking and lighting.

### Implementation Considerations

Implementing these policies will require addressing several practical challenges:

* Initial investment costs may be high but can be mitigated through grants, subsidies, and partnerships with international development organizations.
* Public awareness and education are essential to promote widespread participation in clean energy adoption and water conservation efforts.

### Conclusion

Sub-Saharan Africa's struggle for equitable access to safe drinking water and electricity underscores the urgent need for targeted policies that integrate renewable energy solutions. By focusing on climate-resilient infrastructure development and promoting cleaner technologies, we can significantly improve living standards while bolstering regional resilience against climate-induced shocks.
