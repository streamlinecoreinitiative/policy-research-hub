# climate-resilient school feeding supply chains in the Caribbean SIDS: stabilizing food security during droughts

**Climate-Resilient School Feeding Supply Chains in Caribbean SIDS: Enhancing Food Security During Droughts**

**Executive Summary**

The Caribbean Small Island Developing States (SIDS) are highly vulnerable to climate change impacts, including droughts that compromise food security and exacerbate poverty. This brief explores the integration of agroforestry-based micro-hydro systems for climate-resilient irrigation in school feeding supply chains.

**Climate Change Impacts on Water Scarcity in Caribbean SIDS**

The Caribbean region is characterized by high temperatures, variable rainfall patterns, and increasing drought frequency. Droughts have significant impacts on agriculture, including crop failures, reduced yields, and decreased food security. In the context of school feeding programs, drought-induced water scarcity poses a critical challenge to ensuring consistent food supply.

**Agroforestry-Based Micro-Hydro Systems for Climate-Resilient Irrigation**

Agroforestry practices integrate trees into agricultural landscapes, enhancing soil health, biodiversity, and carbon sequestration while improving water retention. Micro-hydro systems harness small-scale hydroelectric power to reduce reliance on diesel generators and increase energy efficiency.

**Benefits of Agroforestry-Micro-Hydro Integration in School Feeding Supply Chains**

1. **Climate Resilience**: Agroforestry-micro-hydro systems can improve irrigation efficiency, reducing water loss percentages from existing systems.
2. **Food Security**: Climate-resilient irrigation ensures consistent food supply during droughts, supporting school feeding programs and community well-being.
3. **Renewable Energy**: Micro-hydro power reduces reliance on diesel generators, decreasing greenhouse gas emissions and promoting sustainable energy adoption.
4. **Economic Benefits**: Agroforestry-micro-hydro projects demonstrate potential for cost-effectiveness and scaling up.

**Policy Recommendations and Future Research Directions**

1. **Develop Policy Frameworks**: Establish policies supporting the integration of agroforestry-based micro-hydro systems in school feeding supply chains.
2. **Capacity Building**: Provide training and technical assistance to farmers, schools, and local authorities on climate-resilient irrigation practices.
3. **Monitoring and Evaluation**: Establish monitoring systems to track project effectiveness, identify areas for improvement, and inform policy decisions.

**Closing Outlook**

The integration of agroforestry-based micro-hydro systems in school feeding supply chains offers a promising solution to address water scarcity and promote sustainable development goals in Caribbean SIDS. By implementing climate-resilient irrigation practices, we can ensure consistent food supply during droughts, reduce greenhouse gas emissions, and support community well-being.
