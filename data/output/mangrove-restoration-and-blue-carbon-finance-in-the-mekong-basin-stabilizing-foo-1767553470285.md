# mangrove restoration and blue carbon finance in the Mekong basin: stabilizing food security during droughts

**Mangrove Restoration and Blue Carbon Finance in the Mekong Basin: Enhancing Ecosystem Resilience and Food Security**

## Executive Summary

The Mekong Basin is one of the most biodiverse regions on the planet, supporting over 60 million people through its rich fisheries and agricultural resources. Climate change has led to increased frequency and severity of droughts, threatening food security and livelihoods in the region. This brief proposes a holistic approach to addressing these challenges by integrating mangrove restoration with blue carbon finance initiatives.

## Section 1: Introduction to Water Scarcity in the Mekong Basin

The Mekong Basin is characterized by a subtropical climate with high precipitation variability. Average annual rainfall ranges from 800-1200 mm [verify]. Traditional irrigation practices are energy-intensive and costly, consuming significant amounts of electricity per borehole pump.

## Section 2: Current Irrigation Practices and Their Limitations

Traditional irrigation methods in the Mekong Basin are often unsustainable and inefficient. Borehole pumps consume large amounts of energy, while canal systems suffer from significant losses due to evaporation and seepage [verify].

## Section 3: Agroforestry's Role in Climate Resilience

Agroforestry practices have been shown to improve soil health, biodiversity, and carbon sequestration. Local surveys and case studies indicate that agroforestry can increase soil organic matter by 2-8% [verify] and biodiversity by 15-30 native species [verify]. These benefits not only enhance ecosystem resilience but also provide economic returns for farmers through improved crop yields and reduced input costs.

## Section 4: Micro-Hydro Systems for Irrigation Efficiency

Micro-hydro systems offer a sustainable and renewable energy solution for irrigation. Power generation ranges from 10-50 kW per system, with energy efficiency rates of 70-80% [verify]. Cost savings are significant compared to traditional irrigation methods.

## Section 5: Integration of Agroforestry and Micro-Hydro in Climate-Resilient Water Supply

Integrating agroforestry and micro-hydro systems can provide a cost-effective solution for climate-resilient irrigation. Total project costs per hectare range from $10,000-$25,000 [verify]. Long-term sustainability assessments are essential to ensure the continued effectiveness of these integrated systems.

## Actions/Recommendations

1. **Develop and implement policies** to support the adoption of agroforestry-micro-hydro systems.
2. **Invest in infrastructure development**, including roads, storage facilities, and other essential infrastructure to support the deployment of micro-hydro systems.
3. **Establish a blue carbon finance mechanism** to generate revenue streams for local communities through the sale of carbon credits.
4. **Conduct further research and monitoring** to evaluate the effectiveness of these integrated systems and identify areas for improvement.

## Closing Outlook

The proposed approach offers a holistic solution to addressing water scarcity, food insecurity, and climate change in the Mekong Basin. By integrating mangrove restoration with blue carbon finance initiatives, we can enhance ecosystem resilience, improve water quality, and generate new revenue streams for local communities.
