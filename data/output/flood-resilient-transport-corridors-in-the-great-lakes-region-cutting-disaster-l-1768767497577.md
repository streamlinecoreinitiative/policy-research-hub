# Flood-Resilient Transport Corridors in the Great Lakes Region: Enhancing Disaster Resilience for Vulnerable Groups

## Executive Summary

The Great Lakes region is prone to severe flooding, posing significant risks to transportation infrastructure and vulnerable communities. This brief presents a comprehensive approach to enhance flood resilience in transport corridors through integrated agroforestry-based micro-hydro systems.

## Current Challenges and Opportunities

### Introduction to Water Scarcity in the Great Lakes Region

The Great Lakes region faces water scarcity due to historical droughts, limited access to reliable irrigation systems, and economic instability. Farmers rely heavily on traditional methods like surface water and rain-fed systems, which are inefficient and unsustainable.

### Current Practices and Challenges

Existing irrigation systems have limitations, including high dependency on traditional methods, limited efficiency, and sustainability. This reliance exacerbates existing challenges, leading to economic pressures and reduced crop yields.

## Agroforestry-Based Micro-Hydro Systems for Climate-Resilient Irrigation

### Benefits of Integrating Agroforestry Practices

Integrating agroforestry practices can improve water retention and recharge through increased vegetation cover. This approach also enhances ecosystem services such as pollination, pest control, and carbon sequestration.

### Case Studies and Examples from Other Regions

Successful projects in similar climates or contexts demonstrate the potential of integrated agroforestry-micro-hydro systems. For example, case studies from Sub-Saharan Africa show increased agricultural productivity due to improved soil health and water management.

## Integration in Climate-Resilient Water Supply

Agroforestry and micro-hydro systems can work together to enhance resilience by reducing environmental degradation through integrated management practices and increasing water security by diversifying water sources. This approach also reduces the risk of crop failure due to climate variability and empowers communities through local ownership and maintenance.
