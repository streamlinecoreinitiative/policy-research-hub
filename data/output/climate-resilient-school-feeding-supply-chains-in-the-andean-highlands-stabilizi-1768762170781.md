# climate-resilient school feeding supply chains in the Andean highlands: stabilizing food security during droughts

**Climate-Resilient School Feeding Supply Chains in the Andean Highlands: Enhancing Water Security and Food Stability**

Executive Summary:

The Andean highlands face severe water scarcity due to historical droughts and limited access to reliable irrigation systems, exacerbating economic instability among farmers. This brief proposes integrating agroforestry-based micro-hydro systems to ensure sustainable water management, economic development, and climate resilience in the region's school feeding supply chains.

**I. Introduction to Water Scarcity in the Andean Highlands**

The Andean highlands are characterized by limited access to reliable irrigation systems, economic instability due to reduced crop yields, and a historical context of droughts. Farmers rely heavily on traditional methods like surface water and rain-fed systems, which are inefficient and unsustainable.

* **Context of Water Scarcity:**
	+ Historical droughts have led to severe water scarcity in the region.
	+ Current challenges include limited access to reliable irrigation systems and economic instability due to reduced crop yields.
* **Current Challenges Faced by Farmers and Communities:**
	+ Limited dependency on traditional methods like surface water and rain-fed systems.
