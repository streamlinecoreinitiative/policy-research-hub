## Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa is one of the regions most vulnerable to climate change impacts, including droughts, floods, and extreme weather events. The region lacks adequate water security and clean energy infrastructure, leading to health crises, economic stagnation, and social unrest.

* Only 32.4% of Sub-Saharan Africa's population has access to safely managed drinking water (World Bank Open Data, 2024), a stark contrast to the global average of 73.7% [Source: World Bank Open Data].
* While renewable energy consumption globally reached 19.7% in 2020 (World Bank Open Data), Sub-Saharan Africa lags far behind with only 53.3% access to electricity (World Bank Open Data, 2023).

## Analysis

The evidence is clear: Sub-Saharan Africa's water security and clean energy challenges are severe. We examine the data:

* Water scarcity: Only 32.4% of Sub-Saharan Africa's population has access to safely managed drinking water [Source: World Bank Open Data].
* Energy poverty: While renewable energy consumption globally reached 19.7% in 2020 (World Bank Open Data), Sub-Saharan Africa lags far behind with only 53.3% access to electricity [Source: World Bank Open Data, 2023].
* Climate vulnerability: The region faces increasing frequency and intensity of climate-related events such as droughts, floods, and cyclones, which further strain water resources and energy systems.

## Policy Recommendations

1. **Water Security**:
	* Invest in desalination plants to provide safe drinking water for coastal communities [NEEDS SOURCE].
	* Implement rainwater harvesting programs to ensure water supply during dry seasons.
2. **Clean Energy**:
	* Promote solar power generation through subsidies and community solar projects, particularly in rural areas with high potential for solar energy (World Bank Open Data, 2023).
	* Develop microgrids that integrate renewable energy sources like solar PV and small wind turbines to enhance grid stability and reliability.
3. **Climate Adaptation**:
	* Implement early warning systems using low-cost weather stations and mobile apps to alert communities of impending climate events [Source: NOAA Climate data].
	* Support drought-resistant crop varieties through research institutions and agricultural extension services.
