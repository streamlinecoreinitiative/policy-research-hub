# Agroforestry-Based Micro-Hydro Systems for Climate-Resilient Irrigation in the Andean Highlands

## Executive Summary

The Andean Highlands face severe water scarcity due to historical droughts, limited access to reliable irrigation systems, and economic instability caused by reduced crop yields. This brief explores the potential of agroforestry-based micro-hydro systems as a solution to address these challenges.

## Addressing Water Scarcity in the Andean Highlands

### Context of Water Scarcity

The Andean Highlands have experienced numerous droughts throughout history, with severe impacts on agriculture and local economies. Current challenges include limited access to reliable irrigation systems and economic instability due to reduced crop yields. Climate change is projected to increase the frequency and severity of droughts in the region.

### Current Challenges Faced by Farmers and Communities

Farmers in the Andean Highlands rely heavily on traditional methods such as surface water and rain-fed irrigation systems, which have limitations in terms of efficiency and sustainability. Economic pressures exacerbate existing challenges, making it difficult for farmers to adapt to changing climate conditions.

## Micro-Hydro Systems for Efficiency

### Overview of Micro-Hydro Systems and Their Potential in Irrigation

Micro-hydro systems have the potential to reduce water usage compared to traditional methods, while also saving energy through reduced consumption. This approach is particularly beneficial in areas with limited access to reliable electricity.

### Benefits Such as Reduced Water Usage, Cost Savings, and Improved Reliability

Micro-hydro systems offer several benefits, including reduced dependency on imported diesel or other fuels, improved reliability, and cost savings through reduced energy consumption.
