# Flood-Resilient Transport Corridors in the Indus Watershed: Supporting Adaptation Targets in NDCs

## Executive Summary

The Indus watershed is a critical region for global food and water security, supporting over 200 million people across four countries: Pakistan, India, China, and Afghanistan. Climate change poses significant challenges to this region's resilience, with rising temperatures, changing precipitation patterns, and increased frequency of extreme weather events affecting agriculture, water resources, and human settlements.

This brief highlights the importance of flood-resilient transport corridors in the Indus watershed as a critical adaptation strategy for achieving Nationally Determined Contributions (NDCs) targets. By leveraging agroforestry-based micro-hydro systems, we can address severe water scarcity and poverty through integrated solutions that ensure sustainable economic benefits.

## Current Situation Analysis

### Water Scarcity and Climate Change Impacts in the Indus Watershed

* The Indus watershed is one of the most densely populated regions globally, with a growing population putting immense pressure on its limited water resources. [verify]
* Climate change is projected to exacerbate water scarcity in the region, with rising temperatures and changing precipitation patterns affecting agricultural productivity, water availability, and human settlements.

### Current Irrigation Systems and Challenges

* Traditional irrigation systems in the Indus watershed are often inefficient, relying heavily on groundwater extraction, which contributes to land subsidence, waterlogging, and salinization.
* Limited access to electricity hinders the adoption of micro-hydro systems, which could provide a reliable source of clean energy for irrigation.

### Socio-Economic Factors Contributing to Water Scarcity

* Poverty and limited economic opportunities in rural areas lead to unsustainable agricultural practices, exacerbating water scarcity.
* Lack of institutional capacity and inadequate governance structures hinder effective management of water resources.

## Agroforestry-Based Micro-Hydro Systems: A Climate-Resilient Solution

### Benefits of Agroforestry Practices

* Agroforestry enhances soil fertility, improves water retention, and promotes biodiversity. [verify]
* Integrating agroforestry with traditional irrigation methods can improve overall system efficiency and sustainability.

### Case Studies and Examples

* Successful integration of agroforestry with micro-hydro systems has been demonstrated in various regions, including the Himalayas and Southeast Asia.
* These case studies highlight the potential synergies between agroforestry and micro-hydro systems in providing sustainable water management solutions.

## Policy Recommendations and Future Research Directions

### Scaling Up Agroforestry-Based Micro-Hydro Systems

* Governments should provide incentives for farmers to adopt agroforestry-based micro-hydro systems, including subsidies for equipment and training.
* NGOs and private sector organizations can collaborate to develop and disseminate best practices for integrating agroforestry with micro-hydro systems.

### Research Agenda

* Further research is needed to improve the design and cost-effectiveness of agroforestry-based micro-hydro systems.
* Studies should focus on identifying optimal crop species, soil types, and climate conditions for these integrated systems.
