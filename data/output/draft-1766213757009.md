### Executive Summary

The Andean Highlands face severe water scarcity due to climate change's erratic rainfall patterns and increased evaporation rates, exacerbating economic instability and food insecurity. This brief explores the integration of agroforestry and micro-hydro systems as a solution for climate-resilient irrigation.

Climate change has significant impacts on agriculture worldwide. In the Andean Highlands, erratic rainfall patterns and increased evaporation rates exacerbate water scarcity, affecting food security and economic stability.

#### Current Irrigation Practices and Challenges

Traditional irrigation systems in the Andean Highlands include boreholes and canals, often leading to significant water loss through evaporation and seepage.

*   **Key challenges:**
    *   Inefficient use of water resources
    *   High energy consumption for pumping and maintenance
    *   Limited scalability due to high upfront costs

#### Micro-Hydro Systems for Irrigation Efficiency

Micro-hydro technology involves small-scale hydroelectric systems that provide clean energy for irrigation, reducing reliance on diesel generators and increasing energy efficiency.

*   **Key benefits:**

    *   Reduced greenhouse gas emissions
    *   Improved water management
