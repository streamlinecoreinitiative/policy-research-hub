# Climate-Resilient Irrigation in the Andean Highlands: Agroforestry-Based Micro-Hydro Systems

## Executive Summary

Climate change is exacerbating water scarcity in the Andean Highlands, threatening food security and economic stability. This brief explores the potential of agroforestry-based micro-hydro systems as a climate-resilient irrigation solution. By integrating agroforestry practices with small-scale hydroelectric systems, farmers can reduce their reliance on diesel generators, increase energy efficiency, and improve water retention.

## Addressing Water Scarcity in the Andean Highlands

Climate change is altering rainfall patterns in the Andean Highlands, leading to severe water scarcity. Existing irrigation systems are inefficient and prone to water loss through evaporation and seepage. Agroforestry practices have been shown to improve soil health, biodiversity, and carbon sequestration.

### Key Benefits of Agroforestry:

• Improved soil health
• Increased biodiversity
• Enhanced carbon sequestration
• Reduced erosion
• Improved water retention

## Micro-Hydro Systems for Efficient Irrigation

Micro-hydro technology is a small-scale hydroelectric system that can provide reliable and renewable energy for irrigation. This approach reduces reliance on diesel generators, which are expensive and contribute to greenhouse gas emissions.

### Key Benefits of Micro-Hydro:

• Reduced reliance on diesel generators
• Increased energy efficiency
• Renewable energy source
• Lower operational costs
