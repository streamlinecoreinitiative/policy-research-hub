Enhancing Water Security, Clean Energy, and Climate Adaptation in Southeast Asia: A Pathway to Resilience

Executive Summary

Southeast Asia is one of the world's most vulnerable regions to climate change impacts, with significant challenges in water security (32% access to safely managed drinking water), clean energy use (<30%), and climate resilience. To address these pressing issues, we recommend strengthening infrastructure, promoting renewable energy integration, and enhancing community adaptation programs.

Context & Challenge

Southeast Asian countries are grappling with rapid population growth and economic development while being highly dependent on natural resources like water and energy. Climate change exacerbates existing vulnerabilities, leading to more frequent extreme weather events that threaten livelihoods and economies. * Access to safely managed drinking water in Southeast Asia is significantly lower than the global average (73.7%, World Bank Open Data).

  • Climate change impacts like increased frequency and intensity of droughts, floods, and sea-level rise are becoming more pronounced.

    Policy Recommendations

    1. Integrate renewable energy into national plans: Increase the share of renewables in total final energy consumption from 28.5% to 30-35%. Implement policies that favor solar, wind, and hydro power.

  1. Develop climate-resilient infrastructure: Invest in water management systems like dams, reservoirs, and desalination plants to enhance water security.
  2. Promote community-based adaptation programs: Encourage local communities to participate in initiatives like mangrove restoration, early warning systems for floods, and improved irrigation techniques.
