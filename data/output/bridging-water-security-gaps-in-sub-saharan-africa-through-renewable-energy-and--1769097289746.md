## Executive Summary

Sub-Saharan Africa faces significant water security challenges, with only 32.4% of households having access to safely managed drinking water (World Bank Open Data, 2024). This gap can be bridged by integrating renewable energy solutions with climate adaptation strategies. Governments should prioritize the deployment of solar, wind, and hydroelectric projects to meet electricity demand sustainably, particularly in rural areas where energy needs are most critical for water supply and sanitation.

**Key Recommendations:**

1. **Increase investment in renewable energy**: Allocate 20% of energy from renewable sources by 2030 (Kenya’s National Climate Change Action Plan) to ensure sustainable development.
2. **Implement rainwater harvesting systems**: Design and implement rainwater harvesting systems in areas where freshwater scarcity is severe, such as coastal regions.
3. **Design climate-resilient infrastructure**: Incorporate flood-resistant designs and drought-tolerant crops in agriculture projects to withstand climate impacts.

Sub-Saharan Africa struggles with inadequate infrastructure, leading to persistent water scarcity and energy deficits (World Bank Open Data, 2024).
