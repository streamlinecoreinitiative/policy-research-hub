# Micro-insurance for Smallholder Drought Risk in the Sahel: Safeguarding Freshwater Ecosystems

## Executive Summary

The Sahel region is highly vulnerable to climate change, experiencing severe droughts and water scarcity. This brief highlights the importance of integrating agroforestry-based micro-hydro systems into irrigation practices for smallholder farmers. By adopting these integrated solutions, communities can enhance their resilience to climate variability, improve agricultural productivity, and reduce economic instability.

## Addressing Water Scarcity in the Sahel

### Introduction to Water Challenges in the Region

The Sahel region faces severe water scarcity due to droughts, limited access to reliable irrigation systems, and economic instability. Farmers rely heavily on traditional methods such as surface water and rain-fed systems, which are inefficient and unsustainable.

### Current Challenges Faced by Farmers and Communities

- **Limited dependency on traditional methods:** High reliance on surface water and rain-fed systems
- **Economic pressures exacerbate existing challenges:** Reduced crop yields leading to financial instability
- **Reduced economic opportunities:** Limited access to reliable irrigation systems, hindering economic growth
