## Executive Summary

The Ganges-Brahmaputra delta is one of the world's most densely populated and ecologically sensitive regions, facing severe drought risks due to climate change. This brief outlines a comprehensive approach to addressing smallholder drought risk through micro-insurance mechanisms, integrating agroforestry-based micro-hydro systems for climate-resilient irrigation.

## Introduction to Water Scarcity

The Ganges-Brahmaputra delta is experiencing water scarcity issues due to climate change, including erratic rainfall patterns and increased evaporation from wetlands. Traditional irrigation methods are energy-intensive and costly, exacerbating economic challenges for smallholder farmers in vulnerable communities.

### Climate Change Impacts

Climate change affects agriculture in the region through erratic rainfall patterns and increased evaporation from wetlands, impacting water availability for crops. This has led to reduced crop yields, decreased income for farmers, and increased food insecurity.
