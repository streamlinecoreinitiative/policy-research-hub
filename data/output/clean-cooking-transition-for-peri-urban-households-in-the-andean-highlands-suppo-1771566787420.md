**Enhancing Water Security, Renewable Energy Adoption, and Climate Resilience in Sub-Saharan Africa**

## Executive Summary

Sub-Saharan Africa faces significant challenges in accessing safe drinking water and reliable electricity, which exacerbate climate vulnerabilities. To address this issue, we recommend implementing policies that incentivize solar-powered wells, promote decentralized renewable energy projects, and invest in climate-resilient infrastructure. These interventions can significantly improve living standards and build resilient communities against future climate shocks.

Key findings:

* Only 32.4% of Sub-Saharan Africa's population has access to safely managed drinking water [1].
* A mere 53.3% have access to electricity [2].
* The region possesses substantial solar and hydropower potential, yet its adoption remains low due to infrastructure limitations and financial constraints.

## Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa is one of the regions most vulnerable to climate change impacts. Limited access to safe water and electricity compounds existing challenges such as food insecurity, health issues, and economic instability. The United Nations Sustainable Development Goal 6 (SDG 6) aims for universal access to clean water by 2030; current progress is insufficient given the region's specific needs.

## Analysis

Evidence-based examination of the issue reveals:

* Sub-Saharan Africa lags behind other regions in terms of safely managed drinking water, with only 32.4% of the population having access [1].
* Electricity access is even more limited, with only 53.3% of the population having access to electricity [2].
* The region possesses substantial solar and hydropower potential, yet its adoption remains low due to infrastructure limitations and financial constraints.

## Policy Recommendations

We recommend the following interventions:

1. **Incentivize Solar-Powered Wells**: Develop and implement programs that subsidize or provide grants for the installation of solar-powered water wells. These should be designed to replace traditional boreholes, which are often unsustainable.
2. **Promote Decentralized Renewable Energy Projects**: Invest in small-scale renewable energy projects such as solar mini-grids and wind turbines to increase access to electricity across rural areas where grid connectivity is not feasible.
3. **Climate Resilient Infrastructure**: Fund the construction of climate-resilient water infrastructure, including flood-proof reservoirs and drought-resistant agricultural practices.
