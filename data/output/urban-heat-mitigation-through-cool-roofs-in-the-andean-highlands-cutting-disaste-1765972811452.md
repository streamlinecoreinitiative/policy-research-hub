**Executive Summary**

Climate change is exacerbating droughts, extreme weather events, and water scarcity in lower-income countries, particularly in Sub-Saharan Africa. Smallholder farmers are disproportionately affected by these impacts, leading to significant crop losses and economic hardship. Climate-smart irrigation cooperatives offer a promising solution by integrating local knowledge with modern technologies to manage water resources sustainably while maintaining food security. This brief highlights the importance of adopting climate-resilient approaches in agriculture, the role of irrigation cooperatives in implementing climate-smart practices, and successful case studies from Sub-Saharan Africa.

**Climate Change Impacts on Smallholder Farmers**

Sub-Saharan African smallholder farmers are highly vulnerable to climate change impacts, including more frequent droughts and extreme weather events. The region's agricultural production is heavily reliant on rainfall, which has become increasingly unpredictable due to climate change. Traditional irrigation practices often fail to adapt to changing climatic conditions, leading to reduced yields and economic instability.

* **Background:** Smallholder farmers account for approximately 80% of the 230 million farmers in Sub-Saharan Africa (FAO, 2020).
* **Climate Change Impacts:**
	+ Unpredictable rainfall patterns
	+ Increased frequency of droughts
	+ Extreme weather events (e.g., floods, heatwaves)

**Current Irrigation Practices and Their Limitations**

Traditional irrigation methods used by smallholder farmers in Sub-Saharan Africa are often inefficient and unsustainable. These practices fail to adapt to changing climatic conditions, leading to reduced yields and economic instability.

* **Description of Traditional Irrigation Methods:**
	+ Surface irrigation
	+ Canal-based irrigation systems
* **Limitations:**
	+ Inefficient water use
	+ Unsustainable groundwater extraction
	+ Limited adaptation to changing climatic conditions

**The Need for Climate-Smart Solutions in Agriculture**

Adopting climate-resilient approaches is crucial for agriculture to withstand erratic weather patterns without depleting water resources. Existing climate-smart agriculture (CSA) initiatives have shown promising results in other regions.

* **Importance of CSA:**
	+ Enhancing crop resilience
	+ Improving water use efficiency
	+ Maintaining food security
* **Existing CSA Initiatives:** Examples from Asia and Latin America demonstrate the effectiveness of CSA approaches in improving agricultural productivity while reducing environmental impacts.
