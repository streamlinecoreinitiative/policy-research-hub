## Introduction to Water Scarcity in the Mesoamerican Highlands

The Mesoamerican region is characterized by a fragile ecosystem, with limited water resources and high population growth rates. Climate change has further exacerbated these challenges, leading to unpredictable rainfall patterns and reduced water availability.

### Overview of Climate Change Impacts on Agriculture

* Rising temperatures: Increased frequency and severity of droughts and heatwaves
* Changes in precipitation patterns: Unpredictable rainfall and increased risk of flooding
* Soil degradation: Reduced fertility and increased erosion due to intensified agriculture practices

## Current Irrigation Practices and Challenges

Existing irrigation systems suffer from inefficiencies, including high water loss percentages. These inefficiencies exacerbate the region's water scarcity issues.

### Description of Existing Irrigation Systems

* Boreholes: Drilled wells extracting groundwater for irrigation
* Canals: Surface water diversion systems for irrigation
