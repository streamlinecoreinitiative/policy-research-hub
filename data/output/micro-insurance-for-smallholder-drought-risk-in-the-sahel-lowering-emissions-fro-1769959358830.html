Bridging Water Security, Clean Energy, and Climate Resilience in Sub-Saharan Africa: A Comprehensive Policy Blueprint

Executive Summary

To address the pressing issues of water scarcity, limited access to clean energy, and heightened vulnerability to climate change impacts in Sub-Saharan Africa, this policy blueprint recommends increasing investment in small-scale water infrastructure and promoting renewable energy adoption. Specifically, we propose:

* Increasing investment in rainwater harvesting ponds and other low-cost solutions to improve access to clean water for rural populations who lack piped supplies.

  • Promoting solar-powered wells and similar technologies to increase electricity access while reducing reliance on fossil fuels.

    Context & Challenge

    Sub-Saharan Africa is one of the most vulnerable regions globally to climate change impacts, including droughts, floods, and erratic rainfall patterns. Despite significant progress in some areas, widespread challenges remain in ensuring basic water security and electricity access. For instance, only 32.4% of Sub-Saharan Africans have access to safely managed drinking water [Source: World Bank Open Data, 2024], compared to a global average of 73.7% [Source: World Bank Open Data, 2024].
