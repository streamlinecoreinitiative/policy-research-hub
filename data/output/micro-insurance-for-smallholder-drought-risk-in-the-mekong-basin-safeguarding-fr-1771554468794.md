## Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa is one of the most vulnerable regions to climate change (IPCC 2021), with high temperatures, erratic rainfall patterns, and frequent droughts. These conditions compound existing challenges such as limited access to clean drinking water and unreliable power supply. The lack of adequate energy infrastructure hinders efforts to implement sustainable water management practices like rainwater harvesting, which can significantly improve water security in rural areas (UN-Water/WHO 2022).

   - Promote community-based solar installations that provide electricity for household use, improving hygiene practices and reducing waterborne illnesses.

2. **Support Rainwater Harvesting Programs with Solar Power**: Develop hybrid systems combining traditional rainwater collection infrastructure with solar-powered pumps to facilitate water transportation from harvested sources to storage tanks.
