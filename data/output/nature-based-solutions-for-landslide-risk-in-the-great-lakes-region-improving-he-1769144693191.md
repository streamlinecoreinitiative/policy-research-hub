# nature-based solutions for landslide risk in the Great Lakes region: improving health outcomes in heatwaves

**Bridging Water Security, Clean Energy, and Climate Adaptation Gaps in Sub-Saharan Africa**

## Executive Summary

This policy brief highlights critical gaps in water security, clean energy access, and climate adaptation in Sub-Saharan Africa. To address these challenges, we recommend investing in renewable energy projects to support microgrids for rural clinics, promoting agroforestry value chains for cash crops, expanding water loss reduction programs in secondary cities, and enhancing climate resilience initiatives through school feeding supply chains.

## Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa faces significant hurdles in ensuring basic services for its population. According to the World Bank Open Data (2024), only 32.4% of the region's population has access to safely managed drinking water [1], a stark contrast to the global average of 73.7% [2]. Access to electricity is also unevenly distributed, with 53.3% of the population having access in Sub-Saharan Africa compared to 91.6% globally [3]. Climate change exacerbates these challenges by leading to more frequent and intense weather events.

## Analysis

### Water Security

Sub-Saharan Africa lacks adequate infrastructure for water supply and sanitation. The region's water security is further threatened by climate change, which can alter precipitation patterns and increase evaporation rates [4].

*   Access to safely managed drinking water in Sub-Saharan Africa: 32.4% (2024) [1]
*   Global access to safely managed drinking water: 73.7% (2024) [2]

### Clean Energy Access

Access to electricity is unevenly distributed, with only 53.3% of the population having access in Sub-Saharan Africa compared to 91.6% globally [3].

*   Renewable energy consumption worldwide: 19.7% (2020) [5]
*   Global access to electricity: 91.6% (2023) [3]

### Climate Adaptation

Climate change impacts are evident through more frequent and severe weather events, affecting water resources, agricultural productivity, and public health.

## Policy Recommendations

1.  **Invest in Renewable Energy Projects**: Support microgrids for rural clinics to improve access to electricity and reduce reliance on fossil fuels.
2.  **Promote Agroforestry Value Chains**: Invest in agroforestry value chains for cash crops, which can enhance resilience by integrating climate-smart practices.
3.  **Expand Water Loss Reduction Programs**: Implement water loss reduction programs in secondary cities to ensure sustainable water supply and improve health outcomes.
4.  **Enhance Climate Resilience Initiatives**: Develop climate-resilient school feeding supply chains to protect vulnerable communities from extreme weather events.

## Implementation Considerations

*   Financial Constraints: Identify cost-effective financing mechanisms such as grants, concessional loans, and partnerships with international organizations.
*   Technological Barriers: Collaborate with technology providers to ensure microgrids are equipped with the latest energy-efficient technologies.
*   Community Engagement: Engage local communities in planning and implementation processes to foster ownership and sustainability.

## Conclusion

Addressing water security, clean energy access, and climate adaptation requires coordinated efforts. By implementing these recommendations, we can build a more resilient Sub-Saharan Africa capable of adapting to future challenges while improving the quality of life for its inhabitants.
