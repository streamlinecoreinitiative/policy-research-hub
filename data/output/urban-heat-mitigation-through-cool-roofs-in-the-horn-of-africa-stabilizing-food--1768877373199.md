**Urban Heat Mitigation through Cool Roofs in the Horn of Africa: Stabilizing Food Security during Droughts**

## Executive Summary

The Horn of Africa is one of the most vulnerable regions to climate change impacts, including droughts and heatwaves, which significantly affect food security. To mitigate urban heat island effects and stabilize food security, we recommend investing in cool roof technologies for buildings in urban areas of the Horn of Africa.

## Context & Challenge

The Horn of Africa is characterized by high population growth rates, limited access to clean water, and unreliable electricity supplies. Climate change intensifies these issues, with increased frequency of droughts and heatwaves threatening food security and health outcomes. According to the World Bank (2024), only 32.4% of Sub-Saharan Africans have access to safely managed drinking water sources.
