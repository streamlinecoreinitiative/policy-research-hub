**Bridging Water Security, Clean Energy, and Climate Adaptation Gaps in Sub-Saharan Africa**

## Executive Summary

This policy brief highlights the critical need to address water security, clean energy, and climate adaptation gaps in Sub-Saharan Africa. With only 32.4% of the population having access to safely managed drinking water (World Bank Open Data, 2024) [1] and 53.3% accessing reliable electricity (World Bank Open Data, 2023) [2], targeted interventions are essential for enhancing resilience. We recommend investing in clean energy solutions, such as solar panels and wind turbines, to improve access to electricity and reduce carbon emissions.

## Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa is one of the most vulnerable regions globally due to its reliance on natural resources like water and energy. Despite progress towards universal access to electricity (53.3%, World Bank Open Data, 2023) [2], many still lack basic sanitation facilities (32.4% accessing safely managed drinking water, World Bank Open Data, 2024) [1]. Climate change threatens these fragile systems, with frequent droughts and floods impacting agricultural productivity and public health.

## Analysis

Access to safe drinking water is essential for human health and economic development. In Sub-Saharan Africa, only 32.4% of the population has access to safely managed water sources (World Bank Open Data, 2024) [1]. This stark disparity highlights the critical need for improved infrastructure and sustainable solutions such as rainwater harvesting systems and solar-powered desalination plants.

Clean energy remains a major challenge, with just 53.3% of Sub-Saharan Africa's population having reliable electricity (World Bank Open Data, 2023) [2]. The region is heavily dependent on fossil fuels, contributing to both environmental degradation and economic inefficiency. Transitioning to renewable sources like solar and wind could not only improve access but also reduce carbon emissions.

Climate adaptation measures are equally critical. Droughts and floods have become more frequent, threatening food security and public health. Implementing climate-resilient water management practices, such as drought-resistant crops and improved irrigation systems, can enhance resilience. Similarly, microgrids powered by renewable energy sources can provide reliable electricity during grid outages.

## Policy Recommendations

1. **Invest in Water Security**: Allocate funds for the installation of rainwater harvesting systems in rural areas and solar-powered water treatment plants to improve access to safe drinking water.
2. **Promote Clean Energy Solutions**: Introduce policies that encourage investment in renewable energy projects, such as solar panels and wind turbines, both for grid-connected and off-grid applications.
3. **Enhance Climate Adaptation Measures**: Develop early warning systems for climate-related disasters and implement climate-resilient agricultural practices to protect food security.
