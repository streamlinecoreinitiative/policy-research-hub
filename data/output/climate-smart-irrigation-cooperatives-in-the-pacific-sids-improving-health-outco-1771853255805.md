## Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa is one of the most vulnerable regions to climate change, with frequent droughts and floods exacerbating existing development challenges (World Bank Open Data). The region's severe water scarcity affects 2.2 billion people globally, with 32.4% of the population lacking access to safely managed drinking water in Sub-Saharan Africa alone (UN-Water/WHO). Limited access to electricity (53.3%) further hinders economic growth and development (World Bank Open Data).
