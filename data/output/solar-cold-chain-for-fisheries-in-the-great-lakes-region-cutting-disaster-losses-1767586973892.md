# Solar Cold-Chain for Fisheries in the Great Lakes Region: Enhancing Environmental Resilience and Food Security

## Executive Summary

The Great Lakes region is vulnerable to climate-related disasters, including extreme weather events and water scarcity, which disproportionately affect fisheries and their dependent communities. This brief outlines a comprehensive plan to address these challenges by integrating solar-powered cold-chain systems with existing fishing practices.

## Climate Change Impacts on Lower-Income Countries

Climate change exacerbates water scarcity and extreme weather events globally, particularly in lower-income countries where infrastructure and resources are limited. The Central African Rainforest faces severe water scarcity due to climate change, affecting traditional irrigation methods that are energy-intensive and costly for smallholder farmers.

### Water Security Challenges

*   **Climate Change Impacts:**
    *   Erratic rainfall patterns leading to reduced water availability for crops.
    *   Increased evaporation from wetlands exacerbating water scarcity issues.
*   **Traditional Irrigation Systems:**
    *   High energy costs (diesel generators).
    *   Water losses due to poor canal design and maintenance.

### Agroforestry-Based Micro-Hydro Systems

Agroforestry, the practice of integrating trees into agricultural landscapes, can enhance soil health, biodiversity, and water retention. When combined with micro-hydro systems, which harness renewable energy from water flow, these integrated solutions offer a sustainable approach to climate-resilient irrigation.

### Benefits of Agroforestry-Based Micro-Hydro Systems

*   **Soil Health:**
    *   Improved soil structure and water retention capacity due to vegetation cover.
    *   Examples of successful agroforestry projects demonstrating improved water retention and reduced erosion.
*   **Biodiversity Enhancement:**
    *   Increased biodiversity provides natural pest control and pollination services, reducing the need for chemical inputs.
    *   Economic benefits from cost savings on labor, fertilizers, and other inputs due to enhanced soil health.
