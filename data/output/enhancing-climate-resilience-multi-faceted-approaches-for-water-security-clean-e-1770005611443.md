## Executive Summary

To enhance climate resilience in Sub-Saharan Africa, we recommend investing $1 billion annually over the next five years to develop water infrastructure, promote renewable energy integration, and strengthen climate adaptation efforts. This multi-faceted approach will improve access to safe drinking water (currently at 32.4% in the region) and electricity (53.3%), while enhancing the continent's ability to adapt to climate change impacts.

## Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa faces significant challenges in accessing safe drinking water and reliable electricity due to climate variability. According to World Bank data, only [VERIFY] 32.4% of the population in Sub-Saharan Africa has access to safely managed drinking water, with freshwater resources being withdrawn at a rate of 9.2% of total available resources (World Bank Open Data). The region also lags behind in renewable energy consumption, achieving only 19.7% globally in 2020 (World Bank Open Data).

## Policy Recommendations

1. **Investment in Water Infrastructure**:
	* Allocate $200 million annually over the next five years to construct and maintain safe drinking water systems.
	* Implement community-based watershed management programs to enhance local resilience.
2. **Promote Renewable Energy Integration**:
	* Develop microgrids powered by solar, wind, or hydro resources to provide electricity where grid access is limited.
	* Encourage hybrid energy solutions that combine renewable and conventional power sources for stability.
3. **Strengthen Climate Adaptation Efforts**:
	* Implement early-warning systems for droughts and floods using satellite imagery and ground-based sensors.
	* Support indigenous knowledge and traditional practices in climate adaptation initiatives.

## Implementation Considerations

Implementing these recommendations will require partnerships between governments, NGOs, and international organizations to overcome limited financial resources, technical expertise, and political will. Funding mechanisms can be explored through the African Development Bank's New Deal on Energy for Africa and the Green Climate Fund.

## Conclusion

Sub-Saharan Africa must adopt a multi-faceted approach that integrates water security, clean energy, and climate adaptation strategies to build a more resilient future for its people. By investing in infrastructure, promoting renewable energy integration, and strengthening climate resilience efforts, we can improve access to safe drinking water and electricity while enhancing the continent's ability to adapt to climate change impacts.
