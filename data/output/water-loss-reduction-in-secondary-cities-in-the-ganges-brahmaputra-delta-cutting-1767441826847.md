## Executive Summary

The Ganges-Brahmaputra delta is one of the most densely populated regions vulnerable to climate change, with frequent flooding and water scarcity affecting millions. This brief highlights the importance of addressing water security, clean energy, and climate adaptation in lower-income countries like Bangladesh and India.

## Introduction

The Ganges-Brahmaputra delta faces severe water management challenges due to:

* Climate change: Rising temperatures, changing precipitation patterns, and increased frequency of extreme weather events.
* Population growth: Rapid urbanization in secondary cities puts pressure on existing infrastructure.
* Water scarcity: Limited access to clean water sources, exacerbated by inefficient irrigation systems.
