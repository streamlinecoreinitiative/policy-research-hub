# climate-resilient school feeding supply chains in the Horn of Africa: lowering emissions from critical sectors

**Climate-Resilient School Feeding Supply Chains in the Horn of Africa: Lowering Emissions from Critical Sectors**

## Executive Summary

The Horn of Africa, comprising Ethiopia, Kenya, Somalia, and South Sudan, faces unprecedented environmental challenges. Only 32.4% of its population has access to safely managed drinking water, and just 53.3% have access to electricity (World Bank Open Data). These statistics underscore the urgent need for integrated policies addressing water security, clean energy provision, and climate adaptation. This policy brief recommends implementing targeted measures to enhance environmental resilience in the region's school feeding supply chains.

## Context & Challenge

The Horn of Africa is one of the world's most vulnerable regions due to its unique geography, climate change impacts, and rapid population growth (World Bank Open Data). The region's limited access to safe drinking water and electricity hinders development and well-being. Climate change intensifies these issues; droughts, floods, and extreme weather events threaten both water security and energy supply chains.

## Analysis

### Water Security

Sub-Saharan Africa faces significant challenges with access to safe drinking water (32.4%) compared to the global average of 73.7% (World Bank Open Data). This disparity underscores a critical need for improved infrastructure and sustainable water management solutions.

### Clean Energy

Only 53.3% of the population has access to electricity, leaving millions without basic modern conveniences (World Bank Open Data). The region's reliance on fossil fuels for energy production exacerbates environmental degradation.

### Climate Adaptation

Sub-Saharan Africa is particularly vulnerable to climate change impacts such as desertification and extreme weather events (UN-Water/WHO). Without resilient infrastructure and adaptation measures, these challenges will only worsen.

## Policy Recommendations

To address the urgent need for environmental resilience in the Horn of Africa's school feeding supply chains, we recommend:

1. **Invest in water resource management programs**: Focus on small-scale irrigation systems and rainwater harvesting to improve access to safe drinking water.
2. **Promote renewable energy adoption**: Encourage the use of solar, wind, and hydroelectric power through grants, subsidies, and tax incentives.
3. **Implement climate adaptation strategies**: Develop early warning systems for climate-related disasters like droughts and floods, support reforestation efforts, and enhance agricultural research to make crops more resilient.

## Implementation Considerations

These policies require significant investment but offer substantial returns in terms of improved health outcomes, economic growth, and environmental sustainability. Collaboration between governments, NGOs, and the private sector is crucial for successful implementation. The costs will vary by region but are generally lower than conventional energy projects (World Bank Open Data).

## Conclusion

The Horn of Africa's environmental resilience hinges on effective integration of water security, clean energy provision, and climate adaptation strategies. By prioritizing these areas, governments can create a foundation for sustainable development that benefits both present and future generations.
