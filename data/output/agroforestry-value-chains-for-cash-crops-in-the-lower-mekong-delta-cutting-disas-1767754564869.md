**Agroforestry-Based Micro-Hydro Systems for Climate-Resilient Irrigation in the Mesoamerican Highlands**

**Executive Summary**

The Mesoamerican Highlands face severe water scarcity and poverty, exacerbating climate change impacts on agriculture. This brief explores agroforestry-based micro-hydro systems as a climate-resilient irrigation solution. Agroforestry improves soil health, biodiversity, and carbon sequestration while reducing erosion and improving water retention. Micro-hydro technology increases energy efficiency and reduces reliance on diesel generators.

**Water Scarcity in the Mesoamerican Highlands**

The region experiences significant climate change impacts, including droughts and temperature fluctuations. Existing irrigation systems suffer from inefficiency, with high water loss percentages (evaporation and seepage rates vary by location). This inefficiency exacerbates water scarcity, affecting agriculture and local economies.
