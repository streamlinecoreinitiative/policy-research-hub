# Flood-Resilient Transport Corridors in Pacific SIDS: Enhancing Water Security and Climate Resilience

## Executive Summary

The Pacific Small Island Developing States (SIDS) face significant challenges in ensuring water security and adapting to climate change. This brief highlights the importance of integrating flood-resilient transport corridors with freshwater ecosystem conservation, particularly in the context of micro-hydro systems and agroforestry practices.

## Introduction to Water Scarcity in Pacific SIDS

Climate change is exacerbating water scarcity in the Pacific SIDS due to increased evaporation from rising sea levels, altered rainfall patterns, and saltwater intrusion into freshwater sources. This has severe implications for agriculture, food security, and economic stability.

### Current Irrigation Practices and Challenges

Existing irrigation systems in the Pacific SIDS include boreholes, canals, and rainwater harvesting systems. However, these methods suffer from significant water loss through evaporation and seepage, reducing their efficiency and effectiveness.

### Micro-Hydro Systems for Irrigation Efficiency

Micro-hydro technology involves harnessing the energy of small-scale water flows to generate electricity. When integrated with agroforestry practices, micro-hydro systems can provide a reliable and efficient source of renewable energy for irrigation, reducing reliance on diesel generators and minimizing greenhouse gas emissions.

## Integration in Climate-Resilient Water Supply

Pilot projects have demonstrated the effectiveness of combining agroforestry and micro-hydro systems to create climate-resilient water supply infrastructure. These integrated approaches can enhance water security while promoting sustainable development and poverty reduction.
