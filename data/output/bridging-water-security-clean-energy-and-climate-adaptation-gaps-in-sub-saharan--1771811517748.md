**Bridging Water Security, Clean Energy, and Climate Adaptation Gaps in Sub-Saharan Africa**

### Executive Summary

To address the pressing challenges of water security, clean energy access, and climate adaptation in Sub-Saharan Africa, we recommend increasing funding for community-level water projects to improve sanitation infrastructure and access to clean water sources. Specifically, governments and international partners should allocate $10-50 per person for rural and peri-urban areas to enhance water security. This investment is crucial given the region's limited access to safe drinking water, with only 32.4% of the population having safely managed drinking water (World Bank Open Data). Furthermore, we suggest investing in off-grid solar solutions for electricity generation, particularly in remote regions where grid extension is not feasible.

### Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa faces critical challenges related to water security, clean energy access, and climate adaptation. The region's limited access to safe drinking water exacerbates health risks from contaminated sources, while only 53.3% of Sub-Saharan Africans have access to electricity (World Bank Open Data). This disparity underscores the need for targeted interventions to boost access in rural and remote areas where infrastructure is often underdeveloped. Climate change impacts, including erratic rainfall patterns and increased frequency of droughts and floods, further compound these challenges.
