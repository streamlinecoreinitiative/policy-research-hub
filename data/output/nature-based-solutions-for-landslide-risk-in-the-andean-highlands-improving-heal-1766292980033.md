# nature-based solutions for landslide risk in the Andean highlands: improving health outcomes in heatwaves

**Nature-Based Solutions for Landslide Risk in the Andean Highlands: Enhancing Resilience and Health Outcomes**

### Executive Summary

The Andean highlands are vulnerable to landslide risks exacerbated by climate change, affecting water security and agricultural productivity. This brief explores nature-based solutions integrating agroforestry with micro-hydro systems to enhance resilience against landslides and heatwaves.

### Introduction to Water Scarcity in the Andean Highlands

Climate change has significantly impacted the Andean highlands, leading to increased temperature fluctuations, altered precipitation patterns, and heightened landslide risks. These changes have severe consequences for agricultural productivity, water security, and human health, particularly during heatwaves.

### Current Irrigation Practices and Challenges

Existing irrigation systems in the Andean highlands rely heavily on boreholes, canals, and other infrastructure that is often inefficient and prone to water loss through evaporation and seepage. These practices not only strain local resources but also contribute to environmental degradation, further exacerbating landslide risks.

Agroforestry systems have been shown to improve soil health, biodiversity, and carbon sequestration while reducing erosion and increasing water retention. Case studies demonstrate the effectiveness of agroforestry in enhancing climate resilience.

### Micro-Hydro Systems for Irrigation Efficiency

Micro-hydro technology offers a sustainable alternative to traditional irrigation methods by harnessing local energy sources to power pumping systems. This approach reduces reliance on diesel generators and increases energy efficiency, contributing significantly to the overall sustainability of agroforestry-micro-hydro systems.

### Integration in Climate-Resilient Water Supply

Pilot projects have successfully integrated agroforestry with micro-hydro systems to enhance climate resilience in irrigation. These integrated solutions not only improve water security but also offer economic benefits through reduced energy costs and increased crop yields, making them highly scalable.

### Policy Recommendations

1.  **Support for Integrated Agroforestry-Micro-Hydro Projects:** Governments should provide financial incentives and technical support for communities to adopt these integrated systems.
2.  **Regulatory Frameworks:** Establish or amend existing regulations to encourage the adoption of renewable energy sources in irrigation, such as micro-hydro technology.
3.  **Capacity Building:** Offer training programs for farmers and local technicians on the installation, maintenance, and operation of agroforestry-micro-hydro systems.

### Future Research Directions

### Conclusion

The integration of agroforestry with micro-hydro systems offers a promising solution to address landslide risks in the Andean highlands while improving health outcomes during heatwaves. By adopting these nature-based solutions, communities can enhance their resilience against climate change impacts, ensuring sustainable water security and agricultural productivity for generations to come.
