# Agroforestry-Based Micro-Hydro Systems for Climate-Resilient Irrigation in the Andean Highlands

## Executive Summary

The Andean Highlands face severe water scarcity due to historical droughts and limited access to reliable irrigation systems. This brief explores the potential of agroforestry-based micro-hydro systems as a climate-resilient solution for irrigation in this region.

## Introduction to Water Scarcity in the Andean Highlands

The Andean Highlands are characterized by a fragile ecosystem where water scarcity is exacerbated by climate change. The region has experienced numerous droughts throughout history, with significant impacts on agriculture and local economies. Current challenges faced by farmers and communities include:

* Limited access to reliable irrigation systems
* Economic instability due to reduced crop yields
* High dependency on traditional methods (e.g., surface water, rain-fed)
* Limited efficiency and sustainability of existing irrigation systems
