**Agroforestry-Based Micro-Hydro Systems for Climate-Resilient Irrigation in the Mesoamerican Highlands**

**Executive Summary:**
The Mesoamerican Highlands face severe water scarcity and poverty, exacerbated by climate change impacts on agriculture. This brief highlights the potential of agroforestry-based micro-hydro systems to provide climate-resilient irrigation solutions. By integrating agroforestry practices with small-scale hydroelectric systems, communities can reduce their reliance on diesel generators and improve energy efficiency.

**Irrigation Challenges and Climate Resilience**

The Mesoamerican Highlands are characterized by limited water resources, fragile ecosystems, and high levels of poverty. Climate change is projected to exacerbate these challenges (World Bank). Traditional irrigation systems often suffer from significant water losses due to evaporation and seepage [verify]. Agroforestry practices have been shown to improve soil health, biodiversity, and carbon sequestration in the region.

**Micro-Hydro Systems for Efficient Irrigation**

Small-scale hydroelectric systems offer a promising solution for efficient and renewable energy production. By harnessing the power of small water flows, micro-hydro systems can reduce reliance on diesel generators and provide clean energy for irrigation pumps (World Bank). The integration of agroforestry with micro-hydro technology has been successfully demonstrated in pilot projects.
