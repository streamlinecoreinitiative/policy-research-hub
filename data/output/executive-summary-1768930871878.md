## Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa is one of the most vulnerable regions globally in terms of water availability and clean energy access (World Bank Open Data, 2024). Only 32.4% of the population has access to safely managed drinking water, with approximately half of all people lacking safe water worldwide residing in this region (UN-Water/WHO, 2022). Electricity access is also unevenly distributed, with only 53.3% of the population having access to electricity (World Bank Open Data, 2024).

Climate change poses additional threats, including increased frequency and intensity of droughts and floods, further straining water resources and energy supply systems (IPCC, 2019). These challenges underscore the need for integrated approaches that address multiple facets of environmental resilience.

## Policy Recommendations

To enhance water security, clean energy access, and climate adaptation in Sub-Saharan Africa, we suggest the following policy recommendations:

1. **Expand Water Infrastructure**: Invest $5 billion over 3 years to modernize existing water supply systems and build new facilities to improve access to safe drinking water.
	* Building more wells and boreholes equipped with improved filtration technologies
	* Promoting rainwater harvesting ponds for additional water sources during dry periods

2. **Promote Renewable Energy Integration**: Encourage the adoption of renewable energy sources like solar, wind, and hydroelectric power through subsidies, tax incentives, and public-private partnerships.
	* Implementing feed-in tariffs to incentivize private sector investments in renewable projects
	* Establishing community-based microgrids for remote areas that cannot connect to the national grid

3. **Climate Adaptation Measures**: Develop comprehensive climate adaptation plans that include:
	* Building early warning systems and disaster preparedness infrastructure, such as flood barriers and levees.
	* Developing drought-resistant crops and irrigation technologies tailored to local conditions.
