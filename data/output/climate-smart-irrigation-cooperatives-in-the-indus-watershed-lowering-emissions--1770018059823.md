# climate-smart irrigation cooperatives in the Indus watershed: lowering emissions from critical sectors

**Bridging Water Security Gaps in Sub-Saharan Africa Through Renewable Energy and Climate Adaptation**

## Executive Summary

Sub-Saharan Africa faces a pressing water security crisis, with 32.4% of its population lacking access to safely managed drinking water (World Bank Open Data). Urgent action is needed to address climate-induced water insecurity. We recommend implementing renewable energy projects and climate adaptation measures to enhance the region's resilience to water scarcity.

## Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa is home to over 1 billion people, with only 32.4% of its population having access to safely managed drinking water (World Bank Open Data). This is a significant improvement from 2015, when 43% of the population lacked access to improved sanitation facilities (WHO/UNICEF Joint Monitoring Programme). However, the region's rapid population growth and urbanization have put pressure on already strained water resources. Climate change exacerbates this issue, with rising temperatures and erratic rainfall patterns leading to reduced agricultural productivity and increased health risks.

## Analysis

Climate change has severe consequences for water security in Sub-Saharan Africa. In Sub-Saharan Africa, droughts and floods have devastating impacts on agriculture, food security, and human health. Renewable energy solutions can reduce reliance on fossil fuels, improving air quality and lowering emissions from critical sectors such as water treatment and desalination plants.

## Policy Recommendations

We recommend the following policy actions to address water security challenges in Sub-Saharan Africa:

1. **Invest in renewable energy projects**: Implement solar-powered water pumping stations to boost access to clean drinking water.
2. **Establish climate adaptation measures**: Develop community-based disaster risk reduction programs and promote the use of climate-resilient crops.
3. **Strengthen institutional support**: Strengthen regulatory frameworks for renewable energy integration into existing water infrastructure.

## Implementation Considerations

Implementing these policy recommendations will require significant investment in renewable energy projects, which can be substantial (World Bank, 2022). International donors and regional funds may provide financial support to help bridge the initial investment gap. However, resistance to change among traditional stakeholders and lack of technical expertise in implementing climate adaptation measures pose potential barriers.

## Conclusion

Sub-Saharan Africa's water security crisis demands urgent attention. By investing in renewable energy projects and climate adaptation measures, we can enhance the region's resilience to water scarcity and improve living conditions for millions. We call on governments, NGOs, and development practitioners to prioritize water security initiatives that leverage renewable energy and climate adaptation strategies.
