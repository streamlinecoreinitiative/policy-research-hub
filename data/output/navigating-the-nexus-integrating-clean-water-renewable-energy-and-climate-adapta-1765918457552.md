**Navigating the Nexus: Integrating Clean Water, Renewable Energy, and Climate Adaptation Strategies in Sahelian Villages**

Executive Summary:

Sahelian villages are facing severe water scarcity and climate-induced shocks like droughts and floods. The integration of clean water solutions, renewable energy sources, and climate adaptation strategies can significantly enhance the resilience of these communities. This brief outlines a comprehensive approach to address the complex challenges faced by Sahelian villages, focusing on solar-powered wells, rainwater harvesting ponds, and climate adaptation strategies.

**Section 1: Introduction - Overview of Sahelian Villages' Water Scarcity and Climate Challenges**

Sahelian regions in West Africa are experiencing severe water scarcity and climate-induced shocks. Droughts and floods have become more frequent and intense, affecting the livelihoods of millions of people. The current context is characterized by:

* Severe water scarcity: Many villages lack access to clean water, with some communities relying on distant wells or boreholes.
* Climate variability: Sahelian regions are experiencing increasing temperatures, changing rainfall patterns, and rising sea levels.
* Limited infrastructure: Existing water and energy infrastructure in rural areas is often inadequate, inefficient, or non-existent.

The integration of clean water solutions, renewable energy sources, and climate adaptation strategies can provide immediate relief while building sustainable infrastructure for future generations. This project aims to identify and implement comprehensive approaches that address the complex challenges faced by Sahelian villages.

**Section 2: Clean Water Solutions - Solar-Powered Wells and Rainwater Harvesting Ponds**

Clean water solutions are essential for addressing water scarcity in Sahelian villages. Two key approaches are:

* **Solar-powered wells:** These systems use solar energy to pump water from underground, providing a reliable source of clean water.
	+ Key data points:
		- Installed solar capacity
		- Energy efficiency of wells
		- Cost-effectiveness compared to traditional methods
* **Rainwater harvesting ponds:** These systems collect and store rainwater for non-potable uses, reducing the demand on groundwater resources.
	+ Needed evidence: Historical rainfall data, soil moisture levels, and community needs assessments
