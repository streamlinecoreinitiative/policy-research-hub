**# Solar Cold-Chain for Fisheries in the Caribbean SIDS: Safeguarding Freshwater Ecosystems**

## Executive Summary

The Caribbean Small Island Developing States (SIDS) face significant challenges in ensuring water security, clean energy access, and climate adaptation. This brief highlights the importance of integrating solar cold-chain technology with fisheries management to safeguard freshwater ecosystems.

## Environmental Resilience in Lower-Income Countries: Context and Challenges

### Water Security in the Caribbean SIDS

* The Caribbean SIDS are highly vulnerable to climate change impacts, including sea-level rise, increased storm intensity, and altered precipitation patterns.
* Freshwater scarcity is a pressing concern, with many islands experiencing droughts and water shortages during peak tourist seasons.

### Clean Energy Access and Climate Adaptation

* Renewable energy sources, particularly solar power, offer a viable solution for reducing dependence on fossil fuels and mitigating climate change impacts. [verify]
* Climate adaptation measures, such as sea walls and coastal protection infrastructure, are essential for safeguarding against extreme weather events.
