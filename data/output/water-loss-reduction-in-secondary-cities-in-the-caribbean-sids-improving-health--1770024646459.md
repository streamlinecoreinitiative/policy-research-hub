## Executive Summary

Sub-Saharan Africa faces critical challenges related to water security, clean energy access, and climate adaptation. With only 32.4% of the population having access to safely managed drinking water (World Bank Open Data, 2024) and less than half having reliable electricity (World Bank Open Data, 2023), the region is at a significant disadvantage in building resilient infrastructure and sustainable development pathways. To address these pressing issues effectively, we recommend that governments, NGOs, and international organizations implement targeted policies for water infrastructure, renewable energy integration, and climate-resilient adaptation measures.

## Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa is one of the most vulnerable regions to climate change due to its reliance on natural resources like water and energy. Limited access to clean water and electricity threatens health and economic stability (World Bank Open Data, 2024). The region's rapid population growth, coupled with climate-induced changes in rainfall patterns, exacerbates existing challenges, necessitating urgent intervention.

## Policy Recommendations

1. **Invest in Water Infrastructure**: Develop and finance water purification plants and distribution networks to increase access to safely managed drinking water (World Bank Open Data, 2024).
2. **Promote Rainwater Harvesting**: Implement policies to encourage rainwater harvesting, especially for rural areas where access is limited.
3. **Renewable Energy Incentives**: Increase subsidies and tax breaks for renewable energy projects to promote clean energy access (World Bank Open Data, 2024).
4. **Grid Modernization**: Upgrade existing grid infrastructure to support increased electricity demand, particularly in rural areas.
5. **Climate-Smart Agriculture**: Support climate-resilient crops and farming practices to enhance agricultural productivity and reduce food insecurity (UN FAO [2024]).
6. **Early Warning Systems**: Establish early warning systems to mitigate the impacts of extreme weather events.
