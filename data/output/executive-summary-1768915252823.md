# Executive Summary

**Enhancing Environmental Resilience in Sub-Saharan Africa: Prioritizing Water Security, Clean Energy, and Climate Adaptation**

## Executive Summary
To address the pressing environmental resilience gaps in water security, clean energy access, and climate adaptation in Sub-Saharan Africa, we recommend implementing a comprehensive strategy that includes enhancing water scarcity management, promoting clean energy access through solar power, and strengthening climate adaptation measures. This approach will require significant investment and innovative financing mechanisms but is essential for sustainable development and long-term economic stability.

## Context & Challenge
Sub-Saharan Africa is one of the most vulnerable regions globally when it comes to water security, clean energy access, and climate adaptation [1]. With nearly 2.2 billion people lacking safe drinking water [2] and over 74% global coverage for safely managed sanitation services lagging behind [3], addressing these challenges is urgent and critical. The region's reliance on traditional energy sources contributes to indoor air pollution and deforestation, while rising temperatures have intensified heatwaves, droughts, and flooding, threatening food security and livelihoods.

## Policy Recommendations

* **Enhance Water Security**: Implement water scarcity management strategies to reduce wastage and improve access for vulnerable populations. Invest in desalination plants where feasible.
	+ Actors: National governments, international organizations
	+ Timeframe: Short-term (2023-2025), medium-term (2026-2030)
* **Promote Clean Energy Access**: Increase solar power capacity through subsidies and public-private partnerships, especially targeting rural areas with low grid connectivity. Encourage energy-efficient appliances and incentives for renewable installations.
	+ Actors: National governments, private sector, international organizations
	+ Timeframe: Medium-term (2026-2030), long-term (2031-2040)
* **Strengthen Climate Adaptation Measures**: Develop early warning systems for extreme weather events, support indigenous communities in climate-resilient practices, and promote afforestation initiatives to mitigate the impacts of droughts.
	+ Actors: National governments, international organizations, local communities
	+ Timeframe: Short-term (2023-2025), medium-term (2026-2030)
