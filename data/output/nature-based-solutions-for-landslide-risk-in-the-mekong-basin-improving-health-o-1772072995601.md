**Bridging Water Security, Clean Energy, and Climate Resilience in Sub-Saharan Africa**

## Executive Summary

Sub-Saharan Africa is facing a triple challenge of water scarcity, energy poverty, and climate vulnerability. To address these interconnected issues, we recommend implementing cost-effective, scalable solutions for both water security and renewable energy. This policy brief provides actionable recommendations tailored specifically for government officials, NGO leaders, and development practitioners.

Key findings:

* Sub-Saharan Africa struggles with 32.4% access to safely managed drinking water (World Bank Open Data).
* Energy access is even more dire at just 53.3% (World Bank Open Data), leaving many without reliable electricity or clean energy options.
* Climate variability has led to increased frequency of droughts and floods, impacting agriculture and energy production.

Recommendations:

Implement cost-effective, scalable solutions for both water security and renewable energy, emphasizing climate-resilient infrastructure that can withstand expected changes in weather patterns.

## Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa is characterized by limited access to safe drinking water (32.4%) and electricity (53.3%), exacerbating existing challenges. Climate variability has led to increased frequency of droughts and floods, impacting agriculture and energy production. The region's reliance on fossil fuels poses significant risks due to rising global temperatures and potential climate-related disruptions.

## Analysis

### Water Security

Sub-Saharan Africa faces a water scarcity crisis with only 32.4% of the population having access to safely managed drinking water (World Bank Open Data). This is significantly lower than the world average of 73.7%, highlighting urgent needs for interventions.

### Clean Energy Access

The region lags behind globally in electricity access, with just 53.3% of its population having access (World Bank Open Data), compared to 91.6% worldwide. This disparity compounds existing energy poverty and hampers economic growth.
