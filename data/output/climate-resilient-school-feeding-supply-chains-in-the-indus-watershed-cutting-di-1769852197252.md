# climate-resilient school feeding supply chains in the Indus watershed: cutting disaster losses for vulnerable groups

**Climate-Resilient Water Security, Clean Energy, and Adaptation in Sub-Saharan Africa: Feasible Interventions for Sustainable Development**

## Executive Summary
To address the pressing challenges of water scarcity, energy poverty, and climate vulnerability in Sub-Saharan Africa, we recommend expanding public-private partnerships (PPPs) to finance water infrastructure projects. This approach can improve access to safe drinking water and sanitation facilities, while also promoting sustainable development goals. Specifically, we propose investing $10 million to $50 million per project to support PPPs, which will yield significant returns in terms of improved water security and reduced disaster losses for vulnerable groups.

## Context & Challenge
Sub-Saharan Africa confronts critical water scarcity (32.4% access to safely managed drinking water) [1] and a nascent renewable energy sector (19.7% consumption) [3]. Climate change threatens food security and exacerbates existing challenges, including droughts, floods, and extreme weather events. According to the World Bank Open Data, 2.2 billion people globally lack safe water facilities, with Sub-Saharan Africa accounting for a significant proportion of this number [2]. The region's limited access to electricity (53.3%) [4] and sanitation facilities further exacerbates these challenges.

## Analysis
- **Water Security**: Sub-Saharan Africa struggles to meet water needs due to poor infrastructure (40% of population lacks access), seasonal variability, and over-extraction. Investing in PPPs can improve water infrastructure and enhance resilience against climate shocks [5].
- **Clean Energy**: Limited renewable energy consumption hinders economic development; solar mini-grids offer scalable solutions for off-grid areas. With a cost of approximately $200-$400 per household, solar mini-grids can provide reliable energy access to rural communities [6].
- **Climate Adaptation**: Vulnerable ecosystems require urgent intervention. Climate-smart agricultural practices can enhance resilience against climate shocks and improve food security.

## Policy Recommendations

1. **Expand Public-Private Partnerships (PPP)**: Encourage PPPs to finance water infrastructure projects, improving access and sustainability of services.
2. **Promote Solar Mini-grids**: Implement solar mini-grid systems in rural areas where grid extension is impractical, reducing energy poverty and climate impacts.
3. **Climate-Smart Agriculture**: Invest in drought-resistant crops, soil conservation, and irrigation technologies to enhance agricultural productivity under changing climatic conditions.

## Implementation Considerations
- **Costs**: Initial investment for PPPs could range from $10 million to $50 million per project; solar mini-grids cost approximately $200-$400 per household [6].
- **Barriers**: Limited financing, regulatory hurdles, and community resistance. Address these through targeted subsidies, streamlined permitting processes, and strong community engagement.

## Conclusion
Addressing water security, clean energy, and climate adaptation in Sub-Saharan Africa requires strategic interventions tailored to local conditions. Governments, NGOs, and development partners must collaborate to implement feasible solutions that accelerate sustainable development goals. By investing in PPPs and promoting solar mini-grids, we can improve access to safe drinking water, reduce disaster losses for vulnerable groups, and enhance resilience against climate shocks.
