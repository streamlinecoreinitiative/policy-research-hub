# Watershed Co-management with Indigenous Councils in the Sahel: Safeguarding Freshwater Ecosystems

## Executive Summary

The Ganges-Brahmaputra delta faces severe water scarcity and climate change impacts such as rising sea levels. Traditional desalination methods are energy-intensive and costly, exacerbating economic challenges for vulnerable communities. This brief explores an innovative solution that integrates agroforestry with wind-powered desalination systems to enhance both water security and renewable energy use in the region.

## Introduction to Water Scarcity in the Ganges-Brahmaputra Delta

The Ganges-Brahmaputra delta is a critical agricultural hub, supporting millions of people. Climate change has significantly impacted agriculture in this region. Rising sea levels have caused saltwater intrusion into freshwater sources, affecting crop yields and water quality.

*   **Climate Change Impacts:**
    *   Rising sea levels
    *   Increased frequency and severity of floods and droughts
    *   Soil salinization and reduced fertility
*   **Importance of Resilient Desalination Methods:**
    *   Ensure reliable access to clean water for agriculture, industry, and households
    *   Reduce reliance on energy-intensive desalination methods

## Current Desalination Practices and Challenges

Existing desalination technologies include solar distillation and reverse osmosis. However, these methods have significant drawbacks:

*   **Energy Costs:**
    *   High energy requirements for operation and maintenance
    *   Increased greenhouse gas emissions contributing to climate change
*   **Maintenance Requirements:**
    *   Frequent replacement of parts and equipment
    *   High labor costs for maintenance and repair
*   **Environmental Impact:**
    *   Brine discharge affecting marine ecosystems
    *   Potential for chemical contamination

## Integration of Agroforestry and Wind-Powered Desalination in Climate-Resilient Water Supply

Pilot projects have demonstrated the effectiveness of combining agroforestry with wind-powered desalination:

*   **Benefits:**
    *   Reduced energy costs and greenhouse gas emissions
    *   Improved water quality and reduced brine discharge
    *   Enhanced community engagement and benefits sharing
*   **Economic Analysis:**
    *   Cost-effectiveness and potential for scaling up

To support the adoption of these integrated systems, policy recommendations include:

1.  **Supportive Policies:**
    *   Incentives for renewable energy adoption and agroforestry practices

3.  **Research Directions:**
