# agroforestry value chains for cash crops in the Ganges-Brahmaputra delta: safeguarding freshwater ecosystems

**Agroforestry Value Chains for Cash Crops in the Ganges-Brahmaputra Delta: Safeguarding Freshwater Ecosystems**

Executive Summary:

The Ganges-Brahmaputra delta, one of the world's most densely populated and fertile regions, faces significant environmental challenges. Rising temperatures, changing precipitation patterns, and increased salinity threaten freshwater ecosystems, agricultural productivity, and rural livelihoods. This brief explores the potential of agroforestry value chains for cash crops as a strategy to safeguard freshwater ecosystems in the region.

**Section 1: Climate Vulnerability and Water Security**

The Ganges-Brahmaputra delta is highly vulnerable to climate change, with frequent floods, droughts, and saltwater intrusion affecting agricultural productivity and water quality. Rising temperatures are projected to increase evaporation rates, further exacerbating water scarcity. The region's freshwater ecosystems, including wetlands, rivers, and aquifers, are critical for agriculture, drinking water supply, and biodiversity conservation.

**Key Climate Risks in the Ganges-Brahmaputra Delta:**

• Frequent flooding (up to 20 times per year)
• Droughts (average duration: 3-6 months)
• Saltwater intrusion (affecting up to 30% of agricultural land)

**Section 2: Agroforestry Value Chains for Cash Crops**

Agroforestry, the integration of trees into farming systems, can enhance water security and promote sustainable agriculture in the Ganges-Brahmaputra delta. By incorporating cash crops such as mangoes, jute, or mustard into agroforestry value chains, farmers can:

• Improve crop yields and resilience to climate-related stressors
• Enhance biodiversity and ecosystem services (e.g., pollination, pest control)
• Increase income opportunities for rural communities

**Benefits of Agroforestry Value Chains:**

• Improved water retention and reduced soil erosion
• Enhanced carbon sequestration and storage
• Increased crop diversity and resilience to pests and diseases

**Section 3: Case Studies and Success Stories**

Several initiatives in the Ganges-Brahmaputra delta demonstrate the potential of agroforestry value chains for cash crops:

• **Mango Agroforestry Project**, West Bengal, India:
	+ Improved mango yields by up to 30%
	+ Enhanced water retention and reduced soil erosion
	+ Increased income for farmers by up to 50%
• **Jute Agroforestry Initiative**, Bangladesh:
	+ Promoted jute cultivation in saline-affected areas
	+ Improved crop yields and water quality
	+ Created employment opportunities for rural youth

**Section 4: Economic Impact Analysis and Policy Recommendations**

Agroforestry value chains for cash crops can contribute to broader economic resilience in the Ganges-Brahmaputra delta:

• **Cost Savings:** Reduced soil erosion, improved water retention, and enhanced crop yields can lead to cost savings for farmers and communities.
• **Increased Income:** Agroforestry value chains can increase income opportunities for rural communities through sales of cash crops and ecosystem services.

**Policy Recommendations:**

1. **Support agroforestry research and development**: Encourage collaboration between government agencies, research institutions, and private sector organizations to develop and disseminate best practices in agroforestry.
2. **Promote policy frameworks**: Develop and implement policies that incentivize agroforestry adoption, such as subsidies for tree planting or tax breaks for sustainable agriculture practices.
3. **Funding opportunities**: Explore funding sources from international organizations (e.g., World Bank, Global Environment Facility) and domestic governments to support agroforestry initiatives.

**Closing Outlook:**

The Ganges-Brahmaputra delta faces significant environmental challenges that require innovative solutions. Agroforestry value chains for cash crops offer a promising approach to safeguard freshwater ecosystems, promote sustainable agriculture, and enhance rural livelihoods. By supporting research, policy development, and funding opportunities, we can unlock the potential of agroforestry in this critical region.

**Actions/Recommendations:**

1. **Develop a comprehensive agroforestry strategy**: Collaborate with government agencies, research institutions, and private sector organizations to develop a strategic plan for promoting agroforestry value chains in the Ganges-Brahmaputra delta.
2. **Establish monitoring and evaluation frameworks**: Develop indicators and metrics to track progress and assess the impact of agroforestry initiatives on water security, agricultural productivity, and rural livelihoods.
3. **Foster international cooperation**: Encourage collaboration between countries in the region to share best practices, leverage funding opportunities, and develop joint policy frameworks for promoting agroforestry value chains.
