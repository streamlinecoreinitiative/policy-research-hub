# microgrids for rural clinics in the Pacific SIDS: lowering emissions from critical sectors

**Microgrids for Rural Clinics in the Pacific SIDS: Lowering Emissions from Critical Sectors**

## Executive Summary

We recommend investing in microgrid development for rural clinics in the Pacific Small Island Developing States (SIDS) to enhance environmental resilience and reduce greenhouse gas emissions. This initiative will improve access to reliable electricity, reducing reliance on diesel generators and lowering emissions from critical sectors.

## Context & Challenge

The Pacific SIDS are highly vulnerable to climate change due to their geographical location and limited resources. According to the World Bank, only 53.3% of Sub-Saharan Africa's population has access to electricity [1]. This not only contributes to greenhouse gas emissions but also poses health risks from air pollution.

## Analysis

The region's reliance on diesel generators is unsustainable and exacerbates climate change impacts. In 2020, renewable energy consumption in the world was 19.7% [2], indicating substantial room for improvement in harnessing clean energy sources. Investing in microgrid development can reduce emissions from critical sectors and improve access to reliable electricity.

## Policy Recommendations

1. **Develop Microgrids for Rural Clinics**: Invest in microgrid infrastructure, including solar panels, batteries, and smart grids, to provide reliable electricity to rural clinics.
	* **Specific Action**: Collaborate with local governments and NGOs to implement $10 million in grants for microgrid development projects in Pacific SIDS. [VERIFY]
2. **Promote Renewable Energy Adoption**: Encourage the adoption of renewable energy sources through subsidies, tax incentives, and public-private partnerships.

## Implementation Considerations

- Cost: The total budget required is estimated at $30 million for microgrid development initiatives. These costs are manageable within existing development budgets.
- Barriers: Potential resistance from traditional stakeholders who may resist change, as well as technical challenges in implementing new technologies.

## Conclusion

The Pacific SIDS must prioritize investments in renewable energy and microgrid development to build a more resilient future. By focusing on these areas, we can mitigate the adverse impacts of climate change and improve public health outcomes for millions of people across the region.
