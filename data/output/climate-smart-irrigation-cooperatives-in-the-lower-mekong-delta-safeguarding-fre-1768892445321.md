# climate-smart irrigation cooperatives in the Lower Mekong delta: safeguarding freshwater ecosystems

**Climate-Smart Irrigation Cooperatives in the Lower Mekong Delta: Safeguarding Freshwater Ecosystems**

## Executive Summary

Sub-Saharan Africa faces critical challenges in water security, clean energy access, and climate resilience. With 32.4% of the population lacking access to safe drinking water (World Bank Open Data) and only 53.3% having access to electricity (World Bank Open Data), it is essential to prioritize investments in infrastructure, renewable energy, and climate adaptation projects.

We recommend establishing climate-smart irrigation cooperatives in the Lower Mekong delta as a key strategy for enhancing water security and promoting sustainable agriculture practices. This approach can help reduce poverty, improve food security, and mitigate the impacts of climate change on freshwater ecosystems.

## Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa is one of the regions most vulnerable to water scarcity and climate change impacts (World Bank Open Data). The region's harsh environmental conditions and limited resources exacerbate these challenges, making it essential for governments to address them through targeted interventions. Immediate action is needed to mitigate worsening environmental challenges and ensure sustainable development.

## Analysis

Access to safe drinking water in Sub-Saharan Africa has improved marginally from 24.7% in 2015 to 32.4% in 2024 (World Bank Open Data). However, this progress is still inadequate, highlighting the need for continued investment in water supply infrastructure. Electricity access remains low, with only 53.3% of the population having access, exacerbating economic and social disparities.
