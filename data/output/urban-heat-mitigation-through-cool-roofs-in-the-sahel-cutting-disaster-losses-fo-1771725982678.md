## Executive Summary

Sub-Saharan Africa faces severe water security challenges, with only 32.4% of its population having access to safely managed drinking water (World Bank Open Data, 2024). This policy brief outlines actionable interventions to improve water security, clean energy access, and climate adaptation in the region. Our primary recommendation is to implement microgrids for rural clinics, rainwater harvesting systems, solar-powered well technologies, and watershed co-management programs with indigenous councils.

## Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa is acutely vulnerable to climate change due to its high population density and limited infrastructure (UN-Water/WHO). Water scarcity, exacerbated by poor management practices, affects millions daily. Renewable energy remains underutilized, with only 19.7% of the world's total final energy consumption coming from renewables (World Bank Open Data, 2020). These challenges underscore the need for urgent interventions to improve water security, ensure reliable electricity access, and enhance climate resilience.
