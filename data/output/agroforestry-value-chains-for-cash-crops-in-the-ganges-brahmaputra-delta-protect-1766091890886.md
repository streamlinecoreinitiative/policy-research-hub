# agroforestry value chains for cash crops in the Ganges-Brahmaputra delta: protecting livelihoods under climate stress

**Agroforestry Value Chains for Cash Crops in the Ganges-Brahmaputra Delta: Protecting Livelihoods under Climate Stress**

**Executive Summary**

The Ganges-Brahmaputra delta, home to millions of people and a significant portion of India's agricultural production, faces severe climate-related challenges. Rising temperatures, changing precipitation patterns, and increased frequency of extreme weather events threaten the livelihoods of farmers and rural communities. This brief explores how agroforestry value chains for cash crops can enhance environmental resilience in this region. We examine the potential benefits of integrating micro-scale wind turbines into these value chains to improve rural electrification and water supply.

**Climate Variability and Its Impacts on the Ganges-Brahmaputra Delta**

The Ganges-Brahmaputra delta is one of the most densely populated river deltas worldwide, with significant agricultural production and a high dependence on natural resources. Climate change is projected to exacerbate existing vulnerabilities in this region:

• **Temperature increases:** Rising temperatures are expected to lead to increased evapotranspiration, reduced crop yields, and altered growing seasons.
• **Changing precipitation patterns:** Shifts in rainfall distribution and intensity may result in more frequent droughts or floods, affecting agricultural productivity and water availability.
• **Increased frequency of extreme weather events:** More frequent heatwaves, cyclones, and floods will further stress the region's already fragile ecosystem.

**Current Challenges with Water Supply and Electrification**

The Ganges-Brahmaputra delta faces significant challenges in terms of rural electrification and access to clean water:

• **Limited access to electricity:** Only a small percentage of rural households have access to reliable electricity, hindering economic development and quality of life.
• **Unreliable water sources:** Communities rely on often-contaminated groundwater or surface water sources that are vulnerable to pollution and climate-related disruptions.

**Agroforestry Value Chains for Cash Crops and Micro-Scale Wind Turbines**

Agroforestry value chains can enhance environmental resilience by promoting sustainable land use practices, improving biodiversity, and increasing ecosystem services. Integrating micro-scale wind turbines into these value chains can:

• **Improve rural electrification:** Powering irrigation systems, communication devices, and other essential equipment for farmers.
• **Enhance water supply:** Pumping and treating water from reliable sources to meet community needs.

**Policy Recommendations for Implementation**

To support wider adoption of agroforestry value chains with micro-scale wind turbines:

1.  Develop policies that incentivize private sector investment in rural electrification and water supply infrastructure.
2.  Establish clear guidelines for the integration of micro-scale wind turbines into existing energy systems.
3.  Provide training and capacity-building programs for farmers, local communities, and government officials to ensure effective project implementation.

**Closing Outlook**

The Ganges-Brahmaputra delta's agroforestry value chains offer a promising approach to enhancing environmental resilience in the face of climate stress. By integrating micro-scale wind turbines into these value chains, rural communities can improve their access to electricity and clean water, ultimately protecting livelihoods and promoting sustainable development.
