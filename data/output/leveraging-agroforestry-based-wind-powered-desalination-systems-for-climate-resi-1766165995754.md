**Executive Summary:**
The Pacific Islands are highly susceptible to climate change impacts, including rising sea levels and increased storm intensity. Traditional desalination methods can exacerbate economic challenges due to their energy-intensive nature. This brief explores the potential of agroforestry-based wind-powered desalination systems as a sustainable solution for enhancing water security and renewable energy use in coastal communities.

**Climate Change Vulnerability in Pacific Islands**

The Pacific Islands are among the most vulnerable regions to climate change impacts (UN Environment Programme, 2020). Rising sea levels threaten coastal ecosystems, while increased storm frequency poses significant risks to infrastructure and human settlements. The importance of resilient water sources cannot be overstated, as they support both human consumption and agricultural activities.

**Current Desalination Practices and Challenges**

Existing desalination technologies rely heavily on fossil fuels, resulting in high energy costs and significant greenhouse gas emissions. In the Pacific Islands, diesel generators are often used to power desalination plants (World Bank, 2019). The environmental impact of these systems is substantial, with studies indicating water loss during transportation and storage.
