**Enhancing Climate Resilience in Sub-Saharan Africa: Strengthening Water Security, Clean Energy Access, and Adaptation**

## Executive Summary

Sub-Saharan Africa faces severe water scarcity and energy poverty, exacerbating climate-related vulnerabilities. To address this crisis, we recommend prioritizing investments in clean water, renewable energy infrastructure, and climate adaptation programs to reduce poverty and enhance resilience. Specifically, governments and development partners should allocate at least 50% of annual development budgets towards water infrastructure projects that ensure universal access to safe drinking water.

## Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa struggles with inadequate access to safe drinking water (32.4% of population), electricity (53.3%), and renewable energy consumption (9.2%). Climate change intensifies these challenges, increasing vulnerability. Rapid urbanization, climate variability, and economic growth are straining existing infrastructure, necessitating urgent interventions.

* Access to safely managed drinking water in Sub-Saharan Africa is significantly lower than the global average (32.4% vs 73.7%) [1].
* Only 53.3% of the population in Sub-Saharan Africa has access to electricity, compared to 91.6% globally [2].
* Renewable energy consumption in the region is low at 9.2%, far below the global average of 19.7% [3].

Evidence-based examination of the issue reveals:

* Water Security: Sub-Saharan Africa faces severe water scarcity, with only 20% of the population having access to safely managed drinking water (World Bank, 2024). This disparity is a primary driver of poverty and malnutrition.

* Climate Adaptation: The region's climate resilience efforts are insufficient given its vulnerability to extreme weather events like droughts and floods.
