# Effective Early Warning Systems and Last-Mile Alerts in Caribbean Small Island Developing States (SIDS): Enhancing Disaster Resilience

## Executive Summary

The Caribbean SIDS are highly vulnerable to natural disasters, including hurricanes, floods, and droughts. Integrating early warning systems and last-mile alerts can significantly reduce disaster losses and improve resilience in these communities.

## Current Challenges and Opportunities

### Water Security

* Climate change is exacerbating water scarcity in the Caribbean SIDS due to increased evaporation from wetlands and erratic rainfall patterns [verify].
* Traditional irrigation methods are energy-intensive and costly, posing economic challenges for smallholder farmers already vulnerable to droughts.

### Clean Energy

* Renewable energy sources, such as solar and wind power, can provide a sustainable solution for energy needs in the Caribbean SIDS.
* Micro-hydro systems integrated with agroforestry practices can enhance both water security and renewable energy use.

### Climate Adaptation

* Early warning systems and last-mile alerts can help communities prepare for and respond to natural disasters more effectively [verify].
* Climate-resilient infrastructure, such as sea walls and green roofs, can reduce the impact of climate-related hazards.

## Policy Recommendations

1.  **Invest in early warning systems**: Governments and international organizations should invest in developing and implementing effective early-warning systems that provide timely and accurate information on impending natural disasters.
2.  **Promote last-mile alerts**: Last-mile alert systems should be implemented to ensure vulnerable communities receive critical information on disaster risks and response measures.
3.  **Support climate-resilient infrastructure**: Governments and international organizations should support the development of climate-resilient infrastructure, such as sea walls, green roofs, and flood-resistant buildings.

## Actions/Recommendations
