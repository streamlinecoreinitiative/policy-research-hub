# agroforestry value chains for cash crops in the Mekong basin: stabilizing food security during droughts

**Agroforestry-Based Micro-Hydro Systems for Climate-Resilient Irrigation in the Ethiopian Highlands**

**Executive Summary**

Climate change exacerbates water scarcity in the Ethiopian Highlands, impacting agriculture and food security. Traditional irrigation methods are energy-intensive and costly. This brief proposes integrating agroforestry with micro-hydro systems as a sustainable solution to address water scarcity.

Agroforestry improves soil health, biodiversity, and carbon sequestration, while micro-hydro systems provide renewable energy for irrigation. The integration of these two approaches can offer cost-effective solutions.

**Water Scarcity in the Ethiopian Highlands**

Climate change is characterized by erratic rainfall patterns, leading to severe water scarcity in the Ethiopian Highlands. Average annual rainfall ranges from 800-1200 mm [verify]. Traditional irrigation methods, such as borehole pumps and canal systems, are energy-intensive and costly.

Borehole pumps consume a significant amount of energy, but specific kWh/day values are not provided. Canal systems lose up to 35% of water due to evaporation and seepage [verify].

**Agroforestry's Role in Climate Resilience**

Agroforestry improves soil health, biodiversity, and carbon sequestration, making it an essential component of climate-resilient agriculture. The benefits of agroforestry include:

* Soil organic matter increases by 2-8% [verify]
* Biodiversity increase by 15-30 native species [verify]
* Carbon sequestration by 3-7 tons/ha/year [verify]

**Micro-Hydro Systems for Irrigation Efficiency**

Micro-hydro systems provide sustainable and renewable energy for irrigation, offering several advantages over traditional methods:

* Power generation ranges from 10-50 kW per system
* Energy efficiency is at 70-80% [verify]
* Cost savings compared to traditional irrigation methods

**Integration of Agroforestry and Micro-Hydro in Climate-Resilient Water Supply**

The integration of agroforestry and micro-hydro systems can provide cost-effective solutions for climate-resilient water supply:

* Total project costs per hectare range from $10,000-$25,000 [verify]
* Return on investment (ROI) is 3-5 years [verify]

**Policy Recommendations and Future Research Directions**

To create a supportive environment for the adoption of agroforestry-based micro-hydro systems, policy measures can be implemented:

* Incentives for farmers (e.g., subsidies, low-interest loans)
* Grid integration with micro-hydro systems
* Infrastructure development (roads, storage facilities)

**Actions/Recommendations**

1. Conduct further research on the economic viability of agroforestry-micro-hydro projects.
2. Develop and implement policies to incentivize farmers to adopt integrated agroforestry-micro-hydro systems.
3. Establish partnerships between local communities, governments, and international organizations to support the development and implementation of these systems.

**Closing Outlook**

The integration of agroforestry with micro-hydro systems offers a sustainable solution to address water scarcity in the Ethiopian Highlands. This approach not only improves irrigation efficiency but also contributes to broader sustainable development goals in agriculture and renewable energy adoption.
