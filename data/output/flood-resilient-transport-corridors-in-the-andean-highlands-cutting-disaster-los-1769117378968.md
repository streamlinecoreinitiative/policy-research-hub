# flood-resilient transport corridors in the Andean highlands: cutting disaster losses for vulnerable groups

**Flood-Resilient Transport Corridors in the Andean Highlands: Cutting Disaster Losses for Vulnerable Groups**

## Executive Summary

Sub-Saharan Africa faces significant environmental challenges that threaten the livelihoods of millions. With only 32.4% access to safely managed drinking water and 53.3% electricity access, coupled with projected climate stressors such as increasing temperatures and erratic rainfall patterns, urgent action is needed to ensure sustainable water security, reliable energy sources, and resilient communities. To address these challenges, we recommend prioritizing investments in renewable energy, improving water infrastructure, and strengthening climate adaptation measures.

## Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa is one of the most vulnerable regions to climate change with limited resources for water management and energy access (World Bank Open Data). Climate-induced droughts have led to widespread food insecurity and economic instability. Improved resilience requires comprehensive interventions across water, energy, and climate sectors (UN-Water/WHO).

## Analysis

### Water Security

Only 32.4% of the population in Sub-Saharan Africa has access to safely managed drinking water (World Bank Open Data), lagging behind other regions with 73.7% global coverage (World Bank Open Data). This gap is exacerbated by climate change, which threatens agricultural productivity and water resources.

### Clean Energy Access

Despite a global renewable energy consumption rate of 19.7%, only 53.3% of Sub-Saharan African populations have access to electricity (World Bank Open Data), further exacerbating climate vulnerability. Renewable energy can provide a reliable source of power while reducing dependence on fossil fuels.

### Climate Adaptation

Increased temperatures and unpredictable rainfall patterns threaten agricultural productivity and water resources, necessitating immediate action. Developing early warning systems for droughts and floods, promoting climate-resilient agricultural practices, and providing training on water conservation and efficient energy use can help mitigate these impacts (World Bank Open Data).

## Policy Recommendations

### Recommendation 1: Strengthen Renewable Energy Policies

* Implement feed-in tariffs for renewable energy projects to increase investment in solar and wind power infrastructure.
* Increase funding for renewable energy development programs to support the transition to clean energy sources.

Sources:

* World Bank Open Data
* UN-Water/WHO

### Recommendation 2: Improve Water Infrastructure

* Expand water treatment facilities to improve access to safe drinking water.
* Invest in rainwater harvesting systems and drought-resistant crops to enhance water security.

Sources:

* World Bank Open Data
* UN SDG Report 2023

### Recommendation 3: Enhance Climate Resilience Programs

* Develop early warning systems for droughts and floods to support climate adaptation efforts.
* Promote climate-resilient agricultural practices and provide training on water conservation and efficient energy use.

Sources:

* World Bank Open Data
* UN-Water/WHO

## Implementation Considerations

Initial investments in renewable energy infrastructure can be substantial but yield long-term benefits through reduced dependence on fossil fuels and improved health outcomes from clean cooking. Potential barriers to implementation include political resistance to change and lack of funding for climate adaptation programs. Addressing these challenges requires engaging local communities and building coalitions with international partners.

## Conclusion

Urgent action is needed to bridge the gaps in water security, clean energy access, and climate resilience in Sub-Saharan Africa. By implementing targeted policies and investing in infrastructure, we can ensure a more sustainable and resilient future for vulnerable populations.
