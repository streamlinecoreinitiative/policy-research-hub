# early-warning systems and last-mile alerts in the Great Lakes region: raising resilience finance for communities

**Early-Warning Systems and Last-Mile Alerts in the Great Lakes Region: Raising Resilience Finance for Communities**

## Executive Summary

This policy brief highlights the critical need to enhance environmental resilience in Sub-Saharan Africa, focusing on water security, clean energy, and climate adaptation. To address these interconnected challenges, we recommend implementing community-led watershed management programs, promoting off-grid renewable energy solutions, and strengthening climate adaptation infrastructure through early warning systems and last-mile alerts.

### Key Findings

* Only 32.4% of the population in Sub-Saharan Africa has access to safe drinking water [Source: World Bank Open Data].
* Energy poverty persists, with only 53.3% of the region's population having reliable electricity access [Source: World Bank Open Data].
* Renewable energy consumption accounts for only 19.7% of global energy consumption [Source: World Bank Open Data].

## Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa faces significant environmental challenges due to its geographical location and economic development level. The region is vulnerable to climate change, which exacerbates existing water scarcity issues. Moreover, the lack of access to safe drinking water and electricity hinders economic growth and human well-being.

* Water security in Sub-Saharan Africa is compromised due to overexploitation, poor infrastructure, and climate variability [Source: World Bank Report on Water Security].
* Energy poverty persists in the region despite significant investments in renewable energy [REN21].
* Climate change impacts are felt acutely in Sub-Saharan Africa, leading to increased frequency and intensity of droughts, floods, and landslides [UNDP's Climate Resilience Framework].

## Analysis

The analysis reveals that community-led watershed management programs can improve water security by engaging local communities for sustainable resource management. Off-grid renewable energy solutions can provide electricity access where grid connections are not feasible. Strengthening climate adaptation infrastructure through early warning systems and last-mile alerts can build community resilience.

* Community-led watershed management has been shown to be effective in improving water security [Source: World Bank Report on Water Security].
* Off-grid renewable energy solutions have the potential to increase energy access, particularly for rural communities [REN21].
* Early warning systems and last-mile alerts can reduce climate-related disaster impacts by providing timely warnings and enabling evacuation or preparedness measures [UNDP's Climate Resilience Framework].

## Policy Recommendations

### 1. Community-Led Watershed Management

Implement community-led watershed management programs that engage local communities for sustainable water resource management.

* Source: World Bank Report on Water Security
* Actors: Local governments, international organizations, and community groups
* Timeframe: Immediate implementation with long-term commitment (2024-2030)

### 2. Promote Off-Grid Renewable Energy Solutions

Invest in off-grid solar and wind energy projects to provide electricity where grid connections are not feasible.

* Source: REN21
* Actors: Governments, private sector companies, and international organizations
* Timeframe: Short-term implementation (2024-2025) with long-term expansion plans (2026-2030)

### 3. Strengthen Climate Adaptation Infrastructure

Develop early warning systems for climate-related disasters and build community resilience through infrastructure like flood barriers, drought-resistant crops, and improved water storage facilities.

* Source: UNDP's Climate Resilience Framework
* Actors: Governments, international organizations, and community groups
* Timeframe: Immediate implementation with long-term commitment (2024-2030)

## Implementation Considerations

The initial investment in watershed management and off-grid energy solutions can be high but is offset by long-term savings from reduced waterborne diseases and lower electricity costs. High initial capital costs, lack of technical expertise, and limited access to financing are significant barriers. Partnerships with international organizations and local governments will be crucial for overcoming these challenges.

## Conclusion

Sub-Saharan Africa must prioritize environmental resilience to achieve sustainable development. By implementing community-led watershed management programs, promoting off-grid renewable energy solutions, and strengthening climate adaptation infrastructure through early warning systems and last-mile alerts, the region can improve its living standards while building a more resilient future for all.
