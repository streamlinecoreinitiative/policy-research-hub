# Watershed Co-management with Indigenous Councils in the Mekong Basin: Enhancing Resilience through Integrated Solutions

## Executive Summary

This brief highlights the importance of watershed co-management with indigenous councils in the Mekong basin, focusing on enhancing resilience through integrated solutions for water security, clean energy, and climate adaptation. The project aims to address challenges faced by lower-income countries by integrating agroforestry-based micro-hydro systems into irrigation practices.

## Main Sections

### 1. Introduction to Water Scarcity in the Mekong Basin

The Mekong basin is one of the most vulnerable regions to climate change, with severe water scarcity affecting agriculture and food security. Climate change impacts include erratic rainfall patterns, increased evaporation rates, and reduced water availability for irrigation.

### 2. Current Irrigation Practices and Challenges

Existing irrigation systems in the Mekong basin often rely on boreholes, canals, or traditional practices that lead to significant water loss through evaporation and seepage. These inefficiencies exacerbate water scarcity issues, negatively impacting agricultural productivity and local economies.

Agroforestry systems have been shown to improve soil health, biodiversity, and carbon sequestration while reducing erosion and improving water retention. Case studies demonstrate the effectiveness of agroforestry in enhancing climate resilience.

### 4. Micro-Hydro Systems for Irrigation Efficiency

Micro-hydro technology offers a viable solution to reduce reliance on diesel generators and increase energy efficiency in irrigation systems. By harnessing local water resources, micro-hydro systems can provide clean energy while minimizing environmental impacts.

### 5. Integration of Agroforestry and Micro-Hydro in Climate-Resilient Water Supply

Pilot projects have successfully integrated agroforestry-based micro-hydro systems into irrigation practices, demonstrating improved efficiency and reduced costs. Economic analyses highlight the cost-effectiveness and potential for scaling up these integrated solutions.

## Closing Outlook

The integration of agroforestry-based micro-hydro systems into irrigation practices offers a promising solution for addressing water security, clean energy, and climate adaptation challenges in the Mekong basin. By promoting watershed co-management with indigenous councils, we can enhance climate resilience while fostering sustainable development.
