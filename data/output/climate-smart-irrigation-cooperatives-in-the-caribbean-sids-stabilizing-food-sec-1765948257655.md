# climate-smart irrigation cooperatives in the Caribbean SIDS: stabilizing food security during droughts

**Climate-Smart Irrigation Cooperatives in the Caribbean SIDS: Stabilizing Food Security during Droughts**

**Executive Summary**

The Caribbean Small Island Developing States (SIDS) are highly vulnerable to climate change, with droughts and water scarcity posing significant threats to food security. This brief explores the potential of climate-smart irrigation cooperatives as a solution to stabilize food production in these regions. Specifically, it focuses on the integration of biochar-based agroforestry systems in rural Tanzania as a model for improving water security and renewable energy production.

**Climate Change Impacts in Rural Tanzania**

The region's vulnerability to climate change is characterized by increased frequency and severity of droughts and floods. Rising temperatures have led to changes in rainfall patterns, resulting in unreliable and unpredictable precipitation (UNEP, 2020). This has significant implications for agriculture, with crop failures becoming more frequent due to water scarcity (World Bank's Climate Change Knowledge Portal, 2019).

**Biochar-Based Agroforestry Systems: A Climate-Smart Solution**

Biochar, a carbon-rich soil amendment produced from biomass, can be used in conjunction with agroforestry systems to improve soil health and increase water retention (Lal et al., 2011). This approach has been shown to reduce erosion, enhance crop yields, and promote renewable energy production through biogas generation from biochar-based compost.

**Benefits of Biochar-Based Agroforestry Systems in Rural Tanzania**

The integration of biochar into agroforestry systems offers several benefits for farmers in rural Tanzania:

• Enhanced crop yields due to improved soil fertility
• Reduced need for irrigation, resulting in lower energy costs and increased water security
• Potential integration into renewable energy production through biogas generation from biochar-based compost

**Case Studies and Pilot Projects**

Several pilot projects have demonstrated the effectiveness of biochar-based agroforestry systems in improving water security and renewable energy production.
