# Solar Cold-Chain for Fisheries in the Horn of Africa: Enhancing Disaster Resilience and Food Security

## Executive Summary

The Horn of Africa is highly vulnerable to climate change, with frequent droughts and floods affecting fisheries and livelihoods. This brief highlights the potential of solar cold-chain technology to enhance water security, clean energy use, and climate adaptation in fisheries management.

## Introduction to Environmental Resilience in Lower-Income Countries

Lower-income countries face significant challenges in achieving environmental resilience due to limited resources and infrastructure. Climate change exacerbates these challenges, affecting agriculture, water security, and energy access. In the Horn of Africa, fisheries are a critical sector for livelihoods but are highly vulnerable to climate-related disasters.

### Key Challenges:

• **Water Scarcity:** Erratic rainfall patterns and increased evaporation from highland wetlands contribute to water scarcity in the region.
• **Food Security:** Post-harvest losses due to lack of cold storage facilities compromise food security for communities dependent on fisheries.
• **Climate Adaptation:** Limited access to clean energy and climate-resilient infrastructure hinders adaptation efforts.

## Solar Cold-Chain Technology: A Promising Solution

Solar cold-chain technology offers a promising solution to enhance water security, renewable energy use, and climate resilience in the Horn of Africa. By integrating solar power with cold-chain storage, fish can be preserved for longer periods, reducing post-harvest losses and improving food security.

## Policy Recommendations and Future Research Directions

To support the adoption of solar cold-chain technology at scale:

1.  **Policy Support:** Governments should establish policies to incentivize the development and implementation of these integrated systems.
2.  **Capacity Building:** Training programs for farmers, technicians, and policymakers are essential to ensure successful project implementation.
3.  **Community Engagement:** Community-led initiatives can foster acceptance and benefits from these integrated solutions.

## Closing Outlook

The Horn of Africa's fisheries sector is at the forefront of climate-related challenges. By leveraging solar cold-chain technology, we can enhance water security, clean energy use, and climate adaptation in vulnerable communities.
