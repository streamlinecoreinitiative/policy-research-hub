# micro-insurance for smallholder drought risk in the Sahel: improving health outcomes in heatwaves

**Water Security, Clean Energy, and Climate Adaptation in Sub-Saharan Africa: Strengthening Resilience for Sustainable Development**

### Executive Summary

This policy brief highlights the critical need to enhance water security, promote clean energy adoption, and bolster climate adaptation efforts in Sub-Saharan Africa. With only 32.4% of the region's population having access to safely managed drinking water (World Bank Open Data, 2024) [1] and an electricity access rate of 53.3% (World Bank Open Data, 2023), urgent action is required. We recommend investing in infrastructure projects like rainwater harvesting systems, solar-powered wells, and micro-insurance programs for smallholder farmers to enhance water security.

### Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa's population continues to grow while facing severe challenges related to access to safe drinking water and electricity. The region's population has limited access to safely managed drinking water, with only 32.4% of the population having access (World Bank Open Data, 2024) [1], compared to a global average of 73.7% (World Bank Open Data, 2024) [2]. Additionally, just over half (53.3%) of Sub-Saharan Africa's population has access to electricity (World Bank Open Data, 2023), significantly lower than the world's rate of 91.6% (World Bank Open Data, 2023) [3].

### Analysis

The data underscores a pressing need for enhanced water security and clean energy initiatives in Sub-Saharan Africa. Water scarcity is exacerbated by climate variability, leading to reduced agricultural productivity and increased health risks from contaminated drinking water (UN-Water/WHO, 2022). Similarly, the region's limited electricity access hinders economic growth and exacerbates poverty (World Bank Open Data, 2023).

Renewable energy consumption in Sub-Saharan Africa is notably low at just over [VERIFY] 19% of total final energy consumption (World Bank Open Data, 2020), falling far short of global averages. This underutilization not only limits the region's ability to meet growing energy demands but also contributes to climate change impacts such as increased air pollution and biodiversity loss.

### Policy Recommendations

To address these challenges, we recommend the following policy actions:

1. **Enhance Water Security**: Invest in infrastructure projects like rainwater harvesting systems, solar-powered wells, and micro-insurance programs for smallholder farmers.
	* Implement community-based watershed management initiatives to improve water resource governance (World Bank Open Data, 2024).
2. **Promote Clean Energy Adoption**: Encourage the deployment of renewable energy sources such as solar photovoltaics in rural areas.
	* Provide subsidies or concessional loans for microgrids and off-grid systems that support small-scale clinics and schools (World Bank Open Data, 2023).
3. **Support Climate Adaptation Efforts**: Develop early-warning systems using last-mile alerts to inform communities about potential climate impacts like droughts and floods.
	* Implement climate-resilient agricultural practices such as crop diversification and improved water management techniques (NOAA climate data).

### Implementation Considerations

Implementing these recommendations requires a coordinated effort involving multiple stakeholders, including government agencies, international development partners, NGOs, and local communities. Key considerations include:

1. **Funding**: Secure adequate funding from domestic budgets, foreign aid, and private investments to finance water security and energy infrastructure projects.
2. **Capacity Building**: Train local staff in water management, renewable energy technologies, and climate adaptation strategies to ensure sustainable implementation.

### Conclusion

Building resilient societies in Sub-Saharan Africa requires concerted efforts towards enhancing water security, promoting clean energy adoption, and bolstering climate adaptation. By focusing on targeted interventions and leveraging existing resources, the region can make significant strides toward achieving sustainable development goals.
