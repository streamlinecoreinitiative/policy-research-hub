# climate-resilient school feeding supply chains in the Horn of Africa: improving health outcomes in heatwaves

**Enhancing Water Security, Clean Energy Access, and Climate Adaptation in Sub-Saharan Africa: Feasible Interventions for Sustainable Development**

## Executive Summary

Sub-Saharan Africa faces significant environmental challenges, including severe water scarcity, limited access to electricity, and frequent climate-related shocks. To address these issues, we recommend implementing community-based water management systems, promoting renewable energy projects, and enhancing early warning systems for climate events. These interventions can improve health outcomes, increase economic growth, and enhance the region's resilience to climate impacts.

## Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa is characterized by significant water scarcity, with only 32.4% of the population having access to safely managed drinking water [Source: World Bank Open Data, 2024]. Similarly, electricity access stands at 53.3%, underscoring the region's energy poverty [Source: World Bank Open Data, 2023]. These challenges are compounded by climate change, leading to more frequent and severe droughts and floods, which disproportionately affect vulnerable populations.

## Analysis

Sub-Saharan Africa has made progress in renewable energy consumption, with 9.7% of total final energy consumption coming from renewable sources [Source: IRENA, "World Energy Transformation", 2024]. However, this remains far below the global average. The lack of access to safe water and electricity is a major barrier to economic development, education, and health outcomes for millions of people living in poverty.
