# Water Loss Reduction in Secondary Cities in the Lower Mekong Delta: Stabilizing Food Security during Droughts

## Executive Summary

The Lower Mekong delta region is prone to severe droughts, affecting food security and economic stability of secondary cities. This brief highlights the importance of integrated solutions for water management, focusing on agroforestry-based micro-hydro systems. These innovative approaches can enhance irrigation efficiency, reduce water loss, and promote climate resilience in agriculture.

## Introduction to Water Scarcity in the Lower Mekong Delta

The Lower Mekong delta region is characterized by:

* Limited access to reliable water sources
* High population growth rates
* Unsustainable agricultural practices contributing to soil degradation and water pollution
* Increased frequency and severity of droughts due to climate change

These factors exacerbate water scarcity, threatening food security and economic stability.

Existing irrigation systems in the region are often inefficient, with high operational costs. Socio-economic factors, such as poverty and lack of infrastructure, further contribute to water scarcity.
