# Climate-Smart Irrigation Cooperatives in Caribbean SIDS: Enhancing Water Security and Renewable Energy Use

## Executive Summary

Climate change poses significant challenges to small island developing states (SIDS) in the Caribbean, particularly in terms of water security and energy access. Traditional irrigation methods are often energy-intensive and costly, exacerbating economic vulnerabilities for smallholder farmers. This brief explores the potential of climate-smart irrigation cooperatives that integrate agroforestry with micro-hydro systems to enhance both water security and renewable energy use.

## Introduction to Water Scarcity in Caribbean SIDS

The Caribbean region is highly vulnerable to climate change impacts, including rising temperatures, increased frequency of extreme weather events, and altered precipitation patterns. These changes have severe consequences for agriculture, which accounts for a significant portion of the regional economy. Erratic rainfall patterns and increased evaporation from wetlands exacerbate water scarcity, threatening food security and economic stability.

## Current Irrigation Practices and Challenges

Traditional irrigation systems in Caribbean SIDS often rely on diesel generators or fossil fuel-based pumps, leading to high energy costs and greenhouse gas emissions. These methods also result in significant water loss through evaporation and seepage, further straining already limited water resources. Smallholder farmers face substantial economic challenges due to these inefficiencies.

## Micro-Hydro Systems for Irrigation Efficiency

Micro-hydro technology offers a promising solution for irrigation efficiency in Caribbean SIDS. Small-scale hydroelectric systems can provide reliable and renewable energy for pumping water, reducing reliance on diesel generators and lowering emissions.

## Integration of Agroforestry and Micro-Hydro in Climate-Resilient Water Supply

Pilot projects demonstrate the potential of combining agroforestry with micro-hydro to enhance climate resilience. These integrated systems not only improve irrigation efficiency but also promote sustainable land use, biodiversity conservation, and carbon sequestration.

## Policy Recommendations

To support the adoption of climate-smart irrigation cooperatives in Caribbean SIDS:

1.  Develop policies that incentivize the integration of agroforestry with micro-hydro systems.
2.  Provide technical assistance and capacity-building programs for smallholder farmers.
3.  Establish financing mechanisms to support the development of micro-hydro infrastructure.
