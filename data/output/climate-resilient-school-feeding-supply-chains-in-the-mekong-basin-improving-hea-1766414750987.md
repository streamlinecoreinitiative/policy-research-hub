# climate-resilient school feeding supply chains in the Mekong basin: improving health outcomes in heatwaves

**Climate-Resilient School Feeding Supply Chains in the Mekong Basin: Improving Health Outcomes in Heatwaves**

### Executive Summary

The Mekong Basin is highly vulnerable to climate change, with rising temperatures and changing precipitation patterns threatening food security, water availability, and human health. The school feeding program in this region is particularly susceptible to these impacts due to its reliance on fragile supply chains that are often disrupted by extreme weather events.

### Introduction to Water Scarcity

Climate change is altering precipitation patterns in the Mekong Basin, leading to more frequent droughts and floods. This variability affects agricultural production, water availability, and human health, particularly for vulnerable populations such as schoolchildren. The school feeding program relies on locally sourced food, making it essential to address these challenges through climate-resilient supply chains.

### Current Practices and Challenges

Traditional irrigation methods in the Mekong Basin are energy-intensive and costly, exacerbating economic challenges for smallholder farmers. Existing irrigation systems often rely on diesel generators or fossil fuels, contributing to greenhouse gas emissions and air pollution. Water loss through evaporation and seepage is significant, further reducing water availability.
