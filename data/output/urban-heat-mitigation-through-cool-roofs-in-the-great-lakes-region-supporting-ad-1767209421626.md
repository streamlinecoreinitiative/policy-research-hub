# Urban Heat Mitigation through Cool Roofs in the Great Lakes Region: Supporting Adaptation Targets in NDCs

## Executive Summary

Climate change exacerbates urban heat island effects, posing significant challenges to public health, infrastructure, and economic development worldwide. The Great Lakes region is particularly vulnerable due to its unique climate characteristics. This brief highlights cool roofs as a cost-effective solution for mitigating urban heat stress while supporting adaptation targets outlined in Nationally Determined Contributions (NDCs).

## Current Urban Heat Island Challenges

### Climate Change Impacts on Urban Areas

* Rising temperatures due to climate change intensify urban heat island effects, leading to increased mortality rates and heat-related illnesses.
* The Great Lakes region is susceptible to these impacts, with cities like Chicago and Detroit experiencing significant temperature increases.

### Existing Cooling Strategies and Limitations

* Current cooling strategies focus on air conditioning and mechanical ventilation systems, which are energy-intensive and often inaccessible to low-income communities.
* These solutions contribute to greenhouse gas emissions, exacerbating the urban heat island effect.

## Cool Roofs as a Solution for Urban Heat Mitigation

### Benefits of Cool Roofs

* **Energy Efficiency**: Cool roofs reduce building cooling demands by decreasing energy consumption and costs. [verify]
* **Heat Island Reduction**: By reflecting solar radiation, cool roofs can lower ambient temperatures.
* **Cost-Effectiveness**: The initial investment in cool roof materials is often offset by reduced energy bills within a few years.

### Case Studies and Pilot Projects

* Successful implementations of cool roof initiatives have been documented in cities like Chicago, Illinois; Detroit, Michigan; and Toronto, Ontario.
* These projects demonstrate the potential for cool roofs to improve public health and reduce energy consumption.
