### Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa faces significant development challenges, including limited infrastructure and resources. The region's population is severely affected by water scarcity, energy poverty, and climate impacts, exacerbating existing vulnerabilities. According to the World Bank (2024), only 32.4% of Sub-Saharan Africa's population has access to safely managed drinking water, compared to 73.7% globally (World Bank Open Data). This disparity highlights significant gaps in water security.

### Analysis

The region's energy landscape is also characterized by a lack of access to electricity, with only 53.3% of the population having access, significantly lower than the global average of 91.6% (World Bank, 2023).
