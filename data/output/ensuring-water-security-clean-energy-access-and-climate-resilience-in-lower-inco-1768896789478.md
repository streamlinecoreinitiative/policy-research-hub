**Ensuring Water Security, Clean Energy Access, and Climate Resilience in Lower-Income Countries**

## Executive Summary

Immediate action is needed to enhance water security, ensure clean energy access, and build resilience against climate change impacts in lower-income countries. We recommend investing in infrastructure projects like microgrids for rural clinics, which can provide stable electricity and improve healthcare outcomes (e.g., reducing heat stress on health workers). Additionally, we suggest promoting renewable energy investments through grants and subsidies to increase the share of renewables in final energy consumption.

## Context & Challenge

Lower-income countries face significant challenges in ensuring water security, clean energy access, and climate resilience due to limited resources and infrastructure. According to World Bank data, Sub-Saharan Africa has the lowest access to safely managed drinking water (32.4%) compared to 73.7% globally [1]. Similarly, renewable energy use remains underdeveloped in many regions, particularly in lower-income countries, with only 53.3% of the population having access and renewable energy consumption accounting for just 19.7% of total final energy consumption worldwide [2].

Climate change exacerbates existing vulnerabilities and threatens progress on these fronts. Rising temperatures and changing precipitation patterns lead to more frequent droughts and floods, further straining already limited resources.

## Analysis

Water scarcity affects millions of people's health and livelihoods, contributing to poverty and malnutrition (2.2 billion lacking safe water globally) [3]. Limited access to clean energy hinders economic development and increases vulnerability to climate shocks. Climate change impacts amplify existing challenges by causing more frequent droughts and floods.

## Policy Recommendations

1. **Invest in microgrid infrastructure**: Allocate funds for microgrid projects in rural clinics, which can provide stable electricity and improve healthcare outcomes.
	* Costs for microgrids can range from $20,000 to $100,000 per clinic based on project scale and technology type [4].
2. **Promote renewable energy investments**: Offer grants and subsidies to increase the share of renewables in final energy consumption.
	* Renewable energy investments require government support through policy incentives like tax credits and grants [5].
3. **Implement climate adaptation strategies**: Develop early warning systems and community-based disaster risk reduction initiatives.
	* Community engagement is crucial for successful climate adaptation projects; involve local leaders in planning and decision-making processes.

## Implementation Considerations

Implementation of these recommendations requires careful consideration of costs, feasibility, and funding mechanisms. Costs for microgrid projects can range from $20,000 to $100,000 per clinic based on project scale and technology type [4]. Renewable energy investments require government support through policy incentives like tax credits and grants [5].

## Conclusion

Immediate action is needed to enhance water security, ensure clean energy access, and build resilience against climate change impacts in lower-income countries. We urge governments, NGOs, and the private sector to partner and mobilize resources and expertise for sustainable development goals.
