# Flood-Resilient Transport Corridors in the Central America Dry Corridor: Protecting Livelihoods Under Climate Stress

## Executive Summary

The Central America dry corridor is highly vulnerable to climate change, characterized by frequent droughts and floods that severely impact agriculture, economy, and human well-being. This brief highlights the importance of flood-resilient transport corridors in ensuring food security, economic stability, and community resilience under climate stress.

## Introduction to Water Scarcity

Climate change exacerbates water scarcity in the Central America dry corridor through erratic rainfall patterns and increased evaporation from vast wetlands. Traditional irrigation methods are energy-intensive, costly, and unsustainable, further straining economic challenges for smallholder farmers in vulnerable communities.

## Current Irrigation Practices and Challenges

Existing irrigation systems suffer from high water loss through evaporation and seepage. These inefficiencies exacerbate the already dire situation of water scarcity, affecting agriculture, livestock, and human consumption. The lack of reliable and efficient irrigation systems puts smallholder farmers at risk of crop failure, reduced yields, and economic hardship.

## Micro-Hydro Systems for Irrigation Efficiency

Micro-hydro technology provides a cost-effective and efficient solution to traditional irrigation methods. Small-scale hydroelectric systems harness the energy of water flow, reducing reliance on diesel generators and increasing energy efficiency. This approach not only conserves water but also generates renewable energy, contributing to climate resilience.

## Integration of Agroforestry and Micro-Hydro in Climate-Resilient Water Supply

Pilot projects demonstrate the successful integration of agroforestry and micro-hydro systems for efficient irrigation. Economic analysis shows that these integrated solutions are cost-effective and potentially scalable. By combining agroforestry's ecological benefits with micro-hydro's energy efficiency, communities can enhance water security, reduce energy costs, and promote climate resilience.
