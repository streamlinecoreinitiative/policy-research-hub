## Executive Summary

Sub-Saharan Africa faces significant water security challenges, with only 32.4% of the population having access to safely managed drinking water (World Bank Open Data). ## Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa is one of the most vulnerable regions to climate change, with unpredictable rainfall patterns and increased frequency of droughts severely affecting water security (IPCC 2020). The region also struggles with limited access to safe drinking water, with only 32.4% of the population having access to safely managed drinking water (World Bank Open Data). Furthermore, the intersection of climate adaptation, renewable energy, and water management is critical for sustainable development in the region.
