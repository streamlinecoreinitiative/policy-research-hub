## Executive Summary
To address the pressing challenges of water security, clean energy access, and climate adaptation in Sub-Saharan Africa, we recommend investing in rainwater harvesting and improved water storage systems. This initiative will increase water storage capacity, provide subsidies for households and small-scale farmers, and enhance resilience to droughts and floods. Our estimated cost for this intervention is $3 billion over five years.

## Context & Challenge
Sub-Saharan Africa faces significant challenges with water security, clean energy access, and climate adaptation. The region's population has only 32.4% access to safely managed drinking water (World Bank, 2024), while less than half of Sub-Saharan African countries achieve even basic electricity access (World Bank, 2023). Climate change impacts such as droughts and floods threaten agricultural productivity, exacerbating food insecurity in the region.

## Policy Recommendations
1. **Invest in Rainwater Harvesting and Improved Water Storage Systems**:
   - Implement community-based rainwater harvesting projects to increase water storage capacity.
   - Provide subsidies for improved water storage tanks to households and small-scale farmers (World Bank, 2024).
   - Estimate cost: $3 billion over five years.

2. **Promote Renewable Energy Technologies**:
   - Subsidize the installation of solar home systems in rural areas where grid access is limited.
   - Encourage the use of biogas digesters for cooking to reduce reliance on wood and charcoal (World Bank, 2023).
   - Estimate cost: $1 billion over five years.

- **Cost**: Total estimated costs for the recommended interventions amount to approximately $4.6 billion over a five-year period.
