**Ensuring Water Security, Clean Energy, and Climate Adaptation in Sub-Saharan Africa**

## Executive Summary

Sub-Saharan Africa faces significant challenges in water security, clean energy access, and climate adaptation due to its limited infrastructure, economic constraints, and vulnerability to climate change impacts (World Bank Open Data, 2024). To address these issues, we recommend implementing microgrids for rural clinics, investing in watershed co-management with indigenous councils, and promoting solar-powered cold-chain solutions. These interventions can improve health outcomes while stabilizing food security during droughts.

Key Findings:

* Sub-Saharan Africa has significant gaps in access to safe drinking water (32.4%) and electricity (53.3%), with only 19.7% of its total final energy consumption coming from renewable sources.
* [VERIFY] Implementing microgrids for rural clinics can improve healthcare access and outcomes, reducing mortality rates during heatwaves.

Recommendations:

1. **Microgrids for Rural Clinics**:
	* Provide solar-powered microgrids in rural clinics, ensuring reliable power for essential medical services.
	* Cost: $500-$700 per clinic (Solar Sister).
2. **Watershed Co-Management with Indigenous Councils**:
	* Collaborate with indigenous communities for watershed management to enhance natural water resources.
	* Cost: $100-$200 per household (World Bank).
3. **Solar-Powered Cold-Chain Solutions**:
	* Deploy solar-powered refrigeration units for fish processing facilities in peri-urban areas.
	* Cost: $1,500-$2,500 per unit (Solar Sister).

## Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa struggles with inadequate water access and limited energy availability, compounded by climate variability leading to frequent extreme weather events such as floods, droughts, and heatwaves. These challenges exacerbate poverty, food insecurity, and health risks.

* Access to safely managed drinking water in Sub-Saharan Africa: 32.4% [Source: World Bank Open Data, 2024].
* Renewable energy consumption in the region: a mere 19.7%, falling short of SDG targets (World Bank Open Data, 2020).
