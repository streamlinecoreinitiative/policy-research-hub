**Enhancing Water Security, Clean Energy, and Climate Resilience in Sub-Saharan Africa: A Dual Focus on Renewable Energy and Safe Drinking Water**

## Executive Summary
Sub-Saharan Africa faces critical challenges in accessing safe drinking water and reliable electricity, exacerbated by climate variability. To address these issues, we recommend prioritizing renewable energy solutions and safe drinking water initiatives to boost resilience. This policy brief outlines the current state of affairs, evidence-based analysis, and actionable recommendations for governments, NGOs, and international organizations.

## Context & Challenge
Sub-Saharan Africa is severely lacking in essential infrastructure like clean water and electricity. According to World Bank data, only 32.4% of the population has access to safely managed drinking water (World Bank Open Data, 2024), compared to 73.7% globally (World Bank Open Data, 2024). Similarly, only 53.3% of the population has access to electricity, a significant gap from the global average of 91.6% (World Bank Open Data, 2023). Climate change intensifies water scarcity and energy deficits, threatening social stability, health, and economic progress.

## Analysis
Our analysis reveals that Sub-Saharan Africa lags behind other regions in safe drinking water access and electricity availability:

* Electricity Access: Only 53.3% of the population has access to electricity in Sub-Saharan Africa (World Bank Open Data, 2023), whereas globally, 91.6% have access (World Bank Open Data, 2023).
