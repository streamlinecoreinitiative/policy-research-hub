## Policy Recommendations

To address the challenges outlined, government officials and development practitioners should prioritize the following interventions:

1. **Enhance Access to Safe Drinking Water**:
	* Implement large-scale infrastructure projects such as boreholes, rainwater harvesting systems, and hand pumps in underserved areas (World Bank, 2024).
	* Invest in community-led water management programs that promote hygiene practices and sanitation facilities (UN-Water/WHO, 2022).

2. **Promote Renewable Energy Adoption**:
	* Encourage the adoption of solar-powered technologies for off-grid electrification through subsidies and grants (World Bank, 2023).
	* Invest in renewable energy projects that utilize solar, wind, and hydropower to provide reliable electricity services (IRENA, 2024).

3. **Build Climate Resilience**:
	* Develop early warning systems for climate-induced hazards such as droughts and floods using satellite data and ground-based sensors (NOAA, 2026).
	* Implement water harvesting techniques like rainwater collection and storage to mitigate seasonal variations in rainfall (World Bank, 2024).

## Implementation Considerations

To ensure successful implementation of these recommendations, several practical steps must be taken:

- **Funding**: Secure funding through international development agencies, private sector investments, and domestic resources. A dedicated trust fund for climate-resilient infrastructure could leverage additional investment.
- **Community Engagement**: Engage local communities in planning and decision-making processes to ensure their needs are met and ownership of initiatives is high (World Bank, 2024).
- **Capacity Building**: Train personnel from water management agencies and renewable energy companies on best practices for sustainable development projects.
