# Executive Summary

**Strengthening Environmental Resilience in Sub-Saharan Africa: Ensuring Water Security, Promoting Clean Energy, and Enhancing Climate Adaptation**

## Executive Summary
To address the critical water security crisis in Sub-Saharan Africa, we recommend investing $10 billion over the next five years in infrastructure projects such as well drilling and desalination plants to improve access to clean water. This investment will increase access to safely managed drinking water from 32.4% (World Bank Open Data) [1] to at least 50% of the population by 2028, aligning with Sustainable Development Goal 6 (UN SDG Report 2023). Furthermore, we recommend increasing subsidies and providing incentives for renewable energy investments in off-grid areas to promote clean energy.

## Context & Challenge
Sub-Saharan Africa faces a triple challenge of inadequate access to clean water, limited energy sources, and increasing climate variability. The region is home to [VERIFY] % (UN-Water/WHO) of the world's population living on less than $1.90 per day, making it one of the poorest regions globally. Despite being rich in natural resources such as water and renewable energy, these resources are not equitably distributed or accessible to all. Water scarcity, exacerbated by climate change, impacts agriculture, health outcomes, and economic development.

## Analysis
The data shows stark disparities in water and electricity access within Sub-Saharan Africa:
- Access to safely managed drinking water: 32.4% of the population (World Bank Open Data) [1], compared to 73.7% worldwide (World Bank Open Data) [2].
- Access to electricity: 53.3% of the population (World Bank Open Data) [3], while globally it is 91.6% (World Bank Open Data) [4].

These figures highlight significant gaps that need immediate attention. Climate change further compounds these challenges by increasing variability in rainfall and temperature patterns.

## Policy Recommendations
To address the water security crisis:
1. Invest $2 billion over the next two years in infrastructure projects such as well drilling and desalination plants to improve access to clean water.
2. Implement rainwater harvesting systems and water recycling technologies, including greywater reuse and wastewater treatment facilities.
3. Promote community-based initiatives for managing water resources sustainably.

For promoting clean energy:
1. Increase subsidies by [VERIFY] % over the next three years to encourage renewable energy investments in off-grid areas.
2. Encourage the adoption of solar-powered well pumps to reduce reliance on diesel generators and improve energy access.
3. Develop policies that mandate a certain percentage of electricity generation from renewables, particularly small-scale hydroelectric projects in rural regions.

To enhance climate adaptation:
1. Establish early-warning systems and last-mile alerts for droughts, floods, and other extreme weather events.
2. Invest $500 million over the next five years in coastal defenses to protect communities from rising sea levels.
3. Support farmers in adopting climate-resilient agricultural practices through extension services and financial support.

## Implementation Considerations
Implementing these recommendations will require substantial investment. The costs of infrastructure projects can be covered by grants, loans, and domestic resources. For clean energy initiatives, subsidies and tax incentives are effective measures to encourage private sector involvement. Climate adaptation measures should leverage existing networks such as the World Bank and regional development banks.

## Conclusion
Sub-Saharan Africa's environmental resilience is under threat from water scarcity, limited access to electricity, and climate change impacts. Addressing these challenges requires immediate and targeted interventions that balance short-term needs with long-term sustainability goals. By investing in infrastructure improvements, promoting clean energy sources, and enhancing climate adaptation measures, Sub-Saharan Africa can build a more resilient future for its people.
