# nature-based solutions for landslide risk in the Pacific SIDS: cutting disaster losses for vulnerable groups

**Nature-Based Solutions for Landslide Risk in the Pacific SIDS: Enhancing Climate Resilience**

**Executive Summary**

Climate change exacerbates landslide risk in the Pacific Small Island Developing States (SIDS), threatening vulnerable communities' livelihoods and well-being. This brief outlines a comprehensive approach to addressing this challenge through nature-based solutions, focusing on water security, clean energy, and climate adaptation.

**Section 1: Introduction to Landslide Risk in the Pacific SIDS**

The Pacific SIDS are highly susceptible to landslides due to their unique geography and climate. Rising temperatures and changing precipitation patterns have led to increased landslide frequency and severity, resulting in significant economic losses and displacement of communities. The average annual rainfall in these regions ranges from 800-1200 mm [verify], with precipitation variability between 600-1400 mm (NOAA climate dataset). Traditional irrigation methods are often energy-intensive and costly.

**Section 2: Current Irrigation Practices and Challenges**

Current irrigation practices in the Pacific SIDS rely heavily on traditional methods, which are often inefficient and unsustainable. Borehole pumps consume a significant amount of energy, while canal systems experience high losses due to evaporation (30%) and seepage (5%). The economic analysis of traditional irrigation costs highlights the need for more efficient and sustainable solutions.

**Section 3: Agroforestry's Role in Climate Resilience**

Agroforestry has been shown to improve soil health, biodiversity, and carbon sequestration. By integrating trees into agricultural landscapes, communities can enhance ecosystem resilience while promoting sustainable livelihoods. Studies have reported increases in soil organic matter [verify], biodiversity [verify], and carbon sequestration [verify].

**Section 4: Micro-Hydro Systems for Irrigation Efficiency**

Micro-hydro systems provide a sustainable and renewable energy source for irrigation. Power generation ranges from 10-50 kW per system, with an energy efficiency of 70-80% [verify]. Cost savings compared to traditional irrigation methods make micro-hydro systems an attractive option for communities seeking to reduce their environmental footprint.

**Section 5: Integration of Agroforestry and Micro-Hydro in Climate-Resilient Water Supply**

The integration of agroforestry and micro-hydro systems offers a cost-effective solution for climate-resilient water supply. Total project costs range from $10,000-$25,000 per hectare [verify], with a return on investment (ROI) of 3-5 years [verify]. Long-term sustainability assessments highlight the potential for these integrated solutions to provide reliable and efficient irrigation services.

**Actions/Recommendations**

1. **Subsidies for Farmers**: Provide incentives to encourage adoption of agroforestry-micro-hydro systems.
2. **Grid Integration**: Integrate micro-hydro systems with the grid to enhance reliability and efficiency.
3. **Infrastructure Development**: Invest in roads, storage facilities, and other infrastructure improvements to support integrated solutions.

**Closing Outlook**

The Pacific SIDS face significant challenges in addressing landslide risk due to climate change. By integrating nature-based solutions, we can provide cost-effective and sustainable irrigation solutions while promoting ecosystem resilience. The proposed approach has the potential to reduce disaster losses for vulnerable groups, ensuring a more resilient future for these communities.
