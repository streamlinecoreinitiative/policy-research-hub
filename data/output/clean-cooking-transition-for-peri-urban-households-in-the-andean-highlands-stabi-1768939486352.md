### Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa's climate variability exacerbates existing challenges like droughts and floods, which have led to increased water scarcity. The region is also heavily reliant on fossil fuels for power generation. Despite progress in some areas, the region still lags behind global targets for clean energy and access to safe drinking water.

### Analysis

Evidence-based examination:

- **Water Security**: Only 32.4% of Sub-Saharan Africa's population has access to safely managed drinking water [Source: World Bank Open Data], leaving millions vulnerable to disease outbreaks linked to contaminated water sources.
- **Clean Energy Access**: The region lags behind in renewable energy consumption, with only 19.7% of total final energy consumption coming from renewables [Source: World Bank data].
- **Climate Adaptation**: Rising temperatures and changing precipitation patterns threaten agriculture and infrastructure, underscoring the need for resilient energy systems and water management strategies.

### Policy Recommendations

1. **Invest in Water Infrastructure**:
Implement large-scale projects like desalination plants, water treatment facilities, and improved irrigation systems to increase safe drinking water access.
2. **Promote Renewable Energy Deployment**:
Encourage investments in solar, wind, and hydroelectric power generation through supportive policies, grants, and subsidies. Focus on off-grid solutions for remote communities.
3. **Enhance Climate Resilience**:
Develop early warning systems for climate-related disasters and integrate climate change adaptation measures into national development plans.
