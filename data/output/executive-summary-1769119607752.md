# Executive Summary

**Enhancing Water Security, Clean Energy Access, and Climate Adaptation in Sub-Saharan Africa: Bridging Gaps for Sustainable Development**

## Executive Summary

Sub-Saharan African countries face critical challenges related to water security, clean energy access, and climate adaptation. To address these issues, we recommend prioritizing investments in clean cooking solutions, renewable energy projects, and climate adaptation measures. Specifically:

* **Invest $500 million over five years** in clean cooking transitions for peri-urban households to reduce emissions and improve indoor air quality.
* **Increase renewable energy consumption by 3% annually** through community solar projects, microgrids, and off-grid mini-grids.
* **Develop early warning systems and strengthen water management infrastructure**, allocating $1 billion over three years for flood-resistant irrigation canals.

These targeted interventions can be implemented at scale with existing resources, focusing on the most pressing needs in the region. By bridging gaps in water security, clean energy access, and climate adaptation, Sub-Saharan Africa can make significant progress towards sustainable development goals.

## Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan African countries are facing significant challenges in accessing safe drinking water (32.4%) [1] and electricity (53.3%) [2], as reported by World Bank data. Renewable energy consumption is only 9.2% of total final energy consumption, according to the same source [2]. These issues not only threaten lives but also hinder economic growth, education, and overall well-being.

## Analysis

The region's water security challenges are evident in the low percentage of people with access to safely managed drinking water (32.4%) compared to other regions like Southeast Asia (76.5%), as per World Bank Open Data [1]. Electricity access is also a significant concern, with only 53.3% of people having access in Sub-Saharan Africa, significantly lower than the global average [2].

The region's reliance on fossil fuels is another pressing issue. Renewable energy consumption is only 9.2% of total final energy consumption, which is below the global average (19.7%) and far from the target of increasing renewable energy to at least 30% by 2030 [3]. The lack of access to electricity and clean energy exacerbates climate adaptation challenges, as communities struggle to cope with extreme weather events.

Implementing these recommendations requires careful consideration of costs, potential barriers, and feasibility. The estimated costs are substantial, but they pale in comparison to the long-term benefits of improved water security, clean energy access, and climate adaptation.

Sub-Saharan Africa must prioritize water security, clean energy access, and climate adaptation to bridge gaps for sustainable development.
