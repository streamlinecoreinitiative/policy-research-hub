**Solar Cold-Chain for Fisheries in the Andean Highlands: Enhancing Food Security During Droughts**

**Executive Summary**

The Andean highlands face severe water scarcity due to climate change, impacting food security and livelihoods of smallholder farmers and fishers. This brief presents a comprehensive solution through agroforestry-based micro-hydro systems for climate-resilient irrigation in the region.

**Introduction**

Climate change affects agriculture in the Andean highlands through erratic rainfall patterns, increased evaporation from wetlands, and reduced water availability for crops. Traditional irrigation methods are energy-intensive and costly, exacerbating economic challenges for smallholder farmers and fishers in vulnerable communities.

**Climate Resilient Irrigation Solutions**

### Agroforestry-Based Micro-Hydro Systems

Agroforestry-based micro-hydro systems can provide a sustainable solution to address water scarcity issues in the Andean highlands. These systems integrate agroforestry practices with small-scale hydroelectric power generation, providing clean energy for irrigation and other agricultural needs.
