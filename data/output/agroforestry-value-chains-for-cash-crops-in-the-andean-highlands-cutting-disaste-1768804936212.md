# agroforestry value chains for cash crops in the Andean highlands: cutting disaster losses for vulnerable groups

**Agroforestry-Based Micro-Hydro Systems for Climate-Resilient Irrigation in the Andean Highlands**

**Executive Summary**

The Andean highlands face severe water scarcity due to historical droughts and limited access to reliable irrigation systems, exacerbating economic instability caused by reduced crop yields. Agroforestry-based micro-hydro systems offer a solution by integrating vegetation cover with micro-hydroelectric power generation for efficient irrigation. This approach can improve water retention and recharge, enhance ecosystem services, and reduce vulnerability to climate variability.

**Addressing Water Scarcity in the Andean Highlands**

The Andean highlands are characterized by a semi-arid climate with limited rainfall, making irrigation essential for agriculture. However, traditional methods like surface water and rain-fed systems face challenges due to:

* Limited access to reliable irrigation systems
* Economic instability caused by reduced crop yields
* High dependency on imported fuels

Farmers in the region rely heavily on these traditional methods, which exacerbate existing challenges.

**Agroforestry and Micro-Hydro Systems: A Viable Solution**

Agroforestry practices offer several benefits for addressing water scarcity:

* Improved water retention and recharge through increased vegetation cover
* Enhanced ecosystem services such as pollination, pest control, and carbon sequestration

Case studies from similar climates or contexts demonstrate the effectiveness of agroforestry in improving agricultural productivity and reducing vulnerability to climate variability.
