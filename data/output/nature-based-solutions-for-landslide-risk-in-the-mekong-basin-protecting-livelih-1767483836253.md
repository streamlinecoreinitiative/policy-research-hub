# Nature-Based Solutions for Landslide Risk in the Mekong Basin: Protecting Livelihoods Under Climate Stress

## Executive Summary

The Mekong Basin is a disaster-prone region facing severe landslide risks exacerbated by climate change. This brief presents a nature-based solution to mitigate these risks through agroforestry-based micro-hydro systems for climate-resilient irrigation in lower-income countries.

## Introduction to Water Scarcity in the Mekong Basin

Climate change is impacting agriculture in the region, leading to water scarcity. Resilient irrigation methods are crucial for food security and economic stability. Existing irrigation systems suffer from high water loss percentages due to evaporation and seepage.

## Micro-Hydro Systems for Irrigation Efficiency

Micro-hydro technology involves small-scale hydroelectric systems that reduce reliance on diesel generators and increase energy efficiency. Pilot projects showcase the combined use of agroforestry and micro-hydro, highlighting cost-effectiveness and potential for scaling up.
