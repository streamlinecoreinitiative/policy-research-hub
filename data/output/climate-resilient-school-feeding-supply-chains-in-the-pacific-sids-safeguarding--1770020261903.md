# climate-resilient school feeding supply chains in the Pacific SIDS: safeguarding freshwater ecosystems

**Harnessing Renewable Energy for Water Security in Sub-Saharan Africa**

## Executive Summary

Sub-Saharan Africa faces significant challenges in accessing safe drinking water and reliable electricity, with only 32.4% of the population having access to safely managed drinking water [1] and 53.3% having access to electricity [2]. These gaps not only threaten basic human rights but also impede economic development and climate resilience. To address these challenges, we recommend investing in renewable energy for water projects to enhance resilience.

## Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa is experiencing rapid population growth, urbanization, and climate change, which exacerbate existing water scarcity and power deficits [3]. The region's economy heavily relies on agriculture, industry, and energy sectors, all of which require significant amounts of water. Climate change further complicates these challenges by altering precipitation patterns, increasing evaporation rates, and intensifying droughts [4].

## Analysis

Renewable energy can play a pivotal role in addressing water scarcity and enhancing resilience in Sub-Saharan Africa. The region has vast renewable energy resources, including solar, wind, geothermal, and hydroelectric power.

Investing in renewable energy can help reduce water scarcity by:

* Reducing reliance on diesel generators and other fossil fuels that consume large amounts of water for cooling.
* Increasing energy efficiency through hybrid systems combining solar, small-scale hydro, and biomass.
* Promoting sustainable agriculture practices that conserve water resources.

## Policy Recommendations

1. **Integrate renewable energy solutions into national development plans**: Governments should prioritize the integration of renewable energy sources into their national development plans to enhance resilience and address water scarcity.
2. **Prioritize off-grid solar installations for rural areas**: Off-grid solar installations can provide reliable electricity to rural communities, reducing reliance on diesel generators and promoting sustainable development.
3. **Invest in hybrid systems combining solar, small-scale hydro, and biomass**: Hybrid systems can maximize efficiency and cost-effectiveness while minimizing environmental impacts.

## Implementation Considerations

To overcome initial investment costs, governments and stakeholders should explore grants, subsidies, or public-private partnerships to support renewable energy projects. Community participation and ownership are essential for project sustainability; therefore, ensuring community engagement in project design and maintenance is crucial.

## Conclusion

Harnessing renewable energy for water security is critical for Sub-Saharan Africa's development and climate resilience. We urge governments and stakeholders to collaborate on adopting renewable energy solutions to improve water security and reduce reliance on fossil fuels. Developing a multi-year plan with clear milestones and metrics for success will be essential in achieving this goal.
