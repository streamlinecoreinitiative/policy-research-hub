Early-Warning Systems and Last-Mile Alerts in the Caribbean SIDS: Lowering Emissions from Critical Sectors

Executive Summary

This policy brief highlights critical interventions to enhance environmental resilience in lower-income countries, focusing on water security, clean energy, and climate adaptation. The region faces significant challenges with only 32.4% of its population having access to safe drinking water (World Bank Open Data) and a renewable energy consumption rate of 19.7% (World Bank Open Data). To address these issues, we recommend:

* Implementing targeted interventions for improved water security

  • Increasing renewable energy use through private sector investment in solar power plants and other renewable sources
  • Enhancing climate adaptation measures by investing in weather forecasting infrastructure and promoting afforestation and reforestation programs

    Immediate action is necessary to achieve meaningful progress towards global development goals, including the Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs). Governments, NGOs, and development practitioners should prioritize these interventions as part of their overall development strategies.

    Context & Challenge

    Sub-Saharan Africa is one of the most vulnerable regions in terms of access to safe drinking water and electricity. Climate change exacerbates these challenges, with frequent droughts and floods affecting water availability and agricultural productivity (World Bank Open Data). The region's population lacking safe water stands at 2.2 billion people [NEEDS SOURCE], while only 53.3% have access to electricity (World Bank Open Data).

    Analysis

    Water Security

    Despite significant progress, only 32.4% of the Sub-Saharan African population has access to safe drinking water (World Bank Open Data). This is far below global standards (73.7%) and poses health risks [NEEDS SOURCE]. The region's freshwater withdrawal rate stands at 9.2% of resources (World Bank Open Data), highlighting the need for more efficient use.

    Clean Energy

    Renewable energy consumption in the region is low at 19.7%, but this figure can be improved significantly. Increased use of clean energy can reduce greenhouse gas emissions and improve economic stability.

    Climate Adaptation

    Sub-Saharan Africa's climate resilience remains weak, with frequent droughts and floods affecting water availability and agricultural productivity (World Bank Open Data).

    Policy Recommendations

    Water Security

    1. Expand Water Infrastructure Investments: Invest in desalination plants and boreholes to increase access to safe drinking water across the region.

  • Promote Rainwater Harvesting: Implement policies that incentivize households and communities to use rainwater harvesting systems, reducing reliance on groundwater.

    Clean Energy

    3. Increase Renewable Energy Penetration: Encourage private sector investment in solar power plants and other renewable energy sources by offering tax incentives and subsidies.

  • Microgrids for Rural Clinics: Deploy microgrid technologies in rural clinics to provide reliable electricity while lowering carbon emissions from traditional grid connections.

    Climate Adaptation

    5. Enhance Early Warning Systems: Invest in weather forecasting infrastructure to improve early warning systems, enabling timely responses during extreme weather events.

  • Afforestation and Reforestation Programs: Implement programs that promote afforestation and reforestation to increase green cover and mitigate the effects of climate change.
