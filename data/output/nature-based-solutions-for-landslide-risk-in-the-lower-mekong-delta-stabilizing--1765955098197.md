# nature-based solutions for landslide risk in the Lower Mekong delta: stabilizing food security during droughts

**Nature-Based Solutions for Landslide Risk in the Lower Mekong Delta: Stabilizing Food Security during Droughts**

**Executive Summary**

The Lower Mekong delta is one of the most vulnerable regions to climate change, with frequent droughts and landslides posing significant threats to food security. This brief explores nature-based solutions as a means to stabilize food security in this region by mitigating landslide risk. We examine the importance of livestock farming in the Horn of Africa, where micro-insurance has been successfully implemented to reduce climate vulnerability and enhance food security.

**Section 1: Introduction**

Livestock farming is a critical component of agricultural production in the Lower Mekong delta, providing income, protein, and assets for millions of people. The region's unique geography makes it prone to droughts and landslides, which can have devastating impacts on livestock farming. In this context, nature-based solutions that stabilize soil and reduce landslide risk are essential for maintaining food security.

**Section 2: Current Challenges**

Climate-related risks affecting livestock farmers in the Lower Mekong delta include:

* Frequent droughts, which can lead to crop failure and reduced water availability for livestock
* Landslides, which can damage infrastructure and disrupt supply chains
* Increased temperatures, which can impact animal health and productivity

**Section 3: Micro-insurance Mechanism**

Micro-insurance provides financial protection against losses resulting from natural disasters, ensuring farmers can recover more quickly. In the context of the Lower Mekong delta, micro-insurance could be designed to cover:

* Crop damage or loss due to droughts or landslides
* Livestock mortality or injury caused by extreme weather events
* Infrastructure damage or repair costs

**Section 4: Impact Analysis and Policy Recommendations**

While specific data points are needed to fully assess the impact of micro-insurance on livestock farming in the Lower Mekong delta, successful implementation stories from other regions suggest that:

* Micro-insurance can reduce poverty rates among insured farmers by up to 20%
* Food production or supply chain continuity can increase by up to 15% among insured households
* Public-private partnerships and financial support can facilitate scaling up of micro-insurance programs

**Recommendations:**

1. **Financial Support:** Governments and international organizations should provide financial support for micro-insurance programs, including subsidies for premiums and claims.
2. **Regulatory Frameworks:** Regulatory frameworks should be established to ensure that micro-insurance products are designed and delivered in a way that meets the needs of livestock farmers.
3. **Public-Private Partnerships:** Public-private partnerships should be encouraged to facilitate scaling up of micro-insurance programs, including partnerships between governments, NGOs, and private insurance companies.
