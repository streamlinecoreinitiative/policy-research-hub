Improving Water Security, Clean Energy Access, and Climate Adaptation in Sub-Saharan Africa: A Pathway for Resilient Development

Executive Summary

Sub-Saharan Africa faces significant challenges in water security, clean energy access, and climate adaptation. With only 32.4% of the population having access to safely managed drinking water (World Bank, 2024) [1] and 53.3% having access to electricity (World Bank, 2023) [2], there is a critical need for targeted interventions that can enhance these services while also promoting renewable energy use. We recommend implementing community-based water management systems, expanding renewable energy projects, and enhancing climate adaptation strategies.

Context & Challenge

Access to clean water and electricity is severely lacking in Sub-Saharan Africa, exacerbating health risks and hindering economic development (UN-Water/WHO, 2022) [3]. Climate change compounds existing challenges by increasing the frequency of droughts, floods, and other extreme weather events, making current infrastructures more vulnerable. The region's resilience is further compromised by inadequate infrastructure for water management and renewable energy integration.
