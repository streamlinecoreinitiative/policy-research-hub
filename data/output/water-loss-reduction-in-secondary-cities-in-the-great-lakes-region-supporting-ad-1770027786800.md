# water loss reduction in secondary cities in the Great Lakes region: supporting adaptation targets in NDCs

**Strengthening Water Security, Clean Energy, and Climate Adaptation in Sub-Saharan Africa**

### Executive Summary

Immediate investments are needed to address the water security and clean energy crises in Sub-Saharan Africa. Only 32.4% of the population has access to safely managed drinking water, compared to 73.7% globally [1]. Electricity access is even lower at 53.3% [2], hindering economic development and exacerbating health risks. This policy brief recommends investing in renewable energy sources and improving water infrastructure alongside climate adaptation strategies to achieve SDG 6 (Water Supply & Sanitation) with greater success.

### Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa is characterized by extreme climatic conditions, including frequent droughts, floods, and unpredictable rainfall patterns. These challenges have severely impacted water security, making it difficult for communities to access safe drinking water. The region's reliance on non-renewable energy sources further compounds these issues, contributing to environmental degradation.

### Analysis

Access to safely managed drinking water is a critical issue in Sub-Saharan Africa, with only 32.4% of the population having access compared to 73.7% globally [1]. The region's dependence on fossil fuels for electricity generation leads to high carbon emissions, further straining environmental resilience. To address these challenges, we must prioritize investments in renewable energy sources such as solar, wind, and hydroelectric power.

### Policy Recommendations

1. **Investment in Renewable Energy**: Governments should allocate substantial funds towards developing and implementing renewable energy projects, including solar-powered desalination plants for coastal areas and decentralized solar microgrids for remote communities.
2. **Enhanced Water Infrastructure**: Develop comprehensive water supply systems, including rainwater harvesting facilities and wastewater treatment plants. Prioritize the construction of boreholes and pipelines in underserved regions to ensure equitable access to clean drinking water.
3. **Climate Adaptation Programs**: Implement climate adaptation programs that include early warning systems, flood-resistant infrastructure, and drought-resistant crops. These measures will help communities better withstand extreme weather events.

### Implementation Considerations

While these recommendations are feasible, they require significant upfront investments in technology, training for local technicians, and community engagement to ensure successful implementation. Governments should collaborate with international partners to secure funding and expertise. Additionally, cost-effectiveness analysis must be conducted to identify the most sustainable energy sources within the region's economic capabilities.

### Conclusion

By focusing on water security, clean energy, and climate adaptation, Sub-Saharan Africa can build a more resilient future. Immediate action is crucial to closing the gap in access to safe drinking water and reliable electricity while mitigating climate impacts. Through strategic investments and collaborative efforts, we can achieve significant progress towards sustainable development goals.
