Climate-Resilient School Feeding Supply Chains in the Sahel: Lowering Emissions from Critical Sectors

Executive Summary

To build climate-resilient societies in Sub-Saharan Africa, we recommend investing in enhanced water infrastructure, promoting renewable energy, and enhancing climate adaptation strategies. Specifically, governments and development partners should prioritize improving access to safe drinking water (currently at 32.4% of the population) by constructing and upgrading boreholes, wells, and other water extraction facilities in rural areas [1]. This intervention is crucial for addressing the region's critical challenges in water security, clean energy access, and climate adaptation.

Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa faces significant challenges in water security, clean energy access, and climate adaptation. The region's population has inadequate access to safe drinking water (32.4% of the population) and electricity (53.3%), significantly impacting health, economic productivity, and quality of life [1]. Climate change exacerbates these challenges by reducing freshwater resources and increasing the frequency of extreme weather events such as droughts, floods, and heatwaves [2].

Analysis

Recent data from the World Bank indicates that Sub-Saharan Africa's renewable energy consumption stands at 19.7%, far short of global targets [3]. The region also faces significant challenges in meeting SDG 6 goals for safely managed water supply, with only 74% coverage globally compared to 32.4% within the continent [4].
