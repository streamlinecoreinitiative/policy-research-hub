According to World Bank Open Data [1], only 32.4% of the population has access to safely managed drinking water, while 53.3% have reliable access to electricity [2]. ### Analysis

Access to clean water is essential for health and economic stability. In Sub-Saharan Africa, only 32.4% of the population has access to safely managed drinking water (World Bank Open Data) [1], which increases vulnerability to diseases such as cholera and dysentery. Similarly, electricity remains a challenge with only 53.3% having reliable access (World Bank Open Data) [2]. This lack of clean energy impacts economic development by reducing productivity and exacerbating health issues related to poor sanitation.

### Policy Recommendations

To address these challenges, the following policy recommendations should be prioritized:

1. **Increase Access to Safe Drinking Water**:
   - Invest in large-scale water treatment facilities that provide safe drinking water to rural communities (Source: UN SDG Report 2023).
   - Implement community-based initiatives for rainwater harvesting and sanitation improvements.
   - Provide financial incentives for households to adopt improved water storage systems.

2. **Expand Access to Reliable Electricity**:
   - Develop small-scale solar power plants in remote areas where grid connectivity is not feasible (Source: World Bank Open Data).
   - Encourage private sector investment through grants, tax breaks, and favorable regulations.
   - Promote energy-efficient appliances and technologies for improved household use.

3. **Promote Renewable Energy**:
   - Establish national renewable energy targets aligned with SDG 7 (Renewable Energy) goals.
   - Invest in research and development to improve the affordability of solar technology.
   - Provide training programs for local technicians and engineers to maintain and repair renewable energy systems.
