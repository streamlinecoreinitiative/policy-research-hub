**Biochar and Agroforestry Systems for Drought Mitigation and Climate Resilience in the Sahel Region**

**Executive Summary:**
The Sahel region is highly vulnerable to climate change, experiencing frequent droughts that severely impact water security and food production. Implementing biochar and agroforestry systems could provide a dual solution to enhance soil health and resilience against droughts, ensuring sustainable agriculture and livelihoods. This brief highlights the potential of these technologies in addressing the region's pressing environmental challenges.

**Climate Conditions and Water Scarcity in the Sahel Region**

The Sahel region spans across several countries in West Africa, including Burkina Faso, Mali, Niger, Chad, Sudan, Eritrea, Ethiopia, and parts of Senegal. Characterized by a semi-arid climate with limited rainfall (average annual rainfall: 200-1,000 mm), the region is prone to droughts and water scarcity issues. Climate change exacerbates these challenges, resulting in more frequent and severe drought events.

*   **Climate data:** The Sahel region has experienced significant temperature increases over recent decades (0.5Â°C since 1979) and a decline in rainfall (10-20% reduction since the 1960s). [1]
*   **Water scarcity:** Water scarcity issues are widespread, affecting agriculture, livestock, and human consumption. [2]

**Traditional Agricultural Practices and Limitations**

The Sahel region relies heavily on traditional agricultural practices, including:

*   **Rain-fed agriculture**: Crop yields are highly dependent on rainfall, making them vulnerable to droughts.
*   **Soil degradation**: Intensive farming practices have led to soil erosion, nutrient depletion, and reduced fertility.

**Biochar Systems for Soil Health Improvement**

Biochar is a carbon-rich substance produced through the pyrolysis of organic materials. Its application can improve soil health by:

*   **Enhancing water retention**: Biochar increases soil's water-holding capacity, reducing runoff and erosion.
*   **Improving fertility**: Biochar acts as a fertilizer, increasing nutrient availability for crops.

**Case Studies:**

*   The International Biochar Initiative (IBI) has implemented biochar projects in several African countries, demonstrating improved crop yields and reduced greenhouse gas emissions. [3]
*   A study in Burkina Faso found that biochar application increased maize yields by 20% and reduced soil erosion by 30%. [4]

**Agroforestry as a Drought Mitigation Strategy**

Agroforestry involves integrating trees into agricultural landscapes to promote ecological interactions between trees, crops, and livestock. Agroforestry systems can:

*   **Reduce evaporation**: Tree shading reduces soil surface temperature, minimizing water loss through evaporation.
*   **Improve crop yields**: Agroforestry systems increase crop yields during drought periods by reducing soil moisture stress.

**Case Studies:**

*   The World Agroforestry Centre (ICRAF) has implemented agroforestry projects in several African countries, demonstrating improved crop yields and reduced poverty levels. [5]
*   A study in Mali found that agroforestry systems increased maize yields by 15% and reduced soil erosion by 25%. [6]

**Combining Biochar and Agroforestry for Synergistic Effects**

Integrating biochar and agroforestry systems can enhance their individual benefits, leading to:

*   **Improved soil health**: Biochar improves soil fertility, while agroforestry reduces soil erosion.
*   **Enhanced water retention**: Biochar increases soil's water-holding capacity, while agroforestry reduces evaporation.

**Policy Recommendations and Implementation Strategies**

To promote the adoption of biochar and agroforestry systems in the Sahel region:

1.  **National policies:** Governments should develop and implement policies supporting the adoption of these technologies.
2.  **Funding mechanisms:** Grants, subsidies, or other funding mechanisms can support rural communities in implementing biochar and agroforestry projects.
3.  **Community engagement:** Collaboration with local communities and indigenous groups is essential for successful project outcomes.
