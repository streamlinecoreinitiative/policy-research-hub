## Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa is the most water-stressed region globally, with only 32.4% of its population having access to safely managed drinking water (World Bank Open Data). This disparity exacerbates existing challenges such as climate change impacts and economic development. Effective interventions are crucial for fostering sustainable growth, improving public health outcomes, and ensuring long-term resilience.

Background:

* Sub-Saharan Africa's population is projected to reach 2 billion by 2050, putting additional pressure on already strained water resources (World Bank Open Data).
* Climate change is expected to exacerbate droughts and floods in the region, further straining water security (IPCC Report).
