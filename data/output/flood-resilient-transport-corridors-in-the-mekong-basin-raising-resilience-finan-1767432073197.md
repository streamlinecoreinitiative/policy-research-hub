**Flood-Resilient Transport Corridors in the Mekong Basin: Enhancing Resilience through Integrated Solutions**

### Executive Summary

The Mekong Basin is highly vulnerable to climate change, experiencing severe flooding and droughts that impact millions of people. The region's transport infrastructure is crucial for economic growth but often poorly designed and maintained, exacerbating flood risks. This brief presents a comprehensive approach to building flood-resilient transport corridors in the Mekong Basin by integrating agroforestry-based micro-hydro systems with climate-resilient irrigation methods.

### Main Sections

#### **Introduction to Water Scarcity and Climate Change in the Mekong Basin**

The Mekong Basin is home to approximately 60 million people, relying heavily on agriculture and water resources for their livelihoods. Climate change has led to increased frequency and severity of floods and droughts, threatening food security and economic stability.

#### **Current Irrigation Practices and Challenges**

Existing irrigation systems in the Mekong Basin are often inefficient and unsustainable, resulting in significant water loss through evaporation and seepage. Agroforestry-based micro-hydro systems offer a promising solution, providing clean energy while improving soil health and biodiversity.

Micro-hydro technology is a small-scale hydroelectric system that can provide clean energy while reducing reliance on diesel generators. This approach has been successfully implemented in various parts of the world, offering significant potential for scaling up in the Mekong Basin.
