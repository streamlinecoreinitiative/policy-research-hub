**Executive Summary**

This brief focuses on enhancing environmental resilience in lower-income countries, specifically in the context of water security, clean energy, and climate adaptation. The proposal centers around early-warning systems and last-mile alerts for small island developing states (SIDS) in the Caribbean region. By integrating these technologies with national determined contributions (NDCs), SIDS can strengthen their capacity to adapt to climate change impacts.

**I. Introduction**

Climate change poses significant challenges to the environmental resilience of lower-income countries, particularly those in the Caribbean SIDS. Rising sea levels, increased frequency and intensity of extreme weather events, and unpredictable rainfall patterns exacerbate existing development challenges. To address these issues, it is essential to integrate climate-resilient water management practices with clean energy solutions.
