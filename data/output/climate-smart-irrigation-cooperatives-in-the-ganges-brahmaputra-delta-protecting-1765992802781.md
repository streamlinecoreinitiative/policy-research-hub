**Climate-Smart Irrigation Cooperatives in the Ganges-Brahmaputra Delta: Protecting Livelihoods under Climate Stress**

**Executive Summary**

The Ganges-Brahmaputra delta is one of the most densely populated and economically vital regions in South Asia, yet it faces significant environmental challenges due to climate change. Rising temperatures, changing precipitation patterns, and increased salinity levels threaten the livelihoods of millions of people who depend on agriculture, fishing, and other water-based activities. This brief highlights the importance of integrating climate-smart irrigation cooperatives with clean energy solutions and adaptation strategies to enhance water security, reduce energy poverty, and improve resilience in the region.

**Section 1: Introduction to the Ganges-Brahmaputra Delta**

The Ganges-Brahmaputra delta spans across India, Bangladesh, and parts of Nepal and Bhutan. The region's geography is characterized by a vast network of rivers, canals, and wetlands that support rich biodiversity and provide livelihoods for millions of people. However, the delta faces significant climate-related challenges, including:

* Rising sea levels and increased salinity levels affecting agricultural productivity
* Changes in precipitation patterns leading to droughts and floods
* Increased frequency and severity of extreme weather events

**Section 3: Agroforestry Systems and Renewable Energy Integration**

Agroforestry systems offer a promising solution for enhancing water security, energy efficiency, and climate resilience in the Ganges-Brahmaputra delta. By integrating trees with crops and livestock, agroforestry:

* Improves soil health through reduced erosion and increased organic matter
* Increases biodiversity by providing habitat for wildlife and pollinators
* Enhances water cycling through reduced evaporation rates and increased groundwater recharge

Renewable energy solutions, such as solar-powered irrigation systems, can further enhance water security by reducing dependence on fossil fuels and decreasing greenhouse gas emissions.
