**Urban Heat Mitigation through Cool Roofs in the Horn of Africa: Supporting Adaptation Targets in NDCs**

## Executive Summary

The Horn of Africa is one of the most vulnerable regions to climate change, with extreme temperatures and heatwaves posing significant risks to human health and economic development. To address this challenge, we recommend implementing cool roof technologies as a cost-effective solution for urban heat mitigation. By investing in cool roofs, governments can reduce energy consumption, improve air quality, and enhance community resilience.

## Context & Challenge

The Horn of Africa is experiencing rising temperatures, with average annual temperatures projected to increase by 2-3°C by 2050 (IPCC, 2021). This trend exacerbates heat-related illnesses, particularly among vulnerable populations such as the elderly and young children. Moreover, high temperatures reduce crop yields, impacting food security and economic growth.

## Policy Recommendations

1. **Invest in cool roof technologies**:
	* Collaborate with international development organizations to provide grants and technical assistance for large-scale cool roof projects.
	* Encourage private sector investment through tax incentives and low-interest loans for small-scale cool roof installations.
	* Develop and implement building codes that mandate the use of cool roofs in new construction (Source: International Energy Agency).
2. **Promote renewable energy adoption**:
	* Invest in solar power plants, particularly in rural areas where access to electricity is lowest (National Renewable Energy Laboratory, 2020).
	* Provide subsidies or grants for households and small businesses to install solar panels (World Bank Open Data, 2023).
	* Develop mini-grids using renewable energy sources such as solar and wind to provide reliable energy access (International Energy Agency, 2022).

## Conclusion

The Horn of Africa is facing unprecedented climate-related challenges, including rising temperatures and heatwaves. By investing in cool roof technologies and promoting renewable energy adoption, governments can reduce urban heat island effects, improve air quality, and enhance community resilience.
