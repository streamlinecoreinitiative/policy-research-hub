# Climate-Smart Irrigation Cooperatives in the Ganges-Brahmaputra Delta: Enhancing Resilience and Health Outcomes

## Executive Summary

The Ganges-Brahmaputra delta is one of the most densely populated and climate-vulnerable regions globally, facing severe water scarcity and heatwaves. This brief presents a case for integrating agroforestry-based micro-hydro systems to enhance irrigation resilience, improve health outcomes, and promote sustainable development in the region.

## Introduction to Water Scarcity

The Ganges-Brahmaputra delta is characterized by:

* **Severe water scarcity**: Limited water availability due to high evaporation rates, changing precipitation patterns, and inefficient irrigation practices.
* **High labor costs**: Labor-intensive irrigation systems result in significant expenses for farmers.
* **Energy consumption**: Diesel generators are widely used, contributing to greenhouse gas emissions and fuel costs.
