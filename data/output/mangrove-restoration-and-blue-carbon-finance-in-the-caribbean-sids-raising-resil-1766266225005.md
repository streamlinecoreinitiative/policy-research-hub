Mangrove forests, which provide critical coastal protection, habitat for biodiversity, and carbon sequestration services, are particularly susceptible to degradation due to human activities such as deforestation, pollution, and overfishing.

This brief proposes the integration of mangrove restoration with blue carbon finance mechanisms as a means to enhance environmental resilience in Caribbean SIDS. By leveraging financial incentives and promoting sustainable land-use practices, communities can benefit from improved ecosystem services while contributing to global climate change mitigation efforts.
