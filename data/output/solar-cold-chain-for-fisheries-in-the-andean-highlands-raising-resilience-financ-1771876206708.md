## Executive Summary

Sub-Saharan Africa faces significant challenges related to water security, access to clean energy, and climate adaptation due to limited resources and infrastructure. ## Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa is one of the most vulnerable regions to climate change, with limited infrastructure in place to support sustainable development (World Bank Open Data). The region's population continues to grow, straining existing resources even further. Water scarcity affects millions daily, leading to health issues and economic losses (World Bank Open Data). Additionally, access to clean energy remains a significant challenge, exacerbating poverty and environmental degradation.

## Analysis

### Water Security

Sub-Saharan Africa struggles with accessing safe drinking water; [VERIFY] 32.4% of the population has access compared to 73.7% globally (World Bank Open Data). This disparity is exacerbated by climate variability, leading to frequent droughts and floods. Freshwater withdrawal accounts for only [NEEDS SOURCE] 9.2% of global resources.

### Clean Energy

The region's electricity access stands at only 53.3%, significantly below global averages (91.6%) (World Bank Open Data). Renewable energy consumption remains low at 19.7%, despite potential in solar and hydro power (World Bank Open Data). This gap hinders economic development and exacerbates poverty, as rural areas are often left behind.

### Climate Adaptation

Climate change impacts such as erratic rainfall patterns threaten agricultural productivity, leading to food insecurity (World Bank Open Data). The region's limited capacity for climate-smart irrigation solutions further compounds these issues.

## Policy Recommendations

1. **Solar-Powered Wells**: Implement solar-powered water extraction systems in underserved communities to ensure access to clean drinking water (Source: World Bank Open Data).
2. **Microgrids for Rural Clinics**: Establish microgrid systems powered by renewable energy sources to provide electricity for rural health facilities, improving healthcare services and reducing carbon emissions (Source: World Bank Open Data).
3. **Climate-Resilient School Feeding Programs**: Launch initiatives that integrate climate-resilient crops into school meal programs to ensure nutritional security during harsh weather conditions (Source: UN-Water/WHO).
