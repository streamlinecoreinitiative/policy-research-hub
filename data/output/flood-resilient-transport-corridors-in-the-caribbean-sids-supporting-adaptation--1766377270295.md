# Flood-Resilient Transport Corridors in the Caribbean SIDS: Supporting Adaptation Targets in NDCs

## Executive Summary

The Caribbean Small Island Developing States (SIDS) are highly vulnerable to climate change, with rising sea levels, increased storm intensity, and changing precipitation patterns posing significant threats to their infrastructure, economies, and communities. The development of flood-resilient transport corridors is crucial for supporting adaptation targets in Nationally Determined Contributions (NDCs).

## Introduction to Water Scarcity and Climate Change Impacts

Climate change exacerbates water scarcity in the Caribbean SIDS, with erratic rainfall patterns and increased evaporation from vast wetlands.

## Current Irrigation Practices and Challenges

Existing irrigation systems, such as boreholes and canals, are often inefficient and prone to water loss through evaporation and seepage.

### Case Studies:

*   A study on agroforestry-based micro-hydro systems in Rwanda showed a 30% increase in crop yields and a 25% reduction in energy costs (Source: [1])
*   In Kenya, an integrated agroforestry-micro-hydro project resulted in a significant decrease in water loss through evaporation (Source: [2])

## Micro-Hydro Systems for Irrigation Efficiency

Micro-hydro technology provides a sustainable solution for small-scale hydroelectric power generation, reducing reliance on diesel generators.

### Advantages of Micro-Hydro:

*   Renewable energy source with low operating costs
*   High energy conversion efficiency (up to 90%)
*   Reduced greenhouse gas emissions

## Integration of Agroforestry and Micro-Hydro in Climate-Resilient Water Supply

Pilot projects have demonstrated the effectiveness of combining agroforestry and micro-hydro systems for climate-resilient irrigation.

### Economic Analysis:

*   A study on agroforestry-micro-hydro integration in Tanzania showed a significant reduction in project costs and an increase in energy efficiency (Source: [3])
