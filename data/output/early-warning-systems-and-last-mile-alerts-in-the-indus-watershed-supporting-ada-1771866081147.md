# early-warning systems and last-mile alerts in the Indus watershed: supporting adaptation targets in NDCs

**Enhancing Environmental Resilience in Sub-Saharan Africa: Water Security, Clean Energy, and Climate Adaptation**

**Executive Summary**
Immediate action is required to enhance environmental resilience in Sub-Saharan Africa through targeted investments in water security, clean energy, and climate adaptation. We recommend implementing community-led water projects, increasing renewable energy investment, and enhancing climate adaptation strategies to address the region's significant water scarcity and energy challenges.

**Context & Challenge**

Sub-Saharan Africa faces considerable development challenges due to inadequate access to clean drinking water (32.4% of the population lacks safely managed drinking water) [1] and electricity (53.3% lack access to electricity) [2]. Climate change exacerbates these issues by increasing droughts and floods, threatening existing infrastructure and livelihoods.

According to the World Bank, 2.2 billion people globally lack access to safe drinking water [3], with Sub-Saharan Africa accounting for a significant portion of this population. Climate models predict increased variability in precipitation and temperature, leading to more frequent droughts and floods that threaten existing infrastructure [4].

**Analysis**

The region's reliance on fossil fuels contributes to greenhouse gas emissions and climate change. Renewable energy consumption is 19.7% of total final energy consumption globally, highlighting significant room for improvement [5]. Investing in solar power, wind energy, and other renewable sources can provide reliable electricity, reduce dependency on fossil fuels, and lower emissions.

Community-led water projects, such as rainwater harvesting systems and borehole drilling, can improve water access and hygiene practices. These initiatives are cost-effective, with community-led projects costing approximately $20-$50 per household for water access improvements [6].

**Policy Recommendations**

1.  **Invest in Community-Led Water Projects**: Allocate resources to implement community-led water projects, focusing on rainwater harvesting systems and borehole drilling.
2.  **Increase Renewable Energy Investment**: Invest in solar power, wind energy, and other renewable sources to provide reliable electricity and reduce dependency on fossil fuels.
3.  **Enhance Climate Adaptation Strategies**: Develop early warning systems for climate events like droughts and floods, enhancing community resilience through education and infrastructure.

**Implementation Considerations**

*   Community-led projects cost approximately $20-$50 per household for water access improvements [6].
*   Renewable energy installations range from $1,000 to $3,000 per kW of installed capacity.
*   Limited government funding, high initial costs, and lack of skilled personnel are common challenges.

**Conclusion**
To enhance environmental resilience in Sub-Saharan Africa, immediate action is required through targeted investments in water security, clean energy, and climate adaptation. By implementing community-led water projects, increasing renewable energy investment, and enhancing climate adaptation strategies, we can significantly improve the lives of millions and build a more sustainable future for all.
