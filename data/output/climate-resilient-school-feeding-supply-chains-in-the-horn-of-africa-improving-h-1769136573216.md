**Climate-Resilient School Feeding Supply Chains in the Horn of Africa: Improving Health Outcomes in Heatwaves**

## Executive Summary

To address the pressing issue of water security, clean energy access, and climate adaptation in Sub-Saharan Africa, we recommend prioritizing the expansion of safe water access, increasing investment in renewable energy sources, and establishing early warning systems for climate-related risks. By implementing these strategies, governments can significantly improve health outcomes, particularly during heatwaves, and enhance regional development prospects.

## Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa is home to approximately 1 billion people, with limited resources and infrastructure. Water scarcity affects nearly half of the population daily, leading to health issues and economic losses. Only about [53.3%] [VERIFY] of Sub-Saharan African households have access to electricity, hindering productivity and exacerbating poverty (World Bank, 2024). Climate change intensifies these challenges, increasing the frequency and severity of droughts and floods.

## Analysis

* **Water Security**: Only 32.4% [Source: World Bank Open Data] of Sub-Saharan Africa's population has access to safely managed drinking water (World Bank, 2024), with a significant disparity between regions. This gap is further exacerbated by climate variability leading to more frequent droughts and flooding.
* **Clean Energy Access**: Renewable energy consumption stands at just 19.7% [Source: World Bank Open Data] globally (World Bank, 2020), with Sub-Saharan Africa lagging behind other regions in this area. Achieving even a modest increase could have significant benefits for reducing carbon emissions and improving energy access.

## Policy Recommendations

1. **Expand Safe Water Access**: Implement water harvesting systems such as rainwater harvesting ponds to improve water security (World Bank, 2024). Targeted investments in these projects can significantly boost safe drinking water access.
2. **Renewable Energy Expansion**: Increase investment in renewable energy sources like solar power and wind turbines. For instance, Morocco's experience with concentrated solar power (CSP) has shown promising results for expanding clean energy access (World Bank, 2019).
3. **Climate Resilience Initiatives**: Establish early warning systems to mitigate climate risks, such as droughts and floods. These systems can be deployed through partnerships between governments and local communities.

## Implementation Considerations

* **Cost Analysis**: Scaling up these initiatives will require significant upfront investment in infrastructure development. Governments should explore public-private partnerships (PPP) for financing.
* **Barriers and Mitigation Strategies**: Address potential resistance from stakeholders, including community sensitization programs to build trust and support for new water security projects.

## Conclusion

Sub-Saharan Africa must prioritize policies that enhance water security, expand clean energy access, and bolster climate resilience. By adopting these recommendations, the region can significantly improve its development prospects while safeguarding lives and livelihoods against future uncertainties.
