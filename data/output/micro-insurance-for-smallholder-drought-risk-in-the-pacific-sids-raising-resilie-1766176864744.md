**Executive Summary**

The Central African Republic (CAR) is highly vulnerable to climate change impacts, including erratic rainfall patterns and increased evaporation from its vast wetlands. This brief highlights the potential of integrating agroforestry with micro-hydro systems as a sustainable solution for climate-resilient irrigation in CAR.

**Climate Change Impacts on Agriculture in the Central African Republic**

The CAR is one of the countries most affected by climate change in Africa (UNEP). Rising temperatures and changing precipitation patterns have led to increased frequency and severity of droughts, affecting agricultural productivity and food security. Erratic rainfall patterns have resulted in crop failures, reduced yields, and decreased income for smallholder farmers (World Bank).

**Current Irrigation Practices and Challenges**

Traditional irrigation methods in CAR are often energy-intensive and costly, exacerbating poverty among smallholder farmers. Existing irrigation systems suffer from significant water loss through evaporation and seepage.
