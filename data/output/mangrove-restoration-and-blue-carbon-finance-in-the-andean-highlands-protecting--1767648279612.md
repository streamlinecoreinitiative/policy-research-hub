# Agroforestry-Based Micro-Hydro Systems for Climate-Resilient Irrigation in the Mesoamerican Highlands

## Executive Summary

The Mesoamerican Highlands face severe water scarcity and poverty, exacerbated by climate change impacts on agriculture. Existing irrigation systems suffer from high water loss percentages through evaporation and seepage. This brief proposes an integrated solution combining agroforestry and micro-hydro technology to enhance water retention, reduce erosion, and increase energy efficiency.

## Introduction to Water Scarcity in the Mesoamerican Highlands

Climate change has significant impacts on agriculture, leading to severe water scarcity in the Mesoamerican Highlands. Rising temperatures and changing precipitation patterns reduce crop yields and increase water stress. Existing irrigation systems suffer from inefficiencies that exacerbate water scarcity and undermine agricultural productivity.

## Current Irrigation Practices and Challenges

Existing irrigation systems in the Mesoamerican Highlands are often inefficient and unsustainable. Boreholes and canals rely on diesel generators, contributing to greenhouse gas emissions and energy insecurity. The high costs of fuel and maintenance further strain already limited resources.

## Micro-Hydro Systems for Irrigation Efficiency

Micro-hydro technology offers a sustainable alternative to diesel generators. Small-scale hydroelectric systems harness the energy of water flows, providing clean and reliable power. Benefits include reduced reliance on fossil fuels, increased energy efficiency, and lower maintenance costs.

## Integration of Agroforestry and Micro-Hydro in Climate-Resilient Water Supply

Pilot projects demonstrate the combined use of agroforestry and micro-hydro technology in climate-resilient water supply systems. These integrated systems enhance water retention, reduce erosion, and increase energy efficiency. Economic analysis highlights cost-effectiveness.
