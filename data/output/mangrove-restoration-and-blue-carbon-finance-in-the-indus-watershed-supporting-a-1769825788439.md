# mangrove restoration and blue carbon finance in the Indus watershed: supporting adaptation targets in NDCs

**Mangrove Restoration and Blue Carbon Finance in the Indus Watershed: Supporting Adaptation Targets in NDCs**

## Executive Summary

Lower-income countries face significant challenges in accessing safe drinking water, reliable electricity, and adapting to climate change. This policy brief highlights critical areas where targeted interventions are needed to strengthen water security, clean energy access, and climate resilience. We recommend investing in mangrove restoration and blue carbon finance initiatives in the Indus watershed as a key strategy for supporting adaptation targets outlined in Nationally Determined Contributions (NDCs).

## Context & Challenge

Lower-income countries are heavily reliant on natural resources for daily living and economic activities. Water scarcity is a critical issue in many regions, affecting agriculture, industry, and domestic use (UN-Water/WHO, 2022). In Sub-Saharan Africa, only [32.4%](World Bank, 2024) of the population has access to safely managed drinking water, while global coverage stands at [73.7%](World Bank, 2024).

Electricity access is also limited in many lower-income countries, with only [53.3%](World Bank, 2023) of Sub-Saharan Africa's population having access. This disparity contributes to energy poverty and exacerbates economic disparities.

Climate change poses additional challenges, including increased frequency of droughts and floods, which further strain already limited resources. Adaptation measures are essential for mitigating climate impacts on health, food security, and infrastructure.

## Analysis

The combined effects of water scarcity, electricity shortages, and climate vulnerability highlight the need for comprehensive interventions:

*   **Water Security**: Lower-income countries must invest in sustainable water management practices such as rainwater harvesting, wastewater treatment, and desalination technologies. These solutions can improve access to safe drinking water while reducing pollution.
*   **Clean Energy Access**: Renewable energy sources like solar, wind, and hydro offer viable alternatives to traditional fossil fuels. Governments should prioritize grid-connected renewable energy projects and off-grid solutions for rural areas where infrastructure is lacking.
*   **Climate Adaptation**: Implementing climate-resilient agricultural practices, early warning systems for extreme weather events, and improved water management strategies can help lower-income countries better withstand the impacts of climate change.

## Policy Recommendations

1.  **Increase Public Investment in Water Infrastructure**
    *   Allocate dedicated funds to improve access to safe drinking water through infrastructure projects such as wells, pipelines, and treatment plants.
    *   Encourage public-private partnerships (PPPs) for financing these initiatives.

2.  **Expand Renewable Energy Access**
    *   Develop supportive policies that promote the adoption of renewable energy technologies in rural areas where grid connectivity is not feasible.
    *   Invest in off-grid solar systems for households and small businesses to ensure universal access to electricity.

3.  **Enhance Climate Resilience Programs**
    *   Implement climate-resilient agricultural practices, such as drought-resistant crop varieties and improved irrigation techniques.
    *   Strengthen early warning systems for floods and droughts using satellite imagery and weather forecasting technologies.

4.  **Support Research and Development**
    *   Allocate resources to research institutions focused on developing innovative water management solutions and renewable energy technologies tailored to local contexts.

## Implementation Considerations

Implementing these recommendations will require careful planning, coordination among various stakeholders, and addressing potential barriers:

*   **Costs**: Estimate the financial requirements for each recommendation and explore funding opportunities from international donors or domestic budgets.
*   **Barriers**: Identify institutional constraints such as bureaucratic hurdles, resistance to change, and lack of technical expertise. Develop strategies to overcome these obstacles.

## Conclusion

Addressing water security, clean energy access, and climate resilience in lower-income countries is critical for sustainable development. By adopting targeted policies and implementing feasible interventions, governments can significantly improve the well-being of their populations while fostering long-term economic growth and environmental sustainability.
