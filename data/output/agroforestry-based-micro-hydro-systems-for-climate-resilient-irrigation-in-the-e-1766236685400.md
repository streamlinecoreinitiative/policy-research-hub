### Executive Summary

The Ethiopian Highlands face severe water scarcity due to climate change, characterized by erratic rainfall patterns. Traditional irrigation methods exacerbate poverty among smallholder farmers by being energy-intensive and costly. Integrating agroforestry with micro-hydro systems can provide a sustainable solution that enhances both water security and renewable energy use.

### Introduction to Water Scarcity in the Ethiopian Highlands

The Ethiopian Highlands are a crucial agricultural region, but they face severe water scarcity due to climate change. Erratic rainfall patterns have become more frequent, making it challenging for smallholder farmers to rely on traditional irrigation methods.

### Current Irrigation Practices and Challenges

Existing irrigation systems in the Ethiopian Highlands include boreholes and canals. However, these systems have several challenges:

*   **Energy Consumption:** Traditional irrigation methods rely heavily on diesel generators, leading to high energy costs for smallholder farmers.

### Micro-Hydro Systems for Irrigation Efficiency

Micro-hydro technology is an efficient way to generate energy for irrigation systems:

*   **Renewable Energy:** Micro-hydro systems rely on renewable energy sources, reducing reliance on fossil fuels.
*   **Energy Efficiency:** Micro-hydro systems are more energy-efficient than traditional diesel generators.

### Integration of Agroforestry and Micro-Hydro in Climate-Resilient Water Supply

Pilot projects have demonstrated the effectiveness of integrating agroforestry with micro-hydro systems:

*   **Water Retention:** Agroforestry improves water retention, reducing the need for irrigation.
*   **Energy Generation:** Micro-hydro systems generate energy efficiently, reducing costs for smallholder farmers.
