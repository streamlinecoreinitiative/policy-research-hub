**Ensuring Accessible Clean Water, Sustainable Energy, and Climate Resilience in Sub-Saharan Africa**

## Executive Summary

Sub-Saharan Africa faces significant challenges in accessing clean water, reliable electricity, and adapting to climate change impacts. With only 32.4% of the population having access to safely managed drinking water and a mere 53.3% with access to electricity, immediate action is essential to improve environmental resilience and quality of life for its citizens (World Bank Open Data). This policy brief recommends increased investments in renewable energy sources, improved water management systems, and enhanced early warning systems to address these critical gaps.

## Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa is characterized by severe water scarcity and inconsistent electricity supply. The region's freshwater withdrawal accounts for 9.2% of total resources (World Bank Open Data), underscoring the urgent need to enhance water security through sustainable practices like rainwater harvesting and improved irrigation techniques. Additionally, access to electricity remains limited, with only 53.3% of the population having access (World Bank Open Data). This disparity exacerbates health risks, particularly in rural areas where alternative energy sources are often inadequate or non-existent.

## Policy Recommendations

To address the challenges outlined, we recommend:

1. **Increased Investment in Renewable Energy:** Prioritize investments in solar, wind, and hydroelectric projects to reduce carbon emissions and enhance energy security. For instance, a World Bank report (2023) highlights that renewable energy consumption increased by 19.7% globally over the past decade.
2. **Enhanced Water Management Systems:** Implement community-based water management programs to improve access to clean drinking water. This includes expanding rainwater harvesting systems and improving sanitation infrastructure in schools, hospitals, and other public facilities. Data from UN-Water/WHO (2022) shows that 74% of global coverage for safely managed water is achieved through such initiatives.
3. **Early Warning Systems:** Establish robust early warning systems to alert communities about impending climate events like droughts or floods. These systems can be integrated with existing infrastructure and should include telecommunication networks to ensure timely dissemination of information.
