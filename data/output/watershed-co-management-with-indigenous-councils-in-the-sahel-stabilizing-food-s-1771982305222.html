Securing Water Security, Clean Energy, and Climate Adaptation in Sub-Saharan Africa

Executive Summary

Sub-Saharan Africa faces significant challenges in achieving clean energy access (53.3% of the population) [1] and safe drinking water (73.7% global coverage, but only 53.3% in SSA) [2]. To address these gaps, we recommend implementing comprehensive policies focusing on renewable energy development, improved water infrastructure, and climate adaptation measures.

Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa is one of the most vulnerable regions to climate change impacts, with limited access to electricity (53.3%) [1] and clean water severely impacting economic development, health outcomes, and climate resilience [3]. The region's natural environment includes all living and non-living things occurring naturally along with their interactions [4], which are crucial for maintaining ecosystem balance.

Analysis

Renewable energy consumption in Sub-Saharan Africa is only 19.7%, far below global standards (34% in 2020) [5]. Access to safely managed drinking water stands at 73.7% globally, but drops significantly to 53.3% in SSA [2], highlighting stark disparities. Climate impacts are exacerbated by limited infrastructure and energy access, leading to increased vulnerability.

  2. Enhance early warning systems: Deploy climate smart agriculture practices to mitigate droughts and floods.
