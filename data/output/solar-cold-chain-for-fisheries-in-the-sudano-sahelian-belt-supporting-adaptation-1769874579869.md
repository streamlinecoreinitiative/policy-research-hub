### Policy Recommendations

1. **Enhance Community-Based Water Management Programs**:
	* Invest in community-led initiatives that promote safe water practices, including the construction of rainwater harvesting systems and sanitation facilities.
	* Provide training for local communities on sustainable water management techniques such as drip irrigation and conservation farming methods [Source: FAO, 2021].
2. **Promote Solar Mini-Grids**:
	* Implement solar mini-grids in rural areas to provide reliable electricity access at lower costs compared to centralized grid systems.
	* Offer subsidies for households and businesses to offset initial investment costs.
3. **Foster Green Infrastructure Solutions**:
	* Invest in green roofs, permeable pavements, and urban forests to reduce water runoff and improve water quality.
	* Implement green infrastructure projects that integrate climate resilience into existing urban planning processes [Source: IPCC, 2018].

### Implementation Considerations

Initial investments for solar mini-grids and community-based water management programs can be substantial but yield long-term savings through reduced electricity bills and improved sanitation costs. Partnerships between government, NGOs, and private sector entities will be crucial for successful implementation.

### Conclusion

Ensuring water security, clean energy, and climate adaptation in sub-Saharan Africa requires a multifaceted approach that combines community engagement, technological innovation, and policy support. By addressing these interconnected issues, the region can build a more resilient future for its people.
