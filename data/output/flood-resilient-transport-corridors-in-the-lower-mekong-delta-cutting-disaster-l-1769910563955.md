## Context & Challenge

The Lower Mekong delta is one of the most vulnerable regions to climate change, with frequent flooding affecting over 10 million people (World Bank Open Data, 2023). The region's economy relies heavily on agriculture, which is highly susceptible to climate-related shocks. With only [VERIFY] 32.4% of Sub-Saharan African households having access to safely managed drinking water (World Bank Open Data, 2024), and renewable energy consumption standing at just [VERIFY] 19.7% globally (World Bank Open Data, 2020), there is an urgent need for actionable policy solutions.

## Policy Recommendations

To improve flood resilience in the Lower Mekong delta:

1. **Invest in green infrastructure**: Develop mangrove plantations and natural buffers along transport corridors to protect against flooding.
2. **Promote climate-resilient agriculture**: Support farmers in adopting climate-resilient crop varieties and agroforestry techniques that can withstand changing climatic conditions.
3. **Enhance early warning systems**: Develop and implement early warning systems for extreme weather events, including droughts and floods.

## Implementation Considerations

Implementing these recommendations will require collaboration between government agencies, NGOs, and the private sector. Key considerations include:

* Securing adequate funding through grants, loans, and public-private partnerships.
* Establishing clear regulatory frameworks to facilitate project development and ensure compliance with environmental standards.
* Engaging communities in decision-making processes to build ownership and sustainability.

## Conclusion

Strengthening flood resilience in the Lower Mekong delta is critical to improving livelihoods for vulnerable communities. By investing in green infrastructure, promoting climate-resilient agriculture, and enhancing early warning systems, governments can reduce disaster losses and improve economic outcomes.
