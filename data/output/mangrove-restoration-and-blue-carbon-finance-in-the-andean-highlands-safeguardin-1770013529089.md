## Executive Summary

To address the pressing challenges of water security, clean energy access, and climate adaptation in Sub-Saharan Africa, we recommend a comprehensive policy framework that prioritizes community-based water management, increases renewable energy investments, and integrates climate adaptation measures into development plans. This framework is critical to ensuring sustainable development and poverty reduction in the region.

Recommendations:

3. **Integrate climate adaptation measures into development plans**: Incorporate climate-resilient infrastructure into development plans, investing in early warning systems to mitigate the impact of extreme weather events.

## Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa faces significant challenges in ensuring access to safe drinking water, electricity, and sustainable energy sources. The region's population is projected to reach [2.1 billion] by 2050 (UN Department of Economic and Social Affairs), putting immense pressure on already strained resources. Environmental degradation exacerbates these issues, with frequent droughts and extreme weather events threatening water security and agricultural productivity.

## Analysis

### Water Security

* Only 32.4% of Sub-Saharan Africans have access to safely managed drinking water, compared to the world average of [73.7%] (World Bank Open Data).
* The global population lacking safe water stands at [2.2 billion people], with [675 million] in Sub-Saharan Africa (UN-Water/WHO).

### Clean Energy Access

* Renewable energy consumption stands at a mere [9.2%], highlighting the low penetration of clean energy sources in the region (World Bank Open Data).
* Only [53.3%] of Sub-Saharan African populations have access to electricity, compared to the world average of [91.6%] (World Bank Open Data).

### Climate Adaptation

* Climate change impacts are felt acutely in this region, with frequent droughts and extreme weather events threatening water security and agricultural productivity.
* Integrating climate adaptation measures into development plans is crucial for ensuring long-term sustainability.
