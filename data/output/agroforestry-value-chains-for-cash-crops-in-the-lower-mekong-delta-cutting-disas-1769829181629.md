**Enhancing Water Security, Clean Energy Access, and Climate Resilience in Sub-Saharan Africa**

### Executive Summary

Sub-Saharan Africa faces significant challenges related to water security, clean energy access, and climate adaptation. With only 32.4% of the population having access to safely managed drinking water (World Bank, 2024), the need for robust policy interventions is urgent. This brief outlines feasible policy recommendations that prioritize resilient infrastructure development, renewable energy adoption, and enhanced water management systems.

**Recommendation:** Governments should invest heavily in expanding access to safe drinking water and clean energy, with a focus on rural areas and vulnerable populations.

### Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa lags behind other regions globally in terms of access to safe drinking water (32.4%) and electricity (53.3%), according to World Bank data (2024). The region's reliance on non-renewable energy sources exacerbates climate vulnerability, leading to frequent power outages during extreme weather events.

### Analysis

The current situation highlights stark disparities in access to clean water and electricity. According to the World Bank (2024), only 32.4% of the population has access to safely managed drinking water, while 53.3% lacks reliable access to electricity. This underscores the urgent need for comprehensive policy reforms focused on expanding infrastructure.

### Policy Recommendations

To enhance water security, clean energy access, and climate resilience in Sub-Saharan Africa:

1. **Expand Access to Safe Drinking Water:** Implement large-scale desalination projects or improve existing water treatment facilities to ensure safe drinking water for all. Promote rainwater harvesting systems and community-based water management initiatives.
	* Data: World Bank (2024) indicates that only 32.4% of the population in Sub-Saharan Africa has access to safely managed drinking water [Source: World Bank Open Data].
2. **Increase Access to Renewable Energy:** Invest heavily in solar, wind, and hydroelectric power generation projects. Develop microgrids and off-grid solutions to provide electricity where national grids are unreliable.
	* Data: World Bank (2024) shows that only 53.3% of the population in Sub-Saharan Africa has access to reliable electricity [Source: World Bank Open Data].
3. **Enhance Climate Resilience:** Implement early warning systems for droughts, floods, and other climate-related events. Develop climate-resilient infrastructure such as flood barriers and drainage networks.
	* Data: NOAA (2024) data on climate resilience initiatives can be used to support this recommendation.
