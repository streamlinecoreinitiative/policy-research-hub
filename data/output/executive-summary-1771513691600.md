## Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa faces significant challenges in accessing clean water, reliable electricity, and resilient infrastructure to combat climate change impacts. The region is characterized by extreme weather events such as droughts and floods, exacerbated by climate change. These challenges disproportionately affect already vulnerable populations.

Recent data highlights the urgency of this issue:

* Access to safely managed drinking water in Sub-Saharan Africa stands at 32.4% (World Bank Open Data 2024).
* Less than half of the region's population has access to electricity (World Bank Open Data 2023).
