# agroforestry value chains for cash crops in the Horn of Africa: stabilizing food security during droughts

**Agroforestry-Based Micro-Hydro Systems for Climate-Resilient Irrigation in the Ethiopian Highlands**

Executive Summary:

The Ethiopian Highlands face severe water scarcity due to climate change impacts, leading to reduced crop yields and economic losses for farmers. This brief proposes an integrated approach of agroforestry and micro-hydro systems as a solution to address this issue. By combining these two sustainable practices, we can improve soil health, increase biodiversity, sequester carbon, and generate renewable energy for irrigation.

**Climate Change Impacts in the Ethiopian Highlands**

The Ethiopian Highlands experience significant climate variability, with average annual rainfall ranging from 800 to 1200 mm [verify]. Precipitation patterns are highly variable, with an annual range of 600-1400 mm. Traditional irrigation systems suffer from high water loss percentages: 30% due to evaporation and 5% due to seepage.

**Current Irrigation Practices and Challenges**

Existing irrigation methods in the Ethiopian Highlands are energy-intensive and economically burdensome:

* Borehole pumps consume a significant amount of energy, contributing to greenhouse gas emissions.
* Canal systems experience significant water loss through evaporation (30%) and seepage (5%).
* The cost per hectare for traditional irrigation methods is estimated at $100-$200/year [verify].
