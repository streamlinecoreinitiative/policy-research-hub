### Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa faces significant challenges in accessing safe drinking water and reliable electricity. Current statistics show that only 32.4% of the population has access to safely managed drinking water (World Bank Open Data, 2024), while electricity coverage stands at 53.3%, with renewable energy consumption accounting for just 19.7% of total final energy consumption globally (World Bank Open Data, 2020). These disparities exacerbate climate vulnerability and hinder economic growth.

The region's reliance on non-renewable sources for both water and electricity poses substantial risks. Inadequate infrastructure leads to frequent power outages during droughts or extreme weather events, threatening access to essential services like healthcare and education (World Bank Open Data, 2023). Similarly, limited access to clean drinking water increases health risks from contaminated supplies (UN-Water/WHO, 2022).

### Policy Recommendations

1.  **Enhance Water Access through Rainwater Harvesting**: Implement targeted programs to promote rainwater harvesting in rural areas where piped water is not accessible (World Bank Open Data, 2024). Provide subsidies or grants for households and communities to install rainwater collection systems.
2.  **Expand Renewable Energy Initiatives**: Increase investment in solar-powered desalination plants and renewable energy projects to improve access to clean drinking water and electricity. Target regions prone to droughts where traditional sources are unreliable (World Bank Open Data, 2023).
3.  **Promote Climate-Resilient Infrastructure**: Develop early warning systems for climate-related hazards such as floods and droughts using reliable communication technologies. Integrate these into existing infrastructure plans to enhance community preparedness [needs verification].
