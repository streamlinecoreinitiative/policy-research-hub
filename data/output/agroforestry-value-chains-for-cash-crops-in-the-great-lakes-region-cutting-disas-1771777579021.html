Micro-insurance for Smallholder Farmers in Sub-Saharan Africa: Protecting Livelihoods Under Climate Stress

Executive Summary

We recommend implementing micro-insurance programs for smallholder farmers in Sub-Saharan Africa to mitigate drought risk and protect their livelihoods under climate stress. With limited access to safe drinking water (32.4%) [1] and electricity (53.3%) [2], these farmers are disproportionately affected by climate-related shocks, such as droughts and floods. Micro-insurance can provide immediate financial support during crises, helping smallholder farmers recover faster and maintain food security.

Context & Challenge

Smallholder farmers in Sub-Saharan Africa face significant climate vulnerabilities due to limited access to safe water and electricity. Climate change is intensifying weather extremes, threatening food security and livelihoods [3]. Micro-insurance can offer immediate financial relief during droughts and floods, which are becoming more frequent and severe.
