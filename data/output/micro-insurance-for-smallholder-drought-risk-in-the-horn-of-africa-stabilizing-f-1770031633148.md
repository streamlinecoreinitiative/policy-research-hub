## Executive Summary
To address the pressing challenges of water security, clean energy access, and climate resilience in Sub-Saharan Africa, we recommend a dual-focused approach that prioritizes investments in renewable energy and safe drinking water. Specifically, governments and development practitioners should allocate more funds towards building and maintaining water supply systems (UN-Water/WHO, 2022) and encourage the adoption of renewable energy sources such as solar power for clean cooking and cooling solutions in communities lacking electricity (World Bank Open Data, 2023). This integrated approach will not only enhance environmental resilience but also contribute to achieving Sustainable Development Goal 6 (SDG 6), which aims to ensure universal access to safe drinking water and sanitation by 2030.

Implementing these recommendations will require careful planning:
- **Funding Mechanisms**: Establish dedicated funds for both water infrastructure projects and renewable energy initiatives. Public-private partnerships can help mobilize additional resources.
