# Climate-Resilient Irrigation in the Andean Highlands: Integrating Agroforestry and Micro-Hydro Systems for Sustainable Water Management

## Executive Summary

The Andean Highlands face severe water scarcity due to climate change, affecting agriculture and local economies. This project proposes an integrated approach combining agroforestry and micro-hydro systems for climate-resilient irrigation. Pilot projects demonstrate the combined use of these systems, showing potential for scaling up.

## Introduction to Water Scarcity in the Andean Highlands

Climate change has significant impacts on agriculture in the Andean Highlands, including erratic rainfall patterns and increased frequency of droughts [1]. This affects food security and economic stability, as irrigation is a critical component of agricultural production. Resilient irrigation methods are essential for mitigating these effects.

## Current Irrigation Practices and Challenges

Existing irrigation systems in the region include boreholes and canals, which suffer from high water loss through evaporation and seepage [2]. These inefficiencies exacerbate water scarcity and strain local resources.

## Micro-Hydro Systems for Irrigation Efficiency

Micro-hydro technology is a small-scale hydroelectric system that reduces reliance on diesel generators and increases energy efficiency. This technology has been successfully implemented in various regions, including the Andean Highlands.

## Integration of Agroforestry and Micro-Hydro in Climate-Resilient Water Supply

Pilot projects have demonstrated the effectiveness of integrating agroforestry and micro-hydro systems for climate-resilient irrigation [5]. Economic analysis shows potential cost-effectiveness and scalability.
