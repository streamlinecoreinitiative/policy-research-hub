**Enhancing Water Security, Clean Energy Access, and Climate Adaptation in Sub-Saharan Africa: Feasible Interventions for Sustainable Development**

## Executive Summary

Sub-Saharan Africa is facing severe water scarcity, limited access to electricity, and climate-related challenges. To address these issues, we recommend prioritizing the expansion of safe drinking water coverage, enhancing clean energy access through off-grid solar solutions and renewable energy integration, and implementing climate adaptation strategies that include early warning systems.

Key Findings:

* Only 32.4% of Sub-Saharan Africa's population has access to safely managed drinking water (World Bank, 2024).
* The region has limited access to electricity, with only 53.3% of the population having access (World Bank, 2023).
* Renewable energy consumption stands at 19.7% of total final energy consumption (World Bank, 2020).

Recommendations:

1. **Expand Safe Drinking Water Coverage**: Invest in community-based water supply projects to improve access to safe drinking water, focusing on rural areas with limited infrastructure.
2. **Promote Renewable Energy Integration**: Implement off-grid solar solutions and integrate renewable energy into national grids to enhance electricity access.
3. **Enhance Climate Adaptation Strategies**: Invest in early warning systems and climate-resilient infrastructure projects such as flood barriers and improved irrigation systems.

## Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa is grappling with significant challenges in accessing basic services, including safe water (32.4%) and electricity (53.3%), which are critical for sustainable development. Climate change exacerbates these issues by increasing the frequency of droughts and floods, threatening food security and health outcomes.

According to the World Bank (2024), Sub-Saharan Africa lacks adequate infrastructure to provide safe drinking water, with only 32.4% of its population having access. This disparity highlights a critical need for investment in water sanitation projects.
