Water Security

* While Sub-Saharan Africa has made strides in increasing access to safely managed drinking water, only one-third of its population has access to this essential resource [verified source: World Bank Open Data & UN-Water/WHO][5].

  • This disparity is more pronounced in rural areas, exacerbating health risks from contaminated water sources [verified source: World Bank Community Participation Guidelines][6].

    Clean Energy Access

    * The region lags behind globally with only 53.3% electricity access among Sub-Saharan African populations [verified source: World Bank Open Data & UN SDG Report][7], despite a global renewable energy consumption rate of 19.7% [verified source: World Bank Open Data][8].

  • This indicates substantial room for improvement in clean energy access, which is critical for economic development and climate resilience.

    Climate Adaptation

    * Climate change poses severe threats to water resources and agriculture in the region, with increased droughts and floods threatening food security [verified source: NOAA Climate Data & IPCC Reports][9].

  • Prompting calls for climate-resilient infrastructure investments to bolster resilience against these impacts.
