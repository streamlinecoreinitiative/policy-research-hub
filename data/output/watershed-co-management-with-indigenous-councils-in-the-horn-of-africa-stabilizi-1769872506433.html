Ensuring Water Security, Clean Energy, and Climate Adaptation in Rural Communities of East Africa: A Pathway to Resilience

Executive Summary

East African rural communities face significant challenges in ensuring equitable access to clean water, reliable energy, and resilient climate adaptation strategies. To address these issues, we recommend implementing cost-effective water purification technologies, promoting decentralized renewable energy systems, and investing in early warning systems for droughts and floods.

Key Findings:

* Only 32.4% of Sub-Saharan Africans have access to safely managed drinking water, compared to the global average of 73.7% [Source: World Bank Open Data].

  • The region's population is disproportionately affected by energy poverty, with only 53.3% having access to electricity, compared to the world average of 91.6% [Source: World Bank Open Data].

    Context & Challenge

    East Africa is characterized by arid and semi-arid landscapes with limited natural resources. Climate change exacerbates existing vulnerabilities, leading to frequent droughts, unpredictable rainfall patterns, and rising temperatures. The lack of water security and clean energy access in rural areas impedes economic growth, education, healthcare, and overall well-being.
