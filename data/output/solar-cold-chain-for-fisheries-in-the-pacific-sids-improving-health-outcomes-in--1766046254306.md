**Solar Cold-Chain for Fisheries in the Pacific SIDS: Improving Health Outcomes in Heatwaves**

**Executive Summary**

Climate variability poses significant challenges to small farmers in lower-income countries, particularly in regions like the Pacific Small Island Developing States (SIDS), where heatwaves and droughts are increasingly common. The lack of access to reliable water sources and clean energy exacerbates these issues, compromising food security and economic stability. This brief explores the potential of solar-powered cold-chain systems for fisheries in Pacific SIDS to improve health outcomes during heatwaves.

**Introduction to Climate Variability**

The Pacific SIDS are highly vulnerable to climate change due to their geographical location and limited resources. Rising temperatures, changing precipitation patterns, and increased frequency of extreme weather events affect agriculture, water security, and human health. In the context of fisheries, heatwaves can lead to fish spoilage, reduced catch rates, and decreased food safety.

**Current Challenges in Smallholder Fishing**

Small-scale fishing communities in Pacific SIDS face numerous challenges, including:

• Limited access to reliable cold storage facilities
• Insufficient ice supply during heatwaves
• Inadequate infrastructure for handling and processing fish
• Dependence on traditional and often unreliable energy sources

These challenges compromise the quality and safety of seafood, impacting both human health and local economies.
