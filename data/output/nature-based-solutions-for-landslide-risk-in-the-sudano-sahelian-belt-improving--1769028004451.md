# nature-based solutions for landslide risk in the Sudano-Sahelian belt: improving health outcomes in heatwaves

**Enhancing Water Security, Clean Energy Access, and Climate Adaptation in Sub-Saharan Africa: A Strategic Approach**

### Executive Summary

To address the severe water security, clean energy access, and climate adaptation challenges facing Sub-Saharan Africa, we recommend a three-pronged approach:

1. **Increase Access to Safe Drinking Water**: Implement community-led water projects and invest in infrastructure improvements to ensure sustainable access to safe drinking water for all communities.
2. **Expand Clean Energy Access**: Invest heavily in renewable energy sources such as solar and wind power. Prioritize electrification of rural areas through microgrids, ensuring reliable electricity supply across the region.
3. **Enhance Climate Adaptation Measures**: Develop early-warning systems for climate-related hazards like floods and droughts to mitigate their impacts on water security and food production.

### Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa is home to the world's poorest countries in terms of access to safe drinking water [1] and electricity [2]. The region's limited resources exacerbate these challenges, leading to frequent water crises and power outages that severely impact daily life and economic stability [3]. Climate change further compounds these issues by increasing vulnerability to extreme weather events.

According to the World Bank Open Data, only 73.7% of the global population has access to safely managed drinking water, while just 53.3% have reliable electricity [1]. These figures highlight significant disparities within the region and underscore the need for comprehensive interventions. Renewable energy consumption stands at 19.7%, far below the global average [4].

### Analysis

The current situation in Sub-Saharan Africa is dire: only 73.7% of the global population has access to safely managed drinking water, while just 53.3% have reliable electricity [1]. These figures highlight significant disparities within the region and underscore the need for comprehensive interventions. Renewable energy consumption stands at 19.7%, far below the global average [4].

A recent report by UN-Water/WHO notes that 2.2 billion people lack access to safe water, with Sub-Saharan Africa being one of the most affected regions [5]. This disparity not only limits economic development but also hinders climate adaptation efforts.

### Policy Recommendations

To address these challenges, we propose three key policy recommendations:

1. **Increase Access to Safe Drinking Water**: Implement community-led water projects and invest in infrastructure improvements to ensure sustainable access to safe drinking water for all communities.
	* Allocate dedicated funding streams through public-private partnerships for water projects [6].
	* Collaborate with local communities to design and manage microgrids, fostering ownership and sustainability.
2. **Expand Clean Energy Access**: Invest heavily in renewable energy sources such as solar and wind power. Prioritize electrification of rural areas through microgrids, ensuring reliable electricity supply across the region.
	* Establish climate adaptation funds to support early-warning systems and disaster preparedness initiatives [7].
3. **Enhance Climate Adaptation Measures**: Develop early-warning systems for climate-related hazards like floods and droughts to mitigate their impacts on water security and food production.

### Implementation Considerations

To ensure these recommendations are implemented effectively, consider the following practical steps:

* Allocate dedicated funding streams through public-private partnerships for water projects [6].
* Collaborate with local communities to design and manage microgrids, fostering ownership and sustainability.
* Establish climate adaptation funds to support early-warning systems and disaster preparedness initiatives [7].

### Conclusion

By focusing on enhancing water security, expanding clean energy access, and bolstering climate adaptation measures, Sub-Saharan Africa can significantly improve its resilience. Stakeholders must act now to implement these recommendations, ensuring a more sustainable future for the region's populations and economies.
