# Clean Cooking Transition for Peri-Urban Households in the Andean Highlands: Safeguarding Freshwater Ecosystems

## Executive Summary

The Andean highlands face significant environmental challenges due to climate change, including water scarcity and pollution from traditional cooking methods. This brief explores the potential of integrating agroforestry with micro-hydro systems for peri-urban households in this region, focusing on safeguarding freshwater ecosystems.

## Introduction to Water Scarcity

Climate change has severe impacts on agriculture in the Andean highlands, exacerbating water scarcity due to erratic rainfall patterns and increased evaporation from wetlands. Traditional irrigation methods are often energy-intensive and costly, straining economic resources for smallholder farmers in vulnerable communities.

## Current Irrigation Practices and Challenges

Existing irrigation systems in the Andean highlands include boreholes and canals, which suffer from significant water loss through evaporation and seepage. Economic analysis reveals the high costs associated with these traditional methods, making it challenging for smallholder farmers to maintain their livelihoods.
