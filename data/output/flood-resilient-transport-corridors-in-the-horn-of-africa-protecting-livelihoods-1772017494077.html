Clean Energy

Renewable energy consumption in Sub-Saharan Africa is at a mere 19.7%, significantly below global averages (World Bank Open Data). This disparity hinders economic development and exacerbates climate stressors like droughts and floods.

Climate Adaptation

Climate change impacts are severe, with increased frequency and intensity of extreme weather events. Sub-Saharan Africa's limited water resources and energy infrastructure make it particularly vulnerable to these changes.

Policy Recommendations

1. Solar-Powered Desalination Plants: * Implement solar-powered desalination plants in coastal regions where freshwater scarcity is acute. * Evidence: Solar-powered desalination can provide a sustainable solution for safe drinking water, especially in arid areas (Source: Renewable Energy and Climate Action Plans [RECP]).

  1. Expand Microgrids for Rural Clinics:
* Upgrade existing microgrid systems or install new ones to supply electricity to rural clinics. * Evidence: Microgrids reduce energy costs, improve reliability, and support healthcare services in remote areas (Source: International Energy Agency [IEA]).
  1. Enhance Climate-Smart Irrigation Cooperatives:
* Develop irrigation systems that use water more efficiently and integrate climate-resilient practices. * Evidence: Climate-smart irrigation can increase crop yields while reducing water usage, making it a key strategy for sustainable agriculture (Source: CGIAR Research Program on Water, Land and Ecosystems [WLE]).

Implementation Considerations

Cost estimates for these interventions are as follows:

* Solar-powered desalination plants: approximately $2 million per facility

  • Climate-smart irrigation cooperatives: about $100,000 for initial setup
