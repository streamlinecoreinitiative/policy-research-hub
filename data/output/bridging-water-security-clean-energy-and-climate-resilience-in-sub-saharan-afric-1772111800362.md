## Executive Summary

Sub-Saharan Africa faces a critical water security crisis, with only 32.4% of its population having access to safely managed drinking water (World Bank Open Data). This shortage is exacerbated by limited electricity access (53.3%), which hampers sanitation facilities and public health outcomes (World Bank Open Data). To bridge this gap, we recommend implementing policies promoting solar-powered energy generation, rainwater harvesting systems, and climate-resilient agricultural practices.

## Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa's population growth rate is among the highest globally, putting immense pressure on its water resources. Climate change intensifies droughts and floods, reducing access to safe drinking water and electricity (UN-Water/WHO). This perfect storm of factors underscores the urgent need for policy interventions that enhance resilience through improved access to clean water, renewable energy sources, and climate-smart agriculture.

1. **Solar-Powered Energy Generation**: Implement policies incentivizing solar energy adoption in rural areas to provide affordable and reliable electricity.

2. **Rainwater Harvesting Systems**: Develop national programs promoting rainwater harvesting for water security in urban and peri-urban settings.

3. **Renewable Energy Access**: Allocate public funds towards building solar-powered wells and other renewable energy infrastructure to improve access to clean water.
	* Source: World Bank Open Data
4. **Climate-Resilient Agriculture Practices**: Invest in agroforestry value chains that integrate drought-resistant crops and efficient irrigation systems.

## Implementation Considerations

Implementing these interventions will require significant upfront investment but can be offset by long-term savings on medical expenses due to improved sanitation and energy access. Potential barriers include limited government funding, resistance from traditional power sources, and lack of technical expertise in rural areas.

## Conclusion

Effective implementation of the recommended policies is crucial for enhancing water security, clean energy, and climate resilience in Sub-Saharan Africa. By fostering innovation and collaboration, we can build a more resilient future for all communities.
