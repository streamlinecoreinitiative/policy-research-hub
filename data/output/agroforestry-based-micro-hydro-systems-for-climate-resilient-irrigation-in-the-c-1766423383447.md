**Agroforestry-Based Micro-Hydro Systems for Climate-Resilient Irrigation in the Central African Rainforest**

**Executive Summary:**
The Central African Rainforest faces severe water scarcity due to climate change, posing significant challenges to agriculture and food security. Traditional irrigation methods are energy-intensive and costly, exacerbating economic difficulties for smallholder farmers in vulnerable communities. This brief proposes integrating agroforestry with micro-hydro systems as a sustainable solution that enhances both water security and renewable energy use.

**Water Scarcity and Climate Change Impacts**

The Central African Rainforest is characterized by erratic rainfall patterns and increased evaporation from vast wetlands, leading to severe water scarcity. Climate change has exacerbated these conditions, affecting agriculture and food security in the region. Smallholder farmers rely heavily on irrigation systems, but traditional methods are often energy-intensive and costly.

**Current Irrigation Practices and Challenges**

Existing irrigation systems in the Central African Rainforest include boreholes and canals. However, these methods suffer from high water loss through evaporation and seepage, resulting in significant economic costs for smallholder farmers. The use of diesel generators to power pumps further exacerbates the financial burden on these communities.
