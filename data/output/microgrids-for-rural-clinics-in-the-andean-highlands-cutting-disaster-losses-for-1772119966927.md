**Microgrids for Rural Clinics in the Andean Highlands: Cutting Disaster Losses for Vulnerable Groups**

## Executive Summary

Sub-Saharan Africa faces significant challenges in water security, energy access, and climate resilience. Despite progress in some areas, millions lack safe drinking water, electricity remains scarce, and the region is increasingly vulnerable to extreme weather events. To address these issues, we recommend prioritizing clean energy and climate adaptation strategies to enhance water access and reliability.

## Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa lags behind in meeting SDG 6 targets for water security and climate resilience. Recent data highlights the urgency of these challenges:

* Access to safely managed drinking water: [VERIFY] (Sub-Saharan Africa, 2024) vs. 73.7% (World, 2024) [1]
* Access to electricity: 53.3% (Sub-Saharan Africa, 2023) [2] vs. 91.6% (World, 2023) [3]

The region's limited freshwater resources and inefficient water management lead to frequent water shortages. Inadequate energy infrastructure exacerbates these issues, particularly in rural areas. Climate change intensifies droughts and floods, increasing vulnerability.

## Analysis

Evidence suggests that:

* Limited freshwater resources and inefficient water management contribute to frequent water shortages [5]
* Inadequate energy infrastructure worsens these issues, especially in rural areas [6]
* Climate change increases the frequency and severity of extreme weather events [7]

Comparing approaches, we find that:

* Solar-powered mini-grids can improve electricity access and reduce reliance on fossil fuels [8]
* Investing in climate-resilient agriculture practices can enhance food security and reduce vulnerability to climate-related shocks [9]

## Policy Recommendations

To address these challenges, we recommend the following:

1. **Invest in solar-powered mini-grids** for rural clinics and communities, promoting a reliable source of electricity and reducing reliance on fossil fuels.
2. **Promote rainwater harvesting systems** and small-scale irrigation projects to enhance water access and efficiency.
3. **Support the expansion of renewable energy sources**, such as solar, wind, and hydroelectric power, to reduce dependence on fossil fuels.

## Implementation Considerations

To implement these recommendations, governments, NGOs, and development partners should:

1. Allocate funds through international development banks and multilateral organizations.
2. Engage local communities in planning and implementation processes.
3. Address potential barriers, including political resistance, lack of funding, and technical constraints.
