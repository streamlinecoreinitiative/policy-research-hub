## Executive Summary

The Andean highlands are vulnerable to climate change, facing severe water scarcity and poverty. This brief highlights the need for integrated solutions that address these challenges through agroforestry-based micro-hydro systems for climate-resilient irrigation.

## Introduction to Water Scarcity in the Andean Highlands

The region is characterized by limited water resources, making efficient and sustainable irrigation practices essential. Climate change has exacerbated this issue, with rising temperatures and altered precipitation patterns affecting agricultural productivity.

### Key Challenges:

*   **Climate Change Impacts:** Rising temperatures and altered precipitation patterns affect agricultural productivity.
*   **Population Growth:** Increasing demand for water resources strains existing supplies.
*   **Sustainable Agricultural Practices:** Limited adoption of efficient irrigation methods contributes to water scarcity.

## Agroforestry's Role in Climate-Resilient Irrigation

Agroforestry practices have been shown to enhance soil fertility, improve water retention, and promote biodiversity. By integrating agroforestry with traditional irrigation methods, communities can develop more resilient and sustainable water management systems.
