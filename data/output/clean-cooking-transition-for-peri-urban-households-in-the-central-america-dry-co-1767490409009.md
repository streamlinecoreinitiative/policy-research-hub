# Clean Cooking Transition for Peri-Urban Households in the Central America Dry Corridor: Safeguarding Freshwater Ecosystems

## Executive Summary

The Central America dry corridor is highly vulnerable to climate change, facing severe water scarcity and droughts that impact agriculture, food security, and economic stability. This brief highlights the importance of transitioning peri-urban households towards clean cooking practices, emphasizing the integration of agroforestry-based micro-hydro systems for climate-resilient irrigation.

## Policy Recommendations

To support the adoption of integrated agroforestry and micro-hydro systems, policy recommendations include:

1.  **Subsidies:** Governments can provide subsidies for farmers to adopt climate-resilient irrigation practices.
2.  **Low-Interest Loans:** Financial institutions can offer low-interest loans to farmers to invest in micro-hydro technology.
3.  **Infrastructure Development:** Governments can invest in infrastructure development, such as roads and storage facilities, to support the adoption of integrated agroforestry and micro-hydro systems.
