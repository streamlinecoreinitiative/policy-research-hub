Enhancing Environmental Resilience in Lower-Income Countries: Water Security, Clean Energy, and Climate Adaptation

Executive Summary

To address the pressing challenges of water security, clean energy access, and climate adaptation in lower-income countries, we recommend establishing climate-smart irrigation cooperatives in the Mekong basin. These cooperatives will improve health outcomes during heatwaves by providing safe drinking water, promoting clean energy adoption, and bolstering climate adaptation strategies.

Context & Challenge

Lower-income countries face significant challenges related to water security and clean energy access. According to the World Bank Open Data [1], only 32.4% of the population in Sub-Saharan Africa has access to safely managed drinking water [NEEDS SOURCE]. This stark disparity underscores the urgency of improving water security in this region. Furthermore, Sub-Saharan Africa lags behind other regions with 53.3% electricity coverage [2], highlighting significant gaps in clean energy access.

Climate change exacerbates these challenges by reducing water availability, disrupting energy supply chains, and increasing the vulnerability of communities. The global population lacking safe drinking water is estimated at 2.2 billion people [3]. In Sub-Saharan Africa, only 32.4% of the population has access to safely managed drinking water [1].

Analysis

The current state of water security and clean energy access in Sub-Saharan Africa is characterized by stark disparities. While significant progress has been made globally (91.6% electricity coverage worldwide) [2], regions like Sub-Saharan Africa still face considerable challenges. Climate change impacts, such as increased frequency of droughts and floods, further complicate these challenges.
