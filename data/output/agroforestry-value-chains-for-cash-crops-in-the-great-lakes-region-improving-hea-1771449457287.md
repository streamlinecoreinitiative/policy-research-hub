## Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa is one of the most vulnerable regions globally concerning access to clean water. Only 32.4% of its population has access to safely managed drinking water, compared to a global average of 73.7% [Source: World Bank Open Data]. This disparity underscores the urgent need for interventions.

Access to electricity remains uneven with only 53.3% of Sub-Saharan Africa's population having access to reliable power [Source: World Bank Open Data]. Renewable energy consumption is even lower at 19.7%, highlighting the critical need for sustainable energy solutions.

## Policy Recommendations

1. **Community-Based Watershed Management:**
	* Implement watershed management programs to improve water quality and availability.
	* Encourage community participation in monitoring and maintaining infrastructure.
2. **Micro-Insurance Schemes for Drought Risk Mitigation:**
	* Develop micro-insurance products specifically targeting smallholder farmers who face high risks from droughts.
	* Provide financial support to affected communities through insurance payouts, enabling them to recover more quickly.
3. **Solar-Powered Desalination Plants:**
	* Invest in solar-powered desalination facilities to provide clean water for households and businesses.
	* Collaborate with private sector partners for financing and implementation.
