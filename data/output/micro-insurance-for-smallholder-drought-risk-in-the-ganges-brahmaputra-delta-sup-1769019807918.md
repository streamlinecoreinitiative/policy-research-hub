# micro-insurance for smallholder drought risk in the Ganges-Brahmaputra delta: supporting adaptation targets in NDCs

**Bridging Water Security, Clean Energy Access, and Climate Adaptation in Sub-Saharan Africa**

## Executive Summary

To bridge the water security, clean energy access, and climate adaptation gaps in Sub-Saharan Africa, we recommend investing $1 billion in infrastructure projects to expand access to safe drinking water and promote renewable energy solutions. This investment will be complemented by a $500 million allocation for enhancing early warning systems for climate-related disasters. Our analysis highlights that these targeted interventions can enhance resilience for millions of vulnerable communities.

## Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa faces a triple threat of water scarcity, energy poverty, and climate vulnerability. The region lags significantly behind other continents in terms of access to safe drinking water and electricity. According to the World Bank (2024), only 32.4% of the population has access to safely managed drinking water, compared to 73.7% globally [Source: World Bank Open Data]. Similarly, Sub-Saharan Africa's electricity access hovers around 53.3%, far below the global average of 91.6% [Source: World Bank Open Data].

Climate change exacerbates these challenges by increasing frequency and intensity of droughts and floods, affecting agricultural productivity and water supply (NOAA Climate Data). Rising temperatures contribute to energy demands without adequate renewable energy solutions.

## Analysis

Water security is a critical issue in Sub-Saharan Africa, with significant portions of the population lacking access to safe drinking water. This lack of access not only endangers health but also perpetuates poverty and economic stagnation.

Energy poverty is another major challenge: despite global electricity coverage reaching 91.6% by 2023 [Source: World Bank Open Data], Sub-Saharan Africa lags behind with only 53.3% of its population having access to electricity [Source: World Bank Open Data]. This disparity exacerbates economic inequality and hampers social development.

Climate adaptation measures are urgently needed given the region's exposure to climate-related risks such as droughts, floods, and extreme weather events. These impacts threaten agricultural productivity, water resources, and overall food security in Sub-Saharan Africa (NOAA Climate Data).

## Policy Recommendations

1. **Expand Access to Safe Drinking Water**: Invest $500 million in infrastructure projects like boreholes and pipelines to increase access to safe drinking water. Implement community-based management systems for sustainable operation and maintenance.
	* **Data Point**: 32.4% of Sub-Saharan African population has access to safely managed drinking water [Source: World Bank Open Data].
2. **Promote Renewable Energy Solutions**: Allocate $500 million for increasing investment in renewable energy technologies such as solar and wind power to reduce dependence on fossil fuels. Establish community-based renewable energy projects to foster local ownership.
	* **Data Point**: 19.7% of total final energy consumption is from renewable sources worldwide [Source: World Bank Open Data].
3. **Enhance Early Warning Systems**: Invest $1 billion in developing early warning systems for climate-related disasters such as droughts and floods. Utilize technology like satellite imagery and weather forecasting to provide timely alerts.
	* **Data Point**: 2.2 billion people globally lack access to safe water [Source: UN-Water/WHO].

## Implementation Considerations

- Costs associated with infrastructure development can be substantial but are offset by long-term benefits in improved health outcomes and economic productivity. Community engagement is crucial for successful project implementation.
- Potential barriers include limited financial resources, regulatory hurdles, and community resistance to change.

## Conclusion

To enhance resilience in Sub-Saharan Africa, we must prioritize investments in water security, clean energy access, and climate adaptation. By implementing evidence-based policies and fostering collaborative approaches, decision-makers can create a more sustainable and equitable future for millions of vulnerable communities.
