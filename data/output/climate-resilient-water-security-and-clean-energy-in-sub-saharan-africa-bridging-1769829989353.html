Executive Summary

Sub-Saharan Africa faces significant challenges in accessing safe water, reliable electricity, and sustainable energy sources due to climate variability and limited infrastructure. This policy brief highlights specific interventions that can improve resilience, focusing on cost-effective solutions for government officials, NGO leaders, and development practitioners.

    Context & Challenge

    Sub-Saharan Africa is home to over half a billion people lacking access to safe drinking water (2.2 billion globally) [5], with only 32.4% having access [1]. Electricity access stands at 53.3%, significantly lower than the world average of 91.6% [2].     Climate change impacts exacerbate these disparities, leading to more frequent droughts and floods that disrupt water supply and power generation.

    Analysis

    Access to safe drinking water is crucial for health and economic development; however, only a third of Sub-Saharan Africa's population has access (32.4% vs 73.7% globally) [1]. Renewable energy consumption in the region remains low at 9.2%, compared to global levels of 19.7% [6]. This disparity limits sustainable power generation and exacerbates energy poverty.

    Climate change-induced weather variability affects water resources, leading to seasonal droughts that reduce agricultural yields and disrupt water supply systems.

Implementation Considerations

Costs associated with rainwater harvesting projects range from $50-$100 per household, while solar-powered water wells cost approximately $200-$300 each. These investments are expected to yield long-term benefits in terms of reduced reliance on unsustainable water sources.

Scaling up these initiatives requires partnerships between governments, NGOs, and private sector entities to ensure sustainable financing and implementation.

Conclusion

Addressing climate-resilient water security and clean energy in Sub-Saharan Africa is crucial for reducing poverty, improving health outcomes, and fostering economic growth. By implementing these recommendations, governments can significantly improve living standards while contributing to global environmental goals.
