## Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa is characterized by arid conditions and frequent droughts, affecting millions of people's access to clean water. Climate change intensifies these challenges, leading to increased water scarcity and energy demand (IPCC, 2020). The region has limited access to safely managed drinking water (32.4%, World Bank, 2024) and electricity (53.3%, World Bank, 2023), with renewable energy consumption at just [VERIFY] 19.7% (World Bank, 2020).

## Analysis

### Water Security

Sub-Saharan Africa has 32.4% access to safely managed drinking water (World Bank, 2024). This low figure highlights the urgent need for improved infrastructure. Investing in drilling boreholes and building water treatment facilities can increase access to safe drinking water.

### Clean Energy

Only 53.3% of the population in Sub-Saharan Africa has access to electricity (World Bank, 2023), with renewable energy consumption at just [VERIFY] 19.7% (World Bank, 2020). Establishing public-private partnerships for solar panel installation in rural areas and providing subsidies or grants to households and small businesses can promote renewable energy adoption.

### Climate Adaptation

The region is vulnerable to climate-related impacts such as droughts and floods, which strain water resources. Developing early warning systems using satellite data to predict droughts and floods and implementing soil conservation practices in agriculture can increase water retention.
