**Climate-Smart Irrigation Cooperatives in the Ganges-Brahmaputra Delta: Raising Resilience Finance for Communities**

## Executive Summary

To address the pressing challenges of water security, clean energy access, and climate adaptation in Sub-Saharan Africa, we recommend establishing climate-smart irrigation cooperatives in the Ganges-Brahmaputra delta. These cooperatives will enable communities to develop sustainable agriculture practices, improve water management, and increase resilience to climate-related shocks. We estimate that an initial investment of $50-100 million per year can be allocated towards infrastructure projects, with ongoing maintenance costs estimated at 20% of the initial investment.

## Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa is facing significant challenges related to water security, clean energy access, and climate adaptation. According to World Bank data (2024), 32.4% of the population lacks access to safe drinking water [1], while only 53.3% have access to electricity [2]. The region's ecosystems are under threat from climate change, with increased frequency and intensity of droughts, floods, and other extreme weather events [3].

## Analysis

The data reveals significant disparities in access to basic services:

*   **Water Security**: Sub-Saharan Africa has 32.4% of its population lacking safe drinking water [1].
*   **Clean Energy Access**: Only 53.3% have access to electricity, with a further challenge posed by the limited use of renewable energy sources (19.7%) [4].

These statistics highlight the urgent need for interventions that address both immediate and long-term challenges in water security, clean energy, and climate adaptation.

## Policy Recommendations

To bridge these gaps, we recommend implementing the following specific, actionable steps:

1.  **Establish Climate-Smart Irrigation Cooperatives**: Create community-led cooperatives to develop and manage sustainable irrigation systems, using a combination of solar-powered pumps and rainwater harvesting techniques.
2.  **Promote Renewable Energy Use**: Implement solar-powered desalination plants and microgrids in rural areas where grid electricity is not available.
3.  **Enhance Climate Resilience**: Develop early warning systems, drought-resistant crop varieties, and water storage solutions such as rainwater harvesting ponds and cisterns.
