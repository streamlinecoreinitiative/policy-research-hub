**Ensuring Water Security, Clean Energy, and Climate Adaptation in Sub-Saharan Africa: A Comprehensive Strategy**

## Executive Summary

Sub-Saharan Africa faces severe water scarcity, energy poverty, and climate vulnerability, threatening the well-being of millions. To address these interlinked challenges, we recommend prioritizing equitable water supply solutions, expanding renewable energy adoption, and enhancing climate adaptation strategies.

Key Findings:

* Only 32.4% of Sub-Saharan Africa's population has access to safely managed drinking water (World Bank Open Data).
* The region lags behind in electricity access, with only 53.3% of the population having access (World Bank Open Data).

Recommendations:

1. Implement inclusive water supply initiatives to enhance access and equity.
2. Invest in renewable energy programs for sustainable power sources.
3. Develop climate adaptation measures to mitigate extreme weather events.

## Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa is disproportionately affected by global challenges like climate change and energy poverty, with limited resources to address these issues effectively. The region's water scarcity and energy poverty have severe consequences on health outcomes, economic disparities, and environmental degradation.

Background:

* Sub-Saharan Africa has made progress in increasing access to safely managed drinking water, but still lags behind the global average (World Bank Open Data).
* Climate-induced droughts and floods underscore the importance of resilient infrastructure and early warning systems (NOAA Climate Data).
