## Executive Summary

This policy brief highlights the urgent need to enhance environmental resilience in Sub-Saharan Africa, focusing on water security, clean energy, and climate adaptation. With only 32.4% of the region's population having access to safely managed drinking water [Source: World Bank Open Data] and a mere 53.3% with access to electricity [Source: World Bank Open Data], there is an immediate need for policy interventions that can bridge this gap effectively.

## Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa is home to 48 countries with a population of over 1 billion (UN-Water/WHO). Despite progress in some areas, the region faces significant challenges related to access to safe drinking water [Source: World Bank Open Data], electricity [Source: World Bank Open Data], and renewable energy consumption [Source: World Bank Open Data]. These issues exacerbate existing problems such as health risks from contaminated water sources and economic inefficiencies due to unreliable power supply.

## Policy Recommendations

1. **Expand Access to Safe Drinking Water**:
	* Invest in sustainable water infrastructure projects like rainwater harvesting systems and solar-powered wells.
	* Pilot programs in select regions for cost-effectiveness and scalability.
	* Collaborate with local communities to ensure ownership and maintenance.
2. **Promote Renewable Energy Adoption**:
	* Encourage the use of solar energy through subsidies, tax incentives, and public-private partnerships (PPPs).
	* Develop community-based solar microgrids to increase access in remote areas.
	* Conduct feasibility studies for different climate zones to ensure affordability and reliability.
3. **Enhance Climate Adaptation Measures**:
	* Implement early warning systems, drought-resistant crop varieties, and water management practices such as drip irrigation.
	* Provide training and education on sustainable land use and conservation.
	* Invest in research and development for climate-resilient technologies.
