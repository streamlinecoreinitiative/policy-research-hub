# Early-Warning Systems and Last-Mile Alerts in the Horn of Africa: Safeguarding Freshwater Ecosystems

## Executive Summary

The Horn of Africa is one of the most water-scarce regions globally, with severe droughts affecting millions of people. This brief highlights the importance of early-warning systems and last-mile alerts in safeguarding freshwater ecosystems. We propose integrating agroforestry-based micro-hydro systems to ensure climate-resilient irrigation, addressing both immediate water scarcity issues and contributing to broader sustainable development goals.

## I. Introduction to Water Scarcity in the Horn of Africa

The Horn of Africa is characterized by arid and semi-arid climates, making it highly vulnerable to droughts. Climate change exacerbates these conditions, leading to severe water scarcity affecting agriculture, livestock, and human consumption. The region's population relies heavily on rain-fed agriculture, which is often unpredictable and unreliable.
