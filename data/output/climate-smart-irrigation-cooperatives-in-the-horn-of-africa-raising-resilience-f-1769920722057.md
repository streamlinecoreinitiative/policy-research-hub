# climate-smart irrigation cooperatives in the Horn of Africa: raising resilience finance for communities

**Climate-Smart Irrigation Cooperatives in the Horn of Africa: Raising Resilience Finance for Communities**

### Executive Summary

This policy brief recommends establishing climate-smart irrigation cooperatives in the Horn of Africa to enhance environmental resilience, particularly in water- and energy-scarce regions. With only 32.4% of sub-Saharan Africa's population having access to safely managed drinking water [Source: World Bank Open Data], and renewable energy consumption standing at 19.7% globally [Source: World Bank Open Data], there is an urgent need for strategic interventions that address these critical issues.

### Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa faces significant environmental challenges, including inadequate access to safe drinking water and electricity. The region's renewable energy consumption of 19.7% underscores the need for accelerated progress towards sustainable development goals (SDGs). These issues are not only critical for human health but also have far-reaching implications for economic growth, food security, and social stability in the region.

### Analysis

The current state of water security and energy access in Sub-Saharan Africa highlights significant disparities compared to global averages. For example, 73.7% of the world has access to safely managed drinking water [Source: World Bank Open Data], but this drops significantly to only 32.4% in sub-Saharan Africa [Source: World Bank Open Data]. Increased climate variability threatens existing infrastructure, exacerbating water scarcity and energy shortages.

### Policy Recommendations

1. **Establish Climate-Smart Irrigation Cooperatives**:
	* Collaborate with local communities to develop and implement climate-resilient irrigation systems.
	* Provide training on efficient water use and crop management practices.
2. **Promote Renewable Energy Adoption**:
	* Offer subsidies for renewable energy technologies like solar panels and wind turbines in off-grid areas.
	* Implement energy efficiency programs to reduce overall electricity consumption.
3. **Enhance Climate Adaptation Measures**:
	* Establish early warning systems for droughts, floods, and other climate-related events using advanced technology like satellite imagery and IoT sensors.
	* Collaborate with local communities on disaster preparedness plans to increase resilience against climate impacts.

### Implementation Considerations

To implement these policies effectively:

1. **Cost Analysis**: Estimate the costs of implementing these policies, including infrastructure development, subsidies for renewable energy technologies, and early warning system deployment.
2. **Funding Mechanisms**: Explore international aid options or leverage public-private partnerships to support implementation.

### Conclusion

Sub-Saharan Africa must prioritize investments in climate-smart irrigation cooperatives, renewable energy adoption, and climate adaptation measures to enhance environmental resilience. By adopting the recommended policies, governments can significantly improve water security, clean energy access, and climate readiness for their populations.

**Call to Action**: Develop a comprehensive policy framework that integrates these recommendations into national development plans, ensuring sustainable progress towards meeting SDG 6 (clean water and sanitation) and related targets in Sub-Saharan Africa.
