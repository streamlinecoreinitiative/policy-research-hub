# climate-smart irrigation cooperatives in the Pacific SIDS: raising resilience finance for communities

**Climate-Smart Irrigation Cooperatives in Pacific SIDS: Enhancing Resilience through Integrated Solutions**

**Executive Summary**

This brief highlights the importance of climate-smart irrigation cooperatives in the Pacific Small Island Developing States (SIDS) to address water security, clean energy, and climate adaptation challenges. The proposed solution integrates agroforestry with micro-hydro systems to provide sustainable and renewable energy for irrigation, improving resilience and reducing costs for communities.

**Section 1: Water Scarcity and Climate Change Impacts**

The Pacific SIDS are highly vulnerable to climate change impacts, including erratic rainfall patterns, sea-level rise, and increased frequency of extreme weather events. These changes exacerbate water scarcity, affecting agriculture, food security, and livelihoods. In the Pacific region, average annual rainfall ranges from 800-1200 mm [verify], with precipitation variability ranging from 600-1400 mm (NOAA climate dataset). Traditional irrigation practices are energy-intensive and costly.

**Section 2: Agroforestry's Role in Climate Resilience**

Agroforestry improves soil health, biodiversity, and carbon sequestration. Studies have shown that agroforestry can increase soil organic matter by up to 8% [verify] and biodiversity by up to 30 native species [verify]. Community acceptance and benefits derived from agroforestry are essential for its long-term success.

**Section 3: Micro-Hydro Systems for Irrigation Efficiency**

Micro-hydro systems provide sustainable and renewable energy for irrigation, reducing costs and improving efficiency. Power generation ranges from 10-50 kW per system, with an energy efficiency of up to 80% [verify]. The cost savings compared to traditional irrigation methods are significant.

**Section 4: Integration and Policy Recommendations**

The integration of agroforestry and micro-hydro systems can provide cost-effective outcomes. Policy measures can create a supportive environment for the adoption of these integrated solutions. Subsidies for farmers, grid integration, and infrastructure development are essential to enhance reliability and efficiency.

**Actions/Recommendations**

1.  **Develop climate-resilient irrigation cooperatives**: Integrate agroforestry with micro-hydro systems to provide sustainable and renewable energy for irrigation.
2.  **Promote policy support**: Encourage governments to implement policies that incentivize the adoption of climate-smart irrigation cooperatives, including subsidies for farmers and grid integration.
3.  **Invest in infrastructure development**: Improve roads, storage facilities, and other infrastructure to enhance the reliability and efficiency of micro-hydro systems.

**Closing Outlook**

Climate-smart irrigation cooperatives have the potential to address water security, clean energy, and climate adaptation challenges in the Pacific SIDS. By integrating agroforestry with micro-hydro systems, communities can improve resilience and reduce costs. Policymakers, development partners, and local stakeholders must work together to create a supportive environment for the adoption of these integrated solutions.
