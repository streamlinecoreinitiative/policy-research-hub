**Clean Cooking Transition for Peri-Urban Households in the Ganges-Brahmaputra Delta: Protecting Livelihoods under Climate Stress**

### Executive Summary

The Ganges-Brahmaputra delta, a critical region for global food security and livelihoods, faces unprecedented climate stress. Rising temperatures, changing precipitation patterns, and increased frequency of extreme weather events threaten the resilience of peri-urban households reliant on traditional cooking practices. This brief outlines an integrated approach to address water security, clean energy, and climate adaptation in this context.

### Introduction

Climate change impacts are acutely felt in the Ganges-Brahmaputra delta, where precipitation patterns vary significantly from year to year. Traditional irrigation systems suffer from high water loss percentages due to evaporation and seepage. This brief focuses on integrating agroforestry-based micro-hydro systems for climate-resilient irrigation in peri-urban areas.
