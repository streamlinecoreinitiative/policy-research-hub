**Executive Summary**

The Mesoamerican region faces severe water scarcity due to climate change impacts, population growth, and unsustainable agricultural practices. This brief highlights the potential of agroforestry-based micro-hydro systems to address these challenges and promote sustainable economic benefits for vulnerable groups.

**Section 1: Introduction to Water Scarcity in the Mesoamerican Highlands**

The Mesoamerican region has a semi-arid climate with limited rainfall and high evaporation rates. Climate change projections indicate increased temperature and reduced precipitation, exacerbating water scarcity (NOAA Climate Data). Electricity access rates in the Mesoamerican region are below global standards, limiting the adoption of efficient irrigation systems (World Bank: Electricity Access Data).

**Section 2: Current Practices and Challenges**

Existing irrigation systems in the Mesoamerican region are often inefficient, relying on surface water sources or groundwater with high operational costs. Socio-economic factors, such as limited access to electricity for micro-hydro systems, contribute to water scarcity in the region (World Bank: Renewable Energy Consumption Data). Case studies demonstrate that traditional irrigation methods can be improved through agroforestry practices, enhancing soil fertility and water retention.
