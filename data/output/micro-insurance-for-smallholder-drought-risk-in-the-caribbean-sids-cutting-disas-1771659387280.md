**Enhancing Environmental Resilience in Sub-Saharan Africa Through Water Security, Clean Energy, and Climate Adaptation**

## Executive Summary

This policy brief highlights the pressing need to enhance environmental resilience in sub-Saharan Africa by addressing water security, clean energy access, and climate adaptation challenges. With only 32.4% of the region's population having access to safely managed drinking water (World Bank Open Data), and less than half (53.3%) accessing electricity (World Bank Open Data), there is an urgent need for policy interventions that can bridge these gaps. We recommend investing in renewable energy projects, implementing community-based water management initiatives, and strengthening climate adaptation strategies.

## Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa faces significant challenges related to water scarcity, electricity poverty, and climate change impacts. The region's population has limited access to safe drinking water (32.4%) and electricity (53.3%), exacerbating health and economic disparities (World Bank Open Data). Unsustainable freshwater withdrawals of 9.2% highlight the need for more efficient resource management (World Bank Open Data).

## Analysis

### Water Security

Only 32.4% of Sub-Saharan Africa's population has access to safely managed drinking water, with a significant portion (67.6%) lacking this basic service (World Bank Open Data). This is in contrast to the global average of 73.7% (World Bank Open Data).

### Clean Energy Access

Electricity access is also limited, particularly in rural areas where only 53.3% of the population has access to electricity (World Bank Open Data). This disparity exacerbates economic and social inequalities.

### Climate Adaptation

Climate change poses severe threats, including increased frequency of droughts and floods, which further compounds existing water scarcity issues.
