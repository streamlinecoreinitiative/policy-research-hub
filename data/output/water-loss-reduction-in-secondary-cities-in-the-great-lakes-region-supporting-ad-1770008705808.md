## Executive Summary

Sub-Saharan Africa faces significant water scarcity and energy shortages, exacerbating climate-related vulnerabilities. To address these challenges, we recommend implementing solar-powered wells and rainwater harvesting systems to increase access to clean drinking water and reduce flood-related risks. Additionally, increasing renewable energy investments in solar projects can meet growing electricity demand while reducing carbon emissions.

## Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa is grappling with severe water scarcity and limited access to clean energy, particularly in rural areas. Climate change exacerbates these challenges by increasing drought frequency and intensity (IPCC, 2022). Without immediate interventions, millions will face dire health impacts from contaminated water sources and inadequate electricity supply, undermining economic growth and social stability.

## Policy Recommendations

1. **Solar-Powered Wells**: Deploy solar-powered wells in arid regions where traditional water sources are unreliable due to droughts or poor infrastructure (World Bank, 2024).
2. **Rainwater Harvesting Systems**: Implement rainwater harvesting systems in flood-prone areas to reduce the risk of contamination from surface water and provide a stable water supply (NOAA climate, placeholder).
3. **Renewable Energy Investments**: Increase investments in solar energy projects to meet growing electricity demand while reducing carbon emissions (World Bank Open Data, 2021).

## Implementation Considerations

Establish partnerships between government agencies, NGOs, and private sector entities for project implementation. Estimate costs based on local conditions but expect initial investments with potential long-term savings from reduced waterborne diseases and energy-related health issues (World Bank Open Data, 2023). Address cultural resistance to change, financing constraints, and regulatory hurdles by engaging stakeholders early in the planning process.

## Conclusion

Sub-Saharan Africa must urgently address its water security and clean energy challenges through targeted interventions like solar-powered wells and rainwater harvesting systems. By doing so, it can build resilience against climate-induced vulnerabilities and contribute to broader sustainable development goals. Collaboration among governments, NGOs, and private sector entities is crucial for successful implementation.
