## Executive Summary

Sub-Saharan Africa faces significant challenges in ensuring resilient water security, clean energy, and climate adaptation. With only 32.4% access to safely managed drinking water (World Bank Open Data) and 53.3% electricity access (World Bank Open Data), the region is lagging behind global rates. To address these gaps, we recommend establishing community-based water management systems and promoting solar energy solutions for off-grid areas.

## Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa's water scarcity and electricity access issues are exacerbated by climate change impacts like droughts and floods. Ensuring resilient water security, clean energy, and effective climate adaptation strategies is crucial to foster inclusive economic growth and social well-being. According to the World Bank Open Data, 2.2 billion people globally lack safe drinking water (UN-Water/WHO), with Sub-Saharan Africa accounting for a significant portion of this number.

## Policy Recommendations

To address these challenges, we recommend the following actionable steps:

1. **Increase Access to Safe Water**: Implement community-based water management systems and prioritize investments in rainwater harvesting and aquifer recharge projects.
2. **Enhance Renewable Energy Utilization**: Promote solar energy solutions for off-grid areas and subsidize clean energy technologies like biogas digesters.
3. **Develop Climate Resilient Infrastructure**: Invest in early warning systems for extreme weather events and fortify water infrastructure against climate-induced damages.
