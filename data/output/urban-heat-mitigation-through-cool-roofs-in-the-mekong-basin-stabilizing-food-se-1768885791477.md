## Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa faces a severe water crisis, with only 32.4% of the population having access to safely managed drinking water [1]. This disparity is further compounded by limited electricity access (53.3%) [2] and reliance on non-renewable energy sources. Climate variability and extreme weather events have intensified over recent years, threatening agricultural productivity, water supplies, and overall community resilience.

## Analysis

### Water Security

Sub-Saharan Africa has a freshwater withdrawal rate of 9.2%, indicating significant pressure on available resources [3]. Unsafe water sources contribute to malnutrition and disease outbreaks, particularly among children. Investing in clean water infrastructure is crucial for improving public health and reducing poverty.

### Clean Energy Access

Despite global trends towards renewable energy, only 53.3% of the population in Sub-Saharan Africa has access to electricity [2]. This disparity is most pronounced in rural areas, where power infrastructure remains underdeveloped or non-existent. Investing in renewable energy projects can help reduce carbon emissions and enhance energy access.

### Climate Adaptation

Climate variability and extreme weather events have intensified over recent years, threatening agricultural productivity, water supplies, and overall community resilience [5]. Implementing early warning systems and climate-resilient agriculture practices is essential for mitigating the impacts of droughts and floods.
