Sub-Saharan Africa faces significant challenges in water security, clean energy access, and climate adaptation. To address this, we recommend implementing integrated policies that focus on sustainable water management, renewable energy solutions, and climate mitigation strategies.

## Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa is highly vulnerable to climate change impacts such as droughts, floods, and extreme weather events. These challenges affect safe drinking water supply, food security, and economic stability. Despite some progress in SDG 6, millions lack access to clean water, leading to health issues and reduced productivity.

According to the UN-Water/WHO report (2022), 2.2 billion people worldwide lack access to safely managed drinking water [1]. This number is particularly concerning in Sub-Saharan Africa, where only 74% of the global population has safely managed drinking water [3].

## Policy Recommendations

We recommend the following integrated policies:

1. **Clean Cooking Transition**:
	* Implement subsidies for cleaner stoves like LPG or biogas digesters in peri-urban areas (source: UNDP) [2].
	* Encourage community-led initiatives that promote the use of biomass cookstoves with improved designs.
2. **Solar Cold-Chain for Fisheries**:
	* Invest in solar-powered cold storage facilities to reduce post-harvest losses and improve food safety.
	* Provide training programs on proper storage techniques and market access support.
3. **Microgrids for Rural Clinics**:
	* Develop microgrid projects that integrate solar power with energy-efficient lighting, refrigeration units, and communication systems.
	* Collaborate with local health centers to ensure sustainable maintenance and operation of these facilities.
