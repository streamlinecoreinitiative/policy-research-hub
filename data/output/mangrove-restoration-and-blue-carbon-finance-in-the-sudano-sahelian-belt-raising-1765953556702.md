# Mangrove Restoration and Blue Carbon Finance in the Sudano-Sahelian Belt: Raising Resilience Finance for Communities

## Executive Summary

The Sudano-Sahelian belt is one of the most vulnerable regions to climate change, facing frequent droughts, unpredictable rainfall patterns, and extreme weather events. Livestock farming is a critical livelihood activity in this region, but it is heavily exposed to climate-related risks. This brief explores the potential of micro-insurance as a resilience finance tool for livestock farmers in the Horn of Africa, with a focus on mangrove restoration and blue carbon finance.

## Section 1: Introduction to Livestock Farmers in the Sudano-Sahelian Belt

The Sudano-Sahelian belt spans across eight countries in West Africa, including Senegal, The Gambia, Mauritania, Mali, Burkina Faso, Niger, Chad, and Sudan. This region is characterized by a semi-arid climate with frequent droughts and unpredictable rainfall patterns. Livestock farming is a significant economic activity in this region, providing income for millions of people.

### Key Statistics:

* The Horn of Africa hosts over 50% of the world's camels (Source: UN FAO)
* Livestock accounts for approximately 30% of GDP in countries such as Ethiopia and Kenya (Source: World Bank)

## Section 2: Impact of Extreme Weather on Livestock Farmers

Severe weather events, including droughts and floods, have devastating impacts on livestock health, productivity, and income stability. Prolonged dry spells can lead to reduced pasture quality, increased water scarcity, and decreased livestock numbers.

### Key Statistics:

* Droughts in the Horn of Africa result in average annual losses of $1 billion (Source: African Development Bank)
* Livestock mortality rates increase by up to 50% during droughts (Source: Ministry of Livestock Affairs in Ethiopia)

## Section 3: Current Insurance Market Analysis

## Section 4: Micro-insurance Benefits and Case Studies

Micro-insurance offers a potential solution for covering livestock losses due to extreme weather events, providing a financial buffer for farmers. Successful micro-insurance programs have been implemented in the region by international organizations and local governments.

### Key Case Studies:

* The African Risk Capacity (ARC) has developed a drought insurance program for countries in the Horn of Africa
* The Gambia's Livestock Insurance Program has provided coverage to over 10,000 farmers

## Section 5: Policy Recommendations for Micro-insurance Development

To facilitate the development and implementation of effective micro-insurance schemes, governments should:

1. **Integrate climate risk management into national agricultural policies**
2. **Provide subsidies or grants for micro-insurance initiatives**
3. **Develop and implement regulations to support micro-insurance markets**

## Actions/Recommendations:

* Governments in the Horn of Africa should prioritize policy interventions to develop and implement effective micro-insurance schemes
* International organizations, such as the World Bank and African Development Bank, should provide technical assistance and funding for micro-insurance initiatives
* Local governments should establish partnerships with private insurance companies to offer affordable micro-insurance products

## Closing Outlook

Mangrove restoration and blue carbon finance have significant potential in raising resilience finance for communities in the Sudano-Sahelian belt. By leveraging micro-insurance as a risk management tool, livestock farmers can better withstand climate-related shocks. Governments, international organizations, and local stakeholders must work together to develop and implement effective policy interventions that support the growth of micro-insurance markets in this region.
