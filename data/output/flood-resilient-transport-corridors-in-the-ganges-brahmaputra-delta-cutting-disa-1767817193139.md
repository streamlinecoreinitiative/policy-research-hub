# Flood-Resilient Transport Corridors in the Ganges-Brahmaputra Delta: Enhancing Climate Resilience for Vulnerable Communities

## Executive Summary

The Ganges-Brahmaputra delta, one of the world's most densely populated and vulnerable regions to climate change, faces significant challenges in maintaining flood-resilient transport corridors. Rising sea levels, increased precipitation, and saltwater intrusion threaten the region's infrastructure, economy, and human lives. This brief outlines a comprehensive approach to addressing these challenges through integrated agroforestry-based micro-hydro systems for climate-resilient irrigation.

## Introduction

The Ganges-Brahmaputra delta is home to over 150 million people, with approximately 40% of the region's population living in areas prone to flooding. Climate change exacerbates these risks, with projected sea-level rise and increased precipitation expected to intensify flood events. The current irrigation systems rely heavily on diesel generators, contributing to greenhouse gas emissions and water scarcity.
