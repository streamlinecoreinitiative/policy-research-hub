Climate-Resilient School Feeding Supply Chains in the Caribbean SIDS: Lowering Emissions from Critical Sectors

Executive Summary

The Caribbean Small Island Developing States (SIDS) face significant environmental challenges, including water scarcity, limited access to clean energy, and climate-induced shocks. To address these issues, we recommend implementing policies for improved water security, enhancing clean energy access, and promoting climate adaptation strategies in the region's school feeding supply chains. By doing so, we can reduce greenhouse gas emissions from critical sectors, improve living standards, and bolster regional economic resilience.

Key Findings:

* Only 32.4% of the population in Sub-Saharan Africa has access to safely managed drinking water (World Bank Open Data).

  • The region's renewable energy consumption is low at 19.7%, with over 2 billion people lacking access to safe water globally (UN-Water/WHO).

    Recommendations:

    1. Implement policies for improved water security, including sustainable water supply systems and community-based management programs.

  • Enhance clean energy access through subsidies, grants, and public-private partnerships for renewable energy sources like solar photovoltaic (PV) and biogas.
  • Promote climate adaptation strategies, such as early warning systems, flood-resistant infrastructure, and drought-tolerant crop varieties.

    Sub-Saharan Africa is grappling with severe water scarcity, poor energy access, and climate-induced shocks. The region's water security issues stem from inadequate infrastructure, poor governance, and limited financial resources (UN-Water/WHO).
