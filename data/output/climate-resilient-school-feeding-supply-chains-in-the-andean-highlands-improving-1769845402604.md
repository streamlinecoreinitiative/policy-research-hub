# climate-resilient school feeding supply chains in the Andean highlands: improving health outcomes in heatwaves

**Bridging Water Security, Clean Energy, and Climate Adaptation Gaps in Sub-Saharan Africa**

## Executive Summary

To improve health outcomes and enhance economic resilience in Sub-Saharan Africa, we recommend implementing policies that promote solar-powered wells and rainwater harvesting ponds to address the region's water security crisis. Additionally, increasing investment in renewable energy projects and enhancing climate adaptation strategies are crucial for bridging the gaps in clean energy and climate adaptation.

## Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa faces significant challenges in accessing safe water, reliable electricity, and resilient infrastructure against climate change impacts. According to World Bank data (2024), only 32.4% of the population has access to safely managed drinking water [1], while 53.3% have access to electricity [2]. The region's renewable energy consumption rate is at 19.7%, highlighting significant gaps in meeting sustainable development goals [3].

The water security crisis stems from inadequate infrastructure, leading to frequent waterborne diseases and economic losses. Climate change exacerbates these challenges with increasing frequency of droughts and floods. Without robust resilience measures, Sub-Saharan Africa risks falling further behind in meeting sustainable development goals.

## Analysis

### Water Security

Only 32.4% of the population has safe drinking water access [1]. This disparity is exacerbated by poor sanitation facilities and contaminated sources. According to UN-Water/WHO data (2022), 2.2 billion people lack access to safe water, with Sub-Saharan Africa being one of the most affected regions [4].

### Clean Energy

Despite a global renewable energy consumption rate, Sub-Saharan Africa lags behind at just 19.7% [3]. Rural areas are disproportionately affected due to infrastructure constraints.

### Climate Adaptation

Vulnerable regions face frequent climate shocks with limited adaptive capacity, leading to increased poverty and health risks. Without robust resilience measures, the region's economic growth and development are at risk.

## Policy Recommendations

1. **Promote Solar-Powered Wells and Rainwater Harvesting Ponds**: Invest in solar-powered water pumps for irrigation and household use, coupled with rainwater harvesting systems. This reduces reliance on non-renewable energy sources and ensures sustainable water supply.
2. **Enhance Renewable Energy Projects**: Increase government funding for renewable projects such as wind and solar farms to power rural clinics and schools. Implement policies that incentivize private sector participation in these initiatives.
3. **Climate Adaptation Strategies**: Develop early warning systems for extreme weather events, including droughts and floods. Provide climate-resilient infrastructure like flood barriers and levees.

## Implementation Considerations

Initial investments will require substantial public funds but can be offset by long-term savings from reduced healthcare costs and increased economic productivity. Overcoming resistance to change, securing funding, and ensuring equitable distribution of resources are key challenges. Collaboration between government agencies, NGOs, and private sector partners is crucial for successful implementation.

## Conclusion

Addressing the water security, clean energy, and climate adaptation gaps in Sub-Saharan Africa requires immediate and concerted action from all stakeholders. By implementing targeted policies and leveraging renewable energy sources, the region can enhance its resilience and achieve sustainable development goals. We emphasize the importance of promoting solar-powered wells and rainwater harvesting ponds as a primary recommendation to address the water security crisis.
