### Introduction

The Ethiopian Highlands experience high precipitation variability, with average annual rainfall ranging from 800-1200 mm. However, this variability leads to severe water scarcity, affecting agriculture and livelihoods.

### Current Irrigation Practices and Challenges

Existing irrigation methods in the region rely heavily on borehole pumps, consuming energy. Canal systems also experience significant water loss due to evaporation and seepage. Local surveys and case studies have shown that integrated agroforestry-micro-hydro systems can improve community acceptance and benefits.
