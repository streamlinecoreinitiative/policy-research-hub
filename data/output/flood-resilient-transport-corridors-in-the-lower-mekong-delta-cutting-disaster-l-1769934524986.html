Additionally, governments should implement supportive policies that incentivize renewable energy adoption, such as subsidies for installation costs and feed-in tariffs.

Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa is grappling with critical challenges related to water security, clean energy access, and climate adaptation. With only 32.4% of the population having access to safely managed drinking water (World Bank, 2024) and a mere 53.3% accessing electricity (World Bank, 2023), these issues threaten economic growth and public health. Renewable energy consumption stands at just 19.7% globally (World Bank, 2020), highlighting the urgent need for transformational change.
