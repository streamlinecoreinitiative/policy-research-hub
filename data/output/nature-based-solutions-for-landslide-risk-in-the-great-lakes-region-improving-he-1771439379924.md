# nature-based solutions for landslide risk in the Great Lakes region: improving health outcomes in heatwaves

**Enhancing Environmental Resilience: Access to Clean Water, Reliable Energy, and Climate Adaptation in Sub-Saharan Africa**

## Executive Summary

Sub-Saharan Africa faces significant challenges in accessing clean water and reliable energy sources, exacerbating climate-induced vulnerabilities such as droughts. To address this, we recommend prioritizing investments in infrastructure, climate-resilient energy solutions, and sustainable water management practices.

## Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa lags behind the world in access to safe drinking water (32.4% compared to 73.7% globally) [1]. Over 80 million people lack access to clean water, with only 32.4% having safely managed drinking water facilities [2]. Climate change is intensifying droughts and floods, making existing challenges more severe and exacerbating health risks and economic instability.

## Analysis

Evidence shows that Sub-Saharan Africa's electricity access rate of 37% is far below the global average (91.6%) [1]. The region also struggles with renewable energy consumption, which accounts for only 19.7% of total energy consumption globally [3]. Climate finance needs are estimated at $5.8-5.9 trillion annually for developing countries by 2030 [4].

## Policy Recommendations

### Expand Access to Safe Drinking Water:

1. Implement large-scale water purification plants in rural areas with low-cost desalination technologies.
2. Invest in micro-drinking water systems and community-based water management initiatives.

### Promote Renewable Energy Solutions:

1. Focus on solar energy as a primary source of electricity, particularly for off-grid communities.
2. Introduce subsidies or grants to encourage adoption of renewable energy solutions like solar panels and mini-grids.

### Enhance Climate Resilience:

1. Develop early-warning systems for droughts and floods using satellite imagery and local weather data.
2. Implement climate-resilient agricultural practices such as drought-resistant crop varieties and improved irrigation techniques.

## Implementation Considerations

Addressing water security, reliable energy access, and climate resilience in Sub-Saharan Africa requires significant investments in infrastructure development, which can be substantial but are justified by the long-term benefits of reduced health risks, economic stability, and sustainable energy use [5]. Potential barriers include limited funding, political will, and technical expertise. Address these through targeted training programs and international collaboration.

## Conclusion

Addressing water security, reliable energy access, and climate resilience in Sub-Saharan Africa is essential for building a more resilient future. Immediate action is needed to ensure that vulnerable populations have the resources they need to withstand and adapt to changing environmental conditions.
