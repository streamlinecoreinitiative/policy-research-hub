Enhancing Climate Resilience in Sub-Saharan Africa: Strengthening Water Security, Clean Energy Access, and Adaptation

Executive Summary

Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa is particularly vulnerable to climate impacts due to its geography and economic development level. The region faces severe challenges in ensuring access to clean water, reliable energy sources, and resilient infrastructure against climate change. Despite significant progress, millions of people lack safe drinking water, electricity remains scarce, and the region is particularly vulnerable to climate impacts like droughts and floods.

According to World Bank data (2024), only 32.4% of Sub-Saharan Africans have access to safely managed drinking water, while renewable energy consumption in the region stands at just over 19%, far below global averages. Electricity access remains unevenly distributed across the continent, with Sub-Saharan Africa lagging behind other regions in terms of both physical and sustainable energy solutions.

Analysis

The lack of safe drinking water disproportionately affects vulnerable populations, including women and children who spend hours each day collecting water from contaminated sources. In Sub-Saharan Africa, this task often becomes a significant barrier to education and economic opportunities [Source: World Bank Open Data (2024)]. Renewable energy consumption is also a major concern, accounting for only about 19.7% of total final energy consumption globally [Source: World Bank Open Data (2021)].
