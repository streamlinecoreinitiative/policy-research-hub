# Ensuring Water Security, Clean Energy, and Climate Resilience in Sub-Saharan Africa

**Ensuring Water Security, Clean Energy, and Climate Resilience in Sub-Saharan Africa**

## Executive Summary

Sub-Saharan Africa faces significant challenges in ensuring water security, clean energy access, and climate resilience. With only 32.4% of the population having access to safely managed drinking water (World Bank, 2024) [1] and a meager 53.3% access to electricity (World Bank, 2023) [2], there is an urgent need for sustainable interventions that address these pressing issues. This policy brief recommends:

1. **Invest in water supply infrastructure**: Develop boreholes and desalination plants to increase access to safely managed drinking water.
2. **Enhance renewable energy usage**: Promote solar power and other clean energy sources, developing policies that incentivize private sector investment in clean energy projects.
3. **Strengthen climate adaptation measures**: Implement early warning systems for climate-related disasters, particularly focusing on droughts and floods.

## Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa is facing critical challenges in water security, lack of electricity, and vulnerability to climate impacts. The region's economic development, social stability, and environmental sustainability depend on addressing these issues promptly. According to the World Bank (2023), only 53.3% of households in Sub-Saharan Africa have access to electricity [2], and a staggering 2.2 billion people globally lack safe water (UN-Water/WHO, 2022) [3].

## Analysis

* **Water Access**: Only 32.4% of the population in Sub-Saharan Africa has access to safely managed drinking water (World Bank, 2024) [1], far below the global average of 73.7% (World Bank, 2024) [1].
* **Energy Access**: Less than half of households have access to electricity (World Bank, 2023) [2].
* **Climate Impacts**: Despite significant renewable energy potential, only 19.7% of the world's energy consumption is from renewables (World Bank, 2020) [4], leaving the region vulnerable to climate-induced disruptions.

## Policy Recommendations

1. **Water Security**:
	* Invest in water supply infrastructure such as boreholes and desalination plants.
	* Develop policies that support community-led total sanitation initiatives.
2. **Clean Energy**:
	* Promote solar power and other renewable energy sources.
	* Develop policies that incentivize private sector investment in clean energy projects.
3. **Climate Adaptation**:
	* Implement early warning systems for climate-related disasters, particularly focusing on droughts and floods.

## Implementation Considerations

1. **Practical Steps**: Collaboration between governments, NGOs, and the private sector to ensure sustainable implementation.

Sub-Saharan Africa's development trajectory depends on addressing the pressing issues of water security, clean energy access, and climate resilience. The recommended interventions are feasible, cost-effective, and essential for ensuring a more equitable future.
