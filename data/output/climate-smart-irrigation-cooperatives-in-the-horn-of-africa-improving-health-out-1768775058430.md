# climate-smart irrigation cooperatives in the Horn of Africa: improving health outcomes in heatwaves

**Agroforestry-Based Micro-Hydro Systems for Climate-Resilient Irrigation in the Ethiopian Highlands**

Executive Summary
---------------

Climate change exacerbates water scarcity in the Ethiopian Highlands, impacting agriculture and human health. This brief presents a solution to address this challenge through the integration of agroforestry and micro-hydro systems.

**Addressing Water Scarcity in the Ethiopian Highlands**
---------------------------------------------------

### Introduction

Climate change alters rainfall patterns worldwide, including in the Ethiopian Highlands. The region experiences erratic precipitation, with average annual rainfall ranging from 800-1200 mm [verify] and variability between 600-1400 mm (NOAA climate dataset).

### Current Irrigation Practices

Traditional irrigation methods in the region are energy-intensive and costly. Borehole pumps consume 10-20 kWh/day [verify], while canal systems experience losses of up to 35% due to evaporation and seepage.

### Micro-Hydro Systems for Irrigation Efficiency

Micro-hydro systems provide sustainable and renewable energy, generating power ranging from 10-50 kW per system with an efficiency of 70-80% (Economic Analysis of Micro-Hydro Systems).

**Integration of Agroforestry and Micro-Hydro in Climate-Resilient Water Supply**
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------

The integration of agroforestry and micro-hydro systems offers a cost-effective solution for climate-resilient irrigation. Total project costs range from $10,000-$25,000 per hectare [verify], with a return on investment (ROI) of 3-5 years.

**Policy Recommendations and Future Research Directions**
------------------------------------------------------

To support the adoption of agroforestry-micro-hydro systems, policy measures can be implemented to create a supportive environment. These include:

*   Subsidies for farmers to encourage adoption
*   Grid integration with micro-hydro systems to enhance reliability and efficiency
*   Infrastructure development, including roads, storage facilities, and other improvements
