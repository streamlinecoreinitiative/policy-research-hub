## Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa is one of the least developed regions in terms of infrastructure development, particularly in water supply, clean energy access, and climate resilience. The region's population lacks access to basic services, with only 32.4% having access to safely managed drinking water (World Bank Open Data) and 53.3% having access to electricity (World Bank Open Data). This disparity is not only a humanitarian concern but also an economic one, as it hinders the region's ability to achieve sustainable development goals.

## Policy Recommendations

1. **Community Driven Solutions**: Implement community-led water management programs to improve access to safe drinking water.
2. **Infrastructure Investment**: Invest in sustainable infrastructure projects like boreholes, rainwater harvesting systems, and desalination plants.
3. **Renewable Projects**: Encourage investment in solar, wind, and hydroelectric power generation technologies.
4. **Grid Modernization**: Develop smart grid solutions to increase efficiency and reliability of electricity distribution.
5. **Early Warning Systems**: Establish early warning systems for climate-related events such as droughts and floods.
6. **Insurance Mechanisms**: Implement insurance schemes to help communities mitigate financial losses due to extreme weather events.
