**Micro-insurance for Smallholder Drought Risk in the Lower Mekong Delta: Raising Resilience Finance for Communities**

**Executive Summary**

The Lower Mekong delta is one of the most vulnerable regions to climate change, with frequent droughts and floods threatening the livelihoods of millions of smallholder farmers. This brief explores the potential of micro-insurance as a tool to raise resilience finance for communities in this region, focusing on water security, clean energy, and climate adaptation.

Smallholder farmers in the Lower Mekong delta currently rely on:

• Inadequate irrigation systems
• Limited access to clean energy for water pumping and treatment
• Insufficient climate information services for decision-making

These challenges hinder their ability to adapt to changing climate conditions, leading to reduced crop yields and decreased livelihoods.

**Benefits of Micro-insurance for Smallholder Drought Risk**

Micro-insurance can provide smallholder farmers with:

• Financial protection against drought-related losses
• Access to climate-resilient agricultural practices and technologies
• Improved water management and conservation techniques

This approach can also promote:

• Climate information services and early warning systems
• Clean energy solutions for water pumping and treatment
• Sustainable agriculture practices and soil conservation
