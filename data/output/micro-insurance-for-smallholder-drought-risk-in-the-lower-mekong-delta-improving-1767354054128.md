# micro-insurance for smallholder drought risk in the Lower Mekong delta: improving health outcomes in heatwaves

**Agroforestry-Based Micro-Hydro Systems for Climate-Resilient Irrigation in the Central African Rainforest**

**Executive Summary:**
The Central African Rainforest faces severe water scarcity due to climate change, affecting agriculture and local communities' livelihoods. This project proposes integrating agroforestry with micro-hydro systems as a sustainable solution for climate-resilient irrigation.

**Environmental Resilience in Lower-Income Countries: A Key Challenge**

Climate change exacerbates water scarcity in lower-income countries, affecting agriculture and local communities' livelihoods. In the Central African Rainforest, traditional irrigation methods are energy-intensive and costly, challenging economic development for smallholder farmers. This project aims to address these challenges by integrating agroforestry with micro-hydro systems.

**Current Irrigation Practices and Challenges**

Traditional irrigation systems in the region rely on boreholes and canals, which lose significant amounts of water through evaporation and seepage. These systems are also expensive to maintain:

* **Economic Costs:** The average cost of traditional irrigation methods is estimated at $1,500 per hectare annually [verify].
