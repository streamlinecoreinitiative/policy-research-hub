# solar cold-chain for fisheries in the Great Lakes region: stabilizing food security during droughts

**Enhancing Water Security, Clean Energy Access, and Climate Adaptation in Sub-Saharan Africa: A Comprehensive Policy Brief**

## Executive Summary

This policy brief emphasizes the urgent need to address water security, clean energy access, and climate adaptation challenges in Sub-Saharan Africa. With only 32.4% of the population having access to safely managed drinking water (World Bank, 2024) [1] and just 53.3% accessing electricity (World Bank, 2023) [2], the region is severely impacted by climate change. To stabilize food security during droughts, we recommend implementing solar-powered cold-chain systems for fisheries in the Great Lakes region.

## Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa faces significant environmental challenges due to its geographical location and limited resources. The region experiences more frequent droughts and floods (NOAA, 2024) [3], threatening water security and exacerbating existing vulnerabilities. Climate change impacts are already evident, with rising temperatures affecting agricultural productivity and increasing the spread of diseases.

## Analysis

- **Water Security**: Sub-Saharan Africa suffers from insufficient access to clean water sources, primarily due to poor infrastructure, contaminated water supplies, and inadequate sanitation facilities (World Bank, 2024) [1].
- **Clean Energy Access**: Limited electricity availability hinders economic growth and daily living standards in rural areas, contributing to energy poverty (53.3% of population) (World Bank, 2023) [2].
- **Climate Adaptation**: Climate change impacts are already evident, including more frequent droughts and floods, which threaten water security and exacerbate existing vulnerabilities.

## Policy Recommendations

1. **Expand Water Infrastructure Investments**:
   - Invest in building new wells, boreholes, and pipelines to increase access to safe drinking water (World Bank, 2024) [1].
   - Implement community-based management programs for shared water sources to ensure sustainable use.
   - Source: World Bank Open Data
2. **Promote Renewable Energy Solutions**:
   - Increase deployment of solar panels in off-grid areas and rural communities (World Bank, 2023) [2].
   - Encourage public-private partnerships for financing renewable energy projects like mini-grids.
   - Source: World Bank Open Data
3. **Implement Solar-Powered Cold-Chain Systems for Fisheries**:
   - Develop a pilot program to establish solar-powered cold-chain systems in the Great Lakes region, focusing on fisheries that are critical for food security.

## Implementation Considerations

- **Costs**: Estimated costs for water infrastructure projects range from $150-$300 per person served [VERIFY], while renewable energy mini-grids may cost around $200-$500 per household.
- **Barriers**: Limited funding, lack of technical expertise in rural areas, and resistance to change among local communities could hinder implementation.

## Conclusion

Addressing the pressing issues of water security, clean energy access, and climate adaptation requires coordinated efforts at both national and community levels. By implementing targeted policies and leveraging existing resources, Sub-Saharan Africa can build a more resilient future for all its inhabitants. The immediate adoption of solar-powered cold-chain systems for fisheries in the Great Lakes region is crucial to stabilize food security during droughts.
