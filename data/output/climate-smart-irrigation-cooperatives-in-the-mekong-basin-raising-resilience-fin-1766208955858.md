**Climate-Smart Irrigation Cooperatives in the Mekong Basin: Enhancing Water Security and Renewable Energy**

### Executive Summary

The Mekong Basin is a critical agricultural region facing severe water scarcity and climate-related challenges. Traditional irrigation methods are energy-intensive, costly, and inefficient, exacerbating economic vulnerabilities for smallholder farmers. This brief explores the potential of integrating agroforestry with micro-hydro systems to enhance both water security and renewable energy use in the region.

### Introduction to Water Scarcity in the Mekong Basin

The Mekong Basin is a vital agricultural region facing significant water scarcity issues due to climate change. Rising temperatures have altered rainfall patterns, leading to increased evaporation from its vast wetlands. Traditional irrigation methods are inefficient and costly, particularly in communities already vulnerable to droughts.

### Current Irrigation Practices and Challenges

Existing irrigation systems in the Mekong Basin include boreholes and canals, which suffer from high water loss through evaporation and seepage. These traditional methods waste precious resources and contribute to greenhouse gas emissions.
