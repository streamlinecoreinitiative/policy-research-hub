# Nature-Based Solutions for Landslide Risk in the Indus Watershed: Protecting Livelihoods Under Climate Stress

## Executive Summary

The Indus watershed is vulnerable to landslide risk due to climate stress, impacting livelihoods and economic development. This brief explores nature-based solutions, specifically agroforestry-based micro-hydro systems, to address water security challenges and enhance climate resilience in the Andean Highlands.

## Addressing Severe Water Scarcity in the Andean Highlands

The Andean Highlands face severe water scarcity due to historical droughts, limited access to reliable irrigation systems, and economic instability. Farmers rely on traditional methods like surface water and rain-fed systems, which are inefficient and unsustainable.

### Current Challenges Faced by Farmers and Communities

*   Limited dependency on traditional methods
*   High economic pressure due to reduced crop yields
*   Reduced access to reliable irrigation systems

## Agroforestry's Role in Addressing Water Scarcity

Agroforestry practices offer numerous benefits, including improved water retention and recharge through increased vegetation cover. Case studies from similar climates or contexts demonstrate the success of integrated agroforestry-micro-hydro systems.

### Benefits for the Andean Highlands Context

*   Increased agricultural productivity due to improved soil health and water management
*   Reduced vulnerability to climate variability
