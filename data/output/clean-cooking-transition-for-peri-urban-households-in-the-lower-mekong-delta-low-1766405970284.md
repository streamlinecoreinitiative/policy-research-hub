### Executive Summary

The Lower Mekong delta is one of the most vulnerable regions to climate change, facing severe water scarcity and poverty. The transition to clean cooking practices is crucial for reducing emissions from critical sectors such as energy, agriculture, and waste management. This brief explores the potential of agroforestry-based micro-hydro systems in providing climate-resilient irrigation solutions while promoting sustainable cooking practices.

### Introduction to Water Scarcity in the Lower Mekong Delta

The Lower Mekong delta is a densely populated region with limited access to clean water and energy. Climate change has exacerbated these challenges, leading to severe droughts and floods that impact agriculture, food security, and economic stability. The importance of resilient irrigation methods cannot be overstated, as they are critical for ensuring food security and economic stability.

### Micro-Hydro Systems for Irrigation Efficiency

Micro-hydro technology offers a promising solution for increasing irrigation efficiency while reducing reliance on diesel generators and promoting energy independence. These small-scale hydroelectric systems can be integrated with existing irrigation infrastructure to provide clean energy for pumping water, thereby reducing energy costs and emissions.

### Integration of Agroforestry and Micro-Hydro in Climate-Resilient Water Supply

Pilot projects in the region have successfully combined agroforestry and micro-hydro systems to create climate-resilient water supply solutions.
