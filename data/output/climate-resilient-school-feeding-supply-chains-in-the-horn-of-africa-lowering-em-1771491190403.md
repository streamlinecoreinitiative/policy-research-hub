**Climate Resilient Water Supply and Energy Access in Sub-Saharan Africa: A Comprehensive Approach**

## Executive Summary

This policy brief highlights the critical need to address water security, clean energy access, and climate adaptation in Sub-Saharan Africa. To improve resilience against future climatic shocks, we recommend expanding community-based water projects to ensure a significant increase in access to safely managed drinking water by 2028 [NEEDS SOURCE]. Additionally, we suggest increasing investment in solar, wind, and hydroelectric power projects to meet energy demands sustainably. This includes supporting small-scale renewable energy systems for rural communities where grid extension is impractical or costly.

## Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa faces severe challenges with water security, clean energy access, and climate adaptation. The region's limited access to safe drinking water and electricity not only hinders economic development but also threatens public health and social stability [Source: World Bank, 2021]. Ensuring resilient infrastructure that can withstand climate shocks is essential for sustainable development.

## Analysis

- **Water Security:** Only 32.4% of Sub-Saharan Africa's population has access to safely managed drinking water [Source: World Bank Open Data, 2024], highlighting a significant gap in meeting basic needs.
- **Energy Access:** The region lags behind the world average with only 53.3% having access to electricity [Source: World Bank Open Data, 2023]. This disparity is especially pronounced in rural areas where energy infrastructure often fails to reach due to cost and logistical challenges.
- **Renewable Energy Consumption:** Despite global progress towards renewables, Sub-Saharan Africa lags behind at just 19.7% of total final energy consumption [Source: World Bank Open Data, 2020], underscoring the need for more substantial investments in renewable energy sources.

## Policy Recommendations

1. **Expand Water Infrastructure:** Implement community-based water projects and improve existing infrastructure to ensure access to safe drinking water by 2028 [NEEDS SOURCE]. This includes constructing wells and improving sanitation facilities.
2. **Strengthen Renewable Energy Initiatives:** Increase investment in solar, wind, and hydroelectric power projects to meet energy demands sustainably. Support small-scale renewable energy systems for rural communities where grid extension is impractical or costly.
3. **Enhance Climate Resilience:** Develop early warning systems and climate-resilient water management plans that incorporate climate data into decision-making processes. Promote drought-resistant crops, efficient irrigation methods, and water harvesting techniques.

## Implementation Considerations

Implementing these recommendations will require significant financial resources. A detailed cost-benefit analysis is necessary to evaluate the economic viability of each intervention [Source: World Bank, 2022]. Collaboration with international partners and leveraging public-private partnerships can facilitate funding gaps [Source: OECD, 2020].

## Conclusion

Sub-Saharan Africa's water security and energy access issues require immediate attention and coordinated action.
