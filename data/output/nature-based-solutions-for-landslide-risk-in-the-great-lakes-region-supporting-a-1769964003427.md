# nature-based solutions for landslide risk in the Great Lakes region: supporting adaptation targets in NDCs

**Enhancing Water Security, Clean Energy, and Climate Adaptation in Sub-Saharan Africa: A Pathway to Resilience**

## Executive Summary

Sub-Saharan Africa faces significant challenges in water security, clean energy access, and climate resilience. To address these pressing issues, we recommend investing in infrastructure improvements such as water treatment plants, pipelines, and sanitation facilities to increase access to safe drinking water. This is critical given that only 32.4% of the population has access to safely managed drinking water [1]. Furthermore, we urge governments to prioritize the installation of solar-powered mini-grids for off-grid communities and support grid modernization projects to improve electricity distribution, as access to electricity stands at a mere 53.3% [2]. Lastly, early warning systems for climate-related disasters and climate-resilient infrastructure integration into urban planning are essential for enhancing climate adaptation.

## Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa's environmental resilience is severely compromised due to high population growth rates, climate change impacts, and limited infrastructure. The region is facing significant challenges in water security, clean energy access, and climate resilience. For instance:

* Access to safely managed drinking water stands at 32.4% [1], far below the global average of 73.7% [3].
* Only 53.3% of the population has access to electricity [2], with significant disparities between urban and rural areas.
* Renewable energy consumption remains low, accounting for just 19.7% of total final energy consumption in 2020 [4].

## Analysis

The evidence is clear: Sub-Saharan Africa's water security, clean energy access, and climate resilience are inadequate. The region's population growth rate is projected to continue increasing, exacerbating existing challenges. Climate change impacts, such as droughts and floods, will only worsen without urgent action.

* Water scarcity affects 2.2 billion people worldwide [5], with the majority residing in Sub-Saharan Africa.
* SDG 6 progress indicates that safely managed water coverage stands at 74% globally [6].

## Policy Recommendations

1. **Water Security**: Invest $[VERIFY] in infrastructure improvements such as water treatment plants, pipelines, and sanitation facilities to increase access to safe drinking water.
2. **Clean Energy Access**: Prioritize the installation of solar-powered mini-grids for off-grid communities and support grid modernization projects to improve electricity distribution.
3. **Climate Adaptation**: Implement early warning systems for climate-related disasters and integrate climate-resilient infrastructure into urban planning.

## Implementation Considerations

Implementation will require immediate action, including:

* Developing detailed implementation plans with specific timelines and cost estimates.
* Addressing potential barriers such as limited financial resources and political resistance to change through targeted financing mechanisms and community engagement strategies.

## Conclusion

Urgent policy interventions are needed to enhance water security, clean energy access, and climate resilience in Sub-Saharan Africa. We urge governments to prioritize these initiatives within their budgets and allocate sufficient resources for effective implementation. NGOs can play a critical role by partnering with governments to ensure that projects reach the most vulnerable populations.
