**Agroforestry-Based Micro-Hydro Systems for Climate-Resilient Irrigation in the Andean Highlands**

**Executive Summary**

The Andean Highlands face severe water scarcity due to climate change impacts, affecting agriculture and food security. This project proposal presents an innovative solution: integrating agroforestry and micro-hydro systems to provide climate-resilient irrigation. Agroforestry enhances soil health, biodiversity, and carbon sequestration, while micro-hydro technology reduces reliance on diesel generators and increases energy efficiency.

**Addressing Water Scarcity in the Andean Highlands**

Climate change has significantly impacted agriculture in the Andean Highlands, leading to erratic rainfall patterns, droughts, and increased evaporation. Resilient irrigation methods are crucial for food security and economic stability. Existing irrigation systems often suffer from inefficiencies, including water loss through evaporation and seepage.

### Micro-Hydro Systems for Irrigation Efficiency

Micro-hydro technology harnesses the energy of small streams or rivers to generate electricity. Benefits include:

* Reduced reliance on diesel generators, decreasing greenhouse gas emissions
* Increased energy efficiency through hydroelectric power generation
* Potential for off-grid communities to access reliable and clean energy
