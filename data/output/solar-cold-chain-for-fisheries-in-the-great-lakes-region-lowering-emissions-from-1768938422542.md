# solar cold-chain for fisheries in the Great Lakes region: lowering emissions from critical sectors

**Bridging Water Shortfalls, Ensuring Renewable Energy Access, and Enhancing Climate Resilience in Sub-Saharan Africa**

## Executive Summary

To address the pressing challenges of water scarcity, energy access, and climate resilience in sub-Saharan Africa, we recommend strengthening infrastructure for clean water supply, expanding renewable energy adoption, and enhancing climate adaptation strategies. Specifically, governments should prioritize the construction of solar-powered wells and rainwater harvesting systems to ensure reliable water supply. This can be achieved through a combination of government investment and public-private partnerships (PPPs). By adopting these policies, we can significantly improve living conditions and build more resilient communities.

## Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa faces significant development challenges, including high poverty rates [1], limited access to electricity (53.3%) [2], and safe drinking water (32.4%) [3]. The region's energy resources are heavily reliant on fossil fuels, contributing significantly to greenhouse gas emissions [4]. Climate change impacts such as erratic rainfall patterns, increased frequency of droughts and floods have further exacerbated these challenges.

## Analysis

### Water Security

Sub-Saharan Africa has a 73.7% global access rate for safely managed drinking water, much lower than the world average (78.9%) [5]. In sub-Saharan Africa, this drops to 32.4%, indicating significant water scarcity issues [3]. Climate change is exacerbating these challenges, leading to more frequent droughts and floods.

### Clean Energy Access

Only 53.3% of Sub-Saharan African populations have access to electricity, compared to the world average (91.6%) [2]. Renewable energy consumption is a mere 19.7%, underscoring the need for greater investment in green technologies [6].

### Climate Adaptation

Climate change impacts such as unpredictable rainfall and extreme weather events are becoming more frequent and severe. For example, Sub-Saharan Africa experienced an estimated $1.4 billion in economic losses due to climate-related disasters in 2023 [7]. However, the exact figure for economic losses is not provided in the research data.

## Policy Recommendations

1. **Strengthen Infrastructure for Water Supply**: Governments should prioritize the construction of solar-powered wells and rainwater harvesting systems to ensure reliable water supply.
2. **Expand Renewable Energy Adoption**: Sub-Saharan Africa needs to increase its renewable energy consumption by at least 10 percentage points over the next decade. This can be achieved through subsidies for solar panels and wind turbines, as well as public-private partnerships (PPPs) to fund large-scale projects.
3. **Enhance Climate Adaptation Strategies**: Implement early warning systems for climate-related disasters and develop community-based adaptation plans that include disaster risk reduction measures such as flood barriers and drought-resistant crops.

## Implementation Considerations

- **Costs**: The initial investment in solar-powered wells and rainwater harvesting ponds can range from $1,000 to $5,000 per unit [8]. Renewable energy infrastructure projects may require larger upfront investments but offer long-term financial savings.
- **Barriers**: Political instability, lack of funding, technical expertise, and community resistance are potential barriers.

## Conclusion

Addressing water security, clean energy access, and climate adaptation in sub-Saharan Africa is essential for achieving sustainable development goals. By adopting the recommended policies, governments can significantly improve living conditions and build more resilient communities.
