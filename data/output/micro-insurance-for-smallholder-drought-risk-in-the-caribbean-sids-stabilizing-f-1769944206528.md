### Executive Summary

Sub-Saharan Africa faces significant water security challenges due to limited access to safe drinking water ([VERIFY] 32.4%)[1] and inadequate sanitation facilities, compounded by frequent droughts and floods[2]. To address these issues, we recommend implementing community-led water management initiatives, promoting renewable energy integration, and strengthening climate adaptation strategies.

### Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa is one of the most vulnerable regions to climate change impacts such as droughts, floods, and erratic rainfall patterns. Ensuring sustainable access to clean drinking water, reliable electricity, and resilient infrastructure are critical for economic growth and improved health outcomes in the region[3].
