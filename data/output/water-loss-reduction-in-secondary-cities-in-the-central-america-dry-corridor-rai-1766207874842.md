### Executive Summary

The Central America dry corridor is highly vulnerable to climate change, facing severe water scarcity and increased poverty among smallholder farmers. This brief explores integrating agroforestry with micro-hydro systems as a solution to enhance both water security and renewable energy use in secondary cities within this region.

### Water Scarcity in Secondary Cities of the Central America Dry Corridor

The Central America dry corridor spans eight countries, including Guatemala, Honduras, El Salvador, Nicaragua, Costa Rica, Panama, Belize, and parts of Mexico. This region is characterized by a dry tropical climate with limited rainfall, making it prone to droughts and water scarcity.

### Agroforestry and Micro-Hydro Systems: A Sustainable Solution

Agroforestry integrates trees into farming practices, improving soil health, biodiversity, and carbon sequestration. When combined with micro-hydro systems – small-scale hydroelectric power generation – these approaches can enhance water security, reduce energy reliance on diesel generators, and increase energy efficiency.

#### Benefits:

* Reduced water loss through evaporation and seepage
* Improved irrigation efficiency using micro-hydro systems
* Increased energy efficiency and reduced reliance on fossil fuels
* Enhanced soil health and biodiversity through agroforestry practices

### Policy Recommendations and Future Research Directions

To support the adoption of these integrated systems at scale, we recommend:

1. **Policy incentives:** Governments should provide tax breaks or subsidies for communities adopting agroforestry-micro-hydro systems.
2. **Capacity building:** Training programs for farmers and community leaders on agroforestry and micro-hydro system installation and maintenance are essential.
3. **Financial mechanisms:** Establishing a resilience finance mechanism to support the upfront costs of implementing these integrated systems is crucial.

### Closing Outlook

The integration of agroforestry with micro-hydro systems offers a promising solution for enhancing water security, renewable energy use, and climate resilience in secondary cities within the Central America dry corridor. By addressing immediate water scarcity issues and contributing to broader sustainable development goals, this approach can help reduce poverty among smallholder farmers.

### Actions/Recommendations:

1. Conduct a thorough needs assessment of communities in the Central America dry corridor.
2. Develop and implement policy incentives, capacity-building programs, and financial mechanisms to support the adoption of these integrated systems.
3. Establish partnerships between governments, NGOs, and private sector organizations to leverage resources and expertise.
