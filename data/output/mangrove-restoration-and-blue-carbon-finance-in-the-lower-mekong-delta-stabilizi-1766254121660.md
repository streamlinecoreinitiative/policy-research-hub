# Mangrove Restoration and Blue Carbon Finance in the Lower Mekong Delta: Stabilizing Food Security During Droughts

## Executive Summary

The Lower Mekong delta is highly vulnerable to climate change, facing severe droughts that threaten food security and economic stability. This brief explores the potential of mangrove restoration and blue carbon finance as a solution to stabilize food security during droughts in the region.

## Introduction to Water Scarcity in the Lower Mekong Delta

The Lower Mekong delta is one of Southeast Asia's most critical agricultural regions, supporting millions of people. Climate change has exacerbated water scarcity, leading to severe droughts that threaten food security and economic stability. Rising temperatures and changing precipitation patterns have reduced river flows, affecting irrigation systems and agricultural productivity.

### Benefits of Mangrove Restoration

Mangrove restoration can provide several benefits for the environment and local communities:

*   **Carbon Sequestration:** Mangroves are among the most effective ecosystems in sequestering carbon dioxide.
*   **Water Filtration:** Mangroves help filter water, improving its quality and reducing sedimentation.
*   **Shoreline Protection:** Mangroves protect shorelines from erosion and storm damage.

## Micro-Hydro Systems for Irrigation Efficiency

Micro-hydro systems are small-scale hydroelectric systems that can provide efficient and renewable energy for irrigation:

*   **Reduced Energy Consumption:** Micro-hydro systems reduce reliance on diesel generators.
*   **Increased Energy Efficiency:** Micro-hydro systems increase energy efficiency, reducing water loss.
