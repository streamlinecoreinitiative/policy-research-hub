## Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa has limited access to safe drinking water (32.4%) compared to the world average of 73.7% [1]. Electricity coverage is also low at 53.3% in Sub-Saharan Africa, with only 91.6% globally [2]. This gap contributes significantly to economic disparities and public health challenges.

## Analysis

Access to safe drinking water remains a critical challenge, underscoring the need for sustainable solutions [3]. Renewable energy consumption is below global averages (19.7%), indicating substantial room for improvement in meeting energy needs sustainably [4]. Climate change impacts are severe, including increased frequency of droughts and floods, which exacerbate existing challenges.

## Policy Recommendations

### a) Increase Access to Safe Drinking Water

1. **Implement water purification technologies**: Invest in decentralized water treatment systems, such as solar-powered water purification units.
2. **Invest in rainwater harvesting systems**: Develop and implement rainwater harvesting systems for households and communities.
3. **Promote community-led initiatives**: Support community-led initiatives for water management and hygiene education.

### b) Enhance Renewable Energy Infrastructure

1. **Establish public-private partnerships (PPPs)**: Collaborate with private sector companies to finance renewable energy projects, particularly solar and wind farms.
2. **Provide incentives**: Offer tax breaks or grants for households and businesses adopting renewable energy sources.
3. **Develop grid integration solutions**: Invest in infrastructure to ensure reliable electricity supply across remote areas.

### c) Implement Climate Adaptation Strategies

1. **Build early-warning systems**: Develop and implement early warning systems in vulnerable regions to mitigate climate impacts.
2. **Encourage resilient crops**: Support the adoption of resilient crops and agricultural practices that can withstand changing weather patterns.
3. **Invest in disaster risk reduction initiatives**: Invest in infrastructure such as flood control structures, coastal defenses, and irrigation infrastructure improvements.
