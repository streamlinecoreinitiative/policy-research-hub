## Executive Summary

This policy brief highlights the urgent need to address drought risk and water security challenges in the Indus watershed, a critical region for smallholder agriculture and food security. With over 32.4% of the population lacking access to safely managed drinking water [Source: World Bank Open Data, 2024], we recommend implementing micro-insurance programs to protect smallholders from drought-related losses.

## Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa faces significant challenges related to water security, access to clean energy, and climate adaptation [Source: World Bank Open Data, 2023]. The region's limited resources exacerbate vulnerability during extreme weather events, leading to food insecurity, health issues, and economic instability. In the Indus watershed, droughts have severe impacts on agriculture, affecting a significant number of people [FAO, 2020].

## Analysis

Water scarcity is a pressing issue in Sub-Saharan Africa, with only 19.7% of renewable energy consumption coming from renewables globally [Source: World Bank Open Data, 2020]. The region's electrification rate stands at 53.3%, far below the global average [Source: World Bank Open Data, 2023]. Climate change impacts are severe, including droughts and floods that further strain already limited resources.

### Water Security

* Only 32.4% of the population in Sub-Saharan Africa has access to safely managed drinking water [Source: World Bank Open Data, 2024].
* Freshwater withdrawal accounts for 9.2% of global resources [Source: World Bank Open Data, 2021].

### Clean Energy

* Renewable energy consumption stands at 19.7% globally [Source: World Bank Open Data, 2020].
* Only 53.3% of the population in Sub-Saharan Africa has access to electricity [Source: World Bank Open Data, 2023].

## Policy Recommendations

1. **Implement micro-insurance programs** for smallholders to protect against drought-related losses.
	* Partner with local insurance companies and NGOs to develop tailored policies.
	* Target areas with high drought risk and limited access to financial services.
2. **Promote climate-resilient agricultural practices**, such as drip irrigation and crop diversification.
	* Provide training and support for smallholders to adopt these practices.
	* Encourage the use of renewable energy sources for irrigation, reducing reliance on fossil fuels.
3. **Enhance early warning systems** for droughts and floods using IoT sensors and satellite imagery.
	* Collaborate with international partners and local communities to ensure effective dissemination of climate information.
