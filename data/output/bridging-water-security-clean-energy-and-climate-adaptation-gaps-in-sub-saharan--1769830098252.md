## Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa faces severe water scarcity, limited electricity access, and underdeveloped clean energy sectors. The region's population is rapidly growing while its resources are limited, exacerbating existing challenges. According to the World Bank Open Data (2024), only 32.4% of the population has access to safely managed drinking water, and a mere 53.3% have reliable electricity (World Bank Open Data, 2023). The region lags behind other continents in accessing safe drinking water and electricity.

## Analysis

The inadequate water access in Sub-Saharan Africa leads to poor hygiene practices, increasing the risk of diseases like cholera. The limited electricity supply hinders industrial growth and negatively impacts education outcomes. Climate change further exacerbates these challenges, with rising temperatures and more frequent droughts affecting water resources (IPCC, 2022). To address these issues, it is essential to invest in water security infrastructure, expand solar power deployment, and enhance renewable energy consumption.
