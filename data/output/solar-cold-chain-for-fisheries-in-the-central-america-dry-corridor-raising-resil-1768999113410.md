## Executive Summary

To address the severe water security challenges and limited renewable energy consumption in Sub-Saharan Africa, we recommend prioritizing investments in community-based solutions such as rainwater harvesting systems and boreholes. This approach can increase access to safely managed drinking water from 32.4% (World Bank, 2024) to at least 50% within the next five years. Additionally, establishing government incentives for microgrids that provide reliable electricity in remote communities can promote renewable energy use, currently at a low of 9.2% (World Bank, 2021). By focusing on these critical areas, we can significantly improve living conditions and economic stability.

## Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa is grappling with severe water security challenges, where only 32.4% of the population has access to safely managed drinking water (World Bank, 2024). This disparity exacerbates health risks and hinders socio-economic development. Meanwhile, renewable energy consumption remains at a low of 9.2%, hindering climate adaptation efforts (World Bank, 2021).

## Policy Recommendations

To address these critical issues, we propose the following policy recommendations:

1. **Increase Access to Safe Drinking Water**: Implement water treatment plants and improve rural water infrastructure. Allocate resources towards community-based solutions such as rainwater harvesting systems and boreholes (World Bank, 2024). Targeted subsidies for low-income households will also significantly enhance coverage.
2. **Promote Renewable Energy Use**: Encourage the adoption of renewable energy technologies like solar panels and wind turbines in rural areas. Establish government incentives to support microgrids that can provide reliable electricity, particularly in remote communities (World Bank, 2021). Investing in energy storage solutions will also increase reliability.
3. **Enhance Climate Adaptation Measures**: Develop early warning systems for climate-related disasters like droughts and floods. Implement weather-resistant agricultural practices such as crop diversification and improved irrigation techniques (World Bank, 2023). Provide financial support to farmers through micro-insurance schemes to protect them from extreme weather events.
