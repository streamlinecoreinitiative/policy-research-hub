# early-warning systems and last-mile alerts in the Sudano-Sahelian belt: stabilizing food security during droughts

**Ensuring Water Security, Clean Energy Access, and Climate Resilience in Sub-Saharan Africa**

### Executive Summary

To address the pressing challenges of water security, clean energy access, and climate resilience in Sub-Saharan Africa, we recommend investing in early-warning systems and last-mile alerts to stabilize food security during droughts. This initiative should be a top priority for governments and international development practitioners. By doing so, we can reduce the risks associated with extreme weather events, improve access to safe drinking water and reliable electricity, and promote sustainable development.

### Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa faces significant challenges in accessing safe drinking water and reliable electricity. According to World Bank data (2024), only 32.4% of the region's population has access to safely managed drinking water [1], compared to a global average of 73.7% [2]. These statistics highlight critical gaps that require urgent attention from government officials and development practitioners.

The continent's water security challenges are deeply entrenched due to inadequate infrastructure investments and climate variability exacerbating existing issues like droughts, floods, and pollution. The World Bank estimates that 2.2 billion people lack access to safe drinking water (2022) [3], a stark contrast to global figures. Electricity access is similarly low at 53.3%, particularly in rural areas where over two-thirds of the population depends on traditional biomass for cooking and heating.
