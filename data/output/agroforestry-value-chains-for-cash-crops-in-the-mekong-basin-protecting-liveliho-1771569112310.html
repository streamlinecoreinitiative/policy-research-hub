Bridging Water Security Gaps with Renewable Energy in Sub-Saharan Africa: A Pathway to Resilient Development

Executive Summary

To address the severe water security and energy challenges facing Sub-Saharan Africa, we recommend investing in large-scale water harvesting and storage projects using renewable energy sources such as solar-powered pumps and desalination plants. This integrated approach will improve access to clean water for 32.4% of the population lacking safely managed drinking water (World Bank Open Data, 2024) while increasing renewable energy generation capacity.

Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa is grappling with significant water security challenges, with only 32.4% of its population having access to safely managed drinking water (World Bank Open Data, 2024). This disparity is exacerbated by climate change and unsustainable farming practices. The region's reliance on traditional sources of energy often leads to high costs and environmental degradation, underscoring the importance of renewable energy integration.
