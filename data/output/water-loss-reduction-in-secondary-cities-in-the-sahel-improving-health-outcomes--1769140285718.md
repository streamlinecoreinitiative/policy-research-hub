## Executive Summary

Sub-Saharan Africa faces critical challenges in water security, clean energy access, and climate adaptation. To address these issues, we recommend prioritizing infrastructure investments to improve water security and clean energy access, while enhancing climate adaptation measures through renewable energy projects.

* Renewable energy consumption is just 19.7% (World Bank, 2020).

Recommendations:

1. **Increase Access to Safe Drinking Water**: Invest in groundwater and surface water supply systems, promote community-led initiatives for rainwater harvesting and sanitation facilities, and allocate funds towards piped water schemes.
2. **Expand Renewable Energy Access**: Implement solar-powered mini-grids to power schools, clinics, and other public institutions, invest in large-scale renewable energy projects such as hydroelectric dams, wind farms, and solar parks.
3. **Enhance Climate Resilience**: Develop climate-resilient infrastructure, promote community-based adaptation initiatives that integrate traditional knowledge with modern solutions.

## Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa lacks adequate infrastructure for basic services such as drinking water and electricity. Limited access exacerbates health risks, education opportunities, and economic productivity. The region's population is growing rapidly (UN Population Division, 2023), putting further strain on existing resources. Climate change intensifies these challenges, making immediate action imperative.

## Policy Recommendations

1. Increase Access to Safe Drinking Water:
	* Invest in groundwater and surface water supply systems.
	* Promote community-led initiatives for rainwater harvesting and sanitation facilities.
	* Allocate funds towards piped water schemes, especially in rural areas where access is most limited (World Bank, 2024).
2. Expand Renewable Energy Access:
	* Implement solar-powered mini-grids to power schools, clinics, and other public institutions.
	* Invest in large-scale renewable energy projects such as hydroelectric dams, wind farms, and solar parks.
	* Encourage private sector investment through tax incentives and grants (World Bank, [VERIFY] 2023).
3. Enhance Climate Resilience:
	* Develop climate-resilient infrastructure, including flood-proof buildings, early warning systems for extreme weather events.
	* Promote community-based adaptation initiatives that integrate traditional knowledge with modern solutions.
	* Increase public awareness and education on climate change impacts and mitigation strategies (World Bank, 2024).

## Implementation Considerations

1. **Funding**: Allocate specific budget lines in national budgets to finance water infrastructure projects. Private sector partnerships can also be leveraged through PPPs or impact investment funds (World Bank, [VERIFY] 2023).
2. **Technical Assistance**: Provide technical support and training for community-led initiatives to ensure sustainable implementation.
3. **Monitoring & Evaluation**: Establish robust monitoring systems to track progress against set targets, including access rates to safe drinking water and renewable energy consumption.

## Conclusion

Addressing water security, clean energy access, and climate adaptation in Sub-Saharan Africa is imperative. By prioritizing these interventions, governments can create a more resilient future for their populations. Next steps include scaling up successful pilot projects, engaging the private sector, and ensuring sustained political commitment to achieve SDG 6 (UN SDG Report, 2023).
