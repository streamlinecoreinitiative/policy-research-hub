# Executive Summary

**Enhancing Water Security, Clean Energy, and Climate Adaptation in Sub-Saharan Africa: Bridging the Gap**

## Executive Summary

Sub-Saharan Africa faces significant challenges in accessing clean water and electricity, exacerbated by climate change. To bridge this gap, we recommend investing in renewable energy projects to increase access to electricity from 53.3% to at least 70%, improving water management systems to enhance efficient use of available resources, implementing early warning systems for climate events, enhancing climate resilience in water resources through rainwater harvesting and desalination plants, and supporting community-led initiatives for watershed co-management.

## Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa is one of the regions most vulnerable to climate change impacts. Water scarcity affects over 10% of its population [VERIFY] (World Bank, 2024), while only 32.4% have access to safely managed drinking water (World Bank, 2024). Renewable energy consumption stands at just 9.2% of total final energy consumption in the region (World Bank, 2021), far below global levels. These stark statistics highlight urgent needs for improved infrastructure and policy support.

Access to safe drinking water remains unacceptably low in Sub-Saharan Africa, with only 32.4% of the population benefiting from it (World Bank, 2024). Implementing these recommendations will require substantial funding and coordination among various stakeholders. The estimated total cost of implementing these measures is $3 billion over 5 years.
