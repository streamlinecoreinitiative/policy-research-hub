**Nature-Based Solutions for Landslide Risk in the Mekong Basin: Protecting Livelihoods under Climate Stress**

**Executive Summary**

The Mekong River Basin is highly vulnerable to climate change, experiencing severe water scarcity and increased landslide risk due to changing precipitation patterns. Smallholder farmers rely heavily on traditional irrigation methods, which are often energy-intensive and costly. This brief explores nature-based solutions for mitigating landslide risk and enhancing water security through the integration of agroforestry with micro-hydro systems.

**Water Scarcity and Climate Change in the Mekong Basin**

Climate change has significantly impacted the Mekong River Basin, leading to erratic rainfall patterns and increased evaporation from wetlands. This has resulted in severe water scarcity, affecting agriculture and food security for millions of people. Traditional irrigation methods are often inadequate, relying on diesel generators that exacerbate economic challenges.

**Current Irrigation Practices and Challenges**

Existing irrigation systems in the Mekong Basin include boreholes and canals, which suffer from significant water loss through evaporation and seepage. Economic analysis reveals high costs associated with these traditional methods, further straining smallholder farmers' resources.
