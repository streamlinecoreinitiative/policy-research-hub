# Solar Cold-Chain for Fisheries in the Mekong Basin: Enhancing Resilience and Reducing Disaster Losses

## Executive Summary

The Mekong basin is home to millions of people who rely on fisheries as their primary source of income and food. Climate change and extreme weather events pose significant threats to these communities, causing devastating losses to fish stocks and livelihoods. This brief proposes the implementation of solar cold-chain systems for fisheries in the Mekong basin as a cost-effective solution to mitigate disaster losses.

## Current Challenges and Opportunities

### Water Security in the Mekong Basin

* The Mekong River is a vital source of water, supporting agriculture, industry, and households.
* Climate change is altering precipitation patterns, leading to droughts and floods that impact fisheries and communities. [verify]
* Average annual rainfall in the region ranges from 800-1200 mm, with significant variability between years.

### Current Irrigation Practices

* Traditional irrigation methods are energy-intensive and costly, consuming a significant amount of energy per day.
* Canal systems experience losses due to evaporation and seepage.
