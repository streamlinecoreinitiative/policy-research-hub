# Microgrids for Rural Clinics in the Ganges-Brahmaputra Delta: Improving Health Outcomes in Heatwaves

## Executive Summary

Climate change poses significant challenges to rural communities worldwide, particularly those living in low-lying areas like the Ganges-Brahmaputra delta. Severe heatwaves exacerbate existing health issues, and access to reliable clean energy is often limited. This brief explores the potential of microgrids for rural clinics in this region.

## Introduction

The Ganges-Brahmaputra delta faces severe water scarcity due to climate change, including erratic rainfall patterns and increased evaporation from vast wetlands. This has significant implications for agriculture, drinking water supply, and sanitation. In rural areas, where healthcare infrastructure is often limited, heatwaves can be particularly deadly.

## Current Practices and Challenges

Rural clinics in the Ganges-Brahmaputra delta typically rely on diesel generators or grid electricity for power. However, these systems are often unreliable during heatwaves, leading to equipment failures and compromised patient care. Water scarcity affects not only drinking water but also sanitation facilities, further compromising healthcare services.
