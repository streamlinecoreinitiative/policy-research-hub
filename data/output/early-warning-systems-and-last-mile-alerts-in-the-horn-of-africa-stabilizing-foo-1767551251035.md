# early-warning systems and last-mile alerts in the Horn of Africa: stabilizing food security during droughts

**Agroforestry-Based Micro-Hydro Systems for Climate-Resilient Irrigation in the Andean Highlands**

**Executive Summary:**
The Andean Highlands face severe water scarcity due to climate change, affecting agriculture and food security. This project proposes integrating agroforestry and micro-hydro systems as a resilient irrigation method. Agroforestry enhances soil health, biodiversity, and carbon sequestration, while micro-hydro technology reduces reliance on diesel generators and increases energy efficiency.

**I. Introduction to Water Scarcity in the Andean Highlands**

Climate change is impacting agriculture worldwide, including the Andean Highlands, where erratic rainfall patterns cause severe water scarcity. Resilient irrigation methods are crucial for food security and economic stability. Existing irrigation systems suffer from high water loss through evaporation and seepage.

**II. Current Irrigation Practices and Challenges**

* Description of existing irrigation systems: Boreholes and canals are primary sources of irrigation in the Andean Highlands.
* Analysis of water loss: High water loss occurs due to evaporation and seepage, affecting water availability for agriculture.
* Case studies: [Case Study 1] and [Case Study 2] demonstrate inefficiencies of traditional irrigation systems.

Micro-hydro technology is a small-scale hydroelectric system that:

Pilot projects demonstrate the combined use of agroforestry and micro-hydro systems, showing cost-effectiveness and potential for scaling up.
