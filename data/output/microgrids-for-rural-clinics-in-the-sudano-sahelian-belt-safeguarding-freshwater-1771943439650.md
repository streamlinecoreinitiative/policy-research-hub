### Analysis

The interlinked issues of water security, clean energy access, and climate resilience require comprehensive policy responses. Water scarcity forces communities into precarious living conditions, often leading to overreliance on unsafe sources such as surface water or contaminated groundwater, contributing to disease outbreaks like cholera (World Health Organization: 2022). In Sub-Saharan Africa, the prevalence of waterborne diseases is alarming, with an estimated [NEEDS SOURCE] of deaths attributed to unsafe drinking water (Source: UN-Water/WHO).

Clean energy access remains a critical gap. Despite global trends showing increasing renewable energy consumption at [NEEDS SOURCE], Sub-Saharan Africa lags behind with only 53.3% having access to electricity (World Bank Open Data: 2023), leaving millions without reliable power sources.

Climate change-induced variability in rainfall patterns has led to recurring droughts and floods, further straining water resources and energy supply chains. For instance, the 2016-2017 Horn of Africa drought affected millions, disrupting food production and causing widespread humanitarian crises (Source: UNICEF).

### Policy Recommendations

To address these challenges, governments should implement targeted interventions that are both effective and feasible within their contexts.

**Water Security:**

1. **Invest in Water Infrastructure**: Prioritize investment in water infrastructure such as rainwater harvesting systems and borehole drilling to improve access to safe drinking water.
2. **Promote Sustainable Irrigation Practices**: Encourage the adoption of drip irrigation and other sustainable agricultural practices that conserve water resources.

**Clean Energy Access:**

1. **Expand Renewable Energy Deployment**: Increase deployment of renewable energy sources like solar, wind, and hydroelectric power through targeted subsidies or grants for small-scale projects.
2. **Community-Based Solar Projects**: Initiate community-based solar projects where local residents manage and operate their own mini-grids.

**Climate Adaptation:**

1. **Nature-Based Solutions**: Invest in nature-based solutions such as afforestation and wetland restoration to enhance climate resilience.
2. **Early Warning Systems**: Develop and implement early warning systems for droughts, floods, and other climate-related disasters.

### Implementation Considerations

Implementing these recommendations requires careful planning to minimize costs and maximize impact. For instance, community-led water projects often prove more sustainable than top-down approaches due to increased local ownership and maintenance (Source: UN-Water/WHO). Investing in renewable energy technologies that are already prevalent locally can reduce dependency on external funding.

### Conclusion

Lower-income countries must prioritize environmental resilience through targeted interventions for water security, clean energy access, and climate adaptation. By leveraging existing data and best practices, these recommendations offer actionable steps for decision-makers to accelerate progress towards SDGs 6, 7, and 13 within their unique contexts.
