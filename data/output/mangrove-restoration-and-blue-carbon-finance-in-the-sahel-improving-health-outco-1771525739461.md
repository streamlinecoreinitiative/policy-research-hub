## Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa faces a triple threat of water scarcity, energy poverty, and climate vulnerability. The region is home to 2.2 billion people lacking safe drinking water (UN-Water/WHO) [1], where only half of its populace has access to electricity (World Bank Open Data) [2]. Recent heatwaves and floods underscore how these challenges intersect, exacerbating livelihoods and economic stability.

## Analysis

Recent research highlights the critical need for enhanced water security and improved renewable energy access in Sub-Saharan Africa. Access to safely managed drinking water remains abysmally low at 32.4% (World Bank Open Data) [3], with an estimated 2.2 billion people lacking safe water globally (UN-Water/WHO) [1]. Additionally, only 53.3% of the population has access to electricity (World Bank Open Data) [4], a significant disparity compared to the world average of 91.6%. The region's reliance on fossil fuels for energy consumption at 9.2% underscores its vulnerability to climate change impacts.

## Policy Recommendations

1. **Expand Renewable Energy Access**: Invest in solar, wind, and hydroelectric projects to increase renewable energy consumption from 19.7% (World Bank Open Data) [5] to at least 40% by 2030.
2. **Enhance Water Management Systems**: Implement water harvesting and storage solutions to improve access to safe drinking water (targeting a [NEEDS SOURCE] improvement within five years).
3. **Strengthen Climate Adaptation Measures**: Develop early-warning systems, drought-resistant crops, and climate-smart irrigation practices to enhance resilience against extreme weather events.
