## Executive Summary

Sub-Saharan Africa faces severe water security issues, with only 32.4% of its population having access to safely managed drinking water (World Bank Open Data, 2024). The region's renewable energy consumption is a mere 19.7%, leaving millions without reliable electricity (World Bank, 2020). Climate change exacerbates these challenges by increasing the frequency and intensity of droughts and floods. This policy brief recommends implementing solar-powered wells for safe drinking water access and deploying early-warning systems to enhance resilience in critical sectors.

## Policy Recommendations

1. **Solar-Powered Wells**:
   - Install solar-powered water pumps for safe drinking water access, particularly in rural areas.
   - Ensure sustainable financing through partnerships with international donors and local governments.

2. **Early-Warning Systems**:
   - Develop early-warning systems for floods and droughts to provide timely information to communities at risk.
   - Collaborate with meteorological agencies to enhance the accuracy and reliability of these systems.

3. **Renewable Energy Integration**:
   - Expand renewable energy projects, focusing on solar power as a primary source of electricity generation in Sub-Saharan Africa.
   - Encourage community-based initiatives for decentralized renewable energy solutions such as small-scale hydroelectric plants and wind turbines.
