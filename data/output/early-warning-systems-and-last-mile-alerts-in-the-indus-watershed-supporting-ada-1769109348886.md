**Enhancing Water Security, Clean Energy Access, and Climate Resilience in Sub-Saharan Africa: A Comprehensive Policy Framework**

## Executive Summary

Sub-Saharan Africa faces significant challenges in water security, clean energy access, and climate resilience. With only 32.4% of its population having access to safely managed drinking water (World Bank Open Data, 2024) [1] and 53.3% having access to electricity (World Bank Open Data, 2023), the region is vulnerable to frequent droughts, floods, and other extreme weather events. To build a resilient future, governments must prioritize investments in water infrastructure, expand renewable energy sources, and implement climate-resilient agricultural practices.

## Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa's basic services like clean water and reliable power are severely lacking due to limited resources and political instability (World Bank Open Data, 2024) [1]. Climate change exacerbates these challenges, leading to more frequent droughts, floods, and other extreme weather events. Without effective interventions, these issues will further strain already fragile economies.

## Analysis

### Water Security

* **Data**: Only 32.4% of Sub-Saharan Africa's population has access to safely managed drinking water (World Bank Open Data, 2024) [1].
* **Impact**: Poor sanitation and unsafe water sources lead to high rates of disease transmission.
* **Approaches**:
	+ Investing in water infrastructure can improve access to clean water.
	+ Implementing climate-resilient agricultural practices can reduce the pressure on water resources.

### Clean Energy Access

* **Data**: 53.3% of Sub-Saharan Africa's population has access to electricity (World Bank Open Data, 2023) [2].
* **Impact**: Limited power availability hinders economic growth and exacerbates health issues.
* **Approaches**:
	+ Expanding renewable energy sources can improve energy access and reduce greenhouse gas emissions.

### Climate Adaptation

* **Data**: Sub-Saharan Africa is projected to lose more than half of its current freshwater resources by 2050 due to climate change (UN-Water/WHO, 2022) [3].
* **Impact**: Increased frequency and intensity of extreme weather events threaten food security and economic stability.
* **Approaches**:
	+ Implementing climate-resilient agricultural practices can reduce the vulnerability of agriculture to climate-related shocks.
