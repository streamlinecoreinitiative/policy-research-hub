### Executive Summary

To address the significant challenges facing lower-income countries in accessing clean water, reliable electricity, and climate-resilient infrastructure, governments should prioritize investments in water infrastructure, renewable energy, and climate adaptation measures. Specifically, we recommend expanding clean cooking solutions, promoting solar-powered irrigation systems, and implementing watershed co-management programs with indigenous councils to enhance resilience and improve living standards.

### Policy Recommendations

1. **Expand Clean Cooking Solutions**: Governments should invest in programs that promote the use of clean cooking stoves and fuels to reduce indoor air pollution and improve health outcomes.
2. **Promote Solar-Powered Irrigation Systems**: Implementing solar-powered irrigation systems can reduce energy costs for farmers while enhancing agricultural productivity (World Bank, 2024).
3. **Implement Watershed Co-Management Programs**: Engage with indigenous councils to develop watershed co-management programs that integrate local knowledge into resilience strategies.
4. **Invest in Water Infrastructure**: Prioritize investments in water infrastructure, such as solar-powered wells and rainwater harvesting ponds, to ensure equitable access to safe drinking water.

### Implementation Considerations

Implementing these recommendations requires a phased approach. Initial investments in infrastructure should be financed through grants and concessional loans to reduce upfront costs. Training programs for local communities will also be essential to ensure proper maintenance of new systems. To mitigate potential barriers like resistance from traditional power sources, policymakers can engage stakeholders early and provide incentives for adoption.

### Conclusion

Enhancing water security, clean energy access, and climate adaptation in lower-income countries is crucial for sustainable development. By prioritizing targeted investments in infrastructure and leveraging local knowledge, these nations can improve the lives of their citizens while contributing to global environmental goals. Stakeholders must act swiftly to close these critical gaps before they exacerbate existing disparities.
