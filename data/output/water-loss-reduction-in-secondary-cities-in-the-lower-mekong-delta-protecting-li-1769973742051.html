Water Security, Clean Energy, and Climate Adaptation in Sub-Saharan Africa: Strengthening Resilience Against Future Challenges

Executive Summary

Sub-Saharan Africa faces significant challenges in accessing clean water and electricity, exacerbating climate vulnerabilities. To build a more resilient future, this brief recommends implementing cost-effective solutions like solar-powered pumps and renewable energy microgrids, focusing on electrifying rural areas. Key findings highlight the urgent need for interventions: only 32.4% of the region's population has access to safely managed drinking water [1], and electricity access is at 53.3% [2]. Governments and international partners must prioritize these interventions to reduce risks associated with water scarcity and energy insecurity.

Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa struggles with limited access to safe drinking water (32.4%) [1] and electricity (53.3%) [2], exacerbated by climate variability leading to droughts and floods. With over 30% of the global population lacking safe water, urgent action is needed to meet SDG 6 targets. The region's reliance on fossil fuels poses significant challenges for sustainable development.
