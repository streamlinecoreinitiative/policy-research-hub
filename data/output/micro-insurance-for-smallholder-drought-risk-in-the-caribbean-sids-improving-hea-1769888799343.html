Ensuring Water Security, Clean Energy Access, and Climate Resilience in Sub-Saharan Africa

Executive Summary

This policy brief emphasizes the urgent need to address water security and clean energy access challenges in Sub-Saharan Africa. With only 32.4% of the population having access to safely managed drinking water [1] and 53.3% having access to electricity [2], governments, NGOs, and development practitioners must prioritize sustainable interventions that enhance resilience against future shocks. The primary recommendation is to invest in borehole drilling and pipeline projects to improve water supply in rural communities, accompanied by initiatives to promote solar, wind, and hydroelectric power generation.

Sub-Saharan Africa faces significant challenges in accessing safe drinking water and reliable electricity, with climate change exacerbating these issues.
