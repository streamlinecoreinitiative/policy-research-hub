# climate-smart irrigation cooperatives in the Caribbean SIDS: stabilizing food security during droughts

**Strengthening Water Security, Clean Energy, and Climate Adaptation in Sub-Saharan Africa**

## Executive Summary
To address the pressing challenges of water security, clean energy, and climate adaptation in Sub-Saharan Africa, we recommend that governments prioritize the establishment of climate-smart irrigation cooperatives. These cooperatives can help stabilize food security during droughts, improve access to clean water, and enhance renewable energy adoption. By implementing this policy, we can improve living standards for millions of people and contribute to achieving the Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs).

## Context & Challenge
Sub-Saharan Africa faces significant challenges in ensuring access to clean water, reliable electricity, and climate resilience. According to the World Bank Open Data [1], only 53.3% of the region's population has access to electricity, while globally, only 73.7% of people have access to safely managed drinking water (World Bank Open Data) [2]. These statistics underscore a critical need for sustainable development solutions that address both immediate crises and long-term climate resilience.

## Analysis
Climate change exacerbates these challenges by increasing temperatures and altering weather patterns, leading to more frequent droughts and floods. Recent studies indicate a global temperature rise of +1.1°C since pre-industrial levels (IPCC AR6) [3]. In Sub-Saharan Africa, this has resulted in significant impacts on agriculture, water resources, and human settlements.
