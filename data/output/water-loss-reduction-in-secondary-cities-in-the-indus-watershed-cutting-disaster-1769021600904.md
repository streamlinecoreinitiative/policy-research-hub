# water loss reduction in secondary cities in the Indus watershed: cutting disaster losses for vulnerable groups

**Bridging Water Security, Clean Energy Access, and Climate Adaptation in Sub-Saharan Africa**

## Executive Summary

To address the pressing challenges of water security, reliable electricity access, and insufficient clean energy infrastructure in Sub-Saharan Africa, we recommend **expanding public-private partnerships (PPPs) to build resilient water infrastructure projects**. This includes investing in solar-powered wells and rainwater harvesting ponds, which can provide safe drinking water for millions of people while reducing the region's vulnerability to droughts and floods.

## Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa is one of the regions most severely affected by climate change, with significant disparities in access to basic necessities like safe drinking water and reliable electricity. As of 2024, only **32.4% of the population has access to safely managed drinking water** [Source: World Bank Open Data], while just **53.3% have access to electricity** [Source: World Bank Open Data]. Renewable energy consumption stands at approximately **19.7%, far below global targets** [Source: World Bank Open Data]. These statistics underscore pressing needs for enhanced resilience and sustainable development in the region.

## Analysis

The challenge of climate change exacerbates existing vulnerabilities in Sub-Saharan Africa, leading to more frequent droughts, floods, and other extreme weather events. Climate-resilient water infrastructure is crucial for ensuring stable water supplies during periods of scarcity or excess rainfall. Similarly, reliable access to electricity supports economic development by powering industries and enabling telecommunication networks. However, current levels of renewable energy consumption are insufficient to meet future demand while also reducing carbon emissions.

## Policy Recommendations

To address these challenges:

1. **Expand Public-Private Partnerships (PPP)**: Encourage more PPPs focused on building resilient water infrastructure projects such as solar-powered wells and rainwater harvesting ponds.
2. **Invest in Climate-Resilient Infrastructure**: Prioritize investments in climate-resilient water supply systems, including early warning systems for droughts and floods.
3. **Promote Clean Cooking Solutions**: Support the adoption of clean cooking technologies like biogas stoves to reduce indoor air pollution and improve health outcomes.

## Implementation Considerations

Implementing these recommendations will require collaboration across multiple sectors. Public funding from governments and international organizations must be complemented by private sector investment in PPPs. Technical expertise is essential for designing, constructing, and maintaining resilient infrastructure. Additionally, capacity building programs should focus on training local communities to participate effectively in project planning and implementation.

## Conclusion

Enhancing water security, ensuring reliable electricity access, and promoting climate-resilient energy solutions are critical steps towards achieving sustainable development goals in Sub-Saharan Africa. By adopting evidence-based policies and engaging all relevant stakeholders, governments and development partners can significantly improve the resilience of communities to climate change impacts.
