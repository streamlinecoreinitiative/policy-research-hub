Executive Summary

The Great Lakes region is facing unprecedented climate-related challenges, including droughts and heatwaves, which exacerbate existing water scarcity and power outages. To address these interlinked issues, we recommend a comprehensive strategy combining water loss reduction initiatives, solar-powered renewable energy systems, and climate-smart agriculture practices. This integrated approach can significantly improve livelihoods in drylands while aligning with global sustainability goals.

Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa is home to vast arid regions where water scarcity and power outages are common (World Bank Open Data). Climate change intensifies droughts and heatwaves, making it even more challenging to maintain water security and energy supply.
