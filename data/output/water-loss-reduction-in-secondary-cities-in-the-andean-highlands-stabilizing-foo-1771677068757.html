Enhancing Environmental Resilience in Sub-Saharan Africa: Prioritizing Water Security, Clean Energy Access, and Climate Adaptation

Executive Summary

Sub-Saharan Africa faces a triple challenge of water scarcity, energy poverty, and climate vulnerability. To address these issues, we recommend prioritizing the implementation of resilient infrastructure like solar-powered wells, microgrids for clinics, and flood-resilient transport corridors. These interventions are cost-effective, scalable, and sustainable, and can be implemented immediately to improve the lives of millions.

Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa is one of the most water-stressed regions in the world, with only 32.4% of its population having access to safely managed drinking water (World Bank Open Data). Climate change exacerbates these issues, leading to increased frequency and intensity of droughts and floods (IPCC, 2020). The region also lags behind in clean energy access, with only 53.3% of the population having access to electricity, far below global averages (91.6%) (World Bank Open Data).
