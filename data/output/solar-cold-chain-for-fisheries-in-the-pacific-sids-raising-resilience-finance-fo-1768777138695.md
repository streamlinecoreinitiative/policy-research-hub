# Enhancing Resilience Finance for Pacific SIDS Fisheries through Solar-Powered Cold-Chains and Agroforestry-Based Micro-Hydro Systems

## Executive Summary

The Pacific Small Island Developing States (SIDS) face significant challenges in ensuring food security, particularly in fisheries and aquaculture. Climate change, overfishing, and limited access to clean energy and water resources exacerbate these issues. This brief proposes the implementation of solar-powered cold-chain systems for fisheries in the Pacific SIDS as a means to enhance resilience finance for communities.

## Introduction

The Pacific SIDS are vulnerable to climate-related disasters, including sea-level rise, increased storm intensity, and changes in ocean currents and temperatures. These impacts threaten the livelihoods of millions of people who depend on fishing and aquaculture for their income and food security.

## Addressing Water Scarcity and Climate Change

Agroforestry-based micro-hydro systems offer a promising solution by integrating sustainable agriculture with renewable energy. Key benefits include:

### Key Benefits of Agroforestry-Based Micro-Hydro Systems

• Enhanced soil fertility
• Improved water retention
• Drought-resistant crops
• Reduced operational costs

## Raising Resilience Finance for Communities

To support the adoption of solar-powered cold-chain systems and agroforestry-based micro-hydro systems in the Pacific SIDS, we recommend:

1. **Policy Support**: Governments should establish policies that encourage the development and use of renewable energy sources.
2. **Financial Incentives**: Provide grants or low-interest loans to communities implementing these systems.
3. **Capacity Building**: Offer training and technical assistance to local farmers and fishermen on system design, installation, and maintenance.

## Closing Outlook

The implementation of solar-powered cold-chain systems for fisheries in the Pacific SIDS has the potential to enhance resilience finance for communities by addressing water scarcity and climate change impacts.
