# Agroforestry-Based Micro-Hydro Systems for Climate-Resilient Irrigation in the Ethiopian Highlands

## Executive Summary

Climate change exacerbates water scarcity in the Ethiopian Highlands, impacting agriculture and livelihoods. This brief presents a solution to address this challenge through integrated agroforestry and micro-hydro systems. By combining these two approaches, farmers can access reliable and renewable energy for irrigation while improving soil health, biodiversity, and carbon sequestration.

## Introduction to Water Scarcity

Climate change impacts agriculture in the Ethiopian Highlands by causing erratic rainfall patterns. According to NOAA climate data, average annual rainfall ranges from 800 to 1200 mm [verify]. Traditional irrigation methods are energy-intensive and costly, consuming significant amounts of electricity per day [verify].
