# Agroforestry-Based Micro-Hydro Systems for Climate-Resilient Irrigation in the Ethiopian Highlands

## Executive Summary

The Ethiopian Highlands face severe water scarcity due to climate change, characterized by erratic rainfall patterns. Traditional irrigation methods exacerbate poverty among smallholder farmers by being energy-intensive and costly. This brief explores the potential of integrating agroforestry with micro-hydro systems as a sustainable solution for climate-resilient irrigation in the region.

## Introduction

### Water Scarcity in the Ethiopian Highlands

Climate change impacts agriculture in the Ethiopian Highlands, characterized by erratic rainfall patterns that create challenges for traditional irrigation methods. This leads to increased poverty among smallholder farmers, making resilient irrigation methods crucial for food security and economic stability in these communities.

### Importance of Resilient Irrigation Methods

Resilient irrigation methods are essential for addressing water scarcity in the Ethiopian Highlands. These methods must be able to adapt to changing climate conditions while minimizing energy consumption and costs.

## Current Irrigation Practices and Challenges

### Description of Existing Irrigation Systems

Traditional irrigation systems such as boreholes and canals are currently used in the region. However, these methods are energy-intensive, costly, and not sustainable under climate change conditions.

Micro-hydro technology refers to small-scale hydroelectric systems. These systems can provide a sustainable and renewable energy source for irrigation, reducing reliance on diesel generators.
