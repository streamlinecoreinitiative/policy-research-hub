# climate-smart irrigation cooperatives in the Great Lakes region: supporting adaptation targets in NDCs

**Enhancing Water Security, Clean Energy, and Climate Adaptation in Sub-Saharan Africa: A Pathway for Sustainable Development**

### Executive Summary

To address the pressing challenges of water scarcity, unreliable electricity access, and climate-induced vulnerabilities in Sub-Saharan Africa, we recommend implementing solar-powered desalination plants to provide safe drinking water in arid regions. This initiative should be complemented by expanding microgrids for off-grid areas and promoting renewable energy investments. By prioritizing these interventions, the region can mitigate immediate crises while laying the groundwork for sustainable development.

### Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa's infrastructure is underdeveloped, leading to inadequate water supply and unreliable electricity access (World Bank Open Data). The region struggles with climate-induced droughts, floods, and other extreme weather events. Addressing these issues is crucial for reducing poverty, improving health outcomes, and fostering economic growth.

### Policy Recommendations

1.  **Solar-Powered Desalination Plants**: Invest in desalination facilities powered by solar energy to provide safe drinking water in arid regions.
2.  **Microgrids for Off-Grid Areas**: Develop microgrid systems to enhance electricity access in remote, off-grid communities.
3.  **Renewable Energy Investments**: Increase investment in renewable energy sources like solar and wind power to reduce reliance on fossil fuels.

### Implementation Considerations

Initial investments for desalination plants and microgrids can be substantial but offset by long-term savings from reduced water scarcity and improved energy security. Political resistance, lack of funding, and technical expertise are potential hurdles. Collaboration between governments, NGOs, and private sector entities is essential.

### Conclusion

Sub-Saharan Africa must prioritize enhancing water security, clean energy, and climate adaptation to achieve sustainable development goals. By implementing these recommendations, the region can mitigate immediate crises while building a resilient future for its people.
