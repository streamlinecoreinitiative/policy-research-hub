# Clean Cooking Transition for Peri-Urban Households in the Indus Watershed: Protecting Livelihoods under Climate Stress

## Executive Summary

This brief highlights the importance of addressing water security, clean energy, and climate adaptation in lower-income countries, with a focus on peri-urban households in the Indus watershed. The proposed solution integrates agroforestry-based micro-hydro systems to provide sustainable irrigation solutions for agriculture, thereby protecting livelihoods under climate stress.

## Introduction

Climate change is exacerbating water scarcity in many regions worldwide, including the Indus watershed. Peri-urban households are particularly vulnerable to climate-related shocks due to their reliance on traditional energy sources and limited access to clean water. This brief outlines a comprehensive approach to address these challenges through integrated agroforestry-micro-hydro systems.

### Current Irrigation Practices

Traditional irrigation methods in the region rely heavily on borehole pumps, which consume significant amounts of energy (10-20 kWh/day) [verify] and result in substantial losses due to evaporation and seepage. Canal systems also experience significant water loss through these processes.
