**Enhancing Water Security, Clean Energy Access, and Climate Adaptation in Sub-Saharan Africa**

### Executive Summary

This policy brief highlights the pressing need to enhance water security, clean energy access, and climate adaptation measures in Sub-Saharan African countries. With only 32.4% of the population having access to safely managed drinking water (World Bank Open Data, 2024) and a mere 53.3% with electricity (World Bank Open Data, 2023), these countries are struggling to meet basic needs while also coping with climate change impacts such as droughts and extreme weather events. To address this challenge, we recommend increasing public investment in rural water supply infrastructure, expanding electrification efforts through off-grid renewable energy solutions, and promoting climate-resilient agricultural practices.

### Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa faces significant challenges related to access to safe drinking water, electricity, and clean energy. The region's limited resources exacerbate these challenges, particularly in rural areas where infrastructure is often inadequate (World Bank Open Data, 2024). Climate change further compounds the situation by increasing frequency of droughts and other extreme weather events (IPCC, 2020). Ensuring sustainable water security, clean energy access, and climate resilience is crucial for the socio-economic development of Sub-Saharan African countries.

### Analysis

- **Water Security**: Only 32.4% of the population in Sub-Saharan Africa has access to safely managed drinking water (World Bank Open Data, 2024), highlighting a significant gap in meeting basic needs.
- **Clean Energy Access**: With only 53.3% of the region's population having access to electricity (World Bank Open Data, 2023), there is substantial room for improvement.
- **Climate Adaptation**: Climate change impacts such as droughts and extreme weather events are increasing, exacerbating existing challenges.
