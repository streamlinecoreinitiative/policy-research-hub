# early-warning systems and last-mile alerts in the Ganges-Brahmaputra delta: supporting adaptation targets in NDCs

**Bridging Water Security, Clean Energy Access, and Climate Resilience in Sub-Saharan Africa**

### Executive Summary

Sub-Saharan Africa faces significant challenges in accessing safe drinking water (32.4% coverage) [1] and reliable electricity (53.3% availability), exacerbating the impacts of climate change. To address these issues, we recommend expanding public-private partnerships for clean water initiatives, investing in microgrids for rural electrification, and implementing early-warning systems for droughts.

### Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa suffers from a stark disparity in access to safe drinking water (32.4% coverage) compared to the global average of 73.7% [1]. Similarly, only 53.3% of the population has reliable electricity access, leaving millions without basic amenities essential for health and economic stability [2]. Climate change intensifies these challenges by increasing drought frequency and intensity, further impacting already vulnerable populations.

### Analysis

The region's reliance on non-renewable energy sources contributes to both water scarcity and climate vulnerability. For instance, freshwater withdrawal accounts for 9.2% of total resources globally, with Sub-Saharan Africa contributing significantly more than the global average [3]. This overuse strains local ecosystems and exacerbates drought conditions.

### Policy Recommendations

1. **Expand Public-Private Partnerships (PPP) for Clean Water Initiatives**: Governments should collaborate with NGOs and private sector entities to finance and implement projects like rainwater harvesting systems and solar-powered wells.
	* Data Point: 2.2 billion people globally lack safe water; Sub-Saharan Africa's coverage is even lower at [VERIFY] 32.4% [4].
2. **Invest in Microgrids for Rural Electrification**: Develop and deploy microgrid systems to provide reliable electricity, especially in remote areas where grid extension may be impractical or prohibitively expensive.
	* Data Point: Only 53.3% of Sub-Saharan Africa's population has access to electricity [5].
3. **Implement Early-Warning Systems for Droughts**: Establish early warning systems and last-mile alerts to inform farmers about impending drought conditions, enabling them to take precautionary measures such as crop diversification or water conservation practices.
	* Data Point: Climate change increases the frequency and intensity of droughts in Sub-Saharan Africa [6].

### Implementation Considerations

- **Cost**: Initial investments for PPP projects can range from $100K-$500K per well, while microgrid systems may cost between $1M-$3M per installation. These costs are significant but manageable with strategic financing.
- **Barriers**: Political will and institutional capacity must be bolstered to ensure successful implementation of these policies.

### Conclusion

By focusing on water security, clean energy access, and climate resilience in Sub-Saharan Africa, the region can create a more sustainable future for its people. This not only addresses immediate challenges but also lays the groundwork for long-term economic growth and social stability.
