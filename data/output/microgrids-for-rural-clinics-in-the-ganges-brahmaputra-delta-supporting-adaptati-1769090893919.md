### Executive Summary

Lower-income countries face critical challenges in water security, clean energy access, and climate adaptation. To address these issues, we recommend prioritizing equitable investments in clean energy, improved water infrastructure, and climate adaptation strategies to enhance resilience.

- Key findings: Sub-Saharan Africa's water security and energy access are woefully inadequate.
- Recommendations: Prioritize investments in decentralized renewable energy solutions such as solar mini-grids and micro-hydro projects. Implement community-based management schemes for water supply systems and establish public-private partnerships to finance these initiatives.

### Policy Recommendations

To address these challenges, specific and actionable steps are needed:

1. **Enhance Water Security**: Prioritize the construction of clean water supply systems in rural areas, focusing on piped connections, protected wells, or rainwater harvesting systems. Implement community-based management schemes to ensure sustainable use (World Bank Open Data).
2. **Promote Clean Energy Access**: Invest in decentralized renewable energy solutions such as solar mini-grids and micro-hydro projects. Encourage off-grid solar home systems for households that cannot afford grid electricity (World Bank Open Data). Establish public-private partnerships to finance these initiatives.
3. **Support Climate Adaptation**: Develop climate-resilient infrastructure, including flood-resistant buildings and early warning systems. Implement agroforestry value chains in vulnerable regions to enhance food security during droughts (UN-CC:Learn).

### Implementation Considerations

Implementing these recommendations will require careful planning and coordination:

- **Funding**: Mobilize public and private sector investments through grants, concessional loans, and innovative financing mechanisms. Establish dedicated funds for water infrastructure and renewable energy projects (World Bank Open Data).
- **Community Engagement**: Involve local communities in the design and management of water supply and energy systems to ensure sustainability and accountability.
- **Capacity Building**: Provide training programs for community members on maintenance and operation of new infrastructure, promoting long-term effectiveness.

### Conclusion

Addressing water security, clean energy access, and climate adaptation is essential for fostering sustainable development in lower-income countries. By prioritizing equitable investments and collaborative efforts, we can build a more resilient future that safeguards lives and livelihoods against the impacts of climate change.
