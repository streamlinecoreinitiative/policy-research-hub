# Agroforestry-Based Micro-Hydro Systems for Climate-Resilient Irrigation in the Mesoamerican Highlands

## Executive Summary

The Mesoamerican Highlands face severe water scarcity and poverty, exacerbated by climate change impacts on agriculture. This brief explores agroforestry-based micro-hydro systems as a solution to address these challenges through integrated approaches that ensure economic benefits and sustainability.

## Introduction to Water Scarcity in the Mesoamerican Highlands

Climate change has significant impacts on agriculture in the Mesoamerican Highlands, including reduced crop yields due to droughts and heatwaves. Resilient irrigation methods are crucial for food security and economic stability in these regions. Traditional irrigation systems often suffer from high water loss percentages.

## Micro-Hydro Systems for Irrigation Efficiency

Micro-hydro technology harnesses the energy of small-scale water flows to generate electricity. This approach reduces reliance on diesel generators and increases energy efficiency, making it an attractive solution for rural communities in the Mesoamerican Highlands.
