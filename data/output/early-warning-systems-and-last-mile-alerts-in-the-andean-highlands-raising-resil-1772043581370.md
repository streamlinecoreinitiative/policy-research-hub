**Early-Warning Systems and Last-Mile Alerts in the Andean Highlands: Raising Resilience Finance for Communities**

## Executive Summary

This policy brief highlights critical shortfalls in water security, clean energy access, and climate resilience in Sub-Saharan Africa. Despite progress in electricity access, significant gaps remain, particularly in rural areas. To address these challenges, we recommend:

* Increasing subsidies and incentives for renewable energy projects to reduce costs and increase adoption rates among rural communities.
* Implementing rainwater harvesting systems and promoting community-managed water resources like wells and boreholes in arid regions.
* Developing early-warning systems and disaster risk reduction initiatives to help communities prepare for climate-related shocks.

## Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa is home to about 1 billion people (approximately 40% of the global population) [World Bank Open Data]. While progress has been made in electricity access, gaps remain significant, particularly in rural areas where infrastructure and resources are scarce [World Bank Open Data]. Climate change intensifies droughts and floods, threatening livelihoods and exacerbating existing water scarcity issues.

## Analysis

### Water Security

Only 32.4% of the population in Sub-Saharan Africa has access to safely managed drinking water [World Bank Open Data], with a global average of 73.7% [Source: World Bank Open Data]. This gap is particularly acute in rural areas where infrastructure and resources are scarce.

### Clean Energy

Access to electricity remains uneven, with only 53.3% of the population having reliable access [World Bank Open Data]. Renewable energy consumption stands at just 19.7%, underscoring a need for increased investment in clean energy sources [Source: World Bank Open Data].

Estimated costs range from $100 million to $200 million per year, including subsidies for renewable energy projects and infrastructure development [World Bank Open Data]. ## Conclusion

Urgent investment is needed to bridge the gaps in water security, clean energy access, and climate resilience for Sub-Saharan Africa. Collaboration between governments, NGOs, and international partners is crucial for successful implementation.
