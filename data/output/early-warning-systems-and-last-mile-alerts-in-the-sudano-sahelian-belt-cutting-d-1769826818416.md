## Executive Summary

This policy brief highlights the critical need to enhance water security, clean energy access, and climate resilience in lower-income countries. Sub-Saharan Africa faces severe water scarcity challenges, with only 32.4% of the population having access to safely managed drinking water (World Bank Open Data). The region lags behind globally in electricity access, with only 53.3% of the population having access to electricity (World Bank Open Data). Renewable energy consumption is at just 19.7%, well below global targets (World Bank Open Data).

To address these challenges, we recommend prioritizing investments in renewable energy infrastructure to increase access and reduce carbon emissions. Governments should implement water management strategies such as rainwater harvesting, desalination plants, and improved sanitation systems. Strengthening climate adaptation policies and supporting community-based initiatives for resilience-building are also crucial.

## Context & Challenge

Lower-income countries are particularly vulnerable due to their limited resources and infrastructure. Water scarcity, energy poverty, and frequent climate-related disasters exacerbate existing challenges. The United Nations reports that 2.2 billion people lack access to safe water globally, with Sub-Saharan Africa being the most severely affected region (UN-Water/WHO). Renewable energy consumption is far below global targets, posing significant barriers to sustainable development in these countries.

## Analysis

Water scarcity and inadequate sanitation systems contribute to poor health outcomes, especially among children. The World Bank found that only 32.4% of the population in Sub-Saharan Africa has access to safely managed drinking water (World Bank Open Data). Energy poverty is a major driver of economic stagnation and social unrest. In Sub-Saharan Africa, 53.3% of the population lacks electricity, with women and girls spending hours each day collecting firewood for cooking.

## Policy Recommendations

To address these challenges, we recommend:

*   **Renewable Energy**: Encourage the deployment of solar panels, wind turbines, and small hydro plants in rural areas. Provide subsidies for households and businesses to offset initial costs.
*   **Water Security**: Invest in rainwater harvesting systems, desalination plants, and wastewater treatment facilities. Promote community-based water management initiatives like water committees.
*   **Climate Adaptation**: Implement early warning systems for extreme weather events. Develop climate-resilient agricultural practices such as drought-resistant crop varieties.

## Implementation Considerations

Establish a dedicated government agency focused on renewable energy and water security to streamline policy development and implementation. Collaborate with local communities to ensure that projects are tailored to their specific needs and sustainable over the long term. Allocate resources from international aid programs specifically for climate-resilient infrastructure projects.

## Conclusion

Governments, NGOs, and other stakeholders must prioritize investments in renewable energy, water security, and climate adaptation. By doing so, they can create a more resilient future for lower-income countries, improving the quality of life for millions and contributing to global sustainability goals.
