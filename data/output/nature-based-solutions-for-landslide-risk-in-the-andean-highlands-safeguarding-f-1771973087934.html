Bridging Water Security, Clean Energy Access, and Climate Resilience in Sub-Saharan Africa: A Comprehensive Approach

Executive Summary

To address the triple challenge of water scarcity, energy poverty, and climate vulnerability in Sub-Saharan Africa, we recommend prioritizing investments in infrastructure for safe drinking water (SDG 6) and promoting renewable energy access through microgrids for rural clinics. Climate adaptation strategies should focus on enhancing water storage capacity through large-scale reservoirs or small-scale community-based initiatives. This comprehensive approach will ensure that communities are better equipped to withstand future shocks.

Policy Recommendations

1. Prioritize investments in safe drinking water infrastructure: Allocate funds for improving sanitation facilities and investing in solar-powered wells to provide sustainable solutions to address water scarcity.

  1. Promote renewable energy access through microgrids: Support the development of microgrids for rural clinics to improve health outcomes, reduce reliance on fossil fuels, and mitigate climate impacts.
  2. Enhance water storage capacity: Implement large-scale reservoirs or small-scale community-based initiatives to ensure agricultural productivity under changing climatic conditions.
