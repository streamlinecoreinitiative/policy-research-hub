# solar cold-chain for fisheries in the Mekong basin: supporting adaptation targets in NDCs

**Revised Document**

**Strengthening Resilient Communities in Lower-Income Countries: Water Security, Clean Energy, and Climate Adaptation**

## Executive Summary

This policy brief emphasizes the urgent need to strengthen water security, clean energy access, and climate adaptation measures in lower-income countries. With 32.4% of Sub-Saharan Africa's population lacking access to safely managed drinking water [Source: World Bank Open Data], it is essential that governments and development partners invest in infrastructure such as wells, pipelines, and treatment plants. This brief recommends implementing solar-powered cold-chain systems for fisheries in the Mekong basin, which can significantly improve food safety and reduce energy costs.

## Context & Challenge

Lower-income countries face significant challenges related to water security, clean energy access, and climate adaptation. According to the World Bank Open Data, only 53.3% of Sub-Saharan African populations have access to electricity [Source: World Bank Open Data], highlighting significant disparities in energy access. Renewable energy consumption stands at just 19.7%, leaving much room for improvement [Source: World Bank Open Data]. Climate change poses a significant threat to these nations, with rising temperatures and changing precipitation patterns affecting agriculture, water resources, and human settlements.

## Analysis

The challenges faced by lower-income countries underscore the critical need for comprehensive interventions in water security, clean energy access, and climate adaptation strategies. Water scarcity impacts millions directly through health issues related to unsafe drinking water [Source: World Health Organization]. In Sub-Saharan Africa, only 32.4% of populations have access to safe drinking water, contributing to diseases such as cholera and typhoid [Source: World Bank Open Data].

## Policy Recommendations

To address these challenges effectively, we recommend the following policy interventions:

1. **Enhanced Water Security**: Invest in water infrastructure such as wells, pipelines, and treatment plants to improve access to safe drinking water.
2. **Promoting Clean Energy Access**: Prioritize renewable energy sources by supporting the installation of solar panels in rural areas and small-scale hydroelectric projects.
3. **Climate Adaptation Measures**: Implement early warning systems for floods and droughts using advanced weather forecasting technology.

## Implementation Considerations

Implementing these policies requires a coordinated effort involving multiple stakeholders, including governments, international organizations, NGOs, and local communities. Funding will be crucial; leveraging existing development funds like the Green Climate Fund (GCF) can support renewable energy projects. Community participation is vital for successful water security initiatives as they ensure sustainability through collective responsibility.

## Conclusion

Addressing water security, clean energy access, and climate adaptation in lower-income countries requires a multifaceted approach that combines technological solutions with community engagement. By adopting these recommendations, decision-makers can build resilient communities capable of navigating future challenges while fostering economic growth and social stability. We urge governments and development partners to prioritize investments in solar-powered cold-chain systems for fisheries in the Mekong basin.
