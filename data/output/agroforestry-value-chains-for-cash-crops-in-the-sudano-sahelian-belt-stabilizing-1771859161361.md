# agroforestry value chains for cash crops in the Sudano-Sahelian belt: stabilizing food security during droughts

**Enhancing Environmental Resilience in Sub-Saharan Africa: Water Security, Clean Energy, and Climate Adaptation**

### Executive Summary

This policy brief highlights the urgent need to address environmental resilience challenges in sub-Saharan Africa. With only 32.4% of the population having access to safely managed drinking water [Source: World Bank Open Data (2024)] and renewable energy consumption at a mere 9.2% [Source: World Bank Open Data (2021)], the region is facing significant gaps in water security and clean energy access. Climate adaptation efforts are also lagging behind global targets, with SDG 6 progress standing at only 74% global coverage [Source: UN SDG Report (2023)]. We recommend prioritizing investments in renewable energy infrastructure, expanding water supply systems, and implementing climate-resilient agricultural practices.

### Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa is one of the least developed regions globally, with a high dependency on natural resources. Climate change exacerbates existing challenges such as erratic rainfall patterns leading to frequent droughts and floods [Source: World Bank (2022)]. The region's economic importance and population growth make addressing water security, clean energy access, and climate adaptation crucial for sustainable development.

### Analysis

#### Water Security

Sub-Saharan Africa struggles with limited access to safe drinking water. According to World Bank data (2024), only 32.4% of the population has access to safely managed drinking water [Source: World Bank Open Data (2024)]. This is significantly lower than the global average of 73.7% [Source: World Bank Open Data (2024)].

#### Clean Energy

Despite global trends, renewable energy consumption remains low in sub-Saharan Africa at just 9.2% of total freshwater withdrawal [Source: World Bank Open Data (2021)]. This is far below the world average and poses significant challenges for sustainable development.

#### Climate Adaptation

Climate adaptation efforts are underdeveloped with SDG 6 progress standing at only 74% global coverage according to UN SDG Report (2023).

### Policy Recommendations

We recommend the following actionable steps:

* **Expand Water Supply Systems**: Invest in water harvesting, storage, and distribution infrastructure. Support small-scale irrigation projects that use rainwater and groundwater.
* **Promote Renewable Energy Adoption**: Increase funding for solar-powered wells and other renewable energy solutions like biogas plants to reduce dependence on fossil fuels.
* **Implement Climate Resilient Agriculture Practices**: Encourage agroforestry value chains, climate-smart irrigation cooperatives, and clean cooking transition initiatives. Provide training and resources to farmers to adopt these practices.

### Implementation Considerations

Initial investments in water supply systems can range from $100-$500 per household depending on the scale of infrastructure required [Source: World Bank (2020)]. Limited government funding, lack of private sector investment, and community resistance may hinder implementation. Collaboration with NGOs and international organizations will be essential to overcome these challenges.

### Conclusion

Sub-Saharan Africa's environmental resilience hinges on effective interventions in water security, clean energy, and climate adaptation. By prioritizing these areas, policymakers can pave the way for sustainable development that benefits both current and future generations. We urge governments, NGOs, and development practitioners to take action now to build a more resilient sub-Saharan Africa.
