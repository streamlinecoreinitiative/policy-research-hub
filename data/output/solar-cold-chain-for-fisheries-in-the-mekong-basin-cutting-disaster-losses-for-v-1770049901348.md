**Enhancing Environmental Resilience in Sub-Saharan Africa: Water Security, Clean Energy, and Climate Adaptation**

## Executive Summary

Sub-Saharan Africa faces significant challenges in water security and clean energy access, exacerbated by climate change. With 32.4% of the population lacking access to safely managed drinking water (World Bank Open Data) and 53.3% without electricity (World Bank Open Data), urgent investment is needed to address these issues. We recommend investing in renewable energy, expanding clean water infrastructure, and implementing climate adaptation measures.

## Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa is one of the regions most affected by climate change, with limited resources for sustainable development. The region's economic growth and public health are heavily reliant on water security and reliable energy sources. Climate change has increased drought frequency, threatening existing water supplies (World Bank data). Limited access to electricity hinders development and exacerbates poverty cycles.

## Analysis

Access to safely managed drinking water remains low in Sub-Saharan Africa, with only 32.4% of the population having access (World Bank Open Data). In contrast, global coverage is at 73.7% (World Bank Open Data). The region's freshwater withdrawal accounts for 9.2% of global resources (World Bank Open Data). Renewable energy consumption is also low in Sub-Saharan Africa, with only 19.7% of the world's renewable energy consumption coming from the region (World Bank Open Data).
