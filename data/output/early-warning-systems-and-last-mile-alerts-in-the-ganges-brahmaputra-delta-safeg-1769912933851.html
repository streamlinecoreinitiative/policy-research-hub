Climate Resilient Water Security and Renewable Energy in Sub-Saharan Africa: Strengthening Community Livelihoods

Executive Summary To address the pressing challenges of water security, clean energy access, and climate resilience in Sub-Saharan Africa, we recommend investing in infrastructure to improve piped water supply systems and wastewater treatment facilities. This includes extending existing infrastructure networks where feasible and constructing new ones in underserved areas. Governments should prioritize investments in these critical sectors to ensure that 73.7% of the global population has access to safely managed drinking water by 2028 [1].

Context & Challenge Sub-Saharan Africa is grappling with significant disparities in access to safe drinking water and electricity. Only 32.4% of the population has access to safely managed drinking water [2], compared to 73.7% globally [1]. Similarly, just 53.3% have access to electricity [3], lagging behind the global average of 91.6% [3]. These gaps not only impact immediate health outcomes but also stifle economic growth and hinder sustainable development.

For instance, only 38% of households have access to improved sanitation facilities [2], exacerbating health risks. The region also faces severe climate impacts such as droughts and floods, which further complicate efforts to secure water resources [4]. While Sub-Saharan Africa aims to achieve universal electricity access by 2030 under SDG 7, significant progress is needed given the current rate.
