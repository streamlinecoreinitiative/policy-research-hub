### Executive Summary

The Horn of Africa is a disaster-prone region, characterized by frequent droughts, floods, and locust infestations that disproportionately affect vulnerable populations. Early-warning systems and last-mile alerts can significantly reduce disaster losses by providing timely information to communities at risk.

### Environmental Resilience in Lower-Income Countries: A Focus on Water Security

#### Introduction to Water Scarcity in the Horn of Africa

Climate change exacerbates water scarcity in the region through erratic rainfall patterns and increased evaporation due to higher temperatures. The average annual rainfall varies between 200-500 mm, with precipitation variability ranging from 100-800 mm annually.

#### Current Irrigation Practices and Challenges

Traditional irrigation systems are energy-intensive and costly, contributing to poverty among smallholder farmers. Borehole pumps consume a significant amount of energy, while canal systems experience high water loss due to evaporation and seepage.
