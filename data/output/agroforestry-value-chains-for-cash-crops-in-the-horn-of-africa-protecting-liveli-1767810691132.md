# Agroforestry Value Chains for Cash Crops in the Horn of Africa: Enhancing Climate Resilience and Sustainable Water Management

## Executive Summary

The Horn of Africa is highly vulnerable to climate change, facing severe water scarcity and environmental degradation. This brief highlights the potential of agroforestry-based micro-hydro systems to address these challenges and ensure sustainable water management, economic development, and climate resilience for cash crop farmers in the region.

## Water Scarcity Challenges

The Horn of Africa is characterized by a fragile water balance, with frequent droughts and reduced crop yields affecting farmers' livelihoods. Current irrigation systems rely heavily on traditional methods (surface water, rain-fed) with limited efficiency and sustainability.

### Key Challenges:

* Limited access to reliable irrigation systems
* Economic instability due to reduced crop yields
* High dependency on traditional methods (surface water, rain-fed) with limited efficiency and sustainability

## Agroforestry's Role in Addressing Water Scarcity

Agroforestry practices can improve soil health, increase water retention, and enhance ecosystem services such as pollination, pest control, and carbon sequestration. Integrating agroforestry with micro-hydro systems can reduce the risk of crop failure due to climate variability.

### Benefits:

* Improved water retention through increased vegetation cover
* Enhanced ecosystem services (pollination, pest control, carbon sequestration)
* Increased agricultural productivity due to improved soil health and water management
