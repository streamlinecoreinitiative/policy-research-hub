# Climate-Resilient School Feeding Supply Chains in Caribbean Small Island Developing States: Supporting Adaptation Targets in Nationally Determined Contributions

## Executive Summary

Climate change poses significant challenges to food security and sustainable development in the Caribbean Small Island Developing States (SIDS). The school feeding sector is particularly vulnerable, with supply chains often relying on imported goods and energy-intensive transportation. This brief explores the potential of climate-resilient school feeding supply chains, integrating agroforestry-based micro-hydro systems for irrigation efficiency.

## Climate Change Impacts on Agriculture in Caribbean SIDS

Climate change is exacerbating water scarcity, increasing temperatures, and altering precipitation patterns in the Caribbean. These changes have significant implications for agriculture, with crop yields and food security threatened by droughts, floods, and heat stress. In many Caribbean SIDS, school feeding programs rely heavily on imported goods, which can be expensive and unreliable.

## Agroforestry-Based Micro-Hydro Systems for Irrigation Efficiency

Agroforestry practices promote soil health, biodiversity, and carbon sequestration while reducing erosion and water loss. Integrating micro-hydro systems with agroforestry can provide efficient irrigation solutions, reducing reliance on diesel generators and increasing energy efficiency.

### Key Benefits of Agroforestry-Micro-Hydro Systems:

• Reduced water loss from evaporation and seepage
• Increased crop yields and improved food security
• Enhanced energy efficiency through renewable energy sources
• Improved soil health and biodiversity
• Carbon sequestration and reduced greenhouse gas emissions

## Policy Recommendations and Future Research Directions

To support the adoption of climate-resilient school feeding supply chains, we recommend:

1.  **Integrate agroforestry-based micro-hydro systems into national policies and strategies** for sustainable agriculture and renewable energy.
2.  **Develop training programs and capacity-building initiatives** to enhance the skills of farmers, agricultural extension workers, and school administrators in implementing climate-resilient supply chains.
3.  **Establish partnerships between government agencies, NGOs, and private sector organizations** to facilitate access to finance, technology, and expertise for scaling up agroforestry-micro-hydro projects.

## Actions/Recommendations:

1.  Conduct a comprehensive assessment of existing irrigation systems in Caribbean SIDS to identify areas for improvement.
2.  Develop a cost-benefit analysis of agroforestry-micro-hydro systems for school feeding supply chains.
3.  Establish a regional network for sharing knowledge, expertise, and best practices on climate-resilient school feeding supply chains.

## Closing Outlook

Climate-resilient school feeding supply chains can play a critical role in supporting adaptation targets in Nationally Determined Contributions under the Paris Agreement. By integrating agroforestry-based micro-hydro systems into national policies and strategies, Caribbean SIDS can enhance food security, reduce poverty, and contribute to sustainable development goals.
