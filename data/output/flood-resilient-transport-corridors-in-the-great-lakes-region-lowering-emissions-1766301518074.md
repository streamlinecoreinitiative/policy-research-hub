# Flood-Resilient Transport Corridors in the Great Lakes Region: Lowering Emissions from Critical Sectors

## Executive Summary

Climate change is exacerbating water scarcity, energy insecurity, and climate-related disasters worldwide. The Great Lakes region, a critical transportation hub, faces unique challenges due to its geography and climate. This brief explores flood-resilient transport corridors as a solution to mitigate emissions from critical sectors in the region.

### Introduction to Water Scarcity and Climate Change

Climate change is altering precipitation patterns, leading to increased frequency and severity of floods and droughts. The Great Lakes region experiences erratic rainfall patterns, affecting agriculture, water supply, and transportation infrastructure.

*   **Water scarcity:** The region's water resources are under pressure due to increasing demands from urbanization, agriculture, and industry.
*   **Climate-related disasters:** Floods and droughts can damage transport infrastructure, disrupting trade and economic activity.

### Flood-Resilient Transport Corridors

Flood-resilient transport corridors are designed to withstand extreme weather events while reducing emissions from critical sectors. These corridors can be achieved through:

*   **Green infrastructure:** Integrating green roofs, rain gardens, and other green spaces into transportation infrastructure
*   **Smart infrastructure:** Implementing smart technologies, such as sensors and monitoring systems, to predict and respond to flooding
*   **Sustainable materials:** Using sustainable materials for construction and maintenance of transport infrastructure
