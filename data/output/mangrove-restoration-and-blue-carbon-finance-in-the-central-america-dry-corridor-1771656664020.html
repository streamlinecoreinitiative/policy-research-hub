Mangrove Restoration and Blue Carbon Finance in the Central America Dry Corridor: Raising Resilience Finance for Communities

Executive Summary

To enhance environmental resilience in the Central America dry corridor, we recommend prioritizing mangrove restoration and blue carbon finance initiatives. These efforts can help communities adapt to climate change by reducing vulnerability to extreme weather events and promoting sustainable livelihoods. By investing in these projects, governments and international partners can leverage significant financing for community-led conservation efforts.

Context & Challenge

The Central America dry corridor is one of the most vulnerable regions to climate change, with frequent droughts and extreme weather events impacting agriculture, water resources, and human settlements. The region's mangrove forests, which cover approximately 10% of the global total [Source: World Bank Open Data (2022)], play a critical role in maintaining coastal ecosystems and supporting livelihoods for over 1 million people [Source: FAO (2020)]. However, these forests are under threat due to deforestation, pollution, and climate change.
