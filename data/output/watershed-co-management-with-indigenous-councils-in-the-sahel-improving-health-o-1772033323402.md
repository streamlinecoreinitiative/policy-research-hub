## Executive Summary

Sub-Saharan Africa faces significant challenges in accessing safe drinking water and reliable energy sources, with climate change exacerbating these issues. To address this crisis, we recommend investing in infrastructure projects that enhance water security, promoting clean energy solutions to reduce reliance on fossil fuels, and bolstering climate adaptation measures to protect communities from extreme weather events.

## Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa is home to over 1 billion people, with a significant portion living in remote areas far from reliable water sources or electricity grids. Climate change has intensified droughts, floods, and other extreme weather events, further straining existing resources.

Why it matters now: Addressing these challenges is crucial for achieving the United Nations Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs), particularly SDG 6 on clean water and sanitation [4].

### Water Security

* In Sub-Saharan Africa, only 32.4% of the population has access to safely managed drinking water [1], compared to 73.7% worldwide [2].
* Climate change is expected to reduce agricultural productivity in many parts of Sub-Saharan Africa, further straining existing water resources.

## Policy Recommendations

1. **Enhance Water Security**:
	* Invest in infrastructure projects like boreholes, water treatment plants, and rainwater harvesting systems.
	* Promote community-led sanitation and hygiene programs (CLHSP) to improve handwashing facilities and reduce waterborne diseases.
2. **Promote Clean Energy**:
	* Encourage the use of solar-powered energy-efficient cookstoves in households and schools.
	* Develop microgrids for off-grid areas, connecting communities to renewable energy sources like solar panels or wind turbines.
3. **Bolster Climate Resilience**:
	* Implement early warning systems for droughts and floods using advanced climate monitoring technology.
	* Support smallholder farmers with agricultural insurance schemes to mitigate the impacts of climate-induced crop failures.

## Implementation Considerations

* Initial investments in infrastructure projects can be substantial, but long-term savings on healthcare costs due to improved sanitation will offset these expenses.
* High initial capital costs and limited access to finance are significant barriers. Governments should consider public-private partnerships (PPP) to leverage private sector investment.

## Conclusion

Sub-Saharan Africa must prioritize water security, clean energy, and climate resilience to achieve sustainable development goals. By implementing the recommended policies, stakeholders can create a pathway toward healthier, more resilient communities in this region.
