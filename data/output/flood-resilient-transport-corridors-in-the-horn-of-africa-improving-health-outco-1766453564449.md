# Flood-Resilient Transport Corridors in the Horn of Africa: Improving Health Outcomes in Heatwaves

## Executive Summary

The Horn of Africa is highly vulnerable to climate change, experiencing severe droughts and floods that exacerbate water scarcity, food insecurity, and heat-related health issues. This brief explores the importance of flood-resilient transport corridors in improving health outcomes during heatwaves.

## Environmental Resilience in Lower-Income Countries: Water Security, Clean Energy, and Climate Adaptation

### Introduction to Water Scarcity in the Horn of Africa

The region is characterized by arid and semi-arid conditions, with rainfall patterns becoming increasingly erratic due to climate change. This has severe implications for agriculture, food security, and economic stability.

### Current Irrigation Practices and Challenges

Traditional irrigation systems often rely on boreholes and canals, which suffer from high water loss through evaporation and seepage. These inefficiencies exacerbate water scarcity, leading to reduced crop yields and decreased economic stability for farmers.
