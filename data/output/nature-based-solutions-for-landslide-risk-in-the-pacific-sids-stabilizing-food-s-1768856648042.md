# nature-based solutions for landslide risk in the Pacific SIDS: stabilizing food security during droughts

**Ensuring Water Security, Clean Energy, and Climate Adaptation in Sub-Saharan Africa: A Pathway to Sustainable Development**

### Executive Summary

This policy brief emphasizes the urgent need for targeted interventions to address water security, clean energy access, and climate adaptation challenges in Sub-Saharan Africa. By implementing climate-smart irrigation cooperatives, deploying microgrids in rural clinics, and fostering agroforestry value chains, we can enhance resilience and promote sustainable development in the region.

### Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa is one of the most vulnerable regions to climate change. Despite progress in electricity access (53.3% of Sub-Saharan Africans have access compared to 91.6% globally) [1], significant disparities persist [NEEDS SOURCE]. Water scarcity remains a critical issue, with only 32.4% of the population having access to safely managed drinking water [2], hindering economic growth, health outcomes, and social stability.

### Analysis

The data underscore the urgency of addressing these challenges. Sub-Saharan Africa's limited access to safe water hinders economic growth, health outcomes, and social stability. For instance, unsafe drinking water is a leading cause of disease and mortality among children under five years old [3]. Moreover, climate change exacerbates these issues by intensifying droughts and floods, further straining already scarce resources.

In contrast, renewable energy consumption remains low, with only 19.7% of the region's total final energy consumption coming from renewables [4], despite significant potential in solar and hydropower. However, cost barriers and grid infrastructure limitations hinder widespread adoption.

### Policy Recommendations

To enhance water security, clean energy access, and climate resilience, we recommend:

1. **Promote Climate-Smart Irrigation Cooperatives**: Establish cooperative irrigation systems that use solar-powered pumps for water distribution to improve crop yields while reducing environmental footprint [5]. Support these cooperatives through grants and subsidies to ensure affordability.
2. **Deploy Microgrids in Rural Clinics**: Ensure reliable electricity supply by deploying microgrids powered by solar panels or small wind turbines, enabling clinics to maintain essential services such as refrigeration for vaccines and medical equipment [6].
3. **Foster Agroforestry Value Chains**: Support agroforestry value chains that promote sustainable land use practices, enhancing both productivity and resilience against climate shocks [7].

### Implementation Considerations

Implementing these recommendations will require addressing several challenges:
- **Cost**: Initial investments may be prohibitive for local governments and communities. Grants, subsidies, and low-interest loans could help mitigate financial burdens.
- **Infrastructure**: Existing grid infrastructure might need to be upgraded or expanded to accommodate new energy sources like solar and wind. This can involve partnerships with private companies or international donors to facilitate financing.

### Conclusion

Sub-Saharan Africa's challenges related to water security, clean energy access, and climate adaptation demand immediate attention. By implementing evidence-based interventions such as climate-smart irrigation cooperatives, microgrids for rural clinics, and agroforestry value chains, we can enhance resilience and promote sustainable development in the region.
