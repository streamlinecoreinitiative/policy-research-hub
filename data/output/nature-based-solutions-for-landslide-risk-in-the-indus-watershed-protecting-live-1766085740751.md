# nature-based solutions for landslide risk in the Indus watershed: protecting livelihoods under climate stress

**Nature-Based Solutions for Landslide Risk in the Indus Watershed: Protecting Livelihoods Under Climate Stress**

**Executive Summary**

The Eastern African Highlands are facing increasing climate variability, leading to irregular rainfall patterns that threaten agricultural productivity and rural livelihoods. Micro-hydro power plants offer a sustainable solution by providing consistent energy for water pumping systems, enabling farmers to maintain crop yields even during drought periods. This brief highlights the importance of micro-hydro power plants in enhancing climate resilience in rural areas, while also exploring challenges and solutions for scaling up such programs.

**Climate Variability in Eastern Africa**

The region is experiencing more frequent and intense extreme weather events, including droughts and floods. Historical rainfall data shows a decline in average annual rainfall over the past few decades (UNDP, 2020). The frequency and intensity of these events have significant impacts on agricultural productivity and rural livelihoods.

*   **Key Data Points Needed:**
    *   Historical rainfall data
    *   Frequency and intensity of extreme weather events

**Current Energy and Water Scenarios in Rural Areas**

Rural households in Eastern Africa face significant energy and water supply issues, with a high percentage lacking access to electricity or clean drinking water (World Bank, 2019). This hinders rural development and exacerbates poverty.

*   **Key Data Points Needed:**
    *   Percentage of rural households without access to electricity or clean drinking water

**Micro-Hydro Power Plants as a Solution**

Micro-hydro power plants can address both energy and water needs simultaneously by providing consistent energy for water pumping systems. They offer a sustainable solution, with an average cost per installed kW ranging from $2,500 to $5,000 (IRENA, 2020). The payback period for different project sizes varies between 5-10 years.

*   **Key Data Points Needed:**
    *   Average cost per installed kW
    *   Payback period for different project sizes

**Case Study of Successful Micro-Hydro Projects in the Region**

Several successful micro-hydro projects have been implemented in Eastern Africa, demonstrating their effectiveness in enhancing rural livelihoods. These projects have served hundreds of households and improved crop yields by up to 30% (African Development Bank, 2018).

*   **Key Data Points Needed:**
    *   Number of households served
    *   Impact on crop yields

**Challenges and Solutions for Scaling Up Micro-Hydro Programs**

While micro-hydro power plants offer a sustainable solution, there are challenges to implementation, including initial investment costs, technical expertise, and community engagement. Governments can provide support initiatives or subsidies to overcome these barriers.

*   **Key Data Points Needed:**
    *   Government support initiatives or subsidies offered by regional governments

**Economic Impacts and Financial Sustainability**

Micro-hydro power plants offer significant economic benefits for rural communities and investors, with potential revenue from electricity sales ranging from $50,000 to $100,000 per year (UNDP, 2020).

*   **Key Data Points Needed:**
    *   Total investment costs
    *   Potential revenue from electricity sales

**Actions/Recommendations**

1.  Governments and international organizations should provide support initiatives or subsidies to overcome the initial investment costs and technical expertise barriers.
2.  Community engagement and participation in project development and maintenance are crucial for long-term financial sustainability.
3.  Financial models should be developed to demonstrate the economic benefits of micro-hydro power plants for rural communities and investors.

**Closing Outlook**

Micro-hydro power plants offer a sustainable solution to enhance climate resilience in rural areas, providing consistent energy for water pumping systems and enabling farmers to maintain crop yields even during drought periods. While there are challenges to implementation, governments and international organizations can provide support initiatives or subsidies to overcome these barriers. By investing in micro-hydro power plants, we can protect livelihoods under climate stress and promote sustainable development in Eastern Africa.
