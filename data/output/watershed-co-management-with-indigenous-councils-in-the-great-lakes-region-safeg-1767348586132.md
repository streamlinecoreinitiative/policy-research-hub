# Watershed Co-management with Indigenous Councils in the Great Lakes Region: Safeguarding Freshwater Ecosystems

## Executive Summary

This brief outlines the importance of watershed co-management with indigenous councils in the Great Lakes region, emphasizing the need for sustainable freshwater management practices. Climate change poses significant threats to the region's water security and ecosystems.

## Environmental Resilience in Lower-Income Countries: Key Challenges

### Water Security and Clean Energy

*   **Climate Change Impacts:** Erratic rainfall patterns and increased evaporation from wetlands impact water availability for crops.
*   **Traditional Irrigation Practices:** High energy costs, labor-intensive maintenance, and significant water losses through evaporation and seepage.

### Agroforestry's Role in Climate Resilience

*   **Soil Health Benefits:**
    *   Improved soil structure and water retention capacity due to vegetation cover
    *   Examples of successful agroforestry projects demonstrating improved water retention and reduced erosion
*   **Biodiversity Enhancement:**
    *   Increased biodiversity provides natural pest control and pollination services, reducing the need for chemical inputs
    *   Economic benefits from cost savings on labor, fertilizers, and other inputs due to enhanced soil health

### Micro-Hydro Systems for Irrigation Efficiency

*   **Overview of Micro-Hydro Technology:**
    *   Renewable energy source providing power for irrigation systems
    *   Cost savings from reduced reliance on diesel generators
*   **Energy Savings:** Examples of successful micro-hydro projects enhancing irrigation efficiency.
