Bridging Water Security, Clean Energy, and Climate Resilience in Sub-Saharan Africa

Executive Summary

Sub-Saharan Africa faces a triple challenge of water scarcity, energy poverty, and climate vulnerability. With only 32.4% of the region's population having access to safely managed drinking water (World Bank Open Data, 2024) [1] and less than half of the Sub-Saharan African population with electricity access (53.3%, World Bank Open Data, 2023) [2], addressing these interlinked crises is imperative for sustainable development. To bridge this gap, we recommend prioritizing resilient infrastructure projects that integrate renewable energy, promote rainwater harvesting systems for peri-urban households, and enhance early warning systems for climate-related disasters.

Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa is one of the least developed regions globally, lacking basic services like clean water and electricity. Climate change intensifies these challenges, threatening food security and economic stability. The region's population growth rate is among the highest in the world (World Bank Open Data, 2023) [2], exacerbating the strain on already limited resources.
