## Executive Summary

Sub-Saharan African countries are facing severe water insecurity ([VERIFY] 73.7% globally) and limited electricity access (53.3%). To address these pressing issues, we recommend investing in renewable energy sources to meet electricity needs while reducing carbon emissions. Specifically, we propose increasing funding for solar, wind, and hydro power projects by [NEEDS SOURCE] annually over the next five years to reach 30% of total energy production. Additionally, implementing water harvesting systems, improving sanitation facilities, and expanding access to safe drinking water through partnerships with NGOs and government initiatives will enhance water security.

## Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa is grappling with a critical intersection of environmental vulnerabilities. According to World Bank Open Data (2023), 73.7% of the global population lacks access to safely managed drinking water, with Sub-Saharan Africa accounting for a significant portion of this figure (World Bank Open Data). Furthermore, only 53.3% of the region's population has access to electricity, lagging behind other regions (World Bank Open Data). Climate change is exacerbating these challenges by altering weather patterns and increasing the frequency of extreme events.

## Analysis

The region's reliance on fossil fuels for energy generation contributes to air pollution and greenhouse gas emissions, contributing to global temperature rise ([VERIFY] +1.1°C since pre-industrial levels) (IPCC AR6). To achieve the Sustainable Development Goal 6 (SDG 6), which aims to ensure availability and sustainable management of water and sanitation for all by 2030 [UN SDG Report 2023], Sub-Saharan Africa must accelerate efforts in renewable energy integration and infrastructure development. According to UN-Water/WHO, 2.2 billion people globally lack access to safe drinking water (2022).

## Policy Recommendations

1. **Invest in Renewable Energy**: Increase funding for solar, wind, and hydro power projects by [NEEDS SOURCE] annually over the next five years to reach 30% of total energy production.
2. **Enhance Water Security**: Implement water harvesting systems, improve sanitation facilities, and expand access to safe drinking water through partnerships with NGOs and government initiatives.
3. **Climate Adaptation Strategies**: Develop early-warning systems for climate-induced disasters such as floods, droughts, and heatwaves. Promote climate-resilient agriculture practices and support vulnerable communities in disaster response efforts.

## Implementation Considerations

- Initial investments will be critical; however, leveraging existing funds from international development partners can significantly reduce costs.
- Community engagement is crucial to ensure the sustainability of these projects by involving local residents and leaders in planning and implementation processes.
- Monitoring progress through regular data collection and reporting mechanisms will help track effectiveness and allocate resources efficiently.

## Conclusion

Sub-Saharan Africa's environmental resilience hinges on addressing water security, clean energy access, and climate adaptation. By adopting evidence-based policies and innovative solutions, we can accelerate progress towards achieving these goals. Collaboration between governments, NGOs, and international organizations is essential to implement these recommendations effectively. Let us work together to build a more resilient future for Sub-Saharan Africa.
