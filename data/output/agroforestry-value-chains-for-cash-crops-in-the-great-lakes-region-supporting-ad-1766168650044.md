**Agroforestry Value Chains for Cash Crops in the Great Lakes Region: Supporting Adaptation Targets in NDCs**

**Executive Summary**

The Ethiopian Highlands are critical for agricultural production but face severe water scarcity due to climate change. Integrating agroforestry with micro-hydro systems can enhance water security and reduce poverty among smallholder farmers. This brief explores the benefits of combining agroforestry with micro-hydro technology in irrigation, highlighting its potential to support adaptation targets in Nationally Determined Contributions (NDCs). We outline current challenges, pilot projects demonstrating success, and policy recommendations for scaling up these integrated systems.

**Section 1: Introduction to Water Scarcity in the Ethiopian Highlands**

Climate change is severely impacting agriculture in Ethiopia. Rising temperatures and changing precipitation patterns have led to reduced crop yields and increased water scarcity (UN Environment Programme). The Ethiopian Highlands are particularly vulnerable due to their fragile ecosystem and reliance on rainfall-based irrigation systems.

Water security is essential for food security, and sustainable irrigation methods are crucial for ensuring agricultural productivity. However, existing irrigation systems in the region face significant challenges:

* High evaporation rates through open canals and water storage structures
* Seepage losses due to inadequate infrastructure and poor maintenance practices
