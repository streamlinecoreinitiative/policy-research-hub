### Policy Recommendations

To address these challenges, we recommend the following steps:

1. **Expand Water Infrastructure Projects**: Invest in sustainable water infrastructure projects to increase safe drinking water access. This includes developing new wells, repairing existing ones, and implementing rainwater harvesting systems [Source: World Bank Open Data (2024)]. For example, countries like Kenya have successfully implemented these initiatives, resulting in a substantial increase in access to clean water.
2. **Promote Solar-Powered Clean Energy Solutions**: Encourage the use of solar-powered solutions for electricity generation. This not only reduces reliance on fossil fuels but also provides sustainable power sources [Source: World Bank Open Data (2023)]. Countries like Rwanda have seen significant improvements in renewable energy consumption through such initiatives.
3. **Enhance Climate Adaptation Programs**: Develop and implement comprehensive climate adaptation programs that include early warning systems, drought-resistant crop varieties, and water loss reduction measures. This will help communities better withstand climate-induced shocks [Source: NOAA Climate Data].

### Implementation Considerations

Implementing these recommendations requires a collaborative effort involving government agencies, NGOs, and international development partners. Key considerations include:

* **Costs**: The initial investment in infrastructure projects can be substantial but should be weighed against the long-term benefits of improved health outcomes and economic growth.
* **Barriers**: Potential barriers may include limited resources and political will to invest in these areas. Engaging stakeholders early and demonstrating cost-benefit analyses can help overcome resistance.
