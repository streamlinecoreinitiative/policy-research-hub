## Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa faces significant challenges related to water security, clean energy access, and climate adaptation. With only 32.4% of the region's population having access to safely managed drinking water (World Bank Open Data), and just 53.3% with access to electricity (World Bank Open Data), the need for robust policy interventions is urgent. Moreover, Sub-Saharan Africa contributes less than 10% of global greenhouse gas emissions yet bears the brunt of climate impacts such as droughts and floods (IPCC). Addressing these issues not only mitigates immediate human suffering but also lays a foundation for sustainable development in the region.

## Analysis

The region's limited access to clean drinking water and electricity is a critical challenge that disproportionately affects vulnerable populations. The lack of safe drinking water leads to poor health outcomes, particularly among children (UN-Water/WHO). Similarly, inadequate electricity access hinders economic development, education, and healthcare services, exacerbating poverty and inequality (World Bank Open Data). Climate change further compounds these issues by disrupting water cycles and energy production, necessitating resilient infrastructure and policy reforms. ## Policy Recommendations

To address these challenges, we recommend the following policies:

1. **Enhance Water Security:** Implement water supply projects in rural areas to improve access to safe drinking water and sanitation facilities (UN SDG Report 2023). Provide subsidies for water treatment systems, particularly in low-income communities.
2. **Promote Clean Energy Access:** Invest in renewable energy infrastructure such as solar panels, wind turbines, and microgrids to increase access to electricity (World Bank Open Data). Offer concessional loans or grants for households and small businesses to install clean cooking stoves and improve lighting systems.
3. **Strengthen Climate Adaptation:** Develop early warning systems for droughts and floods, integrating climate science into disaster preparedness plans (NOAA climate dataset). Implement water management strategies like irrigation efficiency improvements and soil conservation practices.
