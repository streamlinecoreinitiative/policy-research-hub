**Urban Heat Mitigation through Cool Roofs in the Caribbean SIDS: Safeguarding Freshwater Ecosystems**

## Executive Summary

Millions of people in Sub-Saharan Africa lack access to basic services like electricity and safe drinking water, exacerbating existing vulnerabilities exacerbated by climate change. To address this, we recommend prioritizing equitable investments in clean energy and climate adaptation projects, with a focus on safeguarding freshwater ecosystems through urban heat mitigation strategies such as cool roofs. This brief outlines three policy recommendations for implementing these initiatives.

## Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa faces significant barriers to achieving universal access to safe drinking water and electricity due to resource constraints, political instability, and limited infrastructure (World Bank Open Data). Climate change impacts threaten food security and public health, making it imperative to address these issues now (IPCC AR6).

### Background on the Issue:

- **Access to safely managed drinking water**: 73.7% of the global population has access to safe drinking water, but this percentage is significantly lower in Sub-Saharan Africa [Source: World Bank Open Data].
- **Access to electricity**: Only 53.3% of the population in Sub-Saharan Africa has access to electricity, compared to a global average of 87.2% [Source: World Bank Open Data].

### Why it Matters Now:

Climate change impacts are severe and far-reaching, with a global temperature rise already at +1.1°C since pre-industrial times (IPCC AR6). This poses significant challenges for sustainable development in Sub-Saharan Africa.
