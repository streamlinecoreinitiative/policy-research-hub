# urban heat mitigation through cool roofs in the Indus watershed: protecting livelihoods under climate stress

**Urban Heat Mitigation through Cool Roofs in the Indus Watershed: Protecting Livelihoods under Climate Stress**

**Executive Summary**

The Ganges-Brahmaputra delta is one of the most densely populated regions on Earth, facing severe water scarcity and climate change impacts such as rising sea levels. Traditional irrigation methods are energy-intensive and costly, exacerbating economic challenges for already vulnerable communities.

**Water Scarcity in the Ganges-Brahmaputra Delta: Climate Change Impacts**

The Ganges-Brahmaputra delta is particularly vulnerable to climate change, with rising sea levels threatening agricultural productivity and food security. Rising temperatures alter precipitation patterns, leading to more frequent droughts and floods. Traditional irrigation methods are energy-intensive and costly.

• **Climate Change Impacts on Agriculture:**
	+ Rising sea levels threaten coastal agricultural lands
	+ Changes in precipitation patterns lead to more frequent droughts and floods
	+ Increased temperatures reduce crop yields and quality

• **Importance of Resilient Irrigation Methods:**
	+ Ensure water security for vulnerable communities
	+ Reduce energy consumption and costs associated with traditional irrigation methods
	+ Enhance agricultural productivity and food security

**Agroforestry-Based Micro-Hydro Systems: A Sustainable Solution**

Agroforestry, the practice of integrating trees into farming systems, offers numerous benefits for soil health, biodiversity, and carbon sequestration. When combined with micro-hydro technology (small-scale hydroelectric systems), agroforestry can provide a sustainable solution for irrigation efficiency.

• **Benefits of Agroforestry:**
	+ Improves soil health through increased organic matter and reduced erosion
	+ Enhances biodiversity by providing habitat for wildlife
	+ Sequesters carbon, mitigating climate change impacts

• **Micro-Hydro Technology:**
	+ Reduces reliance on diesel generators and increases energy efficiency
	+ Provides a renewable source of energy for irrigation systems
	+ Can be integrated with existing infrastructure to reduce costs
