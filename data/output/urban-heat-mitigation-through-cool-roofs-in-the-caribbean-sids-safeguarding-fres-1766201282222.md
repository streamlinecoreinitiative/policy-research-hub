**Urban Heat Mitigation through Cool Roofs in Caribbean Small Island Developing States: Safeguarding Freshwater Ecosystems**

**Executive Summary**

The Caribbean Small Island Developing States (SIDS) face significant environmental challenges, including water scarcity and climate change impacts on agriculture. This brief explores an innovative solution for urban heat mitigation and freshwater ecosystem protection: cool roofs. By integrating green roofs with solar panels and other renewable energy sources, SIDS can reduce the urban heat island effect, improve water security, and enhance resilience to climate-related disasters.

**Section 1: Introduction to Water Scarcity in Caribbean SIDS**

The Caribbean region is highly vulnerable to climate change due to its geographical location. Rising sea levels, increased storm frequency, and changing precipitation patterns threaten freshwater ecosystems and agricultural productivity. In the context of water scarcity, traditional irrigation methods can exacerbate poverty among smallholder farmers by being energy-intensive and costly.

* **Climate Change Impacts:**
	+ Erratic rainfall patterns
	+ Increased evaporation from highland wetlands
* **Importance of Resilient Irrigation Methods:**
	+ Food security and economic stability
