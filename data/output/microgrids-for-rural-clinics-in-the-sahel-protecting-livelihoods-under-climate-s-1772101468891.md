**Microgrids for Rural Clinics in the Sahel: Protecting Livelihoods Under Climate Stress**

## Executive Summary

Sub-Saharan Africa faces significant challenges in water security, clean energy access, and climate adaptation. To address these critical issues, we recommend investing in microgrid development for rural clinics in the Sahel region. This initiative will improve access to electricity, enhance water treatment capabilities, and support climate resilience efforts.

## Context & Challenge

The Sahel region in Sub-Saharan Africa struggles with limited access to safe drinking water and electricity. According to the World Bank (2024), only 32.4% of the region's population has access to safely managed drinking water [Source: World Bank Open Data], while a mere 53.3% have access to electricity [Source: World Bank Open Data]. Climate change exacerbates these challenges, with rising temperatures and climate variability increasing vulnerability to droughts and floods.
