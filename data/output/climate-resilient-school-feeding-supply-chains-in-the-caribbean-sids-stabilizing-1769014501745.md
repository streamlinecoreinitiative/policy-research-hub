# climate-resilient school feeding supply chains in the Caribbean SIDS: stabilizing food security during droughts

**Bridging Water Security, Clean Energy Access, and Climate Adaptation in Lower-Income Nations**

### Executive Summary

To address the pressing challenges of climate change, we recommend that governments and development practitioners prioritize investment in water security, clean energy access, and climate adaptation strategies in lower-income countries. By implementing these interventions, we can improve the resilience of millions of people to droughts, floods, and heatwaves. Our primary recommendation is to increase access to safe drinking water through partnerships with NGOs like UNICEF and private sector innovations.

### Context & Challenge

Lower-income countries face significant challenges in ensuring access to safe drinking water (32.4%) [1] and reliable electricity (53.3%) [2]. These gaps exacerbate the effects of climate change, including droughts, floods, and heatwaves. According to World Bank data, sub-Saharan Africa has only 32.4% access to safely managed drinking water [3], while 53.3% of the population in sub-Saharan Africa lacks access to electricity [4]. Renewable energy consumption represents only 19.7% of total final energy consumption worldwide [5].

### Analysis

* **Water Security**: Sub-Saharan Africa's access to safely managed drinking water is a mere 32.4%, highlighting significant disparities (World Bank Open Data) [3].
* **Energy Access**: Electricity coverage in the region remains low, with only 53.3% of the population having access (World Bank Open Data) [4].
* **Renewable Energy Consumption**: Despite global efforts towards renewable energy, only 19.7% of total final energy consumption is derived from renewables (World Bank Open Data) [5].

### Policy Recommendations

To address these challenges, we propose the following recommendations:

1. **Increase Access to Safe Water**: Implement water filtration systems and improve infrastructure in rural areas to ensure access to safe drinking water through partnerships with NGOs like UNICEF and private sector innovations.
2. **Promote Renewable Energy Adoption**: Encourage the installation of solar panels, wind turbines, and biogas systems in off-grid areas to increase renewable energy consumption through grants, subsidies, and public-private partnerships.
3. **Enhance Climate Adaptation Strategies**: Develop early warning systems for climate-related events like droughts and floods using satellite imagery and local weather data. Provide training programs for communities on how to use these tools effectively.

### Implementation Considerations

Implementing these recommendations will require significant upfront investment but can be offset by cost savings from reduced waterborne diseases and lower energy costs due to renewables. Key challenges include securing funding, overcoming political resistance, and ensuring equitable distribution of resources.

### Conclusion

By focusing on improving access to safe drinking water, promoting clean energy adoption, and enhancing climate adaptation strategies, we can significantly improve the resilience of lower-income countries against climate change impacts. Stakeholders must collaborate to make these interventions a reality for all affected communities.

**Call to Action**: Governments, NGOs, and development practitioners should prioritize investment in these areas as part of their national development plans. Early action is critical to mitigating future crises and improving living standards in vulnerable regions.
