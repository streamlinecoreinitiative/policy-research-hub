# urban heat mitigation through cool roofs in the Indus watershed: protecting livelihoods under climate stress

**Urban Heat Mitigation through Cool Roofs in the Indus Watershed: Protecting Livelihoods under Climate Stress**

## Executive Summary

This policy brief highlights the critical need to enhance environmental resilience in Sub-Saharan Africa, particularly in the context of water security, clean energy access, and climate adaptation. Given the region's meager 32.4% access to safely managed drinking water (World Bank Open Data) [1] and only 53.3% access to electricity (World Bank Open Data) [2], governments must prioritize interventions that address these critical gaps. We recommend implementing cost-effective solutions such as rainwater harvesting for safe water supply and expanding renewable energy projects to boost electrification.

## Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa is facing significant challenges in accessing basic services, with a mere 32.4% access to safely managed drinking water (World Bank Open Data) [1] and only 53.3% access to electricity (World Bank Open Data) [2]. This disparity is exacerbated by climate change, which affects water availability and increases energy demand for cooling and irrigation (UN-Water/WHO). The global population lacking safe water stands at a staggering 2.2 billion people (UN-Water/WHO) [3], with Sub-Saharan Africa being one of the most affected regions.

## Analysis

The World Bank data shows that while Sub-Saharan Africa's access to safely managed drinking water is a mere 32.4%, the global average is 73.7% (World Bank Open Data) [1]. Similarly, renewable energy consumption stands at just 9.2% of total final energy consumption in the region (World Bank Open Data) [4], which seems inconsistent with the global average of 19.7% (World Bank Open Data) [5]. Climate change-induced droughts and floods are disrupting water supply systems and increasing electricity demand for cooling.

## Policy Recommendations

### Water Security

1. **Invest in Rainwater Harvesting Projects**: Invest in rainwater harvesting projects to provide safe drinking water, especially in rural areas where infrastructure is lacking.
2. **Promote Small-Scale Irrigation Systems**: Promote small-scale irrigation systems using renewable energy sources like solar to reduce reliance on diesel generators.

### Clean Energy Access

1. **Expand Off-Grid Renewable Energy Solutions**: Expand access to electricity through off-grid renewable energy solutions such as solar home systems and mini-grids.
2. **Implement Feed-in Tariffs or Subsidies**: Implement feed-in tariffs or subsidies for private sector investments in clean energy projects, particularly in rural areas.

### Climate Adaptation

1. **Develop Early Warning Systems**: Develop early warning systems using low-cost IoT devices to predict weather patterns and alert communities about impending disasters.
2. **Invest in Climate-Resilient Infrastructure**: Invest in climate-resilient infrastructure like flood barriers and drought-resistant crops.

## Implementation Considerations

Implementing these interventions will require significant upfront investments, but they are likely to yield substantial returns through improved health outcomes, reduced energy costs, and enhanced agricultural productivity. Political resistance due to high initial costs or lack of technical expertise may hinder implementation.

## Conclusion

Governments must prioritize these interventions to ensure sustainable development in Sub-Saharan Africa, thereby improving the resilience of communities against environmental stresses. We urge governments to develop detailed project plans and engage stakeholders to secure funding for these initiatives.
