# flood-resilient transport corridors in the Indus watershed: protecting livelihoods under climate stress

**Enhancing Resilience in Sub-Saharan Africa: Strengthening Water Security, Clean Energy Access, and Climate Adaptation**

## Executive Summary

Sub-Saharan Africa faces significant challenges in accessing safe drinking water (32.4% of population) [NEEDS SOURCE] and renewable energy consumption (19.7% globally). These deficiencies exacerbate existing vulnerabilities, particularly under climate stress. To enhance resilience, we recommend:

1. **Increase Access to Safe Drinking Water**: Develop and implement water purification technologies and improve distribution networks.
2. **Enhance Renewable Energy Access**: Invest in solar, wind, and hydro projects to reduce reliance on fossil fuels.
3. **Strengthen Climate Adaptation Measures**: Implement early warning systems and improve water management practices.

## Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa is home to 80% of the global population lacking safe drinking water [NEEDS SOURCE]. Only 53.3% of people in this region have access to electricity (World Bank, 2023), highlighting a significant disparity. Climate change impacts such as unpredictable rainfall patterns and rising temperatures exacerbate these challenges.

## Analysis

Despite progress in renewable energy consumption (19.7% in 2020) [1], Sub-Saharan Africa still lags behind other regions globally. For instance, only 32.4% of people have access to safely managed drinking water (World Bank, 2024). Moreover, climate change impacts such as unpredictable rainfall patterns and rising temperatures exacerbate these challenges.
