### Executive Summary

The Mekong Basin is home to millions of people living in secondary cities, where water scarcity and climate change pose significant threats to livelihoods. This brief explores the integration of agroforestry-based micro-hydro systems for climate-resilient irrigation as a solution to address these challenges.

### Environmental Resilience in Lower-Income Countries: Water Security, Clean Energy, and Climate Adaptation

#### Introduction to Water Scarcity in the Mekong Basin

The Mekong Basin is one of the most vulnerable regions to climate change. Rising temperatures and changing precipitation patterns have resulted in severe water scarcity, affecting agriculture, industry, and households.

#### Micro-Hydro Systems for Irrigation Efficiency

Micro-hydro technology is a small-scale hydroelectric system that harnesses energy from moving water. By integrating micro-hydro with agroforestry, farmers can reduce their reliance on diesel generators and increase energy efficiency [verify]. Pilot projects have shown promising results in terms of cost-effectiveness and potential for scaling up.

#### Integration of Agroforestry and Micro-Hydro in Climate-Resilient Water Supply

Combining agroforestry with micro-hydro systems offers a holistic approach to addressing water scarcity and climate change. By promoting sustainable agriculture, reducing energy consumption, and increasing water retention, this integrated system can provide climate-resilient irrigation for secondary cities in the Mekong Basin.
