Bridging Water Security, Clean Energy Gaps in Sub-Saharan Africa: A Pathway Toward Sustainable Development

Executive Summary

Sub-Saharan Africa faces significant challenges in ensuring water security and access to clean energy. According to World Bank Open Data, only 32.4% of the population has access to safely managed drinking water, and a mere 53.3% accesses electricity. To bridge these gaps, we recommend implementing cost-effective renewable energy projects, enhancing water management systems, and integrating climate-resilient infrastructure.

Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa is home to 1 billion people living without access to safe drinking water (UN-Water/WHO), impacting health and economic stability. Climate change exacerbates existing challenges, necessitating immediate action to ensure sustainable development. The region's population is projected to reach 2.5 billion by 2050, further straining resources (World Bank Open Data).
