# Solar Cold-Chain for Fisheries in Pacific SIDS: Supporting Adaptation Targets in NDCs

## Executive Summary

The Pacific Small Island Developing States (SIDS) are among the most vulnerable to climate change, with fisheries being a critical sector for their economies and food security. Integrating solar-powered cold-chain technology can enhance resilience in this sector by reducing reliance on diesel generators, decreasing greenhouse gas emissions, and improving fish quality.

## Current Challenges and Opportunities

### Water Security and Climate Change Impacts

*   The Mesoamerican Highlands are a critical agricultural region facing severe water scarcity due to climate change.
*   Integrating agroforestry with micro-hydro systems can provide sustainable solutions, addressing poverty and promoting renewable energy adoption.
