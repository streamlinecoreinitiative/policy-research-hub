**Climate-Resilient School Feeding Supply Chains in the Indus Watershed: Lowering Emissions from Critical Sectors**

Executive Summary:

The Indus watershed is a critical region for agriculture and food security in lower-income countries, including Pakistan and India. Climate change poses significant challenges to water security, clean energy, and climate adaptation in this region. This brief explores the potential of integrating agroforestry with micro-hydro systems to enhance both water security and renewable energy use.

**Section 1: Introduction to Water Scarcity in the Indus Watershed**

The Indus watershed is a vital region for agriculture, supporting millions of people dependent on its water resources for irrigation. Climate change has led to erratic rainfall patterns and increased evaporation from highland wetlands, exacerbating water scarcity issues.

**Section 2: Current Irrigation Practices and Challenges**

Existing irrigation systems in the Indus watershed include:

* Boreholes: Energy-intensive and costly, with high maintenance requirements.
* Canals: Water loss through evaporation and seepage is significant, ranging from 20-40%.

These traditional methods not only exacerbate water scarcity but also perpetuate poverty among smallholder farmers. The integration of agroforestry with micro-hydro systems can provide a sustainable solution to these challenges.

Agroforestry practices have been shown to improve soil health, biodiversity, and carbon sequestration. Case studies demonstrate how agroforestry systems can:

* Improve water retention: By reducing runoff and increasing infiltration.
* Reduce erosion: By stabilizing soil through tree roots.

Micro-hydro technology offers several benefits, including:

* Reduced reliance on diesel generators: Lowering emissions and energy costs.
* Increased energy efficiency: Micro-hydro systems can achieve efficiencies of up to 90%.

Pilot projects have demonstrated the combined use of agroforestry and micro-hydro in climate-resilient water supply. Economic analysis shows that these integrated solutions are cost-effective.

**Section 4: Integration, Policy Recommendations, and Future Research Directions**

Policy recommendations for supporting the adoption of these systems at scale include:

* Incentivizing the use of renewable energy sources.
* Providing training and capacity-building programs for farmers.
* Encouraging public-private partnerships to finance and implement climate-resilient solutions.

Future research directions should focus on:

* Economic impact: Conducting detailed cost-benefit analyses comparing traditional irrigation methods with agroforestry-micro-hydro systems.
