## Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa's natural environment faces significant challenges from climate change, including extreme weather events and changing precipitation patterns. This has severely impacted access to safe drinking water and reliable electricity. Climate resilience is critical as the region grapples with increasing frequency of droughts and floods, leading to food insecurity and economic instability.

## Analysis

Evidence-Based Examination:

* Access to safely managed water: Only 32.4% of Sub-Saharan African populations have access to such services (World Bank 2024), compared to the world average of [VERIFY] 73.7%. This gap is partly due to limited infrastructure and funding.

1. **Climate Resilient Water Security**: Implement solar-powered water purification systems in rural communities and disaster-prone areas. Invest in rainwater harvesting infrastructure, especially in arid regions.
2. **Renewable Energy Deployment**: Promote solar power as a primary source for electrification of schools, clinics, and small businesses. Develop microgrids to ensure reliable energy supply during grid failures.
