# Agroforestry-Based Micro-Hydro Systems for Climate-Resilient Irrigation in the Mesoamerican Highlands

## Executive Summary

The Mesoamerican Highlands face severe water scarcity due to climate change, posing significant challenges to agriculture and food security. Integrating agroforestry with micro-hydro systems offers a promising solution to address these issues while promoting renewable energy adoption and reducing poverty.

## Water Scarcity in the Mesoamerican Highlands

The region is experiencing water scarcity due to rising temperatures and changing precipitation patterns. This trend is expected to continue, emphasizing the need for resilient irrigation methods. [Verify] rainfall declines over the past decade have impacted crop yields and food security.

## Agroforestry-Based Micro-Hydro Systems: A Solution to Water Scarcity

Agroforestry practices improve soil health, biodiversity, and carbon sequestration (1). Integrating micro-hydro systems with agroforestry can reduce reliance on diesel generators and increase energy efficiency. Pilot projects have demonstrated the effectiveness of this approach.

### Benefits of Agroforestry-Based Micro-Hydro Systems:

• Improved soil health and increased water retention
• Enhanced biodiversity and ecosystem services
• Reduced greenhouse gas emissions through carbon sequestration
• Increased energy efficiency

## Policy Recommendations and Future Research Directions

To support the adoption of agroforestry-based micro-hydro systems, policy recommendations include:

1.  **Financial incentives:** Governments can offer subsidies or low-interest loans to farmers implementing these integrated solutions.
2.  **Training and capacity building:** Provide training programs for farmers on agroforestry practices and micro-hydro system maintenance.
3.  **Regulatory frameworks:** Establish policies supporting the adoption of renewable energy sources.

Future research directions include:

1.  **Economic impact analysis:** Conduct detailed cost-benefit analyses to assess the financial viability of agroforestry-based micro-hydro systems.
2.  **Community engagement:** Investigate the social and cultural factors influencing community adoption of these integrated solutions.
3.  **Scaling up:** Identify strategies for scaling up the implementation of agroforestry-based micro-hydro systems.

## Actions/Recommendations
