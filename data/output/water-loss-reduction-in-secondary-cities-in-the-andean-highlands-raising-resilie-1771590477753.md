**Bridging Water Security Gaps with Climate Adaptation Strategies in Sub-Saharan Africa**

## Executive Summary

To address the critical water security challenges facing Sub-Saharan Africa, we recommend **expanding public health initiatives to improve sanitation facilities and hygiene practices**, particularly in rural areas where access is limited. This should be accompanied by a significant increase in funding for renewable energy projects to reduce dependence on fossil fuels and lower greenhouse gas emissions. Furthermore, developing early warning systems for droughts and floods will provide timely information to communities and help mitigate risks.

## Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa struggles with inadequate access to safe drinking water (32.4% of the population) compared to the world average of 73.7% [1]. This disparity underscores a critical vulnerability that extends beyond basic hygiene; it affects health outcomes, economic productivity, and overall quality of life. Climate change further compounds these issues through its impact on rainfall patterns and agricultural output, leading to more frequent droughts and unpredictable water availability.

## Analysis

The region’s limited access to clean energy sources is another significant challenge, with only 53.3% of the population having access to electricity [3]. This disparity exacerbates existing vulnerabilities and hinders economic development. Renewable energy consumption stands at 19.7%, indicating a need for greater investment in sustainable energy solutions.

- **Renewable energy consumption (%) - World (2020): 19.7** [4]

Climate change impacts also manifest through climate variability, including increased frequency and intensity of droughts and floods. These events disrupt water supply systems, affecting agricultural yields and household water security. Sub-Saharan Africa is particularly vulnerable to these changes due to its geography and socio-economic conditions.

## Policy Recommendations

To address the identified challenges, we propose the following evidence-based policy recommendations:

1.  **Expand Public Health Initiatives**: Increase funding for public health projects that focus on improving sanitation facilities and promoting hygiene practices, particularly in rural areas where access is limited.
2.  **Increase Renewable Energy Funding**: Allocate more resources towards renewable energy projects to reduce dependence on fossil fuels and lower greenhouse gas emissions.
3.  **Develop Early Warning Systems**: Establish comprehensive early warning systems for droughts and floods to provide timely information to communities and help mitigate risks.
