# nature-based solutions for landslide risk in the Great Lakes region: supporting adaptation targets in NDCs

**Climate Resilient Water Security, Clean Energy, and Adaptation in Sub-Saharan Africa**

### Executive Summary

This policy brief highlights the pressing need for climate-resilient water security, clean energy, and adaptation in Sub-Saharan Africa. With less than 32% of the population having access to safely managed drinking water and only about 53% with reliable electricity, there is a critical need for robust interventions. We recommend implementing microgrids for rural clinics, investing in decentralized renewable energy projects, and promoting sustainable water management practices.

### Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa faces significant challenges related to water security and clean energy. According to the World Bank Open Data, only 32.4% of the population has access to safely managed drinking water (World Bank, 2024). This figure is starkly contrasted with a global average of 73.7% (World Bank, 2024). The region also struggles with renewable energy consumption at just 19.7%, far below global averages (World Bank, 2020).

Climate change exacerbates these challenges by increasing the frequency and severity of extreme weather events and altering water resources availability. The region's reliance on fossil fuels for energy generation poses significant environmental risks while limiting economic opportunities.

### Analysis

The data underscores the critical need for enhanced climate resilience in Sub-Saharan Africa:

*   **Water Security**: Only 32.4% of the population has access to safely managed drinking water, compared to a global average of 73.7% (World Bank, 2024).
*   **Clean Energy**: The region's reliance on fossil fuels for energy generation poses significant environmental risks while limiting economic opportunities.
*   **Climate Adaptation**: Climate change exacerbates these challenges by increasing the frequency and severity of extreme weather events and altering water resources availability.

### Policy Recommendations

To enhance resilience in water security, clean energy, and climate adaptation, we recommend:

1.  **Microgrids for Rural Clinics**: Implement microgrid systems that integrate solar panels and battery storage units at rural health centers to ensure reliable power supply and reduce reliance on diesel generators.
2.  **Climate-Resilient Energy Solutions**: Invest in decentralized renewable energy projects such as small-scale wind turbines and hydroelectric plants to improve access to electricity in remote areas.
3.  **Sustainable Water Management Practices**: Encourage the adoption of rainwater harvesting systems and climate-resilient irrigation techniques, such as drip irrigation and contour farming.

### Implementation Considerations

Implementing these recommendations requires a multi-faceted approach:

*   **Financing Mechanisms**: Establish dedicated funds to support microgrids for clinics and climate-resilient energy projects.
*   **Community Engagement**: Engage local communities in planning, design, and implementation of water management systems and renewable energy projects.
*   **Policy Support**: Develop supportive policies that incentivize private sector investments, such as feed-in tariffs or tax exemptions for renewable energy producers.

### Conclusion

Addressing water security, clean energy, and climate adaptation in Sub-Saharan Africa demands concerted efforts from governments, NGOs, and development partners. By adopting the proposed recommendations, decision-makers can create a more resilient future where communities benefit from improved livelihoods while contributing to global environmental goals.
