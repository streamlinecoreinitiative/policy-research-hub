# micro-insurance for smallholder drought risk in the Sudano-Sahelian belt: supporting adaptation targets in NDCs

**Enhancing Water Security, Clean Energy, and Climate Resilience in Sub-Saharan Africa: A Comprehensive Policy Blueprint**

## Executive Summary

To address the pressing water security, energy access, and climate resilience challenges facing Sub-Saharan Africa, we recommend implementing solar-powered desalination units for coastal communities and microgrids for rural clinics. These interventions are crucial to enhancing clean energy access, improving healthcare services, and reducing carbon footprints in the region.

## Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa is disproportionately affected by water scarcity, energy poverty, and climate change impacts. Recent data highlights the urgency of these challenges:

- Only 32.4% of the population in Sub-Saharan Africa has access to safely managed drinking water [1], making it a global priority.
- The region lags behind globally in terms of electricity access, with only 53.3% of its population having access to electricity [2].
- Sub-Saharan Africa suffers from limited freshwater resources, exacerbating scarcity issues in already arid regions like the Sahel.

## Analysis

The region's water supply and demand imbalance is a significant challenge:

- Sub-Saharan Africa is a net importer of freshwater [3], which worsens scarcity issues.
- The region holds significant potential for renewable energy, with 19.7% of total final energy consumption coming from renewables [4].
- Global progress towards SDG 6 (safely managed water) stands at 74%, but Sub-Saharan Africa lags behind due to limited access to safely managed drinking water [5].

## Policy Recommendations

To address these challenges, we recommend the following:

1. **Deploy Solar-Powered Desalination Units**: Implement solar-powered desalination units in coastal areas to ensure sustainable food security and reduce reliance on traditional energy sources.
2. **Introduce Microgrids for Rural Clinics**: Establish microgrid systems in rural clinics to provide reliable electricity, supporting healthcare services and reducing carbon footprints.

## Implementation Considerations

While initial investment costs are substantial, they can be offset by long-term savings on diesel fuel used in traditional generators [6]. High upfront costs and limited access to financing may hinder adoption; targeted subsidies or grants could help overcome these barriers.

## Conclusion

A comprehensive approach combining solar-powered desalination units and microgrids for rural clinics is crucial for enhancing water security, clean energy access, and overall resilience in Sub-Saharan Africa. Immediate action is needed to address the urgent needs of this region.
