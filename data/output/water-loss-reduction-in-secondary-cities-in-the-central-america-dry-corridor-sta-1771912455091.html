Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa is facing significant challenges in providing safe drinking water (32.4%) [1] and electricity access (53.3%), with 2.2 billion people lacking safe water globally [6]. The region's disparities in access to essential services are exacerbated by climate change impacts such as droughts and extreme weather events. According to the World Bank, Sub-Saharan Africa is the lowest performing region globally in terms of access to safely managed drinking water [2].

Analysis

Water Security

The lack of safe drinking water affects public health and productivity. Globally, 73.7% of people have access to safely managed drinking water [1], with Asia-Pacific achieving a coverage rate of 74% [4]. In contrast, Sub-Saharan Africa has the lowest coverage (32.4%) [1].

Clean Energy Access

Only 53.3% of the population in Sub-Saharan Africa has access to electricity, significantly lower than global averages and other regions like Asia-Pacific at 91.6% [5]. This disparity limits economic opportunities and exacerbates poverty.

Climate Adaptation

Climate change impacts such as droughts have forced communities to adopt unsustainable coping mechanisms, leading to reduced water availability and increased reliance on unsafe sources.
