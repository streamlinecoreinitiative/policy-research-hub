## Policy Recommendations

1. **Community-Based Watershed Management Programs**:
	* Integrate traditional knowledge with modern technologies to enhance water harvesting and management.
	* Provide financial incentives for community-led initiatives that improve watershed health.
2. **Microgrids for Rural Electrification**:
	* Increase investment in microgrid systems to provide reliable electricity access to rural areas.
	* Implement solar-powered electrification projects, especially in off-grid regions where grid extension is impractical or too costly.
3. **Clean Cooking Alternatives**:
	* Promote clean cooking technologies such as biogas digesters and improved cookstoves to reduce indoor air pollution from traditional stoves.
	* Provide subsidies for these technologies to make them more accessible to low-income households.

## Implementation Considerations

Implementation costs are significant, including infrastructure development and community engagement. However, microgrids can be funded through a combination of government grants, private sector investment, and public-private partnerships (IRENA 2020). Potential barriers include resistance from communities unfamiliar with modern technologies; addressing this requires involving local leaders in planning and providing training to ensure successful adoption.

## Conclusion

Addressing water security, clean energy access, and climate adaptation is essential for building resilient societies in Sub-Saharan Africa. By implementing evidence-based policies such as community-led watershed management programs, expanding microgrids, and promoting clean cooking alternatives, decision-makers can significantly improve the lives of millions while contributing to sustainable development goals.
