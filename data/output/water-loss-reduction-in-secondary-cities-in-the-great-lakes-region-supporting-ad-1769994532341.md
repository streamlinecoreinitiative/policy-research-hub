## Analysis

### Water Security

Approximately [VERIFY] billion people worldwide lack safe drinking water (UN-Water/WHO), with Sub-Saharan Africa accounting for a significant portion of this population. In fact, only 32.4% of the region's population has access to safely managed drinking water (World Bank, 2024). This is a critical issue that requires immediate attention.

### Clean Energy Access

The region's electricity access is only 53.3%, highlighting the need for sustainable energy solutions to meet growing demand. Renewable energy consumption is also limited at just 19.7% (World Bank, 2020), making it essential to invest in solar, wind, and hydroelectric power generation.

### Climate Adaptation

Climate change impacts such as droughts, floods, and extreme weather events threaten agricultural productivity and public health. In Sub-Saharan Africa, climate-related disasters have resulted in significant economic losses and displacement of people (World Bank, 2020). Implementing early warning systems, drought-resistant crops, and flood mitigation measures is crucial to protect against these impacts.

## Policy Recommendations

1. **Invest in Water Infrastructure**: Develop and finance large-scale water projects like dams, desalination plants, and pipeline networks to improve access to safe drinking water.
2. **Expand Renewable Energy Capacity**: Increase investment in solar, wind, and hydroelectric power generation to reduce reliance on fossil fuels and enhance energy security.
3. **Climate Resilience Programs**: Implement early warning systems, drought-resistant crops, and flood mitigation measures to protect against climate-related disasters.
