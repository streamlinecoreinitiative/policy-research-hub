# early-warning systems and last-mile alerts in the Lower Mekong delta: supporting adaptation targets in NDCs

**Bridging Water Security Gaps in Sub-Saharan Africa through Renewable Energy and Climate Adaptation**

## Executive Summary

This policy brief calls on governments, NGOs, and development practitioners to prioritize water security initiatives and renewable energy projects in Sub-Saharan Africa. With only 32.4% of the population having access to safely managed drinking water (World Bank Open Data), it is essential that we address this critical gap through evidence-based recommendations.

## Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa is home to over 1 billion people, with significant challenges in accessing safe drinking water and electricity. The region's reliance on traditional water sources exposes its inhabitants to waterborne diseases, while inadequate electricity supply limits the availability of clean cooking stoves and refrigeration for safe food storage (World Bank Open Data). Climate change further complicates these challenges with increased frequency and intensity of droughts and floods.

## Analysis

- **Water Security**: The continent lacks access to safely managed drinking water, putting public health at risk [NEEDS SOURCE].
- **Energy Access**: Only 53.3% of Sub-Saharan Africa's population has access to electricity, limiting the ability to provide safe cooking and refrigeration solutions (World Bank Open Data).
- **Renewable Energy Consumption**: The region lags behind in renewable energy consumption ([VERIFY]).

## Policy Recommendations

### Policy 1: Increase Access to Clean Water
Implement water resource management plans that prioritize boreholes, wells, and rainwater harvesting systems.
Allocate funding for community-led initiatives to maintain and rehabilitate existing infrastructure.
Encourage private sector investment in desalination plants and wastewater treatment facilities.

### Policy 2: Expand Renewable Energy Use
Develop a national renewable energy policy framework focused on solar, wind, and hydro power generation.
Invest in off-grid solar home systems (SHS) to improve access to electricity for rural households.
Promote community-based solar projects that can provide reliable electricity supply to schools, health centers, and water treatment plants.

### Policy 3: Implement Early Warning Systems
Establish early warning systems for droughts, floods, and other climate-related events using satellite imagery and ground sensors.
Train local communities in disaster preparedness and response strategies.
Develop contingency plans that include emergency water supply kits and temporary shelters.

## Implementation Considerations

- **Cost**: Initial investments in infrastructure will be substantial but yield long-term benefits in terms of health improvements, economic development, and climate resilience.
- **Barriers**: Limited funding, technical expertise, and political will may hinder implementation. Collaboration between governments, NGOs, and the private sector is essential to overcome these challenges.

## Conclusion

By addressing water security through renewable energy integration and climate adaptation strategies, Sub-Saharan Africa can significantly improve public health outcomes, enhance economic stability, and build more resilient communities. We call on policymakers, development partners, and community leaders to prioritize these interventions as part of broader national development plans.
