# Water Loss Reduction in Secondary Cities in the Mekong Basin: Improving Health Outcomes in Heatwaves

## Executive Summary

The Mekong Basin faces significant water scarcity challenges, affecting millions of people living in secondary cities. Climate change exacerbates this issue, leading to increased frequency and severity of heatwaves that have devastating effects on human health. This brief proposes an integrated approach using agroforestry-based micro-hydro systems to improve water security, reduce energy consumption, and enhance climate resilience.

## Introduction to Water Scarcity in the Mekong Basin

The Mekong Basin is home to a large population, with secondary cities facing significant water scarcity challenges due to altered precipitation patterns, increased temperatures, and more frequent droughts. The region's agricultural sector relies heavily on irrigation systems, which are often inefficient and unsustainable.

## Current Practices and Challenges

Existing irrigation systems in the Mekong Basin face issues such as:

* Inefficient use of water resources
* High operational costs due to energy consumption
* Limited access to electricity for micro-hydro systems
* Unsustainable agricultural practices contributing to soil degradation and reduced crop yields

Socio-economic factors, including poverty and limited infrastructure, exacerbate these challenges.
