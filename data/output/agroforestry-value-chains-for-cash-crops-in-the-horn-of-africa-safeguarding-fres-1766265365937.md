## Executive Summary

This brief explores the integration of agroforestry and micro-hydro systems as a climate-resilient solution to address severe water scarcity in the Horn of Africa. The region's agriculture relies heavily on irrigation, but traditional methods often lead to water loss through evaporation and seepage. Agroforestry-based micro-hydro systems offer a promising approach by combining soil conservation, biodiversity enhancement, and renewable energy production.

## Water Scarcity in the Horn of Africa

The Horn of Africa is characterized by arid to semi-arid conditions, with rainfall patterns becoming increasingly erratic due to climate change [verify]. This has severe implications for agriculture, which relies heavily on irrigation. Traditional irrigation systems often struggle to meet demand, leading to water scarcity and decreased crop yields.

### Key Challenges:

• Erratic rainfall patterns
• Water loss through evaporation and seepage
• Limited access to renewable energy sources

## Agroforestry's Role in Climate Resilience

Agroforestry combines trees with crops or livestock to promote soil health, biodiversity, and carbon sequestration. This approach has been shown to improve water retention and reduce erosion [verify].

### Benefits:

• Soil conservation through tree roots
• Enhanced biodiversity through habitat creation
• Carbon sequestration through forest growth

## Micro-Hydro Systems for Efficiency

Micro-hydro technology involves the use of small-scale hydroelectric systems to generate renewable energy. This approach offers several benefits, including reduced greenhouse gas emissions from fossil fuels and improved energy security through local power generation.

### Advantages:

• Reduced greenhouse gas emissions
• Improved energy security
• Enhanced water management through efficient irrigation
