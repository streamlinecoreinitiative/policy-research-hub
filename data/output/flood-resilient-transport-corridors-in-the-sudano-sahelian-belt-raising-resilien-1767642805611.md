## Executive Summary

The Sudano-Sahelian belt is a highly vulnerable region to climate change, facing severe water scarcity and poverty. The proposed project aims to enhance environmental resilience by developing flood-resilient transport corridors that integrate agroforestry-based micro-hydro systems for climate-resilient irrigation.

## Introduction to Water Scarcity in the Sudano-Sahelian Belt

The Sudano-Sahelian belt is characterized by a semi-arid climate, making it prone to droughts and floods. Climate change has exacerbated these conditions, leading to severe water scarcity and affecting agricultural productivity. The existing irrigation systems are inefficient, with significant losses due to evaporation and seepage.

## Current Irrigation Practices and Challenges

The current irrigation practices in the Sudano-Sahelian belt are characterized by:

* Inefficient use of water resources
* High energy consumption due to reliance on diesel generators
* Limited access to clean water for drinking and sanitation
* Negative impacts on soil health, biodiversity, and carbon sequestration
