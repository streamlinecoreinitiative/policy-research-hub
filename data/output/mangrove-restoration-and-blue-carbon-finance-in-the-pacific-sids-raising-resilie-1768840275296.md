# Mangrove Restoration and Blue Carbon Finance in the Pacific SIDS: Raising Resilience Finance for Communities

## Executive Summary

This brief focuses on environmental resilience in lower-income countries, specifically addressing water security, clean energy, and climate adaptation through mangrove restoration and blue carbon finance in the Pacific Small Island Developing States (SIDS). The document aims to provide a comprehensive overview of the current challenges faced by these communities, the potential benefits of integrated agroforestry-micro-hydro systems, and policy recommendations for implementation.

## Current Challenges and Opportunities

### Introduction to Water Scarcity in the Pacific SIDS

The Pacific SIDS are highly vulnerable to climate change due to their geographical location. Rising sea levels, increased storm intensity, and altered precipitation patterns threaten the very existence of these communities. The scarcity of clean water is a pressing issue, exacerbated by limited access to reliable irrigation systems and economic instability.

### Current Practices and Challenges

Traditional methods of surface water and rain-fed irrigation are still prevalent in the Pacific SIDS. However, these approaches have significant limitations, including low efficiency and sustainability. Farmers rely heavily on these methods, which often fail to provide adequate water supply, leading to reduced crop yields and financial instability.

### Agroforestry's Role in Addressing Water Scarcity

Agroforestry practices offer numerous benefits for addressing water scarcity, including:

*   Improved water retention and recharge through increased vegetation cover
*   Enhanced ecosystem services such as pollination, pest control, and carbon sequestration
*   Increased agricultural productivity due to improved soil health and water management
*   Reduced vulnerability to climate variability
