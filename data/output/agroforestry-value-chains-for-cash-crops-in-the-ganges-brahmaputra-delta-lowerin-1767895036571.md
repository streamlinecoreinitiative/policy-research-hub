# Agroforestry-Based Micro-Hydro Systems for Climate Resilient Irrigation in the Central African Rainforest

## Executive Summary

The Ganges-Brahmaputra delta faces severe water scarcity and climate-related challenges. This brief explores the potential of agroforestry-based micro-hydro systems to promote climate resilience in agriculture by integrating agroforestry practices with micro-hydro technology.

## Addressing Severe Water Scarcity and Climate Change Impacts

### Introduction to Water Scarcity

Water scarcity is a pressing issue in the Ganges-Brahmaputra delta, affecting millions of people. Current irrigation practices are often labor-intensive and expensive [verify]. Diesel generator usage contributes significantly to greenhouse gas emissions.

### Agroforestry's Role

Agroforestry projects have demonstrated improved soil health benefits, including increased moisture retention [verify]. These practices also enhance biodiversity, leading to natural pest control and pollination services. A case study in the Central African Rainforest showed significant increases in crop yields due to agroforestry practices.

### Micro-Hydro Systems

Micro-hydro technology provides a renewable energy source for irrigation systems, reducing reliance on diesel generators [verify]. Pilot micro-hydro systems have demonstrated improved water delivery and energy savings [verify].

## Integration of Agroforestry and Micro-Hydro

Pilot projects have shown that integrating agroforestry practices with micro-hydro systems can lead to increased crop yields while promoting self-sufficiency in renewable energy. Economic benefits include reduced fuel and maintenance costs.
