The Andean highlands face significant water scarcity challenges that impact farmers and communities. This brief highlights the potential of integrating agroforestry practices with micro-hydro systems to ensure sustainable water management, economic development, and climate resilience.

**Introduction to Water Scarcity in the Andean Highlands**

The Andean highlands are characterized by fragile ecosystems, limited water resources, and increasing climate variability. Severe water scarcity affects farmers' livelihoods and communities' well-being due to inadequate irrigation systems that rely on traditional methods.

**Micro-Hydro Systems for Efficiency**

Micro-hydro systems harness energy from small-scale water flows to power irrigation pumps and other applications. This technology offers several benefits, including:

* Reduced water usage
* Cost savings
* Improved reliability

However, installation costs can be high, and ongoing operational expenses require careful consideration. To ensure the long-term sustainability of micro-hydro systems, it is essential to develop robust maintenance strategies and training programs for local communities.

**Integration in Climate-Resilient Water Supply**

* Reducing environmental degradation
