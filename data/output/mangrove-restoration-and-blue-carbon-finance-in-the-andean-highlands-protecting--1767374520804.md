# Mangrove Restoration and Blue Carbon Finance in the Andean Highlands: Protecting Livelihoods under Climate Stress

## Executive Summary

The Andean highlands are experiencing severe water scarcity due to climate change, affecting agriculture, food security, and economic stability. This brief explores the potential of agroforestry-based micro-hydro systems as a solution for climate-resilient irrigation in this region.

## Water Scarcity in the Andean Highlands

Climate change is exacerbating water scarcity in the Andean highlands through erratic rainfall patterns, affecting agriculture and food security. The region's existing irrigation systems are inefficient and prone to water loss through evaporation and seepage.

### Current Irrigation Practices and Challenges

* Existing irrigation systems:
	+ Boreholes: drilled wells that extract groundwater
	+ Canals: gravity-fed channels for water distribution
* Water loss through:
	+ Evaporation: significant losses due to open canals and lack of water storage
	+ Seepage: water leakage from canals and pipes

## Agroforestry's Role in Climate Resilience

Agroforestry practices have been shown to improve soil health, biodiversity, and carbon sequestration. By integrating trees into agricultural landscapes, farmers can:

* Improve water retention: tree roots help retain soil moisture
* Reduce erosion: tree roots hold soil in place, reducing sedimentation
* Increase biodiversity: agroforests support a wider range of plant and animal species

## Micro-Hydro Systems for Irrigation Efficiency

Micro-hydro technology involves harnessing small-scale hydroelectric power to generate electricity. Benefits include:

* Reduced reliance on diesel generators: decreasing energy costs and emissions
* Increased energy efficiency: micro-hydro systems can achieve high energy conversion rates

### Pilot Projects

Several pilot projects have demonstrated the effectiveness of integrating agroforestry with micro-hydro technology for climate-resilient irrigation.

## Integration in Climate-Resilient Water Supply

By combining agroforestry and micro-hydro systems, communities can create a more resilient water supply that:

* Improves water retention: reducing evaporation and seepage losses

* Supports biodiversity: maintaining healthy ecosystems through agroforestry practices
