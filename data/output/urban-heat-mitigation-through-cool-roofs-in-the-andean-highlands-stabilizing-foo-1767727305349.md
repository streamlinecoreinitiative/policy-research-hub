# Urban Heat Mitigation through Cool Roofs in the Andean Highlands: Stabilizing Food Security during Droughts

## Executive Summary

The Andean highlands are experiencing increasing drought frequencies and intensities, threatening food security for vulnerable communities. This brief highlights the potential of urban heat mitigation through cool roofs as a climate-resilient strategy to stabilize food production during droughts.

## Climate Change Impacts on Water Scarcity in the Andean Highlands

Climate change is exacerbating water scarcity in the Andean highlands through erratic rainfall patterns, increased evaporation from wetlands, and altered snowmelt regimes. These changes have severe consequences for agriculture, including reduced crop yields, decreased food security, and increased poverty.

### Current Irrigation Practices and Challenges

Traditional irrigation systems in the region are inefficient, with significant water loss due to poor canal design (evaporation and seepage). High labor costs and fuel costs further exacerbate the challenges faced by farmers.

## Agroforestry-Based Micro-Hydro Systems for Climate-Resilient Irrigation

Agroforestry practices can improve soil health, increase water retention, and enhance biodiversity in agricultural landscapes. By integrating micro-hydro systems with agroforestry, farmers can reduce their reliance on external energy sources and improve crop yields.

### Benefits of Agroforestry-Based Micro-Hydro Systems

*   **Soil Health Benefits:** Improved soil structure with increased moisture retention.
*   **Biodiversity Enhancement:** Increased biodiversity leading to natural pest control and pollination services.
*   **Energy Savings and Cost Reductions:** Reduced reliance on diesel generators.
