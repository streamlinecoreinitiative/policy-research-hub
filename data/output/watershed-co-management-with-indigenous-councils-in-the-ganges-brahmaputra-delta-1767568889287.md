# Watershed Co-management with Indigenous Councils in the Ganges-Brahmaputra Delta: Safeguarding Freshwater Ecosystems

## Executive Summary

The Ganges-Brahmaputra delta is one of the world's most ecologically sensitive regions, facing severe environmental challenges due to climate change. Rising sea levels and increased precipitation patterns threaten freshwater ecosystems, affecting millions of people dependent on these resources for their livelihoods.

## Environmental Resilience and Climate Adaptation

### Introduction to Water Scarcity

Climate change is exacerbating water scarcity issues in the region. Erratic rainfall patterns lead to reduced water availability for crops, while increased evaporation from wetlands further depletes available resources.

Micro-hydro technology provides renewable energy solutions for irrigation systems. These systems reduce diesel generator usage and result in significant fuel cost savings.

## Integration of Agroforestry and Micro-Hydro Systems

Pilot projects integrating agroforestry with micro-hydro systems have shown promising results. These combined solutions enhance both water security and renewable energy use, improving agricultural productivity.

## Policy Recommendations
