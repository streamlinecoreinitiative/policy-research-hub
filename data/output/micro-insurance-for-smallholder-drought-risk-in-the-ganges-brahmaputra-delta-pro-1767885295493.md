**Micro-insurance for Smallholder Drought Risk in the Ganges-Brahmaputra Delta: Protecting Livelihoods under Climate Stress**

**Executive Summary**

The Ganges-Brahmaputra delta, home to millions of smallholder farmers, faces severe drought risks exacerbated by climate change. Traditional irrigation systems and economic instability further exacerbate these challenges. This brief proposes integrating agroforestry-based micro-hydro systems for climate-resilient irrigation as a potential solution.

**Addressing Severe Water Scarcity in the Ganges-Brahmaputra Delta**

### **Introduction to Water Scarcity in the Region**

The Ganges-Brahmaputra delta faces severe water scarcity due to historical droughts and current challenges such as limited access to reliable irrigation systems and economic instability. Farmers and communities rely heavily on traditional methods like surface water and rain-fed systems, which are often inefficient and unsustainable.

### **Current Practices and Challenges**

Existing irrigation systems in the region have limitations, including high dependency on traditional methods and limited efficiency and sustainability. Farmers' reliance on these methods exacerbates existing challenges, leading to economic pressures and reduced crop yields.
