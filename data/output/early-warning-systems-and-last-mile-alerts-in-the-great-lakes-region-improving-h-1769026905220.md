**Enhancing Water Security, Clean Energy Access, and Climate Adaptation in Sub-Saharan Africa**

## Executive Summary

Sub-Saharan Africa faces severe water scarcity and energy poverty, exacerbating climate vulnerability. To build a more resilient future for its populations, we recommend prioritizing climate-resilient infrastructure projects for clean energy and water security. Specifically, we urge governments to integrate renewable energy into national power grids to reduce emissions from critical sectors. This policy brief outlines the key findings, analysis, and recommendations to address these pressing issues.

## Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa lags behind the world in water and electricity access, with significant disparities between urban and rural areas. Climate impacts are already affecting agricultural productivity and water resources, with rising temperatures and changing precipitation patterns expected to worsen in the coming years (IPCC, 2022). Sub-Saharan Africa is expected to face more frequent droughts and floods, threatening both freshwater supplies and crop yields.

## Analysis

Evidence suggests that climate change will continue to exacerbate these challenges. According to the IPCC (2022), Sub-Saharan Africa is projected to experience a [VERIFY] % increase in temperature by 2050, leading to increased water scarcity and decreased agricultural productivity.

Renewable energy potential is significant, with solar and wind resources available throughout the region. However, harnessing these resources for widespread use remains a challenge due to high upfront costs and limited technical expertise (IRENA, 2022).

Innovative approaches like rainwater harvesting and micro-insurance schemes have shown promise in improving access to safe drinking water (World Bank, 2020). These solutions can be scaled up with the right policy support.

## Policy Recommendations

1. **Expand Access to Safe Drinking Water**:
	* Implement rainwater harvesting systems in rural areas, focusing on peri-urban zones where populations are growing rapidly.
	* Promote the use of micro-insurance for smallholder farmers to protect against drought-related losses and invest in community-based water management projects.

2. **Integrate Renewable Energy into National Grids**:
	* Invest in solar-powered desalination plants in coastal regions with limited fresh water availability.
	* Develop microgrids for off-grid communities, leveraging solar energy to reduce reliance on fossil fuels and lower emissions from critical sectors like healthcare.

3. **Enhance Climate Resilience through Water Management**:
	* Establish early warning systems for floods and droughts using satellite data and ground-based sensors.
	* Support the development of climate-smart irrigation cooperatives that use water-efficient technologies and practices to sustainably manage scarce resources.
