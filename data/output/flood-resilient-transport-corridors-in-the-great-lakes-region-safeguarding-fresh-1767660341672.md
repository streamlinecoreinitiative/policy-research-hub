# Agroforestry-Based Micro-Hydro Systems for Climate-Resilient Irrigation in the Mesoamerican Highlands

## Executive Summary

The Mesoamerican Highlands face severe water scarcity and poverty exacerbated by climate change impacts on agriculture. This brief explores the potential of agroforestry-based micro-hydro systems to provide climate-resilient irrigation solutions.

## Introduction to Water Scarcity in the Mesoamerican Highlands

Climate change alters precipitation patterns and increases temperatures, leading to severe water scarcity and reduced agricultural productivity. Existing irrigation systems suffer from significant water loss due to evaporation (30% [verify]) and seepage (5% [verify]). This has devastating consequences for small-scale farmers.

## Micro-Hydro Systems for Irrigation Efficiency

Micro-hydro technology harnesses the energy of small streams or rivers to generate electricity. This approach reduces reliance on diesel generators, decreases greenhouse gas emissions, and increases energy efficiency.

### Benefits:

- Reduced energy costs
- Increased water retention through improved irrigation scheduling
- Enhanced resilience to climate-related shocks

## Integration of Agroforestry and Micro-Hydro in Climate-Resilient Water Supply

Pilot projects demonstrate the potential for combining agroforestry with micro-hydro systems. These integrated approaches can provide reliable, renewable energy for irrigation while promoting sustainable water management practices.

### Economic Analysis:

- Cost savings from reduced diesel generator use
- Increased crop yields and productivity
- Potential for scaling up to larger areas
