# Agroforestry-Based Micro-Hydro Systems for Climate-Resilient Irrigation in the Ganges-Brahmaputra Delta: Enhancing Livelihoods and Reducing Disaster Losses

## Executive Summary

Climate change poses significant threats to water security, clean energy access, and climate adaptation in lower-income countries. The Ganges-Brahmaputra delta is particularly vulnerable due to its unique geography and high population density. This brief outlines the potential of agroforestry-based micro-hydro systems for climate-resilient irrigation, with a focus on cash crops.

## I. Introduction to Water Scarcity in the Ganges-Brahmaputra Delta

The Ganges-Brahmaputra delta is one of the most densely populated regions globally. Climate change has exacerbated water scarcity in this region, leading to reduced crop yields and decreased food security. Rising temperatures and changing precipitation patterns have resulted in:

* Erratic rainfall patterns affecting agricultural productivity
* Increased frequency and severity of floods and droughts
* Decreased water availability for irrigation

## II. Current Irrigation Practices and Challenges

Traditional irrigation systems in the Ganges-Brahmaputra delta rely heavily on groundwater extraction, leading to:

* Over-extraction of groundwater resources [verify]
* Salinization of soil and reduced fertility
* Increased energy consumption through diesel generators

Agroforestry-based micro-hydro systems offer a more sustainable alternative by harnessing renewable energy from local water sources.

Micro-hydro technology provides a reliable and efficient source of renewable energy for irrigation:

* Increased energy efficiency through hydroelectric power generation
* Potential for electrification of rural communities

Pilot projects have demonstrated the effectiveness of integrating agroforestry with micro-hydro systems.
