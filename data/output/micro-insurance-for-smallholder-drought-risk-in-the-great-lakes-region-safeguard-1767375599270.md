# Climate-Resilient Irrigation for Smallholder Farmers in the Great Lakes Region: A Micro-insurance and Agroforestry-Based Approach

## Executive Summary

The Great Lakes region faces significant drought risks due to climate change, affecting smallholder farmers and freshwater ecosystems. This brief proposes integrating micro-insurance with agroforestry-based micro-hydro systems as a climate-resilient solution for irrigation in the region.

## Environmental Resilience in Lower-Income Countries: Key Challenges

### Water Security

*   **Climate Change Impacts:** Erratic rainfall patterns and increased evaporation from wetlands impact water availability for crops.
*   **Current Irrigation Practices:** Traditional irrigation methods are energy-intensive, costly, and contribute to water loss through evaporation and seepage.

### Clean Energy

*   **Renewable Energy Potential:** Micro-hydro systems can provide a reliable source of renewable energy for irrigation.
*   **Energy Efficiency:** These systems reduce reliance on diesel generators, lowering operating expenses.
