## Executive Summary

This policy brief highlights critical challenges in ensuring water security, access to clean energy, and climate adaptation in Sub-Saharan Africa. Despite progress in some areas, the region lags behind globally and regionally in these essential services. To accelerate progress, we recommend increasing renewable energy consumption to at least 20% by 2030 through public-private partnerships and concessional financing (World Bank, 2024). Additionally, investing in rural water supply systems and implementing climate adaptation measures such as early-warning systems and community-based initiatives will significantly boost resilience.

## Analysis

The region's dependence on renewable energy sources remains low at just over a quarter of total final energy consumption globally (19.7%) as per World Bank data (2020). This is concerning given the vast potential for harnessing solar, wind, and hydro resources to meet growing energy demands sustainably. Additionally, the region's climate resilience efforts are also underdeveloped, with only 53.3% of the population having access to electricity, indicating a significant gap in infrastructure development necessary for effective adaptation measures.

## Policy Recommendations

1. **Increase Renewable Energy Consumption**: Accelerate investment in solar and wind projects to reach at least 20% renewable energy consumption by 2030 (World Bank, 2024). This can be achieved through public-private partnerships and concessional financing.
2. **Enhance Water Infrastructure**: Invest in rural water supply systems such as boreholes and rainwater harvesting ponds to ensure access for all communities. Targeted subsidies and grants for these projects can significantly boost coverage.
3. **Climate Adaptation Measures**: Implement early-warning systems, climate-resilient infrastructure investments (e.g., flood-proof roads), and community-based adaptation initiatives like mangrove restoration programs to protect freshwater ecosystems and livelihoods from climate impacts.
