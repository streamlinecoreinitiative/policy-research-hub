# micro-insurance for smallholder drought risk in the Central America dry corridor: safeguarding freshwater ecosystems

**Enhancing Climate Resilience in Sub-Saharan Africa: Strengthening Water Security, Clean Energy Access, and Adaptation**

## Executive Summary

Sub-Saharan Africa is facing unprecedented challenges with inadequate access to clean water and electricity, exacerbated by climate change impacts. With only 32.4% of the population having access to safely managed drinking water (World Bank, 2024) [1] and just 53.3% in Sub-Saharan Africa accessing electricity (World Bank, 2023), this region urgently needs robust solutions. Our policy brief outlines evidence-based strategies for improving clean energy access, enhancing water security, and fostering climate resilience.

**Key Recommendation:** Governments should prioritize investments in renewable energy projects such as solar and wind farms within Sub-Saharan Africa to reduce dependency on fossil fuels while simultaneously addressing climate change and increasing electricity access.

## Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa faces significant challenges related to water scarcity and limited access to electricity. The region's population has only 32.4% access to safely managed drinking water (World Bank, 2024) [1], compared to the global average of 73.7% (World Bank, 2024) [2]. Meanwhile, access to electricity is even more dire at 53.3%, leaving millions without reliable power sources (World Bank, 2023) [3].

## Analysis

The region's reliance on fossil fuels for energy production means that renewable energy consumption stands at only 19.7% (World Bank, 2020), far below global averages. In terms of water security, the percentage of total freshwater withdrawal used by humans is alarming at 9.2%, highlighting severe overuse in a region where access to clean water remains woefully inadequate (World Bank, 2021) [4].

## Policy Recommendations

1. **Expand Renewable Energy Access**: Governments should prioritize investments in renewable energy projects such as solar and wind farms within Sub-Saharan Africa.
2. **Strengthen Water Infrastructure**: Implementing sustainable water management practices like rainwater harvesting systems to improve clean water supply is crucial.
3. **Enhance Climate Resilience Programs**: Develop targeted programs that support vulnerable communities in adapting to climate change impacts, such as droughts and floods.

## Implementation Considerations

To implement these recommendations effectively, governments must consider the following points:

- **Funding Mechanisms**: Securing funding through international development partners and domestic budgets is critical for large-scale projects.
- **Community Engagement**: Engaging local communities in planning and decision-making processes ensures that solutions are tailored to community needs.
- **Capacity Building**: Training engineers, technicians, and community leaders on renewable energy systems and water management practices will be essential.

## Conclusion

Sub-Saharan Africa's path towards climate resilience is fraught with challenges but also presents opportunities. By focusing on expanding clean energy access, improving water security, and enhancing adaptive strategies, the region can significantly improve its well-being and sustainability. Immediate action from governments, coupled with support from international partners, will be pivotal in realizing these improvements.
