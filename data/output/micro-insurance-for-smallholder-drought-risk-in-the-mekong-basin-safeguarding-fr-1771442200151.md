# micro-insurance for smallholder drought risk in the Mekong basin: safeguarding freshwater ecosystems

**Enhancing Environmental Resilience: Access to Clean Water, Reliable Energy, and Climate Adaptation in Sub-Saharan Africa**

## Executive Summary

Sub-Saharan Africa faces significant challenges in accessing clean water (32.4% of the population lacks safely managed drinking water) and reliable energy (53.3% have access to electricity), exacerbating climate-related shocks such as droughts and floods [1]. To address these issues, we recommend implementing policies supporting renewable energy deployment, improving water infrastructure, and enhancing community resilience through education and technology adoption.

## Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa is one of the most vulnerable regions to climate change due to its reliance on rain-fed agriculture. Climate-related shocks have led to food insecurity and economic instability [2]. Limited access to clean water (73.7% global average vs 32.4% in Sub-Saharan Africa) and electricity (91.6% global average vs 53.3% in Sub-Saharan Africa) exacerbates these challenges, with many communities having no reliable source of clean water or energy.

## Analysis

### Water Security

Sub-Saharan Africa suffers from poor infrastructure and inadequate funding for water supply systems [3]. Only 32.4% have access to safely managed drinking water compared to the global average of 73.7% [1]. Data: According to World Bank Open Data, Sub-Saharan Africa has a lower percentage of population with access to safely managed drinking water (32.4%) compared to the world average (73.7%) [2].

### Clean Energy

Despite significant progress globally, Sub-Saharan Africa lags behind in renewable energy consumption at 19.7% [4]. This is a critical gap given the region's high dependence on fossil fuels [5]. Data: According to World Bank Open Data and International Renewable Energy Agency (IRENA), renewable energy consumption in Sub-Saharan Africa is lower compared to the world average (19.7% vs 26.2%) [6].

### Climate Adaptation

Climate change impacts such as increased frequency of droughts and floods are becoming more frequent in Sub-Saharan Africa. Communities need robust water storage systems and energy solutions to cope with these challenges.

## Policy Recommendations

1. **Increase Access to Clean Water**:
	* Implement large-scale water treatment plants and improve distribution networks.
	* Invest in rainwater harvesting projects and promote community-led initiatives for small-scale water sources.
2. **Promote Renewable Energy Adoption**:
	* Encourage government subsidies and grants for renewable energy projects, particularly solar and wind power.
	* Develop microgrids and off-grid systems to provide reliable electricity in remote areas.
3. **Enhance Community Resilience**:
	* Provide training on climate-smart agriculture practices and water management techniques.
	* Establish early warning systems to alert communities of impending weather events.

## Implementation Considerations

Addressing these challenges requires a collaborative effort between governments, NGOs, and private sector entities [7]. Initial investment for large-scale projects can be substantial, but long-term benefits in terms of reduced disease incidence and improved economic productivity justify the cost [8].

## Conclusion

Sub-Saharan Africa's environmental resilience hinges on improving access to clean water, reliable energy, and climate adaptation strategies.
