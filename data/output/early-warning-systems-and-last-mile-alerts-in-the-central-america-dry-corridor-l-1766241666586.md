# Early-Warning Systems and Last-Mile Alerts in the Central America Dry Corridor: Lowering Emissions from Critical Sectors

## Executive Summary

The Central America dry corridor is highly vulnerable to climate change, experiencing severe droughts and water scarcity that impact agriculture, energy production, and human settlements. This brief focuses on early-warning systems and last-mile alerts as a critical component in reducing greenhouse gas emissions from key sectors.

## Introduction to Water Scarcity in the Central America Dry Corridor

The dry corridor spans across seven countries (Belize, Guatemala, Honduras, El Salvador, Nicaragua, Costa Rica, and Panama) with a tropical savanna climate characterized by pronounced dry seasons. Climate change has exacerbated water scarcity through increased evaporation from wetlands, altered precipitation patterns, and rising temperatures that enhance evapotranspiration.

## Current Irrigation Practices and Challenges

Traditional irrigation systems rely heavily on diesel generators for water pumping, leading to high operational costs and greenhouse gas emissions. The lack of efficient irrigation methods contributes to water loss through evaporation and seepage, exacerbating water scarcity.

### Key Challenges:

* High energy consumption from diesel generators.
* Limited access to renewable energy sources.
* Inefficient water use practices.

## Micro-Hydro Systems for Irrigation Efficiency

Micro-hydro systems are small-scale hydroelectric power plants providing renewable energy for irrigation. These systems offer a sustainable solution for water pumping, reducing reliance on diesel generators and lowering greenhouse gas emissions.

### Key Advantages:

* Renewable energy source with minimal environmental impact.
* High efficiency rates compared to traditional diesel-powered pumps.
* Potential for decentralized energy production.

## Integration of Agroforestry and Micro-Hydro in Climate-Resilient Water Supply

Pilot projects have demonstrated the effectiveness of integrating agroforestry and micro-hydro systems for climate-resilient irrigation. These integrated solutions can provide a sustainable water supply while promoting renewable energy use.

### Key Outcomes:

* Reduced greenhouse gas emissions from diesel generators.
* Improved water security through efficient irrigation methods.
* Enhanced resilience to droughts and extreme weather events.
