### Executive Summary

To address the pressing challenges of water scarcity and energy poverty in Sub-Saharan Africa, we recommend prioritizing investments in water infrastructure and clean energy projects. Specifically, governments should allocate at least 20% of their annual budgets towards improving water supply systems and promoting renewable energy adoption by 2025.

• **Investment in Water Infrastructure**: Governments should prioritize funding for improved water supply systems, including wells, boreholes, and pipelines. This includes constructing more robust reservoirs that can withstand climate variability.
• **Promote Renewable Energy Adoption**: Encourage the use of renewable energy sources such as solar and wind power for electricity generation through subsidies, tax incentives, or public-private partnerships.

### Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa faces significant water scarcity and energy poverty, exacerbated by climate change. The region is particularly vulnerable to droughts, floods, and erratic rainfall patterns, which have exacerbated existing water security challenges (World Bank, 2024). ### Analysis

The region's water security and energy access challenges are starkly evident:

* Only 32.4% of the population has access to safely managed drinking water in Sub-Saharan Africa (World Bank, 2024), significantly lower than the world-wide average of 73.7% [Source: World Bank Open Data].
* Only 53.3% of Sub-Saharan Africa's population can access electricity (World Bank, 2023), below the global average of 91.6% [Source: World Bank Open Data].
* Climate change impacts are severe in Sub-Saharan Africa, with frequent droughts and floods affecting millions.

### Policy Recommendations

1. **Investment in Water Infrastructure**: Governments should allocate at least 20% of their annual budgets towards improving water supply systems.
2. **Promote Renewable Energy Adoption**: Encourage the use of renewable energy sources such as solar and wind power for electricity generation through subsidies, tax incentives, or public-private partnerships.
3. **Climate Resilient Agriculture Practices**: Support farmers in adopting drought-resistant crops and irrigation techniques.

### Implementation Considerations

Implementing these interventions will require substantial financial resources. Governments should consider cost-sharing models with international partners or leverage existing funds from global initiatives like the Green Climate Fund (World Bank, 2024). Addressing local resistance to new technologies or practices requires engaging with community leaders and providing clear benefits.

### Conclusion

Sub-Saharan Africa must prioritize investments in water security, clean energy, and climate adaptation to achieve sustainable development goals. By adopting evidence-based policies and fostering collaboration among stakeholders, the region can overcome current challenges and pave the way for a more resilient future.
