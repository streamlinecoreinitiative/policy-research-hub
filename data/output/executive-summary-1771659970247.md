# Executive Summary

**Enhancing Environmental Resilience in Sub-Saharan Africa Through Water Security, Clean Energy, and Climate Adaptation**

## Executive Summary

This policy brief highlights the urgent need to enhance environmental resilience in Sub-Saharan Africa by addressing water scarcity, energy poverty, and climate change impacts. With only 32.4% of the population having access to safely managed drinking water [1] and 53.3% with electricity [2], there is a critical need for targeted interventions that can bridge these gaps while considering local political and institutional constraints.

**Key Recommendations:**

1. **Invest in desalination plants and efficient water treatment systems**: Allocate funds to support the development of desalination plants and efficient water treatment systems in areas with limited access to safe drinking water.
2. **Promote solar-based clean energy solutions**: Support the installation of microgrids powered by solar panels to provide electricity in remote areas, focusing on off-grid solutions to ensure accessibility.
3. **Develop climate adaptation strategies**: Develop early warning systems for climate-related disasters such as droughts and floods, and encourage community-led initiatives that integrate natural resource management practices like afforestation and agroforestry.

## Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa is disproportionately affected by water scarcity, energy poverty, and climate change impacts. The region's high population density and limited resources exacerbate these challenges, making it essential to address them through targeted interventions. According to the World Bank (2024), only 28.6% of the population in Sub-Saharan Africa has access to safely managed drinking water [1], highlighting the critical need for improved water security.
