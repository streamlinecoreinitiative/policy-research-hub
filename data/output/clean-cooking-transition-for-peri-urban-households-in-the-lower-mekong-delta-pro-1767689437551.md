# Clean Cooking Transition for Peri-Urban Households in the Lower Mekong Delta: Protecting Livelihoods under Climate Stress

## Executive Summary

The Lower Mekong delta is highly vulnerable to climate change, facing severe water scarcity and erratic rainfall patterns that threaten agriculture, livelihoods, and food security. This brief highlights the need for a clean cooking transition in peri-urban households, leveraging agroforestry-based micro-hydro systems as a climate-resilient irrigation solution.

## Policy Recommendations for Supporting a Clean Cooking Transition

1. **Subsidies and Grants:** Governments can offer subsidies on initial investments for micro-hydro system installation.
2. **Infrastructure Development:** Community-led micro-hydro system installations with local government support ensure long-term sustainability.
3. **Capacity Building:** Training programs for farmers and community leaders in agroforestry and micro-hydro system management are essential.

## Actions/Recommendations

1.  Develop a comprehensive policy framework to support the adoption of clean cooking technologies, prioritizing peri-urban households.
2.  Establish partnerships between governments, NGOs, and private sector entities to provide subsidies and grants for initial investments in micro-hydro systems.
3.  Implement community-led infrastructure development initiatives to ensure long-term sustainability of integrated agroforestry-micro-hydro systems.

## Closing Outlook

The Lower Mekong delta's peri-urban households face significant climate-related challenges, underscoring the need for a clean cooking transition leveraging agroforestry-based micro-hydro systems. By integrating renewable energy with sustainable agricultural practices, this approach can enhance water security, reduce energy costs, and improve crop yields while protecting livelihoods under climate stress.
