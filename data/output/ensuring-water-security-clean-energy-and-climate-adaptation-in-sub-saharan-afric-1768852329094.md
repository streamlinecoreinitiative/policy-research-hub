## Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa is home to 1 billion people, facing significant challenges in accessing safe water and electricity. Only **32.4% of the region's population** has access to safely managed drinking water [2], while only **53.3% have access to electricity** [3]. These figures highlight a critical need for improved infrastructure and energy solutions. Climate change exacerbates these issues, with frequent droughts and floods affecting agricultural productivity and health outcomes.

## Policy Recommendations

To address these challenges, policy interventions should focus on:

1. **Promoting micro-hydro systems** for decentralized power generation in remote areas.
2. **Expanding solar energy infrastructure**, including solar-powered irrigation pumps, to enhance agricultural productivity and income-generating opportunities for rural communities.
3. **Developing early warning systems** and disaster risk reduction strategies to protect vulnerable populations from climate-induced shocks.

## Implementation Considerations

Implementation will require coordination between government agencies, NGOs, and private sector entities. Funding mechanisms such as grants, concessional loans, and public-private partnerships can support project development and implementation. Training programs for local communities on safe water management and climate resilience practices are essential to ensure long-term sustainability.
