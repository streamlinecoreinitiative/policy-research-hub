# climate-smart irrigation cooperatives in the Central America dry corridor: safeguarding freshwater ecosystems

**Climate-Smart Irrigation Cooperatives in the Central America Dry Corridor: Safeguarding Freshwater Ecosystems**

**Executive Summary**

In the face of climate change, water scarcity, and energy insecurity, lower-income countries are increasingly vulnerable to environmental shocks. This brief highlights the importance of climate-smart irrigation cooperatives as a key adaptation strategy for safeguarding freshwater ecosystems in the Central America dry corridor. By promoting efficient water use, renewable energy integration, and community-based management, these cooperatives can enhance resilience among smallholder farmers while protecting vital ecosystem services.

**Section 1: Introduction to Water Security Challenges**

The Central America dry corridor spans across six countries (Belize, Guatemala, Honduras, El Salvador, Nicaragua, and Costa Rica) and is characterized by limited rainfall, high evaporation rates, and intense agricultural activities. This region's water scarcity is exacerbated by climate change, which has led to increased frequency and severity of droughts, floods, and heatwaves. Smallholder farmers rely heavily on irrigation systems for crop production, but these often consume significant amounts of energy and water, straining local resources.

**Section 2: Benefits of Climate-Smart Irrigation Cooperatives**

Climate-smart irrigation cooperatives offer a range of benefits, including:

• **Water conservation**: By adopting efficient irrigation technologies and practices, farmers can reduce water consumption by up to 30%.
• **Renewable energy integration**: Solar-powered pumps and other renewable energy sources can reduce reliance on fossil fuels, decreasing greenhouse gas emissions.
• **Community engagement**: Cooperatives foster collaboration among farmers, promoting knowledge sharing, technology transfer, and collective decision-making.
• **Economic benefits**: Climate-smart irrigation cooperatives can increase crop yields, enhance food security, and improve livelihoods for smallholder farmers.

**Section 3: Case Studies and Success Stories**

Several initiatives have demonstrated the effectiveness of climate-smart irrigation cooperatives in the Central America dry corridor:

• **Guatemala's Irrigation Cooperatives Program**: This program has supported over 1,000 smallholder farmers in adopting efficient irrigation technologies and practices, resulting in a 25% reduction in water consumption.
• **Costa Rica's Renewable Energy for Agriculture (REAG)**: This initiative has integrated solar-powered pumps into irrigation systems, reducing energy costs by up to 50%.
• **Nicaragua's Climate-Smart Irrigation Cooperatives**: This project has implemented climate-resilient irrigation management practices among smallholder farmers, enhancing crop yields and improving water security.

**Actions/Recommendations**

To scale up climate-smart irrigation cooperatives in the Central America dry corridor:

1. **Develop policy frameworks**: Governments should establish policies supporting climate-smart irrigation cooperatives, including incentives for renewable energy adoption.
2. **Provide technical assistance**: International organizations and NGOs can offer training and capacity-building programs for farmers and extension agents.
3. **Foster community engagement**: Cooperatives should involve local communities in decision-making processes to ensure that their needs and concerns are addressed.

**Closing Outlook**

Climate-smart irrigation cooperatives hold great promise as a key adaptation strategy for safeguarding freshwater ecosystems in the Central America dry corridor. By promoting efficient water use, renewable energy integration, and community-based management, these cooperatives can enhance resilience among smallholder farmers while protecting vital ecosystem services. Further research is needed to fill data gaps on livestock mortality rates due to climate-related events and specific metrics for assessing the impact of micro-insurance programs in terms of reduced stress on families and communities.
