# climate-smart irrigation cooperatives in the Sahel: supporting adaptation targets in NDCs

**Climate-Smart Irrigation Cooperatives in the Sahel: Supporting Adaptation Targets in NDCs**

## Executive Summary

To address the triple threat of water scarcity, energy poverty, and climate vulnerability in Sub-Saharan Africa, we recommend establishing climate-smart irrigation cooperatives in the Sahel region. This approach will enhance water security, promote clean energy access, and strengthen climate resilience. By investing in rainwater harvesting systems, desalination technologies, and renewable energy projects, policymakers can unlock sustainable development pathways that benefit millions of people living in rural and urban areas.

## Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan African countries face significant challenges related to water security, clean energy access, and climate vulnerability. According to World Bank data (2024), only 32.4% of the population has access to safely managed drinking water [Source: World Bank Open Data], while just 53.3% have access to electricity [Source: World Bank Open Data]. These disparities underscore a critical need for improved water management practices and increased investment in renewable energy projects.

Recent data indicates a substantial gap between Sub-Saharan Africa's energy consumption patterns and global trends. ## Policy Recommendations

To address these challenges effectively, we recommend the following policy interventions:

1. **Enhance Water Security**: Invest in rainwater harvesting systems and desalination technologies to increase access to safe drinking water. Implement policies promoting sustainable agriculture practices that reduce water usage while maintaining productivity.
2. **Promote Clean Energy Access**: Increase investment in renewable energy projects such as solar, wind, and hydroelectric power. Establish supportive regulatory frameworks to encourage private sector participation and ensure equitable distribution of benefits.
3. **Strengthen Climate Resilience**: Develop early warning systems for climate-related disasters like floods and droughts. Prioritize the development of resilient infrastructure that can withstand extreme weather events, such as flood-resistant buildings and water storage facilities.

## Implementation Considerations

Implementing these recommendations will require a coordinated effort between national governments, international partners, and local communities. Key considerations include:

* **Resource Allocation**: Secure adequate funding through grants, loans, and public-private partnerships.
* **Capacity Building**: Train and equip local authorities with the necessary skills to implement and monitor water security and climate resilience projects effectively.
* **Community Engagement**: Engage community members in decision-making processes related to water management and energy access initiatives.

## Conclusion

By focusing on enhancing water security, promoting clean energy access, and strengthening climate resilience, Sub-Saharan Africa can pave the way for sustainable development. These efforts not only address immediate challenges but also lay a foundation for future growth and prosperity. Policymakers should prioritize these initiatives to ensure that Sub-Saharan African countries remain resilient in the face of global environmental changes.
