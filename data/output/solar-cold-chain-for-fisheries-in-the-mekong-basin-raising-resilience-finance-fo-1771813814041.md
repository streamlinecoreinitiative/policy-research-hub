# solar cold-chain for fisheries in the Mekong basin: raising resilience finance for communities

**Bridging Water Security, Clean Energy, and Climate Adaptation Gaps in Sub-Saharan Africa**

### Executive Summary

To address the pressing challenges of water scarcity and energy poverty in Sub-Saharan Africa, we recommend implementing policies that promote renewable energy adoption, improve water management systems, and enhance climate resilience strategies. Specifically, governments should invest in solar-powered desalination plants and small-scale hydroelectric projects to provide clean water and electricity for communities. Additionally, community-managed rainwater harvesting systems and drip irrigation techniques can be implemented to reduce reliance on scarce surface water sources.

### Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa faces severe water scarcity and energy poverty, with only 53.3% of the population having access to reliable electricity [Source: World Bank, 2023]. Water security remains a significant issue, as just 32.4% of the region's population has access to safely managed drinking water [Source: World Bank, 2024]. The situation is exacerbated by climate change, leading to more frequent droughts and floods that further strain existing infrastructure.

### Analysis

The lack of clean water and electricity in Sub-Saharan Africa contributes significantly to health risks such as waterborne diseases. According to the UN-Water/WHO [Source: 2022], 2.2 billion people globally are lacking safe water, with a majority residing in developing regions like Sub-Saharan Africa. Moreover, only 73.7% of the world population has access to safely managed drinking water sources [Source: World Bank, 2024].

The region's reliance on fossil fuels for electricity generation and energy consumption poses high greenhouse gas emissions, contributing to climate change impacts such as increased temperature fluctuations and extreme weather events. This exacerbates existing water scarcity issues by reducing surface water availability due to evaporation from dried-up rivers and lakes.

### Policy Recommendations

1. **Promote Renewable Energy Adoption:**
   - Encourage solar-powered desalination plants in coastal areas where access to fresh water is limited.
   - Invest in small-scale hydroelectric projects that can harness renewable energy while providing electricity and irrigation needs.
2. **Enhance Water Management Systems:**
   - Implement community-managed rainwater harvesting systems, particularly in arid regions, to reduce reliance on scarce surface water sources.
   - Promote the use of drip irrigation techniques for agriculture to improve water efficiency and maintain soil moisture levels during dry periods.
3. **Climate Resilience Strategies:**
   - Develop early warning systems for droughts and floods using satellite imagery and climate models to forecast potential impacts.
   - Implement nature-based solutions such as afforestation projects in flood-prone areas to enhance natural water storage and reduce runoff into rivers and streams.

### Implementation Considerations

Implementing these policies requires substantial financial investment, which can be sourced from international development partners. For example, the World Bank [Source: 2024] recommends allocating at least 1% of GDP for climate-resilient infrastructure projects in Sub-Saharan Africa. Additionally, governments should collaborate with local NGOs and community organizations to ensure that interventions are tailored to specific regional needs.

### Conclusion

Addressing water security, clean energy access, and climate adaptation in Sub-Saharan Africa is essential for sustainable development. By implementing the recommended policies and strategies, we can significantly improve the lives of millions living in poverty while reducing environmental degradation and enhancing resilience against future shocks. Collaboration between governments, international organizations, and local communities will be key to realizing these goals.
