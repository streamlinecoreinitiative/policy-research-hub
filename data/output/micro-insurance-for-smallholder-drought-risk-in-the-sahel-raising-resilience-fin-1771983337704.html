    Context & Challenge

    Sub-Saharan Africa is one of the most vulnerable regions globally due to its high population density, low per capita income, and limited access to basic services such as clean water and reliable energy (World Bank Open Data, 2023). The region's rapid urbanization and climate change are intensifying existing challenges. Without urgent interventions, these issues will only worsen, leading to increased poverty, health risks, and economic instability.
