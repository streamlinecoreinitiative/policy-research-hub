**Executive Summary**

The Indus watershed is a critical water system in South Asia, supporting over 180 million people across four countries: India, Pakistan, China, and Afghanistan. The region faces significant environmental challenges, including landslides triggered by climate change, which pose a substantial threat to human settlements, infrastructure, and agricultural productivity. This brief explores nature-based solutions for landslide risk in the Indus watershed, focusing on integrating agroforestry and micro-hydro systems to enhance water security and promote clean energy.

The Indus watershed is prone to landslides due to its unique geology, climate, and land-use patterns.
