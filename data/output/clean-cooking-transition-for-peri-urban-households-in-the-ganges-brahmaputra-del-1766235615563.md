### Executive Summary

The Ganges-Brahmaputra delta faces significant environmental challenges due to climate change, affecting millions of peri-urban households. This brief explores the transition to clean cooking practices as a crucial component of resilience finance in these communities.

### Environmental Resilience in Lower-Income Countries: Water Security, Clean Energy, and Climate Adaptation

#### Overview

Climate change has severe implications for lower-income countries, particularly those with limited access to clean energy and water resources. The Ganges-Brahmaputra delta is a prime example of this phenomenon, where rising temperatures and altered precipitation patterns threaten the livelihoods of millions.

*   **Water Scarcity:** Erratic rainfall patterns and increased evaporation due to warmer temperatures lead to severe water scarcity.
*   **Food Security:** Reduced crop yields and decreased agricultural productivity compromise food security for peri-urban households.
*   **Economic Stability:** Climate-related shocks undermine economic stability, exacerbating poverty and inequality.

#### Current Irrigation Practices and Challenges

Existing irrigation systems in the Ganges-Brahmaputra delta face significant challenges:

*   **Boreholes and Canals:** These traditional methods are often inefficient, prone to water loss through evaporation and seepage.
*   **Diesel Generators:** Relying on diesel generators for energy increases costs and environmental degradation.
