# Strengthening Water Security, Clean Energy Access, and Climate Resilience in Lower-Income Countries

**Strengthening Water Security, Clean Energy Access, and Climate Resilience in Lower-Income Countries**

## Executive Summary

Lower-income countries face significant challenges related to access to basic services such as safe drinking water and reliable electricity. To address these gaps, we recommend investing in clean energy expansion, improving water security, and climate adaptation strategies.

* Expanding access to clean energy by increasing renewable energy consumption to at least 25% by 2030, with a focus on Sub-Saharan Africa where electricity access remains low.
* Improving water security through large-scale water storage facilities and rainwater harvesting systems, targeting areas with the highest need for improved sanitation infrastructure.

## Context & Challenge

Lower-income countries are disproportionately affected by water scarcity, electricity shortages, and climate change impacts. According to World Bank data (World Bank Open Data), in 2024, Sub-Saharan Africa had a significantly lower access rate to safely managed drinking water (32.4%) compared to the global average (73.7%) [1]. Similarly, the region lags behind in electricity access, with only 53.3% of the population having access to electricity, compared to the global average of 91.6% [2].

Climate change further exacerbates these challenges by increasing the frequency and intensity of extreme weather events, leading to disruptions in water supplies and energy production. The World Bank reports that approximately 2.2 billion people lack access to safe water globally (UN-Water/WHO), with Sub-Saharan Africa being one of the regions most impacted [3].

## Analysis

The current situation highlights the urgent need for targeted interventions focused on clean energy expansion, improved water security, and climate adaptation strategies.

## Policy Recommendations

To enhance resilience in lower-income countries:

1. **Expand Access to Clean Energy**: Invest in solar, wind, and geothermal projects to increase renewable energy consumption to at least 25% by 2030. Prioritize these initiatives in Sub-Saharan Africa where electricity access remains low.
2. **Improve Water Security**: Implement large-scale water storage facilities (dams, reservoirs) and promote rainwater harvesting systems. Target areas with the highest need for improved sanitation infrastructure to reduce contamination risks.
3. **Enhance Climate Adaptation Measures**: Develop early warning systems for climate-related disasters such as floods and droughts. Support indigenous knowledge in disaster risk reduction strategies.

## Implementation Considerations

To ensure successful implementation:

* Allocate dedicated funding streams from international development banks, multilateral funds, and bilateral aid programs to support these initiatives.
* Collaborate with local governments and civil society organizations to tailor solutions that align with community needs and capacities.
* Conduct phased rollouts starting in pilot projects before scaling up efforts.

## Conclusion

Addressing water security, clean energy access, and climate adaptation is essential for building resilient societies. By prioritizing targeted investments and collaborative approaches, lower-income countries can achieve significant progress towards sustainable development goals. Immediate action is required to mitigate the adverse effects of climate change and improve living standards for millions affected by these challenges.
