# Water Loss Reduction in Secondary Cities in the Mekong Basin: Enhancing Climate Resilience for Food Security

## Executive Summary

The Mekong Basin is a critical agricultural region supporting over 60 million people through its rich water resources. Climate change has significantly impacted this region, leading to increased frequency and severity of droughts. This brief explores the integration of agroforestry with micro-hydro systems as a sustainable solution for enhancing water security and renewable energy use in secondary cities within the Mekong Basin.

## Introduction to Water Scarcity in the Mekong Basin

The Mekong Basin is experiencing severe water scarcity due to climate change, including erratic rainfall patterns and increased evaporation from its vast wetlands. This has significant implications for agriculture, which accounts for a substantial portion of the region's economy. Smallholder farmers are particularly vulnerable to droughts.

## Current Irrigation Practices and Challenges

Traditional irrigation methods in the Mekong Basin include boreholes, canals, and gravity-fed systems. However, these methods are often energy-intensive and costly, requiring significant fuel consumption for pumping water from wells or using diesel generators for power. Water loss through evaporation and seepage is a major concern.

## Micro-Hydro Systems for Irrigation Efficiency

Micro-hydro technology involves the construction of small-scale hydroelectric systems, which can provide a reliable and renewable source of energy for irrigation. These systems are particularly suitable for secondary cities within the Mekong Basin, where access to electricity is often limited.

## Integration of Agroforestry and Micro-Hydro in Climate-Resilient Water Supply

Pilot projects have demonstrated the effectiveness of integrating agroforestry with micro-hydro systems for climate-resilient irrigation. These integrated systems can provide a reliable source of water while reducing energy consumption and greenhouse gas emissions.

## Policy Recommendations

To support the adoption of agroforestry-based micro-hydro systems for climate-resilient irrigation in secondary cities within the Mekong Basin:

1. **Invest in capacity building**: Provide training and technical assistance to farmers, extension agents, and local government officials on integrated agroforestry-micro-hydro systems.
2. **Support policy development**: Encourage governments to develop policies and regulations that support the adoption of climate-resilient irrigation technologies.
3. **Foster public-private partnerships**: Collaborate with private sector companies to finance and implement large-scale projects.

## Closing Outlook

The integration of agroforestry with micro-hydro systems offers a promising solution for enhancing water security and renewable energy use in secondary cities within the Mekong Basin. By addressing the challenges posed by climate change, this approach can help stabilize food security and improve the livelihoods of smallholder farmers.
