# Climate-Resilient School Feeding Supply Chains in the Ganges-Brahmaputra Delta: Supporting Adaptation Targets in NDCs

## Executive Summary

The Ganges-Brahmaputra delta is highly vulnerable to climate change impacts, threatening food security, economic stability, and human well-being. This brief highlights the importance of integrating water security, clean energy, and climate adaptation in school feeding supply chains to support Nationally Determined Contributions (NDCs) targets.

## Climate Change Impacts on Water Scarcity and Food Security

Climate change is exacerbating water scarcity in the Ganges-Brahmaputra delta. Rising temperatures increase evaporation rates, while changing precipitation patterns lead to more frequent droughts and floods. This has severe consequences for agriculture, with reduced crop yields and decreased food security.

## Current Irrigation Practices and Challenges

Existing irrigation systems face significant challenges:

* Boreholes and canals suffer from high water loss due to evaporation and seepage.
* Diesel generators are often used for pumping, contributing to greenhouse gas emissions.
* Limited access to electricity and energy infrastructure hinders the adoption of efficient irrigation systems.

## Agroforestry-Based Micro-Hydro Systems: A Climate-Resilient Solution

Agroforestry-based micro-hydro systems offer a promising solution:

* Agroforestry improves soil health, biodiversity, and carbon sequestration.
* Case studies demonstrate improved water retention and reduced erosion (e.g., [Case Study 1], [Case Study 2]).
* Micro-hydro technology reduces reliance on diesel generators.

## Integration of Agroforestry and Micro-Hydro in Climate-Resilient Water Supply

Pilot projects showcase the combined use of agroforestry and micro-hydro:

* Economic analysis highlights cost-effectiveness.
* Integrated systems can provide reliable water supply, reduce energy costs, and promote sustainable agriculture practices.

## Policy Recommendations and Future Research Directions

To support adoption at scale:

1. **Policy Support:** Governments should provide incentives for the adoption of agroforestry-based micro-hydro systems in school feeding supply chains.
2. **Capacity Building:** Training programs should focus on integrated water management, energy efficiency, and climate-resilient agriculture practices.
3. **Research and Development:** Further research is needed to assess the economic impact, community engagement, and scalability of these solutions.

## Closing Outlook

Climate change poses significant challenges to food security, economic stability, and human well-being in the Ganges-Brahmaputra delta. Integrating water security, clean energy, and climate adaptation in school feeding supply chains can support NDCs targets and promote sustainable development.
