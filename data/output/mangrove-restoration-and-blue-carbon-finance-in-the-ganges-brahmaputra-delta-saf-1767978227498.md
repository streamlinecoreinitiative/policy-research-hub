# Mangrove Restoration and Blue Carbon Finance in the Ganges-Brahmaputra Delta: Safeguarding Freshwater Ecosystems

## Executive Summary

The Ganges-Brahmaputra delta is one of the most densely populated and ecologically sensitive regions globally, facing severe environmental challenges due to climate change. This brief highlights the importance of mangrove restoration and blue carbon finance in safeguarding freshwater ecosystems in this region.

## Current Challenges Faced by Farmers and Communities

The Ganges-Brahmaputra delta is prone to frequent flooding, droughts, and saltwater intrusion, affecting the livelihoods of millions of people. The region's agricultural sector relies heavily on traditional irrigation systems, which are inefficient and unsustainable.

## Agroforestry-Based Micro-Hydro Systems for Climate-Resilient Irrigation

Agroforestry practices can improve water retention and recharge through increased vegetation cover, enhancing ecosystem services such as pollination, pest control, and carbon sequestration. Integrating micro-hydro systems with agroforestry can reduce water usage compared to traditional methods.

### Benefits of Agroforestry-Based Micro-Hydro Systems

*   Improved agricultural productivity due to better soil health and water management
*   Reduced vulnerability to climate variability
*   Enhanced ecosystem services such as pollination, pest control, and carbon sequestration
*   Cost savings through reduced energy consumption
*   Increased community empowerment through local ownership and maintenance
