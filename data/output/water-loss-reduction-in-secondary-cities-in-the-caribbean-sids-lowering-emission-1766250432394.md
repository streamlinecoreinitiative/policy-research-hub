# Water Loss Reduction in Secondary Cities in the Caribbean SIDS: Lowering Emissions from Critical Sectors

## Executive Summary

The Caribbean Small Island Developing States (SIDS) are highly vulnerable to climate change, facing significant challenges related to water scarcity and energy insecurity. This brief highlights the importance of reducing water loss in secondary cities in the Caribbean SIDS, particularly in critical sectors such as agriculture, industry, and municipal services.

## Introduction to Water Scarcity in the Caribbean SIDS

The Caribbean SIDS are characterized by limited freshwater resources, with many islands facing severe water scarcity due to climate change. Erratic rainfall patterns, increased evaporation rates, and rising sea levels exacerbate these challenges, threatening food security, economic stability, and human well-being.

### Current Irrigation Practices and Challenges

Existing irrigation systems in the Caribbean SIDS are often inefficient, relying on diesel generators or fossil fuels, which contribute to greenhouse gas emissions. Water loss through evaporation and seepage is significant, with estimates suggesting that up to 30% of water is lost due to inefficiencies [verify].

### Micro-Hydro Systems for Irrigation Efficiency

Micro-hydro technology offers a sustainable solution to irrigation needs in the Caribbean SIDS. These small-scale hydroelectric systems harness the power of water flow to generate electricity, reducing reliance on diesel generators and lowering greenhouse gas emissions.

## Integration of Agroforestry and Micro-Hydro in Climate-Resilient Water Supply

Pilot projects have demonstrated the effectiveness of combining agroforestry with micro-hydro systems. These integrated approaches not only enhance water security but also contribute to renewable energy adoption, reducing dependence on fossil fuels and lowering emissions.
