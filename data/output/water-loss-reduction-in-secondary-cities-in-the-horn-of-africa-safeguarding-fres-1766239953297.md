**Water Loss Reduction in Secondary Cities in the Horn of Africa: Safeguarding Freshwater Ecosystems**

**Executive Summary**

The Horn of Africa region is characterized by severe water scarcity, exacerbated by climate change and erratic rainfall patterns. This brief focuses on secondary cities in lower-income countries within this region, exploring integrated solutions to address water security challenges.

**I. Introduction to Water Scarcity in Secondary Cities**

The Horn of Africa is one of the most water-scarce regions globally. Climate change has led to increased frequency and severity of droughts, further exacerbating water scarcity challenges. In secondary cities within this region, existing irrigation systems often rely on boreholes or canals, resulting in significant water losses due to evaporation and seepage.

**II. Current Irrigation Practices and Challenges**

Existing irrigation systems in secondary cities within the Horn of Africa face significant challenges, including:

* Inefficient use of water resources
* High energy costs due to reliance on diesel generators
* Limited scalability and adaptability to changing climate conditions

These systems frequently neglect the importance of soil health, biodiversity, and carbon sequestration – essential components for long-term environmental resilience.
