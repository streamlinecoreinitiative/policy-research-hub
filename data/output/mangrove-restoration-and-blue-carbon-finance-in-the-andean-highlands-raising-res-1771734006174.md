# Enhancing Water Security, Clean Energy Access, and Climate Resilience in Sub-Saharan Africa

## Executive Summary

Sub-Saharan Africa faces significant challenges in water security, clean energy access, and climate resilience. With only 32.4% of the region's population having access to safely managed drinking water [1], and a mere 53.3% accessing electricity [2], urgent action is needed to improve these critical areas. This policy brief recommends implementing policies for universal access to clean water, expanding solar power generation, and promoting climate-smart agricultural practices.

## Context & Challenge

The region suffers from severe water scarcity, with only 32.4% of Sub-Saharan Africa's population having access to safely managed drinking water [1]. Climate change intensifies these challenges, leading to more frequent and intense natural disasters, further straining already limited resources. The global population lacking safe water is approximately 2.2 billion people [3], and SDG 6 progress shows only 74% global coverage of safely managed water [4].

## Analysis

Data points highlight the urgency of these issues:

* **Access to safely managed drinking water (%) - Sub-Saharan Africa (2024)**: 32.4 [1]
* **Access to electricity (%) - Sub-Saharan Africa (2023)**: 53.3 [2]

Water scarcity affects agricultural productivity, leading to food insecurity and economic instability. Limited access to clean energy exacerbates health risks from indoor air pollution due to traditional cooking methods. Climate change-induced droughts and floods threaten water supplies and infrastructure.

## Policy Recommendations

1. **Universal Access to Clean Water**: Invest in large-scale filtration plants, provide subsidies for household water filters, and enhance community-led watershed management projects.
2. **Renewable Energy Expansion**: Develop solar power stations in rural areas, subsidize the installation of solar panels, and mandate renewable energy targets for utilities.
3. **Climate-Smart Agriculture**: Promote drought-resistant crop varieties, develop irrigation systems that use less water, and provide training on sustainable farming practices.
