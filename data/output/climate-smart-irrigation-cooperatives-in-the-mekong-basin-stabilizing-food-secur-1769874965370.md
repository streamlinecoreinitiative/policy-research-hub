# climate-smart irrigation cooperatives in the Mekong basin: stabilizing food security during droughts

**Ensuring Water Security, Clean Energy, and Climate Adaptation in Sub-Saharan Africa: A Pathway to Resilience**

**Executive Summary**

Sub-Saharan Africa faces critical challenges related to water security, clean energy access, and climate adaptation. With only 32.4% of the region's population having access to safely managed drinking water (World Bank, 2024) [1] and less than half having access to electricity (53.3%, World Bank, 2020), there is an urgent need for targeted interventions that address these critical issues while also incorporating renewable energy sources.

**Context & Challenge**

Sub-Saharan Africa is home to a significant portion of the world's population lacking access to safe drinking water and electricity (World Bank, 2024). The region faces severe environmental challenges exacerbated by climate change, including droughts, floods, and other extreme weather events. These challenges threaten food security, health outcomes, and economic stability in Sub-Saharan Africa, necessitating immediate action.

**Analysis**

* **Evidence on Water Security**: Only 32.4% of the sub-region's population has access to safely managed drinking water (World Bank, 2024) [1]. This disparity is more pronounced in rural areas where access drops below 15%. The lack of safe drinking water leads to increased disease incidence and mortality rates.
* **Evidence on Clean Energy Access**: While Sub-Saharan Africa's renewable energy consumption stands at 19.7% (World Bank, 2020), the regionâ€™s electricity access rate is only 53.3%, with Sub-Saharan Africa lagging behind other regions like East Asia and Pacific (68.7%) and South Asia (46.2%). The majority of rural populations lack reliable power supply.
* **Climate Adaptation**: Climate change impacts, including erratic rainfall patterns and rising temperatures, compound water scarcity issues. These environmental stresses threaten food security and increase the vulnerability of communities to climate-induced disasters.

**Policy Recommendations**

1. **Prioritize Renewable Energy Investments**: Invest in solar, wind, and hydroelectric projects to enhance energy access for rural populations. Develop robust grid infrastructure to ensure reliable power supply.
2. **Enhance Public-Private Partnerships (PPP)**: Encourage PPPs between governments, NGOs, and private sector entities to fund clean energy projects, particularly those that provide long-term solutions like solar-powered water pumps and off-grid renewable energy systems.
3. **Promote Climate-Smart Agriculture**: Implement agroforestry value chains for cash crops in areas with sustainable water resources, ensuring resilient food production practices. Provide training on climate-resilient farming techniques to farmers.

**Conclusion**

Sub-Saharan Africa urgently needs targeted interventions that combine water security, clean energy access, and climate adaptation strategies to build resilient communities. By prioritizing renewable energy investments, enhancing PPPs, and promoting climate-smart agriculture, we can make significant progress towards achieving the Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs) in this region.
