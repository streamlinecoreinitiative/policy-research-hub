This policy intervention aims to reduce the urban heat island effect by installing reflective and green roofs, thereby decreasing temperatures and improving air quality. ### Background Information

**Urban Heat Island Effect** (EPA)
The urban heat island effect occurs when built-up areas absorb and retain more heat than surrounding rural areas due to the concentration of pavement, buildings, and other infrastructure.
Source: https://www.epa.gov/heat-islands

**Heatwave Impacts** (WHO)
Heatwaves can have devastating consequences for human health, particularly in vulnerable populations such as the elderly, young children, and those with pre-existing medical conditions.
Source: https://www.who.int/news-room/fact-sheets/detail/heat-and-health
