# Agroforestry-Based Micro-Hydro Systems for Climate-Resilient Irrigation in the Mesoamerican Highlands

## Executive Summary

The Mesoamerican Highlands face severe water scarcity and poverty, exacerbated by climate change impacts on agriculture. This brief proposes an integrated solution to address these challenges through agroforestry-based micro-hydro systems for climate-resilient irrigation.

## Introduction to Water Scarcity in the Mesoamerican Highlands

Climate change has increased drought frequency and severity in the Mesoamerican region, affecting agriculture heavily reliant on irrigation. Current irrigation practices suffer from high water loss percentages, exacerbating water scarcity and perpetuating poverty among rural communities.

## Agroforestry-Based Micro-Hydro Systems for Climate-Resilient Irrigation

Agroforestry has been demonstrated to improve soil health, biodiversity, and carbon sequestration in various regions. Integrating agroforestry with micro-hydro technology can increase water retention, reduce erosion and runoff, and generate renewable energy.

### Benefits of Agroforestry:

*   Improved soil health through organic matter addition
*   Increased biodiversity through habitat creation for wildlife
*   Enhanced carbon sequestration through tree cover expansion

### Micro-Hydro Systems:

*   Reduced water loss due to efficient use of hydroelectric power
*   Increased energy efficiency through reduced reliance on diesel generators
*   Potential for scaling up and replication in other regions

## Integration of Agroforestry and Micro-Hydro in Climate-Resilient Water Supply

Pilot projects have showcased the combined use of agroforestry and micro-hydro, demonstrating significant water savings and increased energy efficiency. Economic analysis highlights the cost-effectiveness of this approach.
