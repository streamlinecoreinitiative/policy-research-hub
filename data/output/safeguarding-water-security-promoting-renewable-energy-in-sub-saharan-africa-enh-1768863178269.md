**Safeguarding Water Security, Promoting Renewable Energy in Sub-Saharan Africa: Enhancing Climate Resilience**

## Executive Summary

To address the pressing challenges of water security and access to clean energy in Sub-Saharan Africa, we recommend investing $1 billion over the next five years in large-scale water supply projects and renewable energy infrastructure. This investment will improve access to safely managed drinking water (currently at 32.4% [Source: World Bank Open Data]) and increase the share of renewable energy consumption (currently at 19% [Source: World Bank Open Data]). By prioritizing these initiatives, we can enhance climate resilience in the region and support sustainable development.

## Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa is one of the most vulnerable regions to climate change, with significant gaps in access to safe drinking water (32.4% [Source: World Bank Open Data]) and clean energy (53.3% [Source: World Bank Open Data]). Climate change exacerbates these challenges, highlighting the urgent need for resilient infrastructure and sustainable development strategies. The region is home to 2.2 billion people lacking safe water sources globally [Source: UN-Water/WHO], and climate-related impacts such as droughts and floods have intensified water scarcity issues and disrupted traditional livelihoods.

## Analysis

### Water Security

* Access to safely managed drinking water remains low at 32.4% [Source: World Bank Open Data], impacting public health and socio-economic well-being.
* Climate change further compounds these issues, with droughts and floods disrupting water supply systems [Source: UN-Water/WHO].

### Clean Energy

* Despite global trends towards renewable energy, Sub-Saharan Africa lags behind with only 53.3% of the population having access to electricity [Source: World Bank Open Data].
* This disparity is particularly acute in rural areas where infrastructure constraints exacerbate existing challenges.

### Climate Adaptation

* Climate change impacts have intensified water scarcity issues and disrupted traditional livelihoods.
* Strengthening policy frameworks and promoting climate-resilient water management practices are crucial for enhancing resilience in the region [Source: UN SDG Report 2023].

## Policy Recommendations

1. **Increase Investments in Water Infrastructure**:
	* Implement large-scale water supply projects, including rainwater harvesting systems and desalination plants (Source: UN-Water/WHO).
	* Invest in wastewater treatment facilities to improve water quality (Source: World Bank).
2. **Promote Renewable Energy Sources**:
	* Encourage solar energy through subsidies for households and communities (Source: International Energy Agency).
	* Develop decentralized renewable energy systems such as mini-grids and off-grid solutions (Source: World Bank).
3. **Strengthen Policy Frameworks**:
	* Integrate climate-resilient water management practices into national development plans (Source: UN SDG Report 2023).
	* Establish cross-sectoral partnerships to address water-energy nexus challenges (Source: International Renewable Energy Agency).

## Implementation Considerations

* **Funding**: Secure additional funding through international and domestic sources, leveraging existing climate funds and donor support.
* **Capacity Building**: Train local communities in sustainable water management practices and renewable energy technologies.

## Conclusion

Sub-Saharan Africa's vulnerability to climate change necessitates immediate action on improving water security and promoting clean energy. By implementing evidence-based policies, we can build a more resilient future for millions of people affected by limited access to safe drinking water and reliable electricity. Let us prioritize these initiatives to ensure sustainable development in the region.
