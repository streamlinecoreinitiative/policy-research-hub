**Executive Summary**

The Sudano-Sahelian belt is one of the most water-scarce regions globally, exacerbating climate change impacts on agriculture and human settlements. This brief presents a comprehensive approach to addressing severe water scarcity through integrated agroforestry and micro-hydro systems, focusing on the Ethiopian Highlands as a case study.

**Climate Change Impacts and Water Scarcity in the Sudano-Sahelian Belt**

The Sudano-Sahelian belt is characterized by high precipitation variability (annual range: 600-1400 mm), with average annual rainfall ranging from 800-1200 mm. Climate change projections indicate increased frequency and severity of droughts, further exacerbating water scarcity.
