# mangrove restoration and blue carbon finance in the Mekong basin: stabilizing food security during droughts

**Mangrove Restoration and Blue Carbon Finance in the Mekong Basin: Stabilizing Food Security During Droughts**

Executive Summary:

The Lower Mekong Basin is highly vulnerable to droughts that affect livelihoods, food security, and economic stability. This brief explores three key interventions to enhance environmental resilience in lower-income countries: climate-smart irrigation cooperatives, micro-insurance programs for drought risk mitigation, and agroforestry practices for climate resilience.

**Section 1: Introduction**

The Mekong Basin is a critical region for global food security, with millions of people relying on its agricultural productivity. However, the basin faces significant challenges due to climate change, including increased frequency and severity of droughts. These events have devastating impacts on local economies and communities, highlighting the need for innovative solutions to enhance environmental resilience.

**Section 2: Climate-Smart Irrigation Cooperatives**

Climate-smart irrigation cooperatives (CSICs) can play a crucial role in addressing water scarcity and energy challenges in the Sahel region. CSICs are community-led systems that promote efficient irrigation practices, reduce reliance on fossil fuels for energy, and enhance water security. Key benefits of CSICs include:

• Improved irrigation efficiency through precision agriculture techniques
• Reduced costs for farmers through shared resources and collective bargaining power
• Community collaboration and knowledge sharing among farmers

However, more data is needed to fully understand the potential impact of CSICs in the Sahel region. Specifically, we require:

• Average annual rainfall variability
• Current per capita water supply (liters/year)
• Cost breakdown for solar power versus traditional fuels like charcoal or wood
• Energy consumption data from existing irrigation systems in the region

**Section 3: Micro-Insurance Programs for Drought Risk Mitigation**

Micro-insurance schemes can help mitigate drought risks by providing financial protection at a low cost. The Lower Mekong Basin has seen significant benefits from micro-insurance programs, including:

• Financial protection for farmers during drought events
• Increased farm productivity due to reduced risk-taking behavior
• Economic stability for local communities

To further develop and scale up these initiatives, we need data on:

• Number of farmers participating in micro-insurance schemes
• Average payout amount per event
• Economic benefits (e.g., increased farm productivity) realized through insurance coverage

**Section 4: Agroforestry Practices for Climate Resilience**

Agroforestry practices can help restore degraded lands while providing multiple benefits such as improved water retention, enhanced biodiversity, and increased resilience against climate shocks. Key advantages of agroforestry include:

• Improved soil health through reduced erosion and increased organic matter content
• Enhanced water conservation through mulching and cover cropping methods
• Carbon sequestration through tree planting and soil enrichment

To better understand the impact of agroforestry in dryland regions, we require data on:

• Soil organic matter content before and after agroforestry intervention
• Reduction in water runoff or erosion rates compared to traditional farming practices
• Percentage of participating households experiencing improved crop yields due to agroforestry

**Actions/Recommendations:**

1. Conduct a comprehensive assessment of the Lower Mekong Basin's climate resilience needs, including drought risk analysis and economic impact assessments.
2. Develop and pilot micro-insurance programs for drought risk mitigation in collaboration with local communities and governments.
3. Scale up agroforestry practices through farmer training and extension services, focusing on dryland regions with high soil degradation risks.

**Closing Outlook:**

Enhancing environmental resilience in lower-income countries requires innovative solutions that address the interconnected challenges of water security, clean energy, and climate adaptation. By implementing climate-smart irrigation cooperatives, micro-insurance programs for drought risk mitigation, and agroforestry practices for climate resilience, we can stabilize food security during droughts and promote sustainable development in the Mekong Basin.
