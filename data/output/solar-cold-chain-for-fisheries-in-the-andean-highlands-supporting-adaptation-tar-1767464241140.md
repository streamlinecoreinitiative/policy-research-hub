**Solar Cold-Chain for Fisheries in the Andean Highlands: Supporting Adaptation Targets in NDCs**

**Executive Summary**

The Andean highlands are vulnerable to climate change impacts, including erratic rainfall patterns and increased temperature fluctuations. Small-scale fisheries in this region face significant challenges in maintaining water security and energy access, compromising their resilience and adaptation capacity. This brief proposes the integration of solar-powered cold-chain systems with existing agroforestry practices as a sustainable solution for supporting small-scale fisheries' adaptation targets.

**Section 1: Introduction to Water Scarcity and Energy Access Challenges**

Climate change is exacerbating water scarcity in the Andean highlands, affecting agriculture and fisheries. Erratic rainfall patterns lead to reduced water availability for crops and fish, while increased evaporation from wetlands further reduces water security. Traditional irrigation methods are energy-intensive and costly, placing additional economic burdens on small-scale farmers.

* **Climate Change Impacts:**
	+ Reduced water availability due to erratic rainfall patterns
	+ Increased evaporation from wetlands exacerbating water scarcity issues
* **Current Irrigation Practices and Challenges:**
	+ Energy-intensive traditional irrigation methods (e.g., diesel generators)
	+ High maintenance costs for manual labor and equipment
