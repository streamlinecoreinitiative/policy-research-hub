# Watershed Co-management with Indigenous Councils in the Sahel: Lowering Emissions from Critical Sectors

## Executive Summary

This brief highlights the importance of watershed co-management with indigenous councils in the Sahel region, focusing on lowering emissions from critical sectors such as water security and clean energy. The Sahel region is particularly vulnerable to climate change impacts, including droughts, desertification, and increased temperatures. By integrating traditional knowledge with modern technologies, communities can enhance their resilience to these changes while reducing greenhouse gas emissions.

## Main Sections

### 1. Introduction to Water Scarcity in the Sahel Region

The Sahel region is characterized by arid and semi-arid conditions, making water scarcity a significant challenge for local communities. Climate change exacerbates this issue through altered rainfall patterns, increased evaporation rates, and decreased groundwater recharge. These changes have severe implications for agriculture, food security, and human livelihoods.

*   **Climate Change Impacts:** Rising temperatures, altered precipitation patterns, and increased frequency of extreme weather events.
*   **Water Scarcity Consequences:**
    *   Reduced crop yields and lower agricultural productivity
    *   Decreased water availability for domestic use, leading to increased competition among users
    *   Negative impacts on biodiversity and ecosystem services

### 4. Micro-Hydro Systems for Efficient Irrigation

Micro-hydro systems harness the energy of small water flows to generate electricity, reducing reliance on diesel generators and increasing energy efficiency.

*   **Benefits of Micro-Hydro Systems:**
    *   Reduced greenhouse gas emissions from fossil fuel combustion
    *   Improved energy access for irrigation and other agricultural activities
    *   Enhanced economic viability through reduced energy costs
