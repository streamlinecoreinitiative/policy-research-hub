**Solar Cold-Chain for Fisheries in the Mekong Basin: Enhancing Resilience Finance through Renewable Energy**

### Executive Summary

The Mekong River basin supports over 60 million people who rely heavily on fisheries as a primary source of income and food security. Climate change poses significant threats to this sector, including increased water temperatures, reduced fish stocks, and altered migration patterns. This brief proposes integrating solar-powered cold-chain technology with existing fisheries management practices in the Mekong basin to enhance resilience finance for communities.

### Introduction to Water Scarcity in Lower-Income Countries

Climate change exacerbates water scarcity in lower-income countries, posing significant threats to food security, economic stability, and human well-being. In the Mekong basin, rising temperatures and altered precipitation patterns affect fisheries productivity, leading to reduced fish stocks and income opportunities for communities dependent on this sector.

### Solar Cold-Chain for Fisheries: A Climate-Resilient Solution
