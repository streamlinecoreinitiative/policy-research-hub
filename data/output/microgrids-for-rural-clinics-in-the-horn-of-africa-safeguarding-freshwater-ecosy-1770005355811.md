## Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa faces significant challenges in accessing safe drinking water and reliable electricity. According to World Bank data (2024), only 32.4% of the population has access to safely managed drinking water, while 53.3% have access to electricity (World Bank Open Data). These statistics underscore the urgent need for enhanced resilience measures. Climate change compounds these challenges by increasing frequency and intensity of extreme weather events, further straining already limited resources.

## Analysis

The region's reliance on traditional sources like groundwater and rainwater harvesting is insufficient given its climate variability and water scarcity issues. Access to safe drinking water remains a critical gap, with 2.2 billion people worldwide lacking access (UN-Water/WHO). In Sub-Saharan Africa, this translates into significant health risks from contaminated water supplies. Renewable energy consumption at just 19.7% in the region is insufficient for meeting growing demand and reducing carbon emissions (World Bank Open Data).

## Policy Recommendations

To address these challenges, policymakers should prioritize:

1. **Enhanced Water Security**: Invest in infrastructure like wells and pipelines to improve access to safe drinking water. Implement rainwater harvesting systems to reduce dependence on unreliable rainfall.

3. **Climate Adaptation Measures**: Develop early-warning systems for droughts and floods, supported by resilient transportation corridors. Integrate climate-resilient agricultural practices into farming communities.
