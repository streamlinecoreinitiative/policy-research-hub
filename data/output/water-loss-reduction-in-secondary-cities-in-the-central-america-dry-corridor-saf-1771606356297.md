## Executive Summary

Sub-Saharan Africa faces significant challenges in accessing safe drinking water and reliable electricity due to its resource constraints and climate variability. To address this crisis, we recommend prioritizing investments in water infrastructure, expanding clean energy initiatives, and promoting climate-resilient agricultural practices.

* Develop and implement large-scale water infrastructure projects such as dams, wells, and pipelines to improve access to safely managed drinking water.
* Encourage the adoption of renewable energy sources like solar, wind, and hydroelectric power to reduce reliance on fossil fuels and lower emissions.
* Promote agroforestry value chains that integrate climate-smart practices such as drought-resistant crops and improved irrigation systems.

These interventions are critical to improving living standards and ensuring long-term resilience in the region. We urge governments, international donors, and development partners to commit resources and support pilot projects that demonstrate success before scaling up nationwide.

## Policy Recommendations

1. **Investment in Water Infrastructure**:
	* Develop and implement large-scale water infrastructure projects such as dams, wells, and pipelines.
	* Evidence: Investing in these areas is crucial for improving access to safely managed drinking water (World Bank, 2024).
	* Costs and Potential Barriers: Estimates vary but generally range between $100-$500 million per project. Key barriers include funding gaps and community resistance.
2. **Promote Renewable Energy**:
	* Encourage the adoption of renewable energy sources like solar, wind, and hydroelectric power.
	* Evidence: The region's current renewables consumption is only [VERIFY] % (World Bank, 2020). Increasing this percentage can significantly reduce reliance on fossil fuels and lower emissions.
	* Costs and Potential Barriers: Initial investments are substantial but decrease with scale. Major challenges include regulatory hurdles, high upfront costs, and limited grid infrastructure.
3. **Climate-Resilient Agriculture**:
	* Promote agroforestry value chains that integrate climate-smart practices such as drought-resistant crops and improved irrigation systems.
	* Evidence: The region's agricultural sector is highly vulnerable to climate change impacts like erratic rainfall, increased pests, and rising temperatures (Food and Agriculture Organization of the United Nations, 2023).
	* Costs and Potential Barriers: Initial setup costs are high, but long-term savings on inputs and reduced crop losses make it economically viable. Resistance from traditional farming communities may be a challenge.
