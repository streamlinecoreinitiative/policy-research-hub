## Context & Challenge
The Central America dry corridor is one of the most vulnerable regions to climate change, with frequent droughts and extreme weather events impacting agriculture, water resources, and human settlements. The region's mangrove forests, which cover approximately 10% of the global total [Source: World Bank Open Data (2022)], play a critical role in maintaining coastal ecosystems and supporting livelihoods for over 1 million people [Source: FAO (2020)]. However, these forests are under threat due to deforestation, pollution, and climate change.
