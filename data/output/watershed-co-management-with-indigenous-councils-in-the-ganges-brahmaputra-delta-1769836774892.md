## Executive Summary

Sub-Saharan Africa faces significant challenges with water security, including limited access to safe drinking water and electricity. To address these issues, we recommend implementing climate-resilient water and energy solutions to enhance water security and promote sustainable development.

## Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa's population has limited access to basic needs such as clean water and reliable electricity. According to World Bank data, only 32.4% of the region's population has access to safely managed drinking water (World Bank Open Data, 2024). ## Policy Recommendations

We recommend the following interventions to enhance water security and promote sustainable development:

1. **Expand solar-powered water supply systems**: Invest in decentralized solar-powered water supply systems to increase access to safe drinking water for rural populations.
2. **Invest in microgrids and off-grid solar solutions**: Support the development of microgrids and off-grid solar solutions to improve electricity access in remote areas.
3. **Implement climate-resilient irrigation technologies**: Promote the adoption of climate-resilient irrigation technologies to maintain agricultural productivity during droughts and floods.

## Implementation Considerations

While initial investments are significant, they can be offset by cost savings from reduced reliance on diesel generators for microgrids. However, potential barriers include limited financial resources, lack of skilled labor, and political will may hinder implementation.

## Conclusion

Sub-Saharan Africa's water security challenges require urgent attention. We must prioritize climate-resilient water and energy solutions to ensure sustainable development outcomes. Governments, NGOs, and development partners must work together to implement these recommendations and safeguard freshwater security in the region.
