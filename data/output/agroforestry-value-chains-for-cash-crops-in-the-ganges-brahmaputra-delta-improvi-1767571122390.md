**Agroforestry-Based Micro-Hydro Systems for Climate-Resilient Irrigation in the Ethiopian Highlands**

**Executive Summary**

The Ethiopian Highlands face severe water scarcity due to climate change impacts, resulting in significant losses from traditional irrigation systems. This brief proposes integrating agroforestry with micro-hydro systems as a cost-effective and sustainable solution.

**Addressing Water Scarcity through Agroforestry and Micro-Hydro Systems**

### Introduction to Water Scarcity

Climate change has significantly impacted the Ethiopian Highlands, resulting in reduced rainfall and increased precipitation variability. The region's average annual rainfall is 800-1200 mm [verify], with an annual range of 600-1400 mm.

### Current Irrigation Practices and Challenges

Existing irrigation methods in the Ethiopian Highlands are energy-intensive and economically burdensome. Borehole pumps consume a significant amount of energy, while canal systems suffer from high water losses due to evaporation and seepage.
