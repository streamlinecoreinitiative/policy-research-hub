# micro-insurance for smallholder drought risk in the Pacific SIDS: stabilizing food security during droughts

**Enhancing Water Security, Clean Energy Access, and Climate Adaptation in Sub-Saharan Africa**

## Executive Summary

To address the significant challenges in water security, clean energy access, and climate adaptation in Sub-Saharan Africa, we recommend **expanding water infrastructure**, particularly through investments in water harvesting systems, desalination plants, and distribution networks. This is essential for improving access to safe drinking water, which currently stands at 32.4% (World Bank Open Data, 2024). We also highlight the need to **accelerate renewable energy deployment** by increasing investment in solar, wind, hydro, and geothermal energy projects. Furthermore, we emphasize the importance of **enhancing climate resilience programs**, including early warning systems, climate-resilient infrastructure, and community-based adaptation strategies.

## Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa faces critical gaps in water security, clean energy access, and climate adaptation. Despite progress in some areas, over 30% of the population lacks safe drinking water (World Bank Open Data, 2024), compared to 73.7% globally (World Bank Open Data). Electricity access is similarly low at 53.3%, with only 91.6% worldwide (World Bank Open Data). Renewable energy consumption stands at just 19.7% (World Bank Open Data), highlighting the need for strategic interventions.

## Analysis

Current data indicate that over two billion people worldwide lack safe drinking water (UN-Water/WHO, 2022), with Sub-Saharan Africa disproportionately affected. The region's limited access to electricity compounds these issues, affecting economic productivity and public health. Renewable energy consumption is also insufficient, particularly in the context of climate change impacts such as increasing temperatures and more frequent extreme weather events.

Climate adaptation measures are essential but often underfunded or poorly implemented. For instance, Sub-Saharan Africa has experienced rising temperatures and increased frequency of droughts and floods (NOAA Climate Data, 2023), necessitating robust strategies to mitigate these effects.

## Policy Recommendations

1. **Expand Water Infrastructure**: Invest in water harvesting systems, desalination plants, and distribution networks to improve access to safe drinking water.
2. **Accelerate Renewable Energy Deployment**: Increase investment in solar, wind, hydro, and geothermal energy projects.
3. **Enhance Climate Resilience Programs**: Implement early warning systems, climate-resilient infrastructure, and community-based adaptation strategies.

## Implementation Considerations

Implementing these recommendations requires careful planning to ensure sustainability and effectiveness. For instance, cost-effective water harvesting technologies like rainwater catchment systems should be prioritized. Additionally, partnerships between governments, NGOs, and the private sector are essential for scaling up renewable energy projects and climate adaptation initiatives.

Potential barriers include limited funding, technical expertise, and political will. Addressing these requires strategic planning, stakeholder engagement, and leveraging international support where necessary.

## Conclusion

Sub-Saharan Africa must prioritize water security, clean energy access, and climate adaptation to achieve sustainable development goals. By adopting evidence-based policies and robust implementation strategies, the region can significantly improve living conditions and foster economic growth while mitigating climate risks.
