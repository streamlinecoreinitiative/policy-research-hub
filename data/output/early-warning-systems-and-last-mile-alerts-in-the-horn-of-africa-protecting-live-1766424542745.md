**Early-Warning Systems and Last-Mile Alerts in the Horn of Africa: Protecting Livelihoods under Climate Stress**

**Executive Summary**

Climate change exacerbates water scarcity, food insecurity, and economic hardship in the Horn of Africa. This brief highlights the importance of early-warning systems and last-mile alerts to protect livelihoods under climate stress. We propose integrating agroforestry with micro-hydro systems as a cost-effective solution to address severe water scarcity.

**Introduction**

The Horn of Africa is characterized by erratic rainfall patterns, resulting in significant precipitation variability (precipitation data from NOAA Climate Dataset). Traditional irrigation systems are energy-intensive and economically burdensome, leading to water loss due to evaporation and seepage.
