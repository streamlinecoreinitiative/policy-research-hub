**Enhancing Environmental Resilience in Sub-Saharan Africa: Investing in Water Security, Clean Energy, and Climate Adaptation**

## Executive Summary

Sub-Saharan Africa faces significant challenges related to water security, clean energy access, and climate adaptation. With only 32.4% of the population having access to safely managed drinking water (World Bank Open Data), and less than half having access to electricity (World Bank Open Data), this region urgently needs targeted interventions to improve these critical areas. To address these challenges, we recommend investing in small-scale renewable energy projects, such as solar panels for households, promoting decentralized clean energy solutions (World Bank Open Data). Additionally, implementing water resource management programs like rainwater harvesting and borehole drilling can improve access to safe drinking water (UN-Water/WHO).

## Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa lags behind globally in access to safe drinking water and electricity. According to the World Bank Open Data, only 32.4% of the population has access to safely managed drinking water, far below the world average of 73.7% ([VERIFY] [Source: World Bank Open Data]). Renewable energy use is also low compared to the global average, with only 9.2% of total final energy consumption in Sub-Saharan Africa coming from renewable sources (World Bank Open Data). Climate change impacts are severe, including increased droughts and floods that threaten water security and agricultural productivity.

Climate change impacts:

* Frequent droughts and floods threaten water security and agricultural productivity
* UN-Water/WHO reports that 2.2 billion people globally lack access to safe drinking water (UN-Water/WHO)
* NOAA climate data indicates increased frequency and intensity of droughts and floods in the region (NOAA)

Economic implications:

* High costs of energy imports and lack of reliable power hinder economic growth and development

## Policy Recommendations

1. **Invest in small-scale renewable energy projects**: Promote decentralized clean energy solutions, such as solar panels for households, to improve access to electricity.
	* Target: 50% of rural communities with access to solar-powered electricity within the next 5 years
2. **Implement water resource management programs**: Implement rainwater harvesting and borehole drilling to improve access to safe drinking water.
	* Target: 75% of rural communities with access to safely managed drinking water within the next 10 years
3. **Build climate-resilient infrastructure**: Develop flood defenses, early warning systems, and drought-resistant crops to enhance resilience to climate-related shocks.

## Implementation Considerations

Address cost barriers through grants and subsidies for low-income households and communities. Engage local communities in planning and implementation to ensure sustainability. Collaborate with international partners, including NGOs and the private sector, to leverage additional resources.

## Conclusion

Immediate action is needed to enhance environmental resilience in Sub-Saharan Africa through investments in water security, clean energy, and climate adaptation. Stakeholders must work together to implement evidence-based solutions that are both feasible and cost-effective. By investing in small-scale renewable energy projects, implementing water resource management programs, and building climate-resilient infrastructure, we can improve access to safe drinking water, electricity, and reduce the impacts of climate-related shocks.
