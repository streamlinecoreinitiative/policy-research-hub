# Agroforestry-Based Micro-Hydro Systems for Climate-Resilient Irrigation in the Andean Highlands

## Executive Summary

Climate change exacerbates water scarcity in the Andean highlands, posing significant challenges to agriculture and food security. This project proposal outlines an innovative approach to address this issue by integrating agroforestry and micro-hydro systems for climate-resilient irrigation. The proposed solution combines the benefits of agroforestry for soil health, biodiversity, and carbon sequestration with the efficiency of micro-hydro technology for water supply.

## Introduction to Water Scarcity in the Andean Highlands

The Andean highlands are experiencing increasing water scarcity due to erratic rainfall patterns and changing temperature regimes. Climate change is projected to worsen these conditions (Source: [verify]). Irrigation systems currently in place, such as boreholes and canals, are often inefficient and prone to water loss through evaporation and seepage.

## Agroforestry-Based Micro-Hydro Systems for Climate-Resilient Water Supply

Agroforestry has been shown to improve soil health, biodiversity, and carbon sequestration (Case Study 1; Case Study 2). By integrating agroforestry with micro-hydro systems, we can reduce water loss through evaporation and seepage while increasing energy efficiency. Micro-hydro technology harnesses the power of small-scale hydroelectric systems to generate electricity.

## Policy Recommendations and Future Research Directions

To support adoption at scale, we recommend:

* Subsidies for agroforestry-based micro-hydro system installation
* Low-interest loans for farmers to invest in climate-resilient irrigation infrastructure
* Infrastructure development to facilitate access to markets and services for smallholder farmers

Further research is needed to:

* Validate the long-term sustainability of integrated agroforestry and micro-hydro systems
* Develop community engagement strategies to ensure equitable benefits sharing among stakeholders

## Actions/Recommendations List

1. Conduct a thorough assessment of existing irrigation systems in the Andean highlands.
2. Establish pilot projects demonstrating the combined use of agroforestry and micro-hydro systems.
3. Develop policy recommendations to support adoption at scale, including subsidies and low-interest loans.
4. Engage with local communities and stakeholders to ensure equitable benefits sharing.

## Closing Outlook

Addressing water scarcity in the Andean highlands requires innovative solutions that balance social, economic, and environmental considerations. By integrating agroforestry and micro-hydro systems, we can improve irrigation efficiency, reduce reliance on diesel generators, and enhance climate resilience.
