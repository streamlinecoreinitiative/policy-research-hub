Climate-Smart Irrigation Cooperatives in the Caribbean SIDS: Cutting Disaster Losses for Vulnerable Groups

Executive Summary

To address pressing water security challenges, we recommend establishing climate-smart irrigation cooperatives as a key strategy for vulnerable communities. These cooperatives will enhance water management and promote renewable energy adoption and climate adaptation measures.

Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa faces critical water security challenges exacerbated by climate change [1]. Only 32.4% of the region's population has access to safely managed drinking water [2], while renewable energy consumption remains low at 9.2% [3]. The urgent need for climate-resilient policies that integrate clean energy and enhance water management systems is undeniable.

Analysis

The disparity in access to safely managed drinking water between Sub-Saharan Africa (32.4%) and the global average (73.7%) is stark [2]. This gap is further exacerbated by the region's limited renewable energy consumption of 9.2% [3], underscoring the critical need for integrated solutions that address both water security and clean energy gaps.

Policy Recommendations

- Enhanced Water Security: Implement community-led watershed management programs to improve water supply reliability.

  • Promoting Renewable Energy: Encourage decentralized solar energy solutions such as off-grid solar home systems and pay-as-you-go models to increase access.
  • Climate Adaptation: Develop climate-resilient agricultural practices that integrate drought-resistant crop varieties and water-efficient irrigation systems.

    Implementation Considerations

    - Funding: Secure dedicated funding from international development partners like the Global Environment Facility (GEF) and bilateral donors.

  • Partnerships: Foster collaboration between government agencies, NGOs, and community-based organizations to ensure sustainability and effective implementation of policies and programs.
  • Capacity Building: Provide training and capacity-building for local stakeholders in areas like water resource management, renewable energy deployment, and climate adaptation strategies.

    Conclusion

    Sub-Saharan Africa requires urgent action on both water security and clean energy fronts. By implementing these recommendations, governments can significantly improve the resilience of their societies against climate change impacts.
