# Water Loss Reduction in Secondary Cities in the Central America Dry Corridor: Safeguarding Freshwater Ecosystems

## Executive Summary

The Central America dry corridor is experiencing severe water scarcity due to climate change, affecting secondary cities and their surrounding freshwater ecosystems. Traditional irrigation methods exacerbate economic challenges for smallholder farmers in vulnerable communities. This brief proposes integrating agroforestry with micro-hydro systems as a sustainable solution that enhances both water security and renewable energy use.

## Introduction to Water Scarcity

Climate change is impacting agriculture in the Central America dry corridor, leading to erratic rainfall patterns and increased evaporation from vast wetlands. Resilient irrigation methods are crucial for food security and economic stability. Existing irrigation systems suffer from water loss through evaporation and seepage, resulting in high costs.
