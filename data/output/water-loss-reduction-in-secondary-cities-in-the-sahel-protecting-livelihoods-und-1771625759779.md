# water loss reduction in secondary cities in the Sahel: protecting livelihoods under climate stress

**Ensuring Water Security, Clean Energy, and Climate Resilience in Sub-Saharan Africa: A Comprehensive Policy Blueprint**

## Executive Summary

This policy brief emphasizes the urgent need to address water security, clean energy access, and climate resilience in sub-Saharan Africa. With only 32.4% of the population having access to safely managed drinking water (World Bank Open Data) [1] and just 53.3% with electricity access (World Bank Open Data) [2], these issues are critical for sustainable development and poverty reduction. To address this challenge, we recommend implementing policies that focus on expanding clean energy access, improving water security, and enhancing climate resilience through nature-based solutions.

## Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa is one of the regions most vulnerable to climate change impacts (World Bank Open Data) [1], including droughts, floods, and extreme weather events. The region's limited infrastructure exacerbates these challenges. Addressing water security, clean energy access, and climate resilience is crucial for achieving sustainable development goals, particularly given low access rates.

## Analysis

### Water Security

Limited access to safe drinking water (32.4%) highlights the urgent need for improved sanitation facilities and infrastructure (World Bank Open Data) [1]. The global average for safely managed drinking water is 73.7% (World Bank Open Data) [3], emphasizing the significant gap in sub-Saharan Africa.

### Clean Energy Access

Only 53.3% of Sub-Saharan Africans have electricity access, with renewable energy consumption at a mere 19.7%, indicating significant room for improvement in meeting energy needs sustainably (World Bank Open Data) [2]. The global average for electricity access is 91.6% (World Bank Open Data) [4], further highlighting the disparity.

### Climate Resilience

Climate change impacts are becoming more pronounced, with natural disasters and extreme weather events threatening water security and energy infrastructure. Enhancing climate resilience through nature-based solutions is essential to mitigate these risks.
