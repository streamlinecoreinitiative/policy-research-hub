# Microgrids for Rural Clinics in the Caribbean SIDS: Improving Health Outcomes in Heatwaves

## Executive Summary

The Caribbean Small Island Developing States (SIDS) are vulnerable to climate change impacts, including increased frequency and severity of heatwaves. These events exacerbate existing health challenges, particularly in rural areas where access to healthcare services is limited. This brief explores the potential of microgrids for rural clinics in the Caribbean SIDS as a solution to improve health outcomes during heatwaves.

## Environmental Resilience in Lower-Income Countries: Water Security, Clean Energy, and Climate Adaptation

The Mesoamerican Highlands region shares similar climate-related challenges with the Caribbean SIDS. Agroforestry-based micro-hydro systems have been proposed as an integrated solution for addressing severe water scarcity and poverty through sustainable irrigation methods.

### Key Features of Agroforestry-Based Micro-Hydro Systems:

*   **Water Retention:** Agroforestry improves soil health, increasing water retention and reducing erosion.
*   **Energy Efficiency:** Micro-hydro technology reduces reliance on diesel generators and increases energy efficiency.
*   **Climate Resilience:** Agroforestry-based micro-hydro systems enhance climate resilience by promoting biodiversity and carbon sequestration.

### Integration of Agroforestry and Micro-Hydro in Climate-Resilient Water Supply

Pilot projects have demonstrated the combined use of agroforestry and micro-hydro, showcasing cost-effectiveness and potential for scaling up. Economic analysis highlights the importance of policy support for adoption at scale.

## Policy Recommendations and Future Research Directions

To promote widespread adoption of agroforestry-based micro-hydro systems:

*   **Policy Framework:** Develop a comprehensive policy framework to support integrated solutions.
*   **Capacity Building:** Provide training and capacity-building programs for local communities and stakeholders.
*   **Financial Incentives:** Offer financial incentives for the adoption of climate-resilient irrigation methods.

The Caribbean SIDS face significant challenges in addressing heatwaves and improving health outcomes. By promoting integrated solutions and policy support, we can enhance the resilience of rural clinics and communities in the Caribbean SIDS.
