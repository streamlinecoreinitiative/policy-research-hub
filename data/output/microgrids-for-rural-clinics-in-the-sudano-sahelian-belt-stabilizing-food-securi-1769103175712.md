**Bridging Water Security Gaps in Sub-Saharan Africa Through Renewable Energy and Climate Adaptation**

## Executive Summary

The region of Sub-Saharan Africa faces a pressing water crisis, with only 32.4% of its population having access to safely managed drinking water (World Bank Open Data). This stark contrast to the global average of 73.7% access (World Bank Open Data) underscores the need for urgent action. To bridge this gap, we recommend prioritizing renewable energy projects, promoting climate adaptation strategies, and enhancing water management systems.

## Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa struggles with inadequate infrastructure for clean drinking water supply, exacerbated by climate change impacts such as droughts and floods (World Bank Open Data). The limited electricity access in the region (53.3% of the population lacks access to electricity) hinders the development of renewable energy solutions critical for sustainable water management and climate resilience (World Bank Open Data).

## Analysis

The region's limited access to safe drinking water is a pressing issue, with only 1 in 4 people having reliable access to it (World Bank Open Data). Renewable energy consumption remains low at 9.2%, highlighting significant room for improvement (World Bank Open Data). This underutilization contributes to the high cost of implementing and maintaining renewable projects. Climate change worsens these challenges by causing more frequent droughts and floods, further straining water resources.
