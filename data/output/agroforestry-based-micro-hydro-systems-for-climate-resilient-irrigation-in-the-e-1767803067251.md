**Agroforestry-Based Micro-Hydro Systems for Climate-Resilient Irrigation in the Ethiopian Highlands**

Executive Summary
-----------------

The Ethiopian Highlands face severe water scarcity due to climate change impacts, exacerbated by inefficient traditional irrigation methods. This brief proposes integrating agroforestry with micro-hydro systems as a cost-effective and sustainable solution to address these challenges.

Main Sections
-------------

### 1. Introduction to Water Scarcity

Climate change has significantly impacted the Ethiopian Highlands, leading to increased precipitation variability and water scarcity. The average annual rainfall ranges from 800-1200 mm (NOAA climate dataset). Traditional irrigation methods are energy-intensive and inefficient, resulting in significant water loss through evaporation and seepage.

### 2. Current Irrigation Practices and Challenges

Existing irrigation practices in the Ethiopian Highlands rely heavily on traditional methods, which are energy-intensive and economically burdensome for farmers. Local surveys and case studies have shown that integrated agroforestry-micro-hydro systems can provide significant benefits to communities, including improved water security and increased crop yields.

Actions/Recommendations
------------------------

1.  **Policy Support:** Governments should provide incentives for farmers to adopt integrated agroforestry-micro-hydro systems.
2.  **Grid Integration:** Micro-hydro systems should be connected to the national grid to ensure reliable energy supply and maximize economic benefits.
3.  **Infrastructure Development:** Roads and storage facilities should be developed to support the implementation of micro-hydro systems.

Closing Outlook
--------------

The integration of agroforestry with micro-hydro systems offers a promising solution for addressing water scarcity in the Ethiopian Highlands. This approach not only provides climate-resilient irrigation but also contributes to broader sustainable development goals.
