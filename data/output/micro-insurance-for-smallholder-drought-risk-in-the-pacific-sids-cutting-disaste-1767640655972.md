# micro-insurance for smallholder drought risk in the Pacific SIDS: cutting disaster losses for vulnerable groups

**Micro-insurance for Smallholder Drought Risk in Pacific SIDS: Enhancing Climate Resilience**

**Executive Summary**

Climate change is exacerbating droughts in small island developing states (SIDS) of the Pacific, threatening food security and livelihoods of vulnerable communities. This brief proposes a micro-insurance scheme to mitigate drought-related losses for smallholder farmers in these regions.

**Section 1: Water Scarcity and Current Irrigation Practices**

Climate change is altering rainfall patterns in the Pacific SIDS, leading to water scarcity. Traditional irrigation methods are energy-intensive and costly, consuming significant amounts of energy [verify] per borehole pump and resulting in losses through evaporation and seepage.

**Section 2: Agroforestry's Role in Climate Resilience**

Agroforestry can improve soil health, biodiversity, and carbon sequestration. Studies have shown that integrating trees into agricultural landscapes can increase soil organic matter [verify], biodiversity [verify], and carbon sequestration [verify].

**Section 3: Micro-Hydro Systems for Irrigation Efficiency**

Micro-hydro systems provide sustainable and renewable energy, generating power ranging from 10-50 kW per system with an efficiency of 70-80%. Compared to traditional irrigation methods, micro-hydro systems offer significant cost savings.

**Recommendations and Actions**

1. **Develop a Micro-insurance Scheme**: Establish a micro-insurance program for smallholder farmers in Pacific SIDS, providing financial protection against drought-related losses.
2. **Promote Agroforestry-Micro-Hydro Integration**: Encourage the integration of agroforestry with micro-hydro systems to enhance irrigation efficiency and promote climate resilience.
3. **Invest in Climate-Resilient Infrastructure**: Support the development of climate-resilient infrastructure, including roads, storage facilities, and other essential infrastructure for sustainable agriculture practices.

**Closing Outlook**

The proposed micro-insurance scheme and agroforestry-micro-hydro integration can significantly reduce drought-related losses for smallholder farmers in Pacific SIDS. By promoting climate resilience and efficient irrigation practices, we can enhance food security and livelihoods of vulnerable communities, ultimately contributing to the achievement of Sustainable Development Goal 2 (Zero Hunger) and other related targets.

**Needed Evidence**

2. **Economic Analysis**: Economic analysis of traditional irrigation costs and micro-hydro systems.
3. **Socioeconomic Studies**: Local surveys and case studies assessing community acceptance and benefits derived from integrated agroforestry-micro-hydro systems.
