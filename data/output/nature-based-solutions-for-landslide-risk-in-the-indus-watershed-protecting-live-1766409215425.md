# nature-based solutions for landslide risk in the Indus watershed: protecting livelihoods under climate stress

**Nature-Based Solutions for Landslide Risk in the Indus Watershed: Protecting Livelihoods Under Climate Stress**

### Executive Summary

The Indus watershed faces significant climate-related challenges, including severe water scarcity and increased landslide risk. This brief explores nature-based solutions to mitigate these risks through integrated agroforestry and micro-hydro systems.

### Introduction to Water Scarcity and Landslide Risk in the Indus Watershed

Climate change has impacted the Indus watershed, leading to erratic rainfall patterns and temperature fluctuations. These changes have resulted in severe water scarcity, affecting agriculture and food security. Moreover, landslides have become a major concern due to heavy rainfall events.

### Nature-Based Solutions for Climate Resilience

#### Agroforestry's Role in Soil Health and Biodiversity

Agroforestry systems improve soil health through enhanced water retention and reduced erosion. By integrating trees into agricultural landscapes, farmers can increase crop yields while reducing the need for fertilizers and pesticides.

*   **Benefits of agroforestry:**
    *   Improved soil fertility
    *   Increased biodiversity
    *   Carbon sequestration

#### Micro-Hydro Systems for Efficient Irrigation

Micro-hydro technology offers a sustainable solution for irrigation, providing clean energy while reducing reliance on diesel generators. This approach minimizes water loss through evaporation and seepage.

*   **Benefits of micro-hydro systems:**
    *   Reduced energy costs
    *   Increased water security
    *   Enhanced energy efficiency

### Integration of Agroforestry and Micro-Hydro in Climate-Resilient Water Supply

Pilot projects have demonstrated the effectiveness of combining agroforestry and micro-hydro systems to develop climate-resilient irrigation methods. These integrated approaches enhance water security while reducing landslide risk.

*   **Benefits of integration:**
    *   Improved water retention
    *   Reduced erosion
    *   Enhanced energy efficiency

### Policy Recommendations

To support the adoption of nature-based solutions, policy recommendations include:

1.  **Subsidies and low-interest loans** to encourage farmers to adopt agroforestry-micro-hydro systems.
2.  **Infrastructure development**, including the construction of micro-hydro dams and agroforestry plantations.
3.  **Capacity building**, providing training and technical assistance to farmers and local communities.

### Future Research Directions

To further validate the effectiveness of integrated agroforestry and micro-hydro systems, research is needed in areas such as:

1.  **Long-term sustainability assessments** to evaluate the long-term impact of these solutions.
2.  **Community engagement strategies** to ensure effective participation and ownership among local communities.

### Closing Outlook

The Indus watershed faces significant climate-related challenges. By leveraging nature-based solutions through integrated agroforestry and micro-hydro systems, communities can develop climate-resilient irrigation methods, enhancing water security while reducing landslide risk. Policy support, capacity building, and further research are essential to scaling up these solutions and protecting livelihoods under climate stress.

### Actions/Recommendations:

1.  **Develop a comprehensive policy framework** to support the adoption of nature-based solutions.
2.  **Establish a training program** for farmers and local communities on agroforestry-micro-hydro systems.
3.  **Conduct long-term sustainability assessments** to evaluate the effectiveness of these integrated approaches.

This brief highlights the potential of nature-based solutions in addressing climate-related challenges in the Indus watershed. By working together, we can develop effective strategies for protecting livelihoods under climate stress and promoting sustainable development in this critical region.
