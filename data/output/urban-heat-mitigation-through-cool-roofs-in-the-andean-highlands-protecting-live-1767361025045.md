# Agroforestry-Based Micro-Hydro Systems for Climate-Resilient Irrigation in the Mesoamerican Highlands

## Executive Summary

Climate change exacerbates water scarcity in the Mesoamerican Highlands, threatening food security and economic stability for communities reliant on agriculture. This brief explores the potential of agroforestry-based micro-hydro systems to address these challenges through integrated solutions that ensure economic benefits and sustainability.

## Introduction to Water Scarcity in the Mesoamerican Highlands

Climate change is having a profound impact on the Mesoamerican Highlands, characterized by increasing temperatures, changing precipitation patterns, and rising evaporation rates. These changes result in severe water scarcity, affecting agricultural productivity and food security for millions of people. Resilient irrigation methods are crucial not only for economic stability but also for ensuring access to clean drinking water.

## Current Irrigation Practices and Challenges

Existing irrigation systems in the Mesoamerican Highlands rely heavily on boreholes and canals, which suffer from significant water loss due to evaporation (30%)[verify] and seepage. These losses reduce the efficiency of these systems and strain local water resources. The high energy costs associated with pumping water from deep wells further exacerbate the problem.

## Micro-Hydro Systems for Irrigation Efficiency

Micro-hydro technology involves the use of small-scale hydroelectric systems to generate electricity from flowing water. This approach offers several advantages over traditional irrigation methods, including reduced reliance on diesel generators and increased energy efficiency. By harnessing the power of local water sources, micro-hydro systems can provide a reliable source of energy for irrigation pumps.

## Integration in Climate-Resilient Water Supply

Pilot projects have demonstrated the potential of combining agroforestry with micro-hydro technology to create climate-resilient water supply systems. These integrated approaches improve irrigation efficiency and enhance soil health and biodiversity, contributing to long-term sustainability.

### Economic Analysis
