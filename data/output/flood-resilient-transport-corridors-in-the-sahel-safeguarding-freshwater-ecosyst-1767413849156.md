## Executive Summary

The Sahel region is highly vulnerable to climate change, facing severe water scarcity and poverty. This brief highlights the importance of integrated solutions that address these challenges through agroforestry-based micro-hydro systems for climate-resilient irrigation.

## Introduction to Water Scarcity in the Sahel

The Sahel region has a semi-arid climate with limited rainfall and high evaporation rates, exacerbated by climate change. Agriculture is critical in the region, but existing irrigation systems are often inefficient and unsustainable.

## Current Irrigation Practices and Challenges

Existing irrigation practices rely heavily on diesel generators, which are expensive and contribute to greenhouse gas emissions. Water loss from these systems is significant, with a notable portion lost through evaporation and seepage.

## Micro-Hydro Systems for Irrigation Efficiency

Micro-hydro technology provides a reliable and efficient source of energy for irrigation systems, reducing reliance on diesel generators and increasing energy efficiency.

## Integration in Climate-Resilient Water Supply

Pilot projects have demonstrated the effectiveness of combining agroforestry and micro-hydro systems for climate-resilient irrigation. These integrated systems improve water retention, reduce erosion, and promote sustainable agriculture practices.

## Policy Recommendations and Future Research Directions

To support the adoption of agroforestry-based micro-hydro systems at scale:

* Develop policies that incentivize renewable energy sources.
* Provide technical assistance and training to farmers on sustainable agriculture practices.
* Establish monitoring and evaluation frameworks to track progress and identify areas for improvement.

Future research directions include:
