# agroforestry value chains for cash crops in the Horn of Africa: safeguarding freshwater ecosystems

**Agroforestry Value Chains for Cash Crops in the Horn of Africa: Safeguarding Freshwater Ecosystems**

**Executive Summary**

Climate change is exacerbating water scarcity and energy shortages in rural Senegal, posing significant challenges to local communities' livelihoods and environmental resilience. This brief presents an innovative approach to address these dual challenges by integrating biochar-based agroforestry systems with renewable energy solutions. By leveraging the benefits of biochar production from agricultural waste, this intervention aims to improve soil health, carbon sequestration, and crop productivity while also enhancing water security through sustainable management practices. The integration of solar photovoltaic (PV) and wind energy can further reduce reliance on fossil fuels and improve energy access for rural households. This approach has the potential to promote economic growth, enhance food security, and contribute to climate adaptation efforts in rural Senegal.

**Environmental and Socio-Economic Context**

Rural Senegal is characterized by limited water resources, with an estimated 70% of the population relying on rainwater harvesting or shallow wells (FAO, 2019). Climate change has exacerbated this issue, leading to increased frequency and severity of droughts. The energy sector faces significant challenges, with an estimated annual demand of 150 GWh for rural households (World Bank, 2020). Renewable energy sources, particularly solar PV, have been identified as a viable solution due to the country's abundant sunlight.

**Biochar-Based Agroforestry Systems and Renewable Energy Integration**

Biochar production from agricultural waste offers several benefits, including improved soil health, increased carbon sequestration, and enhanced crop productivity. Successful case studies in other African regions demonstrate the feasibility of these systems (e.g., Kenya, Tanzania). By integrating biochar-based agroforestry with renewable energy solutions, rural Senegal can reduce its reliance on fossil fuels while improving water security through sustainable management practices.

*   **Biochar Production:**
    *   Biochar is produced from agricultural waste through a process of pyrolysis.
    *   Benefits include improved soil health, increased carbon sequestration, and enhanced crop productivity.
*   **Renewable Energy Integration:**
    *   Solar PV and wind energy have been identified as viable solutions for rural Senegal due to abundant sunlight and wind resources.
    *   Biochar-based agroforestry can be used as a passive cooling system to improve PV efficiency.

**Water Security Improvement**

The integration of biochar-based agroforestry with renewable energy solutions contributes to sustainable water management through soil conservation, reduced erosion, and improved irrigation efficiency. Case studies demonstrate the impact on local food security:

*   **Soil Conservation:**
    *   Biochar reduces soil erosion by improving soil structure and increasing water retention.
*   **Reduced Erosion:**
    *   By reducing soil erosion, biochar-based agroforestry systems minimize sedimentation in water bodies.
*   **Improved Irrigation Efficiency:**
    *   Reduced evaporation rates due to improved soil health and reduced erosion enable more efficient irrigation practices.
