**Microgrids for Rural Clinics in the Central America Dry Corridor: Stabilizing Food Security During Droughts**

## Executive Summary

To address the pressing issue of water security, clean energy access, and climate adaptation in Sub-Saharan Africa, governments should prioritize investments in water infrastructure, promote clean energy solutions, and implement climate adaptation strategies. Specifically, we recommend establishing microgrids for rural clinics in the Central America dry corridor to stabilize food security during droughts. This approach can improve health outcomes, enhance resilience, and foster economic growth.

## Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa faces significant challenges related to water security and clean energy access. According to World Bank data, only 32.4% of the population has access to safely managed drinking water [1], while less than half have access to electricity [2]. The region's renewable energy consumption is a mere 9.2%, far below global standards [3]. These deficits not only affect daily living conditions but also hinder economic growth and public health outcomes.

## Analysis

Recent data from the World Bank indicates that Sub-Saharan Africa lags behind in several key sectors: access to safe water (32.4%) and electricity (53.3%). These figures are stark compared to global averages, underscoring the urgency of intervention [1, 2]. The renewable energy consumption stands at 9.2%, far below international benchmarks [3]. This disparity is compounded by climate change impacts such as droughts, floods, and extreme weather events that further strain these systems.
