# Climate-Resilient School Feeding Supply Chains in Pacific SIDS: Stabilizing Food Security During Droughts

## Executive Summary

Climate change is exacerbating droughts in Pacific Small Island Developing States (SIDS), threatening food security and school feeding programs. This brief explores the integration of climate-resilient water management, clean energy, and adaptation strategies to stabilize school feeding supply chains during droughts.

## Introduction to Climate-Resilient School Feeding Supply Chains

Climate change is altering precipitation patterns in Pacific SIDS, resulting in more frequent and severe droughts. This impacts agriculture, leading to reduced crop yields and increased food prices [verify]. The school feeding program, which provides essential nutrition for children, is particularly vulnerable during droughts.

### Current Challenges:

*   Droughts reduce crop yields by 20-50% [verify]
*   Food prices increase by 15-30% [verify]
*   School feeding programs struggle to maintain food security and quality

## Climate-Resilient Water Management for School Feeding Supply Chains

Integrating climate-resilient water management strategies can help stabilize school feeding supply chains during droughts. This includes:

### Key Strategies:

*   Rainwater harvesting systems
*   Greywater reuse systems
*   Drought-tolerant crop selection and irrigation scheduling

## Clean Energy for School Feeding Supply Chains

Transitioning to clean energy can reduce dependence on fossil fuels, lower emissions, and decrease food costs. This includes:

### Key Strategies:

*   Solar-powered water pumping systems
*   Biogas generation from organic waste
*   Energy-efficient cooking technologies

## Climate Adaptation for School Feeding Supply Chains

Climate adaptation measures can enhance the resilience of school feeding supply chains during droughts. This includes:

### Key Strategies:

*   Early warning systems for droughts and extreme weather events
*   Drought-tolerant crop varieties and irrigation management practices
*   Community-based climate risk assessment and planning

## Actions/Recommendations

1.  **Integrate Climate-Resilient Water Management**: Implement rainwater harvesting, greywater reuse, and drought-tolerant crops to reduce water stress.
2.  **Transition to Clean Energy**: Adopt solar-powered water pumping systems, biogas generation, and energy-efficient cooking technologies to lower emissions and costs.
3.  **Enhance Climate Adaptation**: Develop early warning systems, drought-tolerant crop varieties, and community-based climate risk assessment and planning.

## Closing Outlook

Climate-resilient school feeding supply chains in Pacific SIDS require a comprehensive approach integrating climate-resilient water management, clean energy, and adaptation strategies. By implementing these key strategies, we can stabilize food security during droughts, ensuring the well-being of children and communities.
