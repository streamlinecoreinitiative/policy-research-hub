# climate-resilient school feeding supply chains in the Central America dry corridor: cutting disaster losses for vulnerable groups

**Climate-Resilient School Feeding Supply Chains in the Central America Dry Corridor: Enhancing Water Security and Food Resilience**

**Executive Summary**

The Central America dry corridor is highly vulnerable to climate change, with frequent droughts and floods affecting agricultural production and food security. The school feeding program, which provides meals to millions of children, is particularly susceptible to these impacts. This brief proposes an integrated approach to enhance climate resilience in school feeding supply chains through agroforestry-based micro-hydro systems for irrigation.

**Section 1: Introduction to Water Scarcity in the Central America Dry Corridor**

Climate change is exacerbating water scarcity in the region, affecting agriculture and food production. Rising temperatures and changing precipitation patterns are leading to more frequent droughts and floods, which have severe impacts on crop yields and livestock productivity. The school feeding program relies heavily on local agricultural products, making it vulnerable to climate-related shocks.
