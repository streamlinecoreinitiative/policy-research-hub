### Executive Summary

Sub-Saharan Africa is facing a critical juncture in addressing water scarcity, energy shortages, and climate-related shocks. To ensure sustainable development and improve the quality of life for millions, we recommend investing in renewable energy projects to increase consumption to at least 30% by 2030 (World Bank Open Data). This can be achieved through developing solar, wind, and hydroelectric projects, which are estimated to require $1 billion to $2 billion annually [VERIFY]. Additionally, enhancing water infrastructure is crucial, with a target of achieving 75% of the population with access to safely managed drinking water by 2040 (UN-Water/WHO). Finally, implementing climate adaptation strategies, such as early warning systems for droughts and floods using satellite imagery and ground sensors, is essential for building resilience in the region.

### Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa is characterized by arid and semi-arid climates with limited natural resources. Climate change exacerbates existing challenges such as droughts and floods, which impact access to safe drinking water and electricity. The region's high population density, weak infrastructure, and reliance on traditional energy sources make it particularly vulnerable to these issues. Immediate action is crucial given the region's growth rate and increasing demands for energy and water (World Bank Open Data).
