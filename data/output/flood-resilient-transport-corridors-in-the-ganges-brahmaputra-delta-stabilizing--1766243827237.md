## Executive Summary

The Ganges-Brahmaputra delta is vulnerable to climate change-induced droughts and floods, threatening food security for millions of people. This brief explores the potential of flood-resilient transport corridors in stabilizing food security during droughts by examining current irrigation practices, challenges, and opportunities for integrating agroforestry and micro-hydro systems.

## Introduction to Water Scarcity in the Ganges-Brahmaputra Delta

The Ganges-Brahmaputra delta is a critical agricultural hub, with over 200 million people relying on its fertile lands. Climate change has disrupted traditional rainfall patterns, leading to severe droughts and floods. The region's water scarcity is exacerbated by inefficient irrigation systems.

## Agroforestry's Role in Climate Resilience

Agroforestry systems have been shown to improve soil health, biodiversity, and carbon sequestration. By integrating trees into agricultural landscapes, farmers can:

* Enhance water retention and reduce erosion
* Increase crop yields and resilience to droughts
* Provide habitat for beneficial insects and pollinators

## Micro-Hydro Systems for Irrigation Efficiency

Micro-hydro technology offers a promising solution for irrigation efficiency, particularly in areas with high water flow rates. Benefits include:

* Reduced reliance on diesel generators and increased energy efficiency
* Lower operational costs and improved water management

## Integration of Agroforestry and Micro-Hydro in Climate-Resilient Water Supply

Pilot projects have demonstrated the effectiveness of combining agroforestry and micro-hydro systems for climate-resilient irrigation. These integrated solutions:

* Enhance water security and reduce drought risk
* Improve crop yields and farmer livelihoods
* Provide a model for scaling up climate-resilient agriculture

To support the adoption of flood-resilient transport corridors, we recommend:
