# climate-smart irrigation cooperatives in the Sahel: supporting adaptation targets in NDCs

**Climate-Smart Irrigation Cooperatives in the Sahel: Supporting Adaptation Targets in NDCs**

## Executive Summary

To address the pressing challenges of water security, clean energy access, and climate adaptation in Sub-Saharan Africa, we recommend establishing climate-smart irrigation cooperatives as a key policy intervention. These cooperatives would integrate sustainable water management practices with renewable energy solutions to enhance agricultural productivity while reducing vulnerability to climate-related shocks. By investing in such initiatives, governments can significantly improve livelihoods, build resilient societies, and support the region's Nationally Determined Contributions (NDCs) under the Paris Agreement.

## Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa faces a unique set of challenges related to water security and energy access. The region has seen significant declines in freshwater resources, with only 32.4% of its population having access to safely managed drinking water [Source: World Bank Open Data, 2024]. Moreover, just 53.3% of the population has access to electricity [Source: World Bank Open Data, 2023], and renewable energy consumption stands at a mere 19.7% of total final energy consumption [Source: World Bank Open Data, 2020]. These statistics underscore the urgent need for policy solutions that address both immediate needs and long-term sustainability.

## Policy Recommendations

To address these challenges, governments should prioritize the following policy interventions:

1. **Enhance Water Security**: Invest in robust water infrastructure projects such as rainwater harvesting systems, groundwater recharge schemes, and desalination plants. Implement policies that promote efficient water use through subsidies for low-cost technologies like drip irrigation and provide training on sustainable farming practices.
2. **Promote Renewable Energy**: Expand access to renewable energy sources by incentivizing the installation of solar panels in rural areas and implementing feed-in tariffs for small-scale hydroelectric projects. Collaborate with international partners to secure financing for large-scale renewable energy projects.
3. **Climate Adaptation Measures**: Encourage communities to adopt climate-resilient practices such as early warning systems, drought-resistant crop varieties, and improved water storage facilities. Provide financial support for vulnerable groups through microfinance institutions and community-based organizations.

## Implementation Considerations

Implementing these policies will require careful planning and coordination among various stakeholders including government agencies, non-governmental organizations (NGOs), international development partners, and local communities. Initial investments in infrastructure should be prioritized to ensure immediate benefits while fostering long-term sustainability. Collaborative efforts with regional governments can facilitate knowledge sharing and resource allocation.

## Conclusion

Addressing water security, clean energy access, and climate adaptation requires a multifaceted approach that integrates technological solutions with socio-economic strategies. By investing in sustainable water management practices and renewable energy sources, Sub-Saharan Africa can build resilience against future challenges while fostering inclusive economic growth. Governments must prioritize these interventions to ensure the region's long-term development.
