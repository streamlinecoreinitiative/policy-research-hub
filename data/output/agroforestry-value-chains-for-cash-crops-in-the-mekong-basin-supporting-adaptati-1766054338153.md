# agroforestry value chains for cash crops in the Mekong basin: supporting adaptation targets in NDCs

**Agroforestry Value Chains for Cash Crops in the Mekong Basin: Supporting Adaptation Targets in NDCs**

**Executive Summary**

Climate variability poses significant challenges to water security, clean energy, and climate adaptation in lower-income countries. Indigenous communities in the Andean region have developed sophisticated water management practices that can serve as a model for other regions facing similar challenges. By integrating these traditional knowledge systems with modern technologies and policies, lower-income countries can enhance their adaptive capacities to maintain sustainable water resources. This brief highlights the importance of indigenous water management practices, successful case studies, common challenges faced by indigenous communities, collaborative models for future implementation, policy recommendations, and a call to action.

**Introduction**

The Andean region is home to diverse indigenous communities that have developed unique water management techniques over centuries. These traditional knowledge systems are crucial in maintaining sustainable water resources, particularly in the face of increasing climate variability. By integrating these practices with modern technologies and policies, lower-income countries can enhance their adaptive capacities and achieve their Nationally Determined Contributions (NDCs) under the Paris Agreement.

**Climate-Resilient Water Management Practices: Case Studies and Benefits**

Indigenous communities in the Andes have developed sophisticated water management techniques that can be adapted or integrated with modern technologies to enhance climate resilience. Some successful case studies include:

*   **Irrigation systems**: Indigenous communities have developed efficient irrigation systems using traditional materials such as bamboo and stone.
*   **Water harvesting**: Communities have implemented water harvesting techniques, such as constructing small dams and reservoirs, to collect and store rainwater for agricultural use.
*   **Agroforestry practices**: Agroforestry value chains for cash crops in the Mekong Basin have been successfully integrated with traditional knowledge systems to enhance climate resilience.

These case studies demonstrate economic, social, and environmental benefits, including:

*   Improved water availability
*   Reduced erosion rates
*   Enhanced crop yields
*   Increased income for farmers

**Challenges Faced by Indigenous Communities**

Despite the success of indigenous-led initiatives, common challenges include:

*   **Lack of resources**: Limited access to funding, technology, and expertise can hinder the implementation of climate-resilient water management strategies.
*   **Legal disputes**: Conflicts over land ownership and resource use can create barriers to implementing traditional practices.
*   **Community engagement**: Engaging with indigenous communities in policy-making processes is essential for ensuring successful implementation and sustainability.

**Collaborative Models for Future Implementation**

To overcome these challenges, collaborative models between indigenous groups, government agencies, NGOs, and private sector partners are crucial. Potential roles and responsibilities of each stakeholder include:

*   **Indigenous communities**: Provide traditional knowledge and expertise in water management practices.
*   **Government agencies**: Support policy frameworks that recognize indigenous knowledge as a crucial component in climate adaptation strategies.
*   **NGOs**: Facilitate community engagement and provide technical assistance for implementing climate-resilient water management practices.
*   **Private sector partners**: Invest in modern technologies and infrastructure to support the implementation of traditional practices.

**Policy Recommendations**

To promote the adoption and integration of traditional water management practices into national and regional policies, the following policy recommendations are suggested:

1.  **Recognize indigenous knowledge**: Acknowledge the importance of traditional knowledge systems in climate adaptation strategies.
2.  **Develop policy frameworks**: Establish policy frameworks that support the implementation of climate-resilient water management practices.
3.  **Provide funding and resources**: Allocate funds and resources to support the development and implementation of climate-resilient water management practices.
