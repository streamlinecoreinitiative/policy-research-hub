# flood-resilient transport corridors in the Sudano-Sahelian belt: cutting disaster losses for vulnerable groups

**Flood-Resilient Transport Corridors in the Sudano-Sahelian Belt: A Comprehensive Approach to Disaster Risk Reduction**

Executive Summary:

The Sudano-Sahelian belt is highly vulnerable to climate-related disasters, including frequent floods and droughts that affect millions of people. This brief outlines a comprehensive approach to building flood-resilient transport corridors in this region, focusing on agroforestry-based micro-hydro systems for climate-resilient irrigation.

**Section 1: Introduction to Water Scarcity and Climate Change Impacts**

Water scarcity is a critical challenge in the Sudano-Sahelian belt, with severe impacts on agriculture, human consumption, and ecosystems. Climate change exacerbates this issue, leading to more frequent and intense floods and droughts. Successful agroforestry projects have demonstrated improved soil moisture retention and reduced erosion.

**Section 2: Agroforestry's Role in Climate-Resilient Irrigation**

Agroforestry has proven to be an effective approach to improving soil health, biodiversity, and crop yields. Benefits include:

* Improved soil structure with increased moisture retention
* Higher crop yields
* Increased biodiversity leading to natural pest control and pollination services

**Section 3: Micro-Hydro Systems for Renewable Energy**

Micro-hydro systems offer a reliable and renewable energy source for irrigation, reducing reliance on diesel generators. Benefits include:

* Renewable energy source for irrigation systems
* Improved water delivery
* Reduced fuel costs

**Section 4: Integration of Agroforestry and Micro-Hydro Systems**

Pilot projects have demonstrated the effectiveness of integrating agroforestry and micro-hydro systems, resulting in increased crop yields and reduced fuel and maintenance costs.

**Recommendations for Action:**

1. **Subsidies and Grants:** Provide government subsidies to support initial investment in micro-hydro system installation.
2. **Infrastructure Development:** Support community-led micro-hydro system installations with local government support.
3. **Capacity Building:** Train farmers, engineers, and policymakers in agroforestry-based micro-hydro systems.
4. **Monitoring and Evaluation:** Establish a monitoring framework to track the effectiveness of integrated systems.

**Closing Outlook:**

The Sudano-Sahelian belt is one of the most vulnerable regions to climate-related disasters. By integrating sustainable agriculture practices and renewable energy sources, we can reduce disaster losses, improve livelihoods, and promote community resilience. This brief provides a comprehensive approach to building flood-resilient transport corridors in this region.
