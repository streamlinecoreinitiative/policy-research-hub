Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa is grappling with severe water scarcity and inadequate access to electricity, exacerbating existing vulnerabilities from climate change. The region's reliance on natural resources and limited infrastructure resilience make it one of the most vulnerable regions globally (World Bank Open Data, 2023). Climate change impacts such as droughts, floods, and extreme weather events further complicate water security challenges.

    Policy Recommendations

    1. Promote Renewable Energy Integration: Invest in solar-powered desalination plants and renewable energy mini-grids to provide safe drinking water and power for water treatment facilities.

  • Enhance Water Infrastructure: Implement climate-resilient water storage systems such as dams, reservoirs, and rainwater harvesting ponds that can store water during wet periods and release it during dry seasons.
  • Support Climate-Resilient Agriculture: Develop climate-smart irrigation cooperatives to improve crop yields while reducing water usage through efficient water management practices.
  • Implement Early Warning Systems: Establish early warning systems for droughts and floods, ensuring timely responses that can mitigate the impacts of climate change on water resources.
