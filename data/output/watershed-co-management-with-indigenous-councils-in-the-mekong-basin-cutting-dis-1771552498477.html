Enhancing Water Security, Clean Energy, and Climate Adaptation in Sub-Saharan Africa: A Pathway to Resilience

Executive Summary

To address the pressing challenges of water security, clean energy access, and climate adaptation in Sub-Saharan Africa, we recommend investing in community-based watershed co-management initiatives with indigenous councils. These efforts can be replicated across the region, leveraging local knowledge and traditional practices to enhance resilience. Specifically, we propose a $10 million investment over three years to establish a regional program for watershed co-management in five pilot countries. This initiative will not only improve water security but also promote renewable energy adoption and climate adaptation.

    1. Invest in Community-Based Watershed Co-Management Initiatives:

* Establish a regional program for watershed co-management with indigenous councils in five pilot countries. * Provide training and capacity-building for local communities on sustainable water management practices.

* Encourage the installation of solar-powered mini-grids in rural areas to reduce energy poverty. * Support public-private partnerships to finance off-grid solar solutions.

- Funding: Mobilize public and private sector investments through grants, concessional loans, and low-interest financing mechanisms.
