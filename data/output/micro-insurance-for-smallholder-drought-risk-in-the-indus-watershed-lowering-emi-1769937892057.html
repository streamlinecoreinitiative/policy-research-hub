Executive Summary

This policy brief highlights the urgent need to address drought risk and water security challenges in the Indus watershed, a critical region for smallholder agriculture and food security. With over 32.4% of the population lacking access to safely managed drinking water [Source: World Bank Open Data, 2024], we recommend implementing micro-insurance programs to protect smallholders from drought-related losses.

Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa faces significant challenges related to water security, access to clean energy, and climate adaptation [Source: World Bank Open Data, 2023]. The region's limited resources exacerbate vulnerability during extreme weather events, leading to food insecurity, health issues, and economic instability. In the Indus watershed, droughts have severe impacts on agriculture, affecting a significant number of people [FAO, 2020].
