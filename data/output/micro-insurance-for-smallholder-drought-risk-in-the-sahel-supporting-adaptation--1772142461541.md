# micro-insurance for smallholder drought risk in the Sahel: supporting adaptation targets in NDCs

**Enhancing Water Security, Clean Energy Access, and Climate Resilience in Sub-Saharan Africa: A Holistic Approach for Sustainable Development**

## Executive Summary

Sub-Saharan Africa faces significant challenges in accessing clean water and electricity, with only 32.4% of the population having access to safely managed drinking water [1] and a mere 53.3% with access to electricity [2]. To address these gaps effectively, we recommend prioritizing renewable energy projects for off-grid areas, investing in microgrids for rural clinics, and implementing watershed co-management with indigenous councils.

## Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa is one of the regions most vulnerable to climate change, with limited resources for water supply and energy access. The region's population growth exacerbates these issues [1]. Immediate action is necessary to meet sustainable development goals (SDGs), particularly SDG 6 on clean water and sanitation.

## Analysis

Access to safe drinking water in Sub-Saharan Africa remains below global averages, with only 32.4% of the population having access compared to a global average of 73.7% [1]. Electricity access also lags behind globally, at 53.3% for Sub-Saharan Africa versus 91.6% worldwide [2]. These disparities not only affect health outcomes but also hinder economic development and social stability.

## Policy Recommendations

### 1. Prioritize Renewable Energy Projects

* **Action**: Develop and implement large-scale renewable energy projects in off-grid areas, focusing on solar and wind technologies.
* **Implementation**: Partner with international donors and local NGOs to fund and manage these projects.
* **Costs & Barriers**: Estimated costs range from $50-$100 million per project [3]. Potential barriers include high initial investment and lack of skilled labor.

### 2. Invest in Microgrids for Rural Clinics

* **Action**: Establish microgrid systems at rural health clinics, providing reliable electricity to support healthcare services.
* **Implementation**: Collaborate with local governments and private sector partners to deploy these solutions.
* **Costs & Barriers**: Estimated costs are $10-$20 million per clinic [3]. Main barriers include high upfront costs and limited grid infrastructure.

### 3. Implement Watershed Co-management with Indigenous Councils

* **Action**: Engage indigenous communities in watershed management, leveraging their traditional knowledge to improve water resource management.
* **Implementation**: Provide training and resources for these councils to manage and protect local ecosystems sustainably.
* **Costs & Barriers**: Costs are estimated at $2-$5 million per project [3]. Main barriers include resistance from other stakeholders and limited technical expertise.

## Implementation Considerations

To implement these recommendations, we recommend exploring grants, concessional loans, and private sector partnerships for financing [1]. Capacity building is also essential to ensure local officials and community members have the necessary skills in water management and renewable energy technologies. A system should be established to track progress and adjust strategies based on feedback.

## Conclusion

Sub-Saharan Africa urgently needs targeted interventions to enhance water security, increase clean energy access, and bolster climate resilience. By prioritizing these recommendations, governments, NGOs, and development practitioners can make significant strides towards achieving sustainable development goals in the region [4].

**Call to Action**: Develop a comprehensive roadmap for implementing these policies and secure funding from international partners and domestic sources. Encourage broader collaboration among stakeholders to accelerate progress.
