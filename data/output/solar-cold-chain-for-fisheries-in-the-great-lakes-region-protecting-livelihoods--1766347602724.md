Climate change poses significant threats to fisheries and aquaculture in the Great Lakes region, impacting livelihoods of millions of people dependent on these industries. This brief highlights the importance of solar-powered cold-chain systems for fisheries in this region.

## Solar Cold-Chain Systems for Fisheries

Solar-powered cold-chain systems provide a sustainable solution for fisheries in the Great Lakes region. These systems offer several benefits, including:

* Renewable energy source
* Reduced energy costs
* Improved fish quality and safety
* Increased livelihoods security for smallholder farmers

## Integration of Solar Cold-Chains with Agroforestry Practices

Integrating solar cold-chains with agroforestry practices can enhance the resilience of fisheries to climate change. Agroforestry improves soil health, biodiversity, and carbon sequestration.

## Policy Recommendations and Future Research Directions

Policy measures can create a supportive environment for implementing solar cold-chain systems and agroforestry practices. The following recommendations are suggested:

* Incentives for farmers (e.g., subsidies, low-interest loans)
* Grid integration with micro-hydro systems
* Infrastructure development (roads, storage facilities)

Future research is needed to validate the long-term impacts of integrated solar cold-chains and agroforestry practices on fisheries livelihoods.

## Actions/Recommendations

1. Develop policy measures to support implementation of solar cold-chain systems and agroforestry practices.
2. Conduct economic impact assessments to determine the feasibility of these solutions.
3. Establish community acceptance studies to ensure successful adoption of integrated systems.

## Closing Outlook

The integration of solar cold-chains with agroforestry practices offers a promising solution for protecting livelihoods in fisheries under climate stress. This approach addresses immediate water scarcity issues and contributes to broader sustainable development goals in agriculture and renewable energy adoption.
