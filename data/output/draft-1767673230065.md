**Agroforestry-Based Micro-Hydro Systems for Climate-Resilient Irrigation in the Central African Rainforest**

Executive Summary:

The project aims to address severe water scarcity and climate change impacts on agriculture in the Central African Rainforest by integrating agroforestry with micro-hydro systems. This approach will enhance both water security and renewable energy use, improving agricultural resilience for vulnerable communities.

**Climate Change Impacts and Water Scarcity**

The Central African Rainforest is experiencing severe water scarcity due to erratic rainfall patterns and increased evaporation from wetlands. Climate change projections indicate a reduction in rainfall, but the exact percentage is not specified (NOAA Climate Dataset). This has significant economic implications for agriculture, with high labor costs and fuel costs associated with constructing and maintaining irrigation systems.

**Agroforestry-Based Micro-Hydro Systems**

Agroforestry practices have been shown to improve soil health, increase biodiversity, and reduce erosion. The integration of micro-hydro systems with agroforestry has demonstrated improved water delivery and reduced dependency on external energy sources (Project 1, Project 2). This approach also provides long-term sustainability benefits.
