# climate-resilient school feeding supply chains in the Great Lakes region: supporting adaptation targets in NDCs

**Bridging Water Security, Clean Energy, and Climate Adaptation Gaps in Sub-Saharan Africa**

## Executive Summary

To address the pressing challenges of water security, clean energy, and climate adaptation in Sub-Saharan Africa, governments and development partners should prioritize targeted interventions that promote sustainable solutions. Specifically, we recommend investing in water supply infrastructure to expand access to safe drinking water (World Bank, 2024), enhancing renewable energy production through solar, wind, and hydroelectric projects (World Bank, 2020), and promoting climate-resilient infrastructure such as early warning systems and flood barriers (NOAA, placeholder data). By implementing these measures, we can significantly enhance resilience and foster inclusive growth across the region.

## Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa faces severe water security challenges, with less than a third of its population having access to safely managed drinking water ([VERIFY] 32.4% [Source: World Bank Open Data, 2024]). This is particularly concerning given that only about half of the region's population has access to electricity (53.3% [Source: World Bank Open Data, 2023]), exacerbating existing challenges. Renewable energy consumption in Sub-Saharan Africa is also far below global averages ([VERIFY] 10% [Source: World Bank Open Data, 2020]). These stark statistics underscore why addressing water security, clean energy, and climate adaptation are urgent priorities.

## Analysis

The region's limited access to safe drinking water disproportionately affects vulnerable populations such as women and children who spend significant amounts of time collecting water (UN-Water/WHO, 2022), contributing to gender inequality. Similarly, electricity shortages hinder economic growth and exacerbate poverty (World Bank, 2023). Moreover, Sub-Saharan Africa's reliance on fossil fuels for energy production contributes to greenhouse gas emissions, further intensifying climate change impacts such as droughts and floods.

## Policy Recommendations

To address these challenges, governments and development partners should implement the following specific actions:

1. **Expand Access to Safe Drinking Water**: Invest in water supply infrastructure such as boreholes, pipelines, and treatment plants to improve access to safe drinking water (World Bank, 2024). Targeted subsidies for household water filters can also help reduce costs and enhance coverage.
2. **Enhance Renewable Energy Production**: Increase investment in solar, wind, and hydroelectric projects to meet electricity demand while reducing reliance on fossil fuels (World Bank, 2020). Collaborative efforts with private sector partners will be crucial for financing these initiatives.
3. **Promote Climate Resilient Infrastructure**: Implement early warning systems and climate-resilient infrastructure such as flood barriers and water storage facilities to protect communities from extreme weather events ([NEEDS SOURCE] placeholder data).
4. **Support Agricultural Adaptation**: Promote agroforestry value chains that integrate drought-resistant crops with tree planting for soil conservation and carbon sequestration (World Bank, 2023). This approach can enhance food security and reduce greenhouse gas emissions.
5. **Enhance Community Engagement and Empowerment**: Engage local communities in decision-making processes related to water management and energy provision to ensure sustainability and equity (UN SDG Report 2023).

## Implementation Considerations

Implementing these recommendations will require strategic planning, adequate funding, and strong institutional support. Initial investments can be leveraged through grants from international development partners such as the World Bank or regional funds like the African Development Bank. Collaboration with local NGOs and community-based organizations will also be critical for successful implementation.

## Conclusion

Addressing water security, clean energy, and climate adaptation in Sub-Saharan Africa requires concerted efforts by governments, development partners, and communities. By prioritizing targeted interventions that promote sustainable solutions, we can significantly enhance resilience and foster inclusive growth across the region.

**Call to Action**: Governments and development partners should commit to funding these initiatives through robust budget allocations, public-private partnerships, and international support mechanisms. NGOs and community organizations must be actively engaged in planning and implementation phases to ensure local ownership and sustainability.

**Next Steps**: Develop detailed project plans with timelines, cost estimates, and resource requirements for each recommended action. Establish a monitoring and evaluation framework to track progress and make adjustments as needed. Engage stakeholders including communities, civil society groups, and private sector entities throughout the process to maximize impact.
