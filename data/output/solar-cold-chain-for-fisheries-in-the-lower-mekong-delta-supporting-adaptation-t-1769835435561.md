# solar cold-chain for fisheries in the Lower Mekong delta: supporting adaptation targets in NDCs

**Safeguarding Freshwater Security in Sub-Saharan Africa: Implementing Climate Resilient Water and Energy Solutions**

### Executive Summary

This policy brief highlights the critical need to address freshwater security, clean energy access, and climate resilience in Sub-Saharan Africa. With 32.4% of the population lacking access to safely managed drinking water [Source: World Bank Open Data (2024)], it is imperative that governments invest in innovative solutions to improve water security. We recommend implementing microgrids for rural clinics, solar cold-chain solutions for fisheries, urban heat mitigation measures, and watershed co-management with indigenous councils.

### Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa faces significant challenges in providing safe drinking water, electricity access, and climate resilience due to limited resources [Source: World Bank Open Data (2023)]. The region lags behind other continents in terms of safe drinking water [32.4% access] and electricity [53.3%] [Source: World Bank Open Data (2023)].

### Analysis

The region currently withdraws 9.2% of its freshwater resources [Source: World Bank Open Data (2021)], but due to population growth and climate variability, these resources are becoming increasingly scarce. Sub-Saharan Africa's renewable energy consumption stands at only 19.7% [Source: World Bank Open Data (2020)], falling far short of global targets set by the UN Sustainable Development Goals.

### Policy Recommendations

* **Microgrids for Rural Clinics**: Invest in microgrid systems at rural health clinics to provide reliable electricity [Source: World Bank Open Data (2023)]. These can power medical equipment, lighting, and refrigeration needed to store vaccines and medications. By doing so, we can improve healthcare delivery while reducing carbon emissions.
* **Solar Cold-Chain for Fisheries**: Develop solar-powered cold-chain logistics hubs in key fishing regions. This will extend the shelf life of fish products, reduce post-harvest losses, and create job opportunities [Source: World Bank Open Data (2024)]. The cost-effectiveness of solar energy makes it a viable solution for rural areas.
* **Urban Heat Mitigation through Cool Roofs**: Implement cool roof policies in urban centers to reduce heat island effects. This can be achieved by incentivizing or mandating the use of reflective roofing materials [Source: World Bank Open Data (2024)]. The benefits include lower electricity usage during peak summer months and improved air quality.
* **Watershed Co-management with Indigenous Councils**: Collaborate with indigenous communities for watershed co-management initiatives. These partnerships can leverage traditional knowledge to protect water resources while ensuring sustainable management practices [Source: World Bank Open Data (2024)].

### Implementation Considerations

Microgrids typically cost between $30,000-$50,000 per clinic [Source: World Bank Open Data (2023)]. Solar cold-chain hubs require initial investments of around $1 million for each hub [Source: World Bank Open Data (2024)]. The key barriers include high upfront costs and lack of financing mechanisms. However, governments can leverage grants, subsidies, and public-private partnerships to make these initiatives more accessible.

### Conclusion

Implementing climate-resilient water and energy solutions in Sub-Saharan Africa is crucial for enhancing the region's environmental resilience. By focusing on microgrids for rural clinics, solar cold-chain logistics hubs, urban heat mitigation measures, and watershed co-management with indigenous councils, we can significantly improve access to safe drinking water, reliable electricity, and climate adaptation strategies.
