Urban Heat Mitigation through Cool Roofs in the Lower Mekong Delta: Lowering Emissions from Critical Sectors

Executive Summary

To address the pressing issue of climate change, particularly in the Lower Mekong Delta region, we recommend prioritizing urban heat mitigation strategies, specifically cool roofs. These measures can significantly reduce energy consumption and lower greenhouse gas emissions from critical sectors such as agriculture and transportation.

Key findings:

* The Lower Mekong Delta is highly vulnerable to climate change, with rising temperatures and increased frequency of extreme weather events.

  • Cool roofs can reduce building energy consumption by up to 40% (Source: [1] World Bank Open Data).
  • Implementing cool roofs in urban areas can lower emissions from critical sectors, contributing to the region's climate resilience.

    Context & Challenge

    The Lower Mekong Delta is one of the most densely populated regions in Southeast Asia, with a growing population and increasing urbanization. Climate change poses significant challenges to the region's water security, food production, and economic development. Rising temperatures and extreme weather events exacerbate these challenges, threatening the livelihoods of millions.

  • Transportation: Lower temperatures reduce energy demand for cooling, lowering emissions from vehicles.

Implementing urban heat mitigation strategies like cool roofs can significantly reduce energy consumption, lower emissions from critical sectors, and contribute to the region's climate resilience. We urge governments, NGOs, and private sector entities to prioritize cool roof implementation in their development plans.
