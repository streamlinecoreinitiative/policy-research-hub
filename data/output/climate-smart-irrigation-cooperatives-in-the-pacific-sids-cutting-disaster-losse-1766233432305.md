# Climate-Smart Irrigation Cooperatives in Pacific SIDS: Enhancing Water Security and Resilience

## Executive Summary

The Pacific Small Island Developing States (SIDS) are highly vulnerable to climate change, with frequent natural disasters and water scarcity posing significant challenges to their agricultural sectors. This brief explores the potential of climate-smart irrigation cooperatives that integrate agroforestry practices with micro-hydro systems.

## Introduction to Water Scarcity in Pacific SIDS

Climate change is exacerbating water scarcity issues in the Pacific SIDS through increased evaporation from warming oceans and altered rainfall patterns. This has severe implications for agriculture, which is a critical sector for many of these islands' economies.

## Current Irrigation Practices and Challenges

Existing irrigation practices in Pacific SIDS include boreholes, canals, and small-scale pumps. However, these systems suffer from high energy costs, water loss through evaporation and seepage, and limited scalability.
