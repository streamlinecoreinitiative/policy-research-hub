# nature-based solutions for landslide risk in the Pacific SIDS: improving health outcomes in heatwaves

**Nature-Based Solutions for Landslide Risk in the Pacific SIDS: Improving Health Outcomes in Heatwaves**

**Executive Summary**

Climate change exacerbates water scarcity, heatwaves, and landslide risks in lower-income countries, particularly in the Pacific Small Island Developing States (SIDS). This brief highlights the importance of nature-based solutions to address these pressing issues. We focus on agroforestry-based micro-hydro systems as a climate-resilient approach for irrigation.

**Climate Change Impacts and Water Scarcity in Lower-Income Countries**

Climate change alters precipitation patterns, leading to increased frequency and severity of droughts and floods. In the Pacific SIDS, rising temperatures intensify heatwaves, which can have devastating impacts on human health, agriculture, and infrastructure. Landslides triggered by heavy rainfall or thawing permafrost pose significant risks to communities and ecosystems.

**Current Irrigation Practices and Challenges**

Traditional irrigation systems in lower-income countries often rely on energy-intensive borehole pumps and canal networks, resulting in significant water losses through evaporation and seepage. These methods are costly and unsustainable, exacerbating water scarcity issues.

**Policy Recommendations and Future Research Directions**

Policy measures can create a supportive environment for implementing sustainable irrigation solutions. Future research is needed to validate long-term impacts and sustainability of integrated agroforestry-micro-hydro systems.

*   **Policy Recommendations:**
