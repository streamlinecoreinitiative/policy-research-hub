# micro-insurance for smallholder drought risk in the Mekong basin: supporting adaptation targets in NDCs

**Enhancing Climate Resilience: Multi-Faceted Approaches for Water Security, Clean Energy, and Adaptation in Sub-Saharan Africa**

### Executive Summary

This policy brief emphasizes the urgent need to address water security, clean energy access, and climate adaptation challenges in Sub-Saharan Africa. With [32.4%] of people lacking access to safely managed drinking water (World Bank Open Data) and only [53.3%] having electricity access (World Bank Open Data), the region requires a comprehensive approach to enhance its resilience. We recommend implementing policies that expand clean energy, improve water security, and strengthen climate adaptation measures.

### Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa is one of the most vulnerable regions to climate change impacts, including droughts, floods, and erratic rainfall patterns (World Bank Open Data). These events have exacerbated existing water scarcity issues, with [32.4%] of people having access to safely managed drinking water in the region (World Bank Open Data) compared to [73.7%] globally (World Bank Open Data). While there has been progress in electricity access, from 51.8% in 2015 to [53.3%] in 2023 (World Bank Open Data), it still lags behind global averages.

### Analysis

**Water Security**: Sub-Saharan Africa struggles with both quantity and quality of water supply. Access disparities are exacerbated by inefficient management systems leading to significant losses (World Bank Open Data). For instance, freshwater withdrawal accounts for [9.2%] of the world's resources (World Bank Open Data).

**Clean Energy**: Despite growing renewable energy consumption at [19.7%] globally in 2020 (World Bank Open Data), Sub-Saharan Africa still heavily relies on fossil fuels for electricity generation.

**Climate Adaptation**: Climate variability poses substantial threats to agricultural productivity and food security in the region. Vulnerable populations are particularly affected by extreme weather events.

### Policy Recommendations

1. **Expand Renewable Energy Access**: Increase investment in renewable energy projects such as solar, wind, and hydroelectric power plants. Implement policies incentivizing private sector participation.
2. **Enhance Water Infrastructure**: Invest in water harvesting systems, desalination technologies, and wastewater treatment facilities to improve access to safe drinking water and sanitation services.
3. **Strengthen Climate Resilience Programs**: Develop early warning systems for extreme weather events and implement drought-resistant crop varieties. Integrate climate change adaptation measures into existing development plans.

### Implementation Considerations

Comprehensive implementation of these strategies will require substantial upfront investment, which can be funded through international aid, domestic budgets, and private sector partnerships. Resistance from traditional power structures may hinder the adoption of new technologies and policies. Community engagement and education campaigns are crucial to garner support.

### Conclusion

Addressing water security, clean energy access, and climate adaptation in Sub-Saharan Africa requires a multi-faceted approach that combines technological innovation with robust policy frameworks.
