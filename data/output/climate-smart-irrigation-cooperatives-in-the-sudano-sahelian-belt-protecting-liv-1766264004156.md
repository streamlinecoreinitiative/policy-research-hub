## Executive Summary

The Sudano-Sahelian belt, spanning across several countries in West Africa, faces significant climate-related challenges due to erratic rainfall patterns and increasing temperatures. This brief explores the integration of agroforestry and micro-hydro systems as a climate-resilient solution for irrigation in this region.

## Current Irrigation Practices and Challenges

Existing irrigation systems in the region include boreholes, canals, and traditional water harvesting methods. However, these systems face significant challenges:

* High energy costs: Diesel generators are often used to pump water, increasing operational costs.
* Water loss: Evaporation and seepage losses range from 20-50% of total water usage.

## Micro-Hydro Systems for Irrigation Efficiency

Micro-hydro technology offers a sustainable solution for irrigation, providing:

* Renewable energy: Hydroelectric power reduces reliance on diesel generators and increases energy efficiency.
* Water conservation: Micro-hydro systems can reduce water loss through evaporation and seepage.

## Integration of Agroforestry and Micro-Hydro in Climate-Resilient Water Supply

Pilot projects have demonstrated the effectiveness of integrating agroforestry and micro-hydro systems:

* Improved crop yields: Studies show an increase in crop yields compared to traditional farming practices.

2. **Provide financing mechanisms**: Financial institutions should offer loans or grants to support farmers in adopting these climate-resilient practices.
3. **Capacity building**: Training programs should be implemented to enhance farmers' knowledge on agroforestry-micro-hydro system design, installation, and maintenance.
