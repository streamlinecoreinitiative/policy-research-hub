Sub-Saharan Africa faces significant challenges in water security, clean energy access, and climate adaptation. With only 32.4% of the population having access to safely managed drinking water (World Bank Open Data, 2024), this region is particularly vulnerable to droughts, floods, and other climate-related shocks. Ensuring sustainable water supply and reliable energy sources are crucial for reducing poverty and improving health outcomes in Sub-Saharan Africa.

## Policy Recommendations

To enhance environmental resilience in Sub-Saharan Africa:

1. **Expand Access to Safe Drinking Water**: Invest in infrastructure projects like boreholes, piped water systems, and rainwater harvesting facilities. Implement community-based management models for sustainable use.
2. **Promote Renewable Energy Adoption**: Increase government subsidies for renewable energy sources such as solar and wind power. Encourage private sector investment through tax incentives and grants.
3. **Enhance Climate Resilience Measures**: Develop early warning systems to predict climate-related hazards, invest in drought-resistant crops, and implement water conservation measures.
