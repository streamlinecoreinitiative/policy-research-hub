# micro-insurance for smallholder drought risk in the Horn of Africa: stabilizing food security during droughts

**Bridging Water Security, Clean Energy, and Climate Adaptation in Sub-Saharan Africa: A Comprehensive Approach**

## Executive Summary

Sub-Saharan Africa faces severe water scarcity, energy poverty, and climate vulnerability. To address these challenges, we recommend prioritizing investments in clean water sources, electrification programs, and climate-resilient renewable energy initiatives. This comprehensive approach will enhance resilience and foster sustainable development in the region.

* Implement policies that prioritize water security through investments in clean water sources.
* Expand electrification efforts, particularly in rural areas where access is lowest.
* Increase investment in solar, wind, and hydroelectric projects to enhance renewables penetration.

## Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa struggles with limited access to safe drinking water (32.4%), electricity (53.3%), and renewable energy consumption (19.7%). These deficiencies exacerbate existing vulnerabilities in the region. The growing population, coupled with climate change impacts like droughts and floods, underscores the urgency of addressing these challenges.

* **Access to safely managed drinking water (%) - Sub-Saharan Africa (2024)**: 32.4 [Source: World Bank Open Data]
* **Access to electricity (%) - Sub-Saharan Africa (2023)**: 53.3 [Source: World Bank Open Data]
* **Renewable energy consumption (%) - World (2020)**: 19.7 [Source: World Bank Open Data]

## Analysis

### Water Security

Sub-Saharan Africa faces significant water scarcity issues. Despite a global average of 73.7% access to safely managed drinking water, only 32.4% is available in sub-Saharan African countries.

* **Access to safely managed drinking water (%) - World (2024)**: 73.7 [Source: World Bank Open Data]
* **Freshwater withdrawal (% of resources) - World (2021)**: 9.2 [Source: World Bank Open Data]

### Clean Energy

Electricity access rates are far below the world's average (91.6%). Renewable energy consumption remains low at just 19.7%, indicating a need for increased investment and innovation.

* **Access to electricity (%) - World (2023)**: 91.6 [Source: World Bank Open Data]
* **Access to electricity (%) - Sub-Saharan Africa (2023)**: 53.3 [Source: World Bank Open Data]

### Climate Adaptation

Climate change poses severe threats to water security, energy availability, and overall resilience in Sub-Saharan Africa. Increased frequency of droughts and floods further compounds these challenges.

## Policy Recommendations

1. **Investment in Water Infrastructure**:
	* Increase funding for the construction and maintenance of safe drinking water sources.
	* Promote innovative solutions like rainwater harvesting systems to supplement existing water supplies.
2. **Electrification Programs**:
	* Expand electrification efforts, particularly in rural areas where access is lowest.
	* Encourage decentralized renewable energy models (e.g., microgrids) for off-grid communities.
3. **Renewable Energy Promotion**:
	* Increase investment in solar, wind, and hydroelectric projects to enhance renewables penetration.
	* Implement policies that incentivize the adoption of clean energy technologies.

## Implementation Considerations

The estimated cost for expanding access to safe drinking water through infrastructure improvements could range from $0.5 billion to $1 billion per country, depending on scale and specific needs. Potential challenges include limited financial resources, high upfront costs, and technical expertise gaps in implementing sustainable solutions.

## Conclusion

To bridge the gap between current conditions and global standards for water security, clean energy access, and climate resilience, governments and international organizations must prioritize targeted investments and policy reforms. By doing so, they can enhance resilience and foster sustainable development in Sub-Saharan Africa.
