## Executive Summary

This policy brief highlights the critical need to strengthen environmental resilience in Sub-Saharan Africa, focusing on water security and clean energy. We recommend implementing community-based water management systems, promoting renewable energy projects, and enhancing climate adaptation strategies. The lack of access to safe drinking water (32.4% of the population) [1] and electricity (53.3%) exacerbates the impacts of climate change and poverty in this region [2]. Immediate action is necessary to address these urgent needs.

## Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa is one of the most vulnerable regions to climate change, with droughts, floods, and erratic rainfall patterns affecting millions of people [3]. The region's infrastructure for accessing safe drinking water and electricity is inadequate or non-existent in many areas. This brief focuses on three critical areas: water security, clean energy, and climate adaptation.

## Analysis

### Water Security

Sub-Saharan Africa lags behind the world average in access to safely managed drinking water, with only 32.4% of the population having access [1]. In rural areas, this disparity is particularly acute, where infrastructure is often inadequate or non-existent. The region's freshwater withdrawal rate is also high at 9.2% of available resources [4].

### Clean Energy

Despite global efforts, Sub-Saharan Africa lags behind in electricity access and renewable energy consumption. Only 53.3% of the population has access to electricity [5], while renewable energy consumption stands at only 19.7% globally [6]. The region's reliance on fossil fuels exacerbates climate change impacts.

### Climate Adaptation

Sub-Saharan Africa is highly susceptible to climate-related stresses, including prolonged droughts and unpredictable rainfall patterns. These conditions further compound water scarcity issues, making it essential to enhance climate adaptation strategies.
