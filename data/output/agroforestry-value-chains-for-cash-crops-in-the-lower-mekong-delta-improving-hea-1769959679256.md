## Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa faces significant challenges related to water scarcity, electricity access, and climate change. With [VERIFY] 2.2 billion people lacking safe drinking water (UN-Water/WHO, 2022), the region is highly vulnerable. Climate-induced droughts are exacerbating existing challenges, leading to increased food insecurity, economic instability, and social unrest.

## Analysis

Sub-Saharan Africa's water scarcity is exacerbated by rapid population growth, agricultural intensification, and climate change-induced variability (UN-Water/WHO, 2022). Only [VERIFY] 32.4% of the population has access to safely managed drinking water, with rural areas significantly underserved. Sub-Saharan Africa lags behind other regions in energy access, with only [VERIFY] 53.3% of the population having reliable electricity (World Bank, 2023).

## Policy Recommendations

1. **Enhance Safe Drinking Water Access**:
	* Implement CLTS programs in rural areas to improve sanitation and hygiene practices.
	* Invest in desalination plants for coastal regions facing severe water scarcity.
	* Promote rainwater harvesting and storage systems to increase local water supply resilience.
2. **Increase Electrification Rates**:
	* Prioritize electrification of rural areas through off-grid solar solutions, focusing on key sectors like agriculture and health care.
	* Invest in renewable energy projects such as small-scale hydro, wind, and solar power to diversify the energy mix.
3. **Climate Resilient Infrastructure Development**:
	* Develop early warning systems for droughts and floods using satellite imagery and local weather data.
	* Implement climate-resilient water supply infrastructure like drip irrigation and groundwater management.

## Implementation Considerations

Estimated costs for implementing these interventions range from $50-$100 million per year, including subsidies for renewable energy projects and grants for water access programs. High initial investment in infrastructure development, limited institutional capacity in rural areas, and political will are crucial to securing funding and support.

## Conclusion

Addressing water security, clean energy, and climate resilience requires a coordinated approach that leverages available resources. By investing in sustainable solutions now, Sub-Saharan Africa can build a resilient future for its people and economy.
