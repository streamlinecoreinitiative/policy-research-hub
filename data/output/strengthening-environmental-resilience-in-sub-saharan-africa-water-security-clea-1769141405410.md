## Executive Summary

Sub-Saharan Africa faces a critical need for urgent action on climate adaptation measures such as renewable energy consumption and water security initiatives. Only 32.4% of the population has access to safely managed drinking water (World Bank Open Data), while 53.3% have access to electricity (World Bank Open Data). To address these challenges, we recommend prioritizing investment in clean energy, expanding water security measures, and enhancing climate adaptation strategies.

## Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa is disproportionately affected by inadequate access to safe drinking water (32.4%) and electricity (53.3%), exacerbating existing water scarcity issues (World Bank Open Data). These challenges not only threaten basic human needs but also undermine economic development, health outcomes, and climate resilience. The region's renewable energy consumption remains low at 9.2%, far below the world average of 19.7% (World Bank Open Data).
