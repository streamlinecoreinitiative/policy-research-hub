**Executive Summary**
To mitigate urban heat island effects and reduce greenhouse gas emissions in the Sahel region, we recommend implementing cool roof technologies on buildings. This can be achieved by providing incentives for private sector investment in cool roof installation projects, targeting at least 20% of new building constructions by 2025. By doing so, we can reduce energy consumption, lower carbon emissions, and enhance urban resilience.

**Context & Challenge**
The Sahel region is experiencing rapid urbanization, with cities like Niamey and Ouagadougou facing severe heat island effects due to high temperatures and lack of green spaces (UN Habitat, 2020). This has significant implications for public health, economic productivity, and environmental sustainability. The region's reliance on fossil fuels for energy generation contributes to high carbon emissions, exacerbating climate change impacts.
