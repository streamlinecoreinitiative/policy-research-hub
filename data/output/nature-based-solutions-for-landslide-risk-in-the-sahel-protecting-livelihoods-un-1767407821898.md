**Nature-Based Solutions for Landslide Risk in the Sahel: Protecting Livelihoods Under Climate Stress**

**Executive Summary**

The Central African Rainforest is facing severe water scarcity due to climate change, affecting agriculture and smallholder farmers' livelihoods. This brief proposes integrating agroforestry with micro-hydro systems as a sustainable solution to enhance both water security and renewable energy use.

**I. Introduction to Water Scarcity**

The Central African Rainforest is experiencing severe water scarcity due to climate change. Erratic rainfall patterns and increased evaporation from wetlands are impacting water availability for crops. This has significant implications for agriculture in the region, where smallholder farmers rely heavily on irrigation systems.

* **Climate Change Impacts:**
	+ Rising temperatures are altering precipitation patterns, leading to droughts and floods.
	+ Increased evaporation from wetlands is reducing water availability for crops.
* **Water Scarcity Consequences:**
	+ Reduced crop yields due to inadequate water supply.
	+ Economic losses for smallholder farmers.

**II. Current Irrigation Practices and Challenges**
