**Agroforestry-Based Micro-Hydro Systems for Climate-Resilient Irrigation in the Andean Highlands**

## Executive Summary

The Andean highlands face severe water scarcity due to climate change impacts, including erratic rainfall patterns and increased evaporation rates. This brief highlights the importance of integrating agroforestry and micro-hydro systems for climate-resilient irrigation methods.

## Addressing Water Scarcity in the Andean Highlands

### Climate Change Impacts on Agriculture

Climate change is altering rainfall patterns, leading to increased frequency and severity of droughts and floods, affecting agricultural productivity, food security, and economic stability in the Andean highlands. Rising temperatures also increase evaporation rates, exacerbating water scarcity.

### Importance of Resilient Irrigation Methods

Resilient irrigation methods are crucial for ensuring food security and economic stability in the face of climate change. Traditional irrigation systems often rely on non-renewable energy sources, contributing to greenhouse gas emissions and water loss through evaporation and seepage.

## Agroforestry’s Role in Climate Resilience

Agroforestry combines trees with crops or livestock, improving soil health, biodiversity, and carbon sequestration. Case studies demonstrate how agroforestry systems improve water retention and reduce erosion, enhancing the resilience of agricultural ecosystems.

### Benefits of Agroforestry

• Improved soil fertility and structure
• Increased biodiversity and ecosystem services
• Enhanced carbon sequestration and climate regulation
• Reduced soil erosion and increased water retention

## Micro-Hydro Systems for Irrigation Efficiency

Micro-hydro technology harnesses the energy potential of small-scale hydroelectric systems, providing a reliable and renewable source of power. This reduces reliance on diesel generators and increases energy efficiency in irrigation systems.

### Benefits of Micro-Hydro Technology

• Reduced greenhouse gas emissions from non-renewable energy sources
• Increased energy efficiency and reduced water loss through evaporation
• Improved reliability and consistency of power supply
• Enhanced resilience to climate-related disruptions

Pilot projects demonstrate the combined use of agroforestry and micro-hydro, showcasing cost-effectiveness and potential for scaling up. These integrated systems improve water retention, reduce erosion, and enhance agricultural productivity.
