**Agroforestry-Based Micro-Hydro Systems for Climate-Resilient Irrigation in the Mesoamerican Highlands**

**Executive Summary:**
The Mesoamerican Highlands face severe water scarcity challenges exacerbated by climate change, threatening food security and economic stability. This brief explores the potential of agroforestry-based micro-hydro systems to address these issues through integrated solutions.

**Water Scarcity in the Mesoamerican Highlands**

Climate change impacts on agriculture in the Mesoamerican Highlands are significant. Rising temperatures and altered precipitation patterns have led to reduced crop yields and increased water scarcity (NOAA Climate Dataset). To ensure food security and economic stability, resilient irrigation methods are crucial. Current practices suffer from high water loss percentages due to evaporation and seepage.

Micro-hydro technology is an efficient way to generate energy while reducing reliance on diesel generators. The benefits of micro-hydro include:
