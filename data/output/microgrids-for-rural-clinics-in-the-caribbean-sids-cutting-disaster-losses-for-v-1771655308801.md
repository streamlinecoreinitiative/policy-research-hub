## Executive Summary

This policy brief highlights the critical need to strengthen environmental resilience in Sub-Saharan Africa through water security, clean energy access, and climate adaptation measures. We recommend prioritizing investments in water security, clean energy, and climate adaptation projects that are both effective and feasible within existing resources.

* Accelerating water security initiatives by investing in desalination plants and advanced water treatment technologies to enhance access to safe drinking water.
* Promoting renewable energy adoption through the development of solar-powered mini-grids and decentralized renewable energy systems to increase electricity access, particularly in rural areas.

## Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa faces severe challenges related to water scarcity, inadequate electricity access, and vulnerability to climate change impacts such as droughts and floods. These issues exacerbate poverty, health risks, and economic instability. The region's limited financial resources and infrastructure pose significant barriers to implementing comprehensive solutions for environmental resilience.

## Policy Recommendations

1. **Accelerate Water Security Initiatives**:
	* Invest in desalination plants and advanced water treatment technologies to enhance access to safe drinking water.
	* Implement community-based water resource management programs that promote sustainable use of local water sources.
2. **Promote Renewable Energy Adoption**:
	* Develop solar-powered mini-grids and decentralized renewable energy systems to increase electricity access, particularly in rural areas.
	* Encourage public-private partnerships for financing large-scale renewable energy projects through concessional loans or grants.
