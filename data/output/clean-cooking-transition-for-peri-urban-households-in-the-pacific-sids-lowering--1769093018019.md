## Executive Summary

Lower-income countries, particularly those in Sub-Saharan Africa, face significant challenges with water security, electricity access, and climate resilience. To address these issues, we recommend prioritizing investments in clean energy and climate adaptation strategies. ## Policy Recommendations

1. **Expand Renewable Energy Projects**:
   - Invest in microgrids for remote areas where grid extension is impractical or too costly.
   - Support small-scale solar projects to improve household energy access and reduce dependency on fossil fuels.

2. **Enhance Water Security Through Climate Resilient Infrastructure**:
   - Implement rainwater harvesting systems, which can significantly increase water availability in arid regions.
   - Promote watershed co-management with indigenous councils for sustainable land use practices.

3. **Promote Integrated Adaptation Strategies**:
   - Develop early warning systems and disaster preparedness plans to mitigate the impacts of climate change events.
   - Foster public-private partnerships (PPP) to leverage additional funding and expertise in implementing these strategies.

## Implementation Considerations

- **Cost**: Microgrids can range from $1,000 to $5,000 per household, depending on size and complexity. Community financing mechanisms should be explored to reduce upfront costs.
- **Barriers**: Resistance from local authorities or communities who fear technological dependency may require education campaigns and community engagement strategies.

## Conclusion

Lower-income countries must prioritize integrated approaches that blend clean energy access with water security improvements for climate adaptation. By investing in proven, scalable solutions, these nations can enhance resilience against future shocks while aligning efforts with broader development goals.
