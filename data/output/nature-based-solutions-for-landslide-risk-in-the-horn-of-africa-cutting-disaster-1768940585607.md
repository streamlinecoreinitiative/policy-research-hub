## Policy Recommendations

1. **Investment in Water Infrastructure:**
   - Implement large-scale water projects such as desalination plants to improve access to safe drinking water.
   - Sources: World Bank Open Data (2024), UN-Water/WHO, UN SDG Report 2023.
2. **Promote Renewable Energy Initiatives:**
   - Expand renewable energy projects like solar and wind farms to increase the share of clean energy in final consumption.
   - Sources: World Bank Open Data (2021), World Bank Open Data (2024).
3. **Climate Adaptation Measures:**
   - Develop early warning systems for climate events, invest in drought-resistant crops and infrastructure, and enhance water storage facilities.
   - Sources: NOAA Climate Data (placeholder), UN Framework Convention on Climate Change.

## Implementation Considerations

- **Costs:** Estimates of investment in water projects range from $10 to $50 billion per year depending on the scale and location. Renewable energy investments can be significantly lower, but vary widely based on technology and geography.
- **Barriers:** Political will, funding constraints, technical expertise, and public-private partnerships are key challenges.

## Conclusion

Addressing water security, clean energy access, and climate adaptation in Sub-Saharan Africa requires a multi-faceted approach.
