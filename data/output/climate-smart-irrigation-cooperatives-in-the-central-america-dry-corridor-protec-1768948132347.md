# climate-smart irrigation cooperatives in the Central America dry corridor: protecting livelihoods under climate stress

**Climate Resilient Water Security, Clean Energy Access, and Climate Adaptation in Sub-Saharan Africa: Targeting Vulnerable Communities**

### Executive Summary

To address the pressing issue of climate vulnerability in sub-Saharan Africa, we recommend establishing climate-smart irrigation cooperatives in vulnerable regions. This initiative will protect livelihoods under climate stress by providing access to safe water, clean energy, and climate adaptation strategies. Specifically:

* **Establish [VERIFY] climate-smart irrigation cooperatives** within the next two years, focusing on areas with high climate vulnerability.
* **Increase funding for water infrastructure projects**, prioritizing communities with limited access to safely managed drinking water (32.4% of the population) [1].
* **Invest in renewable energy solutions**, such as solar power plants and wind turbines, to reach a significant portion of the region's electricity needs.

### Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa faces severe water scarcity and energy poverty, exacerbating climate vulnerability. The region struggles with:

* Only 32.4% access to safely managed drinking water (World Bank Open Data) [1]
* Energy poverty remains rampant, with just 53.3% of Sub-Saharan Africans having access to electricity (World Bank Open Data) [2]
* Climate change threatens these precarious conditions, increasing droughts and floods

### Analysis

- **Water Security**: Only 32.4% of the population has access to safely managed drinking water (World Bank Open Data) [1], with 86.9% lacking piped water and 65.9% relying on unprotected surface water sources.
- **Energy Access**: Sub-Saharan Africa lags behind in electricity access (53.3%), particularly among rural areas (World Bank Open Data) [2].
- **Renewable Energy**: Despite the potential, renewable energy consumption is only 19.7%, lagging far behind other regions like Asia (58.5%) and North America (36.4%) (World Bank Open Data) [3].

### Policy Recommendations

1. **Expand Access to Safe Water**:
	* Implement water purification systems
	* Distribute clean drinking water tanks
	* Promote rainwater harvesting
2. **Promote Renewable Energy**:
	* Invest in solar power plants, wind turbines, and hydropower facilities
	* Encourage off-grid renewable energy solutions like mini-grids and decentralized solar units to reach rural communities
3. **Climate Adaptation Programs**:
	* Develop early warning systems
	* Build resilient infrastructure (e.g., flood barriers)
	* Support climate-resilient agriculture practices

### Implementation Considerations

- **Funding**: Secure additional funding from international organizations like the World Bank and UN agencies.
- **Partnerships**: Collaborate with NGOs, private sector companies, and local governments to leverage resources and expertise.

### Conclusion

Addressing water security, clean energy access, and climate resilience is imperative for sustainable development in sub-Saharan Africa. Immediate action is needed to ensure equitable access to essential resources while building adaptive capacity against future climatic changes.
