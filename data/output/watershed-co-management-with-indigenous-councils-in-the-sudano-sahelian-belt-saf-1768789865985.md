# Agroforestry-Based Micro-Hydro Systems for Climate-Resilient Irrigation in the Central African Rainforest

## Executive Summary

The Sudano-Sahelian belt faces significant water scarcity challenges due to climate change, deforestation, and population growth. This brief explores the potential of agroforestry-based micro-hydro systems to address these issues through integrated approaches.

## Introduction to Water Scarcity

The Central African Rainforest is affected by water scarcity due to factors such as climate change, deforestation, and population growth. Current irrigation practices rely heavily on diesel generators, which contribute to greenhouse gas emissions and are costly to maintain. Agroforestry projects have demonstrated potential for increased soil moisture retention and reduced canal losses.
