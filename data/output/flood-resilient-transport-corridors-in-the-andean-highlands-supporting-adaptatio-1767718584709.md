**Agroforestry-Based Micro-Hydro Systems for Climate-Resilient Irrigation in the Ethiopian Highlands**

**Executive Summary**

The proposal focuses on integrating agroforestry with micro-hydro systems as a solution to water scarcity in the Ethiopian Highlands. This approach addresses immediate water scarcity issues and contributes to broader sustainable development goals in agriculture and renewable energy adoption.

**Climate Change Impacts and Water Scarcity in the Ethiopian Highlands**

The Ethiopian Highlands face severe water scarcity due to climate change impacts, including high precipitation variability (annual range of 600-1400 mm) [verify] and average annual rainfall of 800-1200 mm. Traditional irrigation methods are energy-intensive and economically burdensome.

**Policy Recommendations and Future Research Directions**

To create supportive policy environments for sustainable solutions:

*   Incentives for farmers (subsidies, low-interest loans)
*   Grid integration with micro-hydro systems
*   Infrastructure development (roads, storage facilities)

Local surveys and case studies are necessary to assess the impact of these policies in similar regions.

The integration of agroforestry and micro-hydro systems offers a promising solution to water scarcity in the Ethiopian Highlands. Addressing immediate challenges while contributing to broader sustainable development goals is crucial for ensuring food security, economic growth, and environmental resilience.
