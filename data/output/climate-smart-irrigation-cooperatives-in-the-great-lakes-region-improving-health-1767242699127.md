# Climate-Smart Irrigation Cooperatives in the Great Lakes Region: Enhancing Resilience and Health Outcomes in Heatwaves

## Executive Summary

Climate change exacerbates water scarcity and heatwaves in the Great Lakes region, posing significant health risks to vulnerable communities. This brief outlines a comprehensive approach to establish climate-smart irrigation cooperatives that integrate agroforestry with micro-hydro systems.

## Climate Change Impacts on Water Scarcity in the Great Lakes Region

Climate change is projected to alter precipitation patterns and increase evaporation rates in the region, exacerbating existing water scarcity issues. The agriculture sector relies heavily on irrigation systems often plagued by inefficiencies and non-renewable energy sources.

### Current Irrigation Practices and Challenges

Traditional irrigation methods face challenges including:

* High water losses through evaporation and seepage
* Energy-intensive pumping systems relying on fossil fuels
* Limited access to clean water, particularly for smallholder farmers

## Agroforestry and Micro-Hydro Systems: A Climate-Resilient Solution

Integrating agroforestry with micro-hydro systems offers a promising solution:

### Benefits of Agroforestry:

* Improved soil health through vegetation cover
* Enhanced water retention capacity
* Increased biodiversity for natural pest control and pollination services

### Micro-Hydro Systems:

* Renewable energy source, reducing reliance on fossil fuels
* High efficiency rates compared to traditional pumping systems
* Potential for community-based ownership and operation
