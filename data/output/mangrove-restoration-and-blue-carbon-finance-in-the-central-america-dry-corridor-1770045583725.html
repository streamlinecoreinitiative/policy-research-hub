Mangrove Restoration and Blue Carbon Finance in the Central America Dry Corridor: Cutting Disaster Losses for Vulnerable Groups

Executive Summary

To address the pressing issues of water security, clean energy access, and climate resilience in Sub-Saharan Africa, we recommend prioritizing mangrove restoration and blue carbon finance initiatives. These efforts can help reduce disaster losses and enhance livelihoods for vulnerable communities. Specifically, we propose:

* Expanding renewable energy deployment to meet electricity demands sustainably

  • Investing in water supply infrastructure to improve access to safe drinking water
  • Enhancing early warning systems for climate-related disasters

    These interventions are crucial due to the region's 32.4% access to safely managed drinking water (World Bank Open Data, 2024) and only 53.3% of its population having access to electricity (World Bank Open Data, 2023).

    To address these challenges, we propose the following policy recommendations:

    1. Expand Renewable Energy Deployment: Governments should prioritize the development and deployment of renewable energy sources to meet electricity demands sustainably. Policies such as feed-in tariffs and subsidies for solar, wind, and hydro power can incentivize private sector investments.

  • Invest in Water Supply Infrastructure: Significant investments are needed in water supply projects like boreholes, pipelines, and storage facilities to improve access to safe drinking water. Targeted grants and loans from international development banks could support these initiatives.
  • Enhance Early Warning Systems: Establishing robust early warning systems for climate-related disasters can enable timely responses, thereby reducing loss of life and property damage. Collaboration with local communities is essential in developing effective early warning mechanisms.
