Executive Summary:

The Mekong Delta faces significant climate change impacts, including rising sea levels and increased salinity intrusion, threatening water security for its coastal communities. Traditional desalination methods are energy-intensive and costly, exacerbating economic challenges for vulnerable groups. This brief explores the potential of integrating agroforestry with wind-powered desalination systems to provide a sustainable solution that enhances both water security and renewable energy use.
