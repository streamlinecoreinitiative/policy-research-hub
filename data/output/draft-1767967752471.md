**Agroforestry-Based Micro-Hydro Systems for Climate-Resilient Irrigation in the Central African Rainforest**

### Executive Summary

This brief presents an integrated approach to addressing severe water scarcity and climate change impacts through agroforestry and micro-hydro systems in lower-income countries, focusing on the Central African Rainforest. The proposed solution combines agroforestry practices with micro-hydro technology to provide renewable energy for irrigation systems.

### Addressing Severe Water Scarcity and Climate Change Impacts

#### Introduction to Water Scarcity

Water scarcity is a pressing issue in the Central African Rainforest, where agriculture accounts for a significant portion of water usage. Traditional irrigation practices are often labor-intensive and costly, with high maintenance expenses and diesel generator fuel costs.

Combining agroforestry practices with micro-hydro technology has shown promising results:

To support the adoption of agroforestry-based micro-hydro systems:

1.  **Subsidies and Grants:** Government subsidies for initial investment in micro-hydro system installation
2.  **Infrastructure Development:** Community-led micro-hydro system installations with local government support
