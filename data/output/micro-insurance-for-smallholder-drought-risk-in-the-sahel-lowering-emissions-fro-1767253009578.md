# micro-insurance for smallholder drought risk in the Sahel: lowering emissions from critical sectors

**Micro-insurance for Smallholder Drought Risk in the Sahel: Enhancing Resilience and Reducing Emissions**

**Executive Summary**

This brief explores the intersection of environmental resilience, water security, clean energy, and climate adaptation in lower-income countries, with a specific focus on micro-insurance for smallholder drought risk in the Sahel region. The proposed project aims to address severe water scarcity through integrated agroforestry and micro-hydro systems.

**Environmental Resilience in Lower-Income Countries**

The Sahel region is particularly vulnerable to droughts, which have severe impacts on agriculture, food security, and economic stability. Climate change exacerbates this vulnerability through erratic rainfall patterns and increased temperatures [verify].

**Agroforestry-Based Micro-Hydro Systems for Climate-Resilient Irrigation**

Agroforestry practices have been shown to improve soil health, biodiversity, and carbon sequestration while enhancing water retention and reducing erosion [1]. When combined with micro-hydro systems, these approaches can provide efficient and renewable energy for irrigation.

**Policy Recommendations and Future Research Directions**

To support the adoption of integrated agroforestry and micro-hydro systems at scale, policy recommendations include:

* Subsidies and low-interest loans to encourage investment in these technologies
* Infrastructure development to facilitate access to renewable energy sources
* Community engagement strategies to ensure acceptance and benefits are shared equitably

Future research directions should focus on long-term sustainability assessments of integrated agroforestry-micro-hydro systems.
