## Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa's environmental challenges are exacerbated by limited access to safe drinking water, which affects health outcomes and economic productivity. Despite efforts, only 32.4% of the population has access to safely managed drinking water [1], a stark contrast to global averages. Similarly, renewable energy consumption is significantly lower at just 19.7%, falling short of ambitious SDG targets [2].

- **Population Lacking Safe Water**: Approximately 2.2 billion people globally lack safe water sources, with Sub-Saharan Africa accounting for the majority [3].
- **Renewable Energy Consumption**: The world's share of renewable energy consumption is currently around 19.7% [4].

## Analysis

The current situation in Sub-Saharan Africa highlights critical gaps in ensuring environmental resilience through robust water security and clean energy systems. These challenges not only affect daily living conditions but also hinder economic development, particularly in rural areas where access to basic services like electricity remains limited.

- **Water Security**: Only 32.4% of the population has access to safely managed drinking water [1], with Sub-Saharan Africa disproportionately affected.
- **Renewable Energy**: At just 19.7%, renewable energy consumption is far below global targets and essential for sustainable development [2].

To address these challenges, specific interventions are recommended:

### Microgrids for Rural Clinics

### Climate-Smart Irrigation Cooperatives

## Implementation Considerations

To ensure these interventions are effectively implemented within the region's constraints, several practical steps should be considered:
