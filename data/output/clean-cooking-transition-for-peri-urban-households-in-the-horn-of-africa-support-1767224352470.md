# Clean Cooking Transition for Peri-Urban Households in the Horn of Africa: Supporting Adaptation Targets in NDCs

## Executive Summary

The Horn of Africa is highly vulnerable to climate change, with severe water scarcity and food insecurity affecting peri-urban households. This brief argues that a clean cooking transition, supported by integrated agroforestry and micro-hydro systems, can help address these challenges while contributing to climate adaptation targets in Nationally Determined Contributions (NDCs). By leveraging renewable energy sources and improving water management, this approach can enhance the resilience of peri-urban households and promote sustainable development.

## Introduction to Water Scarcity

Climate change has significant impacts on the Ethiopian Highlands. Average annual rainfall ranges from 800-1200 mm [verify]. Precipitation variability is high, with an annual range of 600-1400 mm [verify].

## Current Irrigation Practices and Challenges

Existing irrigation methods in the region rely on borehole pumps, which consume energy. Canal systems experience significant water losses due to evaporation and seepage.

## Agroforestry's Role in Climate Resilience

Agroforestry improves soil health, biodiversity, and carbon sequestration [verify]. Benefits include:

* Soil organic matter: 2-8% [verify]
* Biodiversity increase: 15-30% [verify]

## Micro-Hydro Systems for Irrigation Efficiency

Micro-hydro systems provide sustainable and renewable energy solutions. Energy efficiency is high at 70-80%, and costs savings compared to traditional irrigation methods are substantial.

## Integration of Agroforestry and Micro-Hydro in Climate-Resilient Water Supply

Integrating agroforestry and micro-hydro systems offers cost-effective, sustainable outcomes:

* Total project cost: $10,000-$25,000 per hectare [verify]
* Return on investment (ROI): 3-5 years [verify]

## Policy Recommendations and Future Research Directions

To support the adoption of agroforestry-micro-hydro systems:

1. **Incentives for farmers**: Subsidies, low-interest loans, or tax breaks can encourage the transition to clean cooking and sustainable irrigation practices.
2. **Grid integration with micro-hydro systems**: Developing infrastructure to integrate micro-hydro power into the grid will enhance energy efficiency and reduce costs.
3. **Infrastructure development**: Investing in roads, storage facilities, and other supporting infrastructure is crucial for the long-term success of these integrated solutions.

## Actions/Recommendations

1. Conduct local surveys and case studies to assess community acceptance and benefits derived from integrated agroforestry-micro-hydro systems.
2. Develop a comprehensive cost-benefit analysis to inform policy decisions and investment strategies.
3. Establish partnerships between government agencies, NGOs, and private sector organizations to promote the adoption of clean cooking and sustainable irrigation practices.

## Closing Outlook

The transition to clean cooking and sustainable irrigation practices is critical for addressing climate change impacts in peri-urban households in the Horn of Africa. By integrating agroforestry with micro-hydro systems, we can enhance water security, reduce energy consumption, and promote long-term sustainability. This approach aligns with adaptation targets in NDCs and offers a promising solution to the region's pressing development challenges.
