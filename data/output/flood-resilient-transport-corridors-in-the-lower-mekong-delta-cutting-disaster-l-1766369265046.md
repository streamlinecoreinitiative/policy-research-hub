# Flood-Resilient Transport Corridors in the Lower Mekong Delta: Enhancing Climate Resilience for Vulnerable Groups

## Executive Summary

The Lower Mekong Delta is highly vulnerable to climate change, with frequent flooding posing significant risks to human life, infrastructure, and economic stability. This brief highlights the importance of flood-resilient transport corridors in mitigating these impacts on vulnerable groups.

## Introduction to Water Scarcity

Climate change exacerbates water scarcity in the Lower Mekong Delta through erratic rainfall patterns and increased evaporation from vast wetlands. Traditional irrigation methods are often energy-intensive and costly, straining economic resources for smallholder farmers in vulnerable communities.

### Current Irrigation Practices and Challenges

Existing irrigation systems face inefficiencies due to high evaporation rates and seepage losses, reducing crop yields and straining community resources.

### Micro-Hydro Systems for Irrigation Efficiency

Micro-hydro technology offers a low-cost, energy-efficient solution for powering irrigation pumps. Harnessing the power of small-scale hydroelectric systems reduces reliance on diesel generators and decreases greenhouse gas emissions associated with traditional irrigation methods.

## Integration of Agroforestry and Micro-Hydro in Climate-Resilient Water Supply

Pilot projects have demonstrated the effectiveness of combining agroforestry and micro-hydro systems to enhance water security and promote climate resilience. These integrated solutions improve crop yields, provide economic benefits through reduced energy costs, and increase food security.
