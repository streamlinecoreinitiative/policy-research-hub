### Introduction to Water Scarcity in the Indus Watershed

The Indus watershed spans across several countries, including Pakistan and India, where climate change is projected to increase precipitation variability (IPCC, 2019). Average annual rainfall in this region ranges from 800-1200 mm, with significant variability between years. Traditional irrigation systems are energy-intensive and inefficient, resulting in substantial water losses.

Existing irrigation methods consume significant amounts of energy while also contributing to water loss. Local surveys and case studies have shown that integrated agroforestry-micro-hydro systems can provide cost-effective and sustainable solutions for irrigation.
