Water Security, Clean Energy, and Climate Adaptation: Strengthening Resilience in Sub-Saharan Africa

Executive Summary

To address the pressing issues of water scarcity, energy deficits, and climate change impacts in Sub-Saharan Africa, we recommend implementing solar-powered desalination plants for coastal regions, expanding microgrids in rural areas, and promoting agroforestry value chains to improve energy efficiency and water conservation. These interventions are feasible with existing data and can be scaled up through partnerships between governments, NGOs, and international organizations.

Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa is facing significant challenges related to water security and climate adaptation due to its arid conditions and increasing temperatures. The region has the lowest access to safe drinking water (32.4%) compared to 73.7% globally [1]. In terms of energy access, only 53.3% of Sub-Saharan Africans have reliable electricity supply, far behind global averages [2]. These deficits are exacerbated by climate change, leading to more frequent droughts and floods, further straining already limited resources.
