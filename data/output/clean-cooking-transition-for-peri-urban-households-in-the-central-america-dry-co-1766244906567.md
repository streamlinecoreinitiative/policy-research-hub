# Clean Cooking Transition for Peri-Urban Households in the Central America Dry Corridor: Supporting Adaptation Targets in NDCs

## Executive Summary

The Central America dry corridor is highly vulnerable to climate change, characterized by droughts and water scarcity. Household energy needs are largely met through unsustainable biomass burning, contributing to deforestation and indoor air pollution. This brief highlights the importance of transitioning to clean cooking practices for peri-urban households in the region, focusing on supporting adaptation targets outlined in Nationally Determined Contributions (NDCs). A combination of policy support, technology innovation, and community engagement is necessary to achieve a low-carbon, climate-resilient energy future.

## Current Energy Situation in Peri-Urban Households

*   **Energy Mix:** Biomass (wood, charcoal) accounts for a significant portion of household energy needs. [verify]
*   **Indoor Air Pollution:** Estimated 30% of households experience indoor air pollution-related health issues.
*   **Deforestation:** Widespread deforestation due to biomass collection and land conversion.

## Policy Recommendations

To support a transition to clean cooking practices in peri-urban households, we recommend:

1.  **Policy Frameworks:**
    *   Develop and implement policies supporting the adoption of clean cooking technologies (e.g., solar cookers, biogas).
    *   Establish standards for biomass quality and certification.
2.  **Technology Innovation:**
    *   Encourage research and development of affordable, efficient, and accessible clean cooking solutions.
    *   Support pilot projects demonstrating the effectiveness of new technologies.
3.  **Community Engagement:**
    *   Conduct public awareness campaigns highlighting health benefits and environmental impacts of clean cooking practices.
    *   Foster partnerships between local governments, NGOs, and private sector entities to promote technology transfer and capacity building.

To inform policy decisions and support the transition to clean cooking practices:

## Needed Evidence

To validate the effectiveness of policy recommendations and support scaling up clean cooking practices:

1.  **Case Studies:** Documented examples of successful pilot projects or community-led initiatives promoting clean cooking technologies.
2.  **Economic Analysis:** Cost-benefit analysis of adopting clean cooking technologies, including upfront costs and long-term savings.
3.  **Community Feedback Mechanisms:** Established channels for community feedback and participation in decision-making processes.

## Actions/Recommendations

To accelerate the transition to clean cooking practices in peri-urban households:

1.  **Develop a National Clean Cooking Strategy:** Outline policy objectives, technology innovation priorities, and community engagement strategies.
2.  **Establish a Regional Clean Cooking Network:** Facilitate knowledge sharing, capacity building, and technology transfer among countries in the Central America dry corridor.
3.  **Secure Funding for Clean Cooking Initiatives:** Mobilize resources from international organizations, private sector entities, and local governments to support pilot projects and scaling up efforts.

## Closing Outlook

Transitioning to clean cooking practices in peri-urban households is critical for reducing greenhouse gas emissions, improving indoor air quality, and promoting climate resilience in the Central America dry corridor. By supporting policy frameworks, technology innovation, and community engagement, we can accelerate the adoption of low-carbon energy solutions and contribute to achieving adaptation targets outlined in NDCs.
