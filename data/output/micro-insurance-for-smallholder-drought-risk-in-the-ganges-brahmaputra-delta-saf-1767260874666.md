**Micro-insurance for Smallholder Drought Risk in the Ganges-Brahmaputra Delta: Safeguarding Freshwater Ecosystems**

**Executive Summary**

Climate change exacerbates drought risks in lower-income countries, particularly in regions like the Ganges-Brahmaputra delta. This brief highlights the importance of integrating micro-insurance with climate-resilient water management practices to safeguard freshwater ecosystems and support smallholder farmers.

**Water Scarcity in the Ganges-Brahmaputra Delta**

The Ganges-Brahmaputra delta is one of the most densely populated regions in the world, with a significant portion of Bangladesh's population residing within its boundaries. Climate change has led to increased precipitation variability, resulting in more frequent and severe droughts.

**Current Irrigation Practices**

Traditional irrigation methods in the Ganges-Brahmaputra delta include borehole pumps and canal systems. These systems are energy-intensive, contributing to greenhouse gas emissions and exacerbating water scarcity.

**Agroforestry's Role in Climate Resilience**

Agroforestry practices can improve soil health, biodiversity, and carbon sequestration, making them an essential component of climate-resilient agriculture. Research has shown that agroforestry can increase soil organic matter by 2-8% [verify] and biodiversity by 15-30 native species.

**Micro-Hydro Systems for Irrigation Efficiency**

Micro-hydro systems provide a sustainable and renewable energy source for irrigation, generating power ranging from 10-50 kW per system with an energy efficiency of 70-80%. Integrating micro-hydro systems with agroforestry practices can reduce reliance on fossil fuels and enhance water security.

**Integration of Agroforestry and Micro-Hydro in Climate-Resilient Water Supply**

The integration of agroforestry and micro-hydro systems offers a cost-effective solution to address water scarcity. The total project cost per hectare is estimated to be $10,000-$25,000 [verify], with a return on investment (ROI) of 3-5 years.

**Policy Recommendations and Future Research Directions**

To create a supportive environment for climate-resilient agriculture, policymakers can consider the following recommendations:

* Incentivize farmers through subsidies or low-interest loans to adopt agroforestry-micro-hydro systems.
* Integrate micro-hydro systems with the national grid to ensure a stable energy supply.
* Develop infrastructure to support the adoption of climate-resilient agriculture practices.

**Actions/Recommendations**

**Closing Outlook**

The integration of micro-insurance with climate-resilient water management practices can safeguard freshwater ecosystems and support smallholder farmers in the Ganges-Brahmaputra delta.
