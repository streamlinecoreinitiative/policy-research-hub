Water Security:

* Sub-Saharan Africa lacks sufficient infrastructure to meet growing water demands, leading to frequent waterborne diseases.
* The region's freshwater withdrawal is relatively high, with [VERIFY] 9.2% of resources used globally (World Bank Open Data, 2021).
* Climate change will further exacerbate water scarcity, with projected changes in precipitation patterns and increased evaporation due to warmer temperatures.

* Despite global progress, [VERIFY] 53.3% of Sub-Saharan African populations remain without reliable electricity access.
* Renewable energy consumption stands at [NEEDS SOURCE] globally (World Bank, 2020), but this is largely driven by developed countries.
* Decentralized renewable energy projects can help bridge the gap in clean energy access for off-grid communities.

Climate Adaptation:

* The region is highly vulnerable due to its arid and semi-arid climate, making it susceptible to droughts and floods.
* Climate-smart agriculture practices can enhance agricultural productivity and resilience to climate change impacts (e.g., drought-resistant crops).
* Indigenous knowledge systems for weather forecasting and early warning systems can also be leveraged.

### Policy Recommendations

1. **Infrastructure Development**:
	* Invest in community-based water management systems (e.g., solar-powered wells) to ensure equitable access to clean drinking water.
	* Develop decentralized renewable energy projects using solar panels for electricity generation.
2. **Renewable Energy Promotion**:
	* Encourage the use of renewable energy sources such as hydro and wind power, supported by government grants and subsidies.
3. **Climate Resilience Programs**:
	* Implement climate-smart agriculture practices that enhance agricultural productivity and resilience to climate change impacts (e.g., drought-resistant crops).
	* Support indigenous knowledge systems for weather forecasting and early warning systems.
