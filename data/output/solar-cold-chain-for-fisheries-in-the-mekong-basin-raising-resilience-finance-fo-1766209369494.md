# Solar Cold-Chain for Fisheries in the Mekong Basin: Enhancing Resilience Finance through Integrated Solutions

## Executive Summary

The Mekong Basin supports over 60 million people, including small-scale fisheries communities that rely on water security and clean energy to sustain their livelihoods. Climate change exacerbates water scarcity, erratic rainfall patterns, and increased evaporation from highland wetlands, threatening these communities' existence. This brief explores the potential for solar-powered cold-chain systems in fisheries, highlighting integrated agroforestry-micro-hydro solutions to enhance resilience finance.

## Introduction to Water Scarcity in the Mekong Basin

The Mekong Basin is a critical agricultural region facing severe water scarcity due to climate change. Erratic rainfall patterns and increased evaporation from highland wetlands have significant impacts on agriculture, including:

*   **Water scarcity:** Reduced water availability affects irrigation systems.
*   **Food security:** Climate-related shocks threaten the livelihoods of small-scale farmers and their families.
*   **Economic stability:** Water scarcity exacerbates poverty among rural communities by increasing costs for energy-intensive irrigation methods.

## Micro-Hydro Systems for Irrigation Efficiency

Micro-hydro technology offers:

*   **Renewable energy:** Generate electricity from water flow, reducing reliance on diesel generators.
*   **Increased efficiency:** Improve irrigation system performance through precise water control and reduced energy consumption.

## Integration of Agroforestry and Micro-Hydro in Climate-Resilient Water Supply

Pilot projects integrating agroforestry with micro-hydro systems demonstrate:

*   **Water retention:** Improved soil moisture levels through tree-root networks.
*   **Reduced erosion:** Decreased sedimentation in waterways, maintaining water quality.
*   **Cost-effectiveness:** Lower energy costs and increased crop yields.
