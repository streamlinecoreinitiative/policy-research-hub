# agroforestry value chains for cash crops in the Lower Mekong delta: cutting disaster losses for vulnerable groups

**Agroforestry-Based Micro-Hydro Systems for Climate-Resilient Irrigation in the Ethiopian Highlands**

**Executive Summary**

The Ethiopian Highlands face severe water scarcity due to climate change, characterized by erratic rainfall patterns. Traditional irrigation methods exacerbate poverty among smallholder farmers, making them energy-intensive and costly. This brief outlines a sustainable solution: integrating agroforestry with micro-hydro systems for climate-resilient irrigation. The proposed approach improves soil health, biodiversity, and carbon sequestration while providing renewable energy for irrigation.

**Water Scarcity in the Ethiopian Highlands**

Climate change impacts agriculture in the Ethiopian Highlands, characterized by erratic rainfall patterns. The average annual rainfall ranges from 800-1200 mm [verify], with precipitation variability (annual range) of 600-1400 mm [verify]. Traditional irrigation systems exacerbate water scarcity due to high water loss percentages: evaporation accounts for approximately 30%, and seepage accounts for around 5%.
