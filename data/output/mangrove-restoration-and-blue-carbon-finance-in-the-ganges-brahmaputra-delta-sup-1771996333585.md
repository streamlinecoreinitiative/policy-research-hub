## Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa is the region with the lowest electricity access (53.3%) among all regions globally, impacting nearly 60 million people (World Bank Open Data, 2023). Just 73.7% of the population has access to safely managed drinking water, leaving millions without reliable sources (World Bank Open Data, 2024). The continent's renewable energy consumption stands at only 19.7% of total final energy consumption, far below global averages (World Bank Open Data, 2020).

Climate change is exacerbating these challenges, with a projected temperature rise of +1.1°C since pre-industrial times (IPCC AR6). Without urgent intervention, Sub-Saharan Africa will struggle to meet its Sustainable Development Goal (SDG) targets for water and energy.
