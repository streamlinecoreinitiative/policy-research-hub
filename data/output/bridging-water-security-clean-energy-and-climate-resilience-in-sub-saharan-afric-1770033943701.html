Executive Summary

Sub-Saharan Africa faces significant challenges in water security, clean energy access, and climate adaptation. With only 32.4% of the population having access to safely managed drinking water (World Bank, 2024) and just 53.3% with electricity (World Bank, 2023), it is imperative to address these issues promptly. This policy brief recommends implementing microgrids for rural clinics, adopting clean cooking solutions in peri-urban areas, enhancing climate-resilient school feeding supply chains, and promoting watershed co-management with indigenous councils.

Analysis

The disparity in water security is a critical issue that requires urgent attention. Microgrids for rural clinics can significantly improve access to safe water and electricity. For instance, a study by the World Bank found that such systems can increase access to drinking water and electricity in remote areas (World Bank, 2024). Adoption of clean cooking solutions like biogas stoves reduces reliance on traditional biomass fuels, improving air quality and reducing indoor pollution. Implementation of these solutions is cost-effective and scalable.

Enhancing climate resilience through watershed co-management with indigenous councils can protect freshwater ecosystems from overexploitation. Indigenous knowledge systems are crucial for sustainable resource management. However, data on the effectiveness of such approaches is limited, and further research is needed to fully understand their impact (World Bank, 2023).
