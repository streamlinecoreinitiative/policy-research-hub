# Early-Warning Systems and Last-Mile Alerts in the Lower Mekong Delta: Stabilizing Food Security During Droughts

## Executive Summary

The Lower Mekong delta is highly vulnerable to climate change, experiencing frequent droughts that severely impact food security and economic stability. This brief proposes an integrated approach to address water scarcity and poverty through early-warning systems and last-mile alerts for agroforestry-based micro-hydro irrigation in the region.

### Introduction to Water Scarcity in the Lower Mekong Delta

The Lower Mekong delta is characterized by a monsoon climate with seasonal variations in rainfall and temperature. Climate change has exacerbated these natural fluctuations, leading to more frequent and severe droughts. Agriculture is the primary source of livelihood for millions of people in this region, making water scarcity a critical issue for food security and economic stability.

* **Overview of climate change impacts on agriculture:**
	+ Rising temperatures and changing precipitation patterns
	+ Increased frequency and severity of extreme weather events (droughts, floods)
* **Importance of resilient irrigation methods:**
	+ Ensuring consistent crop yields and quality
	+ Maintaining food security and economic stability
