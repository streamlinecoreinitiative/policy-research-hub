# climate-smart irrigation cooperatives in the Mekong basin: supporting adaptation targets in NDCs

**Climate Resilient Energy and Water Solutions for Sub-Saharan Africa: Strengthening Adaptation Efforts**

## Executive Summary

Sub-Saharan Africa lags behind globally in access to safe drinking water (32.4% vs 73.7%) and electricity (53.3% vs 91.6%). Climate resilience initiatives are crucial for improving these metrics. This policy brief recommends prioritizing investments in clean cooking technologies, renewable energy projects, and climate-smart irrigation systems to enhance adaptation efforts.

## Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa is home to approximately 800 million people, with over two billion lacking access to safe drinking water (UN-Water/WHO). Only about half of the population has access to electricity. Climate change exacerbates existing challenges, leading to increased frequency and intensity of droughts, floods, and other extreme weather events.

## Analysis

Access to safe drinking water is a significant challenge in Sub-Saharan Africa, with only 32.4% of the population having access (World Bank Open Data). This is far behind the global average of 73.7% (World Bank Open Data). Over two billion people worldwide lack access to safe drinking water, with a significant portion in Sub-Saharan Africa (UN-Water/WHO).

Climate change impacts are becoming more frequent and intense, threatening water security and increasing the need for resilient infrastructure.

## Policy Recommendations

1. **Increase Access to Safe Drinking Water**:
   - Invest in clean cooking technologies such as biogas digesters, solar cookers, and improved stoves.
   - Support community-led initiatives that promote rainwater harvesting and sanitation facilities.
2. **Promote Renewable Energy**: 
   - Implement policies encouraging the use of renewable energy sources like wind, solar, and hydro power.
   - Provide subsidies for households to purchase renewable energy solutions like solar home systems.
3. **Enhance Climate Resilient Irrigation Systems**:
   - Develop climate-smart irrigation projects that incorporate drought-resistant crops and efficient water management techniques.
   - Encourage the adoption of rainwater harvesting and groundwater recharge technologies.

## Implementation Considerations

Initial investment in clean cookstoves, renewable energy systems, and improved irrigation infrastructure can be substantial. However, long-term savings on healthcare costs due to reduced respiratory diseases from cleaner cooking practices and increased agricultural productivity through better water management justify the investments (World Health Organization).

Political will, lack of funding, technical expertise, and community engagement are potential challenges.

## Conclusion

Addressing climate resilience in Sub-Saharan Africa requires a multifaceted approach focusing on clean drinking water, renewable energy, and climate-smart agriculture. By implementing these recommendations, governments can significantly improve the lives of millions while laying the groundwork for sustainable development in the region.
