## Executive Summary

Sub-Saharan Africa faces severe water scarcity, limited electricity access, and increasing climate vulnerability. To address these challenges, we recommend implementing community-based water management systems, increasing renewable energy investment, and enhancing climate adaptation measures. These interventions will improve the region's economic stability and quality of life.

* Implement community-managed water systems to increase access to safely managed drinking water (currently at 32.4% in Sub-Saharan Africa) [1].
* Invest in solar panels for rural electrification and subsidize installation costs to expand electricity access (only 53.3% of sub-Saharan Africans have access to electricity) [2].
