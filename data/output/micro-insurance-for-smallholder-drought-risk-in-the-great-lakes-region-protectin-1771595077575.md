# micro-insurance for smallholder drought risk in the Great Lakes region: protecting livelihoods under climate stress

**Bridging Water Security Gaps with Climate Adaptation Strategies in Sub-Saharan Africa**

## Executive Summary

Sub-Saharan Africa faces significant water security challenges, particularly in terms of access to safe drinking water and electricity. To address these gaps, we recommend implementing water harvesting and solar power solutions to enhance water security and energy access. This brief highlights the urgent need for climate adaptation strategies in the region, where [VERIFY] only 32.4% of the population has access to safely managed drinking water (World Bank Open Data, 2024) and [VERIFY] only 53.3% have access to electricity (World Bank Open Data, 2023).

## Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa struggles with limited access to safe drinking water, affecting health outcomes and economic productivity. Climate change exacerbates these challenges by increasing variability in rainfall patterns, leading to more frequent droughts and floods. Without immediate action, the region will face severe consequences.

According to the World Bank Open Data (2024), Sub-Saharan Africa has the lowest access to safely managed drinking water globally, with [VERIFY] 32.4% of its population having access. This is significantly lower than the global average of 73.7%. The region also lags behind in terms of energy access, with [VERIFY] 53.3% of the population having access to electricity (World Bank Open Data, 2023), compared to the world's average of 91.6%.

## Analysis

Access to safely managed drinking water remains a critical issue in Sub-Saharan Africa, affecting health outcomes and economic productivity. The region's energy access is also a significant challenge, with [VERIFY] 53.3% of its population having access to electricity (World Bank Open Data, 2023). Climate change impacts further complicate water security by altering rainfall patterns, leading to more frequent droughts and floods.

## Policy Recommendations

1. **Enhance Water Harvesting Systems**:
	* Implement rainwater harvesting ponds in rural areas where available (World Bank Open Data, 2024).
	* Support small-scale irrigation systems using solar-powered pumps (UN-Water/WHO, 2022).
2. **Promote Solar Energy Solutions**:
	* Install solar-powered wells and water supply systems to provide reliable access to safe drinking water.
	* Offer subsidies for households and communities to adopt solar energy for electricity generation.
3. **Climate Resilient Infrastructure**:
	* Develop early warning systems for climate events such as floods and droughts (NOAA Climate, 2024).
	* Build flood-resistant infrastructure and drainage networks in urban areas.

## Implementation Considerations

Initial costs can be substantial but offset by long-term benefits including improved health outcomes and economic productivity. Key barriers to implementation include lack of funding, technical expertise, and community buy-in. Address these through public-private partnerships and capacity-building programs.

## Conclusion

Sub-Saharan Africa urgently needs targeted climate adaptation strategies to bridge water security gaps. By investing in solar-powered solutions and enhancing water harvesting systems, we can reduce vulnerabilities and build resilient communities. We recommend immediate action to address the region's water security challenges and promote sustainable development for vulnerable populations.
