**Harnessing Renewable Energy for Water Security in Sub-Saharan Africa**

## Executive Summary

Sub-Saharan Africa faces significant challenges in accessing clean water and electricity, exacerbated by climate change. To bridge these gaps, we recommend prioritizing renewable energy projects for water pumping, desalination, and electrification of rural areas. This approach can enhance both water security and sustainable electrification, aligning with SDG 7 (Access to Electricity) and SDG 6 (Water Quality). Specifically, governments should integrate renewable energy into national electrification plans to ensure universal access by 2030.

## Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa is among the regions with the lowest access to safe drinking water (32.4%) [1] and electricity (53.3%) [1], exacerbated by climate variability leading to droughts and floods. This situation puts millions of people at risk, particularly in rural areas where infrastructure is often inadequate. The lack of access to clean water and energy hinders economic growth, education, and healthcare outcomes.

## Analysis

Access to safely managed drinking water is crucial for health outcomes; Sub-Saharan Africa's limited access puts millions at risk [2]. Renewable energy sources such as solar, wind, and hydro can provide sustainable solutions for electricity generation and desalination in arid regions. Climate change increases the frequency of droughts and floods, worsening already inadequate water security.

## Policy Recommendations

To address the urgent need for renewable energy solutions, we recommend:

1. **Integrate renewable energy into national electrification plans**: Ensure universal access to electricity by 2030 through the integration of solar, wind, and hydro power.
2. **Invest in solar-powered pumps for irrigation systems and desalination plants**: Improve water security in coastal areas while reducing greenhouse gas emissions.
3. **Support small-scale hydro projects**: Provide electricity while promoting local economies through agro-industrial activities.
