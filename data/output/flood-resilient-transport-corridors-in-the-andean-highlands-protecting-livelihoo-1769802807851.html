Filling the Water Gap: Enhancing Access to Clean Energy and Climate Adaptation in Sub-Saharan Africa

Executive Summary

Sub-Saharan Africa faces a severe water security crisis, with only 32.4% of its population having access to safely managed drinking water (World Bank Open Data). This region also struggles with limited electricity access (53.3%) and low renewable energy consumption (19.7%), hindering economic development and climate resilience. To address these gaps, we recommend investing in renewable energy infrastructure, improving water supply systems, enhancing climate adaptation strategies, and integrating micro-insurance for smallholder farmers.

Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa is characterized by arid and semi-arid lands with limited freshwater resources (World Bank Open Data). Climate change exacerbates droughts and floods, further threatening food security and increasing the need for climate adaptation measures. The region's water scarcity issues are exacerbated by its growing population, projected to reach 2.1 billion by 2050 (UN DESA).
