# nature-based solutions for landslide risk in the Indus watershed: supporting adaptation targets in NDCs

**Nature-Based Solutions for Landslide Risk in the Indus Watershed: Supporting Adaptation Targets in NDCs**

**Executive Summary**

The Mekong Basin is a critical agricultural region facing severe water scarcity due to climate change, including rising sea levels and increased salinity intrusion. This brief explores the potential of integrating agroforestry with micro-hydro systems as a sustainable solution that enhances both water security and renewable energy use.

**Introduction to Water Scarcity in the Mekong Basin**

The Mekong Basin is home to over 60 million people, relying heavily on agriculture for livelihoods. Climate change has led to rising sea levels, increased salinity intrusion, and altered precipitation patterns, severely impacting agricultural productivity (UN Environment Programme). Rising sea levels are projected to increase saltwater intrusion into freshwater sources [verify].

**Agroforestry-Based Micro-Hydro Systems for Climate-Resilient Irrigation**

Agroforestry systems have been shown to improve soil health, biodiversity, and carbon sequestration. By integrating agroforestry with micro-hydro technology, communities can reduce their reliance on diesel generators and increase energy efficiency. Pilot projects in the Mekong Basin demonstrate the combined use of agroforestry and micro-hydro systems.

**Policy Recommendations and Future Research Directions**

To support adoption at scale:

1. **Develop policy frameworks**: Governments should establish policies supporting the integration of agroforestry and micro-hydro systems in irrigation infrastructure.
2. **Invest in capacity building**: Provide training and technical assistance to communities and local authorities on designing, implementing, and maintaining these integrated systems.
3. **Foster public-private partnerships**: Encourage collaboration between governments, private sector companies, and civil society organizations to finance and implement large-scale projects.

Future research directions:

1. Conduct detailed cost-benefit analyses comparing traditional irrigation methods with agroforestry-micro-hydro systems.
2. Investigate socioeconomic impacts of these integrated solutions on local communities.
3. Develop long-term climate data specifically relevant to selected coastal regions in the Mekong Basin.

**Closing Outlook**

The integration of agroforestry and micro-hydro systems offers a promising solution for enhancing water security and renewable energy use in the Indus Watershed. By supporting adaptation targets in Nationally Determined Contributions (NDCs) and investing in capacity building, governments can help communities build resilience to climate change impacts.

**Actions/Recommendations:**

1. Governments should develop policy frameworks supporting the integration of agroforestry and micro-hydro systems.
2. Capacity building programs should be implemented to support communities in designing, implementing, and maintaining these integrated systems.
