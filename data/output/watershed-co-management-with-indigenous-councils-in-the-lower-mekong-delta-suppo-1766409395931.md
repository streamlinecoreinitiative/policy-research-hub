# Watershed Co-management in the Lower Mekong Delta: Supporting Adaptation Targets in NDCs through Integrated Agroforestry-Micro-Hydro Systems

## Executive Summary

The Lower Mekong delta is highly vulnerable to climate change, facing severe water scarcity and increased flood risks due to rising temperatures and altered precipitation patterns. This brief proposes watershed co-management with indigenous councils as a key strategy for supporting adaptation targets in Nationally Determined Contributions (NDCs). By integrating agroforestry practices with micro-hydro systems, communities can enhance both water security and renewable energy use.

## Introduction to Water Scarcity

The Lower Mekong delta is home to approximately 17 million people, relying heavily on agriculture and fishing for livelihoods. Climate change impacts have been severe, with erratic rainfall patterns and increased evaporation from vast wetlands exacerbating water scarcity. Traditional irrigation methods are often energy-intensive and costly.

## Current Irrigation Practices and Challenges

Existing irrigation systems in the region include boreholes and canals, but these methods suffer from high water loss through evaporation and seepage. Economic analyses show that traditional irrigation methods are expensive, contributing to greenhouse gas emissions.
