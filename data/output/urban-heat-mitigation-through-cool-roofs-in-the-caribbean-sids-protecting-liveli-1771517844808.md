**Enhancing Climate Resilience in Sub-Saharan Africa: Strengthening Water Security, Clean Energy Access, and Adaptation**

## Executive Summary

Sub-Saharan Africa faces severe water scarcity and energy poverty, exacerbating climate impacts like droughts and heatwaves. To mitigate this vulnerability, we recommend prioritizing investments in water infrastructure, renewable energy projects, and climate adaptation programs.

* Invest $1 billion over 5 years in water supply projects such as wells, boreholes, and rainwater harvesting systems to improve access to safely managed drinking water.
* Increase funding for research on climate-resilient technologies like drought-resistant crops and efficient irrigation systems.
* Strengthen early warning systems, build disaster-resilient infrastructure, and promote agroforestry value chains.

Sub-Saharan Africa lags behind the world on access to clean drinking water and electricity.
