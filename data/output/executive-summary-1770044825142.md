## Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa faces severe water scarcity, with only one-third of the population having access to safe drinking water (32.4% vs. 73.7% globally). This disparity is exacerbated by frequent droughts and limited resources. The region's increasing climate variability necessitates urgent action to secure basic needs like clean water and electricity.

## Analysis

Evidence shows that Sub-Saharan Africa has the lowest access to safe drinking water compared to other regions globally (World Bank Open Data). Renewable energy consumption is also notably low at [VERIFY]%, with only one-third of total final energy consumption coming from renewables (World Bank Open Data). Climate change impacts, such as increasing frequency and intensity of extreme weather events, further strain already precarious water security and energy access.

## Policy Recommendations

1. **Invest in Water Infrastructure**: Prioritize the construction of wells, boreholes, and other water supply systems to ensure safe drinking water for all communities. Target areas with low access to safe water.
	* Allocate significant portions of existing development budgets to water security initiatives (World Bank Open Data).
2. **Renewable Energy Projects**: Implement large-scale solar, wind, and hydropower projects to increase electricity access. Focus on off-grid solutions for remote or hard-to-reach regions.
	* Engage with international organizations, NGOs, and the private sector for technical expertise and financial support (World Bank Open Data).
3. **Climate Adaptation Measures**: Develop early warning systems and climate-resilient infrastructure like flood barriers and irrigation systems. Integrate local knowledge in planning processes.

## Implementation Considerations

Funding: Allocate significant portions of existing development budgets to water security, clean energy access, and climate adaptation initiatives (World Bank Open Data).

Partnerships: Engage with international organizations, NGOs, and the private sector for technical expertise and financial support (World Bank Open Data).

Monitoring & Evaluation: Establish robust monitoring systems to track progress and address implementation challenges.

## Conclusion

Sub-Saharan Africa's struggle with basic needs like safe drinking water and electricity underscores the need for immediate and concerted action. By prioritizing investments in water infrastructure, renewable energy projects, and climate adaptation measures, we can build a more resilient future for millions of people across the region.
