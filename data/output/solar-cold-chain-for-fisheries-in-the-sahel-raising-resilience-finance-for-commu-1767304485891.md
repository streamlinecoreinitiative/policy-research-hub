# Enhancing Environmental Resilience in Lower-Income Countries: A Solar Cold-Chain Initiative for Fisheries in the Sahel

## Executive Summary

This project proposal aims to enhance environmental resilience in lower-income countries through integrated solutions for water security, clean energy, and climate adaptation. The proposed initiative, "Solar Cold-Chain for Fisheries in the Sahel," addresses challenges faced by fisheries communities in the Sahel region due to climate change.

## Main Sections

### Introduction to Water Scarcity in Lower-Income Countries

Climate change has severe impacts on agriculture worldwide, including erratic rainfall patterns that exacerbate water scarcity. In lower-income countries, access to clean energy and climate-resilient irrigation systems is limited, making this issue more pressing. The proposed project seeks to address these challenges through the integration of solar-powered cold-chains with existing fisheries infrastructure.

### Current Irrigation Practices and Challenges

Traditional irrigation methods such as boreholes and canals are used in many regions, including the Sahel. However, these systems often suffer from high water loss due to evaporation and seepage. The lack of efficient irrigation systems affects crop yields and increases energy consumption.

The proposed project will integrate solar-powered cold-chains with existing fisheries infrastructure to enhance the quality and shelf life of fish products. This will improve food security and create economic opportunities for communities by enabling them to export high-quality fish products.
