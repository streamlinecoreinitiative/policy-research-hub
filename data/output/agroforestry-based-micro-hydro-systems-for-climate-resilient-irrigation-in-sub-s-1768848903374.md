# Agroforestry-Based Micro-Hydro Systems for Climate-Resilient Irrigation in Sub-Saharan Africa: Supporting Sustainable Development Goals

**Agroforestry-Based Micro-Hydro Systems for Climate-Resilient Irrigation in Sub-Saharan Africa: Supporting Sustainable Development Goals**

## Executive Summary

Sub-Saharan Africa faces significant challenges in water security and climate adaptation, with only 32.4% access to safely managed drinking water [1] and 53.3% electricity access [2]. Agroforestry-based micro-hydro systems offer a dual solution by providing sustainable irrigation and renewable energy to rural communities, enhancing resilience against climate shocks. We recommend investing in agroforestry research, implementing pilot projects, and scaling up successful models with government support.

## Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa is one of the least electrified regions globally [3], limiting agricultural productivity and climate resilience. Climate change exacerbates water scarcity and energy poverty, highlighting an urgent need for innovative solutions like agroforestry-based micro-hydro systems. The region's population lacks access to safe drinking water, with 32.4% of the population having safely managed drinking water [1]. This impacts health and economic productivity.

## Analysis

### Water Security

Sub-Saharan Africa struggles with limited access to safe drinking water (32.4%) [1], impacting health and economic productivity. Freshwater withdrawal accounts for 9.2% of global resources [4], emphasizing the need for efficient irrigation systems like agroforestry-based micro-hydro.

### Energy Access

Low electricity access (53.3%) [2] hinders agricultural practices, exacerbating climate vulnerability. Renewable energy consumption stands at 19.7% globally [5], underscoring potential for increased adoption of renewable sources.

### Climate Adaptation

Climate change poses significant challenges to agriculture and water resources in Sub-Saharan Africa. Agroforestry-based micro-hydro systems can help mitigate these impacts by providing sustainable irrigation and renewable energy.

## Policy Recommendations

1. **Invest in Agroforestry Research**: Develop and promote agroforestry-based micro-hydro systems that integrate crops with water management infrastructure.
2. **Implement Pilot Projects**: Fund demonstration sites to test feasibility, cost-effectiveness, and community acceptance of these systems.
3. **Scale Up Successes**: Support replication of successful models across multiple regions, leveraging existing government initiatives and funding streams.

## Implementation Considerations

- Engage local communities in system design and maintenance.
- Ensure affordability through public-private partnerships.
- Address potential barriers such as high initial costs and lack of technical expertise.

## Conclusion

Governments should prioritize investment in agroforestry-based micro-hydro systems as a key strategy for achieving SDG 6 (Clean Water and Sanitation), SDG 7 (Affordable and Clean Energy), and SDG 13 (Climate Action). Collaboration with NGOs and the private sector is essential for successful implementation.
