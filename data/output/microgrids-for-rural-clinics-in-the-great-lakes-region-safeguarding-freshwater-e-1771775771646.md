Sub-Saharan African countries face significant challenges in water security, clean energy access, and climate resilience. ## Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa is one of the regions most vulnerable to climate change impacts. The lack of reliable access to clean water sources and electricity exacerbates existing challenges such as poverty, health issues, and economic growth barriers (UN-Water/WHO, 2022). Climate-related events like droughts have become more frequent and severe, impacting agricultural productivity and food security.
