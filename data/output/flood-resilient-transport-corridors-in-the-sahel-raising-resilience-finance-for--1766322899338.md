# Flood-Resilient Transport Corridors in the Sahel: Enhancing Resilience through Integrated Solutions

## Executive Summary

The Sahel region is highly vulnerable to climate change, experiencing frequent droughts and floods that disrupt transportation networks, affecting communities' access to basic services and markets. This brief highlights the importance of flood-resilient transport corridors in the Sahel, emphasizing the need for integrated solutions that combine water security, clean energy, and climate adaptation.

## Section 1: Introduction to Water Scarcity and Climate Change Impacts

The Sahel region is characterized by a semi-arid climate with limited water resources. Climate change exacerbates this situation, leading to more frequent droughts and floods that affect agriculture, livestock, and human settlements.

## Section 3: Agroforestry's Role in Climate Resilience

Agroforestry practices have been shown to improve soil health, biodiversity, and carbon sequestration. By integrating trees into agricultural landscapes, communities can enhance their resilience to climate-related shocks.
