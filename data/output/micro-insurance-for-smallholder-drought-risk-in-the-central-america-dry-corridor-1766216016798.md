# Agroforestry-Based Micro-Hydro Systems for Climate-Resilient Irrigation in the Mesoamerican Highlands

## Executive Summary

The Mesoamerican Highlands face severe water scarcity due to climate change, posing significant threats to food security and economic stability. Integrating agroforestry with micro-hydro systems offers a sustainable solution to address these challenges.

## Water Scarcity in the Mesoamerican Highlands

Climate change has led to severe water scarcity in the region, affecting agriculture and food security. Rising temperatures and changing precipitation patterns have resulted in droughts, impacting crop yields and agricultural productivity.

### Impacts on Agriculture

* Reduced crop yields due to drought stress
* Decreased agricultural productivity and income for smallholder farmers
* Increased poverty rates among rural communities

## Current Irrigation Practices and Challenges

Existing irrigation systems in the Mesoamerican Highlands include boreholes, canals, and drip irrigation. These systems suffer from significant water loss through evaporation and seepage.

## Micro-Hydro Systems for Irrigation Efficiency

Micro-hydro technology is a means to increase energy efficiency and reduce reliance on diesel generators. Small-scale hydroelectric systems can provide clean energy for irrigation pumps, reducing water loss through evaporation.

### Benefits of Micro-Hydro Technology

* Reduced energy costs and greenhouse gas emissions
* Increased energy efficiency and reliability
* Improved water management and reduced waste
