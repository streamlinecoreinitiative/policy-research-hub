# Microgrids for Rural Clinics in the Great Lakes Region: Safeguarding Freshwater Ecosystems

## Executive Summary

Climate change exacerbates water scarcity in lower-income countries, threatening food security and human health. In the Great Lakes region, rural clinics are vulnerable to disruptions in water supply, compromising healthcare services. This brief proposes integrating microgrids with renewable energy sources into rural clinic infrastructure to ensure reliable water supply and mitigate climate-related risks.

## Environmental Resilience in Lower-Income Countries: Challenges and Opportunities

### Water Security

* Climate change is projected to increase water scarcity (Source: IPCC).
* Rural clinics rely on unreliable electricity grids, exacerbating water supply disruptions.
* Microgrids with renewable energy sources can provide a stable power source for water treatment and pumping.

### Clean Energy

* Renewable energy sources are becoming increasingly cost-competitive with fossil fuels (Source: IRENA).
* Microgrids can reduce reliance on diesel generators, lowering greenhouse gas emissions and operating costs.
* Integration of microgrids with renewable energy sources can enhance energy security and reduce carbon footprints.

### Climate Adaptation

* Climate change is projected to increase extreme weather events in the Great Lakes region (Source: NOAA).
* Rural clinics are vulnerable to power outages during natural disasters, compromising water supply and healthcare services.
* Microgrids with renewable energy sources can provide a reliable backup power source for critical infrastructure.
