# flood-resilient transport corridors in the Pacific SIDS: supporting adaptation targets in NDCs

**Flood-Resilient Transport Corridors in Pacific SIDS: Supporting Adaptation Targets in NDCs**

**Executive Summary**

The Pacific Small Island Developing States (SIDS) are highly vulnerable to climate change, facing frequent natural disasters and rising sea levels that threaten their existence. Flood-resilient transport corridors are crucial for these countries' economic development and adaptation efforts.

**Introduction to Water Scarcity in Lower-Income Countries**

Water scarcity is a pressing issue in many lower-income countries, particularly in the Sudano-Sahelian belt. Climate change has exacerbated this problem by altering rainfall patterns and reducing water availability for agriculture.

**Policy Recommendations and Future Research Directions**

To support the adoption of climate-resilient irrigation methods in lower-income countries:

*   Develop policies that incentivize the use of agroforestry-based micro-hydro systems.
*   Provide technical assistance and capacity-building programs for farmers, policymakers, and other stakeholders.
*   Conduct further research on economic impact, community acceptance, and scalability.
