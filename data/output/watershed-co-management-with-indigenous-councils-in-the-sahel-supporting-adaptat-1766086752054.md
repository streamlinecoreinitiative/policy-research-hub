## Climate Change Impacts on West African Highlands

Climate change is exacerbating existing vulnerabilities in the West African Highlands. Rising temperatures and altered precipitation patterns are leading to:

* Increased frequency and severity of droughts
* More frequent and intense floods, causing damage to infrastructure and agricultural lands
* Changes in growing seasons and crop yields, affecting agricultural productivity

These impacts have significant consequences for rural communities, including limited access to electricity, water supply issues, and reduced agricultural productivity.
