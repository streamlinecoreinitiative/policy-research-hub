# Watershed Co-management with Indigenous Councils in the Mekong Basin: A Sustainable Approach to Disaster Risk Reduction

## Executive Summary

The Mekong River Basin is one of the most biodiverse regions globally, supporting over 60 million people across six countries. The region's unique geography and climate make it highly vulnerable to natural disasters such as floods, droughts, and landslides. Climate change exacerbates these risks, threatening food security, livelihoods, and human well-being.

## Environmental Resilience in Lower-Income Countries: Water Security, Clean Energy, and Climate Adaptation

### Introduction to Watershed Co-Management

Watershed co-management involves collaborative decision-making between local communities, governments, and other stakeholders to manage water resources sustainably. This approach is particularly relevant in the Mekong Basin, where indigenous councils have traditional knowledge and practices that can inform effective watershed management.

* **Benefits of Watershed Co-Management:**
	+ Improved water quality
	+ Enhanced biodiversity conservation
	+ Increased community resilience to climate-related disasters
	+ Better livelihoods for vulnerable groups

### Current Challenges in the Mekong Basin

The Mekong Basin faces numerous challenges, including:

* **Climate Change Impacts:** Rising temperatures, changing precipitation patterns, and increased frequency of extreme weather events.
* **Water Scarcity:** Decreased water availability due to climate change, population growth, and economic development.

### Watershed Co-Management with Indigenous Councils: A Sustainable Solution

Indigenous councils in the Mekong Basin have traditional knowledge and practices that can inform effective watershed management. By engaging these councils in co-management initiatives, we can:

* **Improve Water Security:** Enhance water quality, reduce pollution, and promote efficient use of water resources.
* **Increase Clean Energy Access:** Develop renewable energy sources to reduce reliance on fossil fuels.
