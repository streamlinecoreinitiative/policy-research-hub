# Urban Heat Mitigation through Cool Roofs in the Ganges-Brahmaputra Delta: Supporting Adaptation Targets in NDCs

## Executive Summary

The Ganges-Brahmaputra delta is one of the most densely populated and vulnerable regions to climate change, facing severe heat stress and water scarcity. This brief explores the potential of cool roofs as a cost-effective solution for urban heat mitigation and adaptation in the region.

## Environmental Resilience in Lower-Income Countries: Water Security, Clean Energy, and Climate Adaptation

### Introduction to Urban Heat Stress in the Ganges-Brahmaputra Delta

* The region experiences extremely high temperatures during summer months, leading to heat-related illnesses and mortality.
* Rising temperatures exacerbate water scarcity, affecting agriculture, industry, and human consumption.

* Cool roofs can reduce surface temperature by up to 10-15°C (18-27°F), mitigating the urban heat island effect.

### Benefits of Cool Roofs for Urban Adaptation

* **Energy Savings:** Reduced cooling energy consumption.
* **Public Health:** Decreased heat-related illnesses and mortality.
* **Economic Benefits:** Increased property values, improved productivity, and reduced healthcare costs.

### Case Studies and Pilot Projects: Scaling Up Cool Roof Implementation

* Successful pilot projects in cities like Kolkata and Dhaka demonstrate the effectiveness of cool roofs in reducing urban heat stress.
* Integration with existing infrastructure, such as green buildings and smart grids, can enhance the impact of cool roof technology.

## Policy Recommendations and Future Research Directions

### Key Actions for Governments and Stakeholders

1. **Develop and Implement Cool Roof Policies:** Establish building codes and incentives to promote cool roof adoption.
2. **Invest in Research and Development:** Encourage innovation in cool roof materials and technologies.
3. **Capacity Building and Training:** Provide training and capacity-building programs for builders, architects, and local authorities.

### Closing Outlook

The Ganges-Brahmaputra delta offers a unique opportunity to integrate climate-resilient infrastructure with urban planning and development. By embracing cool roofs as a key adaptation strategy, cities can mitigate the impacts of heat stress, improve public health, and enhance environmental resilience. This brief provides a starting point for policymakers, stakeholders, and researchers to explore the potential of cool roofs in supporting adaptation targets in Nationally Determined Contributions (NDCs).
