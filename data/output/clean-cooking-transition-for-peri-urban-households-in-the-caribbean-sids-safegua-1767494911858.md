# Clean Cooking Transition for Peri-Urban Households in Caribbean SIDS: Safeguarding Freshwater Ecosystems

## Executive Summary

This brief highlights the critical need to address water security challenges in lower-income countries, particularly in Small Island Developing States (SIDS) in the Caribbean. The focus is on peri-urban households, where access to clean energy and reliable irrigation systems is often limited. Agroforestry-based micro-hydro systems offer a promising solution for climate-resilient irrigation, combining sustainable agriculture practices with renewable energy generation.

## Introduction to Water Scarcity in Lower-Income Countries

Water scarcity is a pressing issue in many lower-income countries, including those in the Caribbean SIDS. Climate change exacerbates this problem by altering rainfall patterns and affecting water quality. In peri-urban areas, households often rely on unreliable irrigation systems, leading to crop losses and economic instability.

### Current Irrigation Practices and Challenges

Existing irrigation systems in lower-income countries are often inefficient and prone to water loss. For example:

* Boreholes and canals are common methods for irrigation.
* Diesel generators power many pumps, contributing to greenhouse gas emissions.

### Agroforestry’s Role in Climate Resilience

Agroforestry practices have been shown to improve soil health, biodiversity, and carbon sequestration. By integrating trees into agricultural landscapes:

* Water retention is enhanced, reducing erosion and runoff.
* Soil fertility increases, supporting crop yields.
* Biodiversity benefits from the complex ecosystem.

Case studies demonstrate the effectiveness of agroforestry in improving water retention and reducing erosion (e.g., [1], [2]).

### Micro-Hydro Systems for Irrigation Efficiency

Micro-hydro technology harnesses the energy potential of small streams and rivers to generate electricity. This approach:

* Reduces reliance on diesel generators, lowering emissions.
* Increases energy efficiency.

## Integration in Climate-Resilient Water Supply

Pilot projects have successfully integrated agroforestry and micro-hydro systems for climate-resilient irrigation. These initiatives demonstrate the potential for scaling up this approach:

* Economic analysis highlights cost-effectiveness.
* Community engagement is essential for long-term success.

## Policy Recommendations and Future Research Directions

To support adoption at scale:

1. **Policy Support:** Governments should provide incentives for agroforestry-based micro-hydro systems, including tax breaks and subsidies.
2. **Capacity Building:** Training programs should be established to educate farmers and technicians on the design, installation, and maintenance of these integrated systems.

Future research directions include:

* Economic impact analysis: Assessing the long-term economic benefits of this approach.
* Community engagement: Exploring effective strategies for engaging local communities in the planning and implementation process.

## Actions/Recommendations

1. **Develop a regional policy framework** to support the adoption of agroforestry-based micro-hydro systems.
2. **Establish training programs** for farmers and technicians on integrated system design, installation, and maintenance.
3. **Conduct economic impact analysis** to assess long-term benefits.
