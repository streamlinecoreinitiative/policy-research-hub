### Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa is the least equipped region in terms of safe drinking water (32.4%) [2] and electricity (53.3%), with only [VERIFY]% of total final energy consumption from renewables. Climate change impacts, such as droughts and floods, further strain already limited resources [3].

### Analysis

#### Water Security

Sub-Saharan Africa's water security is severely compromised due to inadequate infrastructure and frequent water scarcity events [4]. According to World Bank data (2024), only 32.4% of the population has access to safely managed drinking water.

#### Clean Energy

Access to electricity remains a significant challenge, affecting economic productivity and quality of life. Only 53.3% of the population has access to reliable electricity [5].

#### Climate Adaptation

Vulnerable regions face increased risks from climate change impacts like droughts, floods, and extreme weather events [6]. NOAA Climate Data indicates that Sub-Saharan Africa is particularly vulnerable to these impacts.

### Policy Recommendations

1. **Increase Access to Safe Water**:
	* Invest in water infrastructure projects such as boreholes, rainwater harvesting systems, and desalination plants.
	* Implement community-led initiatives for water management to enhance sustainability.
	Source: World Bank Open Data [7]
2. **Expand Renewable Energy Use**:
	* Promote solar-powered mini-grids and off-grid solutions to increase access to electricity in rural areas (World Bank 2023).
	* Encourage public-private partnerships for financing renewable energy projects.
	Source: UN SDG Report 2023 [8]
3. **Enhance Climate Resilience**:
	* Invest in early warning systems and climate-resilient infrastructure to mitigate the impacts of extreme weather events.
	* Implement agricultural insurance schemes to protect farmers from climate-induced losses (World Bank 2021).
	Source: World Bank Open Data [9]
