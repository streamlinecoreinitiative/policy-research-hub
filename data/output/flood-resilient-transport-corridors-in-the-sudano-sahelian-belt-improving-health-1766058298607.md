# flood-resilient transport corridors in the Sudano-Sahelian belt: improving health outcomes in heatwaves

**Flood-resilient Transport Corridors in the Sudano-Sahelian Belt: Improving Health Outcomes in Heatwaves**

Executive Summary:

Climate change is exacerbating heatwaves in Sub-Saharan Africa, disproportionately affecting rural communities along transport corridors. This brief highlights the importance of flood-resilient transport infrastructure and combined micro-insurance and renewable energy solutions to improve health outcomes during extreme weather events.

**Section 1: Climate Change Impacts and Current Challenges**

Sub-Saharan Africa is one of the most vulnerable regions to climate change, with rising temperatures and changing precipitation patterns affecting rural communities. Heatwaves in particular pose significant health risks, particularly for vulnerable populations such as children, pregnant women, and older adults (WHO, 2018). The lack of robust insurance mechanisms and high costs associated with renewable energy solutions hinder their adoption in these areas.

* Climate change impacts:
	+ Rising temperatures
	+ Changing precipitation patterns
	+ Increased frequency and severity of heatwaves
* Current challenges:
	+ Limited access to insurance mechanisms for rural communities
	+ High costs of renewable energy solutions

**Section 2: Micro-insurance and Renewable Energy Solutions**

Micro-insurance schemes designed specifically for farmers and herders provide financial protection against crop failures or livestock losses due to climate-related events. Solar panel installations offer a sustainable energy solution that can power small businesses, improve living conditions, and reduce dependence on fossil fuels.

* Micro-insurance:
	+ Financial protection against climate-related events
	+ Customized products for specific communities
	+ Integration with renewable energy solutions
