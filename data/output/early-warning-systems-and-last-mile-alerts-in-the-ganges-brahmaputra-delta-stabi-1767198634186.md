**Addressing Water Scarcity in the Mesoamerican Highlands**

Water scarcity is a pressing issue in the Mesoamerican Highlands, where climate change has led to reduced rainfall and increased evaporation rates. The region's existing irrigation systems are often inefficient, with significant water loss through evaporation and seepage. This brief will examine the potential of agroforestry-based micro-hydro systems as a more efficient solution for irrigation.

Micro-hydro technology offers a clean and efficient solution for irrigation, reducing reliance on diesel generators and increasing energy efficiency. By harnessing the power of small-scale hydroelectric systems, farmers can generate electricity while pumping water to their crops. Pilot projects in the region have shown promising results.
