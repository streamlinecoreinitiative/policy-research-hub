Fostering Water Security, Clean Energy, and Climate Adaptation in Sub-Saharan Africa: A Path to Resilient Development

Executive Summary

Sub-Saharan Africa faces significant challenges in providing safe drinking water and reliable electricity. With only 32.4% of the population having access to safely managed drinking water (World Bank, 2024), there is an urgent need for innovative solutions that can bridge these gaps while addressing climate resilience. We recommend implementing clean energy solutions, enhancing climate adaptation strategies, and improving water security through robust infrastructure development.

Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa lags behind other regions in terms of access to basic services like safe drinking water and electricity. Climate change exacerbates these challenges by increasing variability in rainfall patterns and temperatures (IPCC, 2021). The region's economic development, health outcomes, and social stability depend heavily on reliable energy sources and adequate water supplies.
