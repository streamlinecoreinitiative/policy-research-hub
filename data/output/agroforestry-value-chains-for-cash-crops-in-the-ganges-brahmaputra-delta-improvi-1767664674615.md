# agroforestry value chains for cash crops in the Ganges-Brahmaputra delta: improving health outcomes in heatwaves

**Agroforestry-Based Micro-Hydro Systems for Climate-Resilient Irrigation in the Ethiopian Highlands**

**Executive Summary:**
This policy brief addresses severe water scarcity through integrated agroforestry and micro-hydro systems, focusing on the Ethiopian Highlands. The proposal highlights climate change impacts, traditional irrigation practices, and their limitations. It then explores how agroforestry improves soil health, biodiversity, and carbon sequestration, while micro-hydro systems provide sustainable energy solutions for irrigation efficiency.

**Climate Change Impacts and Water Scarcity**

The Ethiopian Highlands are characterized by high precipitation variability, with an average annual rainfall of 800-1200 mm (FAO, 2020). Climate change projections indicate increased frequency and severity of droughts and heatwaves (IPCC, 2019). Traditional irrigation systems in the region rely on borehole pumps and canal systems, which are energy-intensive and prone to water loss due to evaporation and seepage.
