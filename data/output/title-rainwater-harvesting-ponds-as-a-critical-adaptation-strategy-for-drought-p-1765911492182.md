## Introduction to Drought Challenges in East Africa

East Africa is characterized by frequent droughts, which have severe impacts on the region's water security. The rationale for focusing on water security in this region lies in its critical importance for human well-being, economic development, and environmental sustainability. Water scarcity affects not only rural communities but also urban areas, where it can lead to food insecurity, displacement of people, and social unrest.

## Rainwater Harvesting Ponds as a Solution

Rainwater harvesting ponds are an effective solution to improve access to clean drinking water in drought-prone villages. These systems work by collecting and storing rainwater runoff from rooftops and other impermeable surfaces, reducing stormwater runoff and increasing groundwater recharge. The benefits of pond-based systems over traditional methods include:

* **Improved water security**: Rainwater harvesting ponds can provide a reliable source of clean drinking water during droughts.
* **Reduced health risks**: By providing access to safe drinking water, these systems can reduce the incidence of water-borne diseases.
* **Increased community participation**: Pond-based systems often involve community-led construction and maintenance, promoting social cohesion and ownership.
