# Brief: Environmental resilience in lower-income countries: water security, clean energy, and climate adaptation

**Agroforestry-Based Micro-Hydro Systems for Climate-Resilient Irrigation in the Andean Highlands**

Executive Summary:

The Andean Highlands face significant water scarcity challenges, impacting agricultural productivity and economic development. This brief presents a comprehensive approach to address these issues through the integration of agroforestry practices and micro-hydro systems.

**Addressing Water Scarcity in the Andean Highlands**

The region's unique geography and climate create a challenging context for agricultural production, with limited access to reliable water sources. Current irrigation systems rely heavily on traditional methods, which have proven ineffective in addressing the region's water needs. As a result, farmers face significant economic losses due to reduced crop yields and increased costs.

Challenges faced by farmers and communities include:

* Limited access to reliable water sources
* Inefficient irrigation systems
* Soil degradation and erosion
* Decreased crop yields and reduced economic productivity

**Agroforestry-Based Micro-Hydro Systems for Climate-Resilient Irrigation**

Agroforestry practices have been shown to improve soil health, increase biodiversity, and enhance ecosystem services. Integrating agroforestry into irrigation systems can benefit the Andean Highlands by:

* Improving water retention and recharge
* Enhancing ecosystem services (e.g., pollination, pest control)
* Increasing agricultural productivity

Micro-hydro systems offer a reliable and efficient solution for irrigation, reducing water usage, costs, and environmental degradation. The integration of agroforestry and micro-hydro systems can enhance climate resilience by:

* Reducing environmental degradation
* Increasing water security
* Empowering local communities through ownership and decision-making
