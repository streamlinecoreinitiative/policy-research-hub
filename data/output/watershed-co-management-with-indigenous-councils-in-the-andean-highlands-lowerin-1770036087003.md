**Bridging Water Security, Clean Energy, and Climate Resilience in Sub-Saharan Africa**

## Executive Summary

Sub-Saharan Africa faces significant challenges in water security, clean energy access, and climate resilience. With only 32.4% of the population having access to safely managed drinking water (World Bank Open Data, 2024) [1] and a mere 53.3% accessing electricity (World Bank Open Data, 2023) [2], the region stands out as a critical area needing urgent intervention. Effective solutions are essential to ensure sustainable development and improve the well-being of communities.

To address these challenges, we recommend implementing targeted policies for improved water management, solar-powered irrigation systems, and renewable energy development to enhance resilience. This brief outlines specific policy recommendations and implementation considerations for governments, international organizations, and local communities to build more resilient water security, clean energy access, and climate resilience in Sub-Saharan Africa.

## Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa is characterized by widespread poverty, limited infrastructure, and frequent climate-related disruptions (World Bank Open Data). These issues threaten food security, health, economic stability, and social cohesion in the region. Addressing these challenges is critical for sustainable development and regional integration.

Recent data highlights the urgency of this issue:

* Only 32.4% of Sub-Saharan Africans have access to safely managed drinking water (World Bank Open Data, 2024) [1].
* Climate change impacts are increasingly common, affecting agricultural productivity and forcing communities into resource scarcity.
* Droughts and erratic weather patterns have led to food insecurity, displacement, and reduced economic opportunities.

## Policy Recommendations

1. **Invest in Infrastructure Development**
	* Implement large-scale irrigation projects using solar-powered pumps to ensure reliable water supply for agriculture.
	* Source: World Bank (2024) [1].
2. **Promote Renewable Energy Integration**
	* Encourage the use of solar, wind, and hydro power by providing subsidies and tax incentives for renewable energy investments.
	* Source: International Renewable Energy Agency (IRENA, 2023) [4].
3. **Strengthen Water Management Systems**
	* Develop comprehensive water resource management plans focusing on efficient water distribution and usage in rural areas.
	* Source: World Bank (2024) [1].
