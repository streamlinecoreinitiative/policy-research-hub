**Agroforestry-Based Wind-Powered Desalination Systems for Climate-Resilient Coastal Communities in the Mekong Delta**

**Executive Summary:**
The Mekong Delta faces severe water scarcity due to climate change, impacting agriculture and coastal communities' livelihoods. Integrating agroforestry with wind-powered desalination systems can provide a sustainable solution for resilient water sources and renewable energy use.

**Climate Change Impacts on Water Scarcity in the Mekong Delta**

The Mekong Delta is one of the world's most critical agricultural regions, supporting millions of people. Climate change exacerbates water scarcity through rising sea levels and increased salinity intrusion (NOAA climate dataset). Rising temperatures alter precipitation patterns, leading to more frequent droughts and floods.

**Current Desalination Practices and Challenges**

Existing desalination technologies are energy-intensive and costly. **Wind-Powered Desalination Systems**

Wind-powered desalination technology uses small-scale wind turbines to generate electricity for desalination processes. This approach reduces reliance on diesel generators, decreasing greenhouse gas emissions and energy costs. Pilot projects in the Mekong Delta have demonstrated the feasibility of wind-powered desalination systems.

**Integration of Agroforestry and Wind-Powered Desalination**

Pilot projects integrating agroforestry and wind-powered desalination have shown promising results. These integrated systems can provide clean water for agriculture and households while promoting renewable energy use and climate resilience. Economic analysis suggests that these systems are cost-effective, with potential for scaling up to meet the needs of coastal communities.
