# Brief: Environmental resilience in lower-income countries: water security, clean energy, and climate adaptation\n\n**Environmental Resilience in Lower-Income Countries: A Framework for Action**

**Executive Summary**

This policy brief highlights the pressing need for environmental resilience in lower-income countries, focusing on water security, clean energy, climate adaptation, financing, policy frameworks, and community engagement. The brief emphasizes the importance of a comprehensive approach to address the complex challenges facing these countries. It provides an overview of key data points, sources to consult, and recommendations for policymakers, international organizations, and development partners.

**Actions/Recommendations**

1. **Collaborative research and policy development**: International organizations (UNEP, UNEP-WCMC) should collaborate with governments, civil society, and the private sector to develop comprehensive research and policy frameworks for environmental resilience in lower-income countries.
2. **Data collection and analysis**: Governments and international organizations should invest in collecting and analyzing data on climate change impacts, financing mechanisms, and community engagement to inform policy decisions.
3. **Capacity building and technical assistance**: International organizations should provide capacity building programs and technical assistance to support the development of environmental resilience policies and projects in lower-income countries.

**Closing Outlook**

Environmental resilience is critical for sustainable development in lower-income countries. A comprehensive approach that addresses water security, clean energy, climate adaptation, financing, policy frameworks, and community engagement is essential for reducing vulnerability and promoting resilience. This brief provides a framework for action, highlighting key data points, sources to consult, and recommendations for policymakers, international organizations, and development partners.
