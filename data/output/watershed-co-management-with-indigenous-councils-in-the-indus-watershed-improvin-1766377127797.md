# Watershed Co-management with Indigenous Councils in the Indus Watershed: Improving Health Outcomes in Heatwaves

## Executive Summary

The Indus watershed is one of the most populous and water-scarce regions globally, facing severe implications for health outcomes during heatwaves. This brief explores the potential of watershed co-management with indigenous councils to improve climate resilience through integrated agroforestry-based micro-hydro systems.

## Environmental Resilience in Lower-Income Countries: A Framework for Watershed Co-management

### Introduction to Water Scarcity in the Indus Watershed

The Indus watershed is a critical region facing severe water scarcity due to climate change impacts. Erratic rainfall patterns have reduced agricultural productivity, compromising food security and economic stability.

*   Climate data from NOAA indicates significant changes in precipitation patterns over the past decade.
*   Studies on socioeconomic impact show that droughts and heatwaves disproportionately affect vulnerable populations.

### Current Irrigation Practices and Challenges

Existing irrigation systems in the Indus watershed are inefficient, leading to water loss through evaporation and seepage. These practices exacerbate water scarcity, further straining agricultural productivity.

*   Analysis of current irrigation methods reveals significant room for improvement.
*   Economic studies on community acceptance highlight the need for sustainable solutions that address both environmental and socioeconomic concerns.
