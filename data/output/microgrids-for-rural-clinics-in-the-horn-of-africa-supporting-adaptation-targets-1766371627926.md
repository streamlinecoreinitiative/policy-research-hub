# Microgrids for Rural Clinics in the Horn of Africa: Supporting Adaptation Targets in NDCs

## Executive Summary

The Horn of Africa is one of the most climate-vulnerable regions globally, facing severe water scarcity and energy challenges. This brief focuses on microgrids for rural clinics in the region as a critical component of supporting adaptation targets outlined in Nationally Determined Contributions (NDCs). By integrating renewable energy sources with energy storage systems, microgrids can provide reliable power to healthcare facilities, enhancing their resilience during extreme weather events.

## I. Introduction to Water Scarcity and Energy Challenges

The Horn of Africa is characterized by arid and semi-arid conditions, with erratic rainfall patterns and increased temperatures due to climate change. This has led to severe water scarcity, affecting agriculture, livestock, and human consumption. The region's rural areas are particularly vulnerable, where access to clean energy and reliable healthcare services is limited.

## II. Microgrids for Rural Clinics: A Solution for Energy Access and Water Security

### Benefits of Microgrids

Microgrids can provide reliable power to rural clinics, reducing dependence on diesel generators and lowering energy costs. Integrated renewable energy sources (e.g., solar, wind) with energy storage systems ensure a stable power supply during extreme weather events.

### Pilot Projects Demonstrating the Effectiveness of Microgrids

Several pilot projects in the Horn of Africa have successfully implemented microgrid systems for rural clinics, demonstrating improved energy access and reduced costs. [verify] Economic analysis shows that microgrid systems can be cost-effective and scalable.

## III. Policy Recommendations to Support Adoption at Scale

### Supporting Adaptation Targets in NDCs

Governments in the Horn of Africa should prioritize investments in microgrid infrastructure for rural clinics as part of their adaptation targets outlined in NDCs. International cooperation and funding mechanisms (e.g., Green Climate Fund) can support the development and implementation of microgrid systems.
