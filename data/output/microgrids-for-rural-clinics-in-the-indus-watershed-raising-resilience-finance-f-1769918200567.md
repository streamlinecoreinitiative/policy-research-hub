## Executive Summary

This policy brief highlights the urgent need to address water security, clean energy access, and climate resilience challenges in Sub-Saharan Africa. With only 32.4% of the population having access to safely managed drinking water (World Bank Open Data, 2024), and just 53.3% with electricity access (World Bank Open Data, 2023), it is crucial that governments, NGOs, and international partners collaborate to implement a multi-pronged approach to address these challenges.

## Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa is characterized by limited resources for clean water supply and electricity generation, exacerbated by climate variability. The region's population faces severe challenges in accessing safe drinking water (World Bank Open Data, 2024), with only 32.4% having access to safely managed drinking water. This underscores the critical importance of developing comprehensive strategies that address water security, clean energy access, and climate resilience.

## Policy Recommendations

1. **Enhance Access to Clean Energy:**
	* Support the installation of microgrids in rural areas to provide reliable electricity (World Bank Open Data, 2023).
	* Implement policies that incentivize renewable energy projects such as solar photovoltaic systems for water pumping and irrigation.
	* Encourage public-private partnerships to finance renewable energy initiatives.

2. **Improve Water Management:**
	* Invest in infrastructure improvements like wells, boreholes, and rainwater harvesting facilities (World Bank Open Data, 2024).
	* Promote community-based water management programs that include regular maintenance and training for local users.
	* Develop early warning systems to alert communities of potential droughts or floods.

3. **Climate Adaptation Measures:**
	* Integrate climate change adaptation measures into agricultural planning to ensure food security (FAO, 2021).
	* Support the development of resilient infrastructure such as flood barriers and drainage systems.
	* Establish early warning systems for extreme weather events.

## Implementation Considerations

Initial investments in renewable energy projects can be substantial but are offset by long-term savings from reduced fuel costs. Water management improvements require upfront capital but yield benefits over time through improved health outcomes and agricultural productivity.

**Potential Barriers:** Political resistance to change, limited financial resources, and technical expertise gaps.

## Conclusion

Sub-Saharan Africa must urgently address its water security, clean energy access, and climate resilience challenges. By implementing comprehensive policy interventions that prioritize renewable energy development, enhanced water management, and climate adaptation measures, the region can achieve significant progress towards sustainable development goals. Governments, NGOs, and international partners must collaborate to ensure these policies are effectively implemented and sustained.
