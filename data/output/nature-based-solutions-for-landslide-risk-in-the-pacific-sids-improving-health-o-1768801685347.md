# Agroforestry-Based Micro-Hydro Systems for Climate-Resilient Irrigation in Pacific Small Island Developing States (SIDS): Enhancing Water Security and Health Outcomes

## Executive Summary

This brief presents an integrated approach to addressing severe water scarcity and enhancing climate resilience in lower-income countries, specifically focusing on the Pacific SIDS. By combining agroforestry with micro-hydro systems, this solution aims to provide sustainable irrigation practices, improve health outcomes during heatwaves, and promote economic development.

## Current Water Security Challenges in Pacific SIDS

Pacific SIDS face significant challenges in ensuring reliable access to clean water due to:

* **Climate Change Impacts**: Erratic rainfall patterns and increased frequency of heatwaves exacerbate water scarcity.
* **Traditional Irrigation Practices**: Energy-intensive methods consume valuable resources, contribute to greenhouse gas emissions, and are often cost-prohibitive for local communities.

* **Subsidies for Farmers**: Incentivize farmers to adopt integrated agroforestry-micro-hydro systems
* **Grid Integration**: Integrate micro-hydro systems with the grid to enhance reliability and efficiency
