# climate-resilient school feeding supply chains in the Ganges-Brahmaputra delta: safeguarding freshwater ecosystems

**Climate-Resilient School Feeding Supply Chains in the Ganges-Brahmaputra Delta: Safeguarding Freshwater Ecosystems**

**Executive Summary**

Climate change poses significant threats to water security and food systems worldwide, particularly in lower-income countries. The Ganges-Brahmaputra delta is one of the most climate-vulnerable regions globally, with frequent floods and droughts affecting agriculture, fisheries, and human settlements. This brief explores the potential of micro-insurance as a catalyst for enhancing water security and climate resilience in school feeding supply chains within this region.

**I. Introduction**

Micro-insurance has emerged as a critical tool for addressing financial vulnerability among small-scale farmers and herders affected by climate shocks. By providing financial protection against crop failures, livestock losses, or other climate-related risks, micro-insurance can help households recover more quickly from disasters and invest in long-term adaptation measures.

**II. Micro-Insurance Models and Their Efficacy**

Several types of micro-insurance schemes have been designed to address water security concerns:

* **Crop insurance**: Policies that compensate farmers for crop losses due to droughts, floods, or other climate-related events.
* **Livestock insurance**: Programs that provide financial protection against livestock mortality or morbidity caused by climate-related factors.
* **Water harvesting and storage insurance**: Schemes that cover the costs of water harvesting and storage infrastructure damaged during extreme weather events.

Studies from the Sahel Region have demonstrated the efficacy of micro-insurance in enhancing water security:

* A World Bank study found that farmers who participated in a micro-insurance program experienced a 25% increase in crop yields compared to those without coverage.
* Research by UNDP highlighted the importance of micro-insurance in reducing poverty and improving food security among small-scale farmers.

**III. Case Studies from the Ganges-Brahmaputra Delta**

While there is limited data on micro-insurance schemes specifically targeting water security in the Ganges-Brahmaputra delta, several initiatives are underway to promote climate-resilient school feeding supply chains:

* The **World Food Programme (WFP)** has implemented a climate-resilient school feeding program in Bangladesh, which includes components of micro-insurance for farmers.
* In India, the **National Bank for Agriculture and Rural Development (NABARD)** has launched a micro-insurance scheme to protect farmers against crop losses due to extreme weather events.

**IV. Economic Impact Analysis**

Quantitative analysis is needed to fully understand the economic impact of micro-insurance on livelihoods in drought-prone areas:

* **Cost-benefit analysis**: A comparison of costs associated with implementing micro-insurance programs versus traditional insurance models.
* **Longitudinal studies**: Tracking the effectiveness of micro-insurance over multiple years to assess long-term sustainability.

**Actions/Recommendations**

1.  **Supportive policies and regulations**: Governments should establish policies and regulatory frameworks that facilitate the development and implementation of micro-insurance schemes targeting water security in school feeding supply chains.
2.  **Capacity building and awareness**: Training programs for farmers, herders, and other stakeholders are essential to raise awareness about micro-insurance benefits and promote its adoption.
3.  **Partnerships and collaboration**: International organizations, governments, and local communities should collaborate to develop and implement climate-resilient school feeding supply chains that incorporate micro-insurance components.

**Closing Outlook**

Climate change poses significant threats to water security and food systems worldwide. Micro-insurance has emerged as a critical tool for enhancing water security among vulnerable populations in lower-income countries. By promoting the development and implementation of micro-insurance schemes targeting water security, we can safeguard freshwater ecosystems and support climate-resilient school feeding supply chains in the Ganges-Brahmaputra delta.
