* Sub-Saharan Africa has one of the lowest access rates to electricity, with only about half of the population having reliable electricity (Source: World Bank Open Data).

### Clean Energy

Renewable energy consumption in the world is at 19.7% (Source: World Bank Open Data), with significant disparities across regions.

* Sub-Saharan Africa lags behind in renewable energy adoption, requiring substantial investments for sustainable development goals.

### Climate Adaptation

Global temperature rise impacts Sub-Saharan Africa’s agricultural systems and water resources, necessitating robust adaptation strategies.

* Increasing frequency of extreme weather events poses additional challenges to infrastructure resilience.

## Policy Recommendations

1. **Enhance Water Security:**
	* Invest in decentralized water supply solutions like rainwater harvesting and small-scale desalination plants.
	* Implement community-based watershed management programs for sustainable groundwater recharge.
2. **Promote Clean Energy:**
	* Accelerate renewable energy projects, particularly solar and wind power, to meet electricity demand sustainably.
	* Encourage off-grid solar home systems and mini-grids in rural areas to reduce reliance on diesel generators.
3. **Build Climate Resilience:**
	* Develop early warning systems for climate-related disasters using advanced weather forecasting technologies.
	* Promote water-efficient agricultural practices, such as drip irrigation and drought-resistant crop varieties, supported by climate-smart agriculture initiatives.
