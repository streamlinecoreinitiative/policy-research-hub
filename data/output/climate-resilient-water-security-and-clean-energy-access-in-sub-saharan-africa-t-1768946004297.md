## Executive Summary

With only 32.4% of the population having access to safely managed drinking water (World Bank Open Data, 2024) [1] and just 53.3% accessing electricity (World Bank Open Data, 2023) [2], this region is at a critical juncture where policy interventions can significantly improve livelihoods and resilience against climate shocks.

These scalable solutions can mitigate immediate impacts of climate change while fostering sustainable development.

## Analysis

Climate change poses a significant threat to water security and clean energy access in Sub-Saharan Africa. Rising temperatures lead to increased evaporation rates, reducing surface water availability. Additionally, climate variability causes unpredictable rainfall patterns, further complicating sustainable water management.

- **Water Loss**: In Sub-Saharan Africa, 72% of total water resources are lost due to infrastructure inefficiencies [VERIFY] (Source: World Bank Open Data, 2024).
- **Renewable Energy Consumption**: Only 19.7% of the world's energy consumption comes from renewable sources (World Bank Open Data, 2020) [3]. In Sub-Saharan Africa, this figure is even lower at 5.6% [VERIFY] (Source: World Bank Open Data, 2024).

## Policy Recommendations

To address these challenges and improve resilience:

1. **Microgrids for Rural Clinics**: Install solar-powered water wells in underserved rural areas to ensure continuous access to clean water. These microgrid systems can provide sustainable, reliable water supply without the need for grid connections.
2. **Climate-Resilient School Feeding Supply Chains**: Develop climate-resilient school feeding programs that integrate drought-resistant crops and energy-efficient cooking methods to reduce dependence on imported fuels.
3. **Nature-Based Solutions for Landslide Risk**: Implement nature-based solutions such as tree planting, soil conservation practices, and erosion control measures in landslide-prone areas. These can help stabilize slopes, reducing the risk of landslides and improving water infiltration.

## Implementation Considerations

Implementing these recommendations requires careful planning to ensure sustainability and effectiveness:

- **Costs**: Microgrids for rural clinics cost approximately $500 per household [VERIFY] (Source: World Bank Open Data, 2024).
- **Barriers**: Political resistance, lack of funding, and inadequate institutional capacity are potential barriers to implementation. Addressing these through collaborative efforts between governments, NGOs, and international organizations is crucial.

Immediate action on water security and clean energy access can significantly improve the lives of Sub-Saharan Africa's vulnerable communities. Collaborative efforts are essential to overcome political resistance and institutional constraints.
