# micro-insurance for smallholder drought risk in the Andean highlands: lowering emissions from critical sectors

**Agroforestry-Based Micro-Hydro Systems for Climate-Resilient Irrigation in the Mesoamerican Highlands**

## Executive Summary

The Mesoamerican Highlands face severe water scarcity challenges exacerbated by climate change, population growth, and unsustainable agricultural practices. This brief explores the potential of agroforestry-based micro-hydro systems as an integrated solution to address these issues.

## Addressing Severe Water Scarcity in the Mesoamerican Highlands

### Introduction to Water Scarcity in the Region

The Mesoamerican Highlands are characterized by limited water resources, making irrigation a critical component of agriculture. Existing irrigation systems suffer from inefficiencies and high operational costs due to reliance on non-renewable energy sources.

### Current Practices and Challenges

Traditional irrigation methods in the region are often inefficient and unsustainable, leading to water scarcity and environmental degradation. Limited access to electricity for micro-hydro systems hinders their adoption, while socio-economic factors such as poverty and lack of infrastructure contribute to these challenges.

Agroforestry-based micro-hydro systems offer a synergistic approach to climate-resilient water supply.
