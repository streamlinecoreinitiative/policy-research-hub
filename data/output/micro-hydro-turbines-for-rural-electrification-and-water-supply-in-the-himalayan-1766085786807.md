**Executive Summary**

The Himalayas are one of the most vulnerable regions to climate change, facing frequent glacial lake outburst floods (GLOFs) that result in loss of life and property. Micro-hydro turbines offer a sustainable solution for both energy and water supply needs in remote communities, which is crucial for maintaining basic services in disaster-prone areas. This brief highlights the importance of micro-hydro turbines in addressing climate change impacts on water availability and providing energy security to rural Himalayan communities.

**Climate Change Vulnerability and Water Supply Challenges**

The Himalayas are experiencing rapid climate change impacts, including glacial melt that affects water availability (1). Rising temperatures have altered precipitation patterns, leading to increased frequency and severity of droughts and floods. Existing water supply systems in the region are often inadequate and unreliable, exacerbating the challenges faced by rural communities.
