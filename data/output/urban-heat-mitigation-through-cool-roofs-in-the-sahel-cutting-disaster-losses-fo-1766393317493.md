# urban heat mitigation through cool roofs in the Sahel: cutting disaster losses for vulnerable groups

**Urban Heat Mitigation through Cool Roofs in the Sahel: Cutting Disaster Losses for Vulnerable Groups**

### Executive Summary

The Sahel region is increasingly vulnerable to climate change impacts, including rising temperatures and heatwaves. This brief highlights the potential of cool roofs as a simple yet effective solution to mitigate urban heat island effects.

### Introduction

The Sahel region is characterized by high temperatures, low precipitation, and limited infrastructure. As climate change intensifies these conditions, urban areas are particularly at risk of heat-related disasters. Cool roofs can help mitigate this issue by reducing the amount of solar radiation absorbed by buildings.

### Benefits of Cool Roofs in the Sahel

*   **Heat Mitigation:** Cool roofs can reduce surface temperatures, providing immediate relief from heatwaves.
*   **Energy Efficiency:** By reducing cooling demands, cool roofs can lower energy consumption and greenhouse gas emissions associated with power generation.
*   **Cost-Effectiveness:** Cool roof technology is relatively inexpensive compared to other climate adaptation measures.
