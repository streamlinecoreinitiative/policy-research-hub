**Agroforestry-Based Micro-Hydro Systems for Climate-Resilient Irrigation in the Central African Rainforest**

### Executive Summary

The Central African Rainforest faces severe water scarcity due to climate change, posing significant challenges to smallholder farmers and vulnerable communities. Traditional irrigation methods are energy-intensive and costly, exacerbating economic difficulties. This project proposal outlines an innovative solution: integrating agroforestry with micro-hydro systems for climate-resilient irrigation.

### Introduction to Water Scarcity

The Central African Rainforest is one of the most biodiverse regions globally, supporting millions of people through agriculture. Climate change has introduced significant challenges, including erratic rainfall patterns and increased evaporation from vast wetlands (NOAA Climate Dataset). These changes have led to severe water scarcity, affecting smallholder farmers who rely heavily on irrigation for crop production.

### Current Irrigation Practices and Challenges

Traditional irrigation methods in the Central African Rainforest are primarily based on boreholes and canals. However, these systems suffer from high energy costs due to diesel generator reliance (World Bank: Electricity Access). Moreover, water loss through evaporation and seepage is substantial, further exacerbating economic challenges for smallholder farmers.

### Micro-Hydro Systems for Irrigation Efficiency

Micro-hydro technology offers a sustainable solution by harnessing the energy potential of rivers and streams (Overview of Micro-Hydro Technology). This approach reduces reliance on diesel generators and increases energy efficiency, providing cost savings compared to traditional irrigation methods.

### Integration of Agroforestry and Micro-Hydro in Climate-Resilient Water Supply

Pilot projects have successfully demonstrated the combined use of agroforestry and micro-hydro systems (Pilot Project 1: [3], Pilot Project 2: [4]). These integrated solutions offer a cost-effective approach to climate-resilient irrigation, with potential for scaling up.

To support the adoption of integrated agroforestry-micro-hydro systems at scale:

*   Provide subsidies and low-interest loans to incentivize investment in these technologies.
*   Develop infrastructure to facilitate access to micro-hydro sites and promote community engagement.
*   Offer training programs for farmers and technicians on maintenance, operation, and management of micro-hydro systems.
