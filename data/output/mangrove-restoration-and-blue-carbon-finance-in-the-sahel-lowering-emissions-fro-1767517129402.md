# Agroforestry-Based Micro-Hydro Systems for Climate-Resilient Irrigation in the Mesoamerican Highlands

## Executive Summary

The Mesoamerican Highlands face severe water scarcity challenges exacerbated by climate change, impacting agriculture and food security. This brief explores an integrated solution combining agroforestry and micro-hydro systems to enhance irrigation efficiency, reduce poverty, and promote economic stability.

## Water Scarcity in the Mesoamerican Highlands

Climate change has significantly impacted agriculture in the Mesoamerican Highlands, leading to severe water scarcity issues. Rising temperatures and changing precipitation patterns have resulted in reduced crop yields, decreased food security, and increased poverty. Resilient irrigation methods are essential for economic stability and ensuring a stable food supply.

### Current Irrigation Practices and Challenges

Existing irrigation systems in the region include boreholes and canals, which experience significant water loss due to evaporation and seepage. Improved irrigation methods are necessary to address these challenges.

### Micro-Hydro Systems for Efficiency

Micro-hydro technology involves small-scale hydroelectric systems that reduce reliance on diesel generators and increase energy efficiency. These systems can be powered by rivers or streams, providing a sustainable source of renewable energy. Integration with agroforestry practices can further improve water retention and reduce erosion.

## Closing Outlook

The combination of agroforestry and micro-hydro systems offers a promising solution to address water scarcity challenges in the Mesoamerican Highlands. By promoting renewable energy, improving irrigation efficiency, and enhancing food security, these integrated solutions can contribute significantly to poverty reduction and economic stability in the region.
