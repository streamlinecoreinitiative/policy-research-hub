## Executive Summary

Sub-Saharan Africa faces significant challenges in water security, clean energy access, and climate resilience. To address these critical issues, we recommend prioritizing investments in renewable energy, expanding clean water supply systems, and enhancing climate adaptation measures.

* Increase subsidies for renewable energy projects to reduce costs and encourage uptake.
* Implement community-led water point construction programs in rural areas where access is most limited.
* Develop early warning systems and disaster preparedness plans to mitigate the impacts of extreme weather events.

## Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa is characterized by high population density but low access to essential services like safe drinking water and electricity. Climate change intensifies these challenges through increased frequency of droughts and floods (IPCC, 2020). According to World Bank data, only about half of the population in Sub-Saharan Africa has access to electricity (53.3%)[1], indicating substantial room for improvement in renewable energy deployment and electrification strategies.
