## Context & Challenge
Climate change exacerbates existing vulnerabilities in lower-income countries, particularly in Sub-Saharan Africa. The lack of access to safe drinking water affects millions, while limited electricity hinders economic growth and public health.

- **Background on the Issue**: Climate change increases the frequency of extreme weather events, threatening water supply and energy infrastructure.
- **Why It Matters Now**: Immediate action is necessary to mitigate climate impacts and ensure sustainable development pathways for lower-income countries.

## Policy Recommendations
1. **Renewable Energy Investment**: Increase government spending on solar, wind, and hydroelectric projects to reduce fossil fuel dependency and improve access to renewable energy [Source: World Bank Open Data].
2. **Water Management Innovations**: Implement rainwater harvesting systems and micro-dams in rural areas to enhance water security. Pilot these initiatives in regions with high vulnerability.
3. **Climate Resilient Infrastructure**: Develop early warning systems for climate-induced disasters, such as floods and droughts. Invest in resilient housing and infrastructure designs to withstand extreme weather events.

## Implementation Considerations
- **Practical Steps**: Allocate dedicated funds from existing budgets or seek external financing mechanisms like international grants. Engage local communities through participatory planning processes.
- **Costs**: Initial investments may range between $50 million for a micro-dam project and $100 million for an early warning system deployment, with potential long-term savings in disaster relief costs.
- **Potential Barriers**: Resistance from traditional power companies or lack of technical expertise. Address these by providing training programs and building partnerships.

## Conclusion
Lower-income countries must prioritize climate resilience to ensure sustainable development. By focusing on water security, clean energy, and adaptation strategies, governments can build resilient communities that thrive in a changing world. Next steps include securing funding and scaling successful pilot projects across regions.
