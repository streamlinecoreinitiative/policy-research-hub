# Climate-Resilient School Feeding Supply Chains in the Sahel: Protecting Livelihoods Under Climate Stress

## Executive Summary

Climate change poses significant threats to food security and human well-being in lower-income countries, particularly in the Sahel region of Africa. The school feeding sector is a critical component of these communities' livelihoods, providing essential nutrition for children while supporting local economies. However, climate-related stressors such as droughts, floods, and temperature fluctuations compromise the reliability and sustainability of food supplies.

## Climate-Resilient Irrigation Methods in the Sahel

Climate change exacerbates water scarcity in the Sahel region, affecting agricultural productivity and food security. Agroforestry-based micro-hydro systems offer a more efficient and sustainable alternative by harnessing rainfall and runoff to generate power for irrigation.

**Key Benefits of Agroforestry-Based Micro-Hydro Systems:**

• Improved water retention and reduced erosion
• Enhanced soil health and biodiversity
• Increased carbon sequestration

## Integration with School Feeding Supply Chains

Integrating agroforestry-based micro-hydro systems into school feeding supply chains can improve the resilience of these critical livelihoods. By providing a reliable source of clean water for irrigation, schools can ensure consistent food supplies.

**Pilot Project Outcomes:**

• Improved crop yields and quality
• Enhanced community engagement and participation in climate-resilient agriculture

## Policy Recommendations and Future Research Directions

To support the adoption of integrated agroforestry-based micro-hydro systems, policymakers should:

**Recommendations:**

1. Develop policies to incentivize the use of renewable energy sources for irrigation.
2. Provide technical assistance and capacity-building programs for farmers and school administrators.

**Future Research Directions:**

1. Conduct socioeconomic studies on community acceptance and benefits derived from integrated solutions.

## Actions/Recommendations

To address climate-related challenges in school feeding supply chains, we recommend:

1. **Scaling up integrated agroforestry-based micro-hydro systems** to support climate-resilient agriculture practices.
2. **Developing policy frameworks** that incentivize the use of renewable energy sources for irrigation.

Climate-resilient school feeding supply chains are critical to protecting livelihoods under climate stress in lower-income countries. By integrating agroforestry-based micro-hydro systems into these critical sectors, we can promote sustainable agriculture practices and improve water security.
