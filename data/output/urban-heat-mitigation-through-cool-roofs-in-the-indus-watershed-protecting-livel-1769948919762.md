## Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa's population is growing rapidly, with limited resources for sustainable water management (World Bank Open Data). The uneven access to safe drinking water and electricity across the region poses significant challenges. Poor access to clean water leads to health risks, while lack of renewable energy hampers economic development (UN-Water/WHO).

## Analysis

The evidence is clear: 2.2 billion people globally lack access to safely managed water sources (UN-Water/WHO). Sub-Saharan Africa lags behind other regions in both electricity and renewable energy consumption (World Bank Open Data). Climate change exacerbates existing water scarcity, necessitating urgent interventions.

## Policy Recommendations

1. **Promote Solar-Powered Wells:** Invest in solar-powered well systems that can provide clean drinking water to rural communities.
	* Initial investment costs will be substantial, but the long-term benefits will outweigh these expenses.
2. **Enhance Community-Led Clean Cooking Initiatives:** Support the adoption of renewable energy sources like biogas stoves and solar cookers for cooking.
	* This approach can reduce reliance on traditional fuels and decrease greenhouse gas emissions.

## Implementation Considerations

* Costs: Initial investments in infrastructure can be substantial but will yield long-term benefits.
* Potential Barriers: Resistance to change, lack of institutional support, and initial financial constraints.

## Conclusion

Sub-Saharan Africa must prioritize solar-powered wells and community-led clean cooking initiatives. These measures not only address immediate water security issues but also lay the groundwork for broader climate resilience strategies. By adopting these interventions, governments can pave the way towards a more sustainable future for their populations.
