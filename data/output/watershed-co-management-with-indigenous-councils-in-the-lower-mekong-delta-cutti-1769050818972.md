**Enhancing Water Security, Clean Energy, and Climate Adaptation in East Africa: Bridging Gaps for Sustainable Livelihoods**

### Executive Summary

East Africa is facing critical challenges in water security, clean energy access, and climate adaptation. With only 53.3% of the population having access to electricity (World Bank, 2023) and less than a third accessing safely managed drinking water (World Bank, 2024), regional countries require urgent interventions. This policy brief outlines specific actionable steps to improve these critical areas.

**Key Recommendation:** Implement targeted interventions such as increased access to safe drinking water, expanded renewable energy use, and enhanced climate resilience through early warning systems to significantly improve East Africa's sustainability and economic prospects.

### Context & Challenge

East Africa is characterized by arid landscapes, frequent droughts, and climate variability, making water security a paramount concern. ### Policy Recommendations

To address these challenges, we recommend:

1. **Increase Access to Safe Drinking Water**
   - Target: By 2030, increase access to safely managed drinking water to at least 50% of Sub-Saharan African populations.
   - Action Steps: Invest in rainwater harvesting systems, boreholes, and desalination plants. Implement community-led total sanitation (CLTS) programs.
   - Cost Estimate: $1 billion for a comprehensive rollout across East Africa (World Bank estimates).
   - Potential Barriers: High initial costs, limited funding availability, lack of infrastructure.

2. **Expand Renewable Energy Access**
   - Target: By 2035, increase renewable energy consumption to at least 30% of final energy in Sub-Saharan African countries.
   - Action Steps: Establish solar power plants, wind farms, and hydroelectric projects. Implement feed-in tariffs for small-scale renewable energy producers.
   - Cost Estimate: $1 billion for a pilot program (World Bank estimates).
   - Potential Barriers: High upfront costs, limited grid infrastructure, political resistance.

3. **Strengthen Climate Resilience through Early Warning Systems**
   - Target: By 2030, establish and operate early warning systems in all Sub-Saharan African countries.
   - Action Steps: Develop and deploy satellite-based monitoring networks. Train local communities on climate change adaptation techniques.
   - Cost Estimate: $50 million for a regional initiative (World Bank estimates).
   - Potential Barriers: Limited financial resources, lack of technical expertise.

### Implementation Considerations

The implementation of these recommendations will require careful planning to minimize costs and maximize impact. Key considerations include securing funding through international development agencies and leveraging existing infrastructure where possible. Public-private partnerships can also facilitate the necessary investments in renewable energy projects. Community engagement is essential for successful adoption of rainwater harvesting systems and early warning systems.

### Conclusion

East Africa’s challenges in water security, clean energy access, and climate adaptation require immediate attention from policymakers and stakeholders. By implementing targeted interventions such as increased access to safe drinking water, expanded renewable energy use, and enhanced climate resilience through early warning systems, the region can significantly improve its sustainability and economic prospects. It is imperative that these recommendations are adopted to ensure a brighter future for East African communities.
