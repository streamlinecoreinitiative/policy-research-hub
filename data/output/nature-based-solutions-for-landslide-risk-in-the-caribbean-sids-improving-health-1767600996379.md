Executive Summary:

The Caribbean Small Island Developing States (SIDS) are highly vulnerable to climate-related disasters, including landslides and heatwaves. This brief highlights the importance of nature-based solutions in addressing these challenges, with a focus on integrating agroforestry with micro-hydro systems for climate-resilient irrigation.

**Climate Change Impacts and Water Scarcity**

The Central African Rainforest is facing severe water scarcity due to climate change, including erratic rainfall patterns and increased evaporation from vast wetlands. Traditional irrigation methods are energy-intensive and costly, exacerbating economic challenges for smallholder farmers in vulnerable communities.

* **Erratic rainfall patterns:** Reduced water availability for crops, leading to decreased crop yields.
* **Increased evaporation:** Exacerbated water scarcity issues due to higher temperatures.
* **Traditional irrigation systems:** Energy-intensive and costly, with significant losses through evaporation and seepage ([verify] 30% loss).

**Agroforestry-Based Micro-Hydro Systems: A Sustainable Solution**

* **Improve soil health:** Vegetation cover enhances soil structure and water retention capacity ([verify] 15% increase).
* **Enhance biodiversity:** Increased biodiversity provides natural pest control and pollination services.
* **Reduce energy costs:** Micro-hydro systems provide renewable energy, reducing reliance on diesel generators.
