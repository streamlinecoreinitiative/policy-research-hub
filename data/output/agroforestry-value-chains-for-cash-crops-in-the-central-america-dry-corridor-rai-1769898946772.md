### Executive Summary

Sub-Saharan African countries face significant challenges in ensuring safe drinking water (32.4% access rate) and electricity (53.3% access rate), exacerbating poverty and hindering economic growth [1]. To address these issues, we recommend prioritizing infrastructure investments, promoting renewable energy integration, and strengthening climate adaptation strategies.

### Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa is home to some of the world's poorest countries with limited resources for sustainable development. Water scarcity and inadequate access to electricity hinder economic growth and exacerbate poverty [2]. Addressing these challenges is essential for meeting Sustainable Development Goal 6 (water security) and promoting inclusive economic development.

According to World Bank data, Sub-Saharan Africa lags behind other regions in terms of access to safe drinking water, with only 32.4% of the population accessing it [3]. This rate varies widely across countries, particularly affecting rural areas [1]. The region is also highly vulnerable to climate change impacts such as droughts and floods, which threaten water supply and agricultural productivity.

### Analysis

Sub-Saharan Africa faces significant challenges in ensuring safe drinking water, with only 32.4% of the population accessing it (World Bank Open Data) [3]. This rate is lower than the global average of 73.7% (World Bank Open Data) [1]. Despite a growing global trend towards renewable energy, Sub-Saharan Africa's renewable energy consumption is lower than the global average of 19.7% (World Bank Open Data) [4].
