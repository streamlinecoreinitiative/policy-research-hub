**Introduction to Water Scarcity in the Ethiopian Highlands**

The Ethiopian Highlands are a critical agricultural region facing severe water scarcity due to climate change (UN Environment Programme, 2020). Erratic rainfall patterns and increased evaporation from highland wetlands have significant impacts on agriculture, leading to reduced crop yields and decreased food security. The importance of resilient irrigation methods for economic stability cannot be overstated; smallholder farmers rely heavily on these systems to maintain livelihoods.

**Current Irrigation Practices and Challenges**

Existing irrigation systems in the Ethiopian Highlands include boreholes and canals. However, these traditional methods are often energy-intensive and costly (World Bank, 2019). **Micro-Hydro Systems for Irrigation Efficiency**

Micro-hydro technology involves small-scale hydroelectric systems that can provide a sustainable source of energy for irrigation (World Bank, 2019). Benefits include reduced reliance on diesel generators and increased energy efficiency. Pilot projects demonstrating the combined use of agroforestry and micro-hydro are crucial for scaling up this approach.
