# Urban Heat Mitigation through Cool Roofs in the Mekong Basin: Raising Resilience Finance for Communities

## Executive Summary

Climate change exacerbates urban heat islands in lower-income countries, posing significant health and economic risks to communities. The Mekong basin is particularly vulnerable due to high population growth and increasing urbanization. This brief explores cool roofs as a cost-effective solution for mitigating urban heat islands.

## Section 1: Introduction to Urban Heat Islands

Urban heat islands are areas within cities that experience higher temperatures than surrounding rural areas due to built-up environments and human activities. ## Section 2: Cool Roofs as a Solution

Cool roofs are designed to reflect solar radiation and reduce the amount of heat absorbed by buildings. By incorporating cool roof technology into existing infrastructure development plans, governments and stakeholders can mitigate urban heat islands while also reducing energy consumption and greenhouse gas emissions.

### Benefits of Cool Roofs:

• Reduced energy consumption
• Extended lifespan of roofing materials
• Improved indoor air quality
• Enhanced aesthetic appeal
