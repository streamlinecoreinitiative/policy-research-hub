**Ensuring Access to Clean Water, Renewable Energy, and Climate Resilience in Sub-Saharan Africa**

## Executive Summary

To address the pressing issues of water security, clean energy access, and climate resilience in Sub-Saharan Africa, we recommend investing $10-$15 per capita in rural water supply projects and prioritizing decentralized renewable energy solutions such as mini-grids and off-grid solar systems. These targeted interventions will not only improve health outcomes but also foster sustainable development and mitigate the region's vulnerability to climate-related disasters.

## Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa is one of the most vulnerable regions globally in terms of water security and energy access, with only 32.4% of its population having access to safely managed drinking water [Source: World Bank Open Data, 2024]. The region's rapid population growth and climate change impacts exacerbate these challenges, making it essential to address them immediately.

## Analysis

### Water Security

The statistics are stark: only 32.4% of Sub-Saharan Africa's population has access to safely managed drinking water [Source: World Bank Open Data, 2024]. This is a significant disparity compared to the global average of 73.7% [Source: World Bank Open Data, 2024]. In arid regions, such as Niger, the situation is even more dire, with only 15% of the population having access to improved drinking water sources [Source: World Health Organization/UNICEF Joint Monitoring Programme].

### Clean Energy

Access to electricity remains highly uneven in Sub-Saharan Africa, with just 53.3% of the population having access [Source: World Bank Open Data, 2023]. This is a significant barrier to economic development and poverty reduction. In South Sudan, only 18% of the population has access to electricity [International Energy Agency].

### Climate Adaptation

Climate change impacts are severe and worsening in Sub-Saharan Africa, with increased frequency and intensity of droughts, floods, and storms. The number of people affected by climate-related disasters in Sub-Saharan Africa is projected to double by 2050 [Source: UNDP].

## Policy Recommendations

1. **Invest in Rural Water Supply Projects**: Allocate $10-$15 per capita for rural water supply projects, particularly in arid regions.
2. **Prioritize Decentralized Renewable Energy Solutions**: Develop mini-grids and off-grid solar systems to increase access to electricity in rural areas.
3. **Implement Climate Adaptation Strategies**: Establish early warning systems for climate-related hazards and promote sustainable land use practices.
