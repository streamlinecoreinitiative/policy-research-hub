By prioritizing these critical issues through targeted policy interventions, Sub-Saharan Africa can improve public health, enhance economic opportunities, and build resilience against future environmental shocks.

Context & Challenge (200 words)

Sub-Saharan Africa struggles with inadequate access to safe drinking water (32.4% of the population), limited electricity (53.3%), and low renewable energy consumption (9.2%). These issues exacerbate climate vulnerability, necessitating immediate interventions. The region's challenges stem from inadequate infrastructure and weak institutional support. Water scarcity exacerbates health risks due to contaminated water sources. Limited access to electricity hinders economic growth, education, and healthcare services. Renewable energy consumption is low, partly due to high initial costs and lack of supportive policies.
