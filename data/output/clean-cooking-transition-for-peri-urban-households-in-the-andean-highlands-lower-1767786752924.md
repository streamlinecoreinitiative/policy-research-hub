# clean cooking transition for peri-urban households in the Andean highlands: lowering emissions from critical sectors

**Clean Cooking Transition for Peri-Urban Households in the Andean Highlands: Lowering Emissions from Critical Sectors**

**Executive Summary**

The Andean highlands face significant environmental challenges, including water scarcity and energy poverty. Peri-urban households rely heavily on traditional cooking methods that contribute to greenhouse gas emissions and air pollution. This brief explores the potential of agroforestry-based micro-hydro systems for climate-resilient irrigation and clean cooking solutions.

**Section 1: Introduction to Water Scarcity in the Andean Highlands**

The Mesoamerican region is characterized by a fragile water balance, exacerbated by climate change impacts, population growth, and unsustainable agricultural practices. Current irrigation systems are often inefficient, leading to high operational costs and environmental degradation. Agroforestry-based micro-hydro systems can provide an integrated solution for sustainable water management.

* **Key Challenges:**
	+ Limited access to electricity for micro-hydro systems
	+ Inefficient use of water resources in traditional irrigation methods
	+ High operational costs associated with existing infrastructure

**Section 2: Current Practices and Challenges**

Peri-urban households in the Andean highlands rely on traditional cooking methods, including wood-burning stoves and charcoal. These practices contribute to air pollution, greenhouse gas emissions, and water scarcity.

* **Current Cooking Methods:**
	+ Wood-burning stoves
	+ Charcoal use
