## Analysis

### Water Security

Sub-Saharan Africa faces severe water scarcity issues, exacerbated by climate change-induced erratic rainfall patterns and poor infrastructure. Only 32.4% of the population has access to safely managed drinking water (World Bank Open Data). This gap is further widened by inadequate water supply systems, with many communities relying on unimproved sources.

### Clean Energy

The region's energy mix heavily relies on fossil fuels, contributing to greenhouse gas emissions while limiting sustainable development opportunities. Renewable energy consumption in Sub-Saharan Africa is limited, with only 19.7% of total final energy coming from renewables (World Bank Open Data).

### Climate Adaptation

Climate-related disasters have led to significant economic losses, highlighting the need for climate adaptation measures. The region's vulnerability to climate shocks is high due to inadequate early warning systems and limited adaptive capacity.
