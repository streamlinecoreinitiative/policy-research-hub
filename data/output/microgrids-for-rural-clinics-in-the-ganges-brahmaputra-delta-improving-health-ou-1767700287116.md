**Microgrids for Rural Clinics in the Ganges-Brahmaputra Delta: Improving Health Outcomes during Heatwaves**

### Executive Summary

Climate change exacerbates water scarcity and heat stress in lower-income countries, particularly in regions like the Ganges-Brahmaputra delta. This brief highlights the importance of integrating agroforestry with micro-hydro systems to enhance water security and renewable energy use.

### Addressing Water Scarcity and Climate Change Impacts

#### Introduction to Water Scarcity

Climate change alters precipitation patterns in the Ganges-Brahmaputra delta, leading to erratic rainfall and increased water scarcity. This impacts agriculture, with significant economic costs associated with constructing and maintaining irrigation systems.

#### Current Irrigation Practices

Traditional irrigation systems rely on boreholes and canals, resulting in substantial water loss due to poor canal design (evaporation and seepage). Economic costs include diesel generator maintenance costs.

### Micro-Hydro Systems for Efficiency and Energy Savings

Micro-hydro technology provides a renewable energy source for irrigation systems, reducing diesel generator usage. Successful micro-hydro projects show improved water delivery and energy savings.

### Integration of Agroforestry and Micro-Hydro Systems

Integrated systems implementation increases crop yields and reduces dependency on external energy sources. Economic benefits include reduced fuel and maintenance costs.

### Policy Recommendations: Subsidies, Grants, Infrastructure Development

- Government subsidies for initial investment in micro-hydro system installation.
- Community-led micro-hydro system installations with local government support.

### Future Research Directions: Long-term Sustainability Assessments, Community Engagement Strategies

- 5-year monitoring period to ensure long-term benefits from integrated systems.
- Participatory decision-making processes and joint committees involving farmers, government officials, and environmental experts.

### Actions/Recommendations:

1. **Invest in micro-hydro system installations** with government subsidies for initial investment.
2. **Develop community-led infrastructure development programs** to support micro-hydro system implementation.
3. **Engage local communities** through participatory decision-making processes and joint committees.
4. **Monitor long-term sustainability** of integrated systems.

### Closing Outlook

Integrating agroforestry with micro-hydro systems is crucial in addressing water scarcity and climate change impacts on agriculture in lower-income countries like those in the Ganges-Brahmaputra delta. By leveraging these technologies, rural clinics can improve health outcomes during heatwaves while promoting climate resilience.
