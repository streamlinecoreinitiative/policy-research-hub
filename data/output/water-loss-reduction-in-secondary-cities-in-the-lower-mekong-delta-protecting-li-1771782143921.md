# water loss reduction in secondary cities in the Lower Mekong delta: protecting livelihoods under climate stress

**Bridging Water Security, Clean Energy Access, and Climate Resilience in Sub-Saharan Africa**

## Executive Summary

Sub-Saharan Africa faces significant challenges in water security, energy access, and climate resilience. To address these interconnected issues, we recommend investing in off-grid solar power for rural areas to improve water supply and sanitation infrastructure. This initiative should be complemented by developing community-based renewable energy projects, such as micro-grids, to reduce costs and increase reliability. Additionally, implementing climate-smart agriculture initiatives that integrate drought-resistant crops and improved irrigation systems can help mitigate the impacts of climate change.

## Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa has the lowest access to safely managed drinking water (32.4%) compared to the global average of 73.7% [1]. Access to electricity is also limited, with only 53.3% of the population having reliable access in Sub-Saharan Africa versus 91.6% globally [2]. Renewable energy consumption remains low at just 19.7%, far below world averages and necessary for sustainable development [3].

The region's water scarcity exacerbates poverty and undermines economic growth, with over 2 billion people lacking safe water resources [4]. High levels of energy poverty contribute to missed job opportunities and increased health risks due to reliance on kerosene lamps or firewood. Climate change intensifies these challenges; droughts and floods affect agricultural productivity and human settlements.

## Analysis

Water scarcity is a pressing issue in Sub-Saharan Africa, with over 2 billion people lacking safe water resources [4]. This not only affects economic growth but also exacerbates poverty. The region's energy access is limited, with most households relying on kerosene lamps or firewood for lighting and cooking. Climate change further complicates these challenges, with droughts and floods affecting agricultural productivity and human settlements.

## Policy Recommendations

1. **Invest in Off-Grid Solar Power for Rural Areas**

* Provide subsidies for solar-powered water pumps and sanitation facilities, focusing initial investments on high-risk areas like arid regions.
* Monitor project impacts closely to identify best practices and scale up successful models.

2. **Develop Community-Based Renewable Energy Projects**

* Encourage community-led micro-grids that combine decentralized renewable energy sources with energy storage solutions.
* Promote cross-border collaborations to share technological knowledge, financing, and maintenance expertise among neighboring countries.

3. **Implement Climate-Smart Agriculture Initiatives**

* Introduce drought-resistant crop varieties supported by improved irrigation systems tailored for local conditions.
* Provide training on sustainable farming practices like intercropping and agroforestry that enhance ecosystem resilience against climate change impacts.

## Conclusion

By prioritizing investments in clean water, renewable energy, and climate adaptation strategies, Sub-Saharan Africa can significantly improve the lives of its citizens while laying a foundation for sustainable development. Governments must lead by example through policy reforms and international cooperation to overcome institutional constraints and financial barriers.
