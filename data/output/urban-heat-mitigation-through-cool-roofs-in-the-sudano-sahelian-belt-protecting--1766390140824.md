# Urban Heat Mitigation through Cool Roofs in the Sudano-Sahelian Belt: Protecting Livelihoods under Climate Stress

## Executive Summary

Climate change exacerbates water scarcity, heat stress, and extreme weather events in lower-income countries, particularly in the Sudano-Sahelian belt. This brief highlights the importance of urban heat mitigation strategies, focusing on cool roofs as a cost-effective solution. By integrating cool roof technology into building design and infrastructure planning, communities can reduce energy consumption and enhance overall resilience to climate stress.

## Environmental Resilience in Lower-Income Countries: Water Security, Clean Energy, and Climate Adaptation

### Introduction to the Sudano-Sahelian Belt

The Sudano-Sahelian belt spans across West Africa, covering countries such as Senegal, Mali, Burkina Faso, Niger, Chad, Sudan, South Sudan, Ethiopia, Eritrea, Djibouti, Somalia, Kenya, Uganda, Rwanda, Burundi, Tanzania, the Democratic Republic of Congo, and the Central African Republic. This region is characterized by a semi-arid climate with limited rainfall, making it vulnerable to droughts, heatwaves, and extreme weather events.

### Water Security and Clean Energy

Water scarcity affects approximately 2 billion people worldwide [verify]. In the Sudano-Sahelian belt, water scarcity is exacerbated by climate change, impacting agriculture, industry, and human consumption. To address this challenge, integrated agroforestry-based micro-hydro systems can provide a reliable source of clean energy and water for irrigation.

### Climate Adaptation and Urban Heat Mitigation

Climate change is projected to increase temperatures in the Sudano-Sahelian belt by 1-2°C by 2050 [verify]. This will lead to increased heat stress, reduced crop yields, and decreased economic productivity. Cool roofs can mitigate urban heat islands by reducing surface temperature during peak summer months.

## Integration of Agroforestry and Micro-Hydro Systems

Pilot projects in the Sudano-Sahelian belt have demonstrated the effectiveness of integrating agroforestry and micro-hydro systems for climate-resilient water supply. These integrated systems:

* Improve soil health, biodiversity, and carbon sequestration
* Enhance water retention and reduce erosion
* Provide a reliable source of clean energy for irrigation and household use

## Policy Recommendations and Future Research Directions

To support the adoption of agroforestry-based micro-hydro systems at scale, we recommend:

1. **Policy Support**: Governments and international organizations should provide incentives and policy frameworks to encourage the adoption of integrated agroforestry-micro-hydro systems.
2. **Capacity Building**: Training programs for local communities, farmers, and policymakers are essential to build capacity and ensure effective implementation.
3. **Research and Development**: Further research is needed to validate the effectiveness of these integrated systems, including economic impact, community engagement, and scalability.

## Actions/Recommendations

2. **Establish Community-Based Training Programs**: Develop training programs for local communities, farmers, and policymakers to build capacity and ensure effective implementation.
3. **Conduct Further Research and Development**: Conduct further research to validate the effectiveness of integrated agroforestry-micro-hydro systems.

## Closing Outlook

The Sudano-Sahelian belt is one of the most vulnerable regions to climate change in Africa. By integrating cool roof technology, agroforestry-based micro-hydro systems, and policy support, communities can reduce energy consumption and enhance overall resilience to climate stress. It is essential to prioritize urban heat mitigation strategies and promote sustainable development practices to protect livelihoods under climate stress.
