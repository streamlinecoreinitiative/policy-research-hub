# Urban Heat Mitigation through Cool Roofs in the Horn of Africa: A Strategy for Food Security during Droughts

## Executive Summary

The Horn of Africa is highly vulnerable to climate change, characterized by frequent droughts and water scarcity. This brief explores the potential of urban heat mitigation through cool roofs as a strategy to stabilize food security during droughts.

## Background: Climate Change Impacts on Agriculture

Climate change has severe implications for agriculture in the Horn of Africa, including:

* Erratic rainfall patterns
* Increased temperatures and frequency of extreme weather events
* Reduced crop yields and decreased water availability

These changes have significant impacts on food security, with millions relying on rain-fed agriculture.

## Urban Heat Mitigation through Cool Roofs: A Strategy for Food Security

Cool roofs can reduce urban heat islands by using materials with high albedo (reflectivity). This can lead to:

* Reduced energy consumption
* Improved air quality
* Enhanced human health and well-being

In the context of food security, cool roofs can help stabilize agricultural productivity during droughts.
