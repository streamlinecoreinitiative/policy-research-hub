# Executive Summary

**Accelerating Climate Resilience in Lower-Income Countries: Harnessing Water Security, Clean Energy, and Adaptation Strategies**

## Executive Summary

Climate change poses significant challenges to lower-income countries, particularly in terms of water security, reliable electricity, and climate adaptation. To address these challenges, we recommend prioritizing the implementation of sanitation infrastructure, promoting clean energy sources for off-grid communities, and developing robust adaptation strategies that incorporate indigenous knowledge.

This policy brief highlights the critical need for immediate and targeted interventions to bolster resilience across these essential sectors. With only 32.4% of the population in Sub-Saharan Africa having access to safely managed drinking water [1], and less than half (53.3%) of the population in Sub-Saharan Africa having access to electricity [2], it is imperative that we take decisive steps toward building more resilient societies.

## Context & Challenge

Lower-income countries face severe challenges in ensuring sustainable access to water, electricity, and climate-resilient infrastructure due to limited resources and weak governance structures. Sub-Saharan Africa is particularly vulnerable with a mere 32.4% of the population having access to safely managed drinking water [1], compared to 73.7% globally [3]. Similarly, only 53.3% of the region's population has access to electricity, far below the global average of 91.6% [2].

## Analysis

Recent data highlights significant gaps in water security and clean energy access across lower-income countries. For instance, Sub-Saharan Africa lags far behind other regions with only 32.4% of the population having access to safely managed drinking water [1], compared to 73.7% globally [3]. In terms of electricity access, only 53.3% of the region's population has reliable power, which is significantly lower than the global average of 91.6% [2].

Furthermore, despite international commitments to renewable energy consumption, Sub-Saharan Africa remains underutilized with just 19.7% of total final energy consumption coming from renewable sources [4]. These statistics indicate that current policies and investments are insufficient to meet the needs of lower-income countries in ensuring reliable access to basic services like water and electricity.

## Policy Recommendations

To bolster climate resilience and support sustainable development, governments and international partners should prioritize the following interventions:

1. **Enhanced Water Security**: Invest in sanitation infrastructure such as improved wells and latrines, particularly in rural areas where access is most limited. Implement community-led total sanitation initiatives to ensure that new water sources are maintained over time.
2. **Promoting Clean Energy**: Encourage the deployment of solar-powered solutions for off-grid communities. Provide subsidies or grants for households and businesses to install solar panels, thereby reducing their reliance on fossil fuels and improving energy access.
3. **Climate Adaptation Strategies**: Collaborate with indigenous councils to develop climate-resilient adaptation plans that incorporate traditional knowledge. This approach can help communities better prepare for and adapt to extreme weather events such as floods and droughts.
