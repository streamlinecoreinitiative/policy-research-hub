## Executive Summary

Sub-Saharan Africa faces significant challenges with access to safe drinking water (32.4%) and electricity (53.3%), contributing to poverty and poor health outcomes. To address these issues, we recommend investing in infrastructure projects that prioritize safe water supply, renewable energy sources, and climate adaptation measures.

### Key Findings:

* Sub-Saharan Africa has one of the lowest rates of access to safely managed drinking water (32.4%) and electricity (53.3%). [1]
* These gaps contribute significantly to poverty and poor health outcomes.
* Improving water security and promoting renewable energy is crucial for sustainable development, reducing health risks, and enhancing economic productivity.

## Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa struggles with inadequate access to clean drinking water and electricity due to limited resources and political instability. Climate change exacerbates these challenges by increasing the frequency of extreme weather events. According to World Bank data (2024), Sub-Saharan Africa lags behind other regions in accessing safe drinking water (32.4% vs 73.7%) and electricity (53.3% vs 91.6%). [1] These gaps correlate with higher rates of malnutrition, disease transmission, and poverty.

## Analysis

The impact of climate change on water resources and energy supplies is evident in the increasing frequency of droughts and floods. Investing in renewable energy sources can reduce reliance on fossil fuels and improve energy efficiency.
