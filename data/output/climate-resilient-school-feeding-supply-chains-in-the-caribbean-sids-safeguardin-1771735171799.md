# climate-resilient school feeding supply chains in the Caribbean SIDS: safeguarding freshwater ecosystems

**Climate-Resilient School Feeding Supply Chains in the Caribbean SIDS: Safeguarding Freshwater Ecosystems**

## Executive Summary

The Caribbean Small Island Developing States (SIDS) face significant challenges in ensuring water security, providing clean energy access, and building climate resilience. With only 32.4% of the population having access to safely managed drinking water [1], and just 53.3% with electricity [2], these issues threaten food security, economic stability, and overall well-being. To address this crisis, we recommend that governments and international partners implement targeted policies focusing on expanding access to clean energy, improving water management systems, and enhancing climate adaptation strategies.

## Context & Challenge

The Caribbean SIDS are particularly vulnerable to the impacts of climate change, including more frequent droughts and floods [3]. These events exacerbate existing vulnerabilities in water security and clean energy access. Despite having a 73.7% global coverage rate in safely managed drinking water [4], only 32.4% of the region's residents can access it [1]. Similarly, just 53.3% of the population has access to electricity [2], leaving millions without reliable power sources.

## Analysis

The situation in the Caribbean SIDS highlights a critical need for robust interventions in both clean energy access and water security. Data from the World Bank reveals that while global renewable energy consumption stands at 19.7% [5], only 53.3% of the region's population has access to electricity, indicating significant disparities. This under-representation is compounded by the fact that only 32.4% of the region's residents have access to safely managed drinking water [1]. These statistics underscore the urgent need for innovative and feasible solutions.

## Policy Recommendations

To address these pressing challenges, we propose the following evidence-based policy recommendations:

1. **Invest in Renewable Energy Infrastructure**: Governments should prioritize investments in renewable energy infrastructure, particularly solar power, to reduce reliance on fossil fuels and improve access to electricity [2]. This includes establishing public-private partnerships (PPPs) for solar panel installation in rural areas where grid extension is impractical.
2. **Enhance Water Management Systems**: Implementing comprehensive water management systems can significantly boost access to safe drinking water, especially in arid regions prone to droughts [1]. This includes developing and implementing rainwater harvesting projects, promoting community-based irrigation schemes, and enhancing the efficiency of existing water distribution networks.
3. **Climate Resilience Programs**: Developing climate-resilient infrastructure is crucial for safeguarding energy access and water security during extreme weather events. Implementing early warning systems can help communities prepare for floods and droughts, reducing risks to life and property [6].

## Implementation Considerations

Implementing these recommendations requires careful planning and coordination among various stakeholders. Key considerations include securing funding from international development banks, engaging local communities in project design and implementation, and ensuring that new infrastructure is sustainable and long-lasting.

1. **Funding Opportunities**: Securing grants and loans from organizations like the International Finance Corporation (IFC) or United Nations Development Programme (UNDP) can facilitate large-scale investments in renewable energy and water management projects [7].
2. **Community Engagement**: Engaging communities in decision-making processes ensures that solutions are tailored to local needs and resources, increasing their acceptance and effectiveness [8].
3. **Sustainability Focus**: Ensuring that all projects adhere to sustainable design principles can minimize environmental impacts while maximizing long-term benefits [9].

## Conclusion

Addressing water security, clean energy access, and climate resilience in the Caribbean SIDS is not only a matter of humanitarian concern but also an essential component of achieving broader development goals. By adopting these evidence-based recommendations, governments can significantly improve the quality of life for millions while laying the groundwork for sustainable future growth.
