**Policy Recommendations**

To address these challenges, we recommend:

1. **Water Security**: Invest in infrastructure like boreholes and rainwater harvesting systems to increase access to clean drinking water for 32.4% of the population (World Bank Open Data). Implement community-managed water points supported by government subsidies or grants.
2. **Clean Energy Access**: Promote solar-powered renewable energy solutions such as mini-grids and off-grid systems for remote areas. Governments should incentivize private sector investment in these technologies through grants, tax breaks, or feed-in tariffs.
3. **Climate Adaptation**: Develop early warning systems and climate-resilient infrastructure such as flood barriers and drought-resistant crops. Encourage the use of indigenous knowledge for climate adaptation practices that have proven successful over generations.

Implementing these policies requires a multi-faceted approach:

- **Cost**: Initial investments in infrastructure can be significant but yield high returns through reduced healthcare costs, increased agricultural productivity, and improved economic stability.
- **Barriers**: Address potential political resistance by engaging local communities in the planning process and ensuring that benefits are shared equitably. Also, address technical challenges such as grid connectivity for solar-powered solutions.
