# climate-resilient school feeding supply chains in the Indus watershed: raising resilience finance for communities

**Climate-Resilient School Feeding Supply Chains in the Indus Watershed: Raising Resilience Finance for Communities**

## Executive Summary

The lack of access to clean water and electricity in Sub-Saharan Africa poses significant challenges to sustainable development. This policy brief argues that investing in climate-resilient school feeding supply chains is crucial for enhancing environmental resilience in lower-income countries. We recommend implementing scalable solutions like microgrids, renewable energy projects, and climate-resilient infrastructure to improve living standards.

## Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa faces critical challenges related to water security, clean energy access, and climate adaptation. With less than 50% of the population having access to electricity (53.3%) and only about 32.4% with safe drinking water ([NEEDS SOURCE]), there is a pressing need for targeted interventions that can build resilience against environmental shocks while promoting sustainable development.

## Policy Recommendations

To address these challenges, we recommend:

*   **Investing in clean energy infrastructure**: Develop microgrids for rural areas, focusing on solar power and wind energy as cost-effective alternatives ([NEEDS SOURCE]).
*   **Enhancing water security**: Support the construction of desalination plants and improve sanitation facilities to reduce contamination risks (UN-Water/WHO).
*   **Strengthening climate adaptation strategies**: Implement early-warning systems, build flood-resistant infrastructure, and promote agroforestry value chains for sustainable livelihoods.

## Implementation Considerations

To ensure successful implementation:

*   Develop detailed project plans with specific timelines.
*   Allocate budgets based on regional needs.
*   Engage local communities in the planning process.
*   Estimate initial investment costs (e.g., $10 million per microgrid project) and outline potential funding sources like international development agencies or private investors.

## Conclusion

Climate-resilient school feeding supply chains are essential for building sustainable, resilient communities in Sub-Saharan Africa. We urge government officials and development practitioners to prioritize these interventions as they are critical for addressing the region's pressing environmental challenges. Conducting pilot projects, gathering lessons learned, and scaling up successful initiatives across the region will be crucial steps towards achieving this goal.
