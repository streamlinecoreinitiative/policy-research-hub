## Context & Challenge

The East African Highlands are a critical region facing significant environmental resilience challenges due to high population density and climate variability. With limited freshwater resources and intermittent rainfall patterns, the region is particularly vulnerable to droughts and floods (NOAA Climate Data). Ensuring reliable clean energy sources and securing safe drinking water are crucial for fostering sustainable development in this area.

Recent data highlights the severity of these challenges:

* 32% of Sub-Saharan Africa residents lack safe drinking water [NEEDS SOURCE].
* Only 53.3% of the population has access to electricity, leaving millions without basic amenities like lighting and power for health care facilities (World Bank, 2023).

## Policy Recommendations

To address these challenges, we recommend the following policy interventions:

1. **Expand Renewable Energy Consumption**: Increase the share of renewable energy consumption in the East African Highlands by at least 15% within five years through subsidies for solar panel installation, community-based wind farms, and grid-connected hydroelectric projects.
2. **Enhance Access to Electricity**: Implement a national electrification program focusing on rural areas with high population density but low infrastructure coverage. Provide grants for home solar systems, improve grid connectivity in underserved regions, and integrate renewable energy into existing power grids.
3. **Promote Sustainable Water Management Practices**: Develop and implement watershed management plans that include conservation techniques like terracing, afforestation, and sustainable irrigation methods to ensure water security during dry periods.
