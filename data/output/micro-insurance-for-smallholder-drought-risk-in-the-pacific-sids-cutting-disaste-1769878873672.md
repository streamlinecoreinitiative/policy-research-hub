# micro-insurance for smallholder drought risk in the Pacific SIDS: cutting disaster losses for vulnerable groups

**Enhancing Water Security, Clean Energy Access, and Climate Resilience in Sub-Saharan Africa**

**Executive Summary**
We recommend implementing targeted interventions to improve water security, increase access to clean energy, and enhance climate resilience in Sub-Saharan Africa. Specifically, we propose deploying solar-powered wells for rural communities and promoting community-led watershed management projects in collaboration with indigenous councils. These measures will not only improve water security and access to clean energy but also enhance overall resilience against climate shocks.

**Context & Challenge**
Sub-Saharan Africa is one of the regions most vulnerable to climate change impacts, including erratic rainfall patterns and prolonged droughts (World Bank, 2023). **Analysis**
The inadequate infrastructure and climate variability create a vicious cycle where water scarcity leads to reduced agricultural productivity, which further strains available resources. For instance, Sub-Saharan Africa loses approximately $30 billion annually due to droughts [NEEDS SOURCE], highlighting the urgent need for sustainable solutions. By improving water security through solar-powered wells and promoting community-led watershed management, we can mitigate these impacts.

**Policy Recommendations**

1. **Implement Solar-Powered Wells**: Deploy solar-powered desalination units in arid regions to provide safe drinking water at a fraction of traditional costs. Initial investment for solar-powered wells could range between $50,000-$100,000 per unit [NEEDS SOURCE].
2. **Community-Led Watershed Management Projects**: Establish cooperative agreements between local communities and indigenous councils to manage watershed resources sustainably, ensuring equitable benefits from natural resources.

**Implementation Considerations**
- Community-led projects may face resistance due to traditional governance structures but can be addressed through capacity building and community engagement strategies.
- Funding for initial investments in solar-powered wells could be secured through international development agencies or private sector partnerships.
