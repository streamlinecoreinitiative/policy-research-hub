# agroforestry value chains for cash crops in the Andean highlands: protecting livelihoods under climate stress

**Agroforestry Value Chains for Cash Crops in the Andean Highlands: Enhancing Water Security and Renewable Energy**

**Executive Summary**

Climate change poses significant threats to agriculture in lower-income countries, particularly in regions like the Andean highlands where water scarcity is a pressing concern. This brief explores the potential of integrating agroforestry with micro-hydro systems as a sustainable solution for climate-resilient irrigation.

**Water Scarcity in the Andean Highlands: A Climate-Resilient Irrigation Imperative**

The Andean highlands are characterized by unpredictable rainfall patterns, increased evaporation from vast wetlands, and limited access to modern irrigation systems. As a result, smallholder farmers face significant challenges in maintaining crop yields and ensuring food security.

**Agroforestry's Role in Enhancing Water Security**

Agroforestry systems offer numerous benefits for soil health, biodiversity, and carbon sequestration. By integrating trees into agricultural landscapes, farmers can improve water retention, reduce erosion, and enhance crop productivity. Case studies from the Andean highlands demonstrate that agroforestry systems can increase crop yields compared to monoculture farming practices.

**Micro-Hydro Systems for Efficient Irrigation**

Micro-hydro technology provides a low-cost and energy-efficient solution for irrigation. By harnessing the power of small-scale hydroelectric systems, farmers can reduce their reliance on diesel generators and lower their energy costs. Economic analyses indicate that micro-hydro systems can save operational expenses compared to traditional irrigation methods.

**Integration of Agroforestry and Micro-Hydro Systems**

Pilot projects in the Andean highlands demonstrate the potential for combining agroforestry with micro-hydro systems to create climate-resilient water supply chains. By integrating these approaches, farmers can enhance their water security and reduce energy costs.

**Policy Recommendations and Future Research Directions**

To support the adoption of integrated agroforestry-micro-hydro systems at scale, we recommend:

* Subsidies for smallholder farmers to invest in micro-hydro technology
* Low-interest loans for community-led initiatives to develop agroforestry value chains
* Infrastructure development to facilitate access to markets and services for smallholder farmers

Future research should focus on:

* Long-term sustainability assessments of integrated agroforestry-micro-hydro systems
* Community engagement strategies to promote adoption and ensure equitable benefits sharing

**Actions/Recommendations:**

1. **Support the development of community-led initiatives** to integrate agroforestry with micro-hydro systems in the Andean highlands.
2. **Provide subsidies for smallholder farmers** to invest in micro-hydro technology and develop climate-resilient irrigation systems.
3. **Invest in infrastructure development** to facilitate access to markets and services for smallholder farmers.

**Closing Outlook**

The integration of agroforestry with micro-hydro systems offers a promising solution for addressing water scarcity and promoting climate resilience in the Andean highlands. By supporting community-led initiatives, providing subsidies for smallholder farmers, and investing in infrastructure development, we can help protect livelihoods under climate stress and ensure food security.
