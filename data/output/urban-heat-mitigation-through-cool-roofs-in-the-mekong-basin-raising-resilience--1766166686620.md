**Executive Summary**

The Mekong basin is highly climate-vulnerable, facing severe water scarcity and rising temperatures that threaten agricultural production and human well-being. This brief highlights the potential of integrating agroforestry with micro-hydro systems to enhance water security and reduce poverty among smallholder farmers in Ethiopia's highlands.

**Section 1: Introduction to Water Scarcity in the Ethiopian Highlands**

Climate change is exacerbating water scarcity in Ethiopia's highlands, crucial for agricultural production and food security. Rising temperatures and changing precipitation patterns have resulted in reduced crop yields, decreased water availability, and increased poverty among smallholder farmers (UN Environment Programme). Sustainable irrigation methods are essential to ensure food security.

**Section 4: Integration of Agroforestry and Micro-Hydro in Climate-Resilient Irrigation**

Pilot projects have demonstrated the potential of combining agroforestry and micro-hydro systems for efficient irrigation. Economic analyses indicate that these systems can be cost-effective.

By investing in resilience finance and supporting policy reforms, we can help communities adapt to climate change while promoting sustainable development.
