# Micro-insurance for Smallholder Drought Risk in the Indus Watershed: Lowering Emissions from Critical Sectors

## Executive Summary

This brief addresses severe water scarcity and its associated environmental impacts in lower-income countries, focusing on critical sectors of water security, clean energy, and climate adaptation. The proposed solution integrates micro-insurance for smallholder drought risk with agroforestry-based micro-hydro systems, providing a cost-effective and sustainable approach to irrigation efficiency.

## Environmental Resilience in Lower-Income Countries: Key Challenges

### Water Scarcity and Climate Change Impacts

Climate change has significantly impacted the Indus watershed, resulting in severe water scarcity. The region experiences significant precipitation variability, with an average annual rainfall of 800-1200 mm. Traditional irrigation systems experience significant water loss due to evaporation (30%) and seepage (5%).

### Current Irrigation Practices and Challenges

Existing irrigation methods in the Indus watershed are energy-intensive, with borehole pumps consuming a high amount of electricity. Local surveys and case studies highlight community acceptance and benefits derived from integrated agroforestry-micro-hydro systems.
