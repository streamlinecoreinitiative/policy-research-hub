# nature-based solutions for landslide risk in the Sudano-Sahelian belt: protecting livelihoods under climate stress

**Ensuring Water Security, Clean Energy Access, and Climate Resilience in Sub-Saharan Africa: A Comprehensive Strategy**

## Executive Summary

Sub-Saharan Africa faces significant challenges related to water security, clean energy access, and climate adaptation. With less than a quarter of the population having access to safely managed drinking water (32.4%) [1] and only 53.3% having access to electricity [2], this region is at heightened risk from climate change impacts such as droughts and extreme weather events. To address these challenges, we recommend prioritizing investments in renewable energy infrastructure, water management systems, and climate adaptation measures.

## Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa lags behind globally in terms of access to clean water and electricity [1]. Climate change exacerbates existing vulnerabilities, necessitating urgent action. The region's water withdrawal rate is significantly higher than the world average (9.2%) [3], leading to overuse and depletion of natural resources. Despite progress, renewable energy consumption remains low at only 19.7% of total final energy consumption worldwide [4], leaving many without reliable power sources.

## Analysis

* **Water Security:** Sub-Saharan Africa's water withdrawal rate is significantly higher than the world average (9.2%) [3], leading to overuse and depletion of natural resources.
* **Clean Energy Access:** Despite progress, renewable energy consumption remains low at only 19.7% of total final energy consumption worldwide [4], leaving many without reliable power sources.
* **Climate Adaptation:** The region is highly vulnerable to climate change impacts such as droughts and floods, which can devastate economies and communities reliant on these resources.

## Policy Recommendations

1. **Invest in Renewable Energy Infrastructure:** Develop solar-powered mini-grids for off-grid areas, utilizing existing technology [5].
2. **Strengthen Water Management Systems:** Implement rainwater harvesting and efficient irrigation technologies to improve access to clean drinking water.
3. **Enhance Climate Resilience:** Build early warning systems and disaster preparedness plans to mitigate the impacts of extreme weather events.

## Implementation Considerations

* Collaborate with local communities for sustainable project implementation [5].
* Provide technical assistance and training to ensure proper maintenance and use of new technologies.
* Estimated costs: $1 billion annually over the next five years for comprehensive renewable energy development programs in Sub-Saharan Africa [6]. **[VERIFY]**

## Conclusion

Urgent investments are needed to address water security, clean energy access, and climate resilience in Sub-Saharan Africa. We recommend developing a multi-year plan with clear milestones for implementation by governments, NGOs, and international development partners.
