Sub-Saharan Africa's population is rapidly growing while resources remain scarce. Limited access to safe drinking water and electricity poses health risks and hinders economic development. Climate change intensifies these challenges, with increasing frequency of droughts and floods affecting water availability and energy supply. The global population lacking safe water is estimated at 2.2 billion people [3], with Sub-Saharan Africa contributing significantly to this number.
