**Executive Summary**

Climate change exacerbates water scarcity in lower-income countries, threatening smallholder farmers' livelihoods. This brief explores integrating agroforestry with micro-hydro systems to address severe water scarcity in the Indus Watershed.

**Addressing Water Scarcity through Agroforestry and Micro-Hydro Systems**

### Introduction to Water Scarcity

Climate change affects the Indus Watershed, characterized by variable rainfall patterns. Traditional irrigation systems are energy-intensive and economically burdensome, resulting in significant water loss due to evaporation and seepage.

### Current Irrigation Practices and Challenges

Existing irrigation methods consume substantial amounts of energy (10-20 kWh/day) and result in economic costs ($100-$200/year per hectare). The integration of agroforestry with micro-hydro systems offers a sustainable solution to address these challenges.
