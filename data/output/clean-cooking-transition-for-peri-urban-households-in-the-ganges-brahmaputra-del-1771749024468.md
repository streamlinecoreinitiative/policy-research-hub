## Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa faces significant challenges in accessing clean water and reliable energy due to persistent water scarcity and high dependency on fossil fuels for electricity generation. Climate change intensifies these conditions, leading to more frequent droughts and floods.

### Background on the Issue:

* Sub-Saharan Africa suffers from limited access to safe drinking water (World Bank Open Data) and electricity (World Bank Open Data).
* The region is particularly vulnerable to climate-related impacts such as increased droughts and floods, which exacerbate existing water scarcity issues (UN-Water/WHO).

## Policy Recommendations

1. **Enhance Water Infrastructure**:
	* Invest in desalination plants and large-scale water storage facilities to improve access to safe drinking water, especially in arid regions.
	* Implement rainwater harvesting systems to reduce reliance on groundwater and surface water sources during droughts (World Bank data).
2. **Promote Renewable Energy Projects**:
	* Encourage the development of solar power plants and wind farms, particularly in rural areas where access is limited.
	* Provide subsidies for small-scale renewable energy projects such as biogas digesters to promote cleaner cooking practices (UN SDG Report 2023).
3. **Climate Adaptation Measures**:
	* Establish early-warning systems for droughts and floods using satellite data and traditional knowledge, ensuring timely responses by local communities.
	* Implement climate-resilient agricultural practices such as crop diversification and improved soil management to enhance food security (NOAA climate dataset query).

## Implementation Considerations

### Cost Analysis:

### Barriers:
