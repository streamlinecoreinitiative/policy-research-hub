## Executive Summary

This policy brief highlights the urgent need to address water security, clean energy access, and climate resilience in Sub-Saharan Africa. With only 32.4% of the population having access to safely managed drinking water (World Bank, 2024) [1] and less than half having access to electricity (53.3%, World Bank, 2023) [2], we must prioritize interventions that combine clean energy solutions, improved water management practices, and climate adaptation strategies targeting both rural and urban populations.

## Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa is one of the most vulnerable regions to climate change due to its reliance on agriculture and natural resources (World Bank, 2021). The region's population growth combined with environmental degradation threatens sustainable development goals. Addressing these issues is crucial for reducing poverty, improving health outcomes, and fostering economic growth.
