# Micro-insurance for Smallholder Drought Risk in Caribbean SIDS: Enhancing Climate Resilience

## Executive Summary

Climate change poses significant threats to water security and climate adaptation in lower-income countries, particularly in the Caribbean Small Island Developing States (SIDS). This brief highlights the importance of micro-insurance solutions for smallholder farmers facing drought risks in these regions.

## Policy Recommendations

To support the adoption of agroforestry-based micro-hydro systems in the Caribbean SIDS:

1.  **Subsidies and Grants:**
    *   Government subsidies or low-interest loans to support initial investments.
2.  **Infrastructure Development:**
    *   Funding for community-based irrigation system improvements.

## Actions/Recommendations

*   Conduct a thorough cost-benefit analysis of integrated agroforestry-micro-hydro systems in the Caribbean SIDS.
*   Develop policy frameworks and guidelines for government subsidies or low-interest loans to support initial investments.
*   Establish partnerships between local governments, farmers' organizations, and environmental experts to facilitate community-led micro-hydro system installation and maintenance.

## Closing Outlook

The implementation of agroforestry-based micro-hydro systems in the Caribbean SIDS has the potential to significantly reduce disaster losses for vulnerable groups. By addressing water security challenges through integrated solutions, we can enhance climate resilience and promote sustainable development in these regions.
