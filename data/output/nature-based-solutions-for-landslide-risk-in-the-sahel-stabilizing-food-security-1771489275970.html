Policy Recommendations

To address these challenges effectively, we recommend the following policy interventions:

1. Expand Access to Safe Drinking Water: Invest in large-scale water supply infrastructure projects such as boreholes and pipelines. Target rural areas where access is lowest and prioritize low-cost solutions like rainwater harvesting systems. Implement community-based management models to ensure sustainable use of resources.

  1. Promote Renewable Energy Integration: Increase the share of renewable energy in electricity generation through subsidies, tax incentives, and public-private partnerships. Encourage small-scale solar-powered water pumping stations for rural communities. Develop robust grid integration strategies to support higher penetration rates of decentralized renewable energy systems.
  2. Enhance Climate Resilience Infrastructure: Invest in early warning systems for droughts and floods, including improved monitoring networks and timely communication channels. Implement climate-resilient infrastructure designs such as elevated buildings and flood-resistant roads. Provide financial assistance through microfinance institutions or community-based insurance schemes to vulnerable groups.
