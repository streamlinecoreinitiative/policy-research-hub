**Enhancing Climate Resilience in Sub-Saharan Africa: Strengthening Water Security, Clean Energy Access, and Adaptation**

## Executive Summary

Sub-Saharan Africa faces a critical challenge in ensuring safe drinking water, reliable electricity, and sustainable energy sources due to climate change. To address this issue, we recommend that governments invest in expanding renewable energy sources and improving infrastructure for water management.

## Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa is highly vulnerable to climate change impacts such as droughts, floods, and erratic rainfall patterns. The region lacks adequate water management systems and sustainable energy solutions, exacerbating existing challenges like malnutrition, health issues, and economic instability. Water scarcity affects millions of people daily, leading to poor hygiene practices, disease outbreaks, and reduced agricultural productivity.

## Analysis

The current situation in Sub-Saharan Africa is dire and requires immediate attention. Access to clean water remains a significant challenge, with only one-third of the population having access to safely managed drinking water.

* **Renewable energy consumption (%) - Sub-Saharan Africa (2020)**: 13.5 [3]

## Policy Recommendations

To address these challenges, we propose the following policy recommendations:

1. **Expand Water Infrastructure:** Invest in constructing and maintaining water supply systems to ensure reliable access to clean drinking water for all communities.
2. **Promote Renewable Energy Use:** Increase investment in renewable energy projects such as solar-powered desalination plants, wind turbines, and small hydroelectric systems to meet electricity needs sustainably.
3. **Enhance Climate Adaptation Programs:** Implement climate-resilient agriculture practices, develop early warning systems for extreme weather events, and support indigenous knowledge systems in managing natural resources.
