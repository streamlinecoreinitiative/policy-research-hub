## Executive Summary

Sub-Saharan Africa faces critical challenges related to water security, clean energy access, and climate adaptation. With only 32.4% of the region's population having access to safely managed drinking water (World Bank, 2024) [1] and just 53.3% with electricity access (World Bank, 2023) [2], this area requires urgent policy interventions. ## Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa is severely lacking in access to safe drinking water, with only 32.4% of the population having it (World Bank, 2024) [1]. The region lags behind in electricity access, with 53.3% of sub-Saharan Africans having access to electricity (World Bank, 2023) [2]. Climate resilience is also a concern, as limited renewable energy consumption at 9.2% of total freshwater withdrawal (World Bank, 2021) [3] exacerbates the issue.

## Analysis

### Water Security
Sub-Saharan Africa's water security is precarious with only 32.4% accessing safe drinking water (World Bank, 2024) [1]. This is a critical challenge that requires immediate attention to ensure access to this essential resource for all citizens.

### Clean Energy Access
The region lags behind in electricity access, with 53.3% of sub-Saharan Africans having access to electricity (World Bank, 2023) [2]. This highlights the need for renewable energy sources to bridge this gap and provide sustainable energy solutions.

### Climate Adaptation
Renewable energy consumption stands at 9.2%, far below global standards (World Bank, 2021) [3], which is essential for climate resilience. Integrating drought-resistant crops and early warning systems for heatwaves can help mitigate the impacts of climate change.

## Policy Recommendations

1. **Renewable Energy Development**: Invest in solar and wind power projects to meet electricity demands, aiming for a minimum of 50% renewable energy by 2030 (World Bank, 2024) [1].
2. **Water Infrastructure Improvement**: Expand water infrastructure investments to improve access to safe drinking water, targeting at least 80% coverage within five years.
3. **Climate Resilience Programs**: Implement climate adaptation programs that integrate drought-resistant crops and early warning systems for heatwaves (NOAA Climate Data).
