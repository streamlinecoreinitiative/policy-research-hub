# nature-based solutions for landslide risk in the Sudano-Sahelian belt: raising resilience finance for communities

**Nature-Based Solutions for Landslide Risk in the Sudano-Sahelian Belt: Raising Resilience Finance for Communities**

**Executive Summary:**

Climate-induced disasters have become more frequent and severe in lower-income countries, particularly in the Sudano-Sahelian belt where landslides pose a significant threat to communities. This brief explores nature-based solutions for landslide risk management, focusing on raising resilience finance for vulnerable households. Micro-insurance schemes are emerging as critical tools for protecting these communities from financial losses due to extreme weather events.

**Micro-Insurance Innovations in the Sudano-Sahelian Belt**

The Sudano-Sahelian belt is a region of high climate-related disaster risk, where landslides and floods often cause significant damage to infrastructure and livelihoods. In this context, micro-insurance schemes can help communities recover more quickly from disasters by providing access to financial resources when they need it most.

### Current Micro-Insurance Initiatives

*   Several organizations are piloting or operating micro-insurance schemes in the Sudano-Sahelian belt, including:
    *   The African Risk Capacity (ARC) Insurance Company, which provides index-based insurance for drought and other climate-related disasters.
    *   The Global Index Insurance Facility (GIIF), a multi-donor initiative that supports the development of index insurance products for smallholder farmers.

### Success Stories and Challenges

*   While micro-insurance initiatives have shown promise in reducing disaster risk, challenges persist, including:
    *   High costs for smallholders, which can limit uptake.
    *   Limited availability of data on climate-related risks, making it difficult to design effective insurance products.

**Economic Benefits and Sustainability**

Micro-insurance schemes can provide economic benefits to households by:

*   Reducing financial losses from disasters
*   Increasing access to credit and other financial services
*   Promoting more resilient livelihoods

However, the long-term sustainability of these initiatives depends on various factors, including:

*   Government support for subsidies or incentives
*   Partnerships with microfinance institutions to increase coverage
*   Integration into broader climate adaptation strategies

**Challenges and Limitations**

Despite their potential benefits, micro-insurance schemes face several challenges, including:

*   High costs for smallholders, which can limit uptake
*   Limited availability of data on climate-related risks, making it difficult to design effective insurance products

### Potential Solutions

To address these challenges, governments and other stakeholders can consider the following solutions:

*   Providing subsidies or incentives for micro-insurance uptake
*   Partnering with microfinance institutions to increase coverage
*   Integrating micro-insurance into broader climate adaptation strategies

**Role of Micro-Insurance in Climate Adaptation Strategies**

Micro-insurance schemes can be integrated into climate adaptation plans by:

*   Providing a financial safety net for households affected by disasters
*   Promoting more resilient livelihoods and infrastructure
*   Supporting the development of early warning systems and other risk management tools

### Examples from Other Regions

Countries like Jamaica and Haiti have successfully integrated micro-insurance into their national strategies, demonstrating the potential for these initiatives to reduce disaster risk.

**Future Directions and Recommendations**

To expand coverage and increase the effectiveness of micro-insurance schemes in the Sudano-Sahelian belt:

*   Governments and other stakeholders should prioritize data collection on climate-related risks
*   Partnerships between insurance companies, governments, and microfinance institutions can help increase coverage and reduce costs for smallholders
*   Integration into broader climate adaptation strategies is essential for long-term sustainability

**Actions/Recommendations:**

1.  **Support Data Collection**: Governments and other stakeholders should prioritize data collection on climate-related risks to inform the design of effective insurance products.
2.  **Promote Partnerships**: Insurance companies, governments, and microfinance institutions should partner to increase coverage and reduce costs for smallholders.
3.  **Integrate into Climate Adaptation Strategies**: Micro-insurance schemes should be integrated into broader climate adaptation strategies to ensure long-term sustainability.

**Closing Outlook:**

Micro-insurance schemes have the potential to significantly reduce disaster risk in lower-income countries, particularly in the Sudano-Sahelian belt where landslides pose a significant threat. By prioritizing data collection, promoting partnerships, and integrating micro-insurance into climate adaptation strategies, governments and other stakeholders can help raise resilience finance for vulnerable households and promote more resilient livelihoods.
