## Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa faces significant environmental challenges due to its limited access to clean water sources. Despite accounting for only 9.2% of global freshwater withdrawals (World Bank Open Data), the region experiences severe scarcity issues. The lack of safe drinking water and electricity not only impacts health but also hinders economic development and social stability, with [VERIFY] billion people lacking access to safely managed water globally (Source needed). Climate change exacerbates these challenges, necessitating urgent intervention to safeguard future generations.

## Analysis

Data from the World Bank indicates a stark disparity in access to clean water between Sub-Saharan Africa (32.4%) and the global average (73.7%). This gap is exacerbated by climate-induced variability, leading to frequent droughts and floods. Renewable energy consumption in Sub-Saharan Africa stands at 19.7% of total final energy consumption (World Bank Open Data), far below the global average.

## Policy Recommendations

1. **Prioritize Water Security Solutions**: Invest in construction of solar-powered wells and rainwater harvesting ponds to ensure access to safe drinking water in remote areas.
2. **Invest in Renewable Energy Projects**: Develop medium-sized solar farms, wind turbines, and mini-grids to reduce reliance on fossil fuels and provide a stable source of electricity for communities.
3. **Enhance Climate Adaptation Strategies**: Develop climate-resilient infrastructure including flood barriers, water storage facilities, and early warning systems to protect against increasingly frequent extreme weather events.

## Implementation Considerations

Initial investment in clean water projects can range from $10,000-$50,000 per well (estimated costs). Renewable energy projects may cost between $2 million to $10 million for a medium-sized solar farm. Political instability and low government funding are significant barriers; collaboration with international donors and NGOs will be crucial in overcoming these obstacles.

## Conclusion

Sub-Saharan Africa must adopt comprehensive policies that prioritize water security, clean energy, and climate adaptation to build a resilient future. Immediate action is required to meet the SDG 6 target of safely managed drinking water for all by 2030 and ensure sustainable development across the region.
