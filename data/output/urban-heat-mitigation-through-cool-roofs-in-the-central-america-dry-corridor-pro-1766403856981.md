**Urban Heat Mitigation through Cool Roofs in the Central America Dry Corridor: Protecting Livelihoods under Climate Stress**

**Executive Summary**

The Central America dry corridor is highly vulnerable to climate change, facing severe droughts and heatwaves that threaten food security, economic stability, and human well-being. This brief highlights the importance of urban heat mitigation through cool roofs as a critical adaptation strategy for protecting livelihoods in this region.

**Section 1: Introduction to Climate Change Impacts in the Central America Dry Corridor**

The Central America dry corridor spans across six countries (Guatemala, Honduras, El Salvador, Nicaragua, Costa Rica, and Panama) and is characterized by a semi-arid climate with limited rainfall. Climate change is exacerbating droughts and heatwaves in this region, leading to significant impacts on agriculture, water resources, and human health.

**Section 2: Current Urban Planning Practices and Challenges**

Urban planning practices in the Central America dry corridor often prioritize economic development over climate resilience. Existing building codes and regulations do not adequately address heat mitigation, leading to increased energy consumption and heat-related discomfort.

*   **Current Practices:**

    *   Insufficient community engagement in decision-making processes
