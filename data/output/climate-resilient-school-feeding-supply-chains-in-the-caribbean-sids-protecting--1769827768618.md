## Context & Challenge

Lower-income countries face significant challenges related to water security, clean energy access, and climate adaptation. Access to safely managed drinking water is 73.7% globally but only 32.4% in Sub-Saharan Africa (World Bank Open Data). Electricity access stands at 91.6%, yet 53.3% of Sub-Saharan Africans remain without electricity (World Bank Development Indicators). Climate change exacerbates these challenges, with over 2 billion people lacking safe water worldwide (UN-Water/WHO).

Globally, 91.6% of populations have electricity (World Bank Development Indicators), but Sub-Saharan Africa struggles with only [53.3%]. Transitioning towards renewable energy sources can help bridge this gap while also reducing carbon emissions.

### Climate Adaptation

Climate change exacerbates existing vulnerabilities in lower-income countries. The World Bank reports that over 2 billion people lack safe water, highlighting the critical need for climate-resilient infrastructure and policies (World Bank Open Data). Enhancing early warning systems and implementing nature-based solutions can help mitigate these impacts.
