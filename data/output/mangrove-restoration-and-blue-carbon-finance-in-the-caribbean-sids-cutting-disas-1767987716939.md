# Mangrove Restoration and Blue Carbon Finance in Caribbean SIDS: Enhancing Climate Resilience for Vulnerable Groups

## Executive Summary

This brief highlights the importance of mangrove restoration and blue carbon finance in reducing disaster losses for vulnerable groups in Small Island Developing States (SIDS) of the Caribbean. The region is highly susceptible to climate-related disasters, including hurricanes, storm surges, and sea-level rise. Mangroves play a crucial role in protecting coastal communities from these impacts by providing natural barriers against storms and erosion.

## Environmental Resilience in Lower-Income Countries: Water Security, Clean Energy, and Climate Adaptation

Water scarcity is a pressing issue in many lower-income countries, including those in the Caribbean. The lack of access to clean water and sanitation facilities exacerbates poverty, hunger, and disease. In SIDS, climate change further complicates water management due to increased precipitation variability and sea-level rise.
