# Water Loss Reduction in Secondary Cities in the Mekong Basin: Supporting Adaptation Targets in NDCs

## Executive Summary

The Mekong Basin, home to over 60 million people, faces significant water security challenges due to climate change, population growth, and unsustainable agricultural practices. This brief outlines an integrated approach to address severe water scarcity and poverty through agroforestry-based micro-hydro systems.

## Introduction to Water Scarcity in the Mekong Basin

The Mekong Basin is a critical region for biodiversity conservation and freshwater resources globally. However, water scarcity poses a significant threat to ecosystems and human populations. Climate change exacerbates this issue through altered precipitation patterns, increased evaporation rates, and more frequent extreme weather events.

### Key Challenges:

• **Climate Change Impacts:** Rising temperatures, altered precipitation patterns, and increased frequency of droughts and floods.
• **Population Growth:** Rapid urbanization and population growth strain water resources, leading to competition among users.
• **Unsustainable Agricultural Practices:** Inefficient irrigation systems, monoculture farming, and lack of crop diversification contribute to soil degradation and water pollution.

## Current Practices and Challenges

Existing irrigation systems in the Mekong Basin are often inefficient, relying on traditional methods that waste significant amounts of water. High operational costs and limited access to electricity for micro-hydro systems further exacerbate these challenges.

### Key Challenges:

• **Inefficient Irrigation Systems:** Traditional methods lead to high water loss rates, straining already scarce resources.
• **Limited Access to Electricity:** Micro-hydro systems are often hindered by inadequate infrastructure, hindering their potential benefits.
• **Socio-Economic Factors:** Poverty and lack of access to education and training programs limit communities' ability to adopt new technologies.

## Agroforestry’s Role in Climate-Resilient Irrigation

Agroforestry practices have been shown to enhance soil fertility, improve water retention, and increase crop yields. Integrating agroforestry with micro-hydro systems can provide sustainable water management solutions, ensuring climate resilience for communities.

### Case Studies:

• **Successful Integration:** Examples of agroforestry-based irrigation systems demonstrate improved efficiency, reduced water loss rates, and increased crop yields.
• **Potential Synergies:** Agroforestry and micro-hydro systems can be integrated to enhance overall system efficiency and sustainability.

## Micro-Hydro Systems for Efficiency

Micro-hydro technology offers a reliable and efficient means of harnessing renewable energy. Its advantages include low capital costs, high reliability, and minimal environmental impact.

### Case Studies:

• **Successful Implementation:** Examples of micro-hydro projects demonstrate improved water management practices, increased crop yields, and enhanced community resilience.
• **Potential Integration:** Micro-hydro systems can be integrated with agroforestry to enhance overall system efficiency and sustainability.
