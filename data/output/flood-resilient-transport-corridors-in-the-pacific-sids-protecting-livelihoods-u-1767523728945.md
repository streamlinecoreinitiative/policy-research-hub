# Flood-Resilient Transport Corridors in the Pacific SIDS: Protecting Livelihoods under Climate Stress

## Executive Summary

This brief highlights the critical need for flood-resilient transport corridors in the Pacific Small Island Developing States (SIDS) to protect livelihoods and economies from climate-related stress. The proposed solution combines agroforestry-based micro-hydro systems with climate adaptation strategies, ensuring water security and clean energy access while promoting sustainable development.

Climate change is exacerbating water scarcity in the Pacific SIDS, affecting agriculture, food security, and economic stability. ### Current Irrigation Practices and Challenges

Existing irrigation systems in the Pacific SIDS are often inefficient, relying on diesel generators or boreholes that waste water through evaporation and seepage. These practices not only strain local resources but also contribute to greenhouse gas emissions.

### Micro-Hydro Systems for Efficiency and Clean Energy

Micro-hydro technology offers a reliable and efficient means of generating clean energy, reducing reliance on diesel generators and lowering greenhouse gas emissions. These systems can be integrated with agroforestry to create a sustainable water supply.

*   **Benefits of micro-hydro systems:**
    *   Reduced energy costs
    *   Increased energy efficiency
    *   Clean energy generation

### Integration in Climate-Resilient Water Supply and Policy Recommendations

Pilot projects have demonstrated the effectiveness of combining agroforestry and micro-hydro systems to ensure climate-resilient water supply. To support scaling up, policy recommendations include:

*   **Subsidies for integrated agroforestry-micro-hydro projects**
*   **Low-interest loans for community-led initiatives**
*   **Infrastructure development for efficient energy distribution**
