## Executive Summary

We recommend immediate investments in cool roof technologies to mitigate urban heat islands and improve health outcomes during heatwaves in the Ganges-Brahmaputra delta. This initiative should prioritize vulnerable communities, particularly those with limited access to healthcare services. By implementing cool roofs, we can reduce the urban heat island effect by up to 30% (Source: [1]).

## Context & Challenge

The Ganges-Brahmaputra delta is one of the most populous regions in the world, with over 40 million people living in urban areas. The region experiences frequent heatwaves, with temperatures often reaching above 45°C (113°F) during the summer months. This has severe consequences for public health, particularly among vulnerable populations such as the elderly and children.
