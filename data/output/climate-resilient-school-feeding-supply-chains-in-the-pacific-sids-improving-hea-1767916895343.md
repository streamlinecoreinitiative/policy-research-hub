# Climate-Resilient School Feeding Supply Chains in Pacific SIDS: Improving Health Outcomes in Heatwaves

## Executive Summary

Climate change poses significant challenges to water security and climate adaptation, particularly in lower-income countries. The Pacific Small Island Developing States (SIDS) are among those most vulnerable due to their unique geography and limited resources. This brief explores the potential of agroforestry-based micro-hydro systems for improving irrigation efficiency and addressing severe water scarcity.

## Introduction to Water Scarcity in the Pacific SIDS

The Pacific SIDS face numerous challenges related to water security, including:

* Severe droughts exacerbated by climate change [verify]
* Limited access to clean energy and electricity for irrigation systems
* Unsustainable agricultural practices contributing to soil degradation and decreased water retention
* Population growth putting pressure on already scarce water resources

## Current Practices and Challenges in the Pacific SIDS

The region's existing irrigation systems are often inefficient, leading to:

* High operational costs due to energy consumption [verify]
* Limited access to electricity for micro-hydro systems
* Unsustainable agricultural practices contributing to soil degradation and decreased water retention
* Socio-economic factors exacerbating water scarcity, such as poverty and limited education on water management

Micro-hydro technology offers several advantages, including:

* High reliability due to reduced dependence on fossil fuels
* Potential for integration with agroforestry practices to enhance overall system efficiency and sustainability

* Promoting drought-resistant crops and improved water management practices
