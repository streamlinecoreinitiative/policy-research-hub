**Strengthening Water Security, Clean Energy, and Climate Adaptation in Sub-Saharan Africa: A Path to Resilient Development**

## Executive Summary

Sub-Saharan Africa faces significant challenges in water security, clean energy access, and climate adaptation. With only 32.4% of the region's population having access to safely managed drinking water (World Bank Open Data), it is imperative that governments and NGOs prioritize interventions to improve these critical sectors. This policy brief recommends focusing on expanding access to safe drinking water, increasing electricity generation from renewable sources, and enhancing early warning systems for climate-related disasters.

## Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa is one of the most vulnerable regions globally due to its limited infrastructure and resources. High population growth rates (2.7% annual rate, World Bank Open Data) coupled with inadequate water supply and electricity access exacerbate existing vulnerabilities. Addressing these challenges is crucial for achieving Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs) 6 (water), 7 (energy), and 13 (climate action).

## Analysis

### Access to Safe Drinking Water

Only 32.4% of Sub-Saharan Africa's population has access to safely managed drinking water, compared to 73.7% globally (World Bank Open Data). This disparity is particularly concerning given the region's high population growth rates.

### Climate Vulnerability

The region is highly susceptible to climate-related disasters such as droughts and floods, affecting both water availability and energy supply stability. Early warning systems are critical for mitigating these impacts, particularly in areas with limited infrastructure.
