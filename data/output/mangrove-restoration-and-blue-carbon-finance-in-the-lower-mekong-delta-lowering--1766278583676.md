# Mangrove Restoration and Blue Carbon Finance in the Lower Mekong Delta: Reducing Emissions from Critical Sectors

## Executive Summary

The Lower Mekong delta is highly vulnerable to climate change, facing severe water scarcity, soil salinization, and frequent flooding. This brief highlights the importance of mangrove restoration and blue carbon finance in addressing these challenges and reducing greenhouse gas emissions.

### **Water Security**

* Current irrigation systems are inefficient, leading to significant water loss through evaporation and seepage.

### **Clean Energy**

* The region's energy mix is heavily reliant on fossil fuels, contributing to greenhouse gas emissions and air pollution.
* Micro-hydro systems offer a viable alternative for clean energy generation, reducing reliance on diesel generators and increasing energy efficiency.
* Agroforestry practices can enhance water retention, reduce erosion, and promote biodiversity.

### **Climate Adaptation**

* Mangrove forests play a critical role in coastal protection, shoreline stabilization, and carbon sequestration.
* Restoration efforts can help mitigate the impacts of climate change, including sea-level rise and increased storm frequency.
* Blue carbon finance mechanisms can provide incentives for sustainable land-use practices and ecosystem restoration.

1.  **Integrate Agroforestry and Micro-Hydro Systems**: Promote integrated agroforestry-micro-hydro projects to enhance water security, reduce energy consumption, and promote clean energy generation.

3.  **Enhance Climate Resilience**: Develop climate-resilient irrigation systems, promote efficient water use, and enhance community engagement in adaptation planning.
