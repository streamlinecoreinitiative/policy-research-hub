# Early-Warning Systems and Last-Mile Alerts in the Sudano-Sahelian Belt: Lowering Emissions from Critical Sectors

## Executive Summary

The Sudano-Sahelian belt is highly vulnerable to climate change, facing severe water scarcity, poverty, and limited access to clean energy. This brief proposes an integrated approach combining early-warning systems, last-mile alerts, and agroforestry-based micro-hydro systems for climate-resilient irrigation in critical sectors. By leveraging technology and nature-based solutions, we can reduce emissions, improve water security, and enhance economic benefits for communities.

## Introduction to Water Scarcity in the Sudano-Sahelian Belt

The region is characterized by a semi-arid climate with limited rainfall, making it prone to droughts and water scarcity. Climate change exacerbates these conditions, leading to crop failures, reduced agricultural productivity, and increased poverty rates. Existing irrigation systems are often inefficient, resulting in significant water loss through evaporation and seepage.

## Current Irrigation Practices and Challenges

The region's current irrigation practices rely heavily on diesel generators, contributing to greenhouse gas emissions. The lack of early-warning systems and last-mile alerts hinders farmers' ability to respond effectively to droughts and other climate-related events, resulting in significant economic losses for communities.

## Micro-Hydro Systems for Irrigation Efficiency

Micro-hydro technology offers a promising solution to reduce reliance on diesel generators and increase energy efficiency. Small-scale hydroelectric systems can be integrated with agroforestry practices to provide clean energy for irrigation, reducing greenhouse gas emissions and improving water security.

## Integration of Agroforestry and Micro-Hydro in Climate-Resilient Water Supply

Pilot projects have demonstrated the potential of combining agroforestry and micro-hydro systems for climate-resilient irrigation. These integrated approaches can improve water retention, reduce erosion, and enhance crop yields while reducing greenhouse gas emissions.
