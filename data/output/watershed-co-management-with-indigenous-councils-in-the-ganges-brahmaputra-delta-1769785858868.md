## Executive Summary

To address the pressing challenges of water security, clean energy access, and climate adaptation in Sub-Saharan Africa, we recommend prioritizing investments in renewable energy infrastructure, enhancing water supply services, and developing early warning systems. This requires a concerted effort from governments, NGOs, and the private sector to allocate dedicated funds for these initiatives.

### Climate Adaptation

* Global temperature rise has already reached +1.1°C since pre-industrial times (IPCC AR6, 2018).
* Sub-Saharan Africa experiences more frequent and intense climate-related events like droughts and floods, exacerbating existing vulnerabilities.

## Policy Recommendations

1. **Invest in Renewable Energy Infrastructure**: Allocate dedicated funds to expand solar, wind, and hydropower projects, particularly in rural areas where access is limited.
2. **Enhance Water Supply and Sanitation Services**: Implement community-based water supply systems and sanitation facilities to improve access to safe drinking water and reduce waterborne diseases.
3. **Develop Early Warning Systems**: Establish early warning networks for droughts, floods, and other climate-related events to enable timely responses and minimize loss of life and property.

## Implementation Considerations

* Costs: Initial investments in renewable energy projects can range from $100 million to $500 million per project (World Bank estimates), while sanitation improvements might require smaller but consistent funding streams.
* Barriers: Limited financial resources, political will, and technical expertise pose significant challenges. Collaboration with international partners and leveraging technology for cost-effective solutions are essential.

## Conclusion

Addressing water security, clean energy access, and climate resilience requires concerted efforts from governments, NGOs, and the private sector. By prioritizing investments in renewable energy infrastructure, enhancing water supply services, and developing early warning systems, Sub-Saharan Africa can enhance its environmental resilience and foster sustainable development for future generations.
