## Executive Summary

Sub-Saharan Africa faces significant challenges in water security, clean energy access, and climate adaptation. With only 32.4% of the population having access to safely managed drinking water (World Bank Open Data), urgent action is needed to address this critical issue. Similarly, limited electricity access (53.3%) hinders economic development and quality of life (World Bank Open Data). Climate change exacerbates these challenges, necessitating comprehensive interventions that are both feasible and sustainable.

## Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa is one of the regions most vulnerable to climate-induced water scarcity due to erratic rainfall patterns and overexploitation of natural resources. The region's reliance on groundwater for agriculture leads to over-extraction, threatening water table levels (World Bank Open Data). Limited access to clean drinking water poses health risks and hinders economic productivity. Electricity shortages disproportionately affect rural areas, limiting educational opportunities and industrial growth.

Climate change accelerates these trends, necessitating urgent policy responses.
