**Micro-insurance for Smallholder Drought Risk in the Great Lakes Region: Protecting Livelihoods under Climate Stress**

**Executive Summary**

The Great Lakes region is highly vulnerable to droughts, which can have devastating impacts on smallholder farmers' livelihoods. This brief explores the potential of micro-insurance as a climate resilience strategy for these farmers.

**Section 1: Introduction to Water Scarcity**

Climate change is characterized by erratic rainfall patterns, which can have severe impacts on agriculture in the Great Lakes region. The average annual rainfall in this area ranges from 800-1200 mm [verify]. Traditional irrigation systems are energy-intensive and costly, with borehole pumps consuming significant amounts of energy.

**Section 2: Current Irrigation Practices**

Traditional irrigation methods in the Ethiopian Highlands are often inefficient and expensive. Borehole pumps consume a substantial amount of energy [verify], while canal systems experience water losses through evaporation and seepage.

**Section 3: Agroforestry's Role in Climate Resilience**

Agroforestry has been shown to improve soil health, increase biodiversity, and enhance carbon sequestration. Studies have demonstrated that agroforestry can:

* Increase soil organic matter by up to 8% [verify]
* Enhance biodiversity
* Sequester carbon

**Section 4: Micro-Hydro Systems for Irrigation Efficiency**

Micro-hydro systems provide a sustainable and renewable energy source for irrigation. These systems can generate power ranging from 10-50 kW per system and achieve energy efficiency of up to 80% [verify].

**Section 5: Integration of Agroforestry and Micro-Hydro in Climate-Resilient Water Supply**

The integration of agroforestry and micro-hydro systems offers a cost-effective solution for climate-resilient water supply. This approach can provide significant cost savings compared to traditional irrigation methods.

**Actions/Recommendations**

1. **Subsidies for Farmers**: Provide incentives to encourage adoption of integrated agroforestry-micro-hydro systems.
2. **Grid Integration**: Integrate micro-hydro systems with the grid to enhance reliability and efficiency.
3. **Infrastructure Development**: Improve roads, storage facilities, and other infrastructure to support the implementation of integrated agroforestry-micro-hydro systems.

**Closing Outlook**

The proposed solution offers a promising approach to addressing water scarcity in the Great Lakes region. By integrating agroforestry and micro-hydro systems, smallholder farmers can build resilience and adaptability in the face of climate change.
