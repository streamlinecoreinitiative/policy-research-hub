# Mangrove Restoration and Blue Carbon Finance in the Indus Watershed: Protecting Livelihoods under Climate Stress

## Executive Summary

The Indus watershed is a critical region for climate resilience, supporting millions of livelihoods through agriculture, fishing, and other water-dependent activities. However, climate change has significantly impacted this region, exacerbating water scarcity, increased frequency of extreme weather events, and loss of biodiversity.

*   **Increased frequency of extreme weather events**: Climate change has led to more frequent and intense floods, droughts, and heatwaves, affecting agriculture, fishing, and other water-dependent activities.

## Mangrove Restoration and Blue Carbon Finance

Mangrove restoration can help address these challenges by:

*   **Improving water quality**: Mangroves act as natural filters, improving water quality and reducing the risk of waterborne diseases.
*   **Enhancing biodiversity**: Mangroves provide habitat for a wide range of plant and animal species, supporting fisheries and agriculture.
*   **Sequestering carbon**: Mangroves are highly efficient at sequestering carbon dioxide, making them an essential component of blue carbon finance.

Blue carbon finance can help support mangrove restoration by providing:

*   **Funding for restoration efforts**: Blue carbon finance can provide the necessary funding to restore degraded mangrove forests and promote sustainable forest management.
*   **Capacity building and technical assistance**: Blue carbon finance can also provide capacity building and technical assistance to local communities, enabling them to manage their natural resources sustainably.
