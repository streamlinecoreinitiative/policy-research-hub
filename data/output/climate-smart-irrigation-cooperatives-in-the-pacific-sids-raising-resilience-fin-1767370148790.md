# Climate-Smart Irrigation Cooperatives in Pacific SIDS: Enhancing Resilience through Integrated Agroforestry and Micro-Hydro Systems

## Executive Summary

Climate change poses significant threats to water security, clean energy access, and climate adaptation in lower-income countries, particularly Small Island Developing States (SIDS) in the Pacific. This brief highlights the importance of integrating agroforestry with micro-hydro systems as a sustainable solution for climate-resilient irrigation in these regions.

## Section 1: Climate Change Impacts on Water Security

Climate change is exacerbating water scarcity in the Pacific SIDS due to increased evaporation from rising sea levels, altered precipitation patterns, and reduced groundwater recharge. Traditional irrigation methods are often energy-intensive and costly, further straining local economies.

## Section 2: Current Irrigation Practices and Challenges

Traditional irrigation methods in the Pacific SIDS rely on boreholes, canals, and diesel generators, which are plagued by high energy costs due to reliance on fossil fuels, water losses through evaporation and seepage, and economic burdens from labor, maintenance, and fuel costs.

## Section 4: Micro-Hydro Systems for Efficiency

Micro-hydro systems can provide a reliable source of renewable energy for irrigation, reducing reliance on fossil fuels and lowering operating costs. Successful micro-hydro projects in the region have demonstrated significant energy savings through reduced diesel generator use and improved water security through efficient irrigation practices.

## Section 5: Integration of Agroforestry and Micro-Hydro in Climate-Resilient Water Supply

The integration of agroforestry with micro-hydro systems can provide a comprehensive solution for climate-resilient water supply. Successful pilot projects have demonstrated improved water security through enhanced soil water retention capacity, reduced energy costs through renewable energy use, and enhanced economic benefits through cost savings and increased crop yields.
