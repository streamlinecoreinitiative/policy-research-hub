# Executive Summary

**Enhancing Water Security, Clean Energy, and Climate Resilience in East African Drylands: A Strategic Approach**

## Executive Summary

To address the severe water scarcity and energy poverty in East Africa's arid regions, we recommend investing in rainwater harvesting systems, groundwater recharge projects, and small-scale desalination plants to increase access to safe drinking water. Additionally, implement solar-powered wells, biogas digesters, and off-grid renewable energy solutions (like mini-grids) to provide reliable electricity without expanding the grid. These strategic investments will not only mitigate immediate health and economic crises but also bolster long-term resilience against future shocks.

## Context & Challenge

East Africa's arid regions are characterized by limited freshwater resources and high variability in rainfall patterns. According to the World Bank (2023), only 53.3% of Sub-Saharan Africans have access to electricity, exacerbating energy poverty. The lack of reliable clean energy sources further compounds these issues. Water scarcity is a major concern, with over [2.2 billion people globally lacking safe drinking water](https://www.unwater.org/water-facts/). In East Africa, the situation is dire: only 32.4% of the population has access to safely managed drinking water (World Bank, 2024).

## Analysis

- **Water Security**: East Africa's arid zones are particularly vulnerable to water scarcity. With only [32.4% of the population having access to safely managed drinking water](https://data.worldbank.org/indicator/SH.H2O.SAFE.ZS?locations=ZG), communities suffer from inadequate sanitation and hygiene facilities.
- **Clean Energy Access**: The region struggles with energy poverty, with less than half of Sub-Saharan Africans accessing electricity (World Bank, 2023). In many areas, the primary sources of power are diesel generators and kerosene lamps, contributing to health hazards and environmental degradation.
- **Climate Adaptation**: Climate change intensifies these challenges. Increased temperatures lead to reduced water availability, while erratic rainfall patterns make traditional irrigation systems unreliable.

## Policy Recommendations

1. **Expand Water Infrastructure**: Invest in rainwater harvesting systems, groundwater recharge projects, and small-scale desalination plants to increase access to safe drinking water.
2. **Promote Renewable Energy Sources**: Implement solar-powered wells, biogas digesters, and off-grid renewable energy solutions (like mini-grids) to provide reliable electricity without expanding the grid.
3. **Enhance Climate Resilience**: Develop early-warning systems for droughts and floods, improve land management practices, and integrate climate-resilient crops into local agricultural systems.
