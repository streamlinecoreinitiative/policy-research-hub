## Executive Summary

The Ganges-Brahmaputra delta is one of the most densely populated regions globally, with over 40% of Bangladesh's population and 20% of India's population living in this area. However, the region is highly vulnerable to droughts, which have devastating impacts on smallholder farmers' livelihoods. To address this challenge, we recommend implementing micro-insurance programs for smallholder farmers to protect them against drought-related losses.

## Context & Challenge

The Ganges-Brahmaputra delta faces significant challenges related to water security, clean energy access, and climate adaptation. The region is heavily dependent on monsoon rains, which are becoming increasingly unpredictable due to climate change (IPCC, 2019). ## Analysis

The current lack of insurance coverage for smallholder farmers exacerbates their vulnerability to drought-related losses. According to a recent study, only 10% of smallholder farmers in the Ganges-Brahmaputra delta have access to any form of insurance (World Bank, 2022). This gap is due to high costs, limited financial literacy among farmers, and inadequate institutional capacity for risk management.

3. **Strengthen Institutional Capacity**:
