# Clean Cooking Transition for Peri-Urban Households in the Pacific SIDS: Enhancing Disaster Resilience and Vulnerability Reduction

## Executive Summary

The Pacific Small Island Developing States (SIDS) face significant challenges in providing clean cooking solutions to their peri-urban populations, exacerbating disaster losses and vulnerability. This brief highlights the importance of transitioning from traditional biomass-based cooking methods to cleaner alternatives, such as solar-powered cookers or biogas systems.

## Introduction

### Water Security in the Pacific SIDS

*   The Pacific SIDS are highly vulnerable to climate change impacts, including sea-level rise, increased frequency of extreme weather events, and droughts.
*   Peri-urban households rely heavily on traditional biomass-based cooking methods, contributing significantly to greenhouse gas emissions and air pollution.

### Climate Change Impacts on Water Security

*   Rising temperatures and changing precipitation patterns exacerbate water scarcity in the Pacific SIDS, affecting agriculture, industry, and human consumption.
*   Traditional irrigation systems are often inefficient, leading to significant water losses through evaporation and seepage.

## Current Cooking Practices and Challenges

### Biomass-Based Cooking Methods

*   Peri-urban households rely heavily on biomass-based cooking methods, including charcoal and wood.
*   These methods contribute significantly to greenhouse gas emissions and air pollution, negatively impacting human health and the environment.

### Agroforestry-Based Micro-Hydro Systems

*   Integrating agroforestry with micro-hydro systems can provide a sustainable solution to water scarcity in the Pacific SIDS.

## Policy Recommendations and Future Research Directions

### Incentives for Transitioning to Clean Cooking Solutions

*   Governments should implement policies to incentivize households to transition from traditional biomass-based cooking methods to cleaner alternatives.
*   This can include subsidies, low-interest loans, or tax credits for the adoption of solar-powered cookers or biogas systems.

### Infrastructure Development and Climate-Resilient Water Management Practices

*   Investments in infrastructure development, including roads and storage facilities, are essential for supporting climate-resilient water management practices.
*   Governments should prioritize the development of integrated agroforestry-micro-hydro systems to enhance both water security and renewable energy use.

## Conclusion

The transition to clean cooking solutions is critical for reducing disaster losses and enhancing vulnerability in peri-urban households in the Pacific SIDS. By integrating climate-resilient water management practices with renewable energy sources, these communities can reduce their reliance on fossil fuels, decrease greenhouse gas emissions, and enhance disaster resilience.
