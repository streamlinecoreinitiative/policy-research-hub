# Agroforestry-Based Micro-Hydro Systems for Climate-Resilient Irrigation in the Mesoamerican Highlands

## Executive Summary

The Mesoamerican Highlands face severe water scarcity and poverty exacerbated by climate change impacts on agriculture. This brief presents an integrated solution to address these challenges: agroforestry-based micro-hydro systems for climate-resilient irrigation. By combining agroforestry practices with small-scale hydroelectric systems, farmers can improve soil health, biodiversity, and carbon sequestration while reducing water loss and increasing energy efficiency.

## Introduction to Water Scarcity in the Mesoamerican Highlands

Climate change is altering precipitation patterns and increasing temperatures in the Mesoamerican Highlands, affecting agricultural productivity and water availability. Resilient irrigation methods are crucial for ensuring food security and economic stability in this region. Traditional irrigation systems experience significant water loss through evaporation and seepage.

## Agroforestry-Based Micro-Hydro Systems for Climate-Resilient Irrigation

### Current Irrigation Practices and Challenges
Existing irrigation systems in the Mesoamerican Highlands are often inefficient, leading to water loss and reduced agricultural productivity. Boreholes and canals account for a significant portion of water usage, but their effectiveness is compromised by high evaporation rates and seepage.

### Micro-Hydro Systems for Irrigation Efficiency
Micro-hydro technology offers a viable alternative to traditional irrigation methods, providing energy-efficient solutions for small-scale hydroelectric systems. By harnessing the power of local water sources, farmers can reduce their reliance on diesel generators and minimize environmental impact.

## Integration of Agroforestry and Micro-Hydro in Climate-Resilient Water Supply

Pilot projects have successfully integrated agroforestry and micro-hydro systems to support climate-resilient irrigation. These initiatives demonstrate the potential for scaling up this approach, providing economic benefits and sustainability for farmers in the Mesoamerican Highlands.
