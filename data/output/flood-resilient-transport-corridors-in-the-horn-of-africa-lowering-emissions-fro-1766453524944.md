# Flood-Resilient Transport Corridors in the Horn of Africa: Enhancing Water Security and Lowering Emissions

## Executive Summary

The Horn of Africa is highly vulnerable to climate change, with frequent droughts and floods impacting critical sectors such as transportation. This brief highlights the importance of flood-resilient transport corridors in the region, focusing on lowering emissions from key sectors through an integrated approach combining agroforestry-based micro-hydro systems for irrigation efficiency, clean energy generation, and climate adaptation measures.

Existing irrigation systems in the Horn of Africa include boreholes and canals. These systems suffer from high water loss through evaporation and seepage, hindering agricultural productivity and contributing to food insecurity and economic instability.
