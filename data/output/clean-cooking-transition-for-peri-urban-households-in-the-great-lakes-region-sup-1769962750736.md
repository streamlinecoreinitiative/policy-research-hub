## Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa suffers from chronic water scarcity, inadequate electricity supply, and growing climate risks. These challenges hinder economic growth and exacerbate poverty. Improved access to clean water and electricity is essential for health and productivity. Climate change threatens agricultural stability and increases the frequency of extreme weather events.

According to World Bank data (2024), Sub-Saharan Africa lags significantly in access to safe drinking water, with only 32.4% of the population having access to safely managed drinking water, compared to a global average of 73.7% (World Bank, 2024).

## Policy Recommendations

### Water Security:

1. Invest in water harvesting, desalination plants, and wastewater treatment facilities to improve access to clean water.
2. Implement community-based water management programs that educate on hygiene practices and sustainable use of resources.
3. Develop policies to promote efficient water use and reduce waste.

### Clean Energy:

4. Encourage the adoption of solar panels, wind turbines, and biogas systems for decentralized power generation.
5. Provide subsidies or incentives for households and small businesses to invest in renewable energy technologies.
6. Promote energy efficiency measures to reduce consumption.

### Climate Adaptation:

7. Develop early warning systems and disaster preparedness plans to mitigate climate impacts on agriculture and infrastructure.
8. Implement drought-resistant crop varieties and improved irrigation techniques.
9. Support climate-resilient infrastructure development.
