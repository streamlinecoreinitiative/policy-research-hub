# micro-insurance for smallholder drought risk in the Horn of Africa: protecting livelihoods under climate stress

**Micro-insurance for Smallholder Drought Risk in the Horn of Africa: Enhancing Resilience under Climate Stress**

**Executive Summary**

The Horn of Africa is one of the most drought-prone regions globally, affecting millions of smallholder farmers and their families. The lack of effective water management systems, combined with climate variability, exacerbates the vulnerability of these communities to drought. This brief proposes an integrated approach that combines agroforestry-based micro-hydro systems with micro-insurance mechanisms to enhance resilience in the face of climate stress.

**Addressing Water Scarcity and Climate Resilience**

The Andean Highlands region within the Horn of Africa faces severe water scarcity due to historical droughts and current challenges such as limited access to reliable irrigation systems. Smallholder farmers rely on traditional methods like surface water and rain-fed systems, which are inefficient and unsustainable. The economic impacts of water scarcity include reduced crop yields leading to financial instability.

**Agroforestry-Based Micro-Hydro Systems: A Solution**

Agroforestry practices offer numerous benefits in addressing water scarcity, including:

* Improved water retention and recharge through increased vegetation cover
* Enhanced ecosystem services such as pollination, pest control, and carbon sequestration
