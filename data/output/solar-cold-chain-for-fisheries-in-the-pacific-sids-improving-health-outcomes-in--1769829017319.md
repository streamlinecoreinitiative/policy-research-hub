**Strengthening Water Security, Clean Energy Access, and Climate Resilience in Sub-Saharan Africa**

## Executive Summary

Sub-Saharan Africa faces significant challenges in water security, clean energy access, and climate adaptation. To address these pressing issues, we recommend prioritizing investments in clean energy, enhancing water security, and adopting resilient infrastructure designs. Specifically, governments and development partners should:

* Invest in renewable energy projects that can be scaled up within local contexts, such as solar-powered mini-grids and micro-hydropower systems.
* Develop water management plans using climate-resilient infrastructure like rainwater harvesting systems and desalination plants.
* Integrate climate change considerations into national development plans to ensure long-term sustainability.

## Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa is one of the most vulnerable regions to climate change due to its reliance on natural resources such as water and electricity. The lack of access to clean energy, safe drinking water, and effective climate adaptation measures exacerbates poverty, health issues, and economic instability in these countries. Recent data highlights the severity of the issue:

* Only 32.4% of Sub-Saharan Africa's population has access to safely managed drinking water (World Bank, 2024).
* Less than half of the region's population has access to electricity (World Bank, 2023).

## Policy Recommendations

1. **Clean Energy Access**:
	* Invest in renewable energy projects that can be scaled up within local contexts, such as solar-powered mini-grids and micro-hydropower systems.
	* Implement policies to incentivize private sector investments in clean energy technologies.
2. **Water Security**:
	* Develop water management plans using climate-resilient infrastructure like rainwater harvesting systems and desalination plants.
	* Enhance public-private partnerships for safe drinking water supply, especially in rural areas with limited access.
3. **Climate Adaptation**:
	* Integrate climate change considerations into national development plans to ensure long-term sustainability.
	* Develop early warning systems and last-mile alerts to mitigate the impacts of extreme weather events on vulnerable communities.

## Implementation Considerations

To implement these recommendations, governments and development partners should:

* Establish task forces to oversee the implementation process.
* Allocate dedicated funding streams for clean energy projects, water security initiatives, and climate adaptation measures.
* Conduct capacity-building workshops for local governments and communities to ensure effective implementation.
