# flood-resilient transport corridors in the Sudano-Sahelian belt: safeguarding freshwater ecosystems

**Enhancing Water Security, Clean Energy, and Climate Adaptation in Sub-Saharan Africa**

## Executive Summary

Sub-Saharan Africa faces significant challenges in water security, clean energy access, and climate resilience. To address these critical issues, we recommend prioritizing infrastructure investments in clean water, electricity access, and renewable energy to enhance resilience.

## Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa is characterized by limited resources for clean water supply, electricity generation, and climate adaptation measures. The region's population has access to only 32.4% of safely managed drinking water [Source: World Bank Open Data (2024)], with the remaining 67.6% relying on contaminated or unimproved sources. Furthermore, only 53.3% of sub-Saharan Africans have access to electricity [Source: World Bank Open Data (2023)], and renewable energy consumption stands at a mere 5.8% [Source: World Bank Open Data (2020)]. These issues threaten economic growth, public health, and environmental sustainability in the region.

## Policy Recommendations

To address these challenges, we recommend the following policy interventions:

1. **Increase investments in water infrastructure**:
	* Implement large-scale desalination plants to improve access to clean drinking water.
	* Promote rainwater harvesting systems for households and communities.
2. **Accelerate electrification efforts**:
	* Prioritize rural electrification projects, including solar-powered mini-grids and decentralized renewable energy solutions.
	* Support the development of community-based renewable energy initiatives.
3. **Promote renewable energy development**:
	* Encourage the adoption of solar power, wind farms, and hydroelectric projects to reduce dependence on fossil fuels.

## Implementation Considerations

Implementing these policy recommendations will require significant investments in infrastructure and human resources:

* Estimated costs for desalination plants range from $20 million to $100 million per plant [Source: World Bank Open Data (2024)].
* High initial investment costs, lack of skilled labor, and political resistance may hinder implementation.

## Conclusion

Sub-Saharan Africa faces critical challenges in water security, clean energy access, and climate resilience. To address these issues, we recommend prioritizing infrastructure investments in clean water, electricity access, and renewable energy to enhance resilience. Collaboration between governments, international organizations, and local communities is essential for successful implementation.
