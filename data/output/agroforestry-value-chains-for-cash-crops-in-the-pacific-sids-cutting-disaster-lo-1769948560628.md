## Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa faces significant challenges with water security and climate resilience. With less than one-third of the region's inhabitants having access to safely managed drinking water (World Bank, 2024), millions remain at risk from waterborne diseases. Similarly, only about half of Sub-Saharan African households have access to electricity (World Bank, 2023). These disparities not only impact daily living conditions but also impede economic growth and sustainable development.

The region's renewable energy consumption is particularly low at just 19.7% (World Bank, 2020), indicating significant potential for increasing clean energy use. However, the challenges of water security and climate resilience remain acute. For instance, Sub-Saharan Africa hosts some of the world's most vulnerable regions to climate change impacts such as droughts and floods.

## Policy Recommendations

1. **Expand Access to Safe Drinking Water:** Implement water treatment plants and promote community-based water management systems that prioritize safe drinking water for all communities (UN-Water/WHO, 2022). Increase investment in sanitation infrastructure like latrines and hygiene promotion programs.

   - Data Point: "Global population lacking safe water" is estimated at 2.2 billion people (UN-Water/WHO, 2022).

2. **Enhance Renewable Energy Use:** Encourage the adoption of solar-powered wells and rainwater harvesting technologies to improve access to clean energy for households and small businesses.

   - Data Point: Sub-Saharan Africa's renewable energy consumption is only 19.7% (World Bank, 2020).

3. **Climate Resilience Programs:** Invest in early-warning systems and climate adaptation projects that help communities cope with extreme weather events and other climate-related risks.

4. **Community-Based Initiatives:** Foster local initiatives where community members are involved in planning and implementation of water security and clean energy projects to ensure sustainability and ownership.

   - Data Point: World Bank data indicates 53.3% of Sub-Saharan African households have access to electricity (World Bank, 2023).
