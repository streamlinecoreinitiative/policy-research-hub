**Climate-Smart Irrigation Cooperatives in the Sudano-Sahelian Belt: Lowering Emissions from Critical Sectors**

### Executive Summary

Sub-Saharan Africa faces significant environmental challenges, including water security, clean energy access, and climate resilience. To address these issues, we recommend implementing comprehensive strategies focusing on water security, clean energy provision, and climate adaptation.

### Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa is characterized by high population growth rates, limited natural resources, and significant disparities in access to basic services. Despite progress in renewable energy consumption (19.7% of total final energy consumption), these efforts are not evenly distributed across regions [1]. The lack of adequate water security and electricity access exacerbates health risks and economic disparities, making it crucial to address these issues for achieving the Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs) and fostering inclusive growth.

### Analysis

**Access to Water**: Sub-Saharan Africa faces significant challenges in accessing safe drinking water, with only 32.4% of the population having access [2]. This stark disparity underscores a critical need for improved water management practices.
**Electricity Access**: Despite progress, electricity remains largely inaccessible or unreliable in many regions. Sub-Saharan Africa's electricity access rate is just 53.3%, with significant disparities across countries and within them [3].
**Renewable Energy Consumption**: While the region has seen an increase in renewable energy consumption to 19.7% of total final energy consumption, this remains lower than global averages, indicating room for improvement [4].
