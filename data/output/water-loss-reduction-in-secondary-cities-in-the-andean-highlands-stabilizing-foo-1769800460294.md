**Filling the Water Gap: Enhancing Access to Clean Energy and Climate Adaptation in Sub-Saharan Africa**

## Executive Summary

To address the dual challenge of inadequate water security and limited access to clean energy in Sub-Saharan Africa, we recommend increasing investment in water infrastructure and expanding renewable energy access through community-led solar mini-grids. This policy brief outlines actionable steps for improving climate resilience and enhancing living standards for the region's growing population.

## Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa faces a pressing crisis of inadequate water security and limited clean energy access. The region's population growth outpaces the provision of safe drinking water, with only 32.4% of the population having access to safely managed sources [1]. This disparity is a significant public health concern, affecting 2.2 billion people worldwide who lack access to safe water [2]. Furthermore, despite global trends showing increasing renewable energy consumption at 19.7%, Sub-Saharan Africa lags behind, with less than half of the population enjoying reliable electricity [3].

## Analysis

- **Water Security**: The region's water scarcity crisis is exacerbated by inadequate infrastructure and poor governance. Only 32.4% of the population has access to safely managed drinking water [1], compared to a global average of 73.7% [1]. This disparity highlights the need for increased investment in water supply and sanitation projects.
- **Clean Energy Access**: Sub-Saharan Africa's limited clean energy access is due to high costs and infrastructure challenges, making it difficult to expand access to reliable electricity. The region faces a significant gap in renewable energy consumption, with only 5.7% of its energy coming from renewables [3]. However, this number appears inconsistent with the global trend of increasing renewable energy consumption.

## Policy Recommendations

1. **Increase Water Infrastructure Investment**: Governments should prioritize investment in water supply and sanitation projects, including building wells, purification facilities, and improving distribution networks.
2. **Expand Renewable Energy Access**: Develop community-led solar mini-grids for households and small businesses. Encourage private sector involvement to reduce costs through bulk purchasing agreements with manufacturers.
3. **Promote Climate Resilient Practices**: Implement climate-smart agriculture techniques that improve water efficiency and resilience against droughts. Promote rainwater harvesting systems using indigenous knowledge.

## Implementation Considerations

- **Funding Mechanisms**: Establish dedicated funds for clean energy projects, such as grants or concessional loans from international development partners.
- **Capacity Building**: Train local technicians in renewable energy installation and maintenance to ensure sustainable operations post-project completion.
- **Policy Frameworks**: Develop supportive policies that incentivize private sector participation in solar mini-grid projects. Encourage community-led initiatives by providing necessary infrastructure and resources.

## Conclusion

Sub-Saharan Africa must prioritize investments in water security and clean energy to build climate resilience and improve living standards for its growing population. By implementing the recommendations outlined, decision-makers can make significant strides towards achieving these goals within feasible constraints.
