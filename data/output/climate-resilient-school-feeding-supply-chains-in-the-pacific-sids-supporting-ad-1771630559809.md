# climate-resilient school feeding supply chains in the Pacific SIDS: supporting adaptation targets in NDCs

**Ensuring Water Security, Clean Energy, and Climate Resilience in Sub-Saharan Africa: A Holistic Policy Blueprint**

**Executive Summary**
To address the pressing challenges of water scarcity, limited access to electricity, and increasing climate instability in Sub-Saharan Africa, we recommend a comprehensive approach that integrates clean energy solutions, improves water security, and enhances climate resilience. This policy brief outlines actionable strategies for governments, international organizations, and development practitioners to support sustainable economic growth and social stability.

**Context & Challenge**
Sub-Saharan African countries face significant challenges in accessing safe drinking water ([VERIFY] 32.4% of the population) and electricity (53.3%), exacerbating poverty, hunger, and malnutrition [Source: World Bank Open Data]. Climate change impacts, including droughts, floods, and rising temperatures, further threaten food security and livelihoods [Source: UN-Water/WHO]. Immediate action is necessary to address these pressing issues.

**Analysis**
### Water Security
Limited access to safe drinking water ([VERIFY] 32.4%) highlights the urgency of improving water infrastructure and management systems. Despite significant global progress (73.7% worldwide) [Source: World Bank Open Data], Sub-Saharan Africa lags far behind.

### Clean Energy
Renewable energy consumption is only 19.7%, significantly below global levels, hindering efforts to reduce carbon emissions and foster sustainable development [Source: World Bank Open Data].

### Climate Adaptation
The region is highly vulnerable to climate change impacts, necessitating robust adaptation measures.

**Policy Recommendations**
1. **Renewable Energy Investment**: Increase investment in solar, wind, hydro, and geothermal energy projects to meet electricity needs while reducing carbon emissions by 2030.
2. **Water Infrastructure Development**: Invest in desalination plants, water treatment facilities, and improved sanitation systems to enhance safe drinking water access by 2025.
3. **Climate Resilience Programs**: Implement early warning systems for climate-related disasters, support drought-resistant crop varieties, and promote resilient agricultural practices by 2028.
4. **Integrated Development Strategies**: Encourage sector collaboration (e.g., linking renewable energy projects with agriculture) to maximize benefits and minimize costs.

**Implementation Considerations**
Initial investments in infrastructure can be substantial but are crucial for long-term sustainability and economic growth. Mobilizing international aid, capacity-building programs, and public-private partnerships can help overcome political resistance, limited financial resources, and technical expertise gaps.

**Conclusion**
Sub-Saharan Africa must prioritize water security, clean energy, and climate resilience to ensure sustainable development. By adopting the recommendations outlined in this brief, governments can make significant progress towards achieving these goals, thereby improving the lives of millions of vulnerable communities.
