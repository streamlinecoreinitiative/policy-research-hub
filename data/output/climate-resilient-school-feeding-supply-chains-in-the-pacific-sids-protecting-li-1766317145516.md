# climate-resilient school feeding supply chains in the Pacific SIDS: protecting livelihoods under climate stress

**Climate-Resilient School Feeding Supply Chains in Pacific SIDS: Enhancing Water Security and Livelihoods**

**Executive Summary**

The Pacific Small Island Developing States (SIDS) are highly vulnerable to climate change, posing significant risks to food security and livelihoods. This brief focuses on integrating environmental resilience strategies into school feeding supply chains in these regions. We propose the adoption of agroforestry-based micro-hydro systems for efficient irrigation, clean energy generation, and climate adaptation.

**Climate Change Impacts on Agriculture and Food Security in Pacific SIDS**

The Pacific SIDS are among the most vulnerable regions to climate change due to their unique geography and socioeconomic characteristics. Rising temperatures, changing precipitation patterns, and increased frequency of extreme weather events threaten agricultural productivity, water availability, and food security. According to the Intergovernmental Panel on Climate Change (IPCC), the Pacific region is projected to experience more frequent and intense droughts, floods, and cyclones.

**Current Irrigation Practices and Challenges**

Existing irrigation systems in the Pacific SIDS often rely on boreholes or canals, which are prone to water loss due to evaporation and seepage. These inefficiencies waste precious water resources and increase energy consumption through diesel-powered pumps. Furthermore, these systems lack climate-resilient design features, making them vulnerable to extreme weather events.

Agroforestry practices have been shown to improve soil health, biodiversity, and carbon sequestration while enhancing water retention and reducing erosion. By integrating trees into agricultural landscapes, farmers can reduce their reliance on external inputs, increase crop yields, and adapt to changing climate conditions.

**Micro-Hydro Systems for Efficient Irrigation**

Micro-hydro technology offers a reliable and renewable source of energy for irrigation systems, reducing the need for diesel generators and increasing water efficiency. These small-scale hydroelectric systems harness the energy generated by flowing water to power pumps, minimizing energy consumption and costs.

**Integration of Agroforestry and Micro-Hydro in Climate-Resilient Water Supply**

Pilot projects integrating agroforestry and micro-hydro have demonstrated significant benefits for farmers, including increased crop yields and reduced energy costs. These integrated solutions enhance climate resilience and promote sustainable development goals in agriculture and renewable energy adoption.

**Policy Recommendations and Future Research Directions**

To support the adoption of these integrated solutions at scale:

1.  **Develop policy frameworks** that incentivize the use of agroforestry-based micro-hydro systems for irrigation.
2.  **Invest in capacity building** programs for farmers, extension agents, and policymakers to enhance their understanding and implementation of climate-resilient practices.
3.  **Conduct further research** on the economic impact, community engagement, and scalability of these integrated solutions.

**Closing Outlook**

Climate change poses significant risks to food security and livelihoods in Pacific SIDS. By integrating agroforestry-based micro-hydro systems into school feeding supply chains, we can enhance water security, reduce poverty, and promote economic stability. This approach addresses immediate climate challenges while contributing to broader sustainable development goals.
