## Executive Summary

This brief highlights the importance of mangrove restoration and blue carbon finance in supporting adaptation targets under Nationally Determined Contributions (NDCs) in lower-income countries, specifically focusing on the Indus watershed. The document emphasizes the critical role of environmental resilience in addressing climate change impacts.

## Environmental Resilience in Lower-Income Countries

Lower-income countries face significant challenges in achieving environmental resilience due to limited financial resources and high vulnerability to climate-related disasters. However, these countries also have opportunities for innovative solutions that can address multiple development goals simultaneously.

### Key Challenges:

• **Water Scarcity:** Limited access to clean water affects agriculture, industry, and human consumption.
• **Energy Access:** Insufficient energy supply hampers economic growth and poverty reduction.
• **Climate Change:** Rising temperatures, changing precipitation patterns, and increased frequency of extreme events threaten food security, infrastructure, and human well-being.

### Opportunities for Environmental Resilience:

• **Mangrove Restoration:** Coastal ecosystems can provide natural barriers against storms and sea-level rise, support fisheries, and sequester carbon.
• **Blue Carbon Finance:** Innovative financing mechanisms can mobilize resources for conservation and restoration efforts while generating returns on investment.
• **Integrated Solutions:** Combining multiple interventions (e.g., agroforestry, micro-hydro systems) can enhance environmental resilience while promoting economic development.
