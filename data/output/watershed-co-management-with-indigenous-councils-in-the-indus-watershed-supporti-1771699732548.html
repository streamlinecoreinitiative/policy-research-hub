    Context & Challenge

    Sub-Saharan Africa is home to 14 countries with less than 50% access to electricity (World Bank Open Data). Less than half of the population has access to safely managed drinking water, leaving millions at risk for diseases like cholera and typhoid. The region's climate variability is increasing, leading to more frequent droughts and floods, further straining existing infrastructure and services.

    Practical steps:

    * Pilot renewable energy projects in a few countries to demonstrate success before scaling up.
