**Ensuring Water Security, Clean Energy, and Climate Adaptation in Sub-Saharan Africa: A Resilient Path Forward**

## Executive Summary

Sub-Saharan Africa lags behind the world in access to safe drinking water (32.4%) [1] and electricity (53.3%), with climate change exacerbating these challenges [2]. To address this crisis, we recommend investing in renewable energy, improving infrastructure for clean water, enhancing early warning systems, and promoting resilient agricultural practices.

## Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa has limited access to safe drinking water, with only 32.4% of the population having access to safely managed water sources [3]. Climate change impacts have worsened in recent years, threatening existing infrastructure and exacerbating water scarcity issues [4].

The region's reliance on non-renewable energy sources contributes significantly to greenhouse gas emissions (9.2% of global resources withdrawn for fresh water) [5]. Climate change-induced weather extremes have led to increased water demand and stress on existing systems.

## Analysis

Evidence shows that Sub-Saharan Africa relies heavily on non-renewable energy sources, hindering progress towards meeting SDG 6 goals [6]. The lack of investment in renewable energy has exacerbated the region's vulnerability to climate-related disasters.

## Policy Recommendations

1. **Renewable Energy Investment:** Increase public-private partnerships to finance large-scale solar, wind, and hydropower projects. Target at least a 25% reduction in fossil fuel consumption by 2030.
2. **Water Infrastructure Development:** Implement cost-effective water storage solutions like rainwater harvesting tanks and small-scale desalination plants where feasible.
3. **Early Warning Systems:** Develop early warning systems for droughts, floods, and other climate-related events to allow timely response measures.
4. **Climate-Smart Agriculture:** Promote agroforestry, conservation agriculture, and climate-resilient crop varieties to enhance agricultural productivity in arid regions.

To ensure a resilient future for Sub-Saharan Africa, urgent action is required on both climate adaptation and mitigation fronts. By investing in renewable energy, improving water infrastructure, enhancing early warning systems, and promoting climate-smart agriculture, we can significantly improve the quality of life for millions living in poverty.
