### Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa is home to 13 countries with less than 50% electricity access (World Bank Open Data) and 27 countries with less than 70% safe drinking water coverage (UN SDG Report 2023). Climate change exacerbates existing vulnerabilities, leading to more frequent droughts and floods. The region's economic growth, poverty reduction targets (SDGs), and climate change commitments necessitate urgent action.

### Analysis

Sub-Saharan Africa lags behind other regions in achieving SDG 6 (Clean Water and Sanitation) with only [VERIFY]% coverage compared to the global average of 80% (UN SDG Report 2023). Limited financial resources, inadequate governance structures, and inefficient public-private partnerships contribute to this gap. Renewable energy consumption stands at 19.7%, significantly below the global average of 26.5% (World Bank Open Data).

Climate-related disasters have become more frequent due to rising temperatures and changing precipitation patterns, impacting agriculture, water supply, and energy production.

### Policy Recommendations

1. **Water Security**: Invest in sanitation infrastructure such as handwashing stations, latrines, and piped water connections. Collaborate with NGOs to provide hygiene education.
	* Data: 26 million people gained access to improved sanitation facilities between 2000-2017 [Source: WHO/UNICEF Joint Monitoring Report].
2. **Clean Energy Access**: Develop rural electrification programs that integrate solar home systems (SHS) and microgrids for remote communities.
	* Data: Sub-Saharan Africa's electricity access rate has increased by 2 percentage points annually over the last decade [Source: World Bank].
3. **Climate Adaptation**: Implement early warning systems for droughts, floods, and other climate-related events. Promote agroforestry practices to enhance food crop resilience.
	* Data: The region's agricultural productivity could increase by up to 30% with improved climate-smart agriculture [Source: FAO].

### Implementation Considerations

Cost estimates for water security interventions range from $1-2 billion annually, primarily through grants and concessional loans from international organizations. Renewable energy projects require substantial upfront investments but offer long-term benefits in terms of cost savings and reduced carbon emissions (estimated at $0.35-$0.48 per kWh). Climate adaptation measures can leverage existing climate funds like the Global Environment Facility and World Bank to achieve scale.

### Conclusion

Policymakers must prioritize investment in water security, clean energy access, and climate resilience. Collaboration between governments, NGOs, and private sector entities is essential for successful implementation. We urge policymakers to convene high-level meetings to allocate resources, establish clear policy frameworks, and monitor progress through regular reports.

By bridging the gap in water security, clean energy access, and climate resilience, Sub-Saharan Africa can achieve sustainable development and improve the quality of life for millions.
