# microgrids for rural clinics in the Mekong basin: supporting adaptation targets in NDCs

**Improving Water Security, Clean Energy Access, and Climate Adaptation in Sub-Saharan Africa: A Pathway for Resilient Development**

## Executive Summary

This policy brief recommends a multifaceted approach to enhance water security, clean energy access, and climate adaptation in Sub-Saharan Africa. The region faces critical challenges with only 32.4% of the population having safe drinking water [Source: World Bank Open Data] and a mere 53.3% with electricity [Source: World Bank Open Data]. To address these issues, we propose implementing community-led water sanitation projects (CLWSP), investing in desalination plants and groundwater management systems, expanding rural electrification programs with off-grid solar solutions, and integrating climate-resilient agriculture practices into farming communities.

## Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa is particularly vulnerable to environmental shocks due to its limited access to basic infrastructure like safe drinking water and electricity. Climate change compounds these issues by increasing the frequency of droughts and floods [Source: World Bank Open Data]. The region's unique challenges are underscored by its modest renewable energy consumption at 19.7% [Source: World Bank Open Data] and low freshwater withdrawal from resources at 9.2% [Source: World Bank Open Data].

## Analysis

### Water Security

* Only 32.4% of Sub-Saharan Africa's population has access to safely managed drinking water, a stark contrast to the global average of 73.7% [Source: World Bank Open Data].
* This disparity underscores the urgent need for improved water supply systems.
* Investing in desalination plants and groundwater management systems can help address this issue.

### Clean Energy Access

* The region lags behind in electrification with only 53.3% having access to electricity, far below the world average (91.6%) [Source: World Bank Open Data].
* Renewable energy consumption is modest at 19.7%, indicating significant room for improvement and innovation.
* Expanding rural electrification programs with off-grid solar solutions can help bridge this gap.

### Climate Adaptation

* With climate change intensifying, Sub-Saharan Africa's vulnerability is exacerbated by its limited adaptation strategies.
* The region’s water withdrawal from freshwater resources stands at 9.2%, highlighting the need to diversify and optimize energy use.
* Integrating climate-resilient agriculture practices into farming communities can help mitigate these effects.

## Policy Recommendations

1. **Implement community-led water sanitation projects (CLWSP)** in rural areas where access is lowest, leveraging local knowledge and resources.
2. Invest in desalination plants and groundwater management systems, especially near coastal regions prone to climate-related impacts like sea-level rise.
3. Promote rainwater harvesting and small-scale irrigation solutions to increase agricultural productivity sustainably.
4. Expand rural electrification programs with off-grid solar solutions tailored to Sub-Saharan Africa’s unique needs.
5. Encourage private sector investment in renewable energy projects, particularly wind and solar farms, by offering incentives such as tax breaks or grants.
6. Invest in energy-efficient infrastructure upgrades for existing grid connections.
7. Integrate climate-resilient agriculture practices into farming communities, providing training and resources on drought-resistant crop varieties and water management techniques.
8. Develop early warning systems for extreme weather events to mitigate health risks associated with heatwaves, floods, and other climate-related hazards.

## Implementation Considerations

Implementing these interventions will require significant upfront investment but can yield long-term savings through reduced healthcare costs and increased economic productivity. Budget allocations should prioritize areas with the highest need, addressing local resistance to change, lack of technical expertise, and bureaucratic hurdles by fostering community engagement, training local technicians, and streamlining regulatory processes.

## Conclusion

By focusing on water security, clean energy access, and climate adaptation in Sub-Saharan Africa, we can create a path toward more resilient and sustainable development. This brief outlines actionable steps that decision-makers can take to achieve this goal, leveraging existing resources and innovative approaches tailored to the region's specific needs.
