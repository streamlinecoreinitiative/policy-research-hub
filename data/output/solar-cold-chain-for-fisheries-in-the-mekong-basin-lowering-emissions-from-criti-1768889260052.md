**Solar Cold-Chain for Fisheries in the Mekong Basin: Lowering Emissions from Critical Sectors**

## Executive Summary

This policy brief highlights the critical need to address water security, clean energy access, and climate adaptation gaps in Sub-Saharan Africa. With only 32.4% of the population having access to safely managed drinking water (World Bank, 2024) [1] and just 53.3% with electricity (World Bank, 2023) [2], urgent action is needed. We recommend implementing community-led initiatives for water supply, renewable energy integration, and climate-smart agriculture practices.

## Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa faces significant challenges in water security and clean energy access. The region has limited access to safe drinking water (32.4% of population) and electricity (53.3%) [1][2]. Climate change exacerbates these issues, with projected increases in extreme weather events affecting water availability and energy infrastructure.

## Analysis

### Water Security

Sub-Saharan Africa faces a severe water crisis, with only 32.4% of the population having access to safely managed drinking water (World Bank, 2024) [1]. This disparity is exacerbated by limited rainfall variability and overexploitation of groundwater resources.

### Clean Energy Access

The region lags behind in clean energy provision, with just 53.3% of the population accessing electricity (World Bank, 2023) [2]. High costs, unreliable grid infrastructure, and limited renewable energy integration hinder progress towards universal access.

## Policy Recommendations

1. **Community-Led Water Supply Initiatives**: Implement community-managed water points that prioritize water quality and hygiene education to improve access in rural areas.
	* Action Steps:
		+ Develop localized water management plans with input from communities.
		+ Allocate funds for infrastructure development, including boreholes and rainwater harvesting systems.
2. **Renewable Energy Integration**: Invest in solar microgrids and off-grid renewable energy solutions (e.g., solar home systems) to increase electricity access.
	* Action Steps:
		- Provide incentives for private sector investment in renewable energy projects.
		- Implement grid extension strategies, such as mini-grids and community-owned power plants.
3. **Climate-Smart Agriculture**: Promote climate-resilient agricultural practices that integrate water management, soil conservation, and drought-resistant crop varieties.
	* Action Steps:
		+ Establish demonstration farms to showcase best practices in agriculture and water use efficiency.
		+ Provide training on sustainable farming techniques to farmers.

## Implementation Considerations

- **Costs**: Community-led initiatives can be funded through grants, donations from international organizations, or local government contributions. Renewable energy projects require upfront investment but offer long-term benefits.
- **Barriers**:
  - Limited financial resources and political will at the national level.
  - Resistance to change from traditional water management systems.

## Conclusion

Addressing the water security, clean energy access, and climate adaptation challenges in Sub-Saharan Africa requires a multi-faceted approach that prioritizes community engagement and sustainable resource management. By implementing evidence-based policies and integrating renewable energy solutions, we can enhance resilience and improve quality of life for millions of people living in these regions.

## Next Steps

Collaborate with local governments, NGOs, and private sector partners to scale up successful projects. Advocate for increased funding, policy support, and technical assistance to accelerate progress towards universal access to safe water and electricity.
