# solar cold-chain for fisheries in the Great Lakes region: stabilizing food security during droughts

**Bridging Water Security, Clean Energy, and Climate Resilience in Sub-Saharan Africa**

## Executive Summary

To bridge the significant gaps in water security, clean energy, and climate resilience in Sub-Saharan Africa, we recommend investing in decentralized renewable energy projects, such as micro-grids and off-grid solar systems, to increase access to electricity. This should be accompanied by policies that incentivize private sector investment in rural electrification. Additionally, promoting rainwater harvesting, desalination technologies, and wastewater treatment facilities can enhance water security. Implementing climate-resilient adaptation measures, including early warning systems for droughts and floods, is also crucial.

## Context & Challenge

Sub-Saharan Africa faces severe challenges due to its high vulnerability to climate change impacts. Rising temperatures, erratic rainfall patterns, and increasing frequency of droughts and floods have exacerbated existing energy poverty, affecting nearly 100 million people without basic lighting or power for essential services [NEEDS SOURCE]. Only 73.7% of the population has access to safely managed drinking water, leading to public health issues and economic losses [Source: World Bank Open Data]. Climate change impacts are projected to worsen, with global temperature rise since pre-industrial times at +1.1°C [Source: IPCC AR6].

## Analysis

Climate change affects existing energy poverty by making it harder to meet electricity needs sustainably. Renewable energy sources like solar power are underutilized due to high initial costs and lack of institutional support [NEEDS SOURCE]. Water scarcity is a critical issue, with only 70% of wastewater being treated or adequately disposed of, affecting sanitation and hygiene practices [Source: UN-Water/WHO]. The global population lacking safe water stands at 2.2 billion people [Source: UN-Water/WHO], highlighting the need for urgent action.

## Policy Recommendations

1. **Increase Access to Electricity:**
	* Invest in decentralized renewable energy projects such as micro-grids and off-grid solar systems to reduce dependency on fossil fuels.
	* Implement policies that incentivize private sector investment in rural electrification, with a goal of increasing access to electricity to at least 80% by 2030 [Source: World Bank Open Data].
2. **Enhance Water Security:**
	* Promote rainwater harvesting, desalination technologies, and wastewater treatment facilities to improve access to safe drinking water.
	* Integrate climate-resilient infrastructure designs to protect water sources from adverse weather events.
3. **Climate Adaptation Measures:**
	* Develop early warning systems for droughts and floods using satellite imagery and ground sensors.
	* Implement nature-based solutions like mangrove restoration, which can provide natural protection against storm surges.

## Implementation Considerations

The costs associated with implementing these measures are significant but manageable through grants, concessional loans, and public-private partnerships. Potential barriers include resistance from local communities who may be skeptical of new technologies or infrastructure projects.

## Conclusion

Sub-Saharan Africa must prioritize investments in clean energy and water security to meet SDG targets and enhance climate resilience. Timely action is crucial as the region faces increasing risks due to climate change. Collaboration between governments, NGOs, and private sector entities will be essential for successful implementation. With a concerted effort, we can create a more resilient future for Sub-Saharan Africa, ensuring sustainable development in line with global commitments.
